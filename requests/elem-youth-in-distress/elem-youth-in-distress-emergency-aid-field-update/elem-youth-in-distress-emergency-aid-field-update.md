## The Ask

### Operational Field Update (Early March 2026)

ELEM provides a war-time operational update following the launch of Operation "Roaring Lion." Across all programs, teams maintain continuous contact with at-risk teens and young people and provide support tailored to emerging needs on the ground. In line with national safety guidelines, most support is delivered remotely. Pre-existing trust and relationships have kept these connections meaningful, responsive, and effective.

To ensure accessible and immediate response after recent events, ELEM has:

- Extended Digital Outreach Support to operate on weekdays until midnight.
- Reinforced digital teams with specially trained staff (Hebrew and Arabic) to handle heightened emotional distress among teens unable to leave home.
- Strengthened 24/7 shelters in Jerusalem, Haifa, and Tel Aviv with full professional staffing on every shift to provide immediate, trauma-informed care, particularly for young people at the highest end of the risk spectrum.
- Continued to support shelter residents who must remain indoors around the clock and are experiencing intensified triggers and emotional distress; graduates who had transitioned to independent living are also returning during the day for support.

The organization requests general support to continue these emergency operations, respond swiftly as conditions evolve, expand services as needed, and maintain operational continuity under highly challenging circumstances.

**Funding Requested (field update):** Not specified.

### Emergency Funding Request (March 8, 2026)

ELEM submits a specific emergency funding request to sustain and expand its war-time response for vulnerable teens and young adults. Since the start of Operation Roaring Lion, ELEM has shifted to emergency operations while maintaining continuous remote contact and has significantly expanded digital and shelter-based services.

The emergency request focuses on four program areas:

1. **Reinforced Teams — 24/7 Shelters (Jerusalem, Haifa, Tel Aviv)**  
   - Increased staffing levels in ELEM’s three 24/7 shelters to ensure every shift is fully staffed with professionals trained to provide immediate, trauma-informed care.  
   - Addresses intensified triggers and emotional distress among residents who must remain indoors around the clock.  
   - Provides additional support to graduates who had transitioned to independent living and are returning during the day for help and to avoid being alone.

2. **Reinforced Teams — ELEM Digital (National Online Support)**  
   - Expansion of ELEM Digital, a nationwide online support service available to all teens in Israel, including those not previously engaged with ELEM.  
   - Reinforced staffing in both Hebrew and Arabic to accommodate increased demand and heightened emotional distress.  
   - Operations extended on weekdays until midnight with specially trained teams for the war period.

3. **Calming Kits for Shelter Residents**  
   - Preparation and distribution of calming kits for youth staying in the shelters.  
   - Kits include practical items to help reduce anxiety and regulate stress (e.g., headphones, coloring booklets, stress balls, grounding tools).  
   - Designated specifically for young people in ELEM’s direct care within the shelters.

4. **Food Baskets**  
   - Provision of food baskets to address growing food insecurity among youth and families served by ELEM in specific areas.  
   - In Eilat, ELEM’s team has already distributed food baskets, with additional needs emerging in other locations.

**Total Emergency Funding Requested:**  
- **Amount:** $50,485 (156,000 NIS)  
- **Budget Breakdown:**  
  - Reinforced teams — 24/7 Shelters: $32,686 (101,000 NIS)  
  - Reinforced teams — ELEM Digital: $8,091 (25,000 NIS)  
  - Food Baskets: $6,472 (20,000 NIS)  
  - Calming kits for youth in shelters: $3,236 (10,000 NIS)

ELEM requests support to enable a swift and flexible emergency response, further expansion of services as needed, and sustained operational continuity under difficult and rapidly changing conditions.

## Context & Background

ELEM (עלם – Youth in Distress) has been updating its funders, including Barak Loozon of the Jewish Federation, on its activities since the onset of Operation "Roaring Lion." The situation remains fluid, yet ELEM emphasizes its unwavering commitment to teens and young people in both routine and emergency periods.

Key contextual points:

- **Continuity of Care:**  
  Teams maintain continuous contact with teens and young adults, adapting support to rapidly changing needs. National safety guidelines require that most services be delivered remotely, but prior relationships ensure that interactions remain meaningful and effective.

- **Expanded Digital Outreach:**  
  ELEM Digital, the organization’s national online support platform, has been expanded to operate on weekdays until midnight, with reinforced Hebrew- and Arabic-speaking teams trained specifically for the current emergency context. This ensures that youth who cannot leave home still receive immediate help.

- **Strengthened 24/7 Shelters:**  
  ELEM’s 24/7 shelters in Jerusalem, Haifa, and Tel Aviv serve young people at the highest end of the risk spectrum. Staffing has been increased so that every shift is fully staffed by professionals capable of providing immediate, trauma-informed care. Residents are facing intensified emotional distress due to being confined indoors, and graduates who previously moved to independent living are returning for daytime support.

- **Calming Kits and Food Assistance:**  
  To address acute stress and basic needs, ELEM is preparing calming kits for youth in shelters and distributing food baskets in areas where needs are emerging, such as Eilat and additional locations as identified.

The March 8, 2026 emergency document provides a preliminary assessment covering approximately two weeks and presents the detailed emergency budget (156,000 NIS / $50,485) for reinforcing digital and shelter teams, calming kits, and food baskets.

**March 8, 2026**  
**ELEM Emergency Response - Current Needs**

In light of the ongoing war, ELEM has shifted to emergency operations to ensure continuity of care for vulnerable teens and young adults.

**Emergency Programs Requiring Additional Funding**

1. **Reinforcement of ELEM Digital (National Service)**  
   ELEM Digital is a nationwide online support service available to all teens in Israel, not only those previously engaged with ELEM. Due to the current situation, staffing is being reinforced to expand support, both in Hebrew and Arabic, for teens who are unable to leave their homes and are experiencing heightened emotional distress. The increase in demand is already evident among both Hebrew- and Arabic-speaking youth.

2. **Strengthening 24/7 Shelters – Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Haifa**  
   The shelters operate around the clock and are staffed by ELEM's professional teams. Staffing levels are being increased to ensure continuous, high-quality, trauma-informed care. Young people in the shelters are experiencing intensified triggers and emotional distress and require significantly more support than usual, particularly as they must remain indoors around the clock. In addition to current residents, graduates who had transitioned to independent living are now returning during the day because they need support and do not want to remain alone.

3. **Calming Kits for Shelter Residents**  
   Calming kits are being prepared for youth staying in the shelters. These kits include practical items designed to help reduce anxiety and regulate stress, such as headphones, coloring booklets, stress balls, and other grounding tools. The kits are designated for young people in ELEM’s direct care.

4. **Food Baskets**  
   A growing need for food assistance has been identified in certain areas among the population served by ELEM. In Eilat, the team has already distributed food baskets, and ELEM is working to respond to additional needs as they arise.

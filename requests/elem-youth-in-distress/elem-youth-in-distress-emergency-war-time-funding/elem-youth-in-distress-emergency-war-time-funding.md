## The Ask

ELEM requests emergency funding to sustain and expand its war-time response for vulnerable teens and young adults. Since the launch of Operation "Roaring Lion," the organization has shifted to emergency operations: reinforcing its nationwide digital support service (including Arabic), strengthening 24/7 shelters with trauma-informed staffing, providing calming kits to shelter residents, and distributing food baskets where needed. Total emergency budget requested is $50,485 (NIS 156,000) to cover reinforced teams, calming kits, and food assistance over the current period.

**Funding Requested:** $50,485

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Reinforced teams - 24/7 Shelters (Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Haifa): $32,686
- Reinforced teams - ELEM Digital (national service): $8,091
- Food Baskets: $6,472
- Calming kits for youth in shelters: $3,236

## Context & Background

ELEM provides support to teens and young adults in distress across Israel. Since the start of Operation "Roaring Lion," circumstances have shifted rapidly; the organization has maintained continuous contact with youth and tailored support to emerging needs, mostly delivered remotely in line with national safety guidelines. Trust built in advance has kept these connections meaningful and effective.

At the same time, ELEM has expanded several services for an accessible, immediate response: Digital Outreach Support now runs on weekdays until midnight with reinforced, specially trained teams; 24/7 shelters in Jerusalem, Haifa, and Tel Aviv have been strengthened so every shift is fully staffed and able to provide trauma-informed care. Shelter residents are facing intensified triggers and emotional distress and need more support than usual, especially while remaining indoors; shelter graduates have also been returning during the day because they need support and do not want to be alone.

The attached document is based on an initial assessment for approximately two weeks; ELEM is updating needs as the situation develops. The request was sent in response to a funder’s ask for details on unique projects and expenses related to the current situation, with the goal of engaging private donors. Support would enable ELEM to respond swiftly, expand services as needed, and maintain operational continuity under highly challenging conditions.

### Emergency Activities _ March 2026.pdf

**March 8, 2026**

**ELEM Emergency Response - Current Needs**

In light of the ongoing war, ELEM has shifted to emergency operations to ensure continuity of care for vulnerable teens and young adults.

**Emergency Programs Requiring Additional Funding**

1. **Reinforcement of ELEM Digital (National Service)**

ELEM Digital is a nationwide online support service available to all teens in Israel, not only those previously engaged with ELEM. Due to the current situation, we are reinforcing staffing to expand support, both in Hebrew and Arabic, for teens who are unable to leave their homes and are experiencing heightened emotional distress. The increase in demand is already evident among both Hebrew and Arabic speaking youth.

2. **Strengthening 24/7 Shelters – Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Haifa**

Our shelters operate around the clock and are staffed by ELEM's professional teams. We are increasing staffing levels to ensure continuous, high-quality, trauma-informed care. The young people in our shelters are experiencing intensified triggers and emotional distress and require significantly more support than usual, particularly as they must remain indoors around the clock. In addition to current residents, graduates who had transitioned to independent living are now returning during the day because they need support and do not want to remain alone.

3. **Calming Kits for Shelter Residents**

We are preparing calming kits for youth staying in our shelters. These kits include practical items designed to help reduce anxiety and regulate stress, such as headphones, coloring booklets, stress balls, and other grounding tools. The kits are designated for the young people in our direct care.

4. **Food Baskets**

We have identified a growing need for food assistance in certain areas among the population we serve. In Eilat, our team has already distributed food baskets, and we are working to respond to additional needs as they arise.

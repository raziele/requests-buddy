## The Ask

ELEM first provided a war-update (Mar 6) on activities since Operation "Roaring Lion," asking for support to respond swiftly, expand services as needed, and maintain operational continuity under highly challenging conditions — **funding amount not specified** in that update.

They then requested **emergency funding** (Mar 8) to sustain and expand the war-time response for vulnerable teens and young adults. They have shifted to emergency operations while maintaining continuous remote contact and have expanded Digital Outreach Support (weekdays until midnight, Hebrew and Arabic) and strengthened 24/7 shelters with trauma-informed staffing. Additional needs include calming kits for shelter residents and food baskets in areas such as Eilat.

**Funding Requested (Mar 8 request):** $50,485 (156,000 NIS)

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Reinforced teams - 24/7 Shelters: $32,686 (101,000 NIS)
- Reinforced teams - ELEM Digital: $8,091 (25,000 NIS)
- Food Baskets: $6,472 (20,000 NIS)
- Calming kits for youth in shelters: $3,236 (10,000 NIS)

## Context & Background

ELEM (עלם – Youth in Distress) has been updating the federation (Barak Loozon) on its activities since the start of Operation Roaring Lion. Circumstances continue to shift rapidly; the organization emphasizes that its commitment to the teens and young people it supports remains unwavering in both routine and emergency periods.

Across all programs, teams maintain continuous contact with teens and provide support tailored to emerging needs on the ground. In line with national safety guidelines, most support is currently delivered remotely. Trust built in advance has kept these connections meaningful, responsive, and effective.

ELEM has expanded several services: Digital Outreach Support now runs on weekdays until midnight with reinforced, specially trained teams and serves Hebrew- and Arabic-speaking youth who cannot leave home and are in heightened distress. The 24/7 shelters in Jerusalem, Haifa, and Tel Aviv have been strengthened with professional staffing so every shift can provide immediate, trauma-informed care. Shelter residents are facing intensified triggers and emotional distress and need more support than usual; graduates who had moved to independent living are returning during the day for support. The organization has requested support to enable a swift response, expansion of services as needed, and operational continuity under difficult conditions.

The attached March 8, 2026 document summarizes a preliminary assessment for approximately two weeks and outlines four emergency program areas: reinforcement of ELEM Digital (national online support), strengthening of the three 24/7 shelters, calming kits for shelter residents (headphones, coloring booklets, stress balls, grounding tools), and food baskets (already distributed in Eilat with more needs emerging). The total emergency budget is 156,000 NIS ($50,485).

### Emergency Activities _ March 2026.pdf

**March 8, 2026**
**ELEM Emergency Response - Current Needs**

In light of the ongoing war, ELEM has shifted to emergency operations to ensure continuity of care for vulnerable teens and young adults.

**Emergency Programs Requiring Additional Funding**

1. **Reinforcement of ELEM Digital (National Service)**
ELEM Digital is a nationwide online support service available to all teens in Israel, not only those previously engaged with ELEM. Due to the current situation, we are reinforcing staffing to expand support, both in Hebrew and Arabic, for teens who are unable to leave their homes and are experiencing heightened emotional distress. The increase in demand is already evident among both Hebrew and Arabic speaking youth.

2. **Strengthening 24/7 Shelters – Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Haifa**
Our shelters operate around the clock and are staffed by ELEM's professional teams. We are increasing staffing levels to ensure continuous, high-quality, trauma-informed care. The young people in our shelters are experiencing intensified triggers and emotional distress and require significantly more support than usual, particularly as they must remain indoors around the clock. In addition to current residents, graduates who had transitioned to independent living are now returning during the day because they need support and do not want to remain alone.

3. **Calming Kits for Shelter Residents**
We are preparing calming kits for youth staying in our shelters. These kits include practical items designed to help reduce anxiety and regulate stress, such as headphones, coloring booklets, stress balls, and other grounding tools. The kits are designated for the young people in our direct care.

4. **Food Baskets**
We have identified a growing need for food assistance in certain areas among the population we serve. In Eilat, our team has already distributed food baskets, and we are working to respond to additional needs as they arise.

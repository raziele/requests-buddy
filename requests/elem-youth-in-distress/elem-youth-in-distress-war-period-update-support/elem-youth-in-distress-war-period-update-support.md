## The Ask

ELEM is providing a war-period update following the launch of Operation Roaring Lion. The organization has maintained continuous contact with at-risk teens across all programs, delivering most support remotely in line with national safety guidelines. It has expanded Digital Outreach Support to operate on weekdays until midnight with reinforced, specially trained teams, and has strengthened 24/7 shelters in Jerusalem, Haifa, and Tel Aviv with full professional staffing and trauma-informed care. ELEM is seeking ongoing support to respond swiftly, expand services as needed, and maintain operational continuity under highly challenging conditions.

## Context & Background

ELEM (עלם), an Israeli association for youth in distress, is updating its federation contact on activities since the launch of Operation Roaring Lion. Circumstances are shifting rapidly; the organization emphasizes that its commitment to the teens and young people it supports remains unchanged in both routine and emergency periods.

Across all programs, teams are maintaining continuous contact with teens and providing support tailored to needs emerging on the ground. Thanks to trust and relationships built in advance, these connections remain meaningful and effective even though most support is currently delivered remotely in accordance with national safety guidelines.

To ensure an accessible and immediate response for those in distress after recent events, ELEM has expanded services in two main areas: Digital Outreach Support now runs on weekdays until midnight with reinforced teams who have received specialized training for this period; and 24/7 shelters for the highest-risk young people in Jerusalem, Haifa, and Tel Aviv have been strengthened with professional staffing so that every shift is fully staffed and able to provide immediate, trauma-informed care. The organization states that support will enable it to respond swiftly, expand services as needed, and maintain operational continuity under highly challenging conditions.

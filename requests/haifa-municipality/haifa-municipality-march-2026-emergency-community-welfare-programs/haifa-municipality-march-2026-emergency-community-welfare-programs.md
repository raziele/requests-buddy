## The Ask

The City of Haifa is requesting support for a slate of emergency and routine programs for March 2026, in the context of the ongoing war. The municipality has outlined initiatives in education and community (e.g. reservist reintegration, pathfinding for released soldiers, support for reservist families, and protected learning spaces) and a detailed welfare section covering resilience and trauma response, mobile play and respite for children in shelters, emotional support for people with special needs, Jewish–Arab youth programming, emergency mediation training, VR-based programming for elderly, elder abuse prevention, emotional resilience for children and early childhood, support for Haredi parents, trauma training for professionals, and a volunteer management system. The sender indicated that items highlighted in yellow in the document are of the highest urgency.

**Funding Requested:** 1,994,000 NIS (welfare programs); education/community program costs to be completed (יושלם בהמשך)

## Context & Background

The City of Haifa is operating under wartime conditions (מלחמת שאגת הארי) and has compiled a unified program document for both emergency and routine activities. The document is organized into education/community initiatives and welfare initiatives, with the welfare section fully costed and the education section indicating that costs will be completed later.

In welfare, the municipality aims to scale resilience and trauma response across six districts (חוסן העיר), build a civil-society incubator for emergency coordination, train welfare staff in trauma and first-line psychological intervention (including EMDR-based methods), deploy a mobile play and resilience space in shelters and parent centers, and support emotional resilience for people with special needs, Jewish–Arab youth, elderly (including VR and elder-abuse prevention), children, early childhood, Haredi parents, and frontline professionals. A dedicated software system for volunteer management is also requested to improve intake, placement, and retention.

The sender (Doron Shapir) forwarded the document to Barak Loozon at the Jewish Federation Israel Office with a short note that items in yellow in the original document are of the highest urgency, indicating prioritization within the overall ask.

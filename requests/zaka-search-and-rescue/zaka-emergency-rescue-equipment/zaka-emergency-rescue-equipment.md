## The Ask

ZAKA is requesting funding to procure essential search-and-rescue equipment for its Home Front Command–trained civilian rescue unit, the only volunteer unit in Israel authorized to conduct engineering-level rescue operations at collapsed structures and direct impact sites. The request covers thermal imaging systems, portable lighting towers, firefighter rescue cutting tools, and ambulance rescue tool kits to equip teams responding to missile strikes, structural collapses, and mass-casualty incidents. The organization is asking whether the Federation has emergency funds or Donor Advised Funds interested in supporting this equipment.

**Funding Requested:** $1,572,900

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Thermal Imaging Systems: $62,400
- Portable Lighting Towers: $1,133,100
- Firefighter Rescue Cutting Tools: $221,400
- Ambulance Rescue Tool Kits: $156,000

## Context & Background

In light of the escalating security situation in Israel and continued attacks on civilian communities, ZAKA volunteers are operating at the front lines of complex emergency scenes across the country, including missile impacts from Iran and its regional proxies, structural collapses, and mass-casualty incidents. Recently, ZAKA teams responded to a direct missile strike on a residential building in Beit Shemesh in which nine civilians were killed, working alongside Israel's security and rescue forces under conditions of structural destruction, darkness, and ongoing danger.

ZAKA's national volunteer network includes more than 3,850 volunteers, approximately 900 of whom are specially trained by Israel's Home Front Command in advanced search-and-rescue operations. These teams frequently operate inside unstable structures following missile strikes and building collapses. The equipment requested is designated for ZAKA's HFC Rescue Unit, which operates in cooperation with and is trained by the Home Front Command. This unit is the only civilian team of its kind in Israel that conducts engineering-level rescue operations at sites of building collapses and direct impacts, working alongside the IDF Home Front Command Rescue Unit.

Given the increasing number of simultaneous emergency incidents across the country, ZAKA must equip dozens of response teams to ensure rapid deployment and effective response nationwide. The tools are essential for enabling teams to operate safely and professionally in complex structural collapse scenarios, including locating trapped victims in rubble and darkness, enabling rescue operations during nighttime missile strikes and blackouts, and breaching metal and reinforced structures to free trapped victims.

The request was relayed to Ofer Lichtig (Israel Representative, Greater Miami Jewish Federation). The Federation had not launched another emergency campaign as of the exchange; ZAKA offered to send a project card for any interested Donor Advised Funds and asked to be kept in mind if an emergency campaign is launched.

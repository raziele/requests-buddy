## The Ask

ZAKA is requesting funding for essential search-and-rescue equipment for its Home Front Command–trained civilian rescue unit, the only volunteer unit in Israel authorized to perform engineering-level rescue at collapsed structures and direct impact sites. The organization needs firefighter rescue cutting tools, thermal imaging systems, portable light towers, and ambulance rescue tool kits to equip teams responding to missile strikes and building collapses nationwide. Total requested is $1,572,900. The ask is directed to the Miami Federation for emergency funds or Donor Advised Funds (DAF) interested in supporting rescue equipment.

**Funding Requested:** $1,572,900

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Thermal Imaging Systems (12 units): $62,400
- Portable Lighting Towers (60 units): $1,133,100
- Firefighter Rescue Cutting Tools (18 units): $221,400
- Ambulance Rescue Tool Kits (60 units): $156,000

## Context & Background

ZAKA volunteers are operating at the front lines of complex emergency scenes across Israel amid escalating security and continued attacks on civilian communities, including missile impacts from Iran and regional proxies, structural collapses, and mass-casualty incidents. Recently, ZAKA teams responded to a direct missile strike on a residential building in Beit Shemesh in which nine civilians were killed, working alongside Israel’s security and rescue forces under conditions of structural destruction, darkness, and ongoing danger.

ZAKA’s national volunteer network includes more than 3,850 volunteers, with approximately 900 specially trained by Israel’s Home Front Command in advanced search-and-rescue operations. These teams frequently operate inside unstable structures following missile strikes and building collapses. The equipment requested is designated for ZAKA’s HFC Rescue Unit, which operates in cooperation with and is trained by the Home Front Command; it is the only civilian team of its kind in Israel that conducts engineering-level rescue operations at sites of building collapses and direct impacts.

Given the increasing number of simultaneous emergency incidents across the country, ZAKA must equip dozens of response teams to ensure rapid deployment and effective response nationwide. The tools are essential for enabling teams to operate safely and professionally in complex structural collapse scenarios. Support would strengthen Israel’s civilian emergency resilience and allow ZAKA to continue responding professionally while upholding its mission of preserving human dignity at disaster sites.

In light of the escalating security situation in Israel and the continued attacks on civilian communities, ZAKA volunteers are operating at the front lines of complex emergency scenes across the country, including missile impacts from Iran and its regional proxies, structural collapses, and mass-casualty incidents.

Recently, ZAKA teams responded to a direct missile strike on a residential building in Beit Shemesh in which nine civilians were killed. Volunteers arrived within minutes and worked alongside Israel's security and rescue forces under extremely difficult conditions of structural destruction, darkness, and ongoing danger.

To ensure these volunteers can continue responding effectively, ZAKA is mobilizing resources to procure essential technical search-and-rescue equipment for its Home Front Command-trained civilian rescue unit — the only volunteer unit in Israel authorized to participate in engineering-level rescue operations at collapsed structures.

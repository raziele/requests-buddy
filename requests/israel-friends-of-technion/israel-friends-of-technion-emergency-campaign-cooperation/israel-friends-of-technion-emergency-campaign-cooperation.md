## The Ask

Israel Friends of Technion is reaching out to federation representatives to explore cooperation and support for the Technion's emergency campaign. Since October 7 and the recent escalation, over 1,500 students and faculty have been called to reserves (Tzav 8), with more expected. The Technion has committed to wrapping reservists in unprecedented financial, academic, and emotional support so that no one is left behind and academic excellence is maintained. The organization frames support for Technion students as a direct investment in national security and resilience, given the institution's role in defense systems (e.g., Iron Dome, Arrow, David's Sling). The CEO offers to discuss details and tailor an appeal to each federation's needs and goals.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Exploration of partnership and support opportunities with federations; willingness to provide further details and tailor a formal appeal to each federation's needs and goals.

## Context & Background

The email is from the CEO of Israel Friends of Technion, writing to former federation colleagues from her "new home" at the Technion. She acknowledges the unusual step of asking for support rather than sharing an update, and the intensity of federation work during the war.

Since October 7 and especially after the recent escalation, the Technion has been on the front line of the home front. Over 1,500 students, faculty, and staff have been (re-)called to reserves under Tzav 8, with more call-ups expected. For the past three years the Technion has made it a priority to support reservists with comprehensive wraparound services—financial, academic, and emotional—so that no one is left behind and academic excellence is not compromised.

The link between the Technion and Israel's security is described as inseparable: defense systems such as Iron Dome, Arrow, and David's Sling are developed by Technion graduates. Supporting Technion students is framed as a direct investment in national security, economic resilience, and the innovations that will drive Israel forward after the war. The writer notes that when Israel missions resume, the Technion will be a key destination to see Israeli human capital at its best.

There is no specific funding figure or itemized budget in the message. The ask is to explore opportunities for federation cooperation and support for the Technion's emergency campaign, with an offer to talk, share more details, and craft an appeal suited to each federation's needs and goals.

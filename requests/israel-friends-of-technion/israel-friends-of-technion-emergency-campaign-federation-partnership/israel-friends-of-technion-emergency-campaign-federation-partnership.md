## The Ask

Israel Friends of Technion is reaching out to federation representatives to explore collaboration and support for the Technion's emergency campaign. Since October 7 and the recent escalation, over 1,500 Technion students and faculty have been called to reserve duty, with more expected. The Technion has spent the last three years providing unprecedented economic, academic, and emotional support to reservists so that no one is left behind and academic excellence is maintained. The letter frames support for Technion students as a direct investment in national security and Israel's future, and asks federations to consider partnership and tailored support aligned with their goals.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Exploration of collaboration and support opportunities with federations; willingness to discuss, share further details, and craft a tailored request that fits each federation's needs and goals.

## Context & Background

The email is sent by the CEO of Israel Friends of Technion (Hilla Perlman Pniel) to former colleagues and contacts across multiple Jewish federations. She notes the awkwardness of sending a support request rather than a routine FCR update, and writes from her "new home" at the Technion, which she describes as being on the front line of the home front since October 7 and the recent escalation.

Over 1,500 students, faculty, and staff have been (re-)called to reserve duty under Tzav 8, with more call-ups expected. For the past three years the Technion has made it a priority to wrap reservists in comprehensive support—economic, academic, and emotional—so that no one is left behind and academic excellence is not compromised. The letter stresses the inseparable link between the Technion and Israel's security: systems such as Iron Dome, Arrow, and David's Sling were developed by Technion graduates. Supporting Technion students is therefore framed as a direct investment in national security, economic resilience, and the innovations that will advance Israel after the war.

The writer anticipates that when Israel missions resume, the Technion will be a key destination. She asks recipients to explore opportunities for collaboration and support from their federations for the Technion's emergency campaign, and offers to discuss, provide more detail, and tailor a request to each federation's needs and objectives. No specific dollar amount or itemized budget is mentioned.

## The Ask

Israel Friends of Technion, through CEO Hilla Perlman Pniel, is reaching out to federation representatives to explore cooperation, collaboration, and support for the Technion's emergency campaign. Since October 7 and the recent escalation, the Technion has been on the front line of the home front: more than 1,500 students and faculty have been (re-)called to reserves under Tzav 8, with more call-ups expected. The institution has committed to wrapping reservists in unprecedented financial, academic, and emotional (economic, academic, and emotional) support so that no one is left behind and academic excellence is maintained. Support for Technion students is framed as a direct investment in national security and resilience and in Israel's future. The CEO offers to discuss details, share more information, and tailor an appeal or proposal to each federation's needs and goals.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Exploration of partnership, collaboration, and support opportunities with federations; willingness to provide further details and to craft an appeal or proposal suited to each federation's needs and goals.

## Context & Background

The email is from the CEO of Israel Friends of Technion, writing from her "new home" at the Technion to former federation colleagues. She frames the message as a request for support rather than a routine update (in one version: rather than a routine FCR update) and acknowledges the unusual step of asking for support and the intensity of federation work during the war; she also hopes the intensive work provides some distraction from the war.

Since October 7 and especially after the recent escalation, the Technion has been at the forefront of the home front. Over 1,500 students, faculty, and staff have been (re-)called to reserves under Tzav 8, with more call-ups expected. For the past three years the Technion has made it a priority to support reservists with comprehensive wraparound services—financial, academic, and emotional (also described as economic, academic, and emotional)—so that no one is left behind and academic excellence is not compromised.

The link between the Technion and Israel's security is described as inseparable: defense systems such as Iron Dome, Arrow, and David's Sling are developed by Technion graduates. Supporting Technion students is framed as a direct investment in national security, economic resilience, and the innovations that will drive Israel forward after the war. When Israel missions resume, the Technion will be a key destination to see Israeli human capital at its best—a future generation from all sectors shaping the country's future.

There is no specific funding figure or itemized budget. The ask is exploratory: to identify opportunities for federation cooperation, collaboration, and support for the Technion's emergency campaign, with an offer to talk, share more details, and craft an appeal or proposal suited to each federation's needs and goals. Contact and organizational details are confirmed from the signature image: Israel Friends of Technion, CEO Hilla Perlman Pniel, Technion City, Haifa.

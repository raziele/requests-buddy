## The Ask

Israel Friends of Technion, through CEO Hilla Perlman Pniel, is reaching out from Technion City to federation representatives to explore cooperation, collaboration, and support for the Technion’s emergency campaign.

Since October 7 and the recent escalation, the Technion has been described as being on the front line of the home front. More than 1,500 students, faculty, and staff have been (re-)called to reserves under Tzav 8, with further mobilizations expected. The Technion has made it a strategic priority over the past three years to provide reservists with comprehensive wraparound support—economic/financial, academic, and emotional—so that no one is left behind and academic excellence is maintained.

The appeal consistently frames support for Technion students and reservists as a direct investment in Israel’s national security, economic resilience, and future innovation. Defense systems such as Iron Dome, Arrow, and David’s Sling are cited as emblematic examples of technologies developed by Technion graduates, underscoring the institution’s central role in Israel’s security ecosystem and post-war recovery.

The request is explicitly exploratory rather than a fixed-budget proposal. Israel Friends of Technion is asking federations to:

- Explore partnership, collaboration, and support opportunities for the Technion’s emergency campaign.
- Engage in conversation to understand each federation’s needs, goals, and priorities.
- Work together to shape a tailored appeal or proposal (including program details and framing) that fits each federation’s context and objectives.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified.

**Non-Financial Ask:** Willingness from federations to engage in dialogue, explore collaboration, and consider partnership opportunities; openness to receive further information and a customized proposal aligned with federation needs and goals.

## Context & Background

The emails are written by Hilla Perlman Pniel from her “new home” at the Technion, reflecting her transition from the federation world to the Israel Friends of Technion. She explicitly acknowledges that she is taking the unusual step of asking for support rather than sharing a routine update, and she recognizes how intensely federations are working during the war and hopes that work provides some measure of distraction and purpose.

Both versions emphasize that since October 7—and especially following the latest escalation—the Technion is at the forefront of Israel’s home front. Over 1,500 students, faculty, and staff have been (or are being) mobilized again under Tzav 8, with additional call-ups anticipated. In response, the Technion has committed to an unprecedented package of support for reservists:

- **Economic/financial support** to reduce or remove financial barriers created by reserve duty and wartime disruption.
- **Academic support** to ensure that reservists can successfully continue and complete their studies without compromising academic excellence or being left behind their peers.
- **Emotional and psychosocial support** to address trauma, stress, and the overall impact of war and reserve service on students and staff.

The connection between the Technion and Israel’s security and resilience is described as inseparable. Systems like Iron Dome, Arrow, and David’s Sling—developed by Technion graduates—are given as concrete examples of how the Technion directly contributes to national defense and technological leadership. Supporting Technion students and reservists is thus framed as:

- A direct investment in **national security**.
- A contributor to **economic resilience**, by sustaining Israel’s high-tech ecosystem.
- A way to fuel the **innovation** that will drive Israel forward once the war ends.

The emails also point to the Technion’s broader role in showcasing Israeli human capital and diversity. When Israel missions and delegations eventually resume, the Technion is expected to be a central stop for visitors to see the “future generation” of Israelis—students from all sectors of society—who are shaping the country’s future through science, technology, and leadership.

The outreach to federations is framed as:

- A personal and professional bridge from Hilla’s prior federation experience to her current leadership role at Israel Friends of Technion.
- An invitation to partner in bolstering Technion reservists and the academic community during an emergency period.
- An opening to ongoing relationship-building between federations and the Technion, with the possibility of mission visits and deeper engagement down the line.

There is no fixed budget breakdown or numerical request in the emails; instead, the focus is on opening a dialogue and co-creating an appeal or proposal that resonates with each federation’s priorities and capacities.

## The Ask

Israel Friends of Technion, through CEO Hilla Perlman Pniel, is reaching out to federation representatives to explore collaboration and support for the Technion’s emergency campaign. Since October 7 and the recent escalation, the Technion has been on the front line of the home front: more than 1,500 students and faculty have been mobilized to reserves, with many more expected. The institution has prioritized wrapping reservists in economic, academic, and emotional support so that no one is left behind and academic excellence is maintained. The appeal frames support for Technion students as a direct investment in national security and Israel’s future, and invites federations to discuss tailored opportunities and a proposal that fits each federation’s needs and goals.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

## Context & Background

The email is sent from the Technion by Hilla Perlman Pniel, who describes having moved from the federation world to the Technion and thus framing the ask as a request for support rather than a routine FCR update. She acknowledges that recipients are busy with federation and community work and hopes the intensive work provides some distraction from the war.

Since October 7 and especially after the recent escalation, the Technion is described as being at the forefront of the home front. Over 1,500 students, faculty, and staff have been (or are being) mobilized again to reserves under Tzav 8, with more call-ups expected. For the past three years the Technion has made it a priority to provide reservists with unprecedented support—economic, academic, and emotional—so that no one is left behind and academic excellence is not compromised.

The link between the Technion and Israel’s security is presented as inseparable: defense systems such as Iron Dome, Arrow, and David’s Sling are cited as products of Technion graduates. Supporting Technion students is therefore framed as a direct investment in national security, economic resilience, and the innovations that will drive Israel forward after the war. The author also notes that when Israel missions resume, the Technion will be a key destination to see Israeli human capital at its best—a future generation from all sectors shaping the country’s future.

The request is explicitly exploratory: to identify opportunities for federation collaboration and support for the Technion’s emergency campaign. The sender offers to talk, provide more details, and craft a proposal tailored to each federation’s needs and objectives. Contact and organizational details are confirmed from the signature image: Israel Friends of Technion, CEO Hilla Perlman Pniel, Technion City, Haifa.

## The Ask

The Haifa Rape Crisis Center (HRCC) is requesting $200,000 to expand its emergency capacity in preparation for a post-operation surge of support needs from survivors of sexual violence following Operation "Shaagat HaAri." The funds will support new volunteer training, expansion of WhatsApp support hours, and reinforcement of immediate response capacity.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- New Volunteer Training Course (recruitment, training, supervision): $95,000
- WhatsApp Expansion (extended hours, additional responders, supervision): $60,000
- Surge Capacity Reinforcement (intake, coordination, flexible staffing): $45,000

## Context & Background

The Haifa Rape Crisis Center (HRCC) has a history of responding to national crises and anticipates a significant increase in demand for their services following Operation "Shaagat HaAri." They note that survivors of sexual violence often experience collective trauma and may hesitate to seek help during acute emergencies, but reach out once a sense of calm returns. This operation comes after nearly two years of sustained conflict, compounding existing trauma.

HRCC is preparing for a concentrated and intense post-operation surge. They are requesting $200,000 to fund three key areas: a new volunteer training course to increase hotline capacity, expansion of WhatsApp support hours to provide a critical lifeline for survivors, and reinforcement of immediate response capacity to ensure timely and responsible support. The organization serves 41 local authorities in Northern Israel and has seen a dramatic increase in demand for services, with a growing need expected to continue.

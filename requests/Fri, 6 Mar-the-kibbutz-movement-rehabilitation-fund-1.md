## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund is requesting support to address the urgent needs of kibbutz communities in northern Israel due to renewed threats and missile attacks. The request focuses on two main areas: equipping public shelters with essential supplies and providing emergency community support to help kibbutzim care for residents during this escalation.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits: $94,500
- Community Emergency Support: $945,000

## Context & Background

The security situation in Israel has intensified, particularly impacting kibbutz communities along the northern border. Families are spending extended periods in public shelters due to incomplete protection programs, leading to a critical need for basic equipment and supplies. The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established after October 7th to support affected kibbutzim.

Currently, the primary needs are equipping public shelters with essentials like mattresses, seating, first aid, and children's activity kits. Additionally, there's a need for emergency community support, including youth activities, assistance for the elderly, community programming, and strengthening local leadership. These efforts are vital for maintaining safety, support, and connection for residents amidst ongoing threats and uncertainty. The fund has identified a need for 27 emergency shelter kits at $3,500 each, totaling $94,500, and requires $945,000 for community emergency support programs.

### Emergency Response for Kibbutz Communities - The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund

**Background**

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim across Israel. The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established to support kibbutzim affected by the October 7th attacks and the "Swords of Iron" war, addressing immediate emergency needs, mid-term community recovery, and long-term rehabilitation.

**The Current Crisis**

Kibbutz communities across Israel have been severely impacted. 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev were damaged, 19 along the northern border were evacuated, and 8 in the central Hula Valley remained under threat. Many residents were killed or injured, and vital services were destroyed. Amidst renewed escalation and missile threats, thousands of residents remain vulnerable, with overcrowded public shelters and persistent uncertainty.

**Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)**

Due to the escalation in northern Israel, 27 kibbutzim along the border and in the Hula Valley lack adequate protected rooms (Mamad). Families are spending long periods in public shelters lacking basic equipment. The Fund seeks support for Emergency Shelter Kits to provide safety, dignity, and essential services.

**Contents of Each Shelter Kit:**
* 20 stackable chairs
* 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
* 3 high-capacity fans
* 2 folding tables
* 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
* 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
* 2 activity and play kits for children

**Cost per kit:** $3,500
**Funding goal:** 27 shelter kits
**Total required:** $94,500

**Community Emergency Support Program**

Kibbutz communities require social, emotional, and communal support. The 19 kibbutzim along the northern border, recently returned from evacuation, face ongoing security threats. The displacement created emotional strain, and ongoing uncertainty affects residents.

The Rehabilitation Fund will implement support initiatives including:
*   **Support for Local Leadership:** Guidance for kibbutz management.
*   **Cultural and Community Activities:** Programs to maintain social cohesion.
*   **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted assistance for vulnerable older residents.
*   **Youth and Educational Activities:** Organized programming for teenagers and youth in protected spaces.

**Budget for Community Emergency Support**
*   **Cost:** $35,000 X 27 kibbutzim
*   **Total funding required:** $945,000

**Expected Impact**

With donor support, the Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund will:
*   Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters.
*   Provide essential services and infrastructure during missile attacks.
*   Strengthen community resilience and social cohesion.
*   Support youth, families, and vulnerable populations.
*   Enable kibbutzim to withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing recovery.

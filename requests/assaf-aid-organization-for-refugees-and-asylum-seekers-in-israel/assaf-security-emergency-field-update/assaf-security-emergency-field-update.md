## Context & Background

Over the past three days leading up to 2 March 2026, communities across Israel have faced continuous missile attacks and repeated sirens. Educational frameworks are closed and most workplaces are currently shut down under emergency regulations. This has a profound impact on the already vulnerable refugee community, which remains largely excluded from national safety nets, emergency assistance, and social protection mechanisms.

In between alarms, ASSAF staff and volunteers are operating in emergency mode. In accordance with Home Front Command guidelines, ASSAF has adapted all services to the current security situation. The organization is mapping urgent needs emerging from community members, maintaining continuous contact with staff and volunteers, and strengthening resilience and emotional support responses during this period of heightened stress.

ASSAF is actively disseminating accessible, multilingual information about emergency guidelines and available services so that community members are not left without clear and reliable updates. The organization has expanded remote psychosocial responses, including increased availability of emotional support, expanded reception hours, and an emergency telephone line to ensure continued accessibility and rapid response for the refugee community. The sender expresses thanks for the recipient's partnership and hope for quieter and more peaceful days.

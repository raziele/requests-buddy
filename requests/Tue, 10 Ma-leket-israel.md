## The Ask

Leket Israel, a national food bank, is requesting emergency funding of $508,210 per week to address critical food shortages caused by the ongoing conflict. Disruptions have reduced the supply of rescued food, necessitating the purchase of additional meals and produce, and the provision of supermarket vouchers to ensure food security for vulnerable families. They seek support from JUF Chicago to sustain these operations during this critical time.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

- Purchased produce: None

## Context & Background

The email details the impact of the 'current situation' (likely referring to the Iran conflict mentioned) on Leket Israel's ability to conduct its food rescue operations. Daily life in Israel is disrupted by sirens and the need for shelters, and Leket's work is further complicated by rocket fire pausing volunteer activities and limiting collection. Farmers have halted harvests, and IDF bases are not permitting entry, significantly reducing the supply of surplus food. This has decreased daily meal distribution from 8,000 to 3,000.

In response, Leket Israel is working to maintain and increase distributions by supplementing rescued food with purchased items and providing supermarket vouchers. This effort requires significant additional funding, estimated at $508,210 per week, to cover the costs of purchasing meals and produce, and to support families who cannot access distribution points.

The request emphasizes the importance of food security for maintaining resilience, health, reducing trauma, and preserving the social fabric during this critical period. The sender expresses deep gratitude for JUF Chicago's partnership and support.

## The Ask

The Israel Women's Network (IWN) is requesting urgent support for its emergency response. With the country at war—education system closed, economy shut down, and mass reserve call-up—women are the first to pay the price: immediate income loss, 24/7 childcare, managing households alone during partners' reserve service, and women in abusive situations with no way out. IWN's "Alice Line" rights center is overwhelmed, having received hundreds of inquiries in just three days. The organization is asking for urgent funding to expand the hotline and legal team, make multilingual information accessible on social media and its website, and influence emergency regulations being drafted in real time.

## Context & Background

Israel entered a state of war, with the education system closed, the economy shut down, and a broad call-up of reserves. In this context, women bear the impact first and most deeply: immediate loss of income, round-the-clock childcare, managing the family alone while partners serve in the reserves, self-employed women whose income disappeared overnight, and women in cycles of violence left without a way out. The impact is immediate, deep, and with no clear horizon.

IWN is already operating in emergency mode. "The Alice Line"—its rights-fulfillment center—is flooded with requests: within three days it received hundreds of inquiries. Women need help with employment rights, furlough (חל״ת), rights of reserve servicewomen and partners of reservists, property damage, business collapse, compensation, and basic legal protections.

The organization must urgently expand in three areas: increase capacity on the hotline and strengthen the legal team; make updated multilingual information accessible on social media and the lobby's website; and influence in real time the emergency regulations currently being written. The sender is requesting urgent support so that no woman is left alone facing the crisis. The donation link (iwn.org.il/join-donate/) is provided for immediate contribution.

## The Ask

Israel Women's Network is requesting urgent financial support to scale up The Alice Line, its emergency legal and bureaucratic assistance hotline for women, during the current war escalation with Iran. With schools closed, 100,000 reservists called up, and the economy in shutdown, women are bearing the brunt—loss of income, full-time caregiving, and heightened risk for those in abusive homes. The hotline has received a month's worth of calls in three days and needs to expand capacity, legal staffing, multilingual outreach, and real-time policy advocacy to ensure women can access their rights and benefits.

## Context & Background

Israel has entered full emergency mode following escalation with Iran: more than 100 missiles launched, over a dozen killed, millions in shelters. Schools are closed indefinitely, only essential workers may report, and 100,000 reserve soldiers are being called up. Women are disproportionately affected: they are losing work as the economy shuts down, carrying full-time childcare, juggling essential-sector jobs with family safety, and—as reserve spouses—managing households alone. Single mothers and women in precarious or abusive situations face immediate income loss and reduced access to support.

The Israel Women's Network runs The Alice Line, an emergency response center that provides direct legal and bureaucratic assistance in multiple languages, gathers real-time data on systemic failures, and intervenes with government and the Knesset to correct discriminatory policies. During the previous phase of war, this model led to concrete legislative and policy changes that secured millions of shekels in benefits for women and their families. Since the current escalation began, the equivalent of a month's worth of emergency calls has come in within three days. Callers need help with employment rights during shutdowns, unpaid leave, reserve spouse benefits, small business collapse, compensation claims, and legal protections.

IWN states that in times of war, women's needs are often not integrated into national decision-making; policy is drafted quickly without gender analysis, and women are left to navigate complex bureaucracies alone. The absence of women in leadership roles in this war means their perspectives are missing from decision-making. The organization is asking for urgent support to scale hotline capacity, increase legal staffing, extend multilingual outreach (including online advertising), provide webinars and accessible online content, and strengthen real-time policy advocacy as new emergency regulations are drafted. No dollar or shekel amount is specified; the email directs readers to an emergency contribution link.

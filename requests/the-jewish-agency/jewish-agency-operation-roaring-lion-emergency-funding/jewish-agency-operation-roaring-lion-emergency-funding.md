## The Ask

The Jewish Agency is requesting emergency funding in response to Operation Roaring Lion, the conflict that began on February 28, 2026, when Iran launched hundreds of long-range ballistic missiles at Israel. The attacks have caused massive damage to civilian population and infrastructure, with ten lives lost and more than 130 injured. The Agency, in coordination with national emergency authorities and local municipalities, has identified three priority areas: (1) an Emergency Fund for Roaring Lion providing immediate and rehabilitation grants to bereaved families, injured parties, and those with property destruction; special assistance for evacuees and resilience interventions; and extra support for elderly and children. (2) Support for Jewish Agency beneficiaries — Amigour residents who cannot leave for food and essentials (food packages) and Olim in absorption centers who need shelter activities for children and mental health support. (3) Financial assistance for Israel Experience participants (Masa, Onward, Birthright) who are stranded in Israel and may need temporary housing or funds to reach Athens or Cyprus to return home. Total estimated needs range from approximately $6.3 million (300 immediate grants scenario) to $12.7 million (2,000 immediate grants scenario), plus $600,000 for beneficiaries and $2,000,000 for Israel Experience programs.

## Context & Background

On Saturday February 28, 2026, Israel, the United States, and Iran entered into a new conflict termed "Operation Roaring Lion." Iran retaliated against an Israeli attack with hundreds of long-range ballistic missiles, causing massive damage to Israel's civilian population and infrastructure. As of the document date (March 1, 2026), ten lives had been lost and more than 130 people injured.

The Jewish Agency has a long-standing role supporting the people of Israel during crises. In this instance it is coordinating with national emergency authorities, local municipalities, beneficiaries, and partners to address three categories of need. First, an Emergency Fund for Roaring Lion would provide financial assistance to bereaved families, injured parties, and those suffering property destruction (both immediate and rehabilitation grants); special assistance and group interventions for evacuees and those who lost their homes, in partnership with municipalities, including resilience strengthening; and assistance for populations requiring extra support such as elderly or children. The scale of this fund depends on the number of immediate grants (300 to 2,000), with total estimated need ranging from $3.7 million to $10.1 million.

Second, the Agency has two populations under its direct care: new immigrants (Olim) in Absorption Centers and elderly residents of Amigour Housing. Amigour residents have been unable to leave their homes to obtain food or other essentials and need delivered food packages. Olim in absorption centers are spending long hours in shelters and need activities for children in shelters as well as mental health assistance. Total estimated need for these beneficiaries is $600,000.

Third, there are approximately 6,000 young people in Israel on Israel Experience programs (Masa, Onward, Birthright). Many are stuck for an unknown period; some need temporary housing and some wish to return to their countries of origin. With airlines expected to take time to resume regular routes, many will need financial assistance to reach Athens or Cyprus to improve their chances of returning home. The Jewish Agency seeks to provide assistance for those who cannot afford the additional costs of an extended, unplanned stay. Total need for this component is $2,000,000.

# Draft as of March 1, 2026

On Saturday February 28, Israel, the United States and Iran entered into a new war entitled "Operation Roaring Lion". Iran retaliated to Israel's attack in the form of hundreds of long-range ballistic missiles that have already caused massive damage to Israel's civilian population and infrastructure and resulted in ten lives lost and more than 130 injured.

The Jewish Agency, in coordination with the national emergency authorities, local municipalities, beneficiaries and partners has identified the following emergency needs:

## 1. Emergency Fund for Roaring Lion

For decades, The Jewish Agency has supported the people of Israel during times of crisis. As we have in the past, The Jewish Agency and its partners will show solidarity with the victims, municipalities and resiliency needs resulting from the Iranian missile attacks. The fund consists of three elements:

- **a.** Financial assistance to bereaved families, injured parties and destruction of property including both immediate and rehabilitation grants.
- **b.** Special assistance and group interventions, in partnership with municipalities, for those that have lost their homes/evacuees including strengthening resilience.
- **c.** Assistance for populations requiring extra support like elderly or children.

## 2. Jewish Agency Beneficiaries

The Jewish Agency has two population groups living under its care that rely on the organization for housing, safety and support: New immigrants (Olim) living in Absorption Centers and the elderly residents of Amigour Housing.

- **a. Amigour** — As a result of the war, residents of Amigour homes have not been able to leave their homes to buy food or other essentials. Amigour is asking for food packages to deliver to its residents.
- **b. Olim** — Olim in absorption centers are stuck for long hours in shelters. We are asking for assistance in activities for children in shelters as well as mental health assistance for Olim who have and will be impacted by the events.

**Total Estimated Need: $600,000**

## 3. Financial Assistance for Participants of Israel Experience Programs

There are currently 6,000 young people in Israel participating in Israel Experience programs (Masa, Onward, Birthright, etc.) Many of them are facing challenges as they are stuck in Israel for an unknown length of time. Some require temporary housing and some are looking to return to their countries of origin. Recognizing that it will take time for airlines to resume flying regular routes, many participants will require financial assistance in flying out of Israel to reach Athens or Cyprus to increase their chances of returning home quickly. The Jewish Agency would like to provide financial assistance for those who cannot afford the additional costs of an extended, unplanned stay in Israel.

**Total Need: $2,000,000**

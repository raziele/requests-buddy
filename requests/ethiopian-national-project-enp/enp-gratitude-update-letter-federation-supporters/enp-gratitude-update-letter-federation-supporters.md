## The Ask

ENP sent a gratitude and update letter to federation leaders and supporters, thanking them for their solidarity and describing the impact of the SPACE program. The letter emphasizes that ENP's educational teams provide stability and empowerment for Ethiopian-Israeli families amid ongoing disruption, and that the community is increasingly integrated into Israeli society. ENP states that designated support from federations and foundations enables them to broaden impact and strengthen the community, and asks for continued partnership to overcome challenges together. No specific funding amount or formal application is included.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Continued solidarity, trust, and designated support from Federations and foundations; relationship maintenance and shared progress updates.

## Context & Background

The Ethiopian National Project (ENP) sent this message during a period it describes as "challenging days" for Israel. The letter is addressed to supporters and federation leaders and was forwarded by Barak Loozon (Jewish Federation Israel Office) on 6 March 2026; the original was sent by Grace Rodnitzki on behalf of ENP on 2 March 2026.

ENP frames its work around the SPACE program, which serves Ethiopian-Israeli schoolchildren and their families. The organization states that Israel's schoolchildren have faced "years of disruption, uncertainty, and ongoing challenges," and that parents strive to model resilience despite the burden. ENP positions its educational teams as "a vital anchor of stability and empowerment" and "a trusted and professional presence" helping families navigate a complex reality with hope and confidence.

ENP reports that the next generation of Ethiopian-Israelis is increasingly integrated into all sectors of Israeli society, including active roles in security and defense, and that the community came to Israel with a sense of mission and Zionism. The letter does not cite specific statistics or budget figures. It states that "in the months ahead" ENP must continue to advance its work and strengthen the community while contributing to Israel's resilience, and that designated support from federations and foundations enables them to broaden impact and further empower the Ethiopian-Israeli community. The tone is appreciative and forward-looking rather than an urgent or formal funding request.

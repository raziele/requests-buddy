## The Ask

Access Israel is urgently requesting $400,000 to provide immediate assistance to individuals with disabilities and the elderly, particularly those without accessible shelters, in light of the recent escalation with Iran. The funds will support the operation of a 24/7 emergency hotline, training for the 'Purple Vest' system, and accessible transportation for evacuations.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency hotline operation (24/7): None
- 'Purple Vest' training and operation: None
- Accessible vehicle operation for evacuations: None

## Context & Background

Access Israel is a non-profit organization dedicated to ensuring accessibility and promoting the rights of people with disabilities and the elderly in Israel. In the context of the ongoing conflict and escalating tensions with Iran, a critical need has arisen to provide immediate, accessible, and protected emergency solutions for individuals with disabilities who lack adequate shelter. Many of these individuals, including the elderly, are disproportionately affected during emergencies. Access Israel's 'Purple Vest' initiative aims to provide immediate response and evacuation services, but requires urgent funding to scale up its operations. The organization emphasizes that during times of crisis, those most in need are often the last to receive institutional support, making immediate philanthropic intervention crucial.

**Subject:** Urgent Request for Donation: Access Israel - Purple Vest - War Assistance.

**Date:** 28/2/2026

**To:** Heads of Federations and Dear Partners,

**Re:** Emergency Call for Donation: Saving the Lives of People and the Elderly with Severe Disabilities During the Current War with Iran.

Dear Donors and Partners,

I am writing to you with urgency on behalf of Access Israel and thousands of individuals with disabilities and elderly citizens, approximately 40% of whom live without accessible shelters. Every time an alarm sounds, they have no real alternative and are left to remain in place, in fear and trembling, praying for their safety.

Since October 7th, Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian aid for the most vulnerable populations. Thanks to your support, we have been able to assist thousands with accessible evacuation, alternative accessible and protected housing, provision of medical equipment, dedicated accessible transportation, and personal and logistical support in severe distress situations. In many cases, no other entity, neither the state nor the local authority, has provided adequate assistance.

Precisely those who need assistance most during the war are often the last to receive institutional support. Now, with the outbreak of war this morning, February 28, 2026, we face a renewed, significant, and urgent need to ensure accessible, professional, and sensitive assistance for the unique needs of people with disabilities and the elderly. Together with you, we will ensure they receive the support they deserve, and no one is left behind.

We urgently need your partnership and immediate support with an initial amount of $400,000 (for ten days of operation) to immediately fund critical activities (and subsequently, depending on developments and the duration of the fighting, we will assess further needs).

**Critical Activities Requiring Immediate Funding:**

1.  **Operation of an accessible emergency command center 24/7:** Funding for operating an accessible call center for all types of disabilities, including professional staff, development and maintenance of the "Purple Vest" application, communication systems, logistical operations, and real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries in the coming days.
2.  **Training and operation of the "Purple Vest" system:** Budget for training and refreshers for municipal volunteers, accessible evacuation equipment, operational management and coordination, to ensure rapid response and accessible field assistance during missile impact events in buildings and neighborhoods.
3.  **Operation of vehicles with lifts for wheelchairs:** Budget for the ongoing operation of accessible evacuation vehicles, including drivers, fuel, insurance, and maintenance, for safe extraction and transfer from non-protected areas to medical treatment and accessible, protected alternative housing.

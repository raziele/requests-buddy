## The Ask

Dror Israel is seeking $250,000 in emergency support for the next two weeks to provide educational and emotional support to 200,000 participants nationwide. This includes emergency childcare for essential workers, educational activities at evacuee hotels, activities in public shelters, and pedagogical guidance for schools. They are also focusing on resilience through community movement and dance.

**Funding Requested:** $250,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers: $125,000
- Pop-up school at hospital: $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels: $10,000
- Activities in public shelters: $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope: $80,000
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance: $10,000

## Context & Background

Israel is facing a deadly missile barrage, leading to widespread fear, uncertainty, and trauma as families repeatedly move into bomb shelters. Dror Israel's trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in various settings, including bomb shelters and evacuee centers. They are actively assessing needs and coordinating emergency responses, reaching over 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and Zoom programming.

Dror Israel is seeking $250,000 for the next two weeks to sustain these efforts. Their current priorities include:

1.  **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers:** Establishing temporary childcare centers in various cities to support medical staff, security personnel, and critical service providers. A specific request includes a pop-up school at a major hospital.
2.  **Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels:** Providing activities for evacuated children and youth to offer normalcy and a break for parents.
3.  **Organizing Activities in Public Shelters:** Conducting activities for children in public shelters across multiple locations, with the deployment of trained teen counselors.
4.  **Pedagogy of Hope:** Providing guidance and trauma support to 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools, especially in light of a direct hit on one school.
5.  **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance:** Offering online resilience sessions in several cities.

Dror Israel emphasizes its role as a leading force for social change with extensive experience in emergency response, aiming to bring stability and hope.

Israel Needs Us Once Again. And We Need You!
Educators, Community Builders, Emotional First Responders - We Are There

March 4, 2026

Current Situation
As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters,
evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, Israelis are living in
a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma as families once
again move into bomb shelters and daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Our trained educators are
providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee
centers, and even in living rooms across the country. Nimble and ready to go wherever we are
needed, we are addressing urgent emotional and educational needs in real time, ensuring that
no child feels alone.

Dror Israel educators, in partnership with local municipalities and government partners, are
actively assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses as
conditions change. Simultaneously, we are activating our nationwide network of more than
200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom
programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted
adults. This is our core educational work adapted to emergency conditions, and sustaining
educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires
continued operating support.

Seeking Support for Escalating Needs
Dror Israel is seeking $250,000 in emergency support for the next two weeks to ensure we
can continue reaching our 200,000 participants nationwide, provide activities and emotional
support for children and families in bomb shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond quickly as
urgent needs continue to emerge.

Current Priorities
1. Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers In partnership with local communities, we
have launched emergency childcare centers for the children of essential workers so that
medical staff, security personnel, and critical service providers can continue their work.
Operating in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula,
Akko, and Be'er Sheva, our counselors and youth movement leaders are staffing temporary
childcare frameworks in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels.
These centers provide safe supervision, structured activities, and emotional support for
children whose parents are serving on the front lines of civilian response.
Funding gap: $125,000 - 10 staff, transportation and supplies

One major hospital has asked Dror Israel to establish a pop-up school on-site so that
working medical teams can continue caring for patients while knowing their children are
nearby in a safe, structured educational environment.
Funding gap: $50,000 – 4 teachers, transportation, supplies

2. Educational activities at evacuee hotels. Israel's new wave of evacuees is growing every
day. We are already distributing donations of clothing and supplies in an evacuee center in
Tel Aviv, and offering daily activities for the evacuated children and youth from Tel Aviv and
Be'er Sheva. Our educators are running emotional-educational support activities for children
to provide some normalcy among the uncertainty and loss, also giving parents a break.
Funding gap: $10,000 for staff, supplies and transportation

3. Organizing activities in public shelters. Many families lack appropriate home shelters and
are staying overnight in public bomb shelters in schools, parking lots, and subway stations.
We are already operating activities for children in public shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion,
Carmiel, Tiberias, and more. Alongside our professional educators, hundreds of Jewish and
Arab teen counselors trained by Dror Israel as emergency junior counselors are now being
deployed with ready-to-use activity kits.
Funding gap: $60,000 – 5 volunteer organizers, transportation and supplies

4. Pedagogy of Hope. Dror Israel Schools Network provides pedagogical guidance and
trauma support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools. One such
school, the Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva, was hit by a missile fragment but
thankfully the school was closed and nobody was harmed. All of our schools, including the
Moledet School, are receiving personalized and trauma-informed guidance in support of
staff and students, teaching towards hope in these times of war.
Funding gap: $80,000 for staff and pedagogical development

5. Resilience through Community Movement and Dance. In Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and
Haifa, Dror Israel's Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers are offering online resilience
sessions through movement and dance. These guided sessions create structured spaces for
emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.
Funding gap: $10,000 for staff

About Dror Israel
We are a leading force for social change: 1,300 trained educators living in 14 educators'
kibbutzim throughout Israel, dedicating ourselves to helping our neighbors and meeting local
needs. With extensive experience in emergency response, especially for traumatized children,
we are bringing immediate stability and hope to the chaos of war.

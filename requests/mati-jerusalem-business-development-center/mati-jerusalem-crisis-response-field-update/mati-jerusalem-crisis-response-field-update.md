## Context & Background

Following the outbreak of war with Iran on Saturday, MATI Jerusalem Business Development Center mobilized immediately. By Sunday morning its management had convened on Zoom and the Jerusalem Municipality had already designated MATI to lead an emergency series of eight webinars for business owners across the city—four for West Jerusalem and four for East Jerusalem—covering Marketing in Times of War, Client Retention in Times of War, Crisis Management, and AI for Business. Business consultants continue to provide one-on-one consulting online.

MATI has moved several programs online for continuity: the Hebrew for Employment course in East Jerusalem resumed via Zoom; within the TIFLI preschool program it launched webinars for families and daycare managers on helping children cope with anxiety and supporting staff mental health; financial literacy workshops continue online; and the Facebook-based financial literacy competition has grown significantly, with 120 new participants.

On International Women's Day the email highlights MATI's work with women entrepreneurs through accelerator courses, dedicated women's forums, and other services. In 2025, 800 women benefited from the Turning Point program, 612 received one-on-one business consulting, women entrepreneurs raised $1 million in business loans, and 4,000 women participated in courses, workshops, and webinars. The message closes by expressing gratitude for the recipient's interest and inviting further involvement.

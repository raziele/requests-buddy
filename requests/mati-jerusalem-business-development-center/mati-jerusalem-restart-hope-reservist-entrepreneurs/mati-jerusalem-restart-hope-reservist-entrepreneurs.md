## The Ask

MATI Jerusalem is requesting partnership and support for ReStart:HOPE, a community-driven business recovery and rehabilitation program for over 200 reservist entrepreneurs in the Jerusalem region. The program addresses the severe impact of the war on small business owners who served in the reserves: 83% have suffered income losses and business closures have doubled. ReStart:HOPE delivers peer support, workshops on financial resilience and digital skills, and direct business assistance (mentoring, loan guidance, automation) through a reservists-for-reservists model. The organization is asking the recipient to review the attached two-pager and schedule a Zoom meeting to discuss potential partnership opportunities.

## Context & Background

The recent war in Israel has devastated the small business sector among reservists. 83% of reservist business owners have suffered income losses, business closures have doubled, and demanding 70-day annual service requirements further hinder their ability to rebuild. National assistance programs often fall short because reservists must navigate applications during or immediately after service when mental capacity and availability are limited. Many reservists have now returned from the front lines and urgently need comprehensive support to rebuild their livelihoods and lives.

MATI Jerusalem’s ReStart:HOPE program is a community-centered, three-pillar rehabilitation initiative built on a reservists-for-reservists model. Pillar A (Community Building) includes shared workspaces, networking events, conferences, a dedicated hotline, and professional headshot sessions—e.g. 4 networking events for 48 reservists, 8 meetups at reservist-owned venues for 200 reservists, 2 conferences for 200 reservists, and a shared workspace for 60 reservists. Pillar B (Tailored Content) offers workshops, courses, and webinars on financial resilience, emotional adjustment, reservists’ rights, AI tools, and digital marketing—20 hands-on workshops for 240 reservists, 5 evening courses for 132, and 8 webinars for 320. Pillar C (Business Support Packages) provides heavily subsidized consulting, business planning for loans, and business automation—mentoring and loan support for 200 reservists and tailor-made automation packages for 50.

All services are delivered primarily by reservists for reservists and hosted at reservist-owned venues, creating both peer support and economic opportunities within the reservist community. The program is framed as a practical, emotional, and economic lifeline for those who defended the front lines and now need to rebuild. The sender is reaching out from MATI to introduce the initiative and seek partnership; no specific funding amount is requested in the email or two-pager.

# ReStart:HOPE - Reservist Business Community & Rehabilitation Program

The war in Israel has devastated the small business sector, leaving reservist owners grappling with overwhelming challenges: 83% have suffered income losses and closures have doubled. The demanding 70-day annual service requirements further hinder their ability to rebuild. National assistance programs often fall short, failing to support reservists who must navigate applications during or immediately after service when mental capacity and availability are limited. Now that the war is over and many reservists have returned from the front lines, they urgently need comprehensive support to rebuild their livelihoods and lives.

To meet this need, MATI Jerusalem's ReStart:Hope Reservist Business Community and Rehabilitation Program provides a community-centered, three-pronged rehabilitation initiative for over 200 reservist business owners in Jerusalem and surrounding areas, built on a "reservists-for-reservists" model.

**The program rests on three pillars:**

**A. Community Building** – fostering a strong peer network through shared workspaces, community campaigns, corporate photography sessions, a dedicated hotline, and networking events and conferences.

- 4 networking events for 48 reservists
- 8 meetup events at reservist-owned venues for 200 reservists
- 2 professional headshot sessions for 50 reservists
- 2 conferences with meaningful content and networking opportunities for 200 reservists
- A shared workspace for 60 reservists

**B. Tailored Content** – offering flexible workshops, courses, and webinars on financial resilience, emotional adjustment, reservists' rights, AI tools, digital marketing, and more.

- 20 Hands-On Workshops for 240 reservists
- 5 Evening Business Courses & Workshops for 132 reservists
- 8 Webinars for 320 reservists

**C. Business Support Packages** – providing first-aid business assistance via heavily subsidized consulting, business planning for loans and business automation packages.

- Business Mentoring & Loan Application Support Package for 200 reservists
- Tailor-Made Business Automation Package for 50 reservists

All services are delivered in priority by reservists for reservists and hosted at reservist-owned venues, ensuring peer support and creating economic opportunities within the reservist community. ReStart:Hope offers a practical, emotional, and economic lifeline for those who defended the front lines and now need to rebuild.

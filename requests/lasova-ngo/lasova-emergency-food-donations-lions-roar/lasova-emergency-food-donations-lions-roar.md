## The Ask

Lasova NGO is requesting financial and in-kind (food) donations to sustain and scale its emergency operations during the Lion's Roar period. Since the start of the campaign, demand has risen sharply: the organization distributes hundreds of hot meals daily, runs soup kitchens and food baskets (with family demand nearly double routine levels), operates shelters for street dwellers in full emergency mode, and continues Kadima youth center programming remotely. They are operating in full emergency mode in the North (Acre, Karmiel) and nationwide, with activity increasingly reliant on community donations due to closed food sources. They ask for donations to enable continued routine operation for thousands of beneficiaries.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

## Context & Background

Against the backdrop of the war (Lion's Roar / שאגת הארי), Lasova has expanded its emergency activity across the country and continues to supply thousands of beneficiaries with basic needs. In South Tel Aviv, the Lasova restaurant now serves about 470 diners daily compared to roughly 250 in routine — with many new families who had not previously needed assistance: mothers with small children, foreign workers left without income, and families affected by economic shutdown and closed education frameworks. Staff describe a sharp increase in requests, reminiscent of the COVID period but at higher intensity. Kitchen teams in the North (Acre, Karmiel) cook from early morning to ensure regular food supply to centers and home deliveries; with many fixed food sources closed, operations rely heavily on community donations.

Lasova's shelters for street dwellers (גגונים) operate in full emergency mode — offering lodging, hot meals, clean clothing, and open safe rooms. They accept residents from across the country, including referrals from hospitals and municipal social services, and staff have proactively reached out to municipal and social-service units to enable referrals. Routine work (individual accompaniment, therapeutic response, department management) continues; for street dwellers there is no "pause" in need during war. The Kadima youth centers network has continued operating without interruption since the start of the campaign: daily Zoom group activities, remote learning support, and one-on-one contact so that every child feels seen and heard. Staff have received training on emotional processes in stress and anxiety and practical tools for calming and support; daily team meetings are held for sharing and morale.

With the prolongation of fighting and deepening economic impact, the number of people seeking assistance is expected to keep rising. The organization is grateful for financial and food donations that will allow it to continue its routine operations for the thousands in need.

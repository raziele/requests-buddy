## The Ask

Galilee Medical Center in Nahariya is seeking philanthropic support for a wide-ranging March 2026 urgent-needs list totaling about $26.7M USD, covering renovation of two fortified underground hospital complexes ($5M), major imaging and oncology capital (including a 3T MRI and a protected hemato-oncology institute with PET-CT), ward upgrades, ICU/pediatric critical care equipment, ventilators, telemetry, surgical tools, and lab (NGS) capacity. The ask is framed around maintaining care during security emergencies when the hospital must operate largely underground, after prolonged wartime strain and reduced operational bed capacity.

## Context & Background

The Galilee Medical Center is the sole public hospital serving Israel’s Western Galilee from Nahariya, near the Lebanese border. During the 2023–2024 war it ran for 14 months under emergency protocols—often from fortified and underground spaces—and treated more than 2,000 seriously injured soldiers, while usable beds reportedly fell from 777 to 465 because above-ground care was not safely sustainable.

The Hebrew cover note explains that urgent needs are updated frequently while the hospital works mainly from underground campuses; each line on the master list has a more detailed brief, and the underground renovation is singled out as work to complete after the current war round ends (the recipient had visited the site with colleagues).

The accompanying PDF and spreadsheet quantify a $5M project to refurbish two protected underground complexes (110 and 80 capacity respectively) with clinical infrastructure, infection control, and staff/patient facilities, alongside dozens of other capital priorities from MRI and PET-CT to PICU modernization—aimed at restoring regional resilience, closing infrastructure gaps versus central Israel, and keeping advanced care local for roughly one million northern residents.

#### Title
Renovation of fortified underground complexes — Galilee Medical Center

#### About the Galilee Medical Center (GMC)
- Government hospital founded **1956**, located in **Nahariya**, Israel’s **Western Galilee**.
- **Only** public medical center in the region; **second-largest** hospital north of Tel Aviv.
- Primary healthcare provider for northern Israel; serves nearly **one million** residents among Israel’s most diverse populations.
- **69** departments, **800** hospital beds, **3,400+** staff; region’s largest employer.
- Main teaching hospital of the **Azrieli Faculty of Medicine at Bar-Ilan University** in the Galilee; trains about **two-thirds** of northern Israel’s medical students.
- Located a few kilometers from the **Lebanese border** — closest hospital to an international border in Israel; frontline readiness for emergencies, mass-casualty events, and complex trauma.

#### War context (2023–2024 “Iron Swords”)
- Operated continuously **14 months** under emergency protocols, often in **underground and fortified** facilities.
- Treated **2,000+** injured **IDF** soldiers (many complex/life-threatening).
- Operational beds reduced from **777** to **465** due to **safety constraints**.

#### Innovation / strategic notes (from document)
- Regional **Research Institute** (clinical and translational research).
- **Medical Innovation Center** — national innovation grants; technologies and healthcare solutions.
- Vision: national model for equitable, state-of-the-art care in the **periphery**.

#### Systemic challenges
- Galilee ranks low nationally on **beds**, **ICU capacity**, and **physicians per capita**.
- Post-war returning evacuees highlighted **infrastructure gaps** vs. central Israel.
- **Aging population**; internal medicine sees many elderly with multimorbidity, including **Holocaust survivors**; rising severely ill patients increasing demand on staff and facilities.

#### Friends of the Galilee Medical Center (NPO)
- Develops the medical center: facilities, advanced technology, community ties, awareness, healing environment.
- Works with hospital management on departments, equipment, technologies.
- Donor-funded renovation and equipment; currently fundraising for **high-priority** renovation/upgrading of services and facilities.

#### Renovation of two fortified underground complexes — rationale
- Main hospital for **Western Galilee** in a region with frequent **security threats**.
- During missile attacks or large-scale conflict, hospitals must sustain **uninterrupted** care and protect patients, staff, and equipment.
- Fortified underground space allows key departments to function **under direct threat** — trauma, critically ill, vulnerable populations; continuity of **life-saving procedures**, **emergency surgery**, and **ICU** when above-ground areas are unsafe.

#### Scope of work
- Renovation/upgrading of **two** protected underground hospital complexes for **continuity of care** in emergencies.
- Includes: ceilings, floors, flooring; **medical furniture**; **nurses’ stations**; modern **lighting**; sanitary facilities (**toilets, showers, changing rooms**); **physiotherapy** corner; **medication rooms**; **antibacterial wall coverings** for hygiene and infection control.

#### Cost and capacity
- **Complex 1:** about **$3 million**, up to **110** people.
- **Complex 2:** about **$2 million**, about **80** people.
- **Total:** **$5 million** (matches spreadsheet line item).

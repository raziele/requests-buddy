## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** in emergency funding for 27 kibbutzim in northern Israel and the Hula Valley facing renewed missile threats and incomplete civil protection infrastructure.

The request includes two coordinated components:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** for public shelters to provide basic safety, dignity, and essential services during prolonged shelter stays.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** to sustain community functioning, psychosocial resilience, and support for vulnerable groups during ongoing emergency conditions.

### Funding Requested

**Total:** $1,039,500

### Funding Breakdown

- **Emergency Shelter Kits:** 27 kits × $3,500 = **$94,500**
- **Community Emergency Support:** 27 kibbutzim × $35,000 = **$945,000**

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim across Israel, from the northern border to the southern Negev. Following the October 7 attacks and the "Swords of Iron" war, the Rehabilitation Fund was established to support communities that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected, with support spanning:

- Immediate emergency needs
- Mid-term community recovery
- Long-term rehabilitation and growth

Documented war impact includes:

- 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev severely damaged during the October 7 massacre
- 19 kibbutzim along the northern border evacuated for extended periods
- 8 kibbutzim in the central Hula Valley under constant threat without evacuation

Hundreds of residents were killed or injured, communities were uprooted, and vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

Amid renewed escalation with Iran and continued missile threats, public shelters remain overcrowded and many families (including children) are spending extended periods in under-equipped shelter environments. The Israeli Home Front Command's Northern Protection Program is still incomplete, leaving 27 kibbutzim without adequate protected rooms (Mamad). Communities that recently returned after prolonged evacuation are again under threat, with ongoing emotional strain and pressure on social structures.

## Program Details

### 1) Emergency Shelter Kits (Northern Border)

Each kit includes:

- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity and play kits for children

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

### 2) Community Emergency Support Program

Planned support activities include:

- **Support for Local Leadership:** Guidance and assistance for kibbutz management and key crisis role holders
- **Cultural and Community Activities:** Programs to preserve social cohesion and a sense of normalcy
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted assistance for vulnerable older adults during prolonged emergency periods
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** Protected-space programming for teenagers and youth to maintain educational and social continuity

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

## Expected Impact

With donor support, the Fund expects to:

- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters
- Provide essential services and infrastructure during missile attacks
- Strengthen community resilience and social cohesion
- Support youth, families, and vulnerable populations
- Enable kibbutzim to withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing recovery

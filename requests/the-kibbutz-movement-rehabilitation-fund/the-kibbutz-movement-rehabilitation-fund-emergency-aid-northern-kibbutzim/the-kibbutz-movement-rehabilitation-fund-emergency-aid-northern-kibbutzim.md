## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** in emergency support for kibbutz communities in northern Israel facing renewed escalation and missile threats. The request combines:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** for 27 public shelters to provide core safety and living essentials.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** across the same 27 kibbutzim to sustain social, emotional, and communal functioning under prolonged emergency conditions.

This funding is intended to improve safety, dignity, service continuity, and resilience while communities remain under active threat.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500): $94,500
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259 kibbutzim** across Israel, guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience. Following the October 7 attacks and the "Swords of Iron" war, the Rehabilitation Fund was established to address:
- Immediate emergency needs
- Mid-term community recovery
- Long-term rehabilitation and growth

War impact cited across sources:
- 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev were severely damaged during the October 7 massacre.
- 19 kibbutzim along the northern border were evacuated for extended periods.
- 8 kibbutzim in the central Hula Valley remained under constant threat without evacuation.
- Hundreds of residents were killed or injured, and vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

Current emergency context:
- Renewed regional escalation (including Iran-related escalation) and continued missile threats have left thousands vulnerable.
- Public shelters are overcrowded.
- The Israeli Home Front Command's Northern Protection Program is incomplete, leaving 27 kibbutzim without adequate protected rooms (Mamad).
- Families, including many children, are spending long periods in shelters lacking basic infrastructure.
- Recently returned northern communities face renewed threat, deep emotional strain, and pressure on social/community systems.

## Program Details

### 1) Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

The Fund seeks donor support to equip shelters so families can remain with basic safety, dignity, and essential services.

**Contents of each kit:**
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity and play kits for children

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

### 2) Community Emergency Support Program

Beyond physical protection, communities require sustained social and emotional support, especially after prolonged evacuation and return under renewed threat.

**Support activities include:**
- **Support for Local Leadership:** Guidance for kibbutz management and key role holders operating under crisis conditions.
- **Cultural and Community Activities:** Programs to preserve cohesion and normalcy.
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted support for vulnerable older adults during prolonged emergencies.
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** Programming for teenagers and youth in protected spaces to preserve safety and continuity.

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

# Emergency Response for Kibbutz Communities
## The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund

### Background
The Kibbutz Movement is a network of 259 communities across Israel. Following October 7 and the subsequent war, the Fund was established to support evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected kibbutzim through immediate, mid-term, and long-term recovery needs.

### The Current Crisis
Severe war impacts, displacement, casualties, and service collapse have been followed by renewed missile threats. Public shelters are overcrowded, and urgent infrastructure plus community resilience support is required.

### Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits
27 kibbutzim in the northern border/Hula Valley area lack adequate Mamad coverage. Shelter kits are designed to provide practical, immediate support for families in prolonged shelter conditions.

### Community Emergency Support Program
Communities that recently returned from evacuation face renewed insecurity and sustained emotional strain. The program supports leadership capacity, social cohesion, elderly care, and youth continuity in protected settings.

### Budget Summary
- Shelter Kits: $94,500
- Community Emergency Support: $945,000
- Total: $1,039,500

### Commitment
The Fund states ongoing commitment to mobilize resources, coordinate support, and help affected kibbutz communities recover and rebuild.

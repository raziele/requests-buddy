## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund is requesting emergency funding to support northern kibbutz communities facing renewed security escalation and missile threats. The request has two integrated components:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits for 27 public shelters** in northern kibbutzim and the Hula Valley, including mattresses, seating, first-aid kits, high-capacity fans, Wi-Fi, folding tables, and children's activity kits, so that families—many with young children—can remain in public shelters with basic safety, dignity, and essential services during extended alerts.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** across the same 27 kibbutzim, providing support for local leadership, cultural and community activities, elderly care, and youth and educational programming in protected spaces, to strengthen social cohesion and resilience under ongoing security uncertainty.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **Emergency Shelter Kits** (27 kits × $3,500): $94,500  
- **Community Emergency Support** ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000  

**Total Request:** $1,039,500 to improve safety, dignity, and resilience while families remain under missile threat and communities confront renewed escalation.

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259 kibbutzim** spread across Israel, from the northern border to the southern Negev. Guided by the principles of mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience, the Movement supports kibbutz communities in both routine times and national emergencies.

Following the devastating October 7th attacks and the subsequent "Swords of Iron" war, the needs of kibbutz communities increased dramatically and new urgent challenges emerged. In response, **The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund** was established to support kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected by the attacks and the ongoing war. The Fund addresses:
- Immediate emergency needs
- Mid-term community recovery
- Long-term rehabilitation and growth

The attacks and subsequent war severely impacted kibbutz communities across Israel:
- **22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev** were severely damaged during the October 7th massacre.
- **19 kibbutzim along the northern border** were evacuated for extended periods.
- **8 kibbutzim in the central Hula Valley** remained under constant threat without being evacuated.

Entire communities were uprooted from their homes. Hundreds of residents were killed or injured, and vital services collapsed or were destroyed. The Fund supports kibbutzim throughout their journey of recovery, rebuilding, and renewal.

Today, amid renewed escalation (including with Iran) and continued missile threats, thousands of northern residents remain vulnerable. Public shelters are overcrowded and communities face persistent uncertainty. The Israeli Home Front Command's **Northern Protection Program** has not yet been completed, leaving **27 kibbutzim along the northern border and in the Hula Valley** without adequate protected rooms (Mamad). As a result, families—many with children—are spending long periods in public shelters that lack basic equipment and infrastructure.

The 19 northern kibbutzim that were previously evacuated have only recently returned home and are again facing severe security threats. The long displacement period created deep emotional strain, and ongoing uncertainty is placing both individuals and community structures under immense pressure.

In this context, the Fund is asking partners worldwide to support:
1. **Emergency Shelter Kits**, so families can remain in shelters with basic safety, dignity, and essential services; and  
2. **Community Emergency Support**, including local leadership guidance, cultural and community activities, support for elderly residents, and youth and educational programming in protected spaces.

Donor support will directly improve shelter conditions, strengthen resilience, and help kibbutzim withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing their recovery.

## Program Details

### 1. Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

Due to the escalation in northern Israel, public shelters are currently housing large numbers of families. Without completed protection infrastructure, these shelters are functioning as de facto living spaces during extended alerts and periods of missile fire.

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund seeks donor support to provide **Emergency Shelter Kits** to **27 kibbutzim** along the northern border and in the Hula Valley. These kits will ensure that families forced to remain in shelters can do so with basic safety, dignity, and essential services.

#### Contents of Each Shelter Kit

Each kit will include:
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity and play kits for children

**Budget for Shelter Kits:**

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Number of kits | 27 |
| Total required | $94,500 |

These kits will significantly improve conditions for families spending extended periods in shelters during missile attacks, helping to maintain dignity, reduce stress, and support basic comfort and connectivity.

### 2. Community Emergency Support Program

Beyond physical protection, kibbutz communities require social, emotional, and communal support to withstand protracted crises. The 19 kibbutzim along the northern border that endured a prolonged evacuation and have only recently returned home are now once again under severe security threat. The combined impact of displacement, loss, and renewed danger is placing exceptional strain on residents and community systems.

The **Community Emergency Support Program** will provide:

- **Support for Local Leadership:**  
  Guidance and assistance for kibbutz management and key community role holders who are managing crisis conditions, including decision-making under uncertainty, communication with residents, and coordination of emergency responses.

- **Cultural and Community Activities:**  
  Programs within the kibbutzim to maintain social cohesion, a sense of normalcy, and shared community life, even when much of daily activity must take place in or near protected spaces.

- **Support for Elderly Residents:**  
  Targeted assistance for vulnerable older residents during periods of prolonged emergency, addressing isolation, anxiety, and practical needs when mobility and access to services may be limited.

- **Youth and Educational Activities:**  
  Organized programming for teenagers and youth within protected spaces, ensuring safety while maintaining educational and social continuity and mitigating trauma and disengagement among young people.

**Budget for Community Emergency Support:**

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

This investment will allow each participating kibbutz to tailor emergency community support to its specific needs while adhering to shared guiding principles around resilience and inclusion.

With donor support, the Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund will:

- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters for families and children.
- Provide essential services and infrastructure (such as Wi-Fi and first-aid) during missile attacks and extended alerts.
- Strengthen community resilience and social cohesion under ongoing security pressure.

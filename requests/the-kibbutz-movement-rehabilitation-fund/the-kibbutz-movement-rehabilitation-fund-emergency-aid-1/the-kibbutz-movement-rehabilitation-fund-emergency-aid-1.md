## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** in emergency support for kibbutz communities facing renewed escalation, missile threats, and prolonged shelter use.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **Emergency Shelter Kits:** $94,500 total (27 kits × $3,500)
- **Community Emergency Support Program:** $945,000 total ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim)

The request combines immediate physical shelter needs with social-emotional and communal resilience programming so families can remain safe, maintain dignity, and sustain community functioning during ongoing security instability.

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259 kibbutzim** across Israel, from the northern border to the Negev, guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience.

Following the October 7 attacks and the "Swords of Iron" war, the Rehabilitation Fund was established to support kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected, spanning:
- Immediate emergency needs
- Mid-term recovery
- Long-term rehabilitation and growth

Reported impacts include:
- **22** Western Negev kibbutzim severely damaged
- **19** northern border kibbutzim evacuated for extended periods
- **8** central Hula Valley kibbutzim under constant threat without evacuation

As renewed regional escalation continues, public shelters are overcrowded and the Israeli Home Front Command's Northern Protection Program remains incomplete, leaving **27** kibbutzim without adequate Mamad coverage. Families, including many children, are spending extended periods in shelters lacking essential infrastructure. Communities that recently returned from evacuation face renewed threats, emotional strain, and pressure on social systems and leadership capacity.

## Program Details

### 1) Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits

Each kit is designed to support basic safety, dignity, and continuity for families in shelters.

**Kit contents (per shelter):**
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity/play kits for children

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

### 2) Community Emergency Support Program

This program addresses social, emotional, and organizational resilience needs during prolonged emergency periods.

**Core activities:**
- Support for local leadership and key crisis role holders
- Cultural/community programming to preserve cohesion and normalcy
- Targeted support for elderly residents
- Youth and educational activities in protected spaces

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

## Expected Impact

With donor support, the Fund aims to:
- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters
- Provide essential services and infrastructure during missile attacks
- Strengthen social cohesion and community resilience
- Support vulnerable populations, including youth, families, and elderly residents
- Enable communities to sustain recovery while managing ongoing security threats

## Commitment

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund states it will continue mobilizing resources, coordinating support, and standing alongside kibbutz communities as long as emergency and recovery needs persist.

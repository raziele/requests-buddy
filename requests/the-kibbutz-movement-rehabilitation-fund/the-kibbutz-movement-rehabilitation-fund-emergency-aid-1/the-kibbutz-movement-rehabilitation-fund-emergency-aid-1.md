## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** in emergency funding for kibbutz communities facing renewed escalation and missile threats.

### Funding Requested
**$1,039,500**

### Funding Breakdown
- **Emergency Shelter Kits:** 27 kits × $3,500 = **$94,500**
- **Community Emergency Support:** $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim = **$945,000**

### What the funding supports
1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** for public shelters housing families (especially those with children), including basic infrastructure and essential supplies.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** for leadership support, cultural/community continuity, support for elderly residents, and youth educational/social programming in protected spaces.

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259 kibbutzim** across Israel, from the northern border to the southern Negev. Following the October 7 attacks and the “Swords of Iron” war, the Rehabilitation Fund was established to support kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected.

Documented impact context includes:
- **22 kibbutzim** in the Western Negev severely damaged during the October 7 massacre
- **19 kibbutzim** along the northern border evacuated for extended periods
- **8 kibbutzim** in the central Hula Valley under constant threat without evacuation

Entire communities were uprooted; hundreds of residents were killed or injured; and vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

Amid renewed escalation with Iran and continued missile threats, vulnerability remains high. The Israeli Home Front Command’s Northern Protection Program is not yet complete, leaving **27 kibbutzim** without adequate protected rooms (Mamad). Families are spending extended periods in overcrowded public shelters lacking basic equipment. Communities that recently returned from long displacement face renewed threat, emotional strain, and pressure on social systems.

Each shelter kit includes:
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity and play kits for children

Support initiatives include:

- **Cultural and Community Activities:** Programming to maintain social cohesion and normalcy
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted support for vulnerable older adults during prolonged emergency periods
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** Teen/youth programming in protected spaces to preserve continuity and safety

## Expected Impact

With donor support, the Fund expects to:
- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters
- Provide essential services and infrastructure during missile attacks
- Strengthen community resilience and social cohesion
- Support families, youth, and vulnerable populations (including elderly residents)
- Help kibbutzim withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing recovery

## Our Commitment

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund states it remains committed to supporting kibbutzim and neighboring communities along Israel’s borders for as long as needed, mobilizing resources and coordinating recovery, rebuilding, and renewal.

## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** in emergency funding to support 27 kibbutzim in northern Israel facing renewed missile threats and incomplete civilian protection infrastructure.

The request includes two components:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** for public shelters used by families during prolonged alerts, to provide basic safety, dignity, and essential services.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** to stabilize community functioning and resilience under sustained threat.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500): $94,500
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim across Israel, from the northern border to the southern Negev, guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience.

Following the October 7 attacks and the subsequent "Swords of Iron" war, the Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established to address:
- Immediate emergency needs
- Mid-term community recovery
- Long-term rehabilitation and growth

Documented impact across kibbutz communities includes:
- 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev severely damaged
- 19 kibbutzim along the northern border evacuated for extended periods
- 8 kibbutzim in the central Hula Valley under constant threat without evacuation

Entire communities were uprooted; hundreds of residents were killed or injured; and vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

Amid renewed escalation with Iran and continued missile threats, thousands remain vulnerable. The Israeli Home Front Command's Northern Protection Program is not yet complete, leaving 27 kibbutzim without adequate protected rooms (Mamad). Families, including many with children, are spending extended periods in overcrowded public shelters that lack basic equipment and infrastructure. Communities that recently returned from evacuation are again under severe pressure, with ongoing emotional strain and social-system fragility.

## Program Details

### 1) Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

The Fund seeks support to equip shelters so displaced or sheltering families can remain safely and with basic dignity.

**Each shelter kit includes:**
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity and play kits for children

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

### 2) Community Emergency Support Program

Beyond physical protection, communities require social, emotional, educational, and governance support during prolonged emergency periods.

**Planned activities include:**
- **Support for Local Leadership:** Guidance for kibbutz management and key role holders operating under crisis conditions.
- **Cultural and Community Activities:** In-community programming to maintain cohesion and normalcy.
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted support for vulnerable older adults.
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** Protected-space programming for teenagers and youth to sustain safety, continuity, and engagement.

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

## Expected Impact

With donor support, the Fund aims to:
- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters
- Provide essential services and emergency infrastructure during missile attacks
- Strengthen community resilience and social cohesion
- Support youth, families, elderly residents, and other vulnerable populations
- Enable kibbutzim to withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing recovery, rebuilding, and renewal

## Our Commitment

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund states it remains committed to standing beside Israel's kibbutzim and neighboring communities along the borders, mobilizing resources and coordinating support as long as needed.

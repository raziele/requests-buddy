## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** for **27** northern kibbutzim facing renewed missile threat **without adequate Mamad**: (1) **Emergency Shelter Kits** — equip public shelters with basic furniture, first aid, fans, Wi‑Fi (with backup power), and children’s activity kits (**27 × $3,500 = $94,500**); (2) **Community Emergency Support Program** — leadership guidance, cultural/community activities, elderly support, youth and educational programming in protected spaces (**$35,000 × 27 = $945,000**). The aim is safety, dignity, essential services in shelters, and stronger community resilience during prolonged emergencies.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500): $94,500  
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000  

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259** kibbutzim across Israel. After October 7 and the “Swords of Iron” war, the **Rehabilitation Fund** was set up for kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected. **22** Western Negev kibbutzim were severely damaged; **19** along the northern border were evacuated for long periods; **8** in the central Hula Valley stayed under constant threat. Hundreds were killed or injured; vital services collapsed.

Today, renewed escalation (including with Iran) and missile threats leave thousands vulnerable. The **Northern Protection Program** is **not yet complete**, so **27** border and Hula Valley kibbutzim lack adequate Mamad; families—many with children—endure **overcrowded public shelters** missing basic equipment. The **19** northern kibbutzim had only recently returned from evacuation and again face severe threats; displacement caused deep strain and ongoing uncertainty stresses individuals and communal structures.

The Fund asks for donor support for **Emergency Shelter Kits** (chairs, mattresses, fans, tables, Wi‑Fi routers, first-aid, children’s kits) and **Community Emergency Support** (leadership, culture/community life, elderly, youth/education in protected spaces) so shelter conditions improve, cohesion strengthens, and communities can endure security pressure while recovering.

### Scope and Fund mission (Background)

The Movement operates on mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience—in routine times and emergencies.

After October 7 and “Swords of Iron,” needs surged; the Rehabilitation Fund addresses **immediate emergency needs**, **mid-term recovery**, and **long-term rehabilitation and growth**, supporting kibbutzim through recovery, rebuilding, and renewal.

### The Current Crisis

- 22 Western Negev kibbutzim severely damaged in the Oct 7 massacre.  
- 19 northern-border kibbutzim evacuated for extended periods.  
- 8 central Hula Valley kibbutzim under constant threat without evacuation.  

Communities were uprooted; hundreds killed or injured; services destroyed. Renewed escalation and missile threats keep thousands vulnerable; shelters are overcrowded; infrastructure and resilience support needs are **urgent**.

### Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

Northern escalation fills public shelters. Until Home Front Command completes the Northern Protection Program, **27** kibbutzim lack adequate Mamad; families—many with children—stay long hours in unequipped shelters.

**Emergency Shelter Kits** target basic safety, dignity, and essential services.

**Each kit includes:** 20 stackable chairs; 50 foam or inflatable mattresses; 3 high-capacity fans; 2 folding tables; 2 Wi‑Fi routers with backup batteries; 2 full first-aid kits; 2 children’s activity/play kits.

### Community Emergency Support Program

Beyond physical protection: social, emotional, and communal support. The **19** northern kibbutzim returned after prolonged evacuation yet face severe threats again. Rebuilding social fabric competes with displacement trauma, ongoing security anxiety, and pressure on individuals and institutions.

**Activities:**  
- **Local leadership:** guidance for management and key role-holders under crisis.  
- **Cultural/community programs** inside kibbutzim for cohesion and normalcy.  
- **Elderly:** targeted help during prolonged emergency.  
- **Youth/education:** programming for teens/youth in **protected spaces** for safety + continuity.

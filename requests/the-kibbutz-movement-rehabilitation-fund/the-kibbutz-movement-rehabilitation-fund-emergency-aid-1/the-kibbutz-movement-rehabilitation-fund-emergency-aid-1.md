## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** in emergency support for kibbutz communities facing renewed escalation and ongoing missile threats in northern Israel. Funding is requested for two parallel components:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** for 27 kibbutzim, equipping public shelters with essential items to support families sheltering for prolonged periods.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** across the same 27 kibbutzim to sustain leadership capacity, social cohesion, and support for vulnerable populations.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500): $94,500
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim across Israel, from the northern border to the Negev, grounded in mutual responsibility, solidarity, and resilience. Following the October 7 attacks and the subsequent "Swords of Iron" war, the Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established to support kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected, across immediate, mid-term, and long-term recovery needs.

Reported impacts include:
- 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev severely damaged during the October 7 massacre
- 19 kibbutzim along the northern border evacuated for extended periods
- 8 kibbutzim in the central Hula Valley under constant threat without evacuation
- Hundreds of residents killed or injured, with vital services collapsed or destroyed

Amid renewed escalation with Iran and continued missile threats, thousands of residents remain vulnerable. The Israeli Home Front Command's Northern Protection Program is not yet complete, leaving 27 northern/Hula Valley kibbutzim without adequate protected rooms (Mamad). Families, including many with children, are spending extended periods in overcrowded public shelters lacking basic infrastructure. Communities recently returned from evacuation are again under severe pressure, with prolonged emotional strain and social systems under stress.

## Program Details

### 1) Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

The Fund seeks support to provide shelter kits that ensure minimum safety, dignity, and essential services for families in prolonged shelter conditions.

**Each shelter kit includes:**
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity and play kits for children

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

### 2) Community Emergency Support Program

Beyond physical protection, the Fund proposes social, emotional, and communal support to stabilize communities under recurring emergency conditions.

**Support activities include:**
- **Support for Local Leadership:** Guidance and assistance for kibbutz management and key role holders managing crisis conditions
- **Cultural and Community Activities:** Programs to preserve social cohesion and normalcy
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted assistance for vulnerable older adults during prolonged emergencies
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** Protected-space programming for teenagers and youth to maintain continuity and wellbeing

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

## Expected Impact

With donor support, the Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund aims to:
- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters
- Provide essential infrastructure and services during missile attacks
- Strengthen community resilience and social cohesion
- Support families, youth, elderly residents, and other vulnerable groups
- Enable kibbutzim to withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing recovery, rebuilding, and renewal

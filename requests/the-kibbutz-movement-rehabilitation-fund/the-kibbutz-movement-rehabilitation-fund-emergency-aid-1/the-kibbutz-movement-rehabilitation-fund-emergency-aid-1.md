## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** for **27** kibbutzim in northern Israel that lack adequate protected rooms (Mamad) and face renewed missile threat. Funding covers:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** — **$94,500** (27 kits @ **$3,500**): equip public shelters with chairs, mattresses, fans, tables, Wi-Fi routers (with backup batteries), first-aid kits, and children’s activity/play kits so families—many with children—can remain in shelters with basic safety, dignity, and essential services.

2. **Community Emergency Support Program** — **$945,000** (**$35,000 × 27** kibbutzim): local leadership guidance, cultural and community activities, targeted support for elderly residents, and youth/educational programming in protected spaces—addressing strain after prolonged evacuation, return, and renewed security pressure.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500): $94,500  
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000  

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259** kibbutzim across Israel (northern border to Negev), guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience. After the October 7th attacks and the **“Swords of Iron”** war, the **Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund** was established for kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected—covering immediate emergency needs, mid-term recovery, and long-term rehabilitation and growth.

War impact (as stated in materials): **22** Western Negev kibbutzim severely damaged in the massacre; **19** northern-border kibbutzim evacuated for extended periods; **8** central Hula Valley kibbutzim under constant threat without evacuation. Communities uprooted; hundreds killed or injured; vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

Today, amid **renewed escalation with Iran** and continued missile threats, thousands remain vulnerable; public shelters are overcrowded. The **Northern Protection Program** is **not yet complete**, so **27** border and Hula Valley kibbutzim still lack adequate Mamad; families spend long periods in underequipped public shelters. The **19** evacuated northern kibbutzim have only recently returned and again face severe threats; displacement and ongoing uncertainty create deep emotional strain and pressure on individuals and community structures.

The Fund asks global partners for the two initiatives above to improve shelter conditions, strengthen resilience and social cohesion, and help kibbutzim withstand ongoing security challenges while recovery continues.

### Background

The Movement supports kibbutzim in routine times and emergencies. After October 7 and “Swords of Iron,” the Rehabilitation Fund addresses immediate emergency needs, mid-term community recovery, and long-term rehabilitation and growth.

### The Current Crisis

- 22 Western Negev kibbutzim severely damaged (Oct 7).  
- 19 northern-border kibbutzim evacuated for extended periods.  
- 8 central Hula Valley kibbutzim under constant threat without evacuation.  

Renewed escalation and missile threats leave thousands vulnerable; shelters overcrowded; need for infrastructure and resilience support described as urgent and unprecedented.

### Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

**27 kibbutzim** along the border and in the Hula Valley lack adequate Mamad while the Northern Protection Program is incomplete. The Fund seeks **Emergency Shelter Kits** at **$3,500** each, **27** kits, **$94,500** total.

**Each kit includes:**
- 20 stackable chairs  
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses  
- 3 high-capacity fans  
- 2 folding tables  
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries  
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits  
- 2 activity and play kits for children

- **Support for Elderly Residents:** targeted assistance during prolonged emergency.  
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** programming for teenagers/youth in protected spaces for safety and continuity.

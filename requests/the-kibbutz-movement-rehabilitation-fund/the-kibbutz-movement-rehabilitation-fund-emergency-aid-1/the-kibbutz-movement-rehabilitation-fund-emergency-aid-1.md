## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** for 27 northern kibbutzim under renewed missile threat that lack adequate protected rooms (Mamad) while the Northern Protection Program remains incomplete.

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** — **$94,500** (27 kits × **$3,500**): equip public shelters with stackable chairs, mattresses, fans, folding tables, Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries, first-aid kits, and children’s activity/play kits so families can shelter with basic safety, dignity, and essential services.

2. **Community Emergency Support Program** — **$945,000** (**$35,000** × 27 kibbutzim): leadership guidance for management and key role holders; cultural and community activities; targeted support for elderly residents during prolonged emergencies; youth and educational programming in protected spaces.

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259** kibbutzim across Israel, guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience. After the October 7 attacks and the “Swords of Iron” war, the **Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund** was established for kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected. The Fund’s scope spans immediate emergency needs, mid-term recovery, and long-term rehabilitation and growth.

War impact (as stated in materials): **22** Western Negev kibbutzim severely damaged; **19** northern-border kibbutzim evacuated for long periods; **8** central Hula Valley kibbutzim under constant threat without evacuation. Hundreds killed or injured; vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

Today, renewed escalation (including with Iran) and continued missile threats leave thousands vulnerable. Public shelters are overcrowded; **27** border and Hula Valley kibbutzim still lack adequate Mamad. Families—many with children—remain for long periods in unequipped shelters. The **19** evacuated northern kibbutzim have recently returned but again face severe threats; displacement caused deep emotional strain, and ongoing uncertainty stresses individuals and communal structures.

Donor support funds physical shelter kits and communal/emotional support so conditions improve, cohesion strengthens, and communities can endure security pressure while recovery continues.

# Emergency Response for Kibbutz Communities

## The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund

### Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim from the northern border to the southern Negev. Guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience, it supports kibbutz communities in routine times and national emergencies.

After the October 7 attacks and “Swords of Iron,” needs surged. The Rehabilitation Fund supports kibbutzim evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected. It addresses immediate emergency needs, mid-term community recovery, and long-term rehabilitation and growth, accompanying kibbutzim through recovery, rebuilding, and renewal.

### The Current Crisis

- 22 Western Negev kibbutzim severely damaged during the October 7 massacre.
- 19 northern-border kibbutzim evacuated for extended periods.
- 8 central Hula Valley kibbutzim under constant threat without evacuation.

Communities were uprooted; hundreds killed or injured; vital services collapsed or were destroyed. Amid renewed escalation with Iran and ongoing missile threats, thousands remain vulnerable. Shelters are overcrowded; uncertainty is persistent. Emergency infrastructure and resilience support are urgent.

### Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

Escalation in the north has filled public shelters. The Home Front Command **Northern Protection Program** is not complete, leaving **27** border and Hula Valley kibbutzim without adequate Mamad. Families—many with children—stay long periods in shelters lacking basic equipment.

The Fund seeks support for **Emergency Shelter Kits** so families can remain with safety, dignity, and essential services.

#### Contents of Each Shelter Kit

- 20 stackable chairs  
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses  
- 3 high-capacity fans  
- 2 folding tables  
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries  
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits  
- 2 activity and play kits for children  

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

### Community Emergency Support Program

Beyond physical protection, communities need social, emotional, and communal support. The 19 northern kibbutzim endured prolonged evacuation and have only recently returned, yet face severe threats again. Rebuilding social fabric continues under strain from displacement, ongoing security uncertainty, and pressure on individuals and institutions.

#### Community Support Activities

- **Support for Local Leadership:** Guidance for kibbutz management and key community role holders under crisis conditions.  
- **Cultural and Community Activities:** Programs within kibbutzim for cohesion and normalcy.  
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted help for vulnerable older adults during prolonged emergencies.  
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** Programming for teenagers and youth in protected spaces for safety, education, and social continuity.

| Total funding required | $945,000 |

### Expected Impact

With donor support, the Fund will improve shelter safety and living conditions; provide essential services and infrastructure during attacks; strengthen resilience and cohesion; support youth, families, and vulnerable populations; and help kibbutzim withstand ongoing challenges while recovering.

### Our Commitment

The Fund remains committed to Israel’s kibbutzim and neighboring border communities, mobilizing resources and coordinating support so they can recover, rebuild, and thrive.

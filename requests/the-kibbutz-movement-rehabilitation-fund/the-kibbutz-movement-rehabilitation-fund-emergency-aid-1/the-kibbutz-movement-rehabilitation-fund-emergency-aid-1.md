## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** for **27 kibbutzim** in northern Israel that lack adequate protected rooms (Mamad) and face renewed missile threat.

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** — **$94,500** (27 kits × **$3,500**): equip public shelters with stackable chairs, foam/inflatable mattresses, high-capacity fans, folding tables, Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries, first-aid kits, and children’s activity/play kits so families—many with children—can remain in shelters with basic safety, dignity, and essential services.

2. **Community Emergency Support Program** — **$945,000** (**$35,000** × 27 kibbutzim): leadership guidance for kibbutz management and key role holders; cultural and community activities; targeted support for elderly residents during prolonged emergencies; youth and educational programming in protected spaces.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 @ $3,500): $94,500  
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27): $945,000  

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259 kibbutzim** across Israel. After the October 7 attacks and the “Swords of Iron” war, the **Rehabilitation Fund** was established for kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected—covering immediate emergency needs, mid-term recovery, and long-term rehabilitation.

Impact snapshot from the war: **22** Western Negev kibbutzim severely damaged in the massacre; **19** along the northern border evacuated for long periods; **8** in the central Hula Valley under constant threat. Communities were uprooted; hundreds killed or injured; vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

Today, renewed escalation (including with Iran) and continued missile threats leave thousands vulnerable. The **Israeli Home Front Command Northern Protection Program** is not yet complete, so **27** border and Hula Valley kibbutzim still lack adequate Mamad; families spend long stretches in **overcrowded public shelters** without basic equipment. The **19** northern kibbutzim that evacuated have **recently returned** and again face severe threats; displacement caused deep emotional strain, and ongoing uncertainty strains individuals and community structures.

The Fund asks global partners for support to improve shelter conditions, strengthen **resilience and social cohesion**, and help kibbutzim withstand security pressure while recovery continues.

### Emergency Response for Kibbutz Communities — The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund

#### Background

The Movement works from principles of mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience—in routine times and emergencies. After October 7 and “Swords of Iron,” the Fund was created for evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected kibbutzim, addressing immediate needs, mid-term recovery, and long-term rehabilitation and growth.

#### The Current Crisis

- 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev severely damaged during the October 7 massacre.  
- 19 along the northern border evacuated for extended periods.  
- 8 in the central Hula Valley remained under constant threat without evacuation.  

Thousands remain vulnerable; shelters are overcrowded; need for emergency infrastructure and resilience support is urgent.

#### Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

Escalation in the north has shelters holding large numbers of families. Until Northern Protection Program work is complete, **27 kibbutzim** lack adequate Mamad; families spend long periods in unequipped public shelters. **Emergency Shelter Kits** aim to provide basic safety, dignity, and essential services.

**Contents of each kit**
- 20 stackable chairs  
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses  
- 3 high-capacity fans  
- 2 folding tables  
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries  
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits  
- 2 activity and play kits for children  

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

#### Community Emergency Support Program

Communities need social, emotional, and communal support—not only physical protection. Returned northern kibbutzim face renewed threats after rebuilding social fabric; displacement strain, ongoing uncertainty, and pressure on individuals and structures make emergency support critical.

**Activities**
- **Support for local leadership** — guidance for management and key role holders under crisis conditions.  
- **Cultural and community activities** — programs within kibbutzim for cohesion and normalcy.  
- **Support for elderly residents** — targeted help during prolonged emergencies.  
- **Youth and educational activities** — programming for teenagers/youth in protected spaces for safety and continuity.  

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

#### Total Funding Request

| Program | Cost |
|---------|------|
| Emergency Shelter Kits | $94,500 |
| Community Emergency Support | $945,000 |
| **Total funding need** | **$1,039,500** |

#### Expected Impact

Improve safety and living conditions in shelters; provide essential services during attacks; strengthen resilience and cohesion; support youth, families, and vulnerable populations; help kibbutzim endure ongoing challenges while recovering.

#### Our Commitment

The Fund commits to standing beside Israel’s kibbutzim and neighboring border communities, mobilizing resources and coordinating support as long as need exists so communities can recover, rebuild, and thrive.

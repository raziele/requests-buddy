## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** for 27 kibbutzim in northern Israel that lack adequate protected rooms (Mamad) and face renewed missile threat. Funding covers:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** — **$94,500** (27 kits × **$3,500**): equip public shelters with basic furniture, first aid, Wi‑Fi, fans, and children’s activity kits so families can stay in shelters with safety and dignity.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** — **$945,000** (**$35,000** × 27 kibbutzim): leadership guidance, cultural and community activities, support for elderly residents, and youth/educational programming in protected spaces during prolonged emergencies.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500): $94,500  
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000  

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259** kibbutzim across Israel. After the October 7 attacks and the “Swords of Iron” war, the **Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund** was established for kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected. The Fund addresses immediate emergency needs, mid-term recovery, and long-term rehabilitation.

War impact (as stated in materials): **22** Western Negev kibbutzim severely damaged in the massacre; **19** northern-border kibbutzim evacuated for extended periods; **8** central Hula Valley kibbutzim under constant threat without evacuation. Hundreds killed or injured; vital services collapsed.

Today, renewed escalation (including with Iran) and missile threats leave thousands vulnerable. The Northern Protection Program is **not** finished, so **27** border and Hula Valley kibbutzim still lack adequate Mamadim. Families—many with children—spend long periods in **overcrowded** public shelters without basic equipment. The **19** northern kibbutzim that returned after long displacement face severe threats again; displacement, uncertainty, and strain affect individuals and community structures.

The ask pairs physical shelter support with communal, emotional, and organizational support so kibbutzim can endure ongoing security pressure while continuing recovery.

### Emergency Response for Kibbutz Communities — The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund

#### Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim from the northern border to the southern Negev. It is guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience in routine times and emergencies.

After October 7 and “Swords of Iron,” needs surged. The Rehabilitation Fund supports kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected, across:

- Immediate emergency needs  
- Mid-term community recovery  
- Long-term rehabilitation and growth  

#### The Current Crisis

- 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev severely damaged during the October 7 massacre.  
- 19 kibbutzim along the northern border evacuated for extended periods.  
- 8 kibbutzim in the central Hula Valley under constant threat without evacuation.  

Communities were uprooted; hundreds killed or injured; vital services collapsed or were destroyed. Amid renewed escalation and missile threats, shelters are overcrowded and uncertainty is persistent—needs for infrastructure and resilience support are urgent.

#### Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

Escalation in the north leaves public shelters housing many families. Until the Northern Protection Program completes Mamad coverage, **27** border and Hula Valley kibbutzim lack adequate protected rooms. Families—many with children—remain for long stretches in shelters without basic equipment.

**Emergency Shelter Kits** aim to provide basic safety, dignity, and essential services.

**Contents of each kit**

- 20 stackable chairs  
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses  
- 3 high-capacity fans  
- 2 folding tables  
- 2 Wi‑Fi routers with backup batteries  
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits  
- 2 activity and play kits for children  

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

#### Community Emergency Support Program

Beyond physical protection, communities need social, emotional, and communal support. The 19 northern kibbutzim endured prolonged evacuation and have recently returned, yet face severe threats again. Rebuilding social fabric continues under displacement-related strain, ongoing uncertainty, and pressure on individuals and institutions.

**Activities**

- **Support for local leadership:** guidance for management and key role holders under crisis conditions.  
- **Cultural and community activities:** programs within kibbutzim for cohesion and normalcy.  
- **Support for elderly residents:** targeted help during prolonged emergency.  
- **Youth and educational activities:** programming for teenagers/youth in protected spaces for safety and continuity.  

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

#### Total Funding Request

| Program | Cost |
|---------|------|
| Emergency Shelter Kits | $94,500 |
| Community Emergency Support | $945,000 |
| **Total funding need** | **$1,039,500** |

#### Expected Impact

- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters  
- Provide essential services and infrastructure during attacks  
- Strengthen resilience and social cohesion  
- Support youth, families, and vulnerable populations  
- Help kibbutzim withstand ongoing security challenges while recovering  

#### Our Commitment

The Fund commits to standing beside Israel’s kibbutzim and neighboring border communities, mobilizing resources and coordination as long as the need exists, so communities can recover, rebuild, and thrive.

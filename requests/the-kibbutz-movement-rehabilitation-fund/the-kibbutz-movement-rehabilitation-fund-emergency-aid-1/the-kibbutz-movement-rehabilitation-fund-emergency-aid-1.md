## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** for **27 kibbutzim** in northern Israel that lack adequate protected rooms (Mamad) and face renewed missile threat. Funding covers:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** — **$94,500** (27 kits × **$3,500**): equip public shelters with basic furniture, first-aid, Wi-Fi, and children’s activity kits so families can remain in shelters with safety, dignity, and essential services.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** — **$945,000** (**$35,000** × 27 kibbutzim): leadership support, cultural and youth activities, targeted assistance for elderly residents, and youth/educational programming in protected spaces during prolonged emergencies.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500): $94,500  
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000  

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259 kibbutzim** across Israel (northern border to southern Negev), guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience. After the October 7th attacks and the “Swords of Iron” war, the **Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund** was established for kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected. The Fund addresses immediate emergency needs, mid-term community recovery, and long-term rehabilitation and growth.

**War impact (as stated in materials):** 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev were severely damaged in the massacre; 19 along the northern border were evacuated for extended periods; 8 in the central Hula Valley remained under constant threat. Hundreds were killed or injured; vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

**Current northern need:** Renewed escalation (including with Iran) and continued missile threats leave thousands vulnerable. The Israeli Home Front Command’s **Northern Protection Program** is not yet complete, so **27** border and Hula Valley kibbutzim lack adequate Mamad. Families—many with children—spend long periods in **overcrowded** public shelters without basic equipment. The 19 northern kibbutzim that evacuated have only recently returned and again face severe threats; prolonged displacement caused deep emotional strain, and ongoing uncertainty stresses individuals and community structures.

The Fund asks global partners for support for physical shelter kits and community emergency programming to improve shelter conditions, strengthen resilience and social cohesion, and help kibbutzim withstand ongoing security challenges while recovery continues.

### Emergency Response for Kibbutz Communities — The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund

#### Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim from the northern border to the southern Negev. After the October 7th attacks and “Swords of Iron” war, needs grew sharply; the Rehabilitation Fund supports evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected kibbutzim across immediate, mid-term, and long-term needs, accompanying communities through recovery, rebuilding, and renewal.

#### The current crisis

- 22 Western Negev kibbutzim severely damaged during the October 7th massacre.  
- 19 northern-border kibbutzim evacuated for extended periods.  
- 8 central Hula Valley kibbutzim under constant threat without evacuation.  

Communities were uprooted; hundreds killed or injured; vital services collapsed or were destroyed. Amid renewed escalation and missile threats, shelters are overcrowded; uncertainty is persistent; emergency infrastructure and resilience support are described as urgent and unprecedented.

#### Immediate need: public shelter emergency kits (northern border)

Escalation in the north leaves large numbers of families in public shelters. The Northern Protection Program is incomplete; **27** border and Hula Valley kibbutzim lack adequate Mamad. Families—many with children—remain for long periods without basic equipment. **Emergency Shelter Kits** are sought so families can stay with basic safety, dignity, and essential services (contents and **$94,500** total as in tables above).

#### Community Emergency Support program

Beyond physical protection, social, emotional, and communal support is needed. Nineteen northern kibbutzim endured long evacuation and have recently returned but again face severe threats. Post-return rebuilding of social fabric is strained by displacement, ongoing security uncertainty, and pressure on individuals and institutions—hence the community support pillars and **$945,000** budget above.

#### Total funding request, expected impact, commitment

As summarized in the tables and bullets above: **$1,039,500** total; impact on shelter conditions, services, cohesion, vulnerable groups, and sustained recovery; ongoing commitment to border-area kibbutzim and neighbors.

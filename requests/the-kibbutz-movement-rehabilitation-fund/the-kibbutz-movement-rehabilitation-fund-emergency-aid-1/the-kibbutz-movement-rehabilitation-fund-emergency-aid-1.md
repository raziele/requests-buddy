## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** in emergency support for northern kibbutz communities under renewed missile threat.

Two funded components:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** for 27 kibbutzim to improve basic shelter conditions and dignity.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** across the same 27 kibbutzim to sustain leadership, community continuity, and vulnerable populations during prolonged emergency periods.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500 each): $94,500
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259 kibbutzim** across Israel, from the northern border to the southern Negev, guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and resilience.

Following the October 7 attacks and the "Swords of Iron" war, the Rehabilitation Fund was created to support kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected. Impact cited across sources includes:

- 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev severely damaged
- 19 northern border kibbutzim evacuated for extended periods
- 8 kibbutzim in the central Hula Valley under constant threat without evacuation
- Hundreds killed or injured; vital services collapsed or were destroyed

With renewed escalation (including references to Iran) and continued missile fire, thousands remain vulnerable. The Home Front Command's Northern Protection Program remains incomplete, leaving 27 kibbutzim without adequate Mamad coverage. Families, including children, are spending prolonged periods in overcrowded public shelters lacking basic infrastructure.

## Program Details

### 1) Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits

Emergency Shelter Kits are intended to provide basic safety, essential services, and humane living conditions in public shelters.

**Each kit includes:**
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity/play kits for children

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

### 2) Community Emergency Support Program

Beyond physical sheltering, the program addresses social and emotional strain after prolonged evacuation and continued insecurity.

**Support activities include:**
- **Local Leadership Support:** Guidance for kibbutz management and key role holders under crisis conditions
- **Cultural and Community Activities:** In-kibbutz programming to preserve cohesion and normalcy
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted assistance for vulnerable older adults during prolonged emergencies
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** Protected-space programming for youth and teenagers to maintain social and educational continuity

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

## Expected Impact

With donor support, the Fund aims to:

- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters
- Provide essential services and infrastructure during missile attacks
- Strengthen community resilience and social cohesion
- Support families, youth, elderly residents, and other vulnerable groups
- Help kibbutzim withstand ongoing security pressure while continuing recovery, rebuilding, and renewal

## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** for **27 kibbutzim** in northern Israel that lack adequate protected rooms (Mamad) and face renewed missile threat. Funding covers:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** — **$94,500** (27 kits @ **$3,500**): equip public shelters with basic furniture, first-aid, Wi-Fi, and children’s activity kits so families can shelter with safety, dignity, and essential services.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** — **$945,000** (**$35,000 × 27** kibbutzim): leadership support, cultural and youth activities, targeted assistance for elderly residents, and educational/social programming in protected spaces during prolonged emergencies.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 @ $3,500): $94,500
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259 kibbutzim** across Israel. After the October 7th attacks and the “Swords of Iron” war, the **Rehabilitation Fund** was established for kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected. It addresses immediate emergency needs, mid-term recovery, and long-term rehabilitation and growth.

**War impact (summarized):** 22 Western Negev kibbutzim severely damaged in the massacre; 19 along the northern border evacuated for long periods; 8 in the central Hula Valley under constant threat. Hundreds killed or injured; vital services collapsed. Entire communities were uprooted.

**Present need:** Escalation (including renewed tension with Iran) and missile threats leave thousands vulnerable. The Israeli Home Front Command’s **Northern Protection Program** is not yet complete, so **27** border and Hula Valley kibbutzim still lack adequate Mamadim. Families—many with children—stay long hours in **overcrowded public shelters** without basic equipment. The **19** northern kibbutzim that evacuated have recently returned but again face severe threats; displacement caused deep emotional strain, and ongoing uncertainty stresses individuals and community structures.

The Fund asks global partners for the two initiatives above to improve shelter conditions, strengthen resilience and social cohesion, and help kibbutzim endure security pressure while continuing recovery.

### Emergency Response for Kibbutz Communities — The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund

#### Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim from the northern border to the southern Negev, guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience—in routine times and national emergencies.

After October 7 and “Swords of Iron,” needs surged. The Rehabilitation Fund supports kibbutzim evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected. It addresses:

- Immediate emergency needs  
- Mid-term community recovery  
- Long-term rehabilitation and growth  

The Fund supports kibbutzim through recovery, rebuilding, and renewal.

#### The Current Crisis

- 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev severely damaged during the October 7 massacre.  
- 19 along the northern border evacuated for extended periods.  
- 8 in the central Hula Valley under constant threat without evacuation.  

Communities were uprooted; hundreds killed or injured; vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

Today, amid escalation with Iran and continued missile threats, thousands remain vulnerable. Shelters are overcrowded; uncertainty is persistent. Emergency infrastructure and resilience support are urgent.

#### Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

Escalation in the north has filled public shelters. The Northern Protection Program is incomplete, leaving **27 kibbutzim** along the border and in the Hula Valley without adequate Mamad. Families—many with children—remain for long periods in shelters lacking basic equipment.

**Emergency Shelter Kits** aim to provide basic safety, dignity, and essential services.

**Contents of each kit**

- 20 stackable chairs  
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses  
- 3 high-capacity fans  
- 2 folding tables  
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries  
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits  
- 2 activity and play kits for children  

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

#### Community Emergency Support Program

Beyond physical protection, communities need social, emotional, and communal support. The 19 northern kibbutzim endured prolonged evacuation and have only recently returned, yet face severe threats again. Post-return rebuilding of social fabric is strained by displacement, ongoing security uncertainty, and pressure on individuals and institutions.

**Activities**

- **Support for local leadership:** Guidance for kibbutz management and key role holders under crisis conditions.  
- **Cultural and community activities:** Programs within kibbutzim for cohesion and normalcy.  
- **Support for elderly residents:** Targeted help during prolonged emergency.  
- **Youth and educational activities:** Programming for teenagers/youth in protected spaces for safety and continuity.  

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

#### Total Funding Request

| Program | Cost |
|---------|------|
| Emergency Shelter Kits | $94,500 |
| Community Emergency Support | $945,000 |
| **Total funding need** | **$1,039,500** |

#### Expected Impact

With donor support, the Fund will:

- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters  
- Provide essential services and infrastructure during attacks  
- Strengthen resilience and social cohesion  
- Support youth, families, and vulnerable populations  
- Help kibbutzim withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing recovery  

#### Our Commitment

The Rehabilitation Fund remains committed to kibbutzim and neighboring border communities, mobilizing resources and coordination so they can recover, rebuild, and thrive as long as need exists.

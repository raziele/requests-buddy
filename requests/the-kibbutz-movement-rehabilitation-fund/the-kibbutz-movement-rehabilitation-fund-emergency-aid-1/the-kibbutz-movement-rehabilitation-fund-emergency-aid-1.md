## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** in emergency support for 27 northern kibbutzim under renewed missile threat and prolonged security instability.

The request includes two components:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** for public shelters in 27 kibbutzim to provide basic safety, dignity, and essential infrastructure for families sheltering for extended periods.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** across the same 27 kibbutzim to stabilize community functioning and psychosocial resilience through leadership support, cultural/community programming, elderly support, and youth educational activities in protected spaces.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500): $94,500
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259 kibbutzim** across Israel. Following the October 7 attacks and the subsequent “Swords of Iron” war, the Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established to address immediate, mid-term, and long-term recovery needs in affected communities.

Reported impacts include:
- **22 kibbutzim** in the Western Negev severely damaged during the October 7 massacre.
- **19 kibbutzim** along the northern border evacuated for extended periods.
- **8 kibbutzim** in the central Hula Valley under constant threat without evacuation.
- Hundreds killed or injured, with major disruption or collapse of essential services.

Amid renewed regional escalation and continued missile threats, thousands remain vulnerable. The Israeli Home Front Command’s Northern Protection Program is not yet complete, leaving 27 northern/Hula Valley kibbutzim without adequate protected rooms (Mamad). Families, including many children, are spending long periods in overcrowded public shelters lacking basic equipment. Recently returned communities are again facing severe threats, emotional strain, and pressure on community systems.

## Program Details

### 1) Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

Each shelter kit includes:
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity/play kits for children

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

### 2) Community Emergency Support Program

Planned activities:
- Support for local leadership and key role holders managing crisis conditions
- Cultural and community activities to sustain cohesion and normalcy
- Targeted support for elderly residents during prolonged emergency periods
- Youth and educational activities in protected spaces to maintain continuity and safety

## Expected Impact

With donor support, the Fund aims to:
- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters
- Provide essential services and infrastructure during missile attacks
- Strengthen community resilience and social cohesion
- Support youth, families, elderly residents, and other vulnerable populations
- Help kibbutzim withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing recovery, rebuilding, and renewal

## Our Commitment

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund states it will continue mobilizing resources, coordinating support, and standing with kibbutzim and neighboring border communities for as long as needed.

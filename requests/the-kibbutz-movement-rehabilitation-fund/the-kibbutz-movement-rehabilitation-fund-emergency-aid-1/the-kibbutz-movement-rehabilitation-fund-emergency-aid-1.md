## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** in emergency support for kibbutz communities under renewed missile threat in northern Israel.

The request includes two components:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** for public shelters in 27 kibbutzim to provide basic safety, dignity, and essential services for families spending extended time in shelters.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** across the same 27 kibbutzim to reinforce leadership capacity, social cohesion, and support for vulnerable populations under prolonged emergency conditions.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500): $94,500
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259 kibbutzim** across Israel, from the northern border to the southern Negev, grounded in mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience.

Following the October 7 attacks and the "Swords of Iron" war, the Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established to support kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected. The crisis impact cited includes:

- **22 kibbutzim** in the Western Negev severely damaged during the October 7 massacre
- **19 kibbutzim** along the northern border evacuated for extended periods
- **8 kibbutzim** in the central Hula Valley under constant threat without evacuation

Entire communities were uprooted, hundreds of residents were killed or injured, and vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

Amid renewed escalation with Iran and continued missile threats, thousands remain vulnerable. The Israeli Home Front Command's Northern Protection Program is still incomplete, leaving **27 kibbutzim** along the northern border and Hula Valley without adequate protected rooms (Mamad). Families, including many with children, are spending prolonged periods in overcrowded shelters lacking basic equipment. Communities that recently returned from evacuation are again under severe pressure, with persistent emotional strain and weakened community structures.

## Program Details

### 1) Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

Each kit includes:
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity and play kits for children

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

### 2) Community Emergency Support Program

Activities include:
- Support for local leadership (guidance for kibbutz management and key role holders)
- Cultural and community activities to maintain social cohesion and normalcy
- Targeted support for elderly residents during prolonged emergencies
- Youth and educational programming in protected spaces

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

## Expected Impact

With donor support, the Fund aims to:
- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters
- Provide essential services and infrastructure during missile attacks
- Strengthen community resilience and social cohesion
- Support youth, families, elderly residents, and other vulnerable populations
- Enable kibbutzim to withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing recovery, rebuilding, and renewal

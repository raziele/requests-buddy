## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** in emergency funding for 27 kibbutzim in northern Israel facing renewed missile threats and insufficient protected-room infrastructure.

The request includes two integrated components:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** for public shelters currently hosting families for prolonged periods, to provide basic safety, dignity, and essential services.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** to stabilize social and emotional functioning under ongoing threat through leadership support, community programming, elderly support, and youth activities in protected spaces.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500 each): $94,500
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim across Israel, from the northern border to the Negev, guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience.

Following the October 7 attacks and the "Swords of Iron" war, the Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established to support kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected, across:
- Immediate emergency needs
- Mid-term community recovery
- Long-term rehabilitation and growth

Reported impacts include:
- 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev severely damaged during October 7
- 19 kibbutzim along the northern border evacuated for extended periods
- 8 kibbutzim in the central Hula Valley under constant threat without evacuation

Entire communities were uprooted, hundreds of residents were killed or injured, and vital services were disrupted or destroyed.

Amid renewed regional escalation and continued missile threats, thousands of residents remain vulnerable. The Israeli Home Front Command's Northern Protection Program is still incomplete, leaving 27 kibbutzim without adequate Mamad coverage. Families, including children, are spending extended periods in overcrowded public shelters lacking basic equipment. Communities that recently returned from evacuation are again under severe security pressure, with sustained emotional strain and weakened community structures.

## Program Details

### 1) Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

Each shelter kit includes:
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity and play kits for children

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

These kits are intended to significantly improve day-to-day conditions for families sheltering during attacks.

### 2) Community Emergency Support Program

The Fund proposes crisis-period support across affected kibbutzim through:
- **Support for Local Leadership:** Guidance for kibbutz management and key role holders
- **Cultural and Community Activities:** Programs to sustain cohesion and normalcy
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted support for vulnerable older adults
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** Safe programming in protected spaces for teenagers and youth

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

## Expected Impact

With donor support, the Fund expects to:
- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters
- Provide essential services and infrastructure during missile attacks
- Strengthen community resilience and social cohesion
- Support youth, families, elderly residents, and other vulnerable populations
- Help kibbutzim withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing recovery and rebuilding

## Our Commitment

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund states its ongoing commitment to stand beside kibbutzim and neighboring communities along Israel's borders, mobilizing resources and coordinating support as long as needs persist.

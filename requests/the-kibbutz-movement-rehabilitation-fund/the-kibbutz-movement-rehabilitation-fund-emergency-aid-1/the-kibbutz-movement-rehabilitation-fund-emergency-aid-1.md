## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund is requesting **$1,039,500** to support 27 kibbutzim in northern Israel that lack adequate protected rooms (Mamad) and are under renewed missile threat. Funding would provide: (1) **Emergency Shelter Kits** ($94,500) to equip public shelters with basic furniture, first-aid, Wi-Fi, and children's activity kits; and (2) a **Community Emergency Support Program** ($945,000) to deliver leadership support, cultural and youth activities, and targeted assistance for elderly residents during prolonged emergencies. With this support, the Fund aims to improve shelter conditions, strengthen community resilience and social cohesion, and enable kibbutzim to withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing recovery.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500 each): $94,500
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim across Israel, from the northern border to the southern Negev. Guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience, the Movement supports kibbutz communities in routine times and national emergencies.

Following the October 7th attacks and the "Swords of Iron" war, the Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established to support kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected. The Fund addresses immediate emergency needs, mid-term community recovery, and long-term rehabilitation and growth.

**Impact of the war:** 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev were severely damaged during the October 7th massacre; 19 along the northern border were evacuated for extended periods; 8 in the central Hula Valley remained under constant threat without evacuation. Entire communities were uprooted, hundreds of residents were killed or injured, and vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

**Current situation:** Amid renewed escalation with Iran and continued missile threats, thousands of northern residents remain vulnerable. The Israeli Home Front Command's Northern Protection Program has not yet been completed, leaving 27 kibbutzim along the border and in the Hula Valley without adequate protected rooms (Mamad). Families—many with children—are spending long periods in overcrowded public shelters that lack basic equipment. The 19 northern kibbutzim that had been evacuated have only recently returned home and are again facing severe security threats; the long displacement created deep emotional strain and ongoing uncertainty is placing individuals and community structures under immense pressure. The Fund is asking partners worldwide to support two urgent efforts: (1) Emergency Shelter Kits so families can remain in shelters with basic safety, dignity, and essential services; and (2) Community Emergency Support including local leadership guidance, cultural and community activities, support for elderly residents, and youth and educational programming in protected spaces.

## Emergency Shelter Kits (Northern Border)

Public shelters are housing large numbers of families. Each **Emergency Shelter Kit** will ensure that families forced to remain in shelters can do so with basic safety, dignity, and essential services.

**Contents of each kit:**
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity and play kits for children

## Community Emergency Support Program

Beyond physical protection, kibbutz communities require social, emotional, and communal support. During the months following their return, communities have worked hard to rebuild their social fabric; however, the long displacement period created deep emotional strain, ongoing security uncertainty continues to affect residents, and both individuals and community structures are under immense pressure. As tensions rise again, emergency support for communities is critical.

**Community support activities:**
- **Support for Local Leadership:** Guidance and assistance for kibbutz management and key community role holders managing crisis conditions.
- **Cultural and Community Activities:** Programs held within the kibbutzim to maintain social cohesion and a sense of normalcy.
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted assistance for vulnerable older residents during periods of prolonged emergency.
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** Organized programming for teenagers and youth within protected spaces to ensure safety while maintaining educational and social continuity.

## Our Commitment

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund remains fully committed to standing beside Israel's kibbutzim and neighboring communities along the country's borders. As long as the need exists, the Fund will continue to mobilize resources, coordinate support, and ensure that these communities can recover, rebuild, and thrive again.

## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests emergency funding totaling **$1,039,500** to support **27 northern kibbutz communities** facing renewed security escalation and missile threats. The request includes two components:

1) **Emergency Shelter Kits** for public shelters (so families can remain safely and with dignity)
- **$94,500 total** for **27 kits @ $3,500 each**

2) **Community Emergency Support Program** across the same **27 kibbutzim**
- **$945,000 total** for **$35,000 × 27 kibbutzim**
- Includes leadership support, cultural and youth activities, targeted support for elderly residents, and youth/educational programming in protected spaces.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500): $94,500  
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000  

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259 kibbutzim** across Israel, from the northern border to the southern Negev. Guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience, the Movement supports kibbutz communities in routine times and during national emergencies.

Following the October 7th attacks and the subsequent **"Swords of Iron"** war, kibbutz communities faced increased needs and new urgent challenges. The **Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund** was established to provide support to kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected by the attacks and the ongoing war. The Fund addresses:
- Immediate emergency needs
- Mid-term community recovery
- Long-term rehabilitation and growth

During the war period, the proposal describes:
- **22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev** were severely damaged during the October 7th massacre.
- **19 kibbutzim along the northern border** were evacuated for extended periods.
- **8 kibbutzim in the central Hula Valley** remained under constant threat without evacuation.

As a result, entire communities were uprooted; hundreds were killed or injured; and vital services collapsed or were destroyed. Today, amid renewed escalation with Iran and continued missile threats, thousands remain vulnerable—public shelters are overcrowded and communities face persistent uncertainty. The proposal emphasizes that the need for emergency infrastructure and resilience support is urgent and unprecedented.

A central issue for this request is that the Israeli Home Front Command’s **Northern Protection Program** has not yet been completed, leaving **27 kibbutzim** (along the northern border and in the Hula Valley) without adequate protected rooms (**Mamad**). Families—many with children—are spending long periods in public shelters that lack basic equipment and infrastructure.

The proposal further notes that the **19 northern kibbutzim** that had been evacuated have only recently returned home, and are again facing severe security threats. The long displacement period created deep emotional strain and ongoing uncertainty, placing both individuals and community structures under immense pressure.

Donor support is requested to fund two urgent efforts:
1) **Emergency Shelter Kits** to enable families to remain in shelters with essential services, basic safety, and dignity.
2) **Community Emergency Support** to strengthen social cohesion and provide leadership, cultural/community activities, elderly care, and youth/educational programming within protected spaces.

## Detailed Proposal Content (Emergency Response for Kibbutz Communities)

### The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund

The proposal describes the Movement’s cooperative structure of **259 kibbutzim**, its guiding principles, and the Rehabilitation Fund’s role in supporting communities throughout their journey of recovery, rebuilding, and renewal.

### The Current Crisis

The document describes the crisis affecting kibbutz communities:

- **22 kibbutzim** in the Western Negev were severely damaged during the October 7th massacre.
- **19 kibbutzim** along the northern border were evacuated for extended periods.
- **8 kibbutzim** in the central Hula Valley remained under constant threat without evacuation.

It also states that renewed escalation and missile threats keep thousands vulnerable, with overcrowded public shelters and continued uncertainty.

### Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

Because of escalation in northern Israel, public shelters are currently housing large numbers of families. The proposal emphasizes that **27 kibbutzim** along the border and in the Hula Valley lack adequate protected rooms (**Mamad**) due to the Northern Protection Program not being completed. As a result, families—many with children—are spending long periods in public shelters lacking basic equipment and infrastructure.

#### Contents of Each Shelter Kit

Each kit will include:
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity and play kits for children

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

### Community Emergency Support Program

Beyond physical protection, the proposal states kibbutz communities need social, emotional, and communal support—especially where communities endured prolonged evacuation and are now returning amid renewed threats.

It highlights:
- The long displacement period created deep emotional strain.
- Ongoing security uncertainty continues to affect residents.
- Both individuals and community structures are under immense pressure.

#### Community Support Activities

The Rehabilitation Fund will implement initiatives in affected kibbutzim, including:
- **Support for Local Leadership:** Guidance and assistance for kibbutz management and key community role holders managing crisis conditions.
- **Cultural and Community Activities:** Programs held within the kibbutzim to maintain social cohesion and a sense of normalcy.
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted assistance for vulnerable older residents during periods of prolonged emergency.
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** Organized programming for teenagers and youth taking place within protected spaces to ensure safety while maintaining educational and social continuity.

### Expected Impact

With donor support, the proposal states the Fund will:
- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters
- Provide essential services and infrastructure during missile attacks
- Strengthen community resilience and social cohesion
- Support youth, families, and vulnerable populations
- Enable kibbutzim to withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing the recovery process

### Our Commitment

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund remains committed to standing beside Israel's kibbutzim and neighboring communities along the country’s borders. As long as the need exists, the Fund will continue to mobilize resources, coordinate support, and help these communities recover, rebuild, and thrive again.

## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** for **27** northern kibbutzim lacking adequate protected rooms (Mamad) under renewed missile threat:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** — **$94,500** (27 kits @ **$3,500**): equip public shelters with basic furniture, first-aid, Wi-Fi, and children’s activity kits so families can remain with greater safety, dignity, and essential services.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** — **$945,000** (**$35,000 × 27** kibbutzim): leadership support for kibbutz management and key role holders; cultural and community activities; targeted assistance for elderly residents during prolonged emergencies; youth and educational programming in protected spaces.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500): $94,500
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259** kibbutzim across Israel. After the October 7 attacks and the “Swords of Iron” war, the **Rehabilitation Fund** was created for kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected—covering immediate emergency needs, mid-term recovery, and long-term rehabilitation and growth.

Impact snapshot from the war: **22** Western Negev kibbutzim severely damaged in the massacre; **19** northern-border kibbutzim evacuated for extended periods; **8** central Hula Valley kibbutzim under constant threat without evacuation. Hundreds killed or injured; vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

Today, renewed escalation (including with Iran) and continued missile threats leave thousands vulnerable. The Israeli Home Front Command’s **Northern Protection Program** is not yet complete, so **27** border and Hula Valley kibbutzim still lack adequate Mamad; families—many with children—endure long stays in **overcrowded** public shelters with **insufficient** equipment. The **19** northern kibbutzim that evacuated have recently returned yet face severe threats again; prolonged displacement caused deep emotional strain, and ongoing uncertainty stresses individuals and community institutions.

Donor support funds both physical shelter readiness and communal resilience so kibbutzim can withstand ongoing security pressure while continuing recovery.

### Emergency Response for Kibbutz Communities — The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund

#### Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim from the northern border to the southern Negev, guided by mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience—in routine times and national emergencies.

After October 7 and “Swords of Iron,” needs surged. The Rehabilitation Fund supports kibbutzim evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected, across recovery, rebuilding, and renewal.

#### The Current Crisis

- 22 Western Negev kibbutzim severely damaged during the October 7 massacre.
- 19 northern-border kibbutzim evacuated for extended periods.
- 8 central Hula Valley kibbutzim under constant threat without evacuation.

Communities uprooted; hundreds killed or injured; services destroyed. Amid renewed escalation and missile threats, shelters are overcrowded; infrastructure and resilience support needs are acute.

#### Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

Northern escalation leaves many families in public shelters. Until Northern Protection Program completion, **27** kibbutzim along the border and in the Hula Valley lack adequate Mamad; shelters lack basic equipment and infrastructure.

**Emergency Shelter Kits** aim to let families stay with basic safety, dignity, and essential services.

**Each kit includes:**
- 20 stackable chairs  
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses  
- 3 high-capacity fans  
- 2 folding tables  
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries  
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits  
- 2 activity and play kits for children  

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

#### Community Emergency Support Program

Beyond physical protection, communities need social, emotional, and communal support. The 19 northern-border kibbutzim endured long evacuation, recently returned, yet face severe threats again. Post-return rebuilding of social fabric is under strain from displacement trauma, ongoing security uncertainty, and pressure on individuals and institutions.

**Planned activities:**
- **Support for Local Leadership:** guidance for management and key community roles under crisis.  
- **Cultural and Community Activities:** in-kibbutz programs for cohesion and normalcy.  
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** targeted help during prolonged emergencies.  
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** programming for teenagers/youth in protected spaces for safety plus educational and social continuity.

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cost | $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim |
| Total funding required | $945,000 |

#### Expected Impact

- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters  
- Provide essential services and infrastructure during attacks  
- Strengthen resilience and social cohesion  
- Support youth, families, and vulnerable populations  
- Help kibbutzim endure ongoing security challenges while recovering  

#### Our Commitment

The Fund commits to standing beside Israel’s kibbutzim and neighboring border communities, mobilizing resources and coordination as long as need exists, so communities can recover, rebuild, and thrive.

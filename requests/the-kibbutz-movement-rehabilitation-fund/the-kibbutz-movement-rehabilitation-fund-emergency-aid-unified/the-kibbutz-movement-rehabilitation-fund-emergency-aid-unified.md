## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund requests **$1,039,500** in emergency support for northern kibbutz communities under renewed missile threat.

Two coordinated funding components:

1. **Emergency Shelter Kits** for 27 kibbutzim/public shelters to provide basic safety, dignity, and essential services for families spending extended periods in shelters.
2. **Community Emergency Support Program** across the same 27 kibbutzim to sustain leadership function, social cohesion, vulnerable-population support, and youth continuity under crisis conditions.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits @ $3,500): **$94,500**
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): **$945,000**

## Context & Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of **259 kibbutzim** across Israel. Following the October 7 attacks and the "Swords of Iron" war, the Rehabilitation Fund was established to support kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected, spanning:
- Immediate emergency needs
- Mid-term community recovery
- Long-term rehabilitation and growth

Reported impact context across sources:
- **22** kibbutzim in the Western Negev severely damaged during Oct 7
- **19** northern-border kibbutzim evacuated for extended periods
- **8** kibbutzim in the central Hula Valley under constant threat without evacuation

Communities experienced deaths and injuries, prolonged displacement, collapsed/disrupted services, and sustained social-emotional pressure.

Current emergency condition:
- Renewed escalation and continued missile threats have left thousands vulnerable.
- Public shelters are overcrowded.
- The Israeli Home Front Command’s Northern Protection Program is not yet complete, leaving **27** kibbutzim without adequate Mamad protection.
- Recently returned northern communities are again operating under severe threat, with persistent uncertainty and strain on individuals and communal structures.

## Program Details

### 1) Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits

Each shelter kit includes:
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity/play kits for children

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cost per kit | $3,500 |
| Funding goal | 27 shelter kits |
| Total required | $94,500 |

### 2) Community Emergency Support Program

Support initiatives include:
- **Local Leadership Support:** Guidance for kibbutz management and key role holders during crisis.
- **Cultural and Community Activities:** Programming to preserve cohesion and normalcy.
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted aid for vulnerable older residents during prolonged emergencies.
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** Safe programming in protected spaces for teens and youth.

## Expected Impact

With donor support, the Fund aims to:
- Improve safety and living conditions in emergency shelters
- Provide essential infrastructure/services during missile attacks
- Strengthen resilience and social cohesion
- Support families, youth, elderly residents, and community leadership
- Enable kibbutzim to withstand ongoing security challenges while continuing recovery

## Commitments

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund states ongoing commitment to mobilize resources, coordinate emergency support, and help affected kibbutz communities recover, rebuild, and renew for as long as needed.

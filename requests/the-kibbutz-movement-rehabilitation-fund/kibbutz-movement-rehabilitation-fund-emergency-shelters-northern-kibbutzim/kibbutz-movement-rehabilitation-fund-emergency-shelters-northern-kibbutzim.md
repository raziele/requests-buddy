## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund is requesting emergency funding to support northern kibbutzim facing renewed missile threats and escalation. The ask has two components: (1) equipping public shelters in 27 kibbutzim with Emergency Shelter Kits (mattresses, seating, first aid, fans, Wi-Fi, children's activity kits) at $94,500 total, and (2) Community Emergency Support across the same 27 kibbutzim for leadership support, cultural and youth activities in protected spaces, and assistance for elderly residents, at $945,000. Total requested is $1,039,500 to improve safety, dignity, and resilience while families remain in shelters and communities face ongoing security uncertainty.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits × $3,500): $94,500
- Community Emergency Support ($35,000 × 27 kibbutzim): $945,000

## Context & Background

Following the October 7th attacks and the "Swords of Iron" war, the Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established to support kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected. The Movement comprises 259 kibbutzim across Israel; 22 in the Western Negev were severely damaged during the massacre, 19 along the northern border were evacuated for extended periods, and 8 in the central Hula Valley remained under constant threat. Hundreds of residents were killed or injured and vital services collapsed.

Amid renewed escalation and continued missile threats, thousands of residents remain vulnerable. The Israeli Home Front Command's Northern Protection Program has not yet been completed, leaving 27 kibbutzim along the border and in the Hula Valley without adequate protected rooms (Mamad). Families—many with children—are spending long periods in public shelters that lack basic equipment. The 19 northern kibbutzim that had been evacuated have only recently returned home and are again facing severe security threats; the long displacement created deep emotional strain and ongoing uncertainty is placing individuals and community structures under immense pressure.

The Fund is asking partners worldwide for support so that shelter conditions can be improved and community resilience—youth activities in protected spaces, elderly support, cultural programming, and local leadership guidance—can be sustained during this difficult period.

# Emergency Response for Kibbutz Communities
**The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund**

## Background

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim spread across Israel, from the northern border to the southern Negev. Guided by the principles of mutual responsibility, solidarity, and community resilience, the Movement supports kibbutz communities in both routine times and national emergencies.

Following the devastating October 7th attacks and the subsequent "Swords of Iron" war, the needs of kibbutz communities increased dramatically. In response, the Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established to provide support to kibbutzim that were evacuated, damaged, or deeply affected. The Fund addresses:
- Immediate emergency needs
- Mid-term community recovery
- Long-term rehabilitation and growth

The Fund supports kibbutzim throughout their journey of recovery, rebuilding, and renewal.

## The Current Crisis

The attacks and subsequent war severely impacted kibbutz communities:
- 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev were severely damaged during the October 7th massacre.
- 19 kibbutzim along the northern border were evacuated for extended periods.
- 8 kibbutzim in the central Hula Valley remained under constant threat without evacuation.

Entire communities were uprooted. Hundreds of residents were killed or injured, and vital services collapsed or were destroyed.

Today, amid the renewed escalation and continued missile threats, thousands of residents remain vulnerable. Public shelters are overcrowded and communities face persistent uncertainty. The need for emergency infrastructure and community resilience support is urgent and unprecedented.

## Immediate Need: Public Shelter Emergency Kits (Northern Border)

Due to the escalation in northern Israel, public shelters are housing large numbers of families. The Israeli Home Front Command's Northern Protection Program has not yet been completed, leaving **27 kibbutzim** along the border and in the Hula Valley without adequate protected rooms (Mamad). Families—many with children—are spending long periods in public shelters that lack basic equipment and infrastructure.

The Fund seeks donor support to provide **Emergency Shelter Kits** to these communities.

**Contents of Each Shelter Kit:**
- 20 stackable chairs
- 50 foam or inflatable mattresses
- 3 high-capacity fans
- 2 folding tables
- 2 Wi-Fi routers with backup batteries
- 2 fully equipped first-aid kits
- 2 activity and play kits for children

**Cost per kit:** $3,500  
**Funding goal:** 27 shelter kits  
**Total required:** $94,500

## Community Emergency Support Program

The 19 kibbutzim along the northern border endured a prolonged evacuation and have only recently returned home. They are again facing severe security threats. Community support activities include:
- **Support for Local Leadership:** Guidance and assistance for kibbutz management and key community role holders managing crisis conditions.
- **Cultural and Community Activities:** Programs within the kibbutzim to maintain social cohesion and a sense of normalcy.
- **Support for Elderly Residents:** Targeted assistance for vulnerable older residents during prolonged emergency periods.
- **Youth and Educational Activities:** Organized programming for teenagers and youth within protected spaces for safety while maintaining educational and social continuity.

**Budget for Community Emergency Support:**  
Cost: $35,000 × 27 kibbutzim  
**Total funding required:** $945,000

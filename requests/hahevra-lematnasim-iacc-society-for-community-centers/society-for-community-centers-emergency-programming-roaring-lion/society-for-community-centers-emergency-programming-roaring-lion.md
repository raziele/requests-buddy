## The Ask

The Society for Community Centers (HaHevra LeMatnasim) is requesting support for emergency community programming during Operation Roaring Lion. Programs include 'Together in the Building' (neighbor-led resilience); early childhood frameworks for reservists' and essential workers' children; children and family activities (Zoom, activity kits, parent workshops); youth (Zoom groups, volunteering, emotional support); elderly (phone outreach, activity kits, sheltered activities); and special needs (adapted Zoom, parent guidance, activity kits). Estimated budget approximately $900,000 per month ($225,000 per week) across ~200 centers.

**Funding Requested:** Approximately $900,000 per month ($225,000 per week)

**Funding Breakdown:**
- All populations – 'Together in the Building' (equipment, local coordinators): $75,000/week ($300,000/month)
- Early childhood (equipment, activities): $18,750/week ($75,000/month)
- Children and family (activity kits, Zoom, sheltered activities): $50,000/week ($200,000/month)
- Youth ('Special Friends', training, materials): $31,250/week ($125,000/month)
- Elderly (hotline, activity kits, visits, basic goods): $25,000/week ($100,000/month)
- Special needs (kits, lecturers, materials, parent support): $25,000/week ($100,000/month)

## Context & Background

Operation Roaring Lion has created multi-front threats, prolonged stays in protected spaces, and disruption of community life. Families are in shelters, parents are mobilized, education systems are closed or remote, and many face stress and uncertainty. The Society for Community Centers operates a nationwide network of community centers and has received many requests from communities and local authorities for tailored, immediate responses. The document details needs by population: neighbor-to-neighbor resilience; early childhood frameworks for reservists and essential workers; children and family emotional and experiential support; youth engagement and volunteering; elderly outreach and activity; and special needs continuity and stability. Budget is estimated for ~200 community centers with potential reach of ~3 million residents.

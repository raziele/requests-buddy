#### About Nitzan

Nitzan is a parents and volunteers' association founded in 1964 to identify, diagnose, treat, and rehabilitate children, adolescents, and adults with learning disabilities, adaptation challenges, and functional difficulties. The organization enables them to realize their personal, academic, and social potential and to successfully integrate into the education system, the community, and the workforce.

Nitzan's work is research-driven, with educational and emotional models combining professional assessment, tailored intervention, and continuous support for the child and their environment. The organization accompanies its target audiences from early childhood through labor market integration.

Nitzan operates 40 branches throughout Israel and provides services to approximately 50,000 individuals each year. Over the years, more than one million Israelis have benefited from the organization's services.

#### The Need

After two and a half years of war, IDF reservists' wives are exhausted, almost depleted of their emotional resilience. They have been functioning as de-facto single mothers throughout this period, carrying immense worry and anxiety for the safety of their loved ones while simultaneously trying to shield their children from the harsh realities of war.

These women are experiencing erosion of parental authority and instability within the family unit. Nitzan seeks to offer them one of its flagship programs, Parent Coaching. This program is currently being implemented with success for Kiryat Shmona's children's parents.

#### The Response

Parent coaching using the E.C.C. (Emotional Cognitive Coaching) method, developed by Dr. Maly Danino, CEO of Nitzan, is a short-term intervention approach that focuses on strengthening the parent. This method offers parents an opportunity for self-reflection, helping them recognize strengths and weaknesses while understanding hindering behavioral patterns. Self-awareness and reframing the problem are the two forces through which the program generates deep behavioral change, influencing participants' relationships with those around them in general and with their children in particular.

The process includes three stages: emotional exploration, insight development and action. Each stage lays the foundation for the next. Throughout the process, the parent's emotional responses are continuously monitored. Insights are refined and translated into practical steps for change. Participants explore alternative approaches and learn new skills that enable them to respond more effectively to family challenges. The transition between stages is flexible and tailored to the individual needs and abilities of each parent.

Research has shown that mothers who participated in this process improved their ability to regulate their children's negative emotions. Improvements were also observed in the overall family climate, which led to better academic, social, and emotional functioning among the children (reduction in aggression and reclusion).

Against this background, we seek to offer the program to 100 IDF reservists' wives, free of charge. The program will include a 12-session coaching process, providing practical tools to strengthen parental authority, improve family dynamics, and reinforce children's sense of capability and confidence. Sessions will take place on a weekly basis and will be run by Nitzan's experienced professional staff.

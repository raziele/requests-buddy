## The Ask

Masa El Haofek (Journey to the Horizon) seeks partners and philanthropic investment to scale a national 10-week program that helps young discharged IDF veterans transition into clear life direction, employment pathways, and entrepreneurship support—especially in Israel’s periphery—during a time of heightened uncertainty following the war.

## Context & Background

As Israel enters the third week of a new war, the organization highlights the transition challenge for young IDF veterans after discharge. Over the past three years, 270,000 young Israelis were discharged from the IDF, yet for many the moment they leave the uniform is when uncertainty begins—linked to instability affecting studies, employment, and mental wellbeing (presented as nearly 23%, 28%, and 50% for the related outcomes).

Journey to the Horizon (Masa El Haofek) has operated for the past 5 years across Israel to transform the transition from military service into a launching point for life, using a structured, high-impact 10-week experience. The program equips participants with clarity of direction, practical tools, professional exposure, and personal support.

They report strong outcomes from prior delivery (93% into employment pathways; 89% sustained workshop engagement) and national reach (62 programs delivered; 1,200+ graduates; 32 programs in 2025 across 35 municipalities). The email includes a work-plan for May 2026 across multiple cities/regions, and the accompanying budget states a funding gap: philanthropic investment required of USD 26,140 to support the total programme cost of roughly USD 63.6k.

## THE MOMENT WE ARE IN
As we enter the third week of a new war, Israel continues to demonstrate extraordinary military capability.
But behind every system, every sortie, and every operational success stand young men and women, many in their early twenties, carrying responsibility far beyond their years—and soon, they will return home.

Over the past three years alone, 270,000 young Israelis have been discharged from the IDF. They leave with resilience, discipline, leadership, and a deep sense of responsibility.
Yet for many, the moment they take off the uniform is the moment uncertainty begins: abandon studies due to instability; lose employment; report declining mental wellbeing (presented as nearly 23%, 28%, 50%).

They served us, now it's our turn to serve them.
This is not only a personal challenge. It is a strategic issue for Israel’s future.
Israel knows how to rebuild military strength after war. The question is whether it will invest with the same seriousness in the people who made that strength possible.

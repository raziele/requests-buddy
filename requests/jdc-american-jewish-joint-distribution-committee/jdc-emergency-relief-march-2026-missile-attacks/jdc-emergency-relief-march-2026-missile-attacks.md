## The Ask

JDC is requesting $6.876 million in urgent emergency funding to respond to the March 2026 missile attacks from Iran and Lebanon that have caused fatalities, injuries, and mass displacement across Israel. The request covers humanitarian relief in five directly hit cities, welfare support for ten northern frontline municipalities, housing transition support for up to 300 vulnerable displaced households (All the Way Home program), accessibility measures and shelter kits for people with disabilities and children, emergency replacement of medical oxygen cylinders for municipal response teams, and heightened security and mental health support for Jewish communities in Europe facing increased threats.

**Funding Requested:** $6,876,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Humanitarian Relief for Directly Hit Cities ($200,000 per city x 5 cities): $1,000,000
- Welfare Support for Northern Frontline Municipalities ($150,000 per city x 10 cities): $1,500,000
- All the Way Home for Families Whose Apartments Were Severely Damaged: $3,300,000
- Increasing Accessibility in Shelters for People with Disabilities and Accessible Shelter Kits for Children: $200,000
- Emergency Replacement of Medical Gas Cylinders: $576,000
- Increased Needs of Global Jewish Communities: $300,000

## Context & Background

Over the past several days Israel was again thrown into war: hundreds of missiles and drones were launched from Iran and Lebanon, sending millions to safe rooms and shelters. As of March 3rd, direct hits in Tel Aviv, Beit Shemesh, Jerusalem, and Be'er Sheva caused 12 fatalities, hundreds of injuries, and extensive damage, forcing over 1,500 citizens to evacuate to hotels. JDC has drawn on established government and local partnerships and unrestricted funding (including from JUF) to act quickly—delivering essential supplies in Beit Shemesh, respite kits in the North, and embedding in national coordination efforts and digital mental health platforms.

New needs continue to emerge. The attached funding menu outlines six urgent priorities: multi-layered humanitarian relief in five directly hit cities; welfare support for ten low-socioeconomic northern municipalities; the All the Way Home program for up to 300 vulnerable displaced households (100 already identified); accessibility equipment and shelter kits for people with disabilities and children; emergency replacement of 1,442 medical oxygen cylinders following a Ministry of Health safety directive; and support for Jewish communities in Europe facing rising security costs and threats. JDC states that the ability to continue this lifesaving work depends on this funding.

Figures and scope are as of March 4, 2026 and subject to change as the situation develops.

# Responding to Emergency in Israel: Urgent Funding Needs | March 4, 2026

## Israel Again in the Shadow of War

Over the past few days, Israel was once again thrown into war. In the last 100 hours, hundreds of missiles and drones were launched from Iran – and more recently, Lebanon – to Israel, sending millions of Israelis to safe rooms and bomb shelters at all hours of the day and night. While most were intercepted, as of March 3rd, direct hits in Tel Aviv, Beit Shemesh, Jerusalem, and Be'er Sheva caused 12 fatalities, hundreds of injuries, and extensive damage to homes and infrastructure, forcing over 1,500 citizens to leave their homes and evacuate to hotels.

As this crisis unfolds, JDC is there. Since the first news of the attacks, JDC drew on its established networks of government ministries and local partners to assess emerging needs and explore possible solutions.

JDC has not worked in a vacuum. Using unrestricted and emergency funding, even before October 7th, JDC has created strong foundations that allowed quick and effective action in real time. For example:

- **Digital Tools:** JDC-developed platforms (websites for education and mental health professionals, AI-powered support apps for reservists' spouses, data dashboards for multi-ministerial round tables) continue to anchor the digital infrastructure being expanded to help vulnerable populations receive critical responses and services.
- **Municipal Partnership:** Work with municipalities, strengthening community resilience through Mashiv Haruach, helping local municipalities meet urgent humanitarian needs, and building local capacity through the Northern Initiative and other programs – reinforcing JDC's role as a trusted convener and facilitator.
- **Leveling the Playing Field in Critical Aid:** To reduce disparities in emergency medical readiness, JDC equipped volunteer response teams in low-income municipalities with Advanced Life Support, Basic Life Support, and CPR kits, and established locally based Emergency Medical Units. JDC continues to monitor these needs to close critical gaps.

The following funding menu outlines the most urgent priorities.

## Highlighted Urgent Funding Needs

### 1. Humanitarian Relief for Directly Hit Cities
- **Amount:** $200,000 per city x 5 cities = **$1,000,000**
- Support for cities absorbing massive, direct missile damage (e.g., Beit Shemesh, Be'er Sheva).
- In response to the fatal strike and severe damage in Beit Shemesh, JDC immediately offered multi-layered emergency support. Similar support has been offered to hard-hit cities since October 7th. JDC has started assistance in Beit Shemesh and is expanding to Be'er Sheva.
- **Components:**
  - Immediate Humanitarian Relief: Clothing and essential supplies for families (toiletries, clothing, basic necessities, special items for children), in partnership with Shinua Hevrati.
  - Emergency Readiness and Medical Equipment: Medical and first response equipment for municipal emergency teams (tourniquets, bandages, stretchers, advanced and basic lifesaving kits, rescue kits).
  - Flexible Humanitarian Assistance Fund: Dedicated flexible fund for the municipality's most urgent needs.
  - Deployment of Community Caseworkers: Two full-time community caseworkers to reinforce the municipal welfare department.
- This response builds on JDC's proven municipal partnership model, activated during the 2025 missile attacks.

### 2. Welfare Support for Northern Frontline Municipalities
- **Amount:** $150,000 per city x 10 cities = **$1,500,000**
- Support for low-socioeconomic authorities in the North struggling after two years of conflict.
- JDC has received urgent appeals from seven municipalities where JDC operates: Ma'ale Yosef, Shlomi, Kiryat Shmona, Nahariya, Hurfeish, Deir al-Asad, and Mevo'ot Hermon. Requested support includes food, essential household supplies, mattresses, biodegradable and accessible medical equipment, community support personnel, resilience and training packages, activity kits, and emergency relief operations. Demand is expected to increase.
- JDC is active in 24 locations along the northern confrontation line and is prepared to launch immediate operations in all areas.

### 3. All the Way Home (Ad HaBayit) Program for Families Whose Apartments Were Severely Damaged
- **Amount:** **$3,300,000**
- A growing number of Israelis are suddenly homeless, forced to flee damaged apartments and take refuge in hotel rooms. In the June war with Iran, some 13,000 Israelis were displaced, including 4,000 particularly vulnerable. Without dedicated support, many risk falling through the cracks.
- The program will provide personalized housing transition support for up to 300 of the most vulnerable households in hard-hit cities, in cooperation with local municipalities and NGOs: access to rights, navigating bureaucracy, finding viable housing. JDC has already identified 100 families in need; demand is expected to grow.

### 4. Increasing Accessibility in Shelters for People with Disabilities and Accessible Shelter Kits for Children
- **Amount:** **$200,000**
- Many people with disabilities lack private shelters and must use public bomb shelters; frequent rocket attacks since October 7 have revealed critical accessibility gaps (physical barriers and emotional strain), including for young children during prolonged stays.
- **Planned response:**
  - Equipping municipalities with accessibility tools: portable ramps, marked stairs and thresholds, guiding strips, specialized signage; training for staff and emergency volunteers.
  - Accessible shelter kits for children: to reduce distress, manage emotions, keep them engaged (e.g., small pop-up tents, weighted blankets, cozy floor mats, noise-canceling headphones).

### 5. Emergency Replacement of Medical Gas Cylinders
- **Amount:** **$576,000**
- Mid-February 2026 Ministry of Health (MOH) directive identified safety concerns with existing cylinders in some kits. JDC seeks to urgently procure new, certified oxygen cylinders for municipal volunteer response teams. JDC will secure 300 cylinders immediately; remaining 1,142 within one to three weeks subject to availability. Plan includes centralized collection and phased replacement, with coordination with MOH and national authorities to ensure uninterrupted lifesaving capacity.

### 6. Increased Needs of Global Jewish Communities
- **Amount:** **$300,000**
- JDC is monitoring the situation across Europe, Asia and Africa, in constant contact with local leadership. In the Netherlands, vulnerable Jewish students and families with close ties to Israel urgently need expanded social work and mental-health assistance and bolstered crisis-response systems. Across Turkey, France, Brussels, and Tunisia, communities are experiencing acute increases in security threats around schools, synagogues, youth movements, and major communal gatherings. JDC aims to help these regions absorb rapidly rising security costs and reinforce protective infrastructure.

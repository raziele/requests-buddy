## The Ask

The Association for the Advancement of the Druze Soldier (DruzeTech) is requesting $300,000 to complete the second phase of its technological center: a Druze-Jewish accelerator named after Lt. Col. Mahmoud Hiraldin (killed in Gaza 2018). The organization already runs an innovation center with 400 soldiers in high-tech training and a skills lab (DTec) that has served ~100 students. The new accelerator will expand programming across inspiration, 21st-century skills, entrepreneurship, technology, health, business, and government tracks; offer experiential opportunities (e.g. MIT summer, NASA challenges, 4YFN, industry internships); and include a Career Catalyst track (up to 25-week internships with stipends). The request aims to integrate more Druze youth into higher education and the tech workforce, with a focus on women and closing the gap between national tech employment (12%) and current Druze representation (3.5%).

**Funding Requested:** $300,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Invitation to visit the center.

## Context & Background

The Druze community in Israel numbers about 150,000 (roughly 1.6% of the population), with a higher share in the 20–35 age group. Despite strong high-school graduation rates (91.3% vs. national 76%) and meaningful military service, few Druze pursue higher education or complete degrees, and only about 3.5% work in tech compared to 12% nationally. The Association attributes this to limited infrastructure, access to quality education, employment and networking opportunities, and few role models within the community.

In late 2022 the organization established the DTec Skills Lab in partnership with academia, industry, philanthropy, and government, creating a program tailored to employers in the north. To date about 100 students have participated in tech training, conversion, and placement programs. The next step is a national innovation center (accelerator) using entrepreneurship as a basis for problem-solving in areas such as water, energy, robotics, transport, and circular economy. All participants are expected to give back to the community.

The accelerator will offer multiple tracks (inspiration, 21st-century skills, entrepreneurship, technology, health, business, government), experiential opportunities including MIT summer semester, NASA challenges, 4YFN at Mobile World Congress, internships at leading tech companies, and youth exchange with Abraham Accords countries. The Career Catalyst component provides up to 25 weeks of project-based internship, industry-led training, mentoring, and living stipends. The organization states that over the past decade hundreds of graduates have successfully joined tech roles and that the goal is 3,000 Druze in Israeli tech within five years. The association holds valid Section 46 (tax-deductible donations) status and a 2026 proper-management approval from the Registrar of Amutot; the registrar notes a cumulative deficit. A bank account confirmation and updated supplier recognition letter are included among the attachments.

## האתגר הדרוזי בישראל

מתוך כמיליון דרוזים בעולם, רק 150,000 חיים בישראל. הדרוזים מהווים כ־1.6% מאוכלוסיית ישראל, אך בקבוצת הגילאים 20–35 חלקם כמעט כפול. הקהילה הדרוזית סובלת ממיעוט תשתיות, נגישות מוגבלת לחינוך איכותי, מחסור בהזדמנויות תעסוקה ונטוורקינג, ומעט מודלים לחיקוי מתוך הקהילה עצמה. למרות הישגים מרשימים בבגרות (91.3% לעומת ממוצע ארצי של 76%) ושירות מוצלח בצה"ל, מעט דרוזים פונים להשכלה גבוהה, פחות מסיימים תואר, ומעטים עוד יותר משתלבים בעבודות טכנולוגיות. בעוד בהיי-טק הישראלי מועסקים 12% מהאוכלוסייה, בקרב הדרוזים רק 3.5% השתלבו.

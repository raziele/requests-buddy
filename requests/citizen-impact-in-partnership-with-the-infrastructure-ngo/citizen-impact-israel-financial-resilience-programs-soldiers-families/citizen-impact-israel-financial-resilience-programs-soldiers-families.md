## The Ask

Citizen Impact and The Infrastructure NGO are requesting matching philanthropic support to close funding gaps for two year-long financial resilience programs in Israel: a national program for active-duty soldiers and a holistic recovery program for war-affected families. They present both programs as proven, scalable interventions and seek support now to expand reach and ensure long-term financial stability outcomes for vulnerable populations.

**Funding Requested:** $411,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- IDF Soldiers - Financial Resilience Program (matching required): $260,000
- War-Affected Households - Holistic Recovery Program (remaining gap): $151,000

## Context & Background

The forwarded material outlines two complementary long-term interventions aimed at reducing financial vulnerability in Israel at critical life stages. The first focuses on active-duty soldiers who are already in financial hardship; the second supports households affected by the war through integrated, year-long recovery services. The package emphasizes continuity of support, personalized plans, and hands-on implementation rather than one-time assistance or referral-only models.

For soldiers, the program is presented as a national three-year model serving 500 soldiers per year, with collaboration from Citizen Impact, The Infrastructure NGO, Bank of Israel, Ministry of Defense, Class Action Fund, and Bank Hapoalim. Participants receive 12 months of personalized support, financial assessment, tailored action plans, human guidance, and AI-driven tools focused on income growth, debt management, expense optimization, and rights realization. Funding notes indicate $650K approved and a required 40% match of $260K.

For war-affected households, the document describes a one-stop, full-year recovery framework spanning financial, employment, legal-rights, and emotional domains. It reports 2,500+ households already supported and an expansion target of 650 additional households. Financial figures include $650 cost per household, $325 secured through Ogen, a matching requirement of $211K, $60K already committed by the Detroit Federation, and a remaining gap of $151K. The request is framed as time-sensitive gap-closing to sustain and expand delivery.

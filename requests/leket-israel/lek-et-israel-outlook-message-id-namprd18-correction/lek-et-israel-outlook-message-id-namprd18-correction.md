## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is seeking emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis. **Two parallel outreach threads** frame the same need: one directed to the **Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia**, the other asking **JUF Chicago** for support.

Since the escalation with Iran, surplus food available for rescue has dropped sharply: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—Leket's largest suppliers of cooked food—are not permitting entry. Daily meal distribution has fallen from **8,000 to 3,000**. Leket is temporarily purchasing additional meals and produce and providing **supermarket vouchers** for families who cannot reach distribution points, while working to keep hundreds of nonprofit partners supplied.

**Funding requested:** **$508,210 per week** (same figure in both threads).

**Funding breakdown (narrative; amounts not itemized per line in sources):**
- **Meals** — Purchase **5,000 additional meals per day** to bridge the gap (rescued meals down from 8,000 to 3,000/day).
- **Produce** — Temporary purchases to maintain supply for partners after harvest disruption.

One source explicitly notes that **several Leket grant requests are already under review with the Federation**; this communication addresses **additional** emergency needs from the current situation.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life in Israel has involved sirens, sheltering, and broad disruption. Leket frames **food security as part of resilience**: reliable access to nutritious food helps families care for children and cope with the period's stress. As the national food bank, Leket aims to keep supply reaching vulnerable households through national crises—supporting health, reducing trauma, and sustaining community fabric.

Rocket fire and security limits have disrupted rescue logistics (collection and distribution). Despite that, the team is trying to **maintain or increase** regular distribution where possible, using purchased food and vouchers alongside rescued supply. **Emergency support** is requested so vulnerable Israelis continue to receive the food they depend on during this critical period.

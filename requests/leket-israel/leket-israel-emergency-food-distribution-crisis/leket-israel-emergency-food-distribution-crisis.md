## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis. The war with Iran has severely disrupted food rescue operations: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—Leket's largest source of surplus cooked meals—are not permitting entry. As a result, daily meal distribution has dropped from 8,000 to 3,000. Leket is temporarily supplementing rescued food with purchased meals and supermarket vouchers to maintain supply to hundreds of nonprofit partners and vulnerable families. Maintaining these operations requires an additional $508,210 per week. They are asking the Federation for any emergency support possible to ensure vulnerable Israelis continue to receive the food they depend on during this critical period.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by deep uncertainty: sirens interrupt routines, families move in and out of shelters, and the instability touches nearly every part of daily life. Food security is a critical part of societal resilience; when parents know that nourishing food will be on the table, they are better able to care for their children and face the challenges of this period. Leket Israel, as Israel's national food bank, is committed to ensuring that vulnerable families continue to receive a reliable supply of nutritious food even during national crises.

However, the current security situation has significantly disrupted the networks that enable food rescue. Rocket fire has forced a pause in volunteer activity and limited collection and distribution operations. Farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—Leket's largest suppliers of cooked food—are not permitting entry. The supply of surplus food available for rescue has declined sharply. Despite these challenges, Leket's team is working to maintain regular distribution levels and, where possible, increase them. To achieve this, Leket is temporarily supplementing rescued food with purchased food and providing supermarket vouchers to families who require assistance but cannot access distribution points.

The funding gap is driven by two main factors: meals and produce. The sharp drop in surplus meals has reduced daily distribution from 8,000 to 3,000 meals per day, requiring Leket to purchase an additional 5,000 meals daily. Farmers' inability to harvest since the escalation began has drastically reduced the produce available for rescue, requiring significant temporary purchases to maintain supply for nonprofit partners. The partnership and solidarity of Jewish communities abroad are especially meaningful during this period, and Leket is grateful for any emergency support the Federation can provide to sustain these efforts.

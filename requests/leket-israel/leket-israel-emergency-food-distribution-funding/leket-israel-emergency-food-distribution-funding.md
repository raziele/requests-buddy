## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency funding to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis. The war with Iran has severely disrupted food rescue operations: volunteer activity is paused, collection and distribution are limited, farmers have halted harvests, and IDF bases are not permitting entry. Surplus food supply has dropped sharply. Leket has reduced daily meal distribution from 8,000 to 3,000 and is temporarily supplementing with purchased meals and supermarket vouchers. They are asking the Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia for emergency support to maintain these efforts at an additional cost of $508,210 per week.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Meals — purchase additional 5,000 meals daily to replace rescue shortfall (distribution dropped from 8,000 to 3,000/day): part of $508,210/week
- Produce — temporary purchases to maintain supply for nonprofit partners after farmers halted harvests: part of $508,210/week

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by deep uncertainty: sirens, shelter use, and instability. Leket frames food security as essential to resilience—when families know basic needs will be met, they can better care for children and face the crisis. As Israel's national food bank, Leket is committed to keeping a reliable supply of nutritious food reaching vulnerable families even during national crises, to protect health, reduce trauma, and maintain social fabric.

The current security situation has significantly disrupted the networks that enable food rescue. Rocket fire forced a pause in volunteer activity and limits on collection and distribution. Farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—Leket's largest suppliers of cooked food—are not permitting entry. The supply of surplus food available for rescue has declined sharply.

Despite this, Leket is working to keep food reaching the hundreds of nonprofit partners who depend on them. The priority is to maintain or increase distribution so vulnerable families do not face additional hardship. To do so, Leket is temporarily supplementing rescued food with purchased food and providing supermarket vouchers for families who cannot reach distribution points. Maintaining these operations requires an additional $508,210 per week, mainly for meals (5,000 additional purchased meals per day) and produce (significant temporary purchases). The organization is asking the Federation for any emergency support it can provide at this time, noting that other grant requests remain under review and that this outreach specifically addresses additional emergency needs created by the current situation.

## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support (emergency funding) to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis. The war with Iran has severely disrupted food rescue operations: volunteer activity is paused, collection and distribution are limited, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—Leket's largest source of surplus cooked meals and largest suppliers of cooked food—are not permitting entry. As a result, surplus food supply has dropped sharply and daily meal distribution has dropped from 8,000 to 3,000 meals per day. Leket is temporarily supplementing rescued food with purchased meals and supermarket vouchers to maintain supply to hundreds of nonprofit partners and vulnerable families. Maintaining these operations requires an additional **$508,210 per week**. They are asking the Federation / Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia / JUF Chicago for any emergency support possible to ensure vulnerable Israelis continue to receive the food they depend on during this critical period. One version notes that other grant requests remain under review and that this outreach specifically addresses additional emergency needs created by the current situation.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **Meals** — Purchase of additional 5,000 meals daily to replace lost surplus (distribution dropped from 8,000 to 3,000/day): part of $508,210/week (line-item amounts not specified in sources).
- **Produce** — Temporary purchases to maintain supply to nonprofit partners after farmers halted harvests: part of $508,210/week.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by deep uncertainty: sirens interrupt routines, families move in and out of shelters, and the instability touches nearly every part of daily life. Food security is a critical part of societal resilience; when parents know that nourishing food will be on the table, they are better able to care for their children and face the challenges of this period. Leket frames food security as essential to resilience—when families know basic needs will be met, they can better care for children and face the crisis. As Israel's national food bank, Leket is committed to ensuring that vulnerable families continue to receive a reliable supply of nutritious food even during national crises, to protect health, reduce trauma, and maintain social fabric.

The current security situation has significantly disrupted the networks that enable food rescue. Rocket fire has forced a pause in volunteer activity and limited collection and distribution operations. Farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—Leket's largest suppliers of cooked food—are not permitting entry. The supply of surplus food available for rescue has declined sharply. Despite these challenges, Leket's team is working to maintain regular distribution levels and, where possible, increase them. The priority is to maintain or increase distribution so vulnerable families do not face additional hardship. To achieve this, Leket is temporarily supplementing rescued food with purchased food and providing supermarket vouchers to families who cannot access distribution points. The funding gap is driven mainly by meals (5,000 additional purchased meals per day) and produce (significant temporary purchases). The partnership and solidarity of Jewish communities abroad are especially meaningful during this period, and Leket is grateful for any emergency support the Federation can provide to sustain these efforts so that vulnerable Israelis continue to receive the food they depend on during this critical time.

## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support from the Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis. The war with Iran has sharply reduced surplus food available for rescue: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—Leket's largest suppliers of cooked food—are not permitting entry. Corporate and IDF base surplus is unavailable. Daily meal distribution has dropped from 8,000 to 3,000, and produce rescue has been drastically reduced. Leket is temporarily supplementing with purchased food and supermarket vouchers. Maintaining operations requires an additional **$508,210 per week**, primarily for 5,000 purchased meals per day and significant temporary produce purchases so that vulnerable families and hundreds of nonprofit partners continue to receive food during this critical period. The organization is asking the Federation for any emergency support to sustain these efforts.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **Meals** — Purchase of 5,000 additional meals daily (surplus rescue dropped from 8,000 to 3,000/day): portion of $508,210/week
- **Produce** — Temporary purchases to maintain supply for nonprofit partners (farmers unable to harvest): portion of $508,210/week

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by deep uncertainty: sirens interrupt routines, families move in and out of shelters, and the instability touches nearly every part of daily life. Food security is a critical part of resilience; when parents know that nourishing food will be on the table, they are better able to care for their children and face the challenges of this period. Leket Israel frames food security as essential to resilience—when families know basic needs will be met, they can better care for children and face the crisis. As Israel's national food bank, Leket is committed to ensuring that vulnerable families continue to receive a reliable supply of nutritious food even during national crises. Consistent access to food during war is essential to protecting health, reducing trauma, and maintaining the social fabric that helps communities endure and recover.

The current security situation has significantly disrupted the networks that enable food rescue. Rocket fire has forced a pause in volunteer activity and limited collection and distribution operations. Farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases are not permitting entry. As a result, the supply of surplus food available for rescue has declined sharply. Despite these challenges, Leket's team is working to ensure that food continues to reach the hundreds of nonprofit partners who depend on them, to maintain regular distribution levels and where possible increase them. The organization is temporarily supplementing rescued food with purchased food and providing supermarket vouchers to support families who cannot access distribution points. Several grant requests from Leket are already under review with the Federation; this outreach specifically addresses the additional emergency needs created by the current situation. Emergency support is requested so that vulnerable Israelis continue to receive the food they depend on during this critical period.

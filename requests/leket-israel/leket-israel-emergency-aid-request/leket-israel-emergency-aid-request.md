## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, requests emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis and war-related escalation with Iran. Reported disruptions include paused volunteer activity, halted harvests, closed corporate offices, and restricted access to IDF bases (a major source of cooked food). As a result, daily meal distribution dropped from 8,000 to 3,000, and produce rescue declined sharply.

To bridge the gap, Leket is temporarily purchasing meals and produce and issuing supermarket vouchers for families who cannot access distribution points, while continuing supply to hundreds of nonprofit partners.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **Meals** — Purchase of 5,000 additional meals daily to offset the drop from 8,000 to 3,000 meals/day.
- **Produce** — Temporary produce purchases due to reduced farm harvest/rescue.
- **Amount detail note** — One source gives only narrative categories and marks specific line-item amounts as not specified/none.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak/escalation of war with Iran, sources describe widespread disruption across Israel (sirens, shelter movement, instability). Leket emphasizes food security as core to resilience, health, and family stability during crisis conditions.

Food-rescue operations have been significantly constrained by security conditions: volunteer pauses, collection/distribution limits, halted farming activity, office closures, and denied entry to IDF bases. These factors materially reduced available surplus food.

Leket reports active mitigation: purchased food, supermarket vouchers, and efforts to maintain or increase regular distribution where possible. The requested weekly emergency funding is positioned as essential to sustain continuity for vulnerable households during this period. One source notes related Leket requests already under Federation review; this request is framed as additional emergency need.

## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, requests emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis/war with Iran. Across both source documents, the core request is consistent: severe disruption to food rescue and distribution has created an additional **$508,210 per week** need to maintain supply for vulnerable families.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown (combined):**
- **Meals** — Purchase **5,000 additional meals daily** to offset the drop in available/rescued meals from 8,000 to 3,000 per day.
- **Produce** — Temporary produce purchases to replace reduced rescue volumes after harvest disruptions.
- **Vouchers** — Supermarket vouchers for families who cannot access distribution points.

_Note on amounts:_ narrative and weekly total are clear in both documents; line-item dollar allocations are not specified in one source and marked as unspecified/none in another.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak/escalation of war with Iran, routine food rescue channels have been heavily disrupted. Reported constraints include paused volunteer activity, halted harvests, corporate office closures, and restricted access to IDF bases (a major source of cooked food). As a result, surplus food available for rescue has declined sharply, and daily meal distribution fell from 8,000 to 3,000.

Leket frames food security as foundational to family and community resilience during wartime uncertainty, including frequent sirens, shelter movements, and broad disruption. To keep food flowing to hundreds of nonprofit partner organizations nationwide, Leket is temporarily purchasing meals and produce and issuing supermarket vouchers where direct distribution is not feasible.

Across both versions of the request, the organization seeks emergency philanthropic support (with specific references to Greater Philadelphia and JUF Chicago) so vulnerable Israelis continue receiving reliable, nutritious food during this period.

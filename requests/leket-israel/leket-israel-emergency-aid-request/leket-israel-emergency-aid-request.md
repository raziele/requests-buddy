## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis and war escalation with Iran. Across both versions, the core request is consistent: food rescue capacity has sharply declined due to paused volunteer activity, halted farm harvests, corporate closures, and restricted access to IDF bases (a major source of cooked food).

Daily meal distribution reportedly dropped from 8,000 to 3,000, creating a gap of 5,000 meals per day. Leket is temporarily filling gaps through purchased meals, temporary produce purchases, and supermarket vouchers for families who cannot reach distribution points, while continuing supply to hundreds of nonprofit partners.

**Funding Requested:** **$508,210 per week**

**Funding Breakdown (combined from sources):**
- **Meals:** Purchase 5,000 additional meals daily to offset drop from 8,000 to 3,000 rescued/distributed meals per day.
- **Produce:** Temporary produce purchases due to major reduction in rescued produce after harvest disruptions.
- **Amount detail variance:** Narrative attributes both categories to the $508,210/week shortfall; line-item amounts were not specified in one source, and another source noted missing explicit category amounts.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak/escalation of war with Iran, daily life in Israel has been marked by sirens, frequent shelter movement, and broad disruption. Leket emphasizes that food security is a core resilience factor: when families can rely on nutritious food, they are better able to protect children, maintain stability, and cope with trauma and uncertainty.

Operationally, the security situation has significantly disrupted the food rescue network:
- Volunteer operations were paused due to rocket fire/security conditions.
- Farmers halted harvests, reducing produce availability.
- Corporate office closures reduced surplus streams.
- IDF bases, normally major cooked-food suppliers, restricted entry.

To maintain continuity, Leket is supplementing rescued food with purchased food and vouchers, with the stated goal of sustaining—and where possible increasing—regular distribution levels during the emergency period. The outreach notes that some Leket grants were already under Federation review; this request is framed as additional emergency support needs created by current conditions.

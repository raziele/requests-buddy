## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, requests emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis/war with Iran. Across both requests, the core need is the same: severe disruption to surplus food rescue has reduced available supply while family needs remain high.

Key operational disruptions cited:
- Volunteer activity has been paused due to security conditions and rocket fire.
- Farmers have halted harvests.
- Corporate offices are closed.
- IDF bases (a major source of cooked food) are not permitting entry.

Immediate impact:
- Daily meal distribution fell from **8,000 to 3,000 meals/day**.
- Leket must **purchase 5,000 additional meals/day** to bridge the gap.
- Rescued produce has dropped sharply, requiring temporary produce purchases.
- Supermarket vouchers are being used for families who cannot reach distribution points.

**Funding Requested:** **$508,210 per week**

**Funding Breakdown (combined from both sources):**
- **Meals:** Purchase of 5,000 additional meals daily to offset the drop from 8,000 to 3,000 rescued/distributed meals per day.
- **Produce:** Temporary produce purchases to maintain supply for nonprofit partners due to harvest disruptions.
- **Note on detail level:** One source provided narrative-only amounts (not line-item quantified), and another explicitly marked per-line amounts as not specified/none.

## Context & Background

Both documents describe widespread disruption to daily life in Israel during the escalation/war with Iran, including sirens, shelter movements, and general instability. Leket positions food security as a resilience and trauma-mitigation necessity: when families can rely on food access, they are better able to care for children and withstand ongoing stress.

As the national food bank, Leket reports that its normal rescue-and-distribution network has been materially constrained by security and access limits. Despite this, the organization is continuing to serve hundreds of nonprofit partners and attempting to maintain regular distribution levels (and increase where possible) by substituting purchased meals/produce and issuing vouchers where direct pickup is not feasible.

Both requests emphasize that emergency external funding is needed immediately to sustain these temporary substitutions and prevent interruptions in food access for vulnerable households.

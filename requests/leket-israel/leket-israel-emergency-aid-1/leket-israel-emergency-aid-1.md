## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis following the escalation with Iran.

Across Israel, day-to-day life has been disrupted: sirens interrupt routines, families move in and out of shelters, and insecurity affects nearly every part of daily life. Leket describes food security as essential to resilience—when basic needs are reliably met, families can better care for their children and face the challenges of this period. The war-related disruption has sharply reduced surplus food available for rescue: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—Leket's largest suppliers of cooked food—are not permitting entry. As a result, daily meal distribution has dropped from 8,000 to 3,000 meals.

To bridge the gap, Leket is temporarily supplementing rescued food with purchased meals and additional produce, and providing supermarket vouchers for families who cannot access distribution points. Maintaining these operations requires an additional **$508,210 per week**—primarily to purchase meals and temporary produce so vulnerable families continue receiving food during this critical period.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown (as described across sources):**
- **Meals** — Purchase of 5,000 additional meals daily to restore supply (surplus/rescued meals availability dropped from 8,000 to 3,000/day); portion of $508,210/week.
- **Produce** — Temporary purchases to maintain supply for nonprofit partners after farmers halted harvests, reducing rescued produce; significant temporary produce purchases; portion of $508,210/week.
  
_Note on specificity:_ One source lists the breakdown amounts as “not specified,” while the combined narrative specifies the meals gap (5,000 additional meals daily) and the need for temporary produce purchases.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, Leket reports that food rescue networks have been severely disrupted. Rocket fire forced a pause in volunteer activity and limited collection and distribution operations. Farmers halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases are not permitting entry—cutting off Leket’s key cooked-food supply routes.

Despite these challenges, Leket’s team is working to keep food reaching the hundreds of nonprofit partners that rely on Leket’s support. They are temporarily supplementing rescued food with purchased food and, where necessary, providing supermarket vouchers to support families who cannot access distribution points. Where possible, the organization is working to maintain or increase regular distribution levels.

Leket frames this emergency request as essential to protecting health, reducing trauma, and maintaining the social fabric that helps communities endure and recover during national crises. The request emphasizes that emergency support is needed now so vulnerable Israelis continue to receive the food they depend on during this critical time. The sender also notes that several grant requests from Leket are already under review with the Federation; this outreach addresses additional emergency needs created by the current situation.

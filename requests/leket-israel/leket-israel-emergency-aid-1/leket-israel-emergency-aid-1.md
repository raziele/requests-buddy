## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis. Since the escalation / war with Iran, surplus food available for rescue has dropped sharply: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—Leket's largest suppliers of cooked food—are not permitting entry. Daily meal distribution has fallen from **8,000 to 3,000**. Leket is temporarily purchasing additional meals and produce and providing **supermarket vouchers** (including for families who cannot reach distribution points) to keep supply flowing to hundreds of nonprofit partners.

**Recipients named in source emails:** Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia; JUF Chicago.

**Funding requested:** **$508,210 per week** — primarily for **~5,000 purchased meals per day** (to bridge the gap from 3,000 to prior rescue levels) and **significant temporary produce purchases** after harvest disruption.

**Funding breakdown (amounts per line item not specified in sources):**

**Other context from correspondence:** Several Leket grant requests were already under review with the Federation (per Greater Philadelphia thread); this outreach addresses **additional** emergency needs from the current situation.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has involved deep uncertainty—sirens, moving in and out of shelters, and disruption touching most routines. Leket frames **food security** as central to resilience: when families know nourishing food will be available, they can better care for children and weather the period. As Israel's national food bank, Leket aims to keep a reliable supply of nutritious food reaching vulnerable families during national crises. Consistent access to food during war supports health, reduces trauma, and helps communities endure and recover.

Despite disruption to rescue networks (volunteers, collection/distribution limits, closed corporate offices, restricted IDF base access), Leket is working to **maintain or increase** regular distribution where possible, using purchased food and vouchers alongside rescued food. Sustaining that requires the additional **$508,210 per week** described above.

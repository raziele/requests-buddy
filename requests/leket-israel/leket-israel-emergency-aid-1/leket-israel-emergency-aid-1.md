## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel’s national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis. Since the escalation / war with Iran, surplus food available for rescue has dropped sharply: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases are not permitting entry (IDF bases are described as Leket’s largest suppliers of cooked food). Daily meal distribution has fallen from **8,000 to 3,000**. Leket is temporarily supplementing rescued food with **purchased meals and produce** and providing **supermarket vouchers** (including for families who cannot access distribution points).

They are seeking federation support to cover an additional **$508,210 per week** to bridge these gaps so vulnerable families continue to receive nutritious food.

**Differences between threads (audience / emphasis):**
- **Greater Philadelphia thread:** explicitly frames the ask to the Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia and notes **several grant requests from Leket are already under review** with the Federation; this outreach is positioned as addressing **additional emergency needs** created by the current situation.
- **JUF Chicago thread:** explicitly asks **JUF Chicago** for support (“any support”) to sustain the effort during this critical time.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown (as described; line-item dollar splits not specified in sources):**
- **Meals** — purchase **5,000 additional meals per day** to offset the drop from 8,000 rescued/served meals/day to 3,000/day.
- **Produce** — **significant temporary purchases** to maintain supply for nonprofit partners after farmers halted harvests and rescued produce dropped drastically.

_Note: Where a source listed breakdown amounts as “not specified” / “None,” the narrative still consistently describes the same weekly shortfall figure._

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by deep uncertainty: sirens interrupt routines, families move in and out of shelters, and disruption is widespread. Leket frames **food security as part of resilience**—when families know nourishing food will be available, they are better able to care for children and navigate the period’s challenges. As Israel’s national food bank, Leket emphasizes a commitment to keeping a **reliable supply of nutritious food** reaching vulnerable families during national crises, including framing consistent access to food during war as important for **health**, **reducing trauma**, and maintaining **community social fabric** to endure and recover.

Operationally, rocket fire has forced pauses/limits on volunteer activity and constrained collection and distribution. Despite disruption, Leket reports working to **maintain regular distribution levels** and, where possible, **increase** them while using purchased food and vouchers as bridges.

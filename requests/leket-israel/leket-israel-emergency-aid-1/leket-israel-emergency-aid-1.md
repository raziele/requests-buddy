## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, requests emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis and war-related escalation with Iran. Across sources, the core request is consistent: severe disruptions to food rescue operations have reduced available surplus food, and immediate funding is needed to keep vulnerable families fed.

Key operational impacts reported:
- Volunteer activity paused due to security conditions and rocket fire.
- Farmers halted harvests, sharply reducing rescued produce.
- Corporate offices closed.
- IDF bases (major cooked-food suppliers) not permitting entry.
- Daily meal distribution dropped from 8,000 to 3,000 meals.

To bridge the gap, Leket is purchasing food and issuing supermarket vouchers where families cannot reach distribution points.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown (combined):**
- **Meals** — Purchase 5,000 additional meals daily to offset the drop from 8,000 to 3,000 rescued meals/day.
- **Produce** — Temporary produce purchases to replace disrupted rescue flow from farms.
- **Note on reporting difference** — Some versions did not provide line-item dollar amounts ("not specified"/"None"), but all sources align on the same total weekly shortfall and priorities.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak/escalation of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by sirens, repeated shelter movements, and broad disruption. Leket emphasizes that food security is foundational to family and community resilience: when households can rely on nourishing food, they are better able to care for children, maintain stability, and cope with trauma during crisis conditions.

As Israel's national food bank, Leket works through hundreds of nonprofit partners and has continued adapting operations despite constraints. The organization reports that crisis conditions have disrupted the normal rescue-and-distribution chain at multiple points, forcing a temporary shift toward purchased meals and produce.

Across all versions, the urgency and rationale are the same:
- Maintain continuity of nutritious food supply for vulnerable populations.
- Prevent further deterioration in household food security during an active national emergency.
- Sustain and, where possible, restore regular distribution levels until normal rescue channels recover.

## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis and war-related disruptions. Across both requests, Leket reports a sharp decline in available surplus food due to paused volunteer activity, halted harvests, office closures, and restricted access to IDF bases.

Daily meal distribution reportedly dropped from 8,000 to 3,000, creating a need to purchase 5,000 additional meals per day. Leket is also making temporary produce purchases and issuing supermarket vouchers for families who cannot access distribution points, in order to maintain supply to hundreds of nonprofit partners nationwide.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week (additional emergency need)

**Funding Breakdown (combined):**
- **Meals** — Purchase of 5,000 additional meals daily to offset decline from 8,000 to 3,000 meals/day.
- **Produce** — Temporary purchases to replace reduced rescued produce due to halted harvests.
- **Note on amounts:** One source explicitly states portions of the $508,210/week are allocated across meals and produce but does not itemize exact dollar amounts; another marks breakdown amounts as not specified.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak/escalation of war with Iran, sources describe daily life in Israel as highly unstable, including sirens, repeated shelter movement, and broad operational disruption. Leket frames reliable food access as a core resilience need: ensuring families can meet basic nutritional needs helps protect health, reduce trauma burden, and support caregivers and children during crisis conditions.

Both sources describe severe disruption in food rescue logistics:
- Volunteer activity has paused due to security conditions and rocket fire.
- Farmers have halted harvests, reducing produce recovery.
- Corporate offices are closed.
- IDF bases (described as major suppliers of cooked food) are not permitting entry.

To sustain operations, Leket is temporarily substituting rescued food with purchased meals and produce, while also issuing supermarket vouchers where direct access is constrained. The requests emphasize that emergency funding is needed immediately to preserve continuity of food support for vulnerable households through nonprofit partner networks.

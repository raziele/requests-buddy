## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting **emergency support** to sustain food distribution during the **current security crisis** (escalation / war with Iran). The war has sharply reduced surplus food available for rescue: **volunteer activity is paused**, **farmers have halted harvests**, **corporate offices are closed**, and **IDF bases are not permitting entry** (IDF bases described as Leket's largest suppliers of cooked food). **Daily meal distribution has dropped from 8,000 to 3,000.** Leket is temporarily **supplementing with purchased food and supermarket vouchers** (vouchers especially for families who cannot access distribution points). The organization needs an additional **$508,210 per week** to bridge the gap so vulnerable families continue to receive nutritious food.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown (same headline figure in all sources):**
- **Meals** — Purchase **5,000 additional meals per day** to offset the drop from 8,000 to 3,000 rescued meals/day; portion of the weekly $508,210. _(One source listed line-item amounts as "not specified" / "None"; narrative consistently ties the weekly total to meals + produce.)_
- **Produce** — **Temporary purchases** to maintain supply for nonprofit partners after farmers' inability to harvest drastically reduced rescued produce; portion of the weekly $508,210.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by **deep uncertainty**: sirens, moving in and out of shelters, and disruption across routines. Leket frames **food security as part of resilience**—when basic needs like nourishing food are met, families can better care for children and face the period's challenges. As the national food bank, Leket is committed to a **reliable supply of nutritious food** for vulnerable families during national crises; consistent access supports **health, trauma reduction, and community cohesion** for endurance and recovery.

**Operational disruption:** Rocket fire and security constraints have disrupted rescue networks—limited collection and distribution, paused volunteers, closed corporate offices, no entry to IDF bases, and halted harvests—so **surplus food for rescue has declined sharply**.

**Response:** Leket is working to **maintain or increase regular distribution** where possible, supplementing rescued food with purchases and vouchers. The **$508,210/week** figure covers the meals gap (5,000 purchased meals/day) and significant temporary produce purchases. Outreach to Federation partners references **prior Leket grants under review** and positions this as **additional** emergency need; the JUF Chicago thread emphasizes a direct appeal for support through that channel.

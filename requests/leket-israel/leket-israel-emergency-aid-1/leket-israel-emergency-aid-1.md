## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel’s national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis linked to the war/escalation with Iran. Across both messages, Leket reports a sharp drop in surplus food rescue due to paused volunteer activity, halted farm harvests, closures in corporate food sources, and restricted access to IDF bases (a major cooked-food source).

Daily meal distribution has dropped from **8,000 to 3,000**, creating a gap of **5,000 meals per day**. To maintain service to hundreds of nonprofit partners and vulnerable families, Leket is temporarily purchasing meals and produce and issuing supermarket vouchers for families unable to reach distribution points.

**Funding Requested:** **$508,210 per week** (additional emergency operating need)

**Funding Breakdown (consolidated):**
- **Meals:** Purchase 5,000 additional meals daily to offset the drop in rescued cooked meals.
- **Produce:** Temporary produce purchases to replace reduced rescued produce while harvests are disrupted.
- **Note on amounts:** Source narratives describe these as the primary cost drivers, but line-item dollar splits are not specified.

## Context & Background

Both source documents describe the same core emergency context: nationwide disruption, recurring sirens, shelter movement, and high uncertainty for families across Israel. Leket frames food security as a resilience and trauma-mitigation need: when families can rely on nutritious food, they are better positioned to care for children and cope during crisis.

Operationally, the disruption affects every major leg of Leket’s rescue model:
- Volunteer collections/distribution are constrained.
- Farmers have halted harvests, reducing fresh produce rescue.
- Corporate office closures reduce surplus channels.
- IDF bases are not permitting entry, cutting access to a major cooked-food stream.

Leket states it is actively trying to maintain (and where possible increase) regular distribution by combining reduced rescue flows with emergency purchases and voucher support. The organization requests immediate philanthropic support to bridge this temporary but severe weekly shortfall so vulnerable Israelis continue receiving food during the crisis period.

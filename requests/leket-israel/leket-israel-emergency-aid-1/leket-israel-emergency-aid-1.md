## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis. **Recipients named in correspondence:** Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia and JUF Chicago.

Since the escalation with Iran, surplus food available for rescue has dropped sharply: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—Leket's largest suppliers of cooked food—are not permitting entry. Daily meal distribution has fallen from **8,000 to 3,000**. Leket is temporarily supplementing with **purchased meals and produce** and **supermarket vouchers** (including for families who cannot reach distribution points). The organization is working to maintain or increase regular distribution to hundreds of nonprofit partners.

**Funding requested:** **$508,210 per week** to bridge these gaps.

**Funding breakdown (narrative; line-item $ not specified in sources):**
- **Meals** — Purchase **5,000 additional meals per day** to offset the drop from 8,000 to 3,000 rescued meals/day.
- **Produce** — Temporary purchases to keep supply for partners after harvest disruption.

One thread notes that **several Leket grant requests are already under review** with the Federation; this ask addresses **additional emergency needs** from the current situation.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has involved deep uncertainty: sirens, moving in and out of shelters, and disruption across routines. Leket frames **food security as part of resilience**—when families know nourishing food will be available, they can better care for children and face the period’s challenges. As Israel’s national food bank, Leket aims to keep a reliable supply of nutritious food reaching vulnerable families during national crises. Consistent access to food during war supports health, reduces trauma, and helps communities endure and recover.

Rocket fire and security measures have disrupted rescue networks: limits on collection and distribution, halted harvests, closed corporate offices, and restricted IDF base access have sharply reduced rescued surplus. Despite this, Leket’s team is working to sustain operations so partners and families continue to receive support.

## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis and war context involving Iran. The crisis has sharply reduced surplus food available for rescue: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases (a major cooked-food source) are not permitting entry. Daily meal distribution has dropped from 8,000 to 3,000.

To maintain supply to hundreds of nonprofit partners and vulnerable families, Leket is temporarily purchasing meals and produce and issuing supermarket vouchers for households that cannot access distribution points. The organization reports an additional requirement of **$508,210 per week** to bridge these gaps.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **Meals** — Purchase 5,000 additional meals daily to offset the drop from 8,000 to 3,000 rescued meals/day.
- **Produce** — Temporary produce purchases due to harvest disruptions and reduced rescued produce.

_Noted difference across versions: one narrative frames this as support requested from Greater Philadelphia; another requests support from JUF Chicago. Numeric request and operational rationale are aligned._

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak/escalation of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by sirens, shelter movements, and widespread uncertainty. Leket emphasizes that food security is a core resilience need: when families can rely on access to nutritious food, they are better able to care for children and cope with ongoing disruption.

The current security environment has significantly disrupted food-rescue operations. Rocket fire and security restrictions have paused volunteer activity and constrained collection/distribution logistics. Farmers have halted harvests, corporate activity is reduced, and IDF bases are inaccessible, cutting off key food sources. As a result, rescued food volumes have declined sharply.

In response, Leket is sustaining continuity by supplementing rescued food with purchased meals and produce, while also providing supermarket vouchers where direct distribution is not feasible. The stated weekly shortfall is tied primarily to:
1. **Meals gap** — 5,000 meals/day now need to be purchased.
2. **Produce gap** — major temporary procurement needed due to harvest stoppages.

One version also notes that several Leket grant requests were already under review, and this outreach addresses additional emergency needs caused by the current crisis.

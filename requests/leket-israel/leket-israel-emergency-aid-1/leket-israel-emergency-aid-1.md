## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis. **Audience differences:** one outreach targets the **Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia**; another targets **JUF Chicago** — the substance of the request is the same.

Since the escalation / war with Iran, surplus food available for rescue has dropped sharply: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases *(Leket’s largest suppliers of cooked food)* are not permitting entry. Daily meal distribution has fallen from **8,000 to 3,000**. Leket is temporarily supplementing with **purchased meals and produce** and **supermarket vouchers** for families who cannot reach distribution points, while working to maintain supply to hundreds of nonprofit partners.

**Funding requested:** **$508,210 per week**, primarily to cover **~5,000 additional purchased meals per day** (to bridge the 8,000→3,000 gap) and **significant temporary produce purchases** after the collapse in rescued produce.

One source notes that **several Leket grant requests are already under review** with the Federation (Philadelphia thread); this communication addresses **additional emergency needs** from the current situation.

**Funding breakdown** *(amounts not split between line items in sources; weekly total is $508,210)*:

- **Meals** — Purchase **5,000 additional meals daily** (surplus rescue dropped from 8,000 to 3,000/day).
- **Produce** — Temporary purchases to keep nonprofit partners supplied while farmers cannot harvest.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life in Israel has been marked by deep uncertainty: sirens, moving in and out of shelters, and disruption across routines. Leket frames **food security as part of resilience** — when basic needs are met, families can better care for children and weather the period. As the national food bank, Leket aims to keep **nutritious food** reaching vulnerable households through national crises; consistent access supports health, reduces trauma, and helps communities endure and recover.

Rocket fire and security limits have disrupted rescue logistics: collection and distribution are constrained, surplus inflow has fallen, and the team is **buying food and issuing vouchers** where rescue cannot fill the gap, **maintaining or increasing distribution where possible**. Emergency support is sought so vulnerable Israelis **continue to receive the food they depend on** during this critical period.

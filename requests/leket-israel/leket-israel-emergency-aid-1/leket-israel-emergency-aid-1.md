## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, requests emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis/war with Iran. Across sources, the reported operational impacts are consistent: volunteer activity paused, farmers halting harvests, corporate office closures, and restricted access to IDF bases (a major cooked-food supplier). As a result, rescued food volumes have dropped sharply, with daily meal distribution falling from 8,000 to 3,000.

To bridge the gap, Leket is temporarily purchasing food and produce and issuing supermarket vouchers for families who cannot access distribution points, while continuing support for hundreds of nonprofit partners nationwide.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown (consolidated):**
- **Meals** — Purchase 5,000 additional meals daily to offset the drop from 8,000 to 3,000 rescued/distributed meals per day.
- **Produce** — Temporary produce purchases due to reduced rescued produce after harvest disruptions.
- **Voucher Support** — Supermarket vouchers for families unable to reach distribution points.
- **Amount Detail Note** — Sources do not provide line-item dollar allocations; one prior source explicitly indicated breakdown amounts as unspecified/none.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak/escalation of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by sirens, shelter movement, and widespread disruption. Leket frames food security as a core resilience need: reliable access to nutritious food helps families care for children, protect health, reduce trauma burden, and maintain community stability during crisis.

The food rescue network has been heavily disrupted by security conditions. Rocket fire and safety constraints reduced volunteer and operational activity; farmers halted harvests; corporate offices closed; and IDF bases limited entry. These disruptions significantly reduced the surplus food available for rescue.

Leket reports that it is working to maintain, and where possible increase, regular distribution by combining reduced rescued food with temporary purchased meals/produce and vouchers. The additional weekly requirement of $508,210 is presented as emergency bridge funding to sustain continuity of food access for vulnerable Israelis during this period.

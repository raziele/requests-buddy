## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis/war with Iran. The escalation has sharply reduced surplus food available for rescue: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases (a major source of cooked food) are not permitting entry.

Daily meal distribution has dropped from 8,000 to 3,000 meals, creating a gap of 5,000 meals per day. To maintain supply to hundreds of nonprofit partners and vulnerable families, Leket is temporarily supplementing with purchased meals, temporary produce purchases, and supermarket vouchers for families unable to reach distribution points.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **Meals** — Purchase of 5,000 additional meals daily to offset the drop from 8,000 to 3,000/day.
- **Produce** — Temporary produce purchases due to halted harvests and reduced rescued supply.
- **Amount detail status** — Source narratives describe the weekly shortfall and components; line-item amounts were not specified in one source and another noted no explicit breakdown amounts.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak/escalation of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by uncertainty, frequent sirens, shelter movement, and broad disruption. Leket emphasizes that food security is a core resilience need: when families can rely on nutritious food, they are better able to care for children and cope with ongoing crisis conditions.

The food rescue system has been significantly disrupted by security conditions:
- Volunteer activity has paused.
- Collection and distribution operations are constrained.
- Farmers have halted harvests.
- Corporate offices are closed.
- IDF bases are not allowing entry.

Despite these constraints, Leket is working to maintain, and where possible increase, regular distribution to nonprofit partners and vulnerable households. The requested emergency support is intended to bridge the operational food gap so families continue receiving dependable nutrition during the crisis period.

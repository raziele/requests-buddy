## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, requests emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis and war-period disruption tied to escalation with Iran. Across both versions, the core request is consistent: severe operational disruptions have sharply reduced rescued food supply, and Leket needs emergency funding to bridge the gap so vulnerable families continue receiving nutritious food.

Key disruptions reported:
- Volunteer activity paused due to rocket fire/security conditions.
- Farmers halted harvests, reducing rescued produce.
- Corporate offices closed.
- IDF bases (major cooked-food suppliers) not permitting entry.
- Daily rescued meal distribution dropped from **8,000 to 3,000**.

Emergency response underway:
- Purchase of **5,000 additional meals daily**.
- Significant temporary produce purchasing.
- Supermarket vouchers for families unable to access distribution points.
- Continued support to hundreds of nonprofit partner organizations nationwide.

**Funding Requested:** **$508,210 per week**

**Funding Breakdown (combined from both sources):**
- **Meals:** Purchase of 5,000 additional meals/day to offset drop from 8,000 to 3,000 rescued meals/day.
- **Produce:** Temporary purchases to replace produce shortfalls caused by halted harvests.
- **Amount detail note:** One source explicitly says line-item amounts are not specified; another references these as the primary components of the $508,210/week gap.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak/escalation of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by sirens, shelter movement, and broad instability. Leket frames food security as a core resilience need: when families are confident food will be available, they are better able to care for children and withstand ongoing trauma and uncertainty.

The security situation has disrupted the infrastructure that normally enables national food rescue and distribution. With volunteers sidelined, harvest activity reduced, and institutional food sources constrained, the surplus pipeline has contracted sharply. Leket reports maintaining and, where possible, increasing distribution through temporary purchases and voucher support despite these constraints.

Both requests describe this outreach as tied to **additional emergency needs** created by the current crisis period. One version also notes that separate Leket grants were already under review, and that this request is specifically for incremental emergency shortfall support.

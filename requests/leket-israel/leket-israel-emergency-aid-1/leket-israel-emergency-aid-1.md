## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis and war with Iran. The disruption has sharply reduced surplus food available for rescue: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases (a major source of cooked food) are not permitting entry. As a result, daily meal distribution has dropped from 8,000 to 3,000.

To maintain supply to hundreds of nonprofit partners and vulnerable families, Leket is temporarily purchasing food (including meals and produce) and issuing supermarket vouchers for families who cannot access distribution points.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **Meals** — Purchase of 5,000 additional meals daily to address the drop from 8,000 to 3,000 meals/day.
- **Produce** — Temporary produce purchases to offset reduced rescue volumes due to halted harvests.
- **Amount detail note** — One source provides the total weekly amount without line-item amounts ("not specified"/"None"), while narratives consistently describe the same meals and produce gaps.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak/escalation of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by sirens, shelter movement, and broad instability. Leket frames food security as foundational to resilience: when families can rely on nutritious food, they are better able to care for children and cope with crisis conditions.

The current security environment has materially disrupted food rescue operations:
- Rocket fire and security restrictions have paused volunteer activity.
- Farmers have halted harvests, reducing rescued produce.
- Corporate offices are closed.
- IDF bases are not permitting entry, reducing access to cooked food sources.

Despite these constraints, Leket reports active efforts to maintain and, where possible, increase regular distribution levels by supplementing rescued food with purchased meals/produce and vouchers. The additional weekly funding request is positioned as urgent crisis-bridge support to prevent interruption of food access for vulnerable Israelis.

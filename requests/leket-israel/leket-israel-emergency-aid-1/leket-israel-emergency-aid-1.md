## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel’s national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis. The war / escalation with Iran has sharply reduced surplus food available for rescue: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases are not permitting entry (IDF bases are described as Leket’s largest suppliers of cooked food). Daily meal distribution has dropped from 8,000 to 3,000, and produce rescue has been drastically reduced. Leket is temporarily supplementing with purchased food and supermarket vouchers (including for families who cannot reach distribution points). Maintaining operations requires an additional **$508,210 per week**, primarily for purchasing about **5,000 additional meals per day** and significant temporary produce purchases so vulnerable families continue to receive food.

**Recipients named in the threads (differences):** one outreach asks the **Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia**; another asks **JUF Chicago** for support toward the same weekly gap.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **Meals** — Purchase of ~5,000 additional meals daily (distribution/rescue effectively down from 8,000 to 3,000/day); portion of $508,210/week. One source listed line-item amounts as “not specified.”
- **Produce** — Temporary purchases to maintain supply for nonprofit partners after farmers halted harvests / reduced rescued produce; portion of $508,210/week.

One version noted that **several grant requests from Leket are already under review with the Federation**; that same thread emphasizes this outreach addresses **additional** emergency needs from the current situation.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by deep uncertainty: sirens interrupt routines, families move in and out of shelters, and instability touches nearly every part of daily life. Leket frames food security as essential to resilience—when parents know nourishing food will be on the table, they can better care for children and face the period’s challenges. As Israel’s national food bank, Leket is committed to ensuring vulnerable families keep a reliable supply of nutritious food during national crises; consistent access to food is described as important for health, reducing trauma, and maintaining community social fabric.

The security situation has severely disrupted food rescue networks. Rocket fire has paused volunteer activity and limited collection and distribution. Farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases are not permitting entry—sharply cutting surplus food available for rescue.

Despite disruption, Leket’s team is working to keep food flowing to hundreds of nonprofit partners, temporarily supplementing rescued food with purchases and vouchers, and working—where possible—to maintain or increase regular distribution levels. Bridging the gap is estimated at **$508,210 per week** (meals + produce as above). Support is requested so vulnerable Israelis continue to receive the food they depend on during this critical period.

## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel’s national food bank, is requesting **emergency support** to sustain food distribution during the **current security crisis** (escalation / war with Iran). Surplus food available for rescue has dropped sharply: **volunteer activity is paused**, **farmers have halted harvests**, **corporate offices are closed** *(stated in one thread)*, and **IDF bases are not permitting entry** — IDF bases are described as Leket’s **largest suppliers of cooked food**. **Daily meal distribution has fallen from 8,000 to 3,000.** Leket is **temporarily supplementing** with **purchased meals and produce** and **supermarket vouchers** (for families who cannot reach distribution points). They need an additional **$508,210 per week** to bridge the gap so vulnerable families keep receiving nutritious food.

**Audience / federation targets (differences between sources):**

- **Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia** — explicit ask; sender notes **several Leket grant requests are already under review** with the Federation; this outreach addresses **additional emergency needs** from the current situation.
- **JUF Chicago** — explicit ask for support toward the same weekly shortfall.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210 per week

**Funding Breakdown (narrative; line-item $ not specified in sources):**

- **Meals** — Purchase **5,000 additional meals per day** to offset the drop from **8,000 to 3,000** rescued/distributed meals per day; primary driver of the weekly need.
- **Produce** — **Temporary purchases** so nonprofit partners still receive supply after **drastically reduced** rescued produce (farmers unable to harvest).

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life in Israel is marked by **deep uncertainty**: sirens, moving in and out of shelters, and disruption across routines. Leket frames **food security as part of resilience** — when basic nourishment is reliable, families can better care for children and cope. As the national food bank, Leket aims to keep **consistent access to nutritious food** during national crises, which they tie to **health, trauma reduction, and community cohesion** for endurance and recovery.

**Operational disruption:** Rocket fire and security constraints have **paused or limited volunteers** and **collection/distribution**. With **harvests halted**, **offices closed**, and **no entry to IDF bases**, **surplus rescue has collapsed**. Despite this, the team is working to **maintain or increase distribution where possible**, using **purchased food** and **vouchers** alongside rescue. **Emergency support** is requested so Israelis who depend on this network **do not lose access** during the critical period.

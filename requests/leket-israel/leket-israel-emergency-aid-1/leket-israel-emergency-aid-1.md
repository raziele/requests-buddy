## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting emergency support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis (escalation / war with Iran). Surplus food available for rescue has dropped sharply: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—Leket's largest suppliers of cooked food—are not permitting entry. Daily meal distribution has fallen from **8,000 to 3,000**. Leket is temporarily supplementing with **purchased meals and produce** and **supermarket vouchers** (including for families who cannot reach distribution points).

**Additional funding required:** **$508,210 per week**, primarily to cover:
- **Meals** — purchase **5,000 additional meals per day** to bridge the gap between former rescued volume and current distribution.
- **Produce** — **temporary purchases** so nonprofit partners still receive supply while rescued produce is drastically reduced.

One source listed monetary splits for meals/produce as “not specified” / “None”; the **$508,210/week** figure and the two main cost drivers are consistent across threads.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life in Israel has been marked by deep uncertainty—sirens, movement in and out of shelters, and widespread disruption. Leket frames **food security as part of resilience**: when families know nourishing food will be available, they can better care for children and weather the period. As national food bank, Leket aims to keep a reliable supply of nutritious food reaching vulnerable families during national crises; consistent access supports health, reduces trauma, and helps communities endure.

Despite disruption to rescue networks, Leket’s team is working to **maintain or increase regular distribution** where possible, using purchased food and vouchers alongside rescue. The organization emphasizes that this outreach addresses **additional emergency needs** on top of other grant conversations already underway (noted in the Federation-facing thread).

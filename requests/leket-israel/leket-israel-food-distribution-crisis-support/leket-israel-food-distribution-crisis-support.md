## The Ask

Leket Israel, Israel's national food bank, is requesting support to sustain food distribution during the current security crisis. Since the escalation with Iran, surplus food supply has dropped sharply: volunteer activity is paused, farmers have halted harvests, and IDF bases are not permitting entry. Daily meal distribution has fallen from 8,000 to 3,000. Leket is temporarily purchasing additional meals and produce and providing supermarket vouchers to maintain supply to hundreds of nonprofit partners. They are asking JUF Chicago for support to cover an additional $508,210 per week to bridge these gaps so vulnerable families continue to receive nutritious food during the crisis.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by deep uncertainty: sirens, shelter movements, and widespread disruption. Leket Israel frames food security as essential to resilience—when families know basic needs will be met, they can better care for children and face the period's challenges. As the national food bank, Leket is committed to keeping a reliable supply of nutritious food reaching vulnerable families even during national crises.

The current security situation has severely disrupted food rescue networks. Rocket fire forced a pause in volunteer activity and limits on collection and distribution. Farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—Leket's largest suppliers of cooked food—are not permitting entry. As a result, surplus food available for rescue has declined sharply.

Leket's team is working to maintain regular distribution levels and, where possible, increase them. To do this they are temporarily supplementing rescued food with purchased food and providing supermarket vouchers for families who cannot access distribution points. Maintaining these operations requires an additional $508,210 per week, primarily to cover the meals gap (daily distribution dropped from 8,000 to 3,000 meals; they need to purchase 5,000 additional meals daily) and the produce gap (farmers' inability to harvest has drastically reduced rescued produce, requiring significant temporary purchases). They are asking JUF Chicago for any support to sustain this effort so vulnerable Israelis continue to receive the food they depend on during this critical time.

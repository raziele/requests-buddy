Dear Barak,

I hope this email finds you and your loved ones safe and well.

These are extremely difficult days. Over the past three days, communities across Israel have faced continuous missile attacks and repeated sirens. Educational frameworks are closed, and most workplaces are currently shut down under emergency regulations. This has a profound impact on the already vulnerable refugee community, which remains largely excluded from national safety nets, emergency assistance, and social protection mechanisms.

In between alarms, ASSAF staff and volunteers are operating in emergency mode. In accordance with Home Front Command guidelines, we adapted all services to the current security situation. We are mapping urgent needs emerging from community members, maintaining continuous contact with staff and volunteers, and strengthening resilience and emotional support responses during this period of heightened stress.

We are also actively disseminating accessible, multilingual information about emergency guidelines <https://links.us1.defend.egress.com/Warning?crId=69a5bc543903e6d030672b2a&Domain=jewishfed.org&Threat=eNpzrShJLcpLzAEADmkDRA%3D%3D&Lang=en&Base64Url=eNoFwtsOgiAAANCP6V1MW0Zba5qUVmtkrU3fUIGYFwhQ5t-3c77WKrMHwDnnMdLQWsrOa-QACsomTqnJx9xoQnugpLEGKFaL1g_qt0LP9AJVF8xCw_sJdwl1IYzT8Sp4JKJlCj4LzqKQ8Meaa6xhafEWZTHZ_TaoP-pWtAc0Tmf_VSXVPKQ3hsvVHyoRMRU%3D&@OriginalLink=www.facebook.com> and available services, to ensure that community members are not left without clear and reliable updates. In addition, we have expanded our remote psychosocial responses, including increased availability of emotional support. We have expanded our reception hours and established an emergency telephone line to ensure continued accessibility and rapid response for the refugee community.

Thank you for your partnership. May we soon see quieter and more peaceful days.
With appreciation,

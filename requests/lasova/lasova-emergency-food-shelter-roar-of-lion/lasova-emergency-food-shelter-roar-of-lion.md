## The Ask

Lasova NGO is requesting support to sustain and expand its emergency food and shelter operations during the Roar of the Lion escalation. Since the start of the campaign, demand has risen sharply: hot meal distribution has nearly doubled in some locations (e.g. South Tel Aviv restaurant now serves 470 diners daily versus 250 in routine), and new populations—including families who had never needed assistance before—are turning to Lasova for meals and basic support. The organization runs restaurants, food baskets, home deliveries, and shelters for street dwellers in full emergency mode. With many fixed food sources closed, operations rely heavily on community donations. They are asking for financial and in-kind (food) donations to continue serving thousands of beneficiaries.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Food donations; open to discuss cooperation and assistance as appropriate.

## Context & Background

Since the start of the Roar of the Lion campaign, Lasova has continued to operate across the country so that thousands of beneficiaries still receive the vital support they need. The organization is experiencing a significant rise in demand: staff and volunteers are in the field to provide a meaningful response.

In South Tel Aviv, the Lasova restaurant now serves about 470 people daily compared to roughly 250 in routine—almost double. Alongside regular diners, new families are seeking help who did not need it before, including mothers with small children, foreign workers left without income, and families affected by economic shutdown and school closures. The restaurant manager notes that the surge in demand resembles the COVID period but at higher intensity, with families and individuals needing a hot meal and a safe place.

The organization operates in full emergency mode. Kitchen teams in the north (Acre and Karmiel) cook from early morning to ensure regular supply to centers and home deliveries. Because many usual food sources are closed, activity now depends mainly on community donations. Lasova shelters for street dwellers remain open and have expanded capacity; they offer lodging, hot meals, clean clothing, and access to a safe room. Staff have proactively reached out to municipal and social services to refer people to shelters. Routine work—individual accompaniment, therapeutic response, and daily operations—continues, with the understanding that for street dwellers there is no pause in need.

With prolonged fighting and deepening economic impact, the number of people seeking assistance is expected to keep rising. Lasova is grateful for financial and food donations to enable continued operations for thousands in need.

#### Lasova (עמותת לשובע)

Greeting to the team and all with us. Against the backdrop of the war, Lasova continues to provide thousands of beneficiaries with the most basic needs and has expanded its emergency activity across the country.

**Food operations:** The organization distributes hundreds of hot meals daily, alongside food baskets and portions to Lasova restaurants. Family demand is almost double routine levels. Example: Lasova restaurant in South Tel Aviv sees about 470 diners daily compared to 250 in routine—roughly 90% increase. Alongside regular diners, new families who had not needed assistance before are now turning to the organization, including mothers with small children, foreign workers left without income, and families affected by economic shutdown and school closures.

Quote from Ravit Reichman, manager of Lasova Tel Aviv: "We see a sharp rise in demand, reminiscent of the COVID period but at higher intensity. Every day families and people who were not here before arrive, and simply need a hot meal and a safe place. We are here to make sure no one is left without a basic response in this complex period."

The organization operates in full emergency mode. Kitchen teams (Acre and Karmiel) in the north cook from early morning to ensure regular food supply both to centers and for home delivery. Due to closure of fixed food sources, activity currently relies mainly on community donations.

Quote from Yifat, manager of Lasova Karmiel: "We make sure our beneficiaries receive everything they need at home—despite the difficulties of the war. We quickly understood there was a different kind of need in the city: most of the food baskets we receive do not include ready-made food, so we started working in the kitchen to cook and supply meals. We work hard and with care so that no one goes without."

**Shelters (gagonim) for street dwellers:** Since the war began, Lasova shelters have been in full emergency mode. Behind the open doors is quiet, consistent, determined work for those without a roof. The shelters are ready to receive residents from across the country—both self-referrals and referrals from hospitals and municipal social service units. In a period of uncertainty when many systems are scaling back, the shelters are expanding: lodging, hot meal, clean clothing for those who need it, and an open safe room for all who need it. Civil mobilization has brought dedicated shipments for street dwellers—sandwiches, packaged meals, toiletries, snacks, drinks. Staff have proactively contacted municipality, Mesila unit, municipal oversight, Yazhar center, and street-dweller units to enable referral to shelter services. Routine work is also maintained: individual accompaniment, therapeutic response, department management, and daily operations. For street dwellers there is no "pause" and no waiting for routine to return; the need is here and now.

**Closing (page 2):** Gilad Harish, CEO and Chair of the organization. With the prolongation of fighting and deepening economic impact, the number of people seeking assistance is expected to continue to rise. The organization is grateful for help with financial donations and food donations to enable continued routine operation for thousands of people in need.

#### רשת בתי הנוער קדימה (Kadima Youth Homes Network)

From the opening of the Roar of the Lion campaign, the Kadima Youth Homes network has been operating continuously and without pause. Already on Saturday they verified with all staff and participants that everyone was safe and well, and from Sunday morning they have been working to a precise schedule adapted to the situation, to continue to be a stable and meaningful anchor. The schedule includes daily Zoom group calls and activities, remote learning support for those who need it, and personal WhatsApp, video, and Zoom calls—so that every child feels seen, heard, and able to see faces. In parallel, staff received dedicated training on the emotional processes that participants experience in stress and anxiety, and practical tools for calming, adapted dialogue, and strengthening respite activities. Team meetings are held daily on Zoom for sharing, support, and morale. Community resilience is built on reciprocity—care for both participants and staff—and a deep commitment to remain a stable and safe home even in times of uncertainty.

## The Ask

Lasova, an Israeli NGO providing meals and shelter to people in need, is requesting partnership and support during the Roar of the Lion military campaign. Since the start of the campaign they have seen a sharp rise in demand: hot meal distribution has nearly doubled, and new populations—including families who never needed help before, foreign workers without income, and people affected by shutdowns—are turning to their restaurants and food programs. They operate in full emergency mode with kitchens in the North (Acre, Karmiel) and South Tel Aviv, run shelters (gagons) for street dwellers with overnight stays and hot meals, and rely heavily on community donations. They are asking for financial and in-kind (food) support to continue serving thousands of beneficiaries.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Open to discuss partnership and assistance options; welcome financial and food donations; happy to talk and explore collaboration as appropriate

## Context & Background

Since the start of the campaign against Iran (Roar of the Lion), Lasova has continued to operate across Israel so that the thousands of people who depend on its services still receive essential support. Demand has risen significantly: staff and volunteers are in the field to provide an adequate response. The attached bulletin and a Ynet article describe this work. As demand for assistance grows, they need support more than ever.

In South Tel Aviv, the Lasova restaurant now serves about 470 diners per day compared with roughly 250 in normal times—almost double. Alongside regular diners, new families are now seeking help who did not need it before, including mothers with small children, foreign workers who lost their income, and families affected by economic shutdown and closed education frameworks. The organization runs kitchens in Acre and Karmiel from early morning to ensure regular food supply both to centers and for home delivery; with many usual food sources closed, operations depend largely on community donations.

Lasova also runs shelters (gagons) for street dwellers in full emergency mode, offering overnight accommodation, hot meals, clean clothing where needed, and access to safe rooms. They have proactively coordinated with municipal units, Misilla, municipal oversight, the Jezreel Valley center, and street-dweller units to refer people to their services. Routine work—individual accompaniment, therapeutic response, department management, daily operations—continues. With the fighting ongoing and economic impact deepening, the number of people seeking help is expected to keep rising. They are asking for financial and food donations to allow continued operations for thousands in need.

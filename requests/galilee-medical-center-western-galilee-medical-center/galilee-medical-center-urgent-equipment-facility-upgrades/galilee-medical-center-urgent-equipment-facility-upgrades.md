## The Ask

Galilee Medical Center in Nahariya has submitted a list of urgent needs for medical equipment, facility upgrades, and infrastructure. The request was relayed by the Jewish Federation of Northern New Jersey’s Israel Office. Needs include renovation of fortified underground complexes, a new Hemato-Oncology Institute with PET-CT scanner, an additional MRI, critical care and monitoring equipment (ventilators, defibrillators, patient monitors, ICU beds), upgrades to high-risk pregnancy and gynecological wards, pediatric ICU upgrades, nuclear medicine and ophthalmology equipment, and sterile pharmacy facilities for cytotoxic drug preparation. The hospital currently has no PET-CT; the new facility would allow Western Galilee residents to access this diagnostic service locally.

## Context & Background

Galilee Medical Center (Western Galilee Medical Center) in Nahariya serves the Northern Israeli population. The Jewish Federation of Northern New Jersey’s Israel Office Director, Ravit Steinmetz Shemla, forwarded a March 2026 list of urgent needs in Hebrew, offering to obtain a different format or additional details if required.

The attached spreadsheet itemizes 24 needs in four columns: Project, Need in $, Description, and Remarks. Major infrastructure items include renovation of fortified underground complexes ($5M), a new MRI ($5.8M), and a new Hemato-Oncology Institute with PET-CT facility ($10M)—the latter covering ~600 m² adjacent to the existing Oncology Institute. The medical center currently has no PET-CT; the project would allow Western Galilee residents to access this critical diagnostic safely and close to home.

Clinical equipment requests range from ICU ventilators (HAMILTON-C6), defibrillators, patient monitors (ePM15, telemetry), and ICU beds to specialized equipment for ophthalmology (Pentacam), neurosurgery (CURVE navigation, microscope), nuclear medicine (Gamma camera upgrade), and liver surgery (CUSA generator, BK Active ultrasound). Ward upgrades include High Risk Pregnancy and Gynecological wards ($700K each), Pediatric ICU upgrade, cytotoxic sterile cleanroom in the pharmacy, and an NGS sequencing machine. Descriptions in the sheet emphasize patient safety, preparedness for emergencies and mass-casualty events, and alignment with modern standards of care.

Support is needed to meet these urgent equipment and infrastructure gaps so the hospital can continue to serve the region effectively.

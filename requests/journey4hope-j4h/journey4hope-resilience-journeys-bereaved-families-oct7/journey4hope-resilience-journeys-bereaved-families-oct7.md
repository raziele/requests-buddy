## The Ask

Journey4Hope (J4H) requests philanthropic support to fund resilience journeys for bereaved mothers and families affected by the October 7 attacks. J4H delivers a seven-day, in-person psychological intervention that builds continuity, competence, movement, and connection. Participants pay $2,000 toward a $5,000 per-participant cost; J4H seeks donations to close the gap and to meet an additional $280,000 in projected 2026 need. The organization is scaling to serve bereaved fathers and to develop leadership pathways for alumni, with 600+ participants and 12+ delegations expected in 2026.

**Funding Requested:** $280,000 (additional philanthropic funding for 2026); cost per participant $5,000 (families contribute $2,000)

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Self Participant Fees: $415,927 (31% of total)
- Partnerships (Municipal & IDF Alumni Programs, 255 and more): $402,510 (30%)
- Donations (Confirmed & Projected): $523,263 (39%)
- Total Revenue to date: $1,341,700
- Additional philanthropic funding required: $280,000

## Context & Background

Since October 7, thousands of families in Israel have been impacted by loss; bereaved parents, siblings, grandparents, and close networks form circles of grief. J4H provides what it describes as "a year's worth of therapy in 7 days" through a structured resilience-building expedition—the first of its kind in the world for this population. Outcomes from the program include 100% of alumni reporting improved family and social relationships, 90% enhanced wellbeing and resilience, 25% making positive employment changes (returning to work, changing careers, or resuming studies), 15% volunteering to support future participants, and 20% initiating new support projects in their communities.

J4H is expanding in 2026: launching a first program for bereaved fathers, growing "From Bereavement to Leadership" initiatives (mentorship, continuity, resource mobilization), and addressing a waiting list of 200+ families. Revenue to date is $1,341,700 from participant fees (~$2,000 avg), 15+ partnerships (municipal and IDF alumni programs), and donations. To fully support projected 2026 demand, an additional $280,000 in philanthropic funding is required.

The email was sent by Liat Sade Sternberg to share the initiative and invite support; the attached deck details impact, program pillars (Continuity, Competence, Movement & Playfulness, Connection), budget, alumni leadership, and partnerships with IDF Alumni Foundations, municipalities, 255, Nova Tribe, and others.

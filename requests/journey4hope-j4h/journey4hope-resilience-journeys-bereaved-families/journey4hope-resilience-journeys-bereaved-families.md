## The Ask

Journey4Hope (J4H) requests philanthropic support to fund resilience journeys for bereaved mothers and families affected by tragedy since October 7. J4H delivers a unique seven-day psychological intervention that builds continuity, competence, movement, and connection. Participants pay $2,000 toward a $5,000 per-person cost; donations close the gap. The organization is scaling to serve bereaved fathers and to develop leadership pathways for alumni. An additional $280,000 in philanthropic funding is required to meet projected 2026 demand.

**Funding Requested:** $280,000 (additional philanthropic funding for 2026); cost per participant $5,000 (families contribute $2,000)

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Self participant fees (avg. ~$2,000 per participant): $415,927
- Partnerships (Municipal & IDF Alumni Programs, 255 and more): $402,510
- Donations (confirmed & projected): $523,263
- Total revenue to date: $1,341,700
- Additional philanthropic funding required for 2026: $280,000

## Context & Background

Since October 7, thousands of families in Israel have been impacted by profound loss. Bereaved parents face severe psychological and practical strain: 34.6% are unable to return to work, and grief circles extend to parents, siblings, grandparents, and close networks. Journey4Hope provides what it describes as 'a year's worth of therapy in 7 days'—an emergency psychological intervention and resilience-building expedition that is framed as the first of its kind in the world.

J4H brings together bereaved mothers (and is expanding to fathers) for a structured journey focused on continuity and connection, competence and empowerment, and movement and playfulness. Impact data cited in the materials: 100% of alumni report improved family and social relationships; 90% report enhanced wellbeing and resilience; 25% make positive employment changes; 15% volunteer to support future participants; and 20% initiate new support projects in their communities. Approximately 30% of the organization's donations are now raised by bereaved mothers themselves.

Revenue to date for 2026 is $1,341,700 from participant fees (31%), partnerships (30%), and donations (39%). To fully support projected 2026 program demand—600+ participants, 12+ delegations, and the new bereaved fathers program—an additional $280,000 in philanthropic funding is required. The contact frames the ask as joining 'this urgent mission of resilience and hope.'

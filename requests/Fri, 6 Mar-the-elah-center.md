## The Ask

The Elah Center proposes a nationwide program of support and supervision for social workers who are facing unprecedented emotional and social burdens due to the ongoing war. The program aims to strengthen their resilience and prevent vicarious trauma, secondary traumatization, and compassion fatigue.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Generous support to enable the center to continue working to strengthen social resilience by supporting social workers.

## Context & Background

The Elah Center, which specializes in treating trauma, bereavement, and loss, operates nationwide with approximately 600 experienced professionals. They propose a complementary program to strengthen social workers in municipal social service departments, who are facing heavy workloads and extreme cases, especially during wartime. Since October 2023, the prolonged warfare has created an unprecedented emotional and social burden on citizens, intensifying the pressures on social workers and placing them at risk of vicarious trauma and compassion fatigue. The proposed support includes ongoing group supervision, individual supervision, short-term workshops, and educational lectures.

# KEDMA'S EMERGENCY RESPONSE TO OPERATION ROARING LION: CORE ACTIVITIES AND UPDATES

Over the past week Israel has entered another complex security chapter. Ongoing military operations involving Iran, alongside active fighting on the northern border with Hezbollah in Lebanon, have created a renewed atmosphere of uncertainty across the country. UAV attacks have also reached communities in the Golan and the Arava, while security operations continue along the Gaza border. The result is a multi-front reality that places many communities across Israel under renewed pressure during an already fragile period.

For communities nationwide, this is a challenging and emotionally demanding period. For those living along Israel's borders, particularly in the North, the impact is immediate, placing renewed pressure on communities that had only just begun to regain stability. After more than a year of evacuation, families who recently returned home now find themselves facing sirens and uncertainty once again, placing additional strain on communities, and heightening concerns about long-term demographic sustainability in the Galilee.

Below is a brief overview of what Kedma has done so far, and how it continues to stand by its communities. Since the beginning of the current escalation, Kedma has activated its community response framework across all districts, with particular focus on the North. The response centers on sustaining daily life and reinforcing community resilience through:

**Supporting the Northern communities**

Kedma is active across the South, East, and national hubs; the Northern District currently demands intensified attention. Educational systems are currently suspended, and residents are operating in proximity to secure rooms and shelters. Kedma's student villages are, in many cases, serving as an operational backbone for local resilience, supporting children during morning hours, assisting elderly residents, and providing one-on-one mentoring where needed. For communities still rebuilding after prolonged evacuation, many of which have not yet completed the physical restoration of homes and local infrastructure, this renewed instability brings both emotional and logistical strain.

**Support for Evacuated Families**

The current security situation has once again created a reality in which families are being forced to leave their homes. As of now, 2,436 civilians have been evacuated; 1,927 are currently staying in hotels in Eilat, while others have relocated temporarily with relatives or host communities. Kedma volunteers are in Eilat, providing ongoing recreational and educational activities for children and supporting families during this period of displacement.

**Strengthening Informal Education Frameworks**

With formal education systems currently closed, Kedma's teams are operating and reinforcing informal education programs for children and youth—providing safe spaces, structured activities, and emotional stability. This effort is especially critical in northern border communities such as Misgav Am, Manara, Metula, Kfar Yuval, Kfar Sold, Lehavot HaBashan, Mitzpeh Ramon, Kfar HaNassi, and Shtula.

**Community Activation & Holiday Programming**

Across multiple districts, Kedma's teams organized Purim activities, community gatherings, and children's events. In the southern border communities, this included holiday parades, children's programming, and support for non-formal education frameworks in kibbutzim and regional councils. In the Ramat Negev region, volunteers operated childcare centers in protected shelters to support parents who are enlisted or essential workers.

**Support for Soldiers, Reservists, and Essential Workers**

From operating "warm corners" for soldiers, to hosting reserve units, to community-led laundry initiatives for reservists—Kedma communities are mobilizing to support those serving in close proximity to border communities.

**Assistance to Vulnerable Populations & Local Agriculture**

Kedma continues supporting elderly residents, individuals with special needs, and local farmers.

---

**Immediate Funding Priorities**

Following a rapid needs assessment, Kedma has identified several urgent intervention areas that require immediate philanthropic support:

1. **Resilience Care Packages**
   - Goal: Assemble and distribute 1,500 resilience care packages for Kedma activists, children and youth in border communities, and soldiers stationed in Kedma's areas of operation.
   - Purpose: Practical comfort items and symbolic encouragement to reinforce solidarity, morale, and emotional strength.

2. **Protected Transportation & Logistics Under Fire**
   - Public transportation has been suspended. Need includes: protected transport for Kedma's volunteers to border communities; fuel coverage, trained drivers, and reinforced logistical support. Daily operational costs for protected vehicles are significantly higher than routine transportation.

3. **Thermal Protection - Winter Gear for Border Community Volunteers**
   - Need: High-quality thermal clothing sets (top and bottom) for prolonged outdoor activity.
   - Target population: 500 Kedma volunteers and young adults, as well as reservists and local rapid-response teams.

4. **Emotional Resilience Support – On-Site Social Workers**
   - Need: Licensed social workers (one per district). Scope: 10 hours of availability per day, for one month (approximately 22 working days). Purpose: professional tools for coping and preventing burnout among field managers and community leaders.

5. **First Aid Kits for Shelters**
   - Aim: Approximately 100 fully equipped first aid kits for shelters in partner communities to ensure immediate access to essential medical supplies and basic emergency care equipment.

## The Ask

The Gush Halav Local Council is urgently requesting 100 computers (80 for elementary students, 20 for high school students) to enable remote learning. The ongoing security situation has disrupted the local education system, and students currently lack the necessary devices.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Donation of 100 computers

## Context & Background

Gush Halav, a community located about 4 km from the border, has suffered greatly during the recent conflict. The ongoing security situation has led to the suspension of the education system, with significant gaps in technological infrastructure in schools and the village as a whole. Access roads to Gush Halav are currently restricted, delaying the return to regular studies. Students lack alternative learning solutions and face a significant risk of educational and social disconnection.

### Gush Halav Local Council Request

Date: March 4, 2026

To Whom It May Concern,

Subject: Urgent Request for Immediate Assistance with Computers for Remote Learning for Gush Halav Students

Dear Sir/Madam,

Gush Halav, a community located about 4 km from the border, has suffered greatly during the recent period. Due to the ongoing conflict, the learning system is shut down: there are significant gaps in technological infrastructure in schools, especially in the village as a whole. The access routes to and from Gush Halav are defined as "red routes." Consequently, the return to regular studies is expected to be significantly delayed, while the children of the village have no practical alternative learning solution.

The only feasible solution at this time is remote learning. However, the village suffers from a severe shortage of computers, which could deepen educational gaps and hinder the return to routine.

Therefore, we request your urgent assistance in supplying:

• 80 computers for elementary school students
• 20 computers for high school students

This assistance will ensure basic educational continuity and equal opportunities for the village's students during this challenging period.

Sincerely,

Nigam Sharif
Head of Gush Halav Local Council

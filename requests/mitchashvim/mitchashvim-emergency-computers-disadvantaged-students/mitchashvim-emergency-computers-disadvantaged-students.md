## The Ask

Mitchashvim, a nonprofit that closes Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities, is requesting **$95,000 in emergency funding** to provide **350 students** with laptops and desktop computers so they can participate in nationwide remote learning during the current war with Iran.

Following devastating missile attacks and the rapid shift to nationwide remote education, thousands of children cannot attend school because they lack a computer. More than **168,000 households** in Israel do not have a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and their peers.

Mitchashvim has distributed **over 48,000 refurbished computers** to families in need across Israel to date. Its experienced operational network is well established and ready to respond rapidly to surging demand during times of national emergency, enabling the organization to refurbish and deliver computers quickly to the children who need them most.

In the past 48 hours, the organization has received urgent requests from municipalities and educational institutions serving displaced families and vulnerable communities, including:

- **Be'er Sheva** — missile strike on March 2, 2026 destroyed residential buildings and forced hundreds of families with children to evacuate immediately without basic equipment; many are currently in hotels in the city.
- **Gush Halav (Jish)** — a northern community approximately 4 km from the border, under full lockdown with "red" access routes and school shelter gaps; no practical alternative to remote learning.
- **Haifa** — municipal systems must remain fully operational to support residents affected by the emergency situation; there is a need for laptops, desktops, and monitors for municipal staff.
- **Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village** — students without computers cannot access their studies and remain disconnected from formal education.

The **immediate goal** is to provide **350 students** with computers, allowing them to reconnect to school and restore a sense of stability, routine, and resilience.

**Funding Requested:** **$95,000** to refurbish and distribute laptops and desktop computers to families who currently have no access to digital learning.

## Context & Background

Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks following the outbreak of war with Iran. As part of the national response, the education system has transitioned to **nationwide remote learning**, creating an urgent and painful reality: thousands of children cannot attend school simply because they do not have a computer.

More than **168,000 households** in Israel lack a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and their peers during one of the most difficult moments in the country's history. The risk is not only an educational gap but also social isolation, heightened anxiety, and long-term harm to children’s prospects.

**Mitchashvim (מתחשבים)** was established to close Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities. In normal times, the organization works to ensure equal educational opportunity; in times of crisis, this mission becomes even more critical. To date, Mitchashvim has distributed **over 48,000 refurbished computers** across Israel.

The organization’s operational network is well established and prepared to respond rapidly in emergencies, enabling swift refurbishment, logistics, and delivery of computers to households in need. In the **48 hours** before the appeal, Mitchashvim received urgent requests from municipalities and educational institutions serving displaced families and vulnerable communities in Be'er Sheva, Gush Halav / Jish, Haifa, and Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village.

- In **Be'er Sheva**, a direct missile strike on **02.03.2026** caused significant destruction and forced hundreds of families to evacuate immediately without basic equipment. A significant portion of families are now housed in city hotels. The affected population includes welfare families, immigrant families, families with children with disabilities, and elderly residents—communities that already face complex challenges. In an emergency, these gaps widen quickly and can lead to significant educational and social disconnection. The municipality identifies an **immediate need for 70 computers** for children staying in hotels to enable remote learning and preserve educational continuity.

- In **Gush Halav (Jish)**, located about **4 km from the border**, the community has been severely affected. Due to the ongoing campaign, the local education system is shut down: there are protection/shelter gaps in schools and across the village, and access routes to and from Gush Halav are designated as "**red routes**." Return to regular studies is expected to be significantly delayed, and the children of the village have no practical alternative. The only solution at this time is **remote learning**, but there is a **severe shortage of computers**, which risks deepening educational gaps and hindering any return to routine. The council requests **20 computers for high school students** and **80 computers for elementary students** (100 in total).

- In **Haifa**, the municipality must ensure the **continuous and efficient operation** of its services for residents during the war. To do so, it requires computer equipment for staff, including **50 laptops**, **50 desktops**, and **standard office monitors**. The laptops are requested with specific specifications (Intel Core i7, 512GB SSD, 16GB RAM, Windows 11 Pro). Desktop specifications are to be determined according to existing inventory and operational needs, and monitors are requested in standard sizes suitable for full office work, depending on stock availability.

- At **Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village**, students who lack computers cannot access their studies at all in the remote-learning environment, causing acute educational disruption and isolation.

Each computer provided is framed as more than just technology: it is a **lifeline to education, routine, hope, and resilience**, enabling participation in classes, connection to educational staff and classmates, access to learning materials, and a measure of emotional stability in a time of insecurity.

# Emergency Appeal: Ensuring Israeli Children Can Continue Learning During War

**Source / Website:** www.mitchashvim.org.il  
**Date:** March 6, 2026  

**Dear Friends and Partners,**

In times of war, one of the most powerful ways to protect a child's future is to ensure they can continue learning.

Over the past week, Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks following the outbreak of war with Iran. At the same time, Israel's education system has transitioned to nationwide remote learning, creating an urgent and painful reality: thousands of children cannot attend school simply because they do not have a computer.

More than **168,000 households** in Israel lack a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and their peers during one of the most difficult moments in the country's history.

Our nonprofit initiative **Mitchashvim (מתחשבים)** was established to close Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities. In normal times, we work to ensure equal educational opportunity. In times of crisis, this mission becomes even more critical.

To date, we have distributed **over 48,000 refurbished computers** to families in need across Israel. Our experienced operational network is well established and ready to respond rapidly to surging demand during times of national emergency, enabling us to deliver computers quickly to the children who need them most.

In the **past 48 hours** alone, we have received urgent requests from municipalities and educational institutions serving displaced families and vulnerable communities, including:

- **Be'er Sheva**, where a missile strike destroyed residential buildings and forced families with children into temporary housing (many in city hotels).
- **Gush Halav (Jish)**, a northern community under full lockdown near the border, with access routes designated as "red routes."
- **Haifa**, whose municipality must remain fully operational to support residents affected by the emergency situation.
- **Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village**, where students without computers cannot access their studies.

Our immediate goal is to provide **350 students** with computers, allowing them to reconnect to school and restore a sense of stability during an otherwise chaotic time.

To meet these urgent needs, we are seeking **$95,000** to refurbish and distribute laptops and desktop computers to families who currently have no access to digital learning.

Each computer distributed represents more than technology. It is a lifeline to education, routine, hope, and resilience.

With deep appreciation,  
Adv. Shelly Dvir  
Chief Executive Officer  
Mitchashvim  
*(organization name appears as "Metkhashvim" in one version of the appeal)*

#### Be'er Sheva Municipality (04 March 2026) — Hebrew Letter (Translated Summary)

**Subject / Re:** Urgent need for computers for evacuated students following the missile strike in Be'er Sheva

On **02.03.2026**, the city of **Be'er Sheva** suffered a **direct missile hit** that caused significant destruction and affected hundreds of families who were forced to evacuate immediately without basic equipment. A significant portion of these families are now staying in hotels in the city, trying to pick up the pieces and restore a sense of security and routine for their children.

The affected population includes **welfare families**, **immigrant families**, **families with children with disabilities**, and **elderly residents**—communities that already face complex challenges. In an emergency, these gaps widen quickly and can lead to significant educational and social disconnection.

The municipality is working with all available tools to ensure **educational continuity** for evacuated children. For them, a computer is not a luxury; it is an **anchor**. It enables participation in remote learning, contact with educational staff and classmates, access to learning materials, and emotional support. Beyond the pedagogical aspect, the computer provides **stability, certainty, and routine** in a reality of insecurity.

**Immediate need:**  
There is an **immediate need for 70 computers** for children staying in the hotels. Without a rapid response, these students may face a significant learning gap and a deepening sense of disconnection and anxiety.

**Contact / Signatory:**  
Shani Ora-Dotan, Be'er Sheva Municipality

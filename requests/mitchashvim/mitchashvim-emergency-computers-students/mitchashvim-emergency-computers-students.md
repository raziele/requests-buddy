# Emergency Appeal: Ensuring Israeli Children Can Continue Learning During War

**www.mitchashvim.org.il**  
**March 6, 2026**

Dear Friends and Partners,

In times of war, one of the most powerful ways to protect a child's future is to ensure they can continue learning.

Over the past week, Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks following the outbreak of war with Iran. At the same time, Israel's education system has transitioned to nationwide remote learning, creating an urgent and painful reality: thousands of children cannot attend school simply because they do not have a computer.

More than **168,000 households** in Israel lack a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and their peers during one of the most difficult moments in the country's history.

Our nonprofit initiative **Mitchashvim** was established to close Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities. In normal times we work to ensure equal educational opportunity. In times of crisis, this mission becomes even more critical.

To date, we have distributed **over 48,000 refurbished computers** to families in need across Israel. Our experienced operational network is well established and ready to respond rapidly to surging demand during times of national emergency, enabling us to deliver computers quickly to the children who need them most.

In the past 48 hours alone, we have received urgent requests from municipalities and educational institutions serving displaced families and vulnerable communities, including:

- **Be'er Sheva**, where a missile strike destroyed residential buildings and forced families with children into temporary housing
- **Gush Halav**, a northern community under full lockdown near the border
- **Haifa**, supporting residents affected by the emergency situation
- **Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village**, where students without computers cannot access their studies

Our immediate goal is to provide **350 students** with computers, allowing them to reconnect to school and restore a sense of stability during an otherwise chaotic time.

To meet these urgent needs, we are seeking **$95,000** to refurbish and distribute laptops and desktop computers to families who currently have no access to digital learning.

Each computer distributed represents more than technology. It is a lifeline to education, routine, hope, and resilience.

With deep appreciation,  
Adv. Shelly Dvir  
Chief Executive Officer  
Mitchashvim

#### Be'er Sheva Municipality (04 March 2026) — Hebrew

**Subject:** Urgent need for computers for evacuated students following the missile strike in Be'er Sheva

On 02.03.2026 the city of Be'er Sheva suffered a direct missile hit that caused significant destruction and affected hundreds of families who were forced to evacuate immediately without basic equipment. A significant portion of the families are now in hotels in the city, trying to pick up the pieces and restore a sense of security and routine for their children.

The affected population includes welfare families, immigrant families, families with children with disabilities, and elderly—communities that already face complex challenges. In emergency, these gaps widen quickly and can lead to significant educational and social disconnection.

The municipality is working with all available tools to ensure educational continuity for evacuated children. For them, a computer is not a luxury; it is an anchor. It enables participation in remote learning, contact with educational staff and classmates, access to learning materials, and emotional support. Beyond the pedagogical aspect, the computer provides stability, certainty, and routine in a reality of insecurity.

At present there is an **immediate need for 70 computers** for children in the hotels. Without a rapid response, these students may face a significant learning gap and deepening sense of disconnection and anxiety.

Signed: Shani Ora-Dotan, Be'er Sheva Municipality

---

#### Gush Halav (Jish) Local Council (4 March 2026) — Hebrew

**Subject:** Urgent request for immediate assistance for the children of Gush Halav — computers for remote learning

Gush Halav, located only ~4 km from the border, has suffered severely recently. Due to the ongoing campaign, the education system is shut down: there are shelter gaps in the schools and across the village, and access routes to and from Gush Halav are defined as "red" routes. Return to regular studies is expected to be significantly delayed, while the children of the village have no practical alternative.

The only possible solution at this time is **remote learning**, but the village has a **severe shortage of computers**, which may deepen educational gaps and hinder return to routine.

Request:
- **20 computers** for high school students
- **80 computers** for elementary students

Signed: Najem Sharif, Head of Gush Halav Local Council

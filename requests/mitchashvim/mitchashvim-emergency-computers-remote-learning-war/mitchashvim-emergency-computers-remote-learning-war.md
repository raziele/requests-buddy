## The Ask

Mitchashvim, a nonprofit that closes Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities, is requesting $95,000 in emergency funding to provide 350 students with computers so they can participate in nationwide remote learning during the current war. Following missile attacks and the shift to remote education, thousands of children cannot attend school because they lack a computer. The organization has distributed over 48,000 computers to date and has received urgent requests in the past 48 hours from Be'er Sheva (where a missile strike destroyed buildings and displaced families), Gush Halav (under lockdown near the northern border), Haifa, and Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village.

**Funding Requested:** $95,000

## Context & Background

Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks following the outbreak of war with Iran. The education system has transitioned to nationwide remote learning. More than 168,000 households in Israel lack a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and peers. Mitchashvim was established to close Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers; to date it has distributed over 48,000 computers across Israel and has an established operational network to respond rapidly during national emergency.

In the past 48 hours the organization has received urgent requests from municipalities and educational institutions: Be'er Sheva, where a missile strike on March 2, 2026 destroyed residential buildings and forced families with children into temporary housing (many in city hotels); Gush Halav, a northern community approximately 4 km from the border, under full lockdown with schools closed and "red" access routes, with no practical alternative for students; Haifa, supporting residents affected by the emergency; and Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village, where students without computers cannot access their studies.

The immediate goal is to provide 350 students with refurbished laptops and desktop computers so they can reconnect to school and restore stability. The requested $95,000 would fund refurbishment and distribution to families with no current access to digital learning. The PDF includes formal Hebrew letters from Be'er Sheva municipality (requesting 70 computers for evacuated children in hotels), Gush Halav local council (20 for high school and 80 for elementary students), and Haifa municipality (50 laptops with specified specs, 50 desktops, and monitors for municipal operations).

### Emergency Appeal Ensuring Israeli Children Can Continue Learning During War.pdf

**Source:** www.mitchashvim.org.il  
**Date:** March 6, 2026  
**Subject:** Emergency Appeal: Ensuring Israeli Children Can Continue Learning During War

**Dear Friends and Partners,**

In times of war, one of the most powerful ways to protect a child's future is to ensure they can continue learning.

Over the past week, Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks following the outbreak of war with Iran. At the same time, Israel's education system has transitioned to nationwide remote learning, creating an urgent and painful reality: thousands of children cannot attend school simply because they do not have a computer.

More than **168,000 households** in Israel lack a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and their peers during one of the most difficult moments in the country's history.

Our nonprofit initiative **Mitchashvim** was established to close Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities. In normal times we work to ensure equal educational opportunity. In times of crisis, this mission becomes even more critical.

To date, we have distributed **over 48,000 refurbished computers** to families in need across Israel. Our experienced operational network is well established and ready to respond rapidly to surging demand during times of national emergency.

In the **past 48 hours** alone, we have received urgent requests from municipalities and educational institutions serving displaced families and vulnerable communities, including:

- **Be'er Sheva** — missile strike destroyed residential buildings and forced families with children into temporary housing
- **Gush Halav** — northern community under full lockdown near the border
- **Haifa** — supporting residents affected by the emergency situation
- **Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village** — students without computers cannot access their studies

**Immediate goal:** Provide **350 students** with computers.

**Funding requested:** **$95,000** to refurbish and distribute laptops and desktop computers to families who currently have no access to digital learning.

*"Each computer distributed represents more than technology. It is a lifeline to education, routine, hope, and resilience."*

**Signatory:** Adv. Shelly Dvir, Chief Executive Officer, Metkhashvim

#### Be'er Sheva municipality (Hebrew letter — March 4, 2026)

**Re:** Urgent need for computers for evacuated students following missile strike in Be'er Sheva

On **02.03.2026** Be'er Sheva sustained a direct missile hit causing significant destruction and affecting hundreds of families who were forced to evacuate immediately without basic equipment. A significant portion of families are now in hotels in the city, trying to pick up the pieces and restore a sense of security and routine for their children.

The affected population includes welfare families, immigrant families, families with children with disabilities, and elderly — communities that already face complex challenges. In emergency, these gaps widen quickly and can lead to significant educational and social disconnection.

**Immediate need:** **70 computers** for children staying in hotels. Without a rapid response, these students may face a significant learning gap and deepening sense of disconnection and anxiety.

**Contact:** Shani Ora-Dotan, Be'er Sheva Municipality

---

#### Gush Halav local council (Hebrew letter — 4/3/2026)

**Re:** Urgent request for immediate assistance for children of Gush Halav — computers for remote learning

Gush Halav, approximately **4 km from the border**, has been severely affected. Due to the ongoing campaign, the education system is shut down: there are protection gaps in schools and in the village generally, and access routes to and from Gush Halav are defined as "red routes." Return to regular studies is expected to be significantly delayed, and village children have no practical alternative.

The only possible solution at this time is **remote learning**, but there is a **severe shortage of computers** in the village, which could deepen educational gaps.

**Requested:**
- **20 computers** for high school students
- **80 computers** for elementary students

**Contact:** Niv Sharif, Head of Gush Halav Council

---

#### Haifa municipality (Hebrew letter — March 5, 2026)

**Re:** Donation of computers to Haifa municipality

In light of the current state of war and accompanying challenges, request for assistance in computer equipment to ensure continuous and efficient operation of the municipality for the benefit of Haifa residents.

**Municipal needs:**
1. **50 laptops** — Spec required: Intel Core i7, 512GB SSD storage, 16GB RAM, Windows 11 Pro
2. **50 desktops** — Spec to be provided according to existing inventory and operational needs
3. **Monitors** — Standard size for full office work, according to stock availability

**Contact:** Dr. Galit Rand, Office of the CEO of the Municipality

## The Ask

Mitchashvim, a nonprofit that provides refurbished computers to close Israel's digital divide, is requesting **$95,000** to provide 350 computers to students affected by the current emergency. Following the transition to nationwide remote learning and recent missile attacks, thousands of children cannot access school because they lack devices. The organization has received urgent requests from Be'er Sheva (evacuated families), Gush Halav (border community under lockdown), Haifa, and Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village. Funds would refurbish and distribute laptops and desktops so students can reconnect to school and restore stability. Mitchashvim has distributed over 48,000 refurbished computers to date and has an operational network ready to respond rapidly to surging demand.

**Funding Requested:** $95,000

## Context & Background

Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks following the outbreak of war with Iran, and the education system has transitioned to nationwide remote learning. More than 168,000 households in Israel lack a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and peers. Mitchashvim was established to close the digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities; in normal times it ensures equal educational opportunity, and in crisis the mission becomes more critical. To date it has distributed over 48,000 refurbished computers across Israel and has an operational network ready to respond rapidly.

In the past 48 hours the organization received urgent requests from municipalities and educational institutions: **Be'er Sheva** — where a missile strike destroyed residential buildings and forced families with children into temporary housing; **Gush Halav** — a northern community under full lockdown near the border; **Haifa** — supporting residents affected by the emergency; and **Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village** — where students without computers cannot access their studies. The immediate goal is to provide 350 students with computers to reconnect to school and restore a sense of stability. Each computer is framed as a lifeline to education, routine, hope, and resilience. Without a rapid response, affected students risk significant learning gaps and deepened anxiety and disconnection.

**Be'er Sheva municipality (Hebrew) — 04 March 2026**

- Re: Urgent need for computers for evacuated students following missile strike in Be'er Sheva.
- On **02.03.2026** Be'er Sheva suffered a direct missile hit causing significant destruction and affecting hundreds of families who were evacuated immediately without basic equipment. Many families are now in hotels in the city, trying to pick up the pieces and restore a sense of security and routine for their children.
- Affected population: welfare families, immigrant families, families with children with disabilities, elderly — communities that already face complex challenges; in emergency these gaps widen and can lead to significant educational and social disconnection.
- For evacuated children, a computer is an anchor: remote learning, contact with educators and classmates, access to materials, emotional support, and stability in an insecure reality.
- **Immediate need:** 70 computers for children in hotels. Without a rapid response, students risk a significant learning gap and deeper disconnection and anxiety.
- Signed: Shani Ora-Dotan, Be'er Sheva municipality.

**Gush Halav (Jish) local council (Hebrew) — 4 March 2026**

- Re: Urgent request for immediate assistance for children of Gush Halav — computers for remote learning.
- Jish is about 4 km from the border and has been severely affected. Due to the ongoing campaign, the education system is shut down: shelter gaps in schools and across the village; traffic routes to and from Jish defined as "red routes." Return to regular schooling is expected to be significantly delayed, with no practical alternative for the children.
- Only possible solution at this time is remote learning, but there is a severe shortage of computers in the village, which may deepen learning gaps and hinder return to routine.
- **Requested:** 20 computers for high school students; 80 computers for elementary students (100 total). Assistance would ensure basic learning continuity and equal opportunities for the children during this challenging period.
- Signed: Najem Sharif, Head of Gush Halav Council.

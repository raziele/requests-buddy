## The Ask

Mitchashvim, a nonprofit that closes Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities, is requesting $95,000 to provide 350 students with computers so they can participate in nationwide remote learning during the current war. Following missile attacks and the shift to remote education, over 168,000 households in Israel lack a computer; the organization has received urgent requests from Be'er Sheva (families displaced after a direct missile strike), Gush Halav (under lockdown near the border), Haifa, and Ayanot. Mitchashvim has distributed over 48,000 computers to date and is seeking funds to refurbish and distribute laptops and desktops to families with no access to digital learning.

**Funding Requested:** $95,000

## Context & Background

Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks following the outbreak of war with Iran, while the education system has moved to nationwide remote learning. More than 168,000 households in Israel lack a computer, leaving thousands of children unable to attend school. Mitchashvim was established to close this digital divide; in normal times it ensures equal educational opportunity, and in crisis its mission becomes critical. To date it has distributed over 48,000 refurbished computers and has an operational network ready to respond rapidly.

In the past 48 hours alone, municipalities and institutions have sent urgent requests. In Be'er Sheva, a missile strike on March 2, 2026 destroyed residential buildings and forced families into temporary hotel housing; the municipality has requested 70 computers immediately for evacuated children to prevent learning gaps and isolation. Gush Halav, a northern community ~4 km from the border with movement corridors defined as "red lines," has no safe return to in-person school; the only option is remote learning, but there is a severe shortage of computers. The local council has requested 20 computers for high school students and 80 for elementary students. The Haifa municipality has requested 50 laptops (spec: Intel Core i7, 512GB SSD, 16GB RAM, Windows 11 Pro), 50 desktops, and standard office monitors to ensure continuous municipal operation for residents.

Each computer is framed as a lifeline to education, routine, hope, and resilience. The immediate goal is to provide 350 students with computers; without a rapid response, affected students risk significant learning gaps and deepened anxiety and disconnection.

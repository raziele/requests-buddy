## The Ask

Gedrot Regional Council presents a strategic community-development funding request to scale three initiatives: an inclusive theater program for youth with and without special needs, a youth entrepreneurship club for teens lacking belonging and meaningful frameworks, and a special-families center providing resilience support and practical services to families raising children with disabilities. The council states it is investing municipal resources but needs external partners to complete infrastructure adaptation, staffing, and multi-year program implementation.

**Funding Requested:** NIS 1,600,000 total stated project costs

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Dream Theater (inclusive theater group institutionalization): NIS 400,000
- Youth Entrepreneurship Club (facility adaptation, staff, ongoing programming): NIS 500,000
- Center for Special Families (facility adaptation, staff, annual/multi-year plan): NIS 700,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** External partnerships and collaboration to help establish and sustain the three initiatives.

## Context & Background

The attached proposal describes Gedrot Regional Council as a rural authority in the Lachish region undergoing a growth and development phase while aiming to preserve its agricultural and community character. It outlines a 2025 strategic process led by the council head with resident and local leadership participation, resulting in six focus areas: identity, community, education, services and infrastructure, demography, and agriculture/business/economy. Core values include agriculture, environmental stewardship, social-religious pluralism, community cohesion, intergenerational connection, and volunteerism.

Within this strategy, the council highlights three projects with immediate resource needs. The first, Dream Theater, began as a pilot integrating youth with and without special needs and now seeks formalization as a permanent theater framework, requiring production and operational resources. The second, a Youth Entrepreneurship Club, responds to a documented gap for teens not engaged by existing movements or activities, with the aim of strengthening belonging, leadership, and practical entrepreneurial skills in social, technological, business, and community domains. The third, a Center for Special Families, is designed as a regional support hub for families of children with disabilities, including respite, support groups, rights-navigation tools, and social belonging.

The council indicates designated buildings already exist for the latter two initiatives and that municipal and public-sector contributions are planned, but external fundraising remains necessary to enable full launch and sustainable operation. The proposal frames support as preventive and resilience-building: reducing youth risk linked to unstructured leisure time, improving family quality of life under sustained caregiving burdens, and deepening inclusive community life across Gedrot and the broader Southern Sorek area.

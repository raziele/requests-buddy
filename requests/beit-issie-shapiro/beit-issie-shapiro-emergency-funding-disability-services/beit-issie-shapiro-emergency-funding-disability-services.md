## The Ask

Beit Issie Shapiro is requesting emergency funding to sustain critical services for people with disabilities during the current security crisis. They have launched a national Emergency Hotline for professionals, caregivers and families; virtual support sessions for families in Hebrew and Arabic; emotional and practical support for staff; and educational/therapeutic support for children. They estimate $150,000–$200,000 (USD) per 10–14 days to sustain these services, plus significant income loss ($100,000/week) and salary continuity costs (~$230,000/week) for staff unable to work.

**Funding Requested:** $150,000–$200,000 per 10–14 days; income loss $100,000/week; salary continuity ~$230,000/week

## Context & Background

As Israel faces renewed attack, people with disabilities and their families face heightened fear and destabilization. Many cannot move quickly, interpret urgency, or seek safety independently; routines collapse and trauma intensifies. Beit Issie Shapiro has launched a national Emergency Hotline (WhatsApp then phone/video) for real-time emotional support and professional consultation; twice-weekly virtual support sessions for families in Hebrew and Arabic; partnership with the Shalem Foundation and Ministry of Welfare for open online guidance sessions nationwide; and emotional/practical support for staff (meals, activity packages, wellness sessions, care packages). They are also providing virtual classes and home visits where possible. Impact stories from the hotline illustrate severe anxiety among adults with disabilities and trauma in children (e.g. refusal to return to shelter after a fall). The organization is not reducing salaries for staff unable to work, at approximately $230,000 per week, and estimates emergency response at $150,000–$200,000 per 10–14 days.

# Emergency Update: Supporting People with Disabilities During the Current Security Crisis – March 2026

**Key programs:**
1. **Emergency Hotline** – National hotline (WhatsApp then phone/video) for professionals, caregivers and families; free real-time emotional support and professional consultation.
2. **Virtual Support Sessions** – Twice weekly for Beit Issie families (Hebrew and Arabic); partnership with Shalem Foundation, Ministry of Welfare and municipalities for four open online guidance sessions nationwide.
3. **Emotional and Practical Support for Staff** – Social workers at Ra'anana and Qalanswa; meals, children's activity packages, wellness (e.g. yoga), care packages.
4. **Educational and Therapeutic Support** – Virtual classes and activities; home visits; emergency virtual activities and take-home kits.

**Funding:** Emergency response estimated at **$150,000–$200,000 (USD) per 10–14 days**. Income loss (closure of services, clinics, postponed courses) at least **$100,000 per week**. Salary continuity for staff unable to work **~$230,000 per week**.

**Impact stories:** 43-year-old man with borderline intellectual disability and wheelchair – anxiety eased via hotline (breathing techniques). Single mother of three – child with autism and cerebral palsy refused to return to shelter after a fall; OT provided strategies (e.g. gradual reintroduction, soap bubbles in shelter).

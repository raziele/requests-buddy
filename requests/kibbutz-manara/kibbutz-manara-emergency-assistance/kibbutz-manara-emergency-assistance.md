## The Ask

Kibbutz Manara’s resource development manager is requesting emergency assistance during an active emergency period. The request has four components: (1) immediate security needs—tactical gear and personal equipment for the local response team (Kitat Konenut) that the army does not provide; (2) shelter equipment to make shelters functional and habitable, with emphasis on children and youth shelters for safety and emotional well-being during long stays; (3) resilience activities in shelters, such as emotional relief and “breathing space” activities via Zoom or by professionals willing to travel to Manara; and (4) practical support for reservist families, including vouchers for food and essential products. Two detailed tables attached in the email outline the first two categories; specific amounts are in the attached documents.

## Context & Background

The request comes from Kibbutz Manara’s Resource Development Manager, Iris Hen, following a prior conversation with the recipient (Barak). The community is in an emergency period and has identified urgent needs that fall into two attached detailed tables plus two additional support areas.

The first table covers immediate security needs: essential tactical gear and personal equipment for the local response team (Kitat Konenut). These items are not provided by the army and are needed for the community’s own security response. The second table covers shelter equipment—items required to make shelters functional and habitable, with a special focus on equipment for children and youth shelters to support both safety and emotional well-being during extended stays.

Beyond the itemized lists, the kibbutz is seeking support for resilience activities in shelters (emotional relief and “breathing space” activities delivered via Zoom or by professionals willing to travel to Manara) and for reservist families (practical assistance such as vouchers for food and essential products for families of those called up for reserve duty).

The sender expresses gratitude for the recipient’s goodwill and readiness to help and looks forward to a return to quieter times so the community can refocus on routine rehabilitation and growth. The primary language of the original message is mixed (Hebrew and English); key request content is in English.

## The Ask

Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village is requesting assistance with computer equipment for its students to enable remote learning. Many students lack suitable personal computers at home, which hinders their ability to participate in distance education and complete their architectural studies.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Donation of computer equipment

## Context & Background

Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village is an educational institution that includes a high school and a college for practical engineering. Many of its students come from peripheral areas and some are from disadvantaged backgrounds. The college's curriculum, particularly in architecture, relies heavily on specialized software that requires capable computers. The current situation, which necessitates remote learning, has highlighted a significant gap in equipment among the student body, impacting their ability to keep up with their studies.

### Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village Request

Date: March 22, 2026

To: Whom It May Concern,

Subject: Urgent need for immediate assistance with computers for remote learning for students at Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village

Dear Sir/Madam,

Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village is an educational institution and a supportive community for the students of the village, including the educational boarding facility, the high school, and the agricultural internship program. A significant part of the educational work at the village is the college for practical engineers, a program for "13-14", whose graduates integrate into professional roles during their military service and have opportunities for personal and professional development.

This year, 21 students are studying in the college for practical engineering at Ayanot. The college provides an opportunity for personal growth and the acquisition of a sought-after profession that opens up diverse employment opportunities. The studies for practical engineers at Ayanot are the only such program in the country that allows for living in the village, and along with the supportive team, it provides an opportunity for every high school graduate in the country.

Most of our students come from the periphery, and some are graduates of boarding schools with complex family backgrounds. As a learning program that trains graduates for the needs of the relevant industry, most of the learning is based on design software, according to the needs of the relevant industry, and in accordance with the requirements of the military service and the job market awaiting graduates after their studies. In the current situation, most students are facing a significant challenge in continuing their studies and working on projects remotely. Most of them do not have the option to use a suitable personal computer at home for their architectural studies.

We would appreciate any assistance with computer equipment or resources, in order to enable and support the continuation of an optimal learning process for our students.

Thank you in advance for any possible assistance.

Sincerely,

Guy Eilon
Director of Ayanot High School

## The Ask

Kibbutz Misgav Am is requesting urgent emergency donations to sustain civilian life under daily cross-border fire at Israel’s northern frontier. The community says it is operating with insufficient state support and needs immediate funding for a protected medical shelter, child and youth shelter activity spaces, fire-response capabilities, emergency squad equipment, and upgrades to public shelters in order to protect lives and maintain resilience.

**Funding Requested:** 525,000 NIS

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Construction of a medical shelter for immediate emergency treatment under fire: 160,000 NIS
- Leisure/activity spaces for toddlers and youth in shelters: 150,000 NIS
- Firefighting and emergency response solutions: 75,000 NIS
- Equipment for the local emergency defense squad: 70,000 NIS
- Equipment for public shelters: 70,000 NIS

**Non-Financial Ask:** Share and distribute the fundraising message to broaden support

## Context & Background

The message describes Kibbutz Misgav Am as being directly on the border fence and under daily fire with little to no warning time, creating acute risk for civilians. Community members report living and raising children in shelters, and they reference a recent painful loss of a community member, underscoring both physical danger and emotional strain.

According to the appeal, residents believe existing state responses are insufficient and that they must secure immediate, community-led solutions to continue living safely in place. They frame the campaign as urgent, life-saving support rather than long-term development.

The requested budget totals 525,000 NIS and is focused on critical protection and continuity needs: a medical shelter (160,000 NIS), protected activity spaces for toddlers and youth (150,000 NIS), firefighting/emergency systems (75,000 NIS), emergency squad equipment (70,000 NIS), and public shelter equipment (70,000 NIS). The email asks recipients to donate quickly and amplify the campaign so the community can endure and continue safeguarding the northern border area.

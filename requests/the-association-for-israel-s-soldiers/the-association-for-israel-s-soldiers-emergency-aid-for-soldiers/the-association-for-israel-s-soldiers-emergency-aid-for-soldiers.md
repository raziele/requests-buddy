## The Ask

The Association for Israel's Soldiers, the sole IDF-authorized nonprofit for soldier welfare, is requesting support for its Emergency Relief Fund / Operation Roaring Lion relief efforts in response to the escalation that began on 28 February 2026 during the prolonged Swords of Iron context (since October 7, 2023). The Emergency Relief Fund enables the IDF and Israel's Ministry of Defense to procure essential supplies without delay, with 100% of donations going directly to soldiers and their families and no administrative overhead.

The fund provides immediate assistance in several main areas:

1. **Basic personal necessities & hygiene** for soldiers in the field: male and female hygiene kits; non-military clothing and personal non-tactical equipment to maintain basic dignity and comfort during extended deployments without access to showers.
2. **Physical & mental recharge ("refresh")**: pop-up respite services at bases (refresh tents, food trucks, and related activities) providing nourishing meals, media entertainment, diversions, hot showers, haircuts, and laundry to re-energize units and boost morale.
3. **Life-saving medical equipment & ambulances**: life-preserving medical gear for combat paramedics and fully equipped military ambulances (Mercedes Sprinter) and mobile medical units to evacuate soldiers and provide emergency medical response.
4. **Financial aid via vouchers for impacted families and soldiers**: evacuated families whose homes were damaged; families of moderately or seriously injured soldiers during hospitalization and rehabilitation; and bereaved families during shiva. Vouchers are redeemable at approximately 60 store chains across Israel for food, clothing, electronics, and basic household needs.

One source also includes a **non-financial ask**: to forward the Roaring Lion portfolio to other federations or allow the Association to send it directly.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

## Context & Background

### IDF Mission & Association Role

The Israel Defense Forces (IDF), Israel's military, was founded to defend the country, shield its residents, protect its independence, and foil attempts by its enemies to disrupt routine life. As the foundation of Israel's Ministry of Defense, the IDF is directed by Israel's democratic civil government. Soldiers are obligated to protect the State of Israel, comply with IDF commands and values, abide by Israeli law, uphold human dignity, and respect Israel's principles as a Jewish and democratic state.

The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the **sole organization authorized by the IDF** to receive and distribute donations for the welfare and well-being of IDF soldiers. It supports, empowers, and cares for soldiers before, during, and after their military service, with numerous programs made possible by donors in Israel and worldwide. Across all three documents, it is emphasized that **100% of donations go directly to beneficiaries** (soldiers and their families), with **no overhead costs and no administrative delays**.

### Operation Roaring Lion

On Saturday, 28 February 2026, IDF and U.S. military forces launched **Operation Roaring Lion**, a broad, coordinated campaign to significantly degrade the Iranian regime and eliminate existential threats to Israel. The operation targeted dozens of military sites and followed months of close joint planning and long-term preparation across all IDF branches. It came in the context of the prolonged Swords of Iron War, which began on 7 October 2023.

Iran has continued advancing its nuclear and missile programs and supporting proxies along Israel's borders. In response, IDF forces remain on high alert across all fronts while the Israeli Air Force continues precision strikes based on accurate intelligence. The IDF urges the public to strictly follow Home Front Command instructions.

The Iranian attack struck Israel's civilian home front, causing fatalities, injuries, and extensive damage to residential buildings, including the homes of IDF service members. This led to the evacuation of many families and the identification of urgent needs for immediate assistance.

### Rationale for the Emergency Relief Fund

During times of crisis, Israel and its supporters worldwide intensify their resolve to support the IDF and its soldiers. While the IDF provides all gear and items needed for war and is solely responsible for procuring personal protective equipment and operational gear, **army policies allow the IDF to receive donations from an emergency fund** that can quickly meet emerging needs.

The Association's Emergency Relief Fund is designed to:

- Provide **immediate, flexible, and targeted support** where standard systems may be slower.
- Address **gaps in personal welfare and family support**, particularly for:
  - Soldiers in the field (hygiene, clothing, recharge)
  - Families of injured soldiers (travel, meals, lost income during hospitalization and rehabilitation)
  - Bereaved families during shiva
  - Evacuated soldiers and families whose homes were damaged
  - Vulnerable populations affected by the conflict
- Enable **rapid procurement and distribution** of life-saving medical equipment (including ambulances and mobile medical units) and welfare items.

## Program Components & Needs

### 1. Personal Hygiene Products & Apparel

**Goal:** Preserve dignity, comfort, and basic well-being for soldiers serving under harsh field conditions, often without access to showers for days.

**Items and Costs:**

- **Male hygiene kit:** 200 NIS
- **Female hygiene kit:** 150 NIS (each kit shared by 4 soldiers)

**Content & Rationale (combined):**

- Hygiene supplies (e.g., wipes, soap, basic toiletries) and **non-military clothing** that allow soldiers to cool down in summer and stay warm in winter during long stretches in the field.
- "Little pleasures" such as a quick wash-up, a chance to freshen up, and the ability to wipe away sweat can provide disproportionate relief and motivation, supporting endurance and resilience.

### 2. Physical & Mental Recharge ("Refresh" Activities)

**Goal:** Provide short, intensive respite experiences that restore physical and emotional energy, improve morale, and prepare soldiers for continued missions.

**Cost Structure:**

- **Approx. 100 NIS per soldier**
- **40,000–250,000 NIS per military unit**, depending on unit size

**Activities (pop-up recharge/refresh services):**

- Nourishing meals
- Media entertainment and diversions
- Hot showers
- Haircuts
- Laundry services
- Social time in a cheerful, relaxed atmosphere away from immediate combat stress

These are described as an **"oasis of relaxation"** where fighters can briefly step away from battlefield conditions, rejuvenate, and then return energized for the next mission. Current refresh activities prioritize units on the most active fronts, including IAF bases and air defense units, intelligence units, and Home Front Command bases.

### 3. Financial Aid for Evacuated Soldiers and Families

**Cost:** **2,000 NIS per family**

**Target Population:** Families (including those of IDF service members) whose homes were damaged in the Iranian attack and subsequent escalations, resulting in evacuations and property loss.

**Mechanism & Purpose:**

- Each affected family receives **vouchers totaling 2,000 NIS**.
- Vouchers can be used for **essential needs**, including:
  - Food
  - Clothing
  - Basic household supplies
- Aid is intended to address **immediate needs during the critical initial period** following evacuation and damage to the home.

The fund is explicitly framed as fulfilling a **moral responsibility** and the IDF's commitment to those who serve.

### 4. Financial Aid for Soldiers Injured Moderately or Seriously

**Cost:** **5,000 NIS per family**

**Target Population:** Families of soldiers injured moderately or seriously who require hospitalization, treatment, and often long-term rehabilitation.

**Mechanism & Purpose:**

- Within **24 hours of hospital admission**, the Emergency Relief Fund provides **5,000 NIS in vouchers** to the family.
- Vouchers are **redeemable at approximately 60 store chains across Israel**, for:
  - Food
  - Clothing
  - Electronics
  - Other essential goods and services
- Recognizes that parents, partners, and siblings frequently **take time off work**, incurring expenses for travel, meals away from home, and miscellaneous costs, on top of potential lost income.
- The fund aims to **lighten the financial burden** so families can focus on supporting their loved one through treatment and rehabilitation.

### 5. Financial Aid for Bereaved Families in Mourning

**Cost:** **2,000 NIS per family**

**Target Population:** Families of soldiers killed in action who are observing shiva (seven days of mourning).

**Mechanism & Purpose:**

- The Emergency Relief Fund provides **2,000 NIS in vouchers** to bereaved families.
- Vouchers can be used for:
  - Food
  - Clothing
  - Other **critical immediate needs** related to hosting visitors and sustaining the household during shiva.
- Vouchers are redeemable at about **60 store chains** throughout Israel, ensuring flexibility for families according to their specific needs.

Across documents, this is presented as **immediate, compassionate support** at a time of profound loss.

### 6. Life-Preserving Medical Gear

**Cost:** **Per item**

**Goal:** Equip combat medics and the IDF Medical Corps with the tools needed to **save lives in the field**.

**Use Cases (combined):**

- Keep injured soldiers **breathing**.
- Ensure **continuous blood circulation**.
- **Halt excessive bleeding**.
- **Monitor and protect** injured soldiers during transport and treatment.
- **Alleviate pain and discomfort** while medications are administered.

The gear is framed as essential for time-critical interventions when soldiers are **seriously wounded in combat**.

### 7. Military Ambulances & Mobile Medical Units

**Cost:** **650,000 NIS per fully equipped vehicle**

**Vehicle Type & Role:**

- **Mercedes Sprinter** IDF operational ambulance.
- High mobility to reach **outposts and bases** across varied terrain.
- Part of the IDF's **frontline medical response**, used to:
  - Evacuate soldiers to hospitals.
  - Provide medical response in emergency scenarios.
  - Support both IDF soldiers and, when required, **civilian populations** needing urgent medical treatment.

**Donor Recognition:**

- **Donor signage** will be prominently inscribed on the vehicle exterior following the war.

### 8. Well-Being & Individual Aid (Combined Category)

In the materials, the **main efforts** of the Emergency Roaring Lion Relief Fund are summarized as:

- Lifesaving medical equipment
- Bereaved families & wounded soldiers
- Well-being & individual aid (hygiene, recharge, evacuated families, and related welfare support)

The **well-being & individual aid** category effectively combines the hygiene, recharge, and family voucher programs, focusing on **direct, tangible benefits** to individual soldiers and families rather than institutional or infrastructure projects.

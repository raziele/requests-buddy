## The Ask

The Association for Israel's Soldiers, the sole IDF-authorized nonprofit for soldier welfare donations, is raising funds through its Emergency Relief Fund (Operation Roaring Lion) following the security escalation from February 28, 2026. The fund supports: (1) basic personal necessities and well-being (hygiene kits, non-military clothing, physical and mental recharge services); (2) lifesaving medical equipment (military ambulances, mobile medical units); (3) support for impacted families—evacuated families (2,000 NIS vouchers), moderately or seriously injured soldiers' families (5,000 NIS within 24 hours of admission), and bereaved families during shiva (2,000 NIS). One hundred percent of donations go directly to soldiers with no overhead.

They run refresh/recharge facilities at bases in the current fighting focus (IAF, Intelligence, Home Front Command) and seek funding for hygiene and textile kits, refresh buildings and food trucks, vouchers for soldiers and families whose homes were damaged, and life-saving medical equipment. The attached portfolio is updated almost daily and is intended for sharing with other Federations.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

## Context & Background

On February 28, 2026, IDF and U.S. forces launched Operation Roaring Lion, a coordinated campaign against the Iranian regime. The operation targeted dozens of military sites; the attack has struck Israel's civilian home front, causing fatalities, injuries, and extensive damage to residential buildings, including homes of IDF personnel. IDF forces remain on high alert; the Israeli Air Force continues precision strikes. The IDF urges the public to follow Home Front Command instructions.

**Data (sources cite March 4 / 4.3.2026; one source has swapped figures for evacuated families vs. rockets—see note below):**
- Israelis injured: 1,325+ (1,325 in one source)
- Israelis killed: 13
- Families evacuated: 571+ in one source / 1,391+ in the other
- Rockets fired into Israel: 1,391+ in one source / 571+ in the other
- UIA into Israel: 1,500+

The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the only organization authorized by the IDF to receive and distribute donations for soldier welfare; 100% of donations go directly to servicepeople. The Emergency Relief Fund enables the IDF and Ministry of Defense to procure essential supplies without delay. Needs fall into: (1) Personal hygiene products and apparel—hygiene kits and comfortable non-military clothing for soldiers who cannot shower for days; (2) Physical and mental recharge—pop-up respite with meals, entertainment, showers, haircuts, laundry to boost morale (refresh buildings and food trucks at bases); (3) Life-preserving medical gear and military ambulances for evacuation and emergency response; (4) Support for affected families—vouchers for evacuated families (2,000 NIS), for families of injured soldiers (5,000 NIS within 24 hours of hospitalization), and for bereaved families during shiva (2,000 NIS), redeemable at ~60 store chains in Israel. The association frames this as moral responsibility and commitment to those who serve.

### Organization & mission

The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the sole IDF-authorized nonprofit for receiving and distributing donations for soldier welfare. 100% of all donations go directly to benefit servicepeople. The IDF was founded to defend the country, shield its residents, protect its independence, and foil attempts by its enemies to disrupt routine life.

### Operation Roaring Lion – context

On Saturday 28 February, IDF and U.S. military forces launched a broad, coordinated campaign to significantly degrade the Iranian regime and eliminate existential threats to Israel. The Israeli home front was hit—fatalities, injuries, damage to residential buildings, including IDF service members' homes.

### Emergency Roaring Lion Relief Fund – main efforts

1. **Lifesaving medical equipment** – military ambulances, mobile medical units, life-preserving medical gear.
2. **Bereaved families & wounded soldiers** – vouchers and emergency assistance.
3. **Well-being & individual aid** – hygiene, apparel, physical/mental recharge, evacuated families.

### The need – Personal hygiene products & apparel

- Male hygiene kit: 200 NIS. Female hygiene kit: 150 NIS (each kit shared by 4 soldiers).
- Comfort clothing for cooling/warming in field; comfort and hygiene for soldiers in the field; non-military clothing.

### The need – Physical & mental recharge

- Pop-up refresh buildings and food trucks: meals, media/entertainment, hot showers, haircuts, laundry. Cost: approx. 100 NIS per soldier (40,000–250,000 NIS per military unit, depending on size).

### The need – Financial aid for evacuated soldiers and families

- 2,000 NIS per family (vouchers for food, clothing, basic household supplies); for families whose homes were damaged in the Feb 28 attack. Redeemable at ~60 store chains in Israel.

### The need – Life-preserving medical gear

- Per-item costs; empowers medics for lifesaving interventions (breathing, circulation, bleeding control, monitoring, medication administration).

### The need – Military ambulances

- 650,000 NIS per fully equipped vehicle (e.g. Mercedes Sprinter). For evacuation and emergency medical response at outposts and bases. Donor recognition on vehicle exterior after war.

### The need – Financial aid for soldiers injured moderately or seriously

- 5,000 NIS per family in vouchers within 24 hours of hospital admission; redeemable at ~60 store chains (food, clothing, electronics).

### The need – Financial aid for bereaved families in mourning

- 2,000 NIS per family during shiva; vouchers for immediate expenses; redeemable at ~60 store chains in Israel.

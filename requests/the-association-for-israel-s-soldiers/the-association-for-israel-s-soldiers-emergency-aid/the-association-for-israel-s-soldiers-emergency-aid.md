## The Ask

The Association for Israel's Soldiers, the sole IDF-authorized nonprofit for soldier welfare, is requesting support for its Emergency Relief Fund in response to Operation Roaring Lion (launched 28 February 2026) and the ongoing Swords of Iron context. The fund provides immediate, zero-overhead assistance in three areas: (1) basic personal necessities and hygiene kits for soldiers in the field, (2) life-saving medical equipment including military ambulances and field medical gear, and (3) direct financial aid for evacuated families, moderately or seriously injured soldiers' families, and bereaved families during shiva. All donations go 100% to beneficiaries with no administrative costs.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Male hygiene kit: 200 NIS
- Female hygiene kit (shared by 4 soldiers): 150 NIS
- Physical & mental recharge (per soldier): approx. 100 NIS (40,000–250,000 NIS per military unit depending on unit size)
- Financial aid for evacuated soldiers and families (vouchers): 2,000 NIS per family
- Life-preserving medical gear: per item
- Military ambulance (fully equipped vehicle, Mercedes Sprinter): 650,000 NIS per vehicle
- Financial aid for families of moderately or seriously injured soldiers: 5,000 NIS per family
- Financial aid for bereaved families during shiva: 2,000 NIS per family

**Non-Financial Ask:** Forward the Roaring Lion portfolio to other federations (sender can send directly if preferred).

## Context & Background

On 28 February 2026, the IDF and U.S. military launched Operation Roaring Lion, a coordinated campaign against the Iranian regime following months of joint planning. The operation follows the prolonged Swords of Iron War that began on 7 October 2023. The Iranian attack struck the civilian home front, causing fatalities, injuries, and extensive damage to residential buildings, including homes of IDF service members.

The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the only organization authorized by the IDF to receive and distribute donations for the welfare and well-being of IDF soldiers; 100% of donations go directly to beneficiaries. Army policies allow the IDF to receive donations from an emergency fund that can deliver immediate solutions. The Emergency Relief Fund supports: hygiene and non-military apparel for soldiers in the field; life-saving medical equipment such as military ambulances (Mercedes Sprinter type) and mobile medical units; and support for impacted families—evacuated families, wounded soldiers' families, and bereaved families—through vouchers redeemable at about 60 store chains across Israel. Aid for evacuated families (2,000 NIS) and for injured soldiers' families (5,000 NIS within 24 hours of hospital admission) is designed to cover essential needs during the critical initial period. The Association frames the ask as a moral and operational necessity to support soldiers and their families "without delay" and with no overhead.

Emails were forwarded through Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia (Talia Lidar, Tamar Broady). The requesting contact Michal Cohen Amir (UFIS) reports that refresh activities are being run for units in the current focus of fighting—IAF bases and air defense, intelligence, and Home Front Command—and offers to update federations on refresh activities at various bases. The portfolio is updated almost daily.

## Israel Defense Forces (IDF) – Our Mission

The IDF, the State of Israel's military, was founded to defend the country, shield its residents, protect its independence, and foil attempts by its enemies to disrupt routine life. As the foundation of Israel's Ministry of Defense, the IDF and its strategies and tactics are directed by Israel's democratic civil government. IDF soldiers are obligated to devote every effort to protecting the State of Israel at all costs, even if it means putting their lives on the line. Military personnel are sworn to comply with the IDF's commands and values and to abide by Israeli law, uphold human dignity, and respect Israel's principles as a Jewish and democratic state.

The Association for Israel's Soldiers nonprofit is the sole organization that the IDF has authorized to receive and distribute donations for the welfare and well-being of IDF soldiers. 100% of all donations to the nonprofit go directly to benefit the servicepeople. The Association supports, empowers, and cares for Israel's soldiers before, during, and after their military service. Numerous programs established and conducted by the association are made possible by the generous donations of supporters, companies, and organizations in Israel and worldwide.

## Operation Roaring Lion – Context

On Saturday 28th of February, IDF and U.S. military forces launched a broad, coordinated campaign to significantly degrade the Iranian regime and eliminate existential threats to Israel. The operation targeted dozens of military sites and followed months of close joint planning and long-term preparation across all IDF branches. Despite previous setbacks, Iran has continued advancing its nuclear and missile programs and supporting proxies along Israel's borders. IDF forces remain on high alert across all fronts; the Israeli Air Force continues precision strikes based on accurate intelligence. The IDF will act wherever and whenever necessary to protect Israel's citizens and urges the public to strictly follow Home Front Command instructions.

## Letter from leadership

Dear Friends,

Your outpouring of support for Israel during the Swords of Iron War has strengthened our soldiers and communities as they fight for our lives and for Israel's very existence. We have no words to fully express our heartfelt gratitude for your unwavering dedication and your immediate response since October 7, 2023. We deeply value and cherish your steadfast commitment to stand with us.

**Operation "Roaring Lion" – Defending Israel**

In light of the security developments since February 28th 2026, the Association for Israel's Soldiers is taking proactive and decisive steps to support our soldiers in the days, weeks, and months ahead. Through the Association's Emergency Relief Fund, we enable the IDF and Israel's Ministry of Defense to procure essential supplies for soldiers without delay, including:

1. **Basic personal necessities** – hygiene supplies, non-military clothing, personal non-tactical equipment, and resources that provide physical and emotional respite.
2. **Life-saving medical equipment** – military ambulances, mobile medical units, and other critical life-preserving medical gear.
3. **Support for impacted families** – assistance for families evacuated from their homes, wounded soldiers and their families, bereaved families in mourning, and individuals from vulnerable populations, including financial vouchers for essential needs and emergency assistance grants.

100% of all donations go directly to benefiting soldiers — with no overhead costs and no administrative delays.

## The IDF donation policy

During times of crisis, Israel and its supporters worldwide strengthen their resolve to support and embrace the people of Israel, the IDF and its dedicated soldiers. The Israeli military provides soldiers with all gear and items needed for war, resupplying based on IDF-defined needs and the gaps that arise. The IDF is the sole organization responsible for procuring personal protective equipment for soldiers and gear required for military operations. Army policies allow the IDF to receive donations from an emergency fund that can deliver immediate solutions and benefit its soldiers.

## Emergency Roaring Lion Relief Fund – main efforts

- Lifesaving medical equipment
- Bereaved families & wounded soldiers
- Well-being & individual aid

## The need – personal hygiene products & apparel

- **Male hygiene kit**: 200 NIS
- **Female hygiene kit**: 150 NIS (each kit shared by 4 soldiers)

Little pleasures mean a lot for service members who cannot shower for days. A quick wash-up, a chance to freshen up, or a moment to wipe the sweat from their brow can be the relief and motivation that help forces endure. Comfortable personal clothing for cooling down in summer and staying warm in winter makes a difference during long stretches in the field.

## The need – physical & mental recharge

- **Cost**: approx. 100 NIS per soldier (40,000–250,000 NIS per military unit, depending on unit size)

Pop-up respite service: nourishing meals, media entertainment, diversions, hot showers, haircuts, laundry. Provides essential comfort and boosts morale. For a few hours, fighters experience an oasis of relaxation and an opportunity to lighten up with fellow soldiers in a cheerful atmosphere. Our forces return rejuvenated for the next mission.

## The need – financial aid for evacuated soldiers and families

- **Cost**: 2,000 NIS per family

Following the 28 February 2026 attack, which caused fatalities, injuries, and damage to residential buildings (including IDF service members' homes), the IDF identified an urgent need to provide immediate assistance. Each affected family receives vouchers totaling 2,000 NIS for essential needs such as food, clothing, and basic household supplies during the critical initial period. (One source refers to the operation as "Lion's Roar.")

## The need – life-preserving medical gear

- **Cost**: per item

Combat paramedics need resources for lifesaving interventions: keeping patients breathing, ensuring blood circulation, halting excessive bleeding, monitoring and protecting injured soldiers, alleviating discomfort during medication administration. When combat fighters are seriously wounded, time is of the essence; this equipment empowers medics to keep soldiers alive.

## The need – military ambulances

- **Cost**: 650,000 NIS per fully equipped vehicle

- Type: Mercedes Sprinter (IDF Operational Ambulance)
- Purpose: evacuate soldiers to hospitals, medical response in emergency scenarios, high mobility to reach outposts and bases. Through these ambulances, medical authorities provide optimal and available treatment to IDF soldiers and to citizens who need medical treatment.
- Donor recognition signage will be prominently inscribed on the vehicle exterior following the war.

## The need – financial aid for soldiers injured moderately or seriously

- **Cost**: 5,000 NIS per family

Within 24 hours of hospital admission, the fund gives 5,000 NIS in vouchers to the family. Vouchers can be used in about 60 store chains throughout Israel for food, clothing, electronics, etc. The fund steps in to support families and lighten their burden (travel, meals, lost income). During the Swords of Iron War, hundreds of soldiers joined the ranks of the wounded; when an injured fighter requires hospitalization, treatment and rehabilitation, family members rush to his or her bedside.

## The need – financial aid for bereaved families in mourning

- **Cost**: 2,000 NIS per family

Immediate support during shiva (seven days of mourning): 2,000 NIS in vouchers for food, clothing, or other critical needs. Redeemable in about 60 store chains throughout Israel. Hundreds of courageous troops have been killed in action in the Swords of Iron War.

## The Ask

The Association for Israel's Soldiers, the sole IDF-authorized nonprofit for soldier welfare, is requesting support for its Emergency Relief Fund in response to Operation Roaring Lion (launched 28 February 2026) and the ongoing Swords of Iron context. The fund provides immediate, zero-overhead assistance in three areas: (1) basic personal necessities and hygiene kits for soldiers in the field, (2) life-saving medical equipment including military ambulances and field medical gear, and (3) direct financial aid for evacuated families, moderately or seriously injured soldiers' families, and bereaved families during shiva. All donations go 100% to beneficiaries with no administrative costs.

## Context & Background

On 28 February 2026, the IDF and U.S. military launched Operation Roaring Lion, a coordinated campaign against the Iranian regime following months of joint planning. The operation follows the prolonged Swords of Iron War that began on 7 October 2023. As of 4 March 2026, the portfolio cites 1,325 Israelis injured, 13 killed, 571+ families evacuated, 1,391+ rockets fired into Israel, and 1,500+ UIA (unmanned aerial incursions) into Israel. The Iranian attack struck the civilian home front, causing fatalities, injuries, and extensive damage to residential buildings, including homes of IDF service members.

The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the only organization authorized by the IDF to receive and distribute donations for soldier welfare; 100% of donations go directly to beneficiaries. Army policies allow the IDF to receive donations from an emergency fund that can deliver immediate solutions. The Association's Emergency Relief Fund supports: hygiene and non-military apparel for soldiers in the field; life-saving medical equipment such as military ambulances (Mercedes Sprinter type) and mobile medical units; and support for impacted families—evacuated families, wounded soldiers' families, and bereaved families—through vouchers redeemable at about 60 store chains across Israel. Aid for evacuated families (2,000 NIS) and for injured soldiers' families (5,000 NIS within 24 hours of hospital admission) is designed to cover essential needs during the critical initial period. The Association frames the ask as a moral and operational necessity to support soldiers and their families "without delay" and with no overhead.

## Israel Defense Forces (IDF) – Our Mission

The IDF, the State of Israel's military, was founded to defend the country, shield its residents, protect its independence, and foil attempts by its enemies to disrupt routine life. As the foundation of Israel's Ministry of Defense, the IDF and its strategies and tactics are directed by Israel's democratic civil government. IDF soldiers are obligated to devote every effort to protecting the State of Israel at all costs. Military personnel are sworn to comply with the IDF's commands and values and to abide by Israeli law, uphold human dignity, and respect Israel's principles as a Jewish and democratic state.

The Association for Israel's Soldiers nonprofit is the sole organization that the IDF has authorized to receive and distribute donations for the welfare and well-being of IDF soldiers. 100% of all donations to the nonprofit go directly to benefit the servicepeople. The Association supports, empowers, and cares for Israel's soldiers before, during, and after their military service.

## The IDF donation policy

During times of crisis, Israel and its supporters worldwide strengthen their resolve to support the IDF and its soldiers. The Israeli military provides soldiers with all gear and items needed for war. Army policies allow the IDF to receive donations from an emergency fund that can deliver immediate solutions and benefit its soldiers.

## Emergency Roaring Lion Relief Fund – main efforts

- Lifesaving medical equipment
- Bereaved families & wounded soldiers
- Well-being & individual aid

## The need – personal hygiene products & apparel

- **Male hygiene kit**: 200 NIS
- **Female hygiene kit**: 150 NIS (each kit shared by 4 soldiers)

Little pleasures mean a lot for service members who cannot shower for days. Comfortable personal clothing for cooling down in summer and staying warm in winter makes a difference during long stretches in the field.

## The need – physical & mental recharge

- **Cost**: approx. 100 NIS per soldier (40–250k NIS per military unit, depending on unit size)

Pop-up respite service: nourishing meals, media entertainment, hot showers, haircuts, laundry. Provides essential comfort and boosts morale.

## The need – financial aid for evacuated soldiers and families

- **Cost**: 2,000 NIS per family

Following the 28 February 2026 attack, which caused fatalities, injuries, and damage to residential buildings (including IDF service members' homes), the IDF identified an urgent need to provide immediate assistance. Each affected family receives vouchers totaling 2,000 NIS for essential needs such as food, clothing, and basic household supplies during the critical initial period.

## The need – life-preserving medical gear

- **Cost**: per item

Combat paramedics need resources for lifesaving interventions: keeping patients breathing, ensuring blood circulation, halting excessive bleeding, monitoring and protecting injured soldiers, alleviating discomfort during medication administration.

## The need – military ambulances

- **Cost**: 650,000 NIS per fully equipped vehicle

- Type: Mercedes Sprinter (IDF Operational Ambulance)
- Purpose: evacuate soldiers to hospitals, medical response in emergency scenarios, high mobility to reach outposts and bases.
- Donor recognition signage will be prominently inscribed on the vehicle exterior following the war.

## The need – financial aid for soldiers injured moderately or seriously

- **Cost**: 5,000 NIS per family

Within 24 hours of hospital admission, the fund gives 5,000 NIS in vouchers to the family. Vouchers can be used in about 60 store chains throughout Israel for food, clothing, electronics, etc. The fund steps in to support families and lighten their burden (travel, meals, lost income).

## The need – financial aid for bereaved families in mourning

- **Cost**: 2,000 NIS per family

Immediate support during shiva (seven days of mourning): 2,000 NIS in vouchers for food, clothing, or other critical needs. Redeemable in about 60 store chains throughout Israel.

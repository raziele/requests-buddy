## The Ask

The Association for Israel's Soldiers (the sole IDF-authorized nonprofit for soldier welfare) is requesting support for Operation Roaring Lion relief efforts following the security escalation that began February 28, 2026. Main efforts are: (1) hygiene and textile kits for soldiers in the field, (2) refresh tents and food trucks for physical and mental respite at bases, (3) vouchers for soldiers and families whose homes were damaged in the attack, and (4) life-saving medical equipment including military ambulances. The association emphasizes that 100% of donations go directly to soldiers with no overhead.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Male hygiene kit: 200 NIS
- Female hygiene kit (shared by 4 soldiers): 150 NIS
- Physical & mental recharge (per soldier): approx. 100 NIS
- Recharge per military unit: 40,000–250,000 NIS (depending on unit size)
- Financial aid per evacuated family (vouchers): 2,000 NIS
- Financial aid per family of moderately/seriously injured soldier: 5,000 NIS
- Financial aid per bereaved family in mourning: 2,000 NIS
- Fully equipped military ambulance (Mercedes Sprinter): 650,000 NIS

**Non-Financial Ask:** Forward the Roaring Lion portfolio to other federations, or the sender can send directly to them.

## Context & Background

Operation Roaring Lion was launched on February 28, 2026, following prolonged fighting since October 7, 2023. The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the sole organization authorized by the IDF to receive and distribute donations for soldier welfare; 100% of donations go directly to soldiers. The emergency relief fund enables the IDF and Ministry of Defense to procure essential supplies without delay.

Current priorities are personal hygiene and apparel, physical and mental recharge (pop-up respite services at bases), financial aid for evacuated and affected families (vouchers for essential needs), and life-saving medical equipment including military ambulances. The attack on Israel's civilian home front resulted in fatalities, injuries, and damage to soldiers' homes; the association provides immediate vouchers (e.g., 2,000 NIS per evacuated family, 5,000 NIS to families of seriously injured soldiers within 24 hours of admission, 2,000 NIS to bereaved families during shiva).

The email was forwarded through Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia (Talia Lidar, Tamar Broady). The requesting contact, Michal Cohen Amir (UFIS), reports that refresh activities are being run for units in the current focus of fighting—IAF bases and air defense, intelligence, and Home Front Command—and offers to update federations on refresh activities at various bases. The portfolio is updated almost daily.

# ROARING LION OPERATION

## Israel Defense Forces (IDF) — Our Mission

The IDF was founded to defend the country, shield its residents, protect its independence, and foil attempts by its enemies to disrupt routine life. The Association for Israel's Soldiers nonprofit is the sole organization the IDF has authorized to receive and distribute donations for the welfare and well-being of IDF soldiers. 100% of all donations go directly to benefit servicepeople. The association supports, empowers, and cares for Israel's soldiers before, during, and after military service.

## Operation Context (as of 4 March 2026)

On Saturday 28 February, IDF and U.S. military forces launched a broad, coordinated campaign. Data cited: **1,325 Israelis injured**, **13 Israelis killed**, **571+ evacuated families**, **1,391+ rockets fired into Israel**, **1,500+** (UIA into Israel). The IDF urges the public to follow Home Front Command instructions.

## Emergency Roaring Lion Relief Fund — Main Efforts

1. **Lifesaving medical equipment**
2. **Bereaved families & wounded soldiers** (financial aid)
3. **Well-being & individual aid** (hygiene, recharge, evacuated families)

### The need — Personal hygiene products & apparel

- Little pleasures for soldiers who cannot shower for days: wash-up, freshen up, non-military clothing.
- **Male hygiene kit:** 200 NIS
- **Female hygiene kit:** 150 NIS (each kit shared by 4 soldiers)

### The need — Physical & mental recharge

- Pop-up respite service: nourishing meals, media, hot showers, haircuts, laundry. Cost: **approx. 100 NIS per soldier** (40,000–250,000 NIS per military unit depending on size).

### The need — Financial aid for evacuated soldiers and families

- Following the attack, vouchers for families whose homes were damaged. **2,000 NIS per family** for food, clothing, basic household supplies.

### The need — Life-preserving medical gear

- Combat paramedics need resources for lifesaving interventions: breathing, blood circulation, bleeding control, monitoring. Cost: per item.

### The need — Military ambulances

- **650,000 NIS per fully equipped vehicle** (Mercedes Sprinter). High mobility for outposts and bases; donor recognition signage on vehicle exterior following the war.

### The need — Financial aid for soldiers injured moderately or seriously

- Within 24 hours of hospital admission, **5,000 NIS in vouchers** to the family; redeemable at ~60 store chains in Israel.

### The need — Financial aid for bereaved families in mourning

- Immediate support during shiva: **2,000 NIS in vouchers** for food, clothing, and critical needs; redeemable at ~60 store chains.

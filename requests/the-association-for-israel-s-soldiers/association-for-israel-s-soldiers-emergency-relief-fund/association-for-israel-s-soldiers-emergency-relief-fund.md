## The Ask

The Association for Israel's Soldiers, the sole IDF-authorized nonprofit for soldier welfare donations, is raising funds through its Emergency Relief Fund (Operation Roaring Lion) following the security escalation from February 28, 2026. The fund supports: (1) basic personal necessities and well-being (hygiene kits, non-military clothing, physical and mental recharge services); (2) lifesaving medical equipment (military ambulances, mobile medical units); (3) support for impacted families—evacuated families (2,000 NIS vouchers), moderately or seriously injured soldiers' families (5,000 NIS within 24 hours of admission), and bereaved families during shiva (2,000 NIS). One hundred percent of donations go directly to soldiers with no overhead.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Male hygiene kit: 200 NIS
- Female hygiene kit: 150 NIS (each kit shared by 4 soldiers)
- Physical & mental recharge (pop-up respite): approx. 100 NIS per soldier (40,000–250,000 NIS per military unit)
- Financial aid for evacuated soldiers and families (vouchers): 2,000 NIS per family
- Military ambulance (fully equipped, e.g. Mercedes Sprinter): 650,000 NIS per vehicle
- Financial aid for families of moderately or seriously injured soldiers (vouchers, within 24h of admission): 5,000 NIS per family
- Financial aid for bereaved families in mourning (shiva, vouchers): 2,000 NIS per family

## Context & Background

On February 28, 2026, IDF and U.S. forces launched Operation Roaring Lion, a coordinated campaign against the Iranian regime. The attack has struck Israel's civilian home front, causing fatalities, injuries, and extensive damage to residential buildings, including homes of IDF personnel. As of March 4, 2026: 1,325 Israelis injured, 13 killed, 571+ families evacuated, 1,391+ rockets into Israel. The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the only organization authorized by the IDF to receive and distribute donations for soldier welfare; 100% of donations go directly to servicepeople.

The Emergency Relief Fund enables the IDF and Ministry of Defense to procure essential supplies without delay. Needs fall into three areas: (1) Personal hygiene products and apparel—hygiene kits and comfortable non-military clothing for soldiers who cannot shower for days; (2) Physical and mental recharge—pop-up respite with meals, entertainment, showers, haircuts, laundry to boost morale; (3) Life-preserving medical gear and military ambulances for evacuation and emergency response; (4) Support for affected families—vouchers for evacuated families (2,000 NIS), for families of injured soldiers (5,000 NIS within 24 hours of hospitalization), and for bereaved families during shiva (2,000 NIS), redeemable at ~60 store chains in Israel. The association frames this as moral responsibility and commitment to those who serve.

## Context & Background

On February 28, 2026, IDF and U.S. forces launched Operation Roaring Lion, a coordinated campaign against Iranian threats. The attack struck Israel’s home front, causing fatalities, injuries, and damage to residential buildings, including IDF service members’ homes. As of March 4, 2026, cited figures include 1,325+ Israelis injured, 13 killed, 571+ rockets fired into Israel, and 1,391+ families evacuated.

The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the sole organization authorized by the IDF to receive and distribute donations for soldier welfare; 100% of donations go to soldiers with no overhead. Through its Emergency Relief Fund it procures hygiene kits, life-saving medical equipment (including military ambulances and field medical gear), support for evacuated and wounded soldiers’ families, and bereaved-family assistance. The fund provides 2,000 NIS vouchers to families whose homes were damaged, 5,000 NIS to families of moderately or seriously injured soldiers within 24 hours of hospitalization, and 2,000 NIS to bereaved families during shiva.

The email from Michal Cohen Amir (UFIS) to Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia updates that refresh/recharge buildings are being run at bases in the current fighting focus—IAF, Intelligence, Home Front Command—and invites support for these efforts and for the portfolio’s main lines. The attached portfolio is updated almost daily and is intended for sharing with other Federations.

## The Ask

The Association for Israel's Soldiers, the sole IDF-authorized nonprofit for soldier welfare, is requesting support for its Emergency Relief Fund following Operation Roaring Lion (late February 2026). The fund provides immediate, no-overhead assistance to soldiers and their families: hygiene kits and personal necessities in the field, physical and mental recharge services, life-saving medical equipment and military ambulances, and financial vouchers for evacuated families, moderately or seriously injured soldiers' families, and bereaved families in mourning. 100% of donations go directly to beneficiaries.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

- Financial aid per evacuated family: 2,000 NIS
- Life-preserving medical gear: per item

## Context & Background

On Saturday, February 28, 2026, IDF and U.S. forces launched Operation Roaring Lion, a coordinated campaign against the Iranian regime following months of joint planning. The operation came after prolonged fighting since October 7, 2023 (Swords of Iron). As of March 4, 2026, the portfolio cites 1,325 Israelis injured, 13 killed, 571+ rockets fired into Israel, 1,391+ evacuated to Israel, and 1,500+ evacuated families. The Iranian attack struck the civilian home front, causing fatalities, injuries, and damage to residential buildings, including IDF service members' homes.

The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the only organization authorized by the IDF to receive and distribute donations for soldier welfare. Its Emergency Relief Fund allows the IDF and Ministry of Defense to procure essential supplies without delay. Army policies permit the IDF to receive donations from this emergency fund for immediate solutions. The association provides hygiene kits and non-military clothing, life-saving medical equipment (ambulances, mobile medical units), and support for impacted families—evacuated, wounded, and bereaved—through vouchers redeemable at about 60 store chains across Israel. Disbursement to families of injured soldiers occurs within 24 hours of hospital admission.

Donations go 100% to beneficiaries with no overhead or administrative delays. The request is framed as continued partnership after previous support during Swords of Iron, with urgency driven by the post–Roaring Lion security situation and ongoing high alert across all fronts.

## ISRAEL DEFENSE FORCES (IDF) — OUR MISSION

The IDF, the State of Israel's military, was founded to defend the country, shield its residents, protect its independence, and foil attempts by its enemies to disrupt routine life. As the foundation of Israel's Ministry of Defense, the IDF and its strategies and tactics are directed by Israel's democratic civil government. IDF soldiers are obligated to devote every effort to protecting the State of Israel at all costs, even if it means putting their lives on the line. Military personnel are sworn to comply with the IDF's commands and values. As such, soldiers are required to abide by Israeli law, uphold human dignity, and respect Israel's principles as a Jewish and democratic state.

The Association for Israel's Soldiers nonprofit is the sole organization that the IDF has authorized to receive and distribute donations for the welfare and well-being of IDF soldiers. This means that 100% of all donations to the nonprofit go directly to benefit the servicepeople. The Association for Israel's Soldiers is proud to support, empower, and care for Israel's soldiers before, during, and after their military service. Numerous programs established and conducted by the association are made possible by the generous donations of supporters, companies, and organizations in Israel and worldwide.

## OPERATION ROARING LION

On Saturday 28th of February, IDF and U.S. military forces launched a broad, coordinated campaign to significantly degrade the Iranian regime and eliminate existential threats to Israel. The operation targeted dozens of military sites and followed months of close joint planning and long-term preparation across all IDF branches. Despite previous setbacks, Iran has continued advancing its nuclear and missile programs and supporting proxies along Israel's borders, posing a threat to regional and global security. IDF forces remain on high alert across all fronts, while the Israeli Air Force continues precision strikes based on accurate intelligence. The IDF will act wherever and whenever necessary to protect Israel's citizens and urges the public to strictly follow Home Front Command instructions.

## THE IDF DONATION POLICY

During times of crisis, Israel and its supporters worldwide strengthen their resolve to support and embrace the people of Israel, the IDF and its dedicated soldiers. The Israeli military provides its soldiers with all the gear and items they need for war, resupplying based on IDF-defined needs and the gaps that arise. The IDF is the sole organization responsible for procuring personal protective equipment for soldiers and gear required for military operations. In light of the Swords of Iron War, army policies allow the IDF to receive donations from an emergency fund that can deliver immediate solutions to the IDF's needs and benefit its soldiers.

## THE EMERGENCY ROARING LION RELIEF FUND — MAIN EFFORTS

- Lifesaving medical equipment
- Bereaved families & wounded soldiers
- Well-being & individual aid

## THE NEED — PERSONAL HYGIENE PRODUCTS & APPAREL

- **Cost: 200 NIS (Male hygiene kit)**
- **Cost: 150 NIS (Female hygiene kit)** — each kit shared by 4 soldiers

Little pleasures mean a lot for dedicated service members who are fighting tirelessly to protect Israel's sovereignty. When soldiers cannot shower for days on end, a quick wash-up, a chance to freshen up, or even a moment to wipe the sweat from their brow can be the relief and motivation that help our forces endure. Comfortable personal clothing for cooling down in the summer and staying warm in winter can make a big difference during long stretches in the field.

## THE NEED — PHYSICAL & MENTAL RECHARGE

- **Cost: approx. 100 NIS per soldier (40,000–250,000 NIS per military unit**, depending on size)

When our forces sacrifice so much for us, they deserve quick breaks. The physical and mental recharge is a pop-up service that offers a welcome escape from the battlefield. For a few hours during this getaway, the fighters experience an oasis of relaxation and an opportunity to lighten up with fellow soldiers in a cheerful atmosphere. Nourishing meals, media entertainment, diversions, hot showers, haircuts and laundry help re-energize our valiant battalions. This initiative provides essential comfort, boosting soldier morale. Our forces return rejuvenated for the next mission at hand.

## THE NEED — FINANCIAL AID FOR EVACUATED SOLDIERS AND FAMILIES

- **Cost: 2,000 NIS per family**

On Saturday, February 28, 2026, Operation "Lion's Roar" was launched following a prolonged and intense period of fighting that began on October 7, 2023. The attack by Iran struck Israel's civilian home front, resulting in fatalities, injuries, and extensive damage to residential buildings across the country, including the homes of IDF service members. Out of moral responsibility and in fulfillment of its commitment to those who serve, the IDF identified an urgent need to provide immediate assistance to affected families. This support is intended to help them cope with the sudden loss of property and to meet their essential needs during the critical initial period. Each family whose home was damaged in the attack will receive assistance in the form of vouchers totaling 2,000 NIS for essential needs such as food, clothing, and basic household supplies.

## THE NEED — LIFE-PRESERVING MEDICAL GEAR

- **Cost: per item**

When combat fighters are seriously wounded, time is of the essence. The IDF Medical Corps team springs into action to keep patients alive. Combat paramedics need resources for lifesaving interventions. Life-preserving medical gear empowers medics to keep soldiers breathing, ensure continuous blood circulation and halt excessive bleeding. This equipment also helps monitor and protect injured soldiers and alleviate discomfort while medications are administered.

## THE NEED — MILITARY AMBULANCES

- **Cost: 650,000 NIS per fully equipped vehicle**

The IDF's military ambulances are intended to evacuate soldiers to hospitals and provide a medical response in emergency scenarios. They have high mobility capability to reach the variety of outposts and bases and are part of the medical response to any emergency military activity. Through these ambulances, the medical authorities are able to provide optimal and available treatment to IDF soldiers, as well as to the citizens of the country who need medical treatment. Vehicle type: Mercedes Sprinter. Donor recognition signage will be prominently inscribed on the vehicle exterior following the war.

## THE NEED — FINANCIAL AID FOR SOLDIERS INJURED MODERATELY OR SERIOUSLY

- **Cost: 5,000 NIS per family**

During the Swords of Iron War, hundreds of soldiers joined the ranks of the wounded. When an injured fighter requires hospitalization, treatment and rehabilitation, family members rush to his or her bedside. Parents, partners and siblings take time off from work; expenses can mount from travel, eating out and other inescapable outlays – on top of potential lost income. The Emergency Relief Fund steps in to support families of injured soldiers. Within 24 hours of hospital admission, the fund gives 5,000 NIS in vouchers to the family whose loved one was injured moderately or seriously. The vouchers can be used in about 60 store chains throughout Israel for food, clothing, or electronics.

## THE NEED — FINANCIAL AID FOR BEREAVED FAMILIES IN MOURNING

- **Cost: 2,000 NIS per family**

Hundreds of courageous troops have been killed in action in the Swords of Iron War. The Emergency Relief Fund extends immediate support to the family during shiva, the seven days of mourning. The association provides 2,000 NIS in vouchers for immediate expenses such as food, clothing, or other critical needs incurred by the mourning family. The vouchers can be redeemed in about 60 store chains throughout Israel.

## The Ask

HaOgen for Reservist Families is requesting funding to support reservist families during a period of increased mobilization due to an operation in Iran. They are providing immediate relief, meals, educational programs, and activity kits for children, and are seeking $200,000 to sustain and expand these efforts.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Immediate relief (meals and essential supplies) for families whose homes were hit by missiles: $650 per family
- Grocery coupons for meals for reservist families: 100,000 coupons
- Daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists: $900 per session
- Activity kits for children in bomb shelters: $12 per family

## Context & Background

Israel is experiencing a large-scale mobilization of reservists due to an ongoing operation. HaOgen for Reservist Families is experiencing unprecedented demand for its services, supporting families dealing with the overwhelming challenges of this situation. These challenges include ensuring children's safety in shelters, pet care, and childcare while parents are away or schools are canceled.

The organization has been actively expanding its volunteer support, matching volunteers with families in close proximity and extending services to new cities. They are running several new programs that require additional funding to address the immediate needs arising from the conflict.

The email details four specific programs needing financial support: providing immediate relief to families whose homes have been destroyed, increasing the provision of meals due to the high number of reservists drafted and the difficulty for single parents to prepare food, offering daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists to provide a fun activity and relieve parents, and supplying activity kits for children spending extended periods in bomb shelters. The organization is seeking a total of $200,000 to bolster these critical support efforts during this time of heightened emergency.

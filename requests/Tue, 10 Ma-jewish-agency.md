## The Ask

The Jewish Agency is requesting immediate funding to address emergency needs arising from the "Operation Roaring Lion" conflict. This includes financial assistance for victims of the attacks, support for new immigrants and elderly residents affected by the war, and aid for young people participating in Israel Experience programs who are stranded or need to return home.

**Funding Requested:** $10,100,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Fund for Roaring Lion (2000 immediate grants): $10,100,000
- Assistance for Amigour housing residents and Olim in absorption centers: $600,000
- Financial assistance for participants of Israel Experience Programs: $2,000,000

## Context & Background

Following a new war titled "Operation Roaring Lion" initiated by Iran's retaliation to an Israeli attack, Iran launched hundreds of long-range ballistic missiles causing significant damage to Israel's civilian population and infrastructure, resulting in ten lives lost and over 130 injuries. The Jewish Agency, in coordination with national emergency authorities, local municipalities, beneficiaries, and partners, has identified critical emergency needs. These include financial assistance for victims, support for those who have lost homes, and aid for vulnerable populations like the elderly and children. Additionally, the Agency is addressing the needs of new immigrants in absorption centers and elderly residents in Amigour housing who are unable to leave their homes, as well as providing activities for children in shelters and mental health assistance. Furthermore, assistance is requested for approximately 6,000 young people participating in Israel Experience programs who are stuck in Israel and may require temporary housing or financial aid to return to their countries of origin.

### Operation Roaring Lion - Jewish Agency Needs Document - Draft as of March 1, 2026

**Background:**
On Saturday, February 28, Israel, the United States, and Iran entered into a new war entitled "Operation Roaring Lion." Iran retaliated to Israel's attack with hundreds of long-range ballistic missiles, causing massive damage to Israel's civilian population and infrastructure, resulting in ten lives lost and more than 130 injured.

**Identified Emergency Needs:**

1.  **Emergency Fund for Roaring Lion:**
    *   **a. Financial assistance:** For bereaved families, injured parties, and property destruction (immediate and rehabilitation grants).
    *   **b. Special assistance and group interventions:** In partnership with municipalities, for evacuees and strengthening resilience.
    *   **c. Assistance for vulnerable populations:** Requiring extra support, such as the elderly or children.
    *   **Total Estimated Need:**
        *   300 immediate grants: $3,700,000
        *   500 immediate grants: $4,500,000
        *   1000 immediate grants: $6,300,000
        *   2000 immediate grants: $10,100,000

2.  **Jewish Agency Beneficiaries:**
    *   **a. Amigour Housing:** Residents cannot leave their homes; requesting food packages.
    *   **b. Olim in Absorption Centers:** Stuck in shelters; requesting activities for children and mental health assistance.
    *   **Total Estimated Need:** $600,000

3.  **Financial Assistance for Participants of Israel Experience Programs:**
    *   Assistance for 6,000 young people stuck in Israel, requiring temporary housing or financial aid to return home.
    *   **Total Need:** $2,000,000
    *   **Total Estimated Need (for 2000 participants):** $12,700,000

**Funding Sources:** The Jewish Agency is funded by Jewish Federations of North America/UIA, Keren Hayesod, foundations, and individual donors.

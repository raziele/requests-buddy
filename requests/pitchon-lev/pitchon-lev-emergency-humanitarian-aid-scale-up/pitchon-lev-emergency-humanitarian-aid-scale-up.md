## The Ask

Pitchon Lev is urgently requesting philanthropic support to scale its emergency humanitarian response during the current national crisis in Israel, including large-scale food and essential-aid distribution and mobile rights-fulfillment teams that help displaced families access emergency benefits and compensation. The organization reports rapidly increasing needs, active field operations with municipal welfare coordination, and immediate funding requirements to sustain and expand lifesaving support.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Program A – Emergency Lifeline Food and Aid (national emergency response budget): NIS 6,601,875 / US$ 2,136,529
- Program B – Rights Utilization Mobile Hubs (per team, first month): NIS 101,550 / US$ 32,864
- Program B – Rights Utilization Mobile Hubs (per team, each subsequent month): NIS 95,550 / US$ 30,922

**Non-Financial Ask:** Request to raise this appeal with philanthropists, donor-advised fund holders, and foundations (local and national) who may support urgent humanitarian and welfare efforts in Israel.

## Context & Background

Pitchon Lev describes a worsening national emergency in Israel, with missile impacts across neighborhoods, widespread disruption to schools and workplaces, and thousands of families evacuated from their homes. The organization emphasizes that vulnerable groups are bearing disproportionate harm, including elderly residents, people with disabilities, new immigrants, and families already under economic stress. It states that food supplies are running out for many households and that needs are increasing rapidly.

The organization reports that since the first day of the war it has maintained continuous field operations in coordination with municipal welfare departments across Israel, distributing food parcels and humanitarian assistance at scale. In parallel, it has deployed a Mobile Rights Utilization Hub in Jerusalem for evacuated families from Beit Shemesh and is deploying two additional hubs to support evacuees from Dimona and Arad in Dead Sea evacuation centers. These hubs provide hands-on help navigating benefits, grants, compensation, and other entitlements during displacement.

Pitchon Lev positions itself as an apolitical humanitarian actor with over 28 years of nationwide experience and support to more than 200,000 people annually through food security, rights fulfillment, and education initiatives. It highlights existing logistics infrastructure, professional teams, and municipal partnerships as enabling immediate implementation. The proposal frames support as time-critical and directly actionable, with indicative budgets of NIS 6,601,875 (US$ 2,136,529) for Program A, and Program B costs of NIS 101,550 (US$ 32,864) per team in the first month and NIS 95,550 (US$ 30,922) per team in each subsequent month.

#### Urgent Support Request
- Appeal asks for emergency support and proposes support for one or both of two response tracks:
  - Program A: Emergency Lifeline Food and Aid.
  - Program B: Emergency Mobile Hubs for Rights Fulfillment.
- Claimed direct outputs of donations: food parcels packed, trucks dispatched, families fed, and emergency benefits unlocked.

#### Program A – Emergency Lifeline Food and Aid
- Purpose: Emergency food parcels and essential aid for vulnerable families affected by the war.
- Delivery model: rapid distribution coordinated with municipal welfare departments.
- Components:
  - Emergency Passover food parcels for families identified by municipal welfare departments in impacted cities and vulnerable communities.
  - Nationwide distribution via Pitchon Lev logistics hubs, staff, and volunteers.
  - Prioritization of vulnerable groups: elderly residents, people with disabilities, new immigrants, and economically struggling families.
  - Additional kits (based on need): baby essentials, personal hygiene, and senior necessities.

#### Program B – Emergency Mobile Hubs for Rights Fulfillment
- Purpose: Field teams helping evacuees and affected families access emergency benefits and rights.
- Services include:
  - Face-to-face support by trained rights fulfillment experts.
  - End-to-end case management for families experiencing income loss, displacement, disability-related challenges, language barriers, and complex eligibility processes.
  - Practical assistance with emergency benefits, injury compensation, and social welfare benefits.
- Scalability notes:
  - Time in field can be expanded; current plan is 3 months in the field.
  - Number of teams can be expanded; currently 1 team in the field.
- Claimed outcomes: benefits accessed, claims submitted, entitlements secured, and guided pathways to renewed independence.

#### Organization Profile ("Who we are")
- Pitchon Lev describes itself as an apolitical Israeli humanitarian organization.
- More than 28 years of national-scale experience.
- Supports over 200,000 people annually.
- Program domains: weekly food security, rights fulfillment, and long-term educational initiatives.
- Claimed strengths: national logistics capacity, professional teams, and deep partnerships with municipalities and welfare departments.

#### Why Support Matters Now

- Direct coordination with municipal welfare departments to identify vulnerable families and avoid duplication.
- Emphasis on measurable outputs (parcels delivered, cases resolved).

## The Ask

Dror Israel requests **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and scale crisis response during the current escalation. Their network of **1,300 educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim** is providing educational, emotional, and trauma support for over **200,000** children, teens, and families in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, schools, and temporary childcare sites.

Support will cover emergency childcare for essential workers, a pop-up school at a hospital, activities at evacuee hotels and public shelters, trauma-informed pedagogy for their schools network, and resilience programming through movement and dance.

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school at hospital/on-site for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for schools network (10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools; staff, pedagogical development): $80,000
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa (staff): $10,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Introductions/recommendations to individuals, foundations, or emergency funds that may support this work; guidance to additional supporters; optional short presentation/briefing/update call (including with Detroit community or leadership) about field conditions and educator response.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, families across Israel are repeatedly moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and facing ongoing fear, disruption, and trauma. Dror Israel educators are delivering educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and homes, while coordinating with municipalities and government partners to adapt response in real time.

Through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming, Dror Israel is maintaining contact and support for over 200,000 participants nationwide, helping ensure children and families remain safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.

Current priorities include:
1. **Emergency childcare for essential workers** in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, plus a requested on-site hospital pop-up school so medical teams can work while children remain in safe structured settings.
2. **Daily educational-emotional activities at evacuee hotels/centers**, including support for evacuees from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva, plus distribution of clothing and supplies.
3. **Programming in public shelters** (including Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more), with trained educators and hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen emergency junior counselors using activity kits.
4. **Pedagogy of Hope trauma-informed school support** across 29 schools; includes support to Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva, which was struck by missile fragment damage while closed.
5. **Online resilience sessions through movement and dance** via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.

Sustaining educator hours, transportation, supplies, digital platforms, and coordination requires continued emergency operating support.

# Israel Needs Us Once Again. And We Need You!
Educators, Community Builders, Emotional First Responders – We Are There  
March 4, 2026

## Current Situation

As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis are again in bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and bracing for ongoing sirens. Dror Israel's trained educators are delivering educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms nationwide. In partnership with municipalities and government partners, they are assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses in real time.

Simultaneously, Dror Israel is activating a nationwide network of over 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. Sustained operations require continued support for educator hours, digital platforms, logistics, and coordination.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs

Dror Israel seeks **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to continue serving 200,000 participants, provide activities and emotional support for children and families in bomb shelters and evacuee settings, and respond quickly as needs evolve.

## Current Priorities

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers**
   - Childcare frameworks for children of medical staff, security personnel, and critical service providers in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva.
   - Settings include schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels.
   - **Funding gap: $125,000** — 10 staff, transportation, supplies.
   - Hospital request for on-site pop-up school to support working medical teams.
   - **Funding gap: $50,000** — 4 teachers, transportation, supplies.

2. **Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels/Centers**
   - Daily activities and emotional-educational support for evacuated children and youth (including Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva evacuees), plus distribution of clothing and supplies.
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** — staff, supplies, transportation.

3. **Activities in Public Shelters**
   - Programming for children in public shelters (schools, parking lots, subway stations) in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional locations.
   - Deployment of hundreds of trained Jewish and Arab teen emergency junior counselors with activity kits.
   - **Funding gap: $60,000** — 5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies.

4. **Pedagogy of Hope**
   - Trauma-informed pedagogical guidance and support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools.
   - Includes Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva, which sustained missile fragment impact while school was closed.
   - **Funding gap: $80,000** — staff, pedagogical development.

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance**
   - Online resilience sessions through Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.
   - Focus on emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** — staff.

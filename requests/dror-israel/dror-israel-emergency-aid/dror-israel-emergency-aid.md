## The Ask

Dror Israel requests emergency support for a two-week period during ongoing Iranian missile barrages, to sustain and scale educational, emotional, and trauma-support response across shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, and emergency childcare settings for essential workers’ children.

Two funding versions were submitted:

- **Version A (Mar 4): $250,000 total**
- **Version B (Mar 6): $360,000 total**

### Consolidated Funding Breakdown (all line items appearing across both versions)

- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels/centers (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- **Additional in Version B:** 200 structured activity kits for shelters (training and supplies): **$25,000**
- Pedagogy of Hope (trauma support/pedagogical development for schools network): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): **$10,000**

### Noted Difference

  - inclusion of the **$25,000 activity-kits line item**, and
  - broader/updated framing in the Mar 6 proposal indicating expanded emergency response scope.

## Context & Background

As missile attacks continue, families are repeatedly moving into bomb shelters, evacuating homes, and facing sustained trauma and disruption. Dror Israel reports a nationwide emergency deployment of trained educators providing real-time educational, emotional, and trauma-informed support in shelters, evacuee hotels/centers, schools, and homes.

The organization operates with:

- **1,300 trained educators**
- **14 educators’ kibbutzim**
- **200,000+ children, teens, and families** reached through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming
- coordination with local municipalities, government partners, and (in one version) Ministry of Education Youth and Society Administration

### Current Priorities (combined)

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers**  
   Active in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, including schools/community centers/boarding schools/evacuee hotels; includes requested hospital pop-up school.

2. **Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels/Centers**  
   Daily activities, emotional-educational support, and distribution of supplies/clothing for evacuated children and youth (including Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva).

3. **Activities in Public Shelters**  
   Programming for children in public shelters (Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional locations), with hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors deployed as emergency junior counselors.

4. **Structured Activity Kits (Version B detail)**  
   Rapid production/distribution of **200 kits** for public shelters/residential buildings, plus facilitator/volunteer/youth-leader training for safe operation.

5. **Pedagogy of Hope**  
   Trauma-informed pedagogical guidance across Dror Israel’s school network (10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools), including support to Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva after missile-fragment impact (school closed, no injuries).

6. **Resilience Through Movement and Dance**  
   Online resilience sessions via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa for emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.

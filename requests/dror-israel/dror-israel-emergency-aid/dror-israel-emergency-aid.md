## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and scale its crisis response during the current escalation. Its network of 1,300 trained educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim is providing educational activities, emotional and trauma support, and emergency childcare for over 200,000 children, teens, and families across Israel.

Programming includes:
- Emergency childcare for children of essential workers so medical, security, and critical service personnel can continue working
- A requested on-site pop-up school at a major hospital for medical teams' children
- Daily activities and emotional support for evacuees in hotels/centers
- Activities for families and children in public bomb shelters
- Trauma-informed pedagogy and support through the Dror Israel Schools Network
- Online resilience sessions through movement and dance

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school on-site at hospital (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for schools network (10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools; staff, pedagogical development): $80,000
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa; staff): $10,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Introductions/recommendations to individuals, foundations, emergency funds, or other supporters; willingness to provide a short presentation/briefing or update call (including with Detroit community/leadership) about on-the-ground educator response.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are again moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and facing repeated disruption and trauma. Dror Israel educators are delivering educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, and living rooms nationwide.

In partnership with local municipalities and government partners, Dror Israel is continuously assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted responses. At the same time, it is activating a nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to keep participants safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.

Current priorities include:
1. Emergency childcare frameworks in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva
2. A pop-up school requested at a major hospital so medical teams can work while children remain nearby in a safe setting
3. Daily activities and support for evacuees (including in Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva)
4. Activities in public shelters (Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more), including deployment of hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors with activity kits
5. Pedagogical and trauma-informed support for 29 schools; Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva was hit by a missile fragment while closed, with no injuries
6. Online resilience programming through movement and dance in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa

Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires continued emergency operating support.

# Israel Needs Us Once Again. And We Need You!

## Current Situation

As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, families are living in constant fear and uncertainty, experiencing repeated trauma as daily life is disrupted. Dror Israel's trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms across the country, addressing urgent needs in real time so children are not left alone.

In partnership with local municipalities and government partners, Dror Israel is actively assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses. Simultaneously, it is activating its nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. Sustaining this emergency-adapted core educational work requires ongoing operating support.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs

Dror Israel seeks **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to continue reaching 200,000 participants nationwide, provide activities and emotional support for children and families in bomb shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond rapidly as urgent needs evolve.

## Current Priorities

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers**  
   Temporary childcare frameworks for children of essential workers (medical staff, security personnel, critical services) in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva; staffed by counselors and youth movement leaders in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels.  
   - **Funding gap: $125,000** — 10 staff, transportation, supplies  
   - Hospital-requested pop-up school for medical teams' children  
   - **Funding gap: $50,000** — 4 teachers, transportation, supplies

2. **Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels**  
   Daily emotional-educational activities and support for evacuated children and youth (including Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva), alongside distribution of clothing and supplies in evacuee settings.  
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** — staff, supplies, transportation

3. **Activities in Public Shelters**  
   Programming for children in public bomb shelters (schools, parking lots, subway stations) including Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more; deployment of hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors trained as emergency junior counselors with ready-to-use activity kits.  
   - **Funding gap: $60,000** — 5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies

4. **Pedagogy of Hope**  
   Trauma-informed pedagogical support for Dror Israel's school network (10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools), including support for Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva after a missile fragment strike when school was closed.  
   - **Funding gap: $80,000** — staff, pedagogical development

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance**  
   Online resilience sessions via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa, creating structured spaces for emotional release, regulation, and connection.  
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** — staff

## About Dror Israel

Dror Israel describes itself as a leading force for social change, with **1,300 trained educators** living in **14 educators' kibbutzim** throughout Israel, dedicated to meeting local needs. With extensive emergency-response experience, especially for traumatized children, the organization aims to provide immediate stability and hope during wartime disruption.

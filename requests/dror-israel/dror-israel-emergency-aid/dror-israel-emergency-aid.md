## The Ask

Dror Israel requests **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and scale crisis response during the current escalation. Their network of **1,300 educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim** provides educational activities, emotional and trauma support, emergency childcare for essential workers, support in evacuee settings and public shelters, trauma-informed school guidance, and resilience programming through movement and dance.

The response reaches **200,000+ children, teens, and families** via in-person support, direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming, in partnership with municipalities and government partners.

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at/on-site hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools (staff, pedagogical development): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa (staff): **$10,000**

**Non-Financial Ask:** Introductions/recommendations to individuals, foundations, emergency funds, or other supporters; Dror Israel can also provide a short presentation/briefing/update call for community or leadership audiences, including the Detroit community.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, families across Israel are repeatedly moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and coping with ongoing trauma and disruption. Dror Israel's educators are delivering real-time educational and emotional first response in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, public shelters, schools, and living rooms nationwide.

Current priorities include:
1. **Emergency childcare for essential workers** in multiple cities (Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be'er Sheva), including temporary frameworks in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels.
2. **Hospital pop-up school** requested by a major hospital so medical teams can work while children remain in a safe, structured learning setting.
3. **Evacuee hotel programming** including daily activities and emotional-educational support for displaced children and youth (including evacuees from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva), plus distribution of clothing/supplies.
4. **Public shelter activities** in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional locations, with hundreds of trained Jewish and Arab teen emergency junior counselors using ready-to-use activity kits.
5. **Pedagogy of Hope** trauma-informed support for school staff and students across the network; includes Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva, which was hit by a missile fragment while closed.
6. **Online resilience sessions** through Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers, focused on emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.

Sustaining educator hours, transportation, supplies, digital platforms, and operational coordination requires immediate emergency operating support.

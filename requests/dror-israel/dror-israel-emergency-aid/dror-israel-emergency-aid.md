## The Ask

Dror Israel requests emergency support for a two-week response window during ongoing missile attacks. Across both versions of the request, the organization describes deployment of 1,300 trained educators supporting 200,000+ children, teens, and families through emergency childcare, shelter and evacuee programming, trauma-informed school support, and resilience sessions.

### Funding Request (Difference Noted)

- **Version A (Mar 4 document / one source email): $250,000**
- **Version B (Mar 6 document / second source email): $360,000**

The difference is driven by an added line item in Version B for structured shelter kits and associated training.

### Consolidated Funding Breakdown

- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- 200 structured activity kits for shelters (training and supplies): **$25,000** *(included in Version B only)*
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for schools network (staff and pedagogical development): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): **$10,000**

### Non-Financial Ask

- Introductions from Philadelphia-area high schools, synagogues, and youth groups to Dror Israel’s **“Ask Me Anything”** dialogue program connecting Israeli and American teens in conversation about the war.
- Referral contact: **contact-en@drorisrael.org.il**

## Context & Background

As Iran’s missile barrage continues, families across Israel are repeatedly moving into shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and coping with sustained uncertainty and trauma. Dror Israel reports active partnership with municipalities and government bodies to assess local needs and deploy targeted responses in real time.

Key operational elements across both documents include:

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers**  
   Active in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels. Includes a requested hospital-based pop-up school.

2. **Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels/Centers**  
   Daily programming and emotional-educational support for evacuated children and youth, with references to Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva; includes distribution of clothing and supplies.

3. **Public Shelter Activities**  
   Programming in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional locations, with deployment of trained Jewish and Arab teen counselors and activity kits.

4. **Pedagogy of Hope (Schools Network Support)**  
   Trauma-informed guidance and pedagogical support across 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools. Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva was hit by a missile fragment; no casualties were reported.

5. **Resilience Through Movement and Dance**  
   Online sessions via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa for emotional release, regulation, and community connection.

Additional implementation detail present in one version: coordination with municipalities and the Ministry of Education’s Youth and Society Administration to distribute 200 structured activity kits and train local facilitators/volunteers.

## About Dror Israel

Dror Israel describes itself as a leading social-change movement with **1,300 trained educators** living in **14 educators’ kibbutzim** across Israel, with substantial emergency-response experience, particularly supporting traumatized children and youth.

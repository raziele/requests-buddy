## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **$250,000–$360,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and expand its crisis response during the current Iranian missile escalation. Amounts cited in sources: $250,000; $350,000; $360,000. The higher figure includes 200 activity kits and training for shelters ($25,000). As Israelis move into bomb shelters and evacuee centers, the organization's educators are providing educational activities, emotional and trauma support, and emergency childcare for the children of essential workers. Through its nationwide network it is supporting more than 200,000 children, teens, and families. The request covers emergency childcare centers, a pop-up school at a hospital, activities at evacuee hotels and public shelters, trauma-informed pedagogy for its school network, and resilience programming through movement and dance.

**Funding Requested:** $250,000–$360,000 (sources vary; see breakdown.)

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- 200 activity kits + training for shelters: $25,000 *(included in $360K ask only)*
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for 10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools: $80,000
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): $10,000

**Non-Financial Asks:**
- Guidance or introductions to others who may be interested in supporting this work; optional short presentation or briefing for community or leadership.
- Introduction to Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia or other Philadelphia/community partners who might support.
- North American high school groups interested in the 'Ask Me Anything' dialogue program connecting Israeli and American teens (contact-en@drorisrael.org.il).

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and bracing for repeated sirens. Families across the country are living in fear and uncertainty, with trauma compounded as daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Dror Israel's trained educators are delivering educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms nationwide, in partnership with local municipalities and government. The organization is also activating its nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.

Current priorities include: emergency childcare for essential workers in multiple cities (Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be'er Sheva), including a pop-up school on-site at a major hospital; daily activities for evacuee children in Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva (including distribution of donations of clothing and supplies at an evacuee center in Tel Aviv); organized activities in public shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias and elsewhere, with hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors deployed with activity kits; and trauma-informed pedagogical guidance for the Dror Israel school network. One school, Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva, was hit by a missile fragment while closed; nobody was harmed. Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa are offering online resilience sessions through movement and dance.

Dror Israel comprises 1,300 trained educators living in 14 educators' kibbutzim, with extensive experience in emergency response for traumatized children. Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires emergency operating support.

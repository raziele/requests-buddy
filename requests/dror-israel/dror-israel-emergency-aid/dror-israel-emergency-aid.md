## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and expand/scale its crisis response during the current escalation. Its network includes **1,300 trained educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim**, providing educational activities, emotional and trauma support, emergency childcare for essential workers, support in bomb shelters and evacuee settings, trauma-informed pedagogy for schools, and resilience programming through movement and dance.

The organization is operating with municipalities and government partners, and is also reaching participants via direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming. Funding is requested to close immediate gaps in staffing, transportation, supplies, pedagogical support, and operational coordination.

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at/on-site hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels/centers (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools (staff, pedagogical development): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa (staff): **$10,000**

**Non-Financial Ask:** Introductions/recommendations to individuals, foundations, or emergency funds interested in supporting this work; Dror Israel can also provide a short presentation/briefing/update call (including for Detroit community/leadership) on what educators are seeing and doing on the ground.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, families across Israel are repeatedly moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and facing ongoing fear, disruption, and layered trauma. Dror Israel reports active emergency educational and emotional support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, schools, and community settings, with rapid adaptation to changing needs.

Current priorities include:
1. Emergency childcare frameworks for children of essential workers in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, including a requested hospital-based pop-up school.
2. Daily emotional-educational activities for evacuee children and youth, including evacuees from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva.
3. Activities for children in public shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional locations, including deployment of hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors with activity kits.
4. Trauma-informed pedagogical guidance through the Dror Israel Schools Network; Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva was hit by a missile fragment while closed.
5. Online resilience sessions through movement and dance via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.

Dror Israel emphasizes that sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and national coordination during this intensified period requires immediate operating support.

# Israel Needs Us Once Again. And We Need You!
Educators, Community Builders, Emotional First Responders – We Are There  
March 4, 2026

## Current Situation

As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, families are experiencing repeated trauma as daily life is disrupted. Dror Israel educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms, while coordinating with municipalities and government partners to respond in real time.

The organization is activating its nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through outreach, calls, and structured Zoom programming to keep participants safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs

Dror Israel is seeking **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to continue reaching participants nationwide, provide activities and emotional support in shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond quickly as needs evolve.

## Current Priorities

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers**  
   Childcare centers in multiple cities for children of medical staff, security personnel, and critical workers; includes temporary frameworks in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels.  
   - **Funding gap: $125,000** — 10 staff, transportation, supplies  
   - Hospital on-site pop-up school request  
   - **Funding gap: $50,000** — 4 teachers, transportation, supplies

2. **Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels**  
   Activities and support for evacuated children and youth; includes distribution of donated clothing/supplies and daily programming.  
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** — staff, supplies, transportation

3. **Organizing Activities in Public Shelters**  
   Programming for children in public bomb shelters (schools, parking lots, subway stations), with professional educators and trained Jewish/Arab teen counselors using activity kits.  
   - **Funding gap: $60,000** — 5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies

4. **Pedagogy of Hope**  
   Trauma-informed pedagogical guidance and support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools, including Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva after missile-fragment impact.  
   - **Funding gap: $80,000** — staff, pedagogical development

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance**  
   Online resilience sessions in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa through Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers.  
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** — staff

Dror Israel describes itself as a leading force for social change with 1,300 trained educators living in 14 educators' kibbutzim throughout Israel, with extensive emergency-response experience supporting traumatized children and communities.

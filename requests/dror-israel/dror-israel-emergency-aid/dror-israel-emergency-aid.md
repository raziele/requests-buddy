## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and scale crisis response during the current escalation. Their network of **1,300 educators in 14 kibbutzim** is providing educational activities, emotional and trauma support, and emergency childcare for over **200,000** children, teens, and families in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, and temporary childcare sites for essential workers.

The request supports emergency childcare for essential workers, a pop-up school at a hospital, activities at evacuee hotels and public shelters, trauma-informed pedagogy for their schools network, and resilience programming through movement and dance.

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school on-site at hospital for medical teams' children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for school network (10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools; staff, pedagogical development): $80,000
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa; staff): $10,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Introductions/recommendations to individuals, foundations, emergency funds, or other supporters; willingness to provide a short presentation/briefing/update call for community or leadership (including Detroit community/leadership context in one source).

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are again moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and living with repeated disruption, fear, and trauma. Dror Israel's trained educators provide educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms across the country.

In partnership with local municipalities and government partners, Dror Israel is assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses in real time. Their nationwide network is activated through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming so participants remain safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.

Current priorities include:
- Emergency childcare for essential workers in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, including a requested hospital-based pop-up school.
- Daily educational and emotional-support activities for evacuees, including evacuee centers/hotels in Tel Aviv and support for evacuees from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva.
- Activities in public shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional locations, with hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors deployed with activity kits.
- Trauma-informed pedagogical guidance across 29 schools (10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools), including support to Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva after a missile fragment strike (while school was closed; no injuries).
- Online resilience sessions through movement and dance via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.

Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires continued emergency operating support.

# Israel Needs Us Once Again. And We Need You!
Educators, Community Builders, Emotional First Responders - We Are There  
March 4, 2026

## Current Situation

As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, Israelis are living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma as families once again move into bomb shelters and daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and in living rooms across the country. Nimble and ready to go wherever needed, they are addressing urgent emotional and educational needs in real time to ensure no child feels alone.

Dror Israel educators, in partnership with local municipalities and government partners, are actively assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses as conditions change. Simultaneously, they are activating a nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. This is core educational work adapted to emergency conditions, and sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires continued operating support.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs

Dror Israel is seeking **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to continue reaching 200,000 participants nationwide, provide activities and emotional support for children and families in bomb shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond quickly as urgent needs continue to emerge.

## Current Priorities

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers** - In partnership with local communities, emergency childcare centers have launched for children of essential workers so medical staff, security personnel, and critical service providers can continue their work. Operating in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, counselors and youth movement leaders are staffing temporary childcare frameworks in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels.  
   - **Funding gap: $125,000** - 10 staff, transportation and supplies  
   - One major hospital has asked Dror Israel to establish a pop-up school on-site so medical teams can continue caring for patients while their children are nearby in a safe, structured educational environment.  
   - **Funding gap: $50,000** - 4 teachers, transportation, supplies

2. **Educational activities at evacuee hotels** - A growing wave of evacuees is arriving daily. Activities include distribution of clothing/supplies in an evacuee center in Tel Aviv and daily programming for evacuated children and youth from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva.  
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** for staff, supplies, and transportation

3. **Organizing activities in public shelters** - Many families lacking home shelters are staying overnight in public bomb shelters in schools, parking lots, and subway stations. Activities are already running in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more, with hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors deployed with ready-to-use activity kits.  
   - **Funding gap: $60,000** - 5 volunteer organizers, transportation and supplies

4. **Pedagogy of Hope** - Dror Israel Schools Network provides pedagogical guidance and trauma support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools. Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva was hit by a missile fragment; school was closed and no one was harmed. Schools receive personalized, trauma-informed guidance for staff and students, teaching toward hope during war.  
   - **Funding gap: $80,000** for staff and pedagogical development

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance** - In Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa, Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers offer online resilience sessions through movement and dance, supporting emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.  
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** for staff

## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and scale crisis response during the current escalation. Their network of **1,300 trained educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim** is providing educational activities, emotional and trauma support, and emergency childcare for over **200,000** children, teens, and families nationwide—in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, temporary childcare sites, and community settings.

Requested support covers emergency childcare for essential workers, a pop-up hospital school, activities at evacuee hotels/centers and public shelters, trauma-informed pedagogy for their schools network, and resilience programming through movement and dance.

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school at/on-site hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for schools network (10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools; staff, pedagogical development): $80,000
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa; staff): $10,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Introductions/recommendations to individuals, foundations, emergency funds, or others interested in supporting this work; Dror Israel can provide a short presentation/briefing/update call for community or leadership (including the Detroit community) on what educators are seeing and doing on the ground.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are again moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty. Families are experiencing repeated trauma as daily life is disrupted. Dror Israel's educators are delivering educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, hotels, and living rooms across the country.

In partnership with local municipalities and government partners, Dror Israel is assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses in real time. Simultaneously, they are activating a nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to keep participants safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.

Current priorities include:
1. **Emergency childcare for essential workers** in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, including temporary childcare in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels.
2. **Hospital pop-up school** requested by a major hospital so medical teams can continue patient care while children remain nearby in a safe educational setting.
3. **Educational activities at evacuee hotels/centers**, including support for evacuees from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva, plus distribution of clothing and supplies in at least one Tel Aviv evacuee center.
4. **Public shelter programming** in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and other locations, with hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors deployed with activity kits.
5. **Pedagogy of Hope** trauma-informed guidance across the Dror Israel Schools Network (10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools), including support for Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva after a missile fragment strike (while school was closed, with no injuries).
6. **Resilience through movement and dance** via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa (online sessions for emotional release, regulation, and connection).

Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, logistics, and coordination during this intensified period requires urgent operating support.

### Sheagat HaAri update March 2026.pdf

# Israel Needs Us Once Again. And We Need You!
Educators, Community Builders, Emotional First Responders – We Are There  
March 4, 2026

## Current Situation

As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, Israelis are living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma as families once again move into bomb shelters and daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Dror Israel's trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and even in living rooms across the country. Nimble and ready to go wherever needed, they are addressing urgent emotional and educational needs in real time, ensuring that no child feels alone.

Dror Israel educators, in partnership with local municipalities and government partners, are actively assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses as conditions change. Simultaneously, they are activating a nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. This is core educational work adapted to emergency conditions, and sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires continued operating support.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs

Dror Israel is seeking **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to continue reaching 200,000 participants nationwide, provide activities and emotional support for children and families in bomb shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond quickly as urgent needs emerge.

## Current Priorities

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers**  
   In partnership with local communities, emergency childcare centers are operating for children of essential workers so medical staff, security personnel, and critical service providers can continue their work. These frameworks operate in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva.  
   - **Funding gap: $125,000** — 10 staff, transportation, supplies  
   - One major hospital requested an on-site pop-up school.  
   - **Funding gap: $50,000** — 4 teachers, transportation, supplies

2. **Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels**  
   Daily activities and emotional-educational support are provided for evacuated children and youth (including evacuees from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva), with donations distribution in at least one Tel Aviv evacuee center.  
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** — staff, supplies, transportation

3. **Organizing Activities in Public Shelters**  
   Activities are being run in public bomb shelters (including Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more), with deployment of trained Jewish and Arab teen emergency junior counselors using ready-to-use activity kits.  
   - **Funding gap: $60,000** — 5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies

4. **Pedagogy of Hope**  
   The Dror Israel Schools Network provides pedagogical guidance and trauma support to 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools. Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva was hit by a missile fragment while closed, with no injuries; schools are receiving personalized trauma-informed guidance.  
   - **Funding gap: $80,000** — staff, pedagogical development

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance**  
   Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa offer online resilience sessions through movement and dance for emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.  
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** — staff

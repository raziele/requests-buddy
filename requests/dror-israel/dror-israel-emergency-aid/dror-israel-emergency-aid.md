## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and scale crisis response during the current escalation. Their network of **1,300 trained educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim** is providing educational activities, emotional and trauma support, emergency childcare for essential workers, and resilience programming for over 200,000 participants nationwide. Activities are delivered in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, schools, community settings, and via structured outreach including phone and Zoom programming.

They are also seeking **non-financial support**: introductions/recommendations to individuals, foundations, or emergency funds, and are available for a short presentation/briefing/update call (including with Detroit community or leadership) on what educators are seeing and doing on the ground.

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school at/on-site hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for school network (10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools; staff and pedagogical development): $80,000
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa; staff): $10,000

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are again moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and enduring repeated disruption, fear, and trauma. Dror Israel educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms across the country. In partnership with municipalities and government partners, they assess evolving needs and coordinate targeted emergency responses in real time.

Their nationwide emergency activation includes direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure children, teens, and families are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, transportation, supplies, and coordination during this intensified period requires continued operating support.

### Current Priorities

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers**  
   Operating in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, with temporary childcare frameworks in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels so essential workers can remain on duty.  
   - Funding gap: **$125,000** (10 staff, transportation, supplies)  
   - Additional request: hospital-based pop-up school for staff children  
   - Funding gap: **$50,000** (4 teachers, transportation, supplies)

2. **Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels/Centers**  
   Daily emotional-educational support for evacuated children and youth (including evacuees from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva), plus distribution of clothing/supplies in evacuee settings.  
   - Funding gap: **$10,000** (staff, supplies, transportation)

3. **Activities in Public Shelters**  
   Programming for children in public bomb shelters (including schools, parking lots, subway stations) in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional locations; deployment of hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen emergency junior counselors with activity kits.  
   - Funding gap: **$60,000** (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies)

4. **Pedagogy of Hope**  
   Trauma-informed pedagogical guidance and support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools. Includes support to Moledet Elementary School (Be'er Sheva), which was hit by a missile fragment while closed.  
   - Funding gap: **$80,000** (staff, pedagogical development)

5. **Resilience Through Community Movement and Dance**  
   Online resilience sessions via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa to support emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.  
   - Funding gap: **$10,000** (staff)

## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and scale crisis response during the current escalation. Their network of **1,300 trained educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim** is providing educational activities, emotional and trauma support, and emergency childcare for more than **200,000 children, teens, and families** across Israel.

Programming includes:
- Emergency childcare for children of essential workers so medical, security, and critical service teams can continue working
- A hospital-based pop-up school requested by a major hospital
- Daily educational and emotional support activities in evacuee hotels/centers
- Activities for children and families in public bomb shelters
- Trauma-informed pedagogical support across Dror Israel's school network
- Online resilience programming through movement and dance via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers

They also welcome non-financial support through introductions/recommendations to potential funders (individuals, foundations, emergency funds) and can provide a short presentation/briefing/update call (including for Detroit community/leadership) on real-time ground conditions and educator response.

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000

- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools (staff, pedagogical development): $80,000
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa (staff): $10,000

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are again moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and facing repeated fear, uncertainty, and disruption. Dror Israel educators are delivering educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, and living rooms across the country.

In partnership with local municipalities and government partners, teams are assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses in real time. Simultaneously, they are activating a nationwide network through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to keep participants safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.

Current priorities include:
1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers** in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva (including temporary frameworks in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels)
2. **Hospital Pop-Up School** so medical staff can work while children remain nearby in a safe, structured setting
3. **Evacuee Hotel/Center Activities** including support for evacuated children and youth from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva, alongside aid distribution in Tel Aviv
4. **Public Shelter Activities** in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional sites, with hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors deployed with activity kits
5. **Pedagogy of Hope** trauma-informed support for school communities, including Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva (hit by a missile fragment while school was closed; no injuries)
6. **Resilience Through Movement and Dance** online sessions supporting emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection

Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, logistics, and coordination during this intensified period requires immediate operating support.

## About Dror Israel

Dror Israel describes itself as a leading force for social change, with 1,300 trained educators living in 14 educators' kibbutzim throughout Israel, dedicated to meeting local needs. With extensive emergency-response experience, especially with traumatized children, the organization is focused on providing stability and hope amid wartime conditions.

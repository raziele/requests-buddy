## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and scale crisis response during the current escalation. Its network of **1,300 trained educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim** is delivering educational activities, emotional and trauma support, and emergency childcare for more than **200,000** children, teens, and families across Israel.

The request supports operations in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, temporary childcare settings for essential workers, and schools requiring trauma-informed support. Dror Israel is also seeking introductions to potential funders and is available to provide a short presentation/briefing (including an update call with Detroit community/leadership).

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school on-site at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for school network (10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools; staff and pedagogical development): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa (staff): **$10,000**

**Non-Financial Ask:** Introductions/recommendations to individuals, foundations, or emergency funds; optional short presentation or briefing/update call on conditions and educator response on the ground.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are again moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and living with repeated disruption and trauma. Dror Israel educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms across the country, while partnering with municipalities and government to assess changing needs and coordinate targeted emergency responses.

In parallel, Dror Israel is activating its nationwide network through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming so participants remain safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. Sustaining educator hours, transportation, supplies, digital platforms, and coordination requires immediate operating support.

Current priorities include:
1. Emergency childcare for essential workers in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva.
2. A requested hospital-based pop-up school so medical teams can work while children are nearby in a safe setting.
3. Daily educational and emotional support activities for evacuees, including in Tel Aviv and for evacuees from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva.
4. Public shelter activities in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional locations, including deployment of hundreds of trained Jewish and Arab teen counselors with activity kits.
5. Pedagogy of Hope trauma-informed guidance across Dror Israel Schools Network; Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva was hit by a missile fragment while closed, with no injuries.
6. Online resilience sessions through movement and dance via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.

# Israel Needs Us Once Again. And We Need You!
Educators, Community Builders, Emotional First Responders – We Are There  
March 4, 2026

## Current Situation

As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, Israelis are living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma as families once again move into bomb shelters and daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Dror Israel's trained educators provide educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms, ensuring children are not alone.

In partnership with local municipalities and government partners, Dror Israel is assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses. Simultaneously, it is activating a nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to keep participants safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs

Dror Israel is seeking **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to continue reaching 200,000 participants nationwide, provide emotional and educational support in bomb shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond rapidly as urgent needs evolve.

## Current Priorities

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers**  
   Launched in partnership with local communities for children of medical staff, security personnel, and critical service providers; operating in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva.  
   - **Funding gap: $125,000** — 10 staff, transportation, supplies  
   - **Hospital pop-up school funding gap: $50,000** — 4 teachers, transportation, supplies

2. **Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels**  
   Activities and emotional-educational support for evacuated children and youth, including Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva evacuees.  
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** — staff, supplies, transportation

3. **Activities in Public Shelters**  
   Programming for children in public bomb shelters (schools, parking lots, subway stations), including Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more; includes trained Jewish and Arab teen counselors with activity kits.  
   - **Funding gap: $60,000** — 5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies

4. **Pedagogy of Hope**  
   Trauma-informed pedagogical support across 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools; includes support to Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva after missile fragment impact (while closed).  
   - **Funding gap: $80,000** — staff, pedagogical development

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance**  
   Online resilience sessions through Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.  
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** — staff

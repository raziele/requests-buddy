## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and expand/scale its crisis response during the current escalation. Their educators are running activities for children in bomb shelters and evacuee hotels, providing educational, emotional, and trauma support, operating emergency childcare for essential workers so medical and security personnel can continue working, and delivering pedagogical and resilience programming.

They are seeking funding to close gaps in staffing, transportation, and supplies across emergency childcare, a hospital-based pop-up school, evacuee hotel activities, public shelter programming, school trauma support, and community movement/dance resilience sessions.

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school at/on-site at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for school network (10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools; staff, pedagogical development): $80,000
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa; staff): $10,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Guidance, introductions, or recommendations to individuals, foundations, or emergency funds interested in supporting this work; Dror Israel also offers a short presentation/briefing/update call for community or leadership (including Detroit community leadership) on what educators are seeing and doing on the ground.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are again moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and living with repeated disruption, fear, and uncertainty. Dror Israel's trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms across the country. In partnership with local municipalities and government, they assess evolving needs and coordinate targeted emergency responses in real time.

Their nationwide network reaches more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to keep participants safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.

Current priorities include:
- Emergency childcare for essential workers in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, including a requested on-site pop-up school at a major hospital.
- Daily activities for evacuees, including children and youth in Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva evacuee settings.
- Activities in public shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional locations, including deployment of hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors with ready-to-use activity kits.
- Trauma-informed pedagogical guidance and support for Dror Israel's schools network (10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools), including Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva, which was hit by a missile fragment while closed.
- Online resilience sessions through movement and dance in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers.

Dror Israel reports 1,300 trained educators living in 14 educators' kibbutzim throughout Israel, with extensive emergency response experience for traumatized children. Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires continued operating support.

## Current Situation

As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, Israelis are living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma as families once again move into bomb shelters and daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms across the country, addressing urgent emotional and educational needs in real time to ensure no child feels alone.

Dror Israel educators, in partnership with local municipalities and government partners, are actively assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses as conditions change. Simultaneously, they are activating a nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires continued operating support.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs

Dror Israel is seeking **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to continue reaching 200,000 participants nationwide, provide activities and emotional support for children and families in bomb shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond quickly as urgent needs emerge.

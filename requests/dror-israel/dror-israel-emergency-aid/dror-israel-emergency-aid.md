## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **$350,000** in emergency support for the **next two weeks** to sustain, expand, and scale its crisis response during the current escalation. Their network of **1,300 educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim** provides educational activities, emotional and trauma support, and emergency childcare for **over 200,000 children, teens, and families**—in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and temporary childcare sites for essential workers—working with local municipalities and government partners.

The request covers: emergency childcare for essential workers; a **pop-up school on-site at a hospital** so medical teams can work while children are nearby; educational activities at **evacuee hotels** (including distribution of clothing/supplies and daily activities for evacuated children and youth); activities in **public shelters** (with teen counselors and activity kits); **Pedagogy of Hope**—trauma-informed pedagogy for the schools network; and **resilience programming through movement and dance** (Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers).

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and living with repeated disruption, fear, and trauma. Dror Israel's trained educators deliver educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms nationwide. They assess evolving needs with municipalities and government and coordinate targeted responses; they also reach participants through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming so children and families stay safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires emergency operating support.

**Operational detail (from materials):** Emergency childcare frameworks operate in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva. Evacuee support includes a Tel Aviv evacuee center (donations of clothing/supplies; daily activities for children/youth from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva). Public shelter activities run in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more; **hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors** trained as emergency junior counselors deploy with ready-to-use kits alongside professional educators. **Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva** was hit by a missile fragment while **closed**; no one was harmed—all schools in the network receive personalized, trauma-informed guidance. **Online** resilience sessions through movement and dance are offered in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.

### Sheagat HaAri update March 2026.pdf

# Israel Needs Us Once Again. And We Need You!
Educators, Community Builders, Emotional First Responders – We Are There  
March 4, 2026

## Current Situation

As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, Israelis are living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma as families once again move into bomb shelters and daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Our trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and even in living rooms across the country. Nimble and ready to go wherever we are needed, we are addressing urgent emotional and educational needs in real time, ensuring that no child feels alone.

Dror Israel educators, in partnership with local municipalities and government partners, are actively assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses as conditions change. Simultaneously, we are activating our nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. This is our core educational work adapted to emergency conditions, and sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires continued operating support.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs

Dror Israel is seeking **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to ensure we can continue reaching our 200,000 participants nationwide, provide activities and emotional support for children and families in bomb shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond quickly as urgent needs continue to emerge.

## Current Priorities

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers** — In partnership with local communities, we have launched emergency childcare centers for the children of essential workers so that medical staff, security personnel, and critical service providers can continue their work. Operating in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, our counselors and youth movement leaders are staffing temporary childcare frameworks in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels. These centers provide safe supervision, structured activities, and emotional support for children whose parents are serving on the front lines of civilian response.
   - **Funding gap: $125,000** — 10 staff, transportation and supplies
   - One major hospital has asked Dror Israel to establish a pop-up school on-site so that working medical teams can continue caring for patients while knowing their children are nearby in a safe, structured educational environment.
   - **Funding gap: $50,000** — 4 teachers, transportation, supplies

2. **Educational activities at evacuee hotels** — Israel's new wave of evacuees is growing every day. We are already distributing donations of clothing and supplies in an evacuee center in Tel Aviv, and offering daily activities for the evacuated children and youth from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva. Our educators are running emotional-educational support activities for children to provide some normalcy among the uncertainty and loss, also giving parents a break.
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** for staff, supplies and transportation

3. **Organizing activities in public shelters** — Many families lack appropriate home shelters and are staying overnight in public bomb shelters in schools, parking lots, and subway stations. We are already operating activities for children in public shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more. Alongside our professional educators, hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors trained by Dror Israel as emergency junior counselors are now being deployed with ready-to-use activity kits.
   - **Funding gap: $60,000** — 5 volunteer organizers, transportation and supplies

4. **Pedagogy of Hope** — Dror Israel Schools Network provides pedagogical guidance and trauma support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools. One such school, the Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva, was hit by a missile fragment but thankfully the school was closed and nobody was harmed. All of our schools, including the Moledet School, are receiving personalized and trauma-informed guidance in support of staff and students, teaching towards hope in these times of war.
   - **Funding gap: $80,000** for staff and pedagogical development

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance** — In Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa, Dror Israel's Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers are offering online resilience sessions through movement and dance. These guided sessions create structured spaces for emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.
   - **Funding gap: $10,000** for staff

## The Ask

Dror Israel is seeking $250,000 in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain educational and emotional support for 200,000 participants, emergency childcare for essential workers, activities in evacuee hotels and public shelters, trauma-informed pedagogy for schools, and resilience sessions (movement/dance). Funding gaps are itemized for childcare ($125,000 + $50,000 for pop-up school), evacuee activities ($10,000), shelter activities ($60,000), Pedagogy of Hope ($80,000), and dance/wellbeing ($10,000).

**Funding Requested:** $250,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school at hospital (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope – trauma support for schools: $80,000
- Resilience through movement and dance (Tamara centers): $10,000

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are in shelters, evacuating, and under repeated trauma. Dror Israel's educators deliver educational and trauma-support programming in shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms, and are activating a network of 200,000+ children, teens, and families via outreach, calls, and Zoom. They run emergency childcare for essential workers in multiple cities; distribute donations and run daily activities at evacuee centers (e.g. Tel Aviv, Be'er Sheva); operate activities in public shelters (Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, etc.) with Jewish and Arab teen counselors and activity kits; provide pedagogical and trauma guidance to 10 elementary, 9 high, and 10 preschools (including Moledet in Be'er Sheva, which was hit by a fragment); and offer online resilience sessions (movement/dance) in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa. Dror Israel comprises 1,300 educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim.

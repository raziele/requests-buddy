## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting $360,000 in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and expand its crisis response following the latest Iranian missile escalation. The organization has 1,300 educators across 14 kibbutzim and is already operating in dozens of shelters and emergency childcare sites for essential workers' children, plus online programming for 200,000 participants. The ask covers emergency childcare, educational and emotional support in public shelters and evacuee hotels, trauma-informed pedagogy for its school network, and resilience programming (e.g. movement and dance). They also seek introductions to the Federation or other Philadelphia partners, and North American high school groups interested in their 'Ask Me Anything' dialogue program connecting Israeli and American teens.

**Funding Requested:** $360,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school at major hospital (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- 200 activity kits + training for shelters: $25,000
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for schools network: $80,000
- Resilience through community movement and dance (Tamara centers): $10,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Introduction to Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia or other community partners who might support; North American high school groups to participate in 'Ask Me Anything' dialogue program (contact-en@drorisrael.org.il).

## Context & Background

Following Iran's missile barrage in late February–March 2026, Israelis are again living in bomb shelters and evacuee centers, with daily life repeatedly disrupted. Dror Israel, a movement of 1,300 trained educators living in 14 educators' kibbutzim, has pivoted to emergency response: staffing temporary childcare for essential workers in multiple cities, running educational and emotional-support activities in public shelters (including with Jewish and Arab teen counselors), supporting evacuee families in Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva, and providing trauma-informed guidance to its network of 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools. One of its schools (Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva) was hit by a missile fragment; the school was closed and no one was harmed. The organization is also activating its nationwide network of over 200,000 children, teens, and families via direct outreach and Zoom. Funding is needed to sustain educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination for the next two weeks while needs continue to evolve.

### Dror Israel- Sheagat HaAri Emergency Response March 2026 (1).pdf

**Israel Needs Us Once Again. And We Need You!**
Educators, Community Builders, Emotional First Responders – We Are There
March 6, 2026

**Current Situation**
As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, Israelis are living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma as families once again move into bomb shelters and daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Our trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and even in living rooms across the country. Nimble and ready to go wherever we are needed, we are addressing urgent emotional and educational needs in real time, ensuring that no child feels alone. Dror Israel educators, in partnership with local municipalities and government partners, are actively assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses as conditions change. Simultaneously, we are activating our nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.

**Seeking Support for Escalating Needs**
Dror Israel is seeking **$360,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to ensure we can continue reaching our 200,000 participants nationwide, provide activities and emotional support for children and families in bomb shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond quickly as urgent needs continue to emerge.

**Current Priorities**

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers**
   In partnership with local communities, we have launched emergency childcare centers for the children of essential workers so that medical staff, security personnel, and critical service providers can continue their work. Operating in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva. Funding gap: **$125,000** (10 staff, transportation and supplies). One major hospital has asked Dror Israel to establish a pop-up school on-site. Funding gap: **$50,000** (4 teachers, transportation, supplies).

2. **Educational activities at evacuee hotels**
   Distributing donations and offering daily activities for evacuated children and youth from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva. Funding gap: **$10,000** for staff, supplies and transportation.

3. **Organizing activities in public shelters**
   Operating in public bomb shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more. Hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors trained as emergency junior counselors are being deployed with activity kits. Funding gap: **$60,000** (5 volunteer organizers, transportation and supplies). Producing and distributing 200 structured activity kits; training local facilitators. Funding gap: **$25,000**.

4. **Pedagogy of Hope**
   Pedagogical guidance and trauma support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools. Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva was hit by a missile fragment (school was closed, nobody harmed). Funding gap: **$80,000** for staff and pedagogical development.

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance**
   In Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa, Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers offering online resilience sessions. Funding gap: **$10,000** for staff.

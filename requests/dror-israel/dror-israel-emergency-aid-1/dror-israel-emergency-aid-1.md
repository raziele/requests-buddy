Dror Israel is requesting emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and expand its crisis response as Iran’s missile barrages continue. Their educators and network (1,300 trained educators; 200,000+ participants) are delivering educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, evacuee hotels, and emergency childcare settings for essential workers’ children.

## Context & Background

As Iran’s missile barrage continues, Israelis are moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and bracing for repeated sirens. Families are experiencing trauma upon trauma alongside constant disruption. Dror Israel’s educators are providing educational and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms across the country, coordinated in partnership with local municipalities and government partners as needs evolve.

Dror Israel is activating a nationwide network of more than **200,000** children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to keep participants safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. The organization emphasizes that sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during the intensified period requires the requested emergency operating support.

### Current Priorities (merged and deduplicated)

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers**
   - Operates in multiple cities including **Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be'er Sheva**.
   - Implemented in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels with safe supervision, structured activities, and emotional support so parents can continue front-line civilian response.
   - **Pop-up school at a major hospital** requested so working medical teams can care for patients while knowing children are nearby in a safe, structured educational environment.
2. **Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels**
   - Distribution of clothing and supplies and daily activities for evacuated children and youth (including evacuees from **Tel Aviv** and **Be'er Sheva**).
   - Emotional-educational support to provide normalcy and give parents a break.
3. **Organizing Activities in Public Shelters**
   - Activities for children in public bomb shelters in **Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more**.
   - Hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors trained as emergency junior counselors are deployed with ready-to-use activity kits.
4. **200 Structured Activity Kits for Shelters**
   - Rapid production and distribution of **200** structured shelter activity kits for use in public shelters and residential buildings.
   - Training and deployment planned for local facilitators, volunteers, and youth leaders to operate safe, educational, and emotional-support activities in accordance with safety regulations, coordinated with local municipalities and the **Ministry of Education’s Youth and Society Administration**.
5. **Pedagogy of Hope**
   - Pedagogical guidance and trauma support across Dror Israel’s schools network: **10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools**.
   - **Moledet Elementary School (Be'er Sheva)** was hit by a missile fragment; the school was closed and nobody was harmed. Personalized trauma-informed guidance is provided for staff and students.
6. **Resilience Through Community Movement and Dance**
   - Online resilience sessions through movement and dance offered via Dror Israel’s Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in **Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa** for emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.

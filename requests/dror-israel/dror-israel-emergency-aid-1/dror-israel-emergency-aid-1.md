## The Ask

Dror Israel requests emergency support for a two-week surge response during ongoing missile attacks, delivering educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee hotels/centers, schools, and emergency childcare settings nationwide.

Two circulated versions list different totals:
- **Version A (Mar 4): $250,000**
- **Version B (Mar 6): $360,000**

Both versions share the same core five priorities; the $360,000 version additionally includes a dedicated line for structured shelter activity kits and training, and includes a non-financial partnership ask.

### Funding Breakdown (Unified)

- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels/centers (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- 200 structured activity kits for shelters (training and supplies): **$25,000** *(included in $360,000 version)*
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for schools network (staff and pedagogical development): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): **$10,000**

**Total if all listed lines are included: $360,000.**

### Non-Financial Ask

Introductions to Philadelphia-area high school groups, synagogues, or youth groups interested in joining Dror Israel’s **“Ask Me Anything”** dialogue program (Israeli and American teens in open conversation about the war).  
Referral contact: **contact-en@drorisrael.org.il**

## Context & Background

As Iran’s missile barrage continues, families across Israel are repeatedly moving into shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and facing ongoing trauma and disruption. Dror Israel reports that its educators are operating in bomb shelters, evacuee locations, schools, and community settings, in partnership with municipalities and government actors, while also running direct outreach, phone check-ins, and structured Zoom programming.

Dror Israel describes itself as a network of **1,300 trained educators** living in **14 educators’ kibbutzim**, with **200,000+ children, teens, and families** in its national reach and substantial emergency-response experience, especially with traumatized children.

Current operational priorities include:
1. Emergency childcare in multiple cities for children of essential workers, including a requested hospital-based pop-up school.
2. Daily educational and emotional-support activities for evacuee children and youth (including Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva).
3. Public shelter programming in cities including Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, and Tiberias, with deployment of trained Jewish and Arab teen counselors and activity kits.
4. Pedagogical and trauma support across Dror Israel’s schools network (10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools), including support after missile-fragment impact at Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva (no injuries reported).
5. Online resilience sessions through movement and dance via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.

Dror Israel is requesting **emergency support for the next two weeks** to sustain and scale crisis response as Iran’s missile barrages continue. The organization’s network of **1,300 educators** and **200,000+ participants** delivers educational and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and emergency childcare for essential workers. Funding closes gaps for staff, transportation, supplies, and coordination during an active emergency.

## Context & Background

As Iran’s missile barrage continues, Israelis move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for repeated sirens. Families face trauma upon trauma and constant disruption. Dror Israel’s trained educators provide **educational, emotional, and trauma-support** programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms nationwide, with **local municipalities and government** partners assessing evolving needs.

The organization activates its network of **200,000+ children, teens, and families** through **direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom** so participants stay safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. Operations draw on **1,300 trained educators** in **14 educators’ kibbutzim**, with deep experience in **emergency response for traumatized children**.

**Current priorities** (combined detail from both threads):

- **Emergency childcare** for essential workers in **Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be'er Sheva** — schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels; **pop-up school** requested at a **major hospital** for medical staff children.
- **Evacuee hotels / centers** — including **distribution of clothing and supplies**; **daily activities** for evacuated children and youth from **Tel Aviv** and **Be'er Sheva**; emotional-educational support and respite for parents.
- **Public shelters** — **Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias**, and more; **hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors** trained as emergency junior counselors with **activity kits**; overnight stays where families lack home shelters (schools, parking lots, subway stations, per March 6 PDF).
- **Pedagogy of Hope** for the schools network; **Moledet Elementary** in **Be'er Sheva** hit by a **missile fragment** (school closed; **nobody harmed**); personalized trauma-informed guidance for staff and students.
- **Resilience through movement and dance** — online sessions via **Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers** in **Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa** for emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.

Sustaining **educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination** during this intensified period requires the requested emergency operating support.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and bracing for repeated sirens. Families are experiencing trauma upon trauma and constant disruption. Dror Israel's trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms across the country, in partnership with local municipalities and government. They are activating their nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. The organization operates from 14 educators' kibbutzim with 1,300 trained educators and has extensive experience in emergency response for traumatized children.

Current priorities include: emergency childcare for essential workers in multiple cities (Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be'er Sheva), including a requested pop-up school at a major hospital; daily activities for evacuee children in Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva; activities in public shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias and elsewhere with teen counselors and activity kits; in one version, production and distribution of 200 structured activity kits in coordination with local municipalities and the Ministry of Education's Youth and Society Administration, with training for local facilitators, volunteers, and youth leaders; pedagogical and trauma guidance for their schools network (Moledet in Be'er Sheva was hit by a missile fragment; school was closed, nobody harmed); and online resilience sessions through movement and dance in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa. Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires the requested emergency support.

# Israel Needs Us Once Again. And We Need You!
Educators, Community Builders, Emotional First Responders – We Are There
March 4, 2026 (one source) / March 6, 2026 (other source)

## Current Situation

As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, Israelis are living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma as families once again move into bomb shelters and daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Dror Israel's trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and even in living rooms across the country. Nimble and ready to go wherever needed, they are addressing urgent emotional and educational needs in real time, ensuring that no child feels alone.

Dror Israel educators, in partnership with local municipalities and government partners, are actively assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses as conditions change. Simultaneously, they are activating the nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. This is core educational work adapted to emergency conditions; sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires continued operating support.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs

Dror Israel is seeking **$250,000** (one version) / **$360,000** (other version) in emergency support for the next two weeks to continue reaching 200,000 participants nationwide, provide activities and emotional support for children and families in bomb shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond quickly as urgent needs emerge.

## Current Priorities

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers** — In partnership with local communities, emergency childcare centers for children of essential workers (medical staff, security personnel, critical service providers). Operating in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels. One major hospital has asked Dror Israel to establish a pop-up school on-site so working medical teams can continue caring for patients while their children are nearby in a safe, structured educational environment.
   - Funding gap: $125,000 (10 staff, transportation and supplies)
   - Funding gap: $50,000 (4 teachers, transportation, supplies — pop-up school)

2. **Educational activities at evacuee hotels** — Distribution of clothing and supplies; daily activities for evacuated children and youth from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva; emotional-educational support to provide some normalcy and give parents a break.
   - Funding gap: $10,000 (staff, supplies, transportation)

3. **Organizing activities in public shelters** — Activities for children in public bomb shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more. Hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors trained as emergency junior counselors with activity kits. In coordination with local municipalities and the Ministry of Education's Youth and Society Administration, rapid production and distribution of 200 structured activity kits for use in public shelters and residential buildings, with training for local facilitators, volunteers, and youth leaders to run safe, educational and emotional-support activities in accordance with safety regulations.
   - Funding gap: $60,000 (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies)
   - Funding gap: $25,000 (training and supplies for 200 kits — in $360k version)

4. **Pedagogy of Hope** — Dror Israel Schools Network: pedagogical guidance and trauma support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools. Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva was hit by a missile fragment (school was closed, nobody harmed). Personalized trauma-informed guidance for staff and students, teaching towards hope.
   - Funding gap: $80,000 (staff and pedagogical development)

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance** — Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa offering online resilience sessions through movement and dance for emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.
   - Funding gap: $10,000 (staff)

## The Ask

Dror Israel requests emergency support for a two-week response window during Iran's missile barrage, to sustain and expand educational and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, schools, and emergency childcare settings for children of essential workers.

Two versions report different total funding asks:
- **Version A:** **$250,000**
- **Version B:** **$360,000**

Both versions align on core interventions and most budget lines; the $360,000 version adds a dedicated line for structured shelter activity kits and training.

### Funding Breakdown (Consolidated)

- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- **Additional line in one version:** 200 structured activity kits for shelters (training and supplies): **$25,000**
- Pedagogy of Hope - trauma support for schools network (staff and pedagogical development): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): **$10,000**

### Non-Financial Ask

- Introduction of Philadelphia-area high schools, synagogues, and youth groups to Dror Israel's **"Ask Me Anything"** dialogue program connecting Israeli and American teens in open conversation about the war.  
- Referral contact: **contact-en@drorisrael.org.il**

## Context & Background

As missile attacks continue, families across Israel are repeatedly moving into shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and coping with prolonged uncertainty and trauma. Dror Israel's educators provide real-time educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in shelters, evacuee hotels/centers, schools, and community settings, in partnership with municipalities and government stakeholders.

The organization reports:
- A nationwide activation of over **200,000** children, teens, and families through direct outreach, calls, and structured Zoom programming.
- A workforce of **1,300 trained educators** living in **14 educators' kibbutzim**, with substantial emergency-response experience, especially with traumatized children.
- Ongoing need to cover educator hours, transportation, supplies, digital platforms, training, and coordination under rapidly changing emergency conditions.

### Current Priorities

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers**  
   Childcare frameworks for children of medical staff, security personnel, and other critical-service workers in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva; includes a requested hospital-based pop-up school.

2. **Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels/Centers**  
   Daily activities and emotional-educational support for evacuated children and youth (including Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva), plus distribution of clothing and supplies.

3. **Activities in Public Shelters**  
   Programming for children in public shelters (including Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional sites), supported by professional educators and hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors trained as emergency junior counselors.

4. **Pedagogy of Hope (Schools Network)**  
   Trauma-informed pedagogical support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools; includes support following missile-fragment impact at Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva (school closed at the time, no injuries).

5. **Resilience Through Community Movement and Dance**  
   Online sessions through Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa focused on emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.

6. **Structured Shelter Activity Kits (reported in one version)**  
   Rapid production/distribution of 200 kits, with facilitator/volunteer/youth-leader training, coordinated with municipalities and Israel Ministry of Education Youth and Society Administration.

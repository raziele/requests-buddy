## The Ask

Dror Israel requests emergency support for a two-week response window during ongoing missile attacks, to sustain and expand educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming for children and families in shelters, evacuee hotels, schools, and community settings. Their emergency infrastructure includes 1,300 trained educators living in 14 educators' kibbutzim and a nationwide network reaching 200,000+ participants through in-person operations, direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are repeatedly moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and facing ongoing fear, disruption, and cumulative trauma. Dror Israel educators, in partnership with municipalities and government partners, are assessing changing needs and deploying targeted emergency interventions in real time.

Current priority operations include:

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers**  
   Emergency childcare frameworks in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, including a requested hospital-based pop-up school.

2. **Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels**  
   Distribution of clothing and supplies, plus daily emotional-educational activities for evacuated children and youth, including evacuees connected to Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva centers.

3. **Activities in Public Shelters**  
   Child-focused programming in shelters (including schools, parking lots, and subway settings) in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional localities. Hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors trained as emergency junior counselors are being deployed with activity kits.

4. **Pedagogy of Hope (Schools Network)**  
   Trauma-informed pedagogical guidance for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools. Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva was hit by a missile fragment (school closed; no injuries reported).

5. **Resilience Through Movement and Dance**  
   Online resilience sessions via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa to support emotional release, regulation, and community connection.

## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting emergency support for a two-week response period during Iran's ongoing missile barrage, to sustain and scale educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming for children, youth, and families in shelters, evacuee sites, schools, and community frameworks nationwide.

Two versions of the funding ask appear in source materials:

- **Version A total:** **$250,000**
- **Version B total:** **$360,000**

Both versions include core emergency components (childcare for essential workers, hospital pop-up school, evacuee hotel programming, public shelter activities, Pedagogy of Hope support, and resilience-through-movement sessions). The **$360,000** version additionally includes a dedicated line for **200 structured activity kits** and includes a **non-financial ask** for U.S.-Israel teen dialogue introductions.

### Funding Breakdown (Consolidated)

- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for schools network (staff and pedagogical development): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): **$10,000**
- Structured activity kits for shelters (200 kits; training and supplies): **$25,000** *(included in the $360,000 version)*

### Non-Financial Ask

- Introductions of Philadelphia-area high schools, synagogues, or youth groups to Dror Israel's **"Ask Me Anything"** dialogue program (Israeli and American teens in open conversation about the war).  
  Referral contact: **contact-en@drorisrael.org.il**

## Context & Background

As missile attacks continue, families are repeatedly moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and coping with sustained uncertainty and trauma. Dror Israel reports that its educators are providing real-time educational and emotional support in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, and home-based/online environments.

Operational model described across documents:

- Partnership with local municipalities and government actors to assess and respond to changing needs
- Nationwide outreach to 200,000+ children, teens, and families through calls, direct outreach, and structured Zoom programming
- Deployment of 1,300 trained educators living in 14 educators' kibbutzim
- Emergency-response emphasis on traumatized children, continuity of care, and safe structured activity

### Current Priorities

1. **Emergency childcare for essential workers**  
   Active in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, including temporary frameworks in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels. Includes requested hospital-based pop-up school for medical staff children.

2. **Educational activities at evacuee hotels/centers**  
   Daily activities and emotional-educational support for evacuated children and youth (including Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva), with clothing/supplies distribution and caregiver relief.

3. **Activities in public shelters**  
   Child-focused programming in public shelters (including Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, and Tiberias), with deployment of trained Jewish and Arab teen counselors and activity kits.

4. **Pedagogy of Hope (schools network)**  
   Trauma-informed pedagogical support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools. Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva was struck by missile fragments (school closed at the time; no injuries reported).

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance**  
   Online resilience sessions via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa, focused on emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.

6. **Structured shelter-activity kits (additional item in one source)**  
   Rapid production and distribution of 200 structured kits, plus facilitator/volunteer/youth-leader training aligned with safety regulations and municipal coordination.

## About Dror Israel

Dror Israel describes itself as a nationwide social-educational movement and emergency responder, with **1,300 trained educators** in **14 educators' kibbutzim**, supporting children and communities under wartime conditions through educational continuity, trauma support, and community resilience programming.

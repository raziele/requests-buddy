## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting short-term emergency operating support to sustain and expand its crisis response during Iran’s ongoing missile barrages, as Israelis again move into bomb shelters and evacuee centers and experience repeated trauma and disruption.

Two closely related budget versions are presented:

- **Initial emergency request (March 4, 2026):**  
  - **Total Funding Requested:** $250,000  
  - Focus: emergency childcare for essential workers, activities in evacuee hotels and public shelters, trauma-informed pedagogy (“Pedagogy of Hope”) in the Dror Israel schools network, and resilience-through-movement sessions.

- **Updated emergency request (March 6, 2026 — Sheagat HaAri Emergency Response):**  
  - **Total Funding Requested:** $360,000  
  - Builds on the initial framework and **adds** funding for rapidly producing and distributing **200 structured activity kits** for public and residential shelters, including training for local facilitators and youth leaders.

Across both versions, the core programmatic components remain consistent:

**Combined Funding Breakdown (All Line Items Across Both Versions)**

- **Emergency childcare for essential workers** — emergency childcare centers for children of medical staff, security personnel, and critical service providers, in partnership with local communities; operating in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels.  
  - Funding gap: **$125,000** (10 staff, transportation, supplies)

- **Pop-up school at a major hospital** — requested by a large hospital to allow medical teams to continue caring for patients while their children are nearby in a safe, structured educational environment.  
  - Funding gap: **$50,000** (4 teachers, transportation, supplies)

- **Educational activities at evacuee hotels / evacuee centers** — distribution of clothing and supplies; daily activities and emotional-educational support for evacuated children and youth from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva, providing normalcy and giving parents a break.  
  - Funding gap: **$10,000** (staff, supplies, transportation)

- **Organizing activities in public shelters** — activities for children in public bomb shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional locations. Many families without appropriate home shelters are staying overnight in public shelters in schools, parking lots, and subway stations. Dror Israel operates activities with its professional educators, alongside hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors trained as emergency junior counselors and deployed with ready-to-use activity kits.  
  - Funding gap: **$60,000** (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies)

- **Structured activity kits for shelters (added in updated request)** — rapid production and distribution of **200 structured activity kits** for use in public shelters and residential buildings, with quick training of local facilitators, volunteers, and youth leaders to run safe, educational, and emotional-support activities in accordance with safety regulations.  
  - Funding gap: **$25,000** (training and supplies)

  - Funding gap: **$10,000** (staff)

## Context & Background

As Iran’s deadly missile barrages continue, Israelis must repeatedly move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Families are living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma as daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Many lack appropriate home shelters and are forced to stay overnight in public shelters in schools, parking lots, and subway stations.

Dror Israel’s trained educators — 1,300 educators living in 14 educators’ kibbutzim — are acting as educators, community builders, and emotional first responders. In partnership with local municipalities, the Ministry of Education’s Youth and Society Administration, and other government partners, they are:

- Providing **educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming** in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and even in living rooms across the country.  
- Activating a **nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families** through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.  
- Operating emergency childcare centers so essential workers (medical, security, critical infrastructure) can continue their work while knowing their children are in safe, structured environments.  
- Running daily activities and emotional-educational support for evacuated children and youth in hotels and evacuee centers in **Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva**.  
- Organizing activities in public bomb shelters in **Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias**, and other locations, with hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors trained and deployed with activity kits.  
- Delivering **Pedagogy of Hope** support across the Dror Israel schools network (10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools), including Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva, which sustained damage from a missile fragment while closed.  
- Offering **online resilience sessions** through movement and dance at Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in **Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa**.

This work represents Dror Israel’s core educational mission adapted to emergency conditions. Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, transportation, supplies, coordination, and pedagogical development during this intensified period requires the requested emergency support.

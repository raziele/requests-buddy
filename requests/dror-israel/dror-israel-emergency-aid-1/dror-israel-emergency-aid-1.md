## The Ask

Dror Israel seeks **emergency support for the next two weeks** to sustain and scale crisis response while Iran’s missile barrage continues. Educators (network of **1,300** across **14 educators’ kibbutzim**, **200,000+** participants) deliver educational and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, living rooms, emergency childcare for essential workers, evacuee-hotel activities, public-shelter activities, **Pedagogy of Hope** for the schools network, and **resilience-through-movement/dance** sessions (Tamara centers online).

## Context & Background

As the missile crisis continues, Israelis move into shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and face repeated sirens and layered trauma. Dror Israel works with **local municipalities and government partners**, assesses evolving needs, and coordinates emergency response.

Programming includes: **emergency childcare** in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be’er Sheva (schools, community centers, boarding schools, evacuee hotels); **pop-up school** requested at a major hospital; **evacuee-hotel / center** support (including Tel Aviv and Be’er Sheva; March 6 text adds **clothing/supply distribution** in a Tel Aviv evacuee center); **public shelter** activities (Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, etc.) with **hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors** trained as emergency junior counselors with kits; **Pedagogy of Hope** for the school network — **Moledet Elementary, Be’er Sheva** struck by missile fragment while closed (**no injuries**); **Tamara** dance/wellbeing online sessions for regulation and connection.

Core operations require sustaining **educator hours**, **digital platforms** (Zoom, etc.), and **coordination** during the intensified period.

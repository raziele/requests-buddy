## The Ask

Dror Israel is seeking emergency support over a two-week period to sustain and expand crisis response during ongoing Iranian missile attacks. The organization reports active deployment of 1,300 educators across emergency childcare frameworks, bomb shelters, evacuee hotels/centers, school trauma response, and online resilience programming.

### Funding Requested (reported in sources)

- **Version A:** **$250,000**
- **Version B:** **$360,000**

### Consolidated Funding Breakdown (all unique budget lines preserved)

- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels/centers (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- 200 structured activity kits for shelters/residential buildings (training and supplies): **$25,000** *(appears in $360,000 version only)*
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for Dror Israel schools network (staff and pedagogical development): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): **$10,000**

### Non-Financial Ask

Introduction of Philadelphia-area high schools, synagogues, or youth groups to Dror Israel’s **“Ask Me Anything”** dialogue program connecting Israeli and American teens in open discussion about the war. Referrals: **contact-en@drorisrael.org.il**.

## Context & Background

As missile attacks continue, families are repeatedly moving into shelters, evacuating homes, and coping with ongoing trauma and disruption. Dror Israel describes a nationwide emergency education and psychosocial response carried out with municipalities and government partners, including direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to keep children and families connected and emotionally supported.

Current operational priorities include:

1. **Emergency childcare for essential workers** in multiple cities (Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be'er Sheva), including a requested hospital pop-up school.
2. **Evacuee support** in Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva with daily educational-emotional activities and basic supplies.
3. **Public shelter programming** in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and elsewhere, including deployment of hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors.
4. **Trauma-informed school support (Pedagogy of Hope)** for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools; includes Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva, reportedly struck by missile fragment while closed.
5. **Online resilience sessions** through Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.

Dror Israel describes itself as a social-change movement with **1,300 trained educators in 14 educators’ kibbutzim**, emphasizing prior emergency-response experience with traumatized children.

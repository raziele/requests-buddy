## Context & Background

As Iran’s missile barrage continues, Israelis move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and face repeated sirens, trauma, and disruption. Dror Israel’s trained educators provide educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in **bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms**, with **local municipalities and government** partners, assessing evolving needs and coordinating response.

The organization activates a nationwide network of **more than 200,000** children, teens, and families via **direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom**, aiming to keep participants safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. It operates from **14 educators’ kibbutzim** with **1,300** trained educators and emphasizes experience in **emergency response for traumatized children**.

**Current priorities** (consistent across sources, with added detail where only one source had it):

1. **Emergency childcare for essential workers** in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be’er Sheva — schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels; **one major hospital** requested an on-site **pop-up school** for medical staff children. One source adds that frameworks provide **safe supervision, structured activities, and emotional support** while parents serve in civilian response roles.

2. **Educational activities at evacuee hotels** — **distribution of clothing and supplies** (e.g., evacuee center in Tel Aviv cited in Mar 6 PDF); **daily activities** for evacuated children/youth from **Tel Aviv and Be’er Sheva**; **emotional-educational** support and respite for parents.

3. **Public shelter activities** — Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more; **hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors** as emergency junior counselors with **activity kits**. The **Mar 6** document adds: coordination with **local municipalities** and the **Ministry of Education’s Youth and Society Administration**; **200 structured activity kits** for shelters/residential buildings; rapid **training** of facilitators/volunteers/youth leaders per safety regulations (**$25,000** gap in that document).

4. **Pedagogy of Hope** — network of **10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools**; **Moledet Elementary** in Be’er Sheva **hit by a missile fragment** (school closed, **nobody harmed**); **personalized trauma-informed guidance** for staff and students.

5. **Resilience through movement/dance** — **Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers** in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa; **online** sessions for emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.

Sustaining **educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination** during the intensified period is cited as requiring emergency operating support.

# Israel Needs Us Once Again. And We Need You!  
Educators, Community Builders, Emotional First Responders – We Are There  
March 6, 2026

## Current Situation

As Iran’s deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, Israelis are living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma as families once again move into bomb shelters and daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and even in living rooms across the country — nimble and ready to go wherever needed, addressing urgent emotional and educational needs in real time so **no child feels alone**.

Dror Israel educators, with local municipalities and government partners, **assess evolving needs** and **coordinate targeted emergency responses** as conditions change. Simultaneously, the nationwide network of **more than 200,000** children, teens, and families is activated through **direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom** so participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. Core educational work is adapted to emergency conditions; **sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination** requires continued operating support.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs

**$360,000** in emergency support for the **next two weeks** to keep reaching **200,000 participants** nationwide, provide activities and emotional support in **bomb shelters and evacuee hotels**, and respond as urgent needs emerge.

## Current Priorities

1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers** — centers for children of essential workers so medical, security, and critical-service personnel can continue work; Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be’er Sheva; temporary frameworks in schools, community centers, boarding schools, evacuee hotels; **$125,000** (10 staff, transportation, supplies). Hospital pop-up school: **$50,000** (4 teachers, transportation, supplies).

2. **Educational activities at evacuee hotels** — growing evacuee wave; clothing/supplies distribution (Tel Aviv center example); daily activities for evacuated children/youth from Tel Aviv and Be’er Sheva; **$10,000** (staff, supplies, transportation).

3. **Organizing activities in public shelters** — overnight stays in public shelters (schools, parking lots, subway stations); Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, etc.; teen counselors deployed with kits; **$60,000** (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies). With municipalities and Ministry of Education Youth and Society: **200 structured activity kits** + training for facilitators/volunteers/youth leaders per safety rules; **$25,000** (training and supplies).

4. **Pedagogy of Hope** — 10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools; Moledet Elementary Be’er Sheva hit by fragment (closed, no injuries); personalized trauma-informed guidance; **$80,000** (staff and pedagogical development).

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance** — Tamara centers (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa); online movement/dance sessions; **$10,000** (staff).

## About Dror Israel

Leading force for social change: **1,300** trained educators in **14 educators’ kibbutzim**; extensive **emergency response** experience, especially for traumatized children; bringing stability and hope amid war.

## The Ask

Dror Israel requests emergency support for a two-week response window during ongoing missile attacks, to sustain and expand educational, emotional, and trauma-support operations in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, emergency childcare settings, and school communities nationwide. Their network includes 1,300 trained educators across 14 educators’ kibbutzim and reaches more than 200,000 children, teens, and families.

### Funding Request (difference noted)

Two source versions report different totals:

- **Version A total:** **$250,000**
- **Version B / updated proposal total:** **$360,000**

### Combined Funding Breakdown (all unique line items preserved)

- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels/centers (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- 200 structured activity kits for shelters/residential buildings (training and supplies): **$25,000** *(appears in $360,000 version only)*
- Pedagogy of Hope (trauma support for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): **$10,000**

### Non-Financial Ask

Dror Israel requests introductions from the Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia network (high schools, synagogues, youth groups) to join the **“Ask Me Anything”** Israeli-American teen dialogue program about the war.  
Referral contact: **contact-en@drorisrael.org.il**

## Context & Background

As Iran’s missile barrage continues, Israelis are repeatedly moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and living under frequent sirens and uncertainty. Dror Israel educators are delivering real-time educational and trauma-support programming in shelters, evacuee settings, and homes, in partnership with municipalities and government.

Key operational activities include:

1. **Emergency childcare for essential workers** in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva, including a requested hospital pop-up school.
2. **Evacuee-center/hotel support** in Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva, including distribution of clothing/supplies and daily activities for children and youth.
3. **Public shelter programming** in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and elsewhere, with deployment of trained Jewish and Arab teen counselors.
4. **Structured activity-kit scale-up** (200 kits) with facilitator training and municipal/Ministry of Education coordination.
5. **Pedagogy of Hope** trauma-informed guidance for Dror Israel’s school network; includes support after a missile fragment struck Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva (school closed; no injuries).
6. **Online resilience programming** via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.
7. **Nationwide outreach and continuity** through direct calls and structured Zoom programming to maintain safety, emotional support, and trusted adult connection.

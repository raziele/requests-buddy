## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **emergency support for the next two weeks** to sustain and scale crisis response as Iran’s missile barrages continue. Their network of **1,300 educators** and **200,000+ participants** delivers educational and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and emergency childcare for essential workers. Funding closes gaps for staff, transportation, supplies, and coordination during an active emergency.

**Funding requested (reconcile both PDFs):**

- **$250,000** — March 4, 2026 proposal (“Israel Needs Us Once Again…”)  
- **$360,000** — March 6, 2026 proposal (“Sheagat HaAri Emergency Response March 2026”); **+$110,000** vs March 4 aligns with the added **$25,000** activity-kit line plus updated total (see breakdown).

**Unified funding breakdown** (union of both documents):

- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- **200 structured activity kits** for shelters — training and supplies: **$25,000** *(in March 6 materials; includes coordination with municipalities and Ministry of Education Youth and Society Administration, rapid production/distribution, facilitator training)*
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for schools network (10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools; staff and pedagogical development): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Tamara centers — Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): **$10,000**

**Non-financial ask** (March 6 / Philadelphia thread): Introductions for high schools, synagogues, or youth groups interested in the **“Ask Me Anything”** dialogue program (Israeli and American teens). Refer to **contact-en@drorisrael.org.il**.

## Context & Background

As Iran’s missile barrage continues, Israelis move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for repeated sirens. Families face trauma upon trauma and constant disruption. Dror Israel’s trained educators provide educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms nationwide, with **local municipalities and government** partners assessing evolving needs.

The organization activates **200,000+** children, teens, and families via direct outreach, phone calls, and structured **Zoom** programming so participants stay safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. It operates from **14 educators’ kibbutzim** with **1,300** trained educators and deep experience in emergency response for traumatized children.

**Current operational priorities** (both sources align):

- Emergency childcare for essential workers in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be’er Sheva (schools, community centers, boarding schools, evacuee hotels), including a **pop-up school** requested at a **major hospital**
- **Evacuee hotels / centers:** distributing clothing and supplies; **daily activities** for evacuated children and youth from **Tel Aviv** and **Be’er Sheva**; emotional-educational support
- **Public shelters:** Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more — **hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors** as emergency junior counselors with **activity kits**; March 6 adds **200 structured kits** and scaled facilitator training
- **Pedagogy of Hope** for the schools network; **Moledet Elementary** in Be’er Sheva was **hit by a missile fragment** (school closed; **nobody harmed**); personalized trauma-informed guidance for staff and students
- **Resilience through movement and dance** online from Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa

Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination in this intensified period requires the requested emergency operating support.

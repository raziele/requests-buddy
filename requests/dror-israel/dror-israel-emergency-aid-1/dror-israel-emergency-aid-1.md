## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **emergency support for the next two weeks** to sustain and scale crisis response as Iran's missile barrages continue. Two versions of the formal materials circulated:

| Version | Stated total | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| March 4 materials / first email summary | **$250,000** | Line items listed below **without** the separate $25k “200 structured activity kits” row sum to **$335,000** — an internal inconsistency in the source document. |
| March 6 materials / second email summary | **$360,000** | Adds **$25,000** for **200 structured activity kits** (training and supplies); line items sum to $360,000. |

Programming covers: emergency childcare for frontline workers; pop-up school at a major hospital for medical staff children; activities in evacuee hotels and public shelters; **Pedagogy of Hope** trauma-informed support for the schools network; **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance** (Tamara centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa). The March 6 PDF adds that, **in coordination with local municipalities and the Ministry of Education's Youth and Society Administration**, the organization is producing and distributing **200 structured activity kits** for public shelters and residential buildings, with rapid training for facilitators, volunteers, and youth leaders per safety regulations.

**Non-Financial Ask** *(second source / Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia framing):* Introductions for Philadelphia-area **high schools, synagogues, or youth groups** interested in the **“Ask Me Anything”** dialogue program (Israeli and American teens discussing the war). Refer interested parties to **contact-en@drorisrael.org.il**.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and face repeated sirens; families experience layered trauma and disruption. Dror Israel's **1,300 trained educators** on **14 educators' kibbutzim** deliver educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and homes, with **local municipalities and government** partners assessing needs and coordinating response. The network reaches **200,000+** children, teens, and families through outreach, calls, and structured Zoom programming so participants stay safe, supported, and connected to trusted adults.

**Operational priorities** (both sources, merged): emergency childcare in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be'er Sheva (schools, community centers, boarding schools, evacuee hotels) including **safe supervision, structured activities, and emotional support**; **pop-up school** requested by a major hospital; **daily activities** for evacuated children/youth in **Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva** (including clothing/supply distribution per March 6); **public shelter** activities in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias and elsewhere with **hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors** as emergency junior counselors with activity kits; **Pedagogy of Hope** for the schools network — **Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva** hit by a missile fragment while **closed**, no injuries; **online movement/dance** resilience sessions via Tamara centers. Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination requires emergency operating support.

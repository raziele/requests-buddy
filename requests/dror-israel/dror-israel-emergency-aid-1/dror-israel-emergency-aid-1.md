## Context & Background

As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis are moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and bracing for repeated sirens. Across the country, families are living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma and repeated disruption. Dror Israel’s trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in:
- bomb shelters
- evacuee centers
- living rooms and community settings

Dror Israel educators work in partnership with local municipalities and government partners, actively assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses as conditions change. In parallel, Dror Israel is activating its nationwide network of more than **200,000** children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured **Zoom** programming—so participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.

Dror Israel emphasizes that it is nimble and ready to go wherever needed, addressing urgent emotional and educational needs in real time to ensure **no child feels alone**. Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires emergency operating support.

## Program Components (Current Priorities)

1. **Emergency childcare for essential workers**
   - Emergency childcare centers enable medical staff, security personnel, and critical service providers to continue their work.
   - Operating in multiple cities including **Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be'er Sheva**.
   - Childcare frameworks are staffed by counselors and youth movement leaders in **schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels**.
   - Provides safe supervision, structured activities, and emotional support for children whose parents are on the front lines of civilian response.
   - Includes a **pop-up school at a major hospital** requested for medical staff children so that working medical teams can care for patients knowing children are nearby in a safe, structured environment.
   - Funding gaps reflected in sources:
     - $125,000 (10 staff, transportation, supplies)
     - $50,000 (4 teachers, transportation, supplies)

2. **Educational activities at evacuee hotels**
   - Distribution of clothing and supplies, plus daily activities for evacuee children and youth from **Tel Aviv** and **Be'er Sheva**.
   - Emotional-educational support provides normalcy amid uncertainty and loss and gives parents a break.
   - Funding gap: $10,000 (staff, supplies, transportation)

3. **Organizing activities in public shelters**
   - Many families lack appropriate home shelters and stay overnight in **public bomb shelters** in **schools, parking lots, and subway stations**.
   - Dror Israel is already operating activities in **Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias**, and more.
   - Alongside professional educators, **hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors** trained as **emergency junior counselors** are deployed with **ready-to-use activity kits**.
   - Funding gaps reflected in sources:
     - $60,000 (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies)
     - $25,000 (only in the $360,000 variant): production and distribution of **200 structured activity kits**, coordination with local municipalities and the **Ministry of Education’s Youth and Society Administration**, and training local facilitators/volunteers/youth leaders to use kits for safe, educational, emotional-support activities in line with safety regulations.

4. **Pedagogy of Hope**
   - Dror Israel Schools Network provides pedagogical guidance and trauma support for:
     - **10 elementary schools**
     - **9 high schools**
     - **10 preschools**
   - Includes **Moledet Elementary School** in **Be'er Sheva**, hit by a missile fragment; the school was closed and **nobody was harmed**.
   - Personalized, trauma-informed guidance supports staff and students, with teaching oriented toward hope in these times of war.
   - Funding gap: $80,000 (staff and pedagogical development)

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance**
   - Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in **Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa** offer **online resilience sessions** through movement and dance.
   - Guided sessions create structured spaces for:
     - emotional release
     - physical regulation
     - community connection
   - Funding gap: $10,000 (staff)

## About Dror Israel

Dror Israel describes itself as a leading force for social change, with **1,300 trained educators** living in **14 educators’ kibbutzim** throughout Israel. With extensive experience in emergency response—especially for traumatized children—Dror Israel brings immediate stability and hope to the chaos of war.

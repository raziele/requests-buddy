## The Ask

Dror Israel requests emergency funding for a two-week response window during ongoing Iranian missile barrages. Across Israel, its 1,300 educators are delivering educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, schools, and online formats, while supporting children of essential workers and local emergency systems.

Two versions of the request were circulated:

- **Version A (March 4):** **$250,000** total request
- **Version B (March 6):** **$360,000** total request

The shared core programming areas are identical; the higher total includes an additional dedicated line for structured shelter activity kits and related training.

### Funding Breakdown (Unified)

- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels/centers (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- Pedagogy of Hope (trauma-informed school support, staff/pedagogical development): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): **$10,000**
- Structured shelter activity kits (200 kits) plus facilitator/youth leader training: **$25,000** *(included in March 6 version)*

### Total Scenarios

- **Without activity-kit line:** **$250,000**
- **With activity-kit line:** **$275,000** (line-item sum)
- **Stated in March 6 PDF/email:** **$360,000** (total stated, exceeds listed line items)

### Non-Financial Ask

Dror Israel also requested introductions to Philadelphia-area high schools, synagogues, and youth groups for the **“Ask Me Anything”** Israeli-American teen dialogue program; interested groups should contact **contact-en@drorisrael.org.il**.

## Context & Background

As missile attacks continue, families are repeatedly displaced to shelters and evacuee settings, with severe emotional strain and disruption to normal life. Dror Israel reports active coordination with municipalities, government partners, and (in one source) the Ministry of Education’s Youth and Society Administration. Their teams conduct direct outreach, phone contact, and structured Zoom programming to maintain safety, psychosocial support, and trusted adult connection for participants nationwide.

Operational priorities include:

1. **Emergency childcare for essential workers** in multiple cities, including a requested hospital-based pop-up school.
2. **Evacuee support programming** with daily educational/emotional activities and distribution of basic supplies.
3. **Public shelter programming** in cities including Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, and Tiberias, with deployment of trained Jewish and Arab teen counselors.
4. **Pedagogy of Hope** across Dror Israel’s schools network, including trauma-informed support after missile-fragment impact at Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva (no injuries reported).
5. **Online movement/dance resilience sessions** through Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers.

Dror Israel describes itself as a long-standing social-change movement with **1,300 trained educators** living in **14 educators’ kibbutzim**, with deep emergency-response experience for traumatized children.

## The Ask

Dror Israel requests urgent two-week emergency operating support to sustain and expand trauma-informed educational response during ongoing missile attacks. Their 1,300 educators (across 14 educators' kibbutzim) are delivering emergency childcare, shelter and evacuee programming, school-based trauma pedagogy, and resilience sessions through movement and dance, in partnership with municipalities and government.

Two funding versions appear in the source materials:

- **Version A (March 4 document): $250,000**
- **Version B (March 6 document): $360,000**

The shared core budget lines are identical across both versions, while Version B adds a dedicated line for structured shelter kits.

### Funding Breakdown (Combined)

- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- Pedagogy of Hope (staff and pedagogical development / trauma support for 10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): **$10,000**
- 200 structured activity kits for shelters (training and supplies): **$25,000** *(present in Version B only)*

### Non-Financial Ask

- Introductions from Philadelphia-area high schools, synagogues, and youth groups to Dror Israel’s **“Ask Me Anything”** dialogue program (Israeli and American teens in open conversation about the war), via **contact-en@drorisrael.org.il**.

## Context & Background

As Iran’s missile barrage continues, families across Israel are repeatedly moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and coping with ongoing trauma and disruption. Dror Israel reports active emergency programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, schools, and home-based/online settings.

Core operational elements include:

1. Emergency childcare frameworks for essential workers in multiple cities, including a requested hospital pop-up school.
2. Daily educational and emotional-support activities for evacuee children and youth (including Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva).
3. Public-shelter programming in cities such as Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, and Tiberias, including deployment of hundreds of trained Jewish and Arab teen counselors.
4. Pedagogical and trauma-informed support across Dror Israel’s schools network; Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva was hit by a missile fragment (school closed at the time; no injuries reported).
5. Online resilience sessions through Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.
6. Continued nationwide outreach to 200,000+ participants via direct contact, phone, and structured Zoom support.

A noted difference between documents is the expanded shelter-kit initiative in the March 6 version: rapid production/distribution of **200 structured activity kits**, with facilitator training in coordination with municipalities and the Ministry of Education’s Youth and Society Administration.

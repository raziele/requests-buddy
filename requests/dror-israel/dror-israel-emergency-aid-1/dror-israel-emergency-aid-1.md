## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting emergency support to sustain (and in one message, expand) its crisis response over the next two weeks as missile attacks continue and Israelis again move into bomb shelters and evacuee centers.

They report that their trained educators are delivering educational and trauma-support programming in:
- bomb shelters and public shelter settings
- evacuee centers / evacuee hotels
- emergency childcare frameworks for children of essential workers
- school-network support (including trauma-informed guidance for staff and students)

They also emphasize coordination and staffing continuity (educator hours, digital platforms, and on-the-ground coordination) during the intensified period.

## Context & Background

As Iran’s missile barrage continues, Israelis report a repeated cycle of entering bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and adjusting to ongoing disruption and fear—described as “trauma upon trauma.”

Dror Israel positions its response as nimble emergency education and emotional first response, implemented through:
- partnerships with local municipalities and government partners
- ongoing needs assessment as conditions evolve
- direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to keep children, teens, and families connected to trusted adults

### Current priorities (combined)
1. **Emergency childcare for essential workers**  
   Emergency childcare centers/frameworks so medical staff, security personnel, and critical service providers can continue frontline work. Operating in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be’er Sheva, in settings such as schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels.  
   - Source 1 and Source 2 report the same core funding gaps for staffing/transportation/supplies (**$125,000**).
   - A major hospital is described as requesting a **pop-up school** on-site for medical teams’ children.  
     - Source 1 and Source 2 report the same funding gap (**$50,000**).

2. **Educational activities at evacuee hotels / evacuee centers**  
   Distribution of clothing and supplies alongside daily emotional-educational support activities to provide some normalcy and give parents a break.  
   - Funding gap reported as **$10,000** in both sources.

3. **Organizing activities in public shelters**  
   Activities for children in public bomb shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and more, supported by hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors trained as emergency junior counselors with ready-to-use activity kits.  
   - Funding gap reported as **$60,000** for volunteer organizers, transportation, and supplies (both sources).
   - **Source 2 adds:** rapid production and distribution of **200 structured activity kits** for public shelters and residential buildings, with training and supplies (**$25,000**).

4. **Pedagogy of Hope (schools network trauma support)**  
   Trauma-informed pedagogical guidance and support for staff and students across:
   - 10 elementary schools,
   - 9 high schools,
   - 10 preschools.  
   A referenced example is **Moledet Elementary School in Be’er Sheva**, which was hit by a missile fragment; one source notes the school was closed and nobody was harmed.  
   - Funding gap reported as **$80,000** (both sources).

5. **Resilience through Community Movement and Dance**  
   Online resilience sessions through movement and dance (Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers) in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa—intended for structured emotional release, physical regulation, and community connection.  
   - Funding gap reported as **$10,000** (both sources).

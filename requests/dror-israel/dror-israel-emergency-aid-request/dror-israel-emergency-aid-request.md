## The Ask

Dror Israel requests emergency support for a two-week period during ongoing missile attacks, to sustain and scale educational, emotional, and trauma-support response for children and families in shelters, evacuee centers, and emergency childcare settings across Israel.

Two versions of the financial ask were provided:
- **Version A:** **$250,000**
- **Version B:** **$360,000** (includes an additional line item for 200 structured activity kits)

Both versions describe the same core emergency response platform: emergency childcare for essential workers, evacuee-hotel programming, public-shelter activities, pedagogy/trauma support for schools, and resilience-through-movement sessions.

### Funding Breakdown (Combined)

- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): **$125,000**
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): **$50,000**
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): **$10,000**
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): **$60,000**
- 200 structured activity kits for shelters/residential buildings (training and supplies): **$25,000** *(included in $360,000 version)*
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for schools network (staff and pedagogical development): **$80,000**
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): **$10,000**

### Non-Financial Ask

Introductions to Philadelphia-area high school groups, synagogues, or youth groups interested in joining Dror Israel’s **“Ask Me Anything”** dialogue program (Israeli and American teens in open conversation about the war).  
Contact: **contact-en@drorisrael.org.il**

## Context & Background

As Iran’s missile barrage continues, families are repeatedly moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and coping with prolonged uncertainty and trauma. Dror Israel’s educators are delivering educational and emotional support in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, schools, and home-based/online settings.

In partnership with municipalities and government stakeholders, Dror Israel is assessing changing needs and coordinating rapid response while activating a nationwide network of more than 200,000 participants through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming.

Current operational priorities include:
- Emergency childcare for essential workers across multiple cities, including a requested hospital-based pop-up school.
- Daily support activities for evacuated children and youth in evacuee centers/hotels (including Tel Aviv and Be’er Sheva), alongside clothing/supplies distribution.
- Programming in public shelters (including Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and others), with trained Jewish and Arab teen counselors and activity kits.
- Pedagogical and trauma-informed support for Dror Israel’s schools network (10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools), including support after missile-fragment impact at Moledet Elementary School in Be’er Sheva (no injuries reported).
- Online resilience sessions through movement and dance via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.

Dror Israel reports 1,300 trained educators living in 14 educators’ kibbutzim and frames this as emergency-adapted core educational work requiring operating support for staffing, transportation, supplies, digital platforms, and coordination.

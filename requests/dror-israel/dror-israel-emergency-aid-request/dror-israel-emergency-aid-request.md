## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and scale its crisis response during the current escalation. Their network of **1,300 educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim** is providing educational activities, emotional and trauma support, and emergency childcare for over 200,000 children, teens, and families in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, schools, and temporary childcare sites for essential workers.

The request is intended to close urgent gaps in staffing, transportation, supplies, educator hours, digital platforms, and operational coordination while needs continue to evolve.

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school on-site at a hospital for children of medical staff (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels/centers (staff, supplies, transportation): $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support and pedagogical development for 10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, and 10 preschools: $80,000
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (staff): $10,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Introductions, guidance, or recommendations to individuals, foundations, or emergency funds that may support this work; availability to provide a short presentation, briefing, or update call (including for Detroit community/leadership) on field conditions and response activities.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, families across Israel are repeatedly moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and living in ongoing fear and uncertainty. Dror Israel educators are delivering educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, and living rooms across the country.

In partnership with municipalities and government actors, Dror Israel is continuously assessing needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses. At the same time, it is activating a nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming so participants remain safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults.

Current priorities include:
1. Emergency childcare for essential workers in cities including Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva.
2. A hospital-requested pop-up school so medical teams can work while children remain nearby in a safe educational setting.
3. Daily educational and emotional support activities for evacuees, including displaced children and youth from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva.
4. Activities in public bomb shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional locations, including deployment of hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors with activity kits.
5. Trauma-informed school support through the Pedagogy of Hope framework, including support to Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva after a missile fragment strike while the school was closed.
6. Online resilience sessions through movement and dance in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers.

## Current Situation

As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, families are experiencing repeated trauma and disruption. Dror Israel's trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms, ensuring children are not left alone during crisis conditions.

In partnership with municipalities and government partners, Dror Israel is assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted responses. Simultaneously, it is activating a nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs

Dror Israel is seeking **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to continue reaching participants nationwide, provide activities and emotional support for children and families in shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond rapidly to emerging needs.

   - **Funding gap: $125,000** (10 staff, transportation, supplies).
   - Hospital pop-up school request for children of medical teams.
   - **Funding gap: $50,000** (4 teachers, transportation, supplies).

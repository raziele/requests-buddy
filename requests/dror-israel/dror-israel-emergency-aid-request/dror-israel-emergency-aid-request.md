## The Ask

Dror Israel requests emergency operating support for a two-week period to sustain and expand real-time crisis response during continued missile attacks and displacement. Activities include emergency childcare for essential workers, educational/emotional support in evacuee hotels and public shelters, trauma-informed school support through “Pedagogy of Hope,” and online resilience sessions via movement and dance.

## Context & Background

As Iran’s missile barrage continues, families across Israel are repeatedly moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and experiencing ongoing trauma and disruption. Dror Israel reports deploying trained educators to provide educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, schools, and homes.

In partnership with municipalities and government actors, Dror Israel is assessing changing needs and coordinating targeted responses. The organization is also activating its network of more than 200,000 participants via direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to maintain safety, emotional support, and trusted adult connection.

Operational highlights include:

1. Emergency childcare for children of essential workers in multiple cities.
2. Requested pop-up hospital school to support medical teams’ children.
3. Daily educational/emotional activities in evacuee settings (including Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva).
4. Activities in public shelters, including deployment of hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors with activity kits.
5. Pedagogical and trauma-informed support for Dror Israel’s schools network; Moledet Elementary (Be'er Sheva) was hit by a missile fragment while closed (no injuries reported).
6. Online resilience programming through Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers.

Dror Israel describes itself as a social-change movement with **1,300 trained educators** living in **14 educators’ kibbutzim**, with substantial emergency-response experience, especially for traumatized children.

## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and expand its crisis response during the current escalation. Their network of **1,300 educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim** is delivering educational activities, emotional and trauma support, and emergency childcare for over **200,000** children, teens, and families across Israel.

Programming includes:
- Emergency childcare for children of essential workers so medical, security, and critical-service personnel can continue working
- A requested pop-up school at a hospital for medical teams' children
- Daily educational and emotional-support activities in evacuee hotels/centers
- Activities for children and families in public bomb shelters
- Trauma-informed pedagogical guidance across Dror Israel's school network
- Online resilience sessions through movement and dance

They also request non-financial support through introductions/recommendations to potential funders (individuals, foundations, emergency funds), and offer briefings/presentations (including an optional Detroit community/leadership update call).

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school on-site at hospital (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope — trauma support for schools network (10 elementary schools, 9 high schools, 10 preschools; staff and pedagogical development): $80,000
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (staff): $10,000

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, families across Israel are moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and coping with repeated trauma and disruption. Dror Israel educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers/hotels, and living rooms, while partnering with local municipalities and government to assess needs and coordinate real-time emergency responses.

In parallel, Dror Israel is activating its nationwide network through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to keep participants safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. Sustaining educator hours, coordination, transportation, supplies, and digital platforms during this period requires immediate operating support.

Current operational priorities and locations include:
1. **Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers** in Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, and Be'er Sheva; including temporary frameworks in schools, community centers, boarding schools, and evacuee hotels.
2. **Hospital Pop-up School** requested by a major hospital so medical teams can work while children are nearby in a safe educational setting.
3. **Evacuee Hotel Activities** including support for evacuees from Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva, with emotional-educational programming and practical distribution support.
4. **Public Shelter Activities** in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias, and additional locations, with hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors deployed with activity kits.
5. **Pedagogy of Hope** trauma-informed school support; includes Moledet Elementary in Be'er Sheva, which was hit by a missile fragment while closed.
6. **Resilience Through Movement and Dance** via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.

## The ask

Dror Israel seeks **emergency support for approximately the next two weeks** to sustain and scale crisis response while missile barrages continue: educational and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters and evacuee settings, **emergency childcare** for essential workers (including a **requested on-site pop-up school at a major hospital**), **activities at evacuee hotels**, **public-shelter activities** (including teen counselor deployment and, in the later budget, **200 structured activity kits**), **Pedagogy of Hope** support across its school network, and **online resilience-through-movement** sessions (Tamara centers in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa). Funding closes gaps for **staff, transportation, supplies, training, and coordination** during an active emergency.

**Non-financial ask (Philadelphia / JFGP thread):** Introductions for high schools, synagogues, or youth groups interested in the **“Ask Me Anything”** dialogue program (Israeli and American teens discussing the war). Refer interested parties to **contact-en@drorisrael.org.il**.

## Context and background (merged)

As Iran’s missile barrage continues, Israelis move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and face repeated sirens and disruption. Families experience compounded trauma. Dror Israel’s trained educators provide **educational, emotional, and trauma-support** programming in **bomb shelters, evacuee hotels/centers, and homes (“living rooms”)**, partnering with **local municipalities and government** to assess evolving needs.

The organization activates a nationwide network of **200,000+ children, teens, and families** via **direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom** so participants stay safe, supported, and connected to trusted adults. It operates from **14 educators’ kibbutzim** with **1,300 trained educators** and emphasizes experience in **emergency response for traumatized children**.

**Operational priorities (consistent across both threads):**

- Emergency childcare for essential workers across **Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be’er Sheva** (schools, community centers, boarding schools, evacuee hotels), plus **hospital pop-up school** request.
- **Evacuee-hotel / center** programming (notably **Tel Aviv** and **Be’er Sheva** children/youth), including donations distribution where described.
- **Public shelter** activities (**Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias**, and more), with **hundreds of Jewish and Arab teen counselors** as emergency junior counselors and **activity kits**; version B adds **200 kits** with **Ministry of Education Youth and Society** coordination language.
- **Pedagogy of Hope** for the schools network; **Moledet Elementary (Be’er Sheva)** hit by missile fragment — **school closed, nobody harmed** — with personalized trauma-informed guidance for staff and students.
- **Tamara** online **movement/dance** resilience sessions (**Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa**).

Sustaining **educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination** during intensified operations is the rationale for emergency operating support.

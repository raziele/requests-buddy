## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting $250,000 in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and scale its crisis response as Iran's missile barrages continue. Their network of 1,300 educators and 200,000+ participants is delivering educational and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and emergency childcare for essential workers. The request covers emergency childcare for frontline workers, activities in evacuee hotels and public shelters, trauma-informed pedagogy for their schools network, and resilience-through-movement sessions. Funding would close gaps for staff, transportation, supplies, and coordination during an active emergency.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and bracing for repeated sirens. Families are experiencing trauma upon trauma and constant disruption. Dror Israel's trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms across the country, in partnership with local municipalities and government.

Dror Israel is activating its nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. They operate from 14 educators' kibbutzim with 1,300 trained educators and have extensive experience in emergency response for traumatized children.

Current priorities include emergency childcare for essential workers in multiple cities (Rosh HaAyin, Ashdod, Mazkeret Batya, Ramat Yishai, Afula, Akko, Be'er Sheva), including a requested pop-up school at a major hospital; daily activities for evacuee children in Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva; activities in public shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias and elsewhere with teen counselors and activity kits; pedagogical and trauma guidance for their schools network (one school, Moledet in Be'er Sheva, was hit by a missile fragment); and online resilience sessions through movement and dance in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa. Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires the requested emergency support.

## Current Situation
As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, Israelis are living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma as families once again move into bomb shelters and daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Our trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and even in living rooms across the country. Nimble and ready to go wherever we are needed, we are addressing urgent emotional and educational needs in real time, ensuring that no child feels alone.

Dror Israel educators, in partnership with local municipalities and government partners, are actively assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses as conditions change. Simultaneously, we are activating our nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. This is our core educational work adapted to emergency conditions, and sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires continued operating support.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs
Dror Israel is seeking **$250,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to ensure we can continue reaching our 200,000 participants nationwide, provide activities and emotional support for children and families in bomb shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond quickly as urgent needs continue to emerge.

## The Ask

Bizchut requests:
1. **A meeting/introduction call** to present current projects.
2. **Emergency war-related funding support** (amount not specified), including small grants if available.

The organization explains it has redirected significant work to urgent disability-rights issues during the current war period (referred to as "Lion's Roar" / "Roar of the Lion") and asks whether emergency Federation funds are available for this work.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified  
**Non-Financial Ask:** Meeting to present active projects and discuss support pathways.

## Context & Background

Bizchut is a leading Israeli disability-rights organization focused on dignity, equality, autonomy, freedom, and inclusion. It has over 30 years of work and is recognized in the Jewish Federations of North America (JFNA) Designated Allocations Task Force report (February 2026) as one of 18 recommended organizations for disability-related support in Israel.

They report prior contact with Federation stakeholders about a year earlier and are now following up in light of:
- wartime escalation of disability-related needs,
- the new Task Force recommendations,
- and urgent policy and legal advocacy needs.

Bizchut emphasizes that it **receives no government funding** in order to preserve advocacy independence. Reported org profile in the attachment: **8 staff** and approximately **$600,000 annual budget**.

## Current Wartime Advocacy Priorities (as described)

1. **Accessible missile/emergency alerts for deaf and hard-of-hearing people**
   - Bizchut petitioned the Israeli Supreme Court (including a recent urgent motion).
   - State authorities reportedly began action toward a solution, including pilot efforts for accessible alerting.
   - Media coverage referenced (including Ynet).

2. **Priority return of foreign home-care workers**
   - Bizchut appealed to the Transportation/Transport Minister to prioritize return of caregivers stranded abroad.
   - Rationale: tens of thousands of people with disabilities and elderly people depend on these workers; absences create severe risk and family burden.
   - Media coverage referenced (including Israel Hayom).

3. **Accessible protected spaces (shelter/infrastructure access)**
   - Bizchut states accessibility regulations have remained unimplemented for many years.
   - Advocacy included appeals to the Prime Minister’s Office and Ministry of Defense, plus request for urgent Knesset discussion.
   - One source notes a discussion scheduled for the following week.

# Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations (February 2026)

## Core Findings

- Up to **20% of Israel’s population** (about **2 million people**) lives with disabilities.
- Israel has substantial disability-rights legislation, but implementation remains uneven across sectors and geographies.
- Since Oct 7, 2023, disability-related needs have grown sharply (physical and psychosocial).
- Government is the primary funder; philanthropy is most impactful in underfunded areas like advocacy, accessibility, rights access, innovation, and capacity building.

## Selected Background Data

- Key legal/policy framework includes:
  - Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (1998)
  - Rehabilitation in the Community Law (2000)
  - Inclusion Amendment to Special Education Law (2002)
  - UN CRPD ratification
  - Welfare Services for People with Disabilities Law (2012)
  - Additional employment/accessibility measures
- Approx. **20,000** people with disabilities still in closed institutions.
- Multiple agencies share responsibility (NII, Welfare, Health, Education, Labor, Defense, Justice, Transportation, local authorities).

## War-Related Impact Metrics (report figures)

- As of Dec 2025:
  - **20,000+** soldiers wounded since Oct 7
  - **80,000** total wounded veterans (all conflicts)
  - **58%** of wounded soldiers experienced emotional harm
  - **38%** dealing exclusively with PTSD
  - **1,000** new disability claims monthly
  - **$2.5B** annual government budget to treat wounded soldiers

## Funding Landscape & Federation Allocations

- Ministry of Welfare Disabilities Administration budget references:
  - **$671M** over five years
  - plus **$135M annually**
- Jewish Federations designated disability allocations:
  - 2021: **$1.27M**
  - 2022: **$1.49M**
  - 2023: **$1.87M**
  - 2024: **$1.65M**
- JDC Israel Unlimited (core allocation share):
  - 2023: **2.4%** of $29.5M core (**$713,414**)
  - 2024: **3.4%** of $32.5M core (**$1,098,277**)

## Recommendations Framework

- Prioritize disability programming **not funded by government**.
- For government-funded organizations, prioritize:
  - innovation with adoption pathway,
  - infrastructure/human-resource leverage,
  - clearly unmet needs,
  - underserved populations (including minorities and adults).

## Bizchut Profile in Report (relevant to this request)

- Mission: secure rights to dignity, equality, autonomy, and freedom for people with disabilities across all disability types.
- Focus areas include:
  - independent living,
  - fair employment,
  - participation in policymaking (“nothing about us without us”),
  - ending restraint use in institutional settings,
  - access to justice.
- Receives **no government funding** (independence rationale).
- Reported challenges:
  - keeping disability issues visible during wartime/national crisis,
  - raising philanthropy when donors prioritize direct relief over systemic advocacy.
- Team and budget: **8 staff**, approximately **$600,000/year**.
- Cited example: Supreme Court petition for accessible missile alerts (Tamar case), with subsequent government action/pilot.

## The Ask

Bizchut—Israel’s human rights center for people with disabilities, operating **without government funding** to preserve advocacy independence—is following up on contact from roughly a year earlier about possible support. It references the **Jewish Federations of North America** Israel & Overseas Committee **Designated Allocations Task Force** report on supporting Israelis with disabilities (February 2026), in which **Bizchut is one of 18 recommended organizations** (writers consulted Bizchut on needs on the ground).

Bizchut has **temporarily redirected** work toward **wartime disability issues** and asks:

1. Whether the Federation / recipient has **emergency funds for war-time support** that could support Bizchut’s activity (any amount helps).
2. A **meeting** to present **current projects**.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Meeting to present current projects; inquiry whether emergency war-related funds exist for disability-rights work during the conflict.

## Context & Background

### Relationship to the Task Force report

The email to **Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia** (Talia Lidar) and parallel forwarding paths both attach the same strategic guide. The report positions philanthropy to complement government: prioritize what government does not fund (accessibility, advocacy, public awareness, technology, accessing rights, research); for government-funded nonprofits, favor innovation with a path to adoption, capital/human resources to leverage public funding, and attention to underserved groups (e.g. adults, minorities).

### Wartime focus — three advocacy lines (merged detail)

1. **Accessible Home Front Command alerts (deaf and hard-of-hearing)**  
   Alerts were/are inaccessible. Bizchut petitioned the Supreme Court (one source: ~2 years ago; both: state began working on a solution). **Recent urgent motion** filed; state engagement continues. **Media:** Ynet coverage noted in one ingest.

2. **Foreign / home-care workers stranded abroad**  
   Letter to the **Transport(ation) Minister** asking **priority return** for workers who left (e.g. vacation) and cannot return—essential for tens of thousands of people with disabilities and elderly; severe family burden. **Media:** Israel Hayom cited in one ingest.

3. **Accessible protected spaces**  
   Long-standing gap: regulations under the Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law **not enacted for 20+ years**; Bizchut petitioned with others, pressed the **Prime Minister’s Office** and **Ministry of Defense**, and sought an **urgent Knesset** debate. One source notes a **discussion scheduled for the following week** (with Ynet link in original email — not stored here).

### Bizchut in the report (facts repeated in both extracts)

- Mission: dignity, equality, autonomy, freedom; rights across all disability types and life domains.  
- Five focus areas: independent living in the community; fair employment; participation in policymaking (“nothing about us without us”); elimination of restraints in institutions; access to justice.  
- **30+ years** of work; credibility with officials, civil society, academia.  
- **No government funding** (independence).  
- **~8 staff; ~$600,000** annual budget.  
- **Challenge:** maintaining attention to systemic disability rights during national crisis/war when donors favor direct aid.  
- **Example case:** Tamar (deaf; one version says age 63)—Supreme Court petition on accessible missile alerts; government committed to act; **pilot of accessible emergency alert devices**.

### Report-level context (single merged summary)

- Up to **~20%** of Israel’s population (~**two million**) live with disabilities (multiple categories including chronic illness).  
- Strong laws; **uneven implementation**, regional disparity, bureaucracy, gaps; post–Oct 7, 2023 surge in physical and psychosocial disability and service demand.  
- **Government** remains primary funder (billions annually); **philanthropy** fills gaps, pilots, infrastructure, advocacy.  
- **Welfare Disabilities Administration:** **$671M** over five years plus **~$135M/year** (2022 law framing).  
- **Federation designated allocations** (2021–2024): **$1.27M → $1.65M** range; **28–31** grantees/year; **Philadelphia** among Federations allocating in **2023–2024**.  
- **JDC Israel Unlimited:** 2023 **2.4%** of **$29.5M** core (**$713,414**); 2024 **3.4%** of **$32.5M** (**$1,098,277**).  
- **Wounded soldiers snapshot (Dec 2025):** **20,000+** wounded since Oct 7; **80,000** wounded veterans total; **58%** emotional harm; **38%** PTSD-only; **~1,000** new disability claims/month; **~$2.5B/year** government spend on treating wounded soldiers.  
- **Institutions:** ~**20,000** still in closed settings; policy push toward community models.  
- **Other recommended orgs** (with budgets where the second extract adds them): Access Israel (~$10M); Kesher (~$750K); AlManarah (~$1.5M); Beit Issie Shapiro (~$13M); Chimes Israel (>$25M); Israel Elwyn (>$70M); Kivunim (>$7M); Shalva (>$25M); SHEKEL (>$35M); OTI (>$95M); AKIM (>$60M); ILAN (>$30M); Enosh (~$60M); Etgarim (>$6M); Nitzan (~$15M); Krembo Wings (~$15M); Simcha Layeled (~$4.5M); plus **JDC-IU** and others named without figures in the first extract (Krembo Wings, etc. already listed).

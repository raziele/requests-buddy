## The Ask

Bizchut—Israel’s human rights center for people with disabilities, operating without government funding to preserve advocacy independence—is following up on contact from roughly a year earlier about possible support. It references the Jewish Federations of North America **Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force** report on supporting Israelis with disabilities (February 2026), in which Bizchut was consulted and is **one of 18 recommended organizations** (plus continued support for JDC Israel Unlimited is discussed in the report).

Bizchut has **temporarily redirected / pivoted** part of its work toward **wartime disability issues** during the current conflict (referred to as **“Lion’s Roar”** in one document and **“Roar of the Lion”** in the other—same operation, different English phrasing). It asks:

1. Whether the Federation (or recipient organization) has **emergency funds** for **war-time / war-related** support applicable to Bizchut’s work.  
2. **Any support, including small amounts**, would help continue advancing rights and welfare for people with disabilities.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** A **meeting** to present **current projects** and explore fit with available funding.

## Context & Background

**Report and relationship:** The Task Force report writers consulted Bizchut on the situation and needs of people with disabilities in Israel. Bizchut is listed among recommended organizations. The organization stresses it runs **only on donations**, receives **no government funding**, and faces the challenge of **directing public attention to disability issues during national crisis and war**—when risks are heightened but attention and donor priorities often favor direct aid over systemic change. Report context: **~20% of Israel’s population (~two million people)** live with disabilities; since October 7, 2023, **tens of thousands** have sustained **permanent physical and psychosocial** disabilities, increasing urgency and service demand.

**Institutional facts from the report (Bizchut):** **Eight staff**; annual budget on the order of **~$600,000**; **30+ years** of work; five focus areas: independent living in the community, fair employment, participation in policymaking (“nothing about us without us”), elimination of use of restraints in institutional settings, access to justice.

**Recent / wartime advocacy (combined detail from both intakes):**

1. **Accessible Home Front Command / missile alerts for deaf and hard-of-hearing people** — Alerts described as inaccessible; Bizchut **petitioned the Supreme Court** (one source notes a petition **~two years** prior; the other describes a petition with a **recent urgent motion**). The **state began working on a solution**; one source adds **media coverage (Ynet)** and that a **pilot of accessible emergency alert devices** is mentioned in the report (Tamar / deaf case example).

2. **Foreign care / home-care workers** — Letter to the **Transport(ation) Minister** requesting **priority for returning** foreign care workers **stranded abroad** (e.g., after vacation), given essential support for **tens of thousands** of people with disabilities and elderly; families severely burdened. One source cites **Israel Hayom** coverage.

3. **Accessible protected spaces** — Advocacy on **lack of accessible protected spaces**; regulations under the **Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law** described as **not enacted for 20+ years**; demands to **Prime Minister’s Office** and **Ministry of Defense**; request for **urgent Knesset** debate—one source states a discussion was **scheduled for the following week** and references a **Ynet** link in the original email.

**Philanthropy framing (from report extracts):** Government is the **primary funder** (billions annually); philanthropy fills gaps—**accessibility, advocacy, public awareness, technology, accessing rights, research**—aligned with Bizchut’s role.

# Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations  
**February 2026**

**Table of Contents (per one extraction):** Executive Summary, Introduction, Background Information, Current Funding Landscape, Jewish Federations' Historic Partners, Task Force Methodology, Recommendations, Appendix (Task Force Members & Interviewees).

## Executive Summary

- Guide and recommendations from the **Designated Allocations Task Force** for supporting people in Israel with disabilities.  
- Up to **20%** of Israel’s population—**~two million**—live with disabilities (psychosocial, physical, sensory, developmental, learning, intellectual, chronic illness).  
- **Strong legislative framework**; **uneven implementation**, regional disparities, bureaucratic complexity, persistent gaps.  
- Urgency intensified since **October 7, 2023**: many with permanent physical and psychosocial disabilities; opportunity for philanthropy to **complement government**, strengthen nonprofits, accelerate change.  
- **Guiding principles:** prioritize programs **not** funded by government; support **innovation** with pathway to government adoption; invest in **capital and human resources** to maximize public funding; increase attention to **underserved populations**.  
- **18 vetted nonprofits** recommended, **plus** continued support for **JDC Israel Unlimited (JDC-IU)**.

## Introduction

The Task Force supports Federations’ overseas work with a more **data-informed** approach; this topic followed earlier work on **Ethiopian Israeli integration (2023)**. Goal: comprehensive resource introducing leading nonprofits, strategic funding priorities, allocation recommendations.

## Background Information

- **~20%** of **~10 million** residents live with disabilities.  
- **Laws / frameworks:** Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (**1998**), Rehabilitation in the Community of Persons with Mental Disabilities Law (**2000**), Inclusion Amendment to Special Education Law (**2002**), **UN CRPD** ratification, Welfare Services for People with Disabilities Law (**2012**), employment integration and accessibility legislation (including **2022** references in one extraction).  
- **~20,000** Israelis with disabilities still in **closed institutions**; government committed to **community-based** frameworks.  
- Disparities worse for **Arab citizens**, people in **poverty**, those struggling with **bureaucracy**.  
- Many authorities: **National Insurance Institute**, **Ministry of Welfare and Social Affairs**, **Ministry of Health**, **Kupot Holim**, **Ministry of Education**, **Ministry of Labor**, **Ministry of Defense**, **Ministry of Justice** (Commission for Equal Rights), **Ministry of Transportation**, **local authorities**.

## October 7th: Impact on Israelis with Disabilities

- As of **December 2025**: **20,000+** soldiers wounded since Oct. 7; **80,000** total wounded veterans (all conflicts); **58%** of wounded soldiers experienced emotional harm; **38%** dealing exclusively with PTSD; **~1,000** new disability claims **monthly**; **$2.5B** annual government budget to treat wounded soldiers.

## Current Funding Landscape

- Israeli government **primary funder** (billions/year).  
- **Ministry of Welfare Disabilities Administration:** **$671M** over five years plus **$135M** annually (2022 Welfare Services law context in extractions).  
- **Jewish Federations designated allocations (2021–2024):**  
  - **2021:** **$1.27M** (one extraction: 67 grants / 28 grantees)  
  - **2022:** **$1.49M** (70 / 31)  
  - **2023:** **$1.87M** (82 / 27)  
  - **2024:** **$1.65M** (81 / 28)  
  - (Alternate summary in other extraction: **28–31** nonprofits per year.)  
- **JDC-IU (core allocations):** **2023** — **2.4%** of **$29.5M** → **$713,414**; **2024** — **3.4%** of **$32.5M** → **$1,098,277**.  
- List of Federations allocating to disability organizations **2023–2024** includes (non-exhaustive): **Ann Arbor, Atlanta, Boston, Chicago**, and **Philadelphia** (among others named in the longer extraction).

## Recommendations

- Support disability programming **not** government-funded: **accessibility, advocacy, public awareness, technology, accessing rights, research**.  
- For government-funded nonprofits: prioritize **new/innovative** approaches with **pathway to adoption**; **physical infrastructure** or **human resources** to **maximize** government funding; **additional services** only where government does **not** fund and need is clear; consider **underserved** populations (**adults, minorities**).

## Recommended Organizations — Bizchut (this request)

- **Mission:** dignity, equality, autonomy, freedom; rights for **all** disability types across life domains.  
- **Five focus areas:** independent living in the community; fair employment; participation in policymaking; elimination of restraints in institutions; access to justice.  
- **No government funding** (independence).  
- **Challenges:** visibility during crisis/war; fundraising when donors favor direct aid over systemic change.

- **Example:** Supreme Court petition re **accessible missile/alerts** for **Tamar (deaf)**; government commitment; **pilot** of accessible emergency alert devices.

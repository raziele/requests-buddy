## The Ask

Bizchut — described as Israel’s leading / primary human rights center for people with disabilities — is following up after roughly a year without support and references the **Jewish Federations of North America Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force** report on supporting Israelis with disabilities (February 2026), in which **Bizchut is one of 18 recommended organizations** (plus continued support for JDC Israel Unlimited). The Philadelphia-thread email explicitly names engagement with **Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia** (Talia Lidar) and the **North American Federations Task Force** framing.

The organization receives **no government funding** (to preserve independence in advocacy) and runs on donations. It has **temporarily redirected / pivoted** work toward wartime disability issues during the current conflict — referred to as **"Lion's Roar"** in one document and **"Roar of the Lion"** in the other (**wording difference only**).

They ask whether the recipient has **emergency / war-related funds** for this work and state that **any support, including small amounts**, would help advance rights and welfare for people with disabilities.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** A **meeting** to present current projects; inquiry whether **emergency war-related / war-time** funds are available.

## Context & Background

**Mission (from report / summaries):** dignity, equality, autonomy, full inclusion, and freedom; advancing rights across all disability types and life domains. **Scale (from Task Force report):** ~**8 staff**; annual budget about **$600,000**; **30+ years** of work; credibility with civil servants, officials, academics, and nonprofits.

**Report context:** Up to **~20%** of Israel’s population (**~two million people**) live with disabilities; strong laws but uneven implementation, regional disparities, bureaucracy, and gaps; since **October 7, 2023**, tens of thousands with new permanent physical and psychosocial disabilities; government is the primary funder (**billions/year**); philanthropy is urged for gaps — advocacy, accessibility, accessing rights, innovation, etc.

**Recent / wartime advocacy (combined detail from both threads):**

1. **Accessible Home Front Command / missile alerts for deaf and hard-of-hearing people** — alerts described as inaccessible; **Supreme Court petition** (~two years ago in one version); state began working on a solution; **recent urgent motion**; state progress noted in both; one thread mentions **media coverage (Ynet)**.
2. **Foreign / home-care workers abroad** — letter to the **Transport(ation) Minister** asking **priority to return** workers stranded after vacation; essential for tens of thousands of people with disabilities and elderly; severe family burden; **Israel Hayom** cited in one thread.
3. **Accessible protected / secure spaces** — **Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law** regulations not enacted for **20+ years**; petitions with others; demands to **Prime Minister’s Office** and **Ministry of Defense**; request for **urgent Knesset** debate; one thread notes a **discussion scheduled for the following week** and a **Ynet** link (not reproduced here).

**Philadelphia-specific note:** Philadelphia is listed among Federations that **allocated funds** to organizations serving Israelis with disabilities in **2023–2024** (per report summary).

## Executive Summary

- Task Force guide and recommendations for supporting people in Israel with disabilities.
- Up to **20%** of Israel’s population — **~two million** — live with disabilities (psychosocial, physical, sensory, developmental, learning, intellectual, chronic illness).
- Strong **legislative framework**; uneven **implementation**, **regional disparities**, **bureaucratic complexity**, persistent **gaps**.
- Urgency intensified since **October 7, 2023**: many with permanent physical and psychosocial disabilities; opportunity for philanthropy to complement government, strengthen nonprofits, accelerate change.
- **Guiding principles:** prioritize programs **not** funded by government; support **innovation** with pathways to **government adoption**; invest in **capital and human resources** to **maximize public funding**; increase attention to **underserved populations**.
- **18 vetted nonprofits** recommended, plus continued support for **JDC Israel Unlimited (JDC-IU)**.

## Background Information

- ~**20%** of **~10 million** residents live with disabilities.
- **Laws / frameworks:** Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (**1998**); Rehabilitation in the Community of Persons with Mental Disabilities Law (**2000**); Inclusion Amendment to Special Education Law (**2002**); **UN CRPD** ratification; Welfare Services for People with Disabilities Law (**2012**); employment integration and accessibility legislation (**2022** in one summary).
- Nearly **20,000** Israelis with disabilities still in **closed institutions**; government committed to **community-based** alternatives.
- Disparities worse for **Arab citizens**, people in **poverty**, those struggling with **bureaucracy**.
- **Authorities:** National Insurance Institute; Ministry of Welfare and Social Affairs; Ministry of Health; Kupot Holim; Ministry of Education; Ministry of Labor; Ministry of Defense (veterans); Ministry of Justice (Commission for Equal Rights); Ministry of Transportation; **local authorities**.

## October 7 — impact on Israelis with disabilities

- As of **December 2025**: **20,000+** soldiers wounded since Oct. 7; **80,000** total wounded veterans (all conflicts); **58%** of wounded soldiers experienced emotional harm; **38%** dealing exclusively with PTSD; **~1,000** new disability claims **monthly**; **$2.5B** annual government budget to treat wounded soldiers.

## Current funding landscape

- Israeli government primary funder (**billions/year**).
- Ministry of Welfare **Disabilities Administration:** **$671M** over five years plus **$135M** annually (2022 Welfare Services for People with Disabilities Law); **person-centered** approach (one extraction).

## Recommendations

- Support disability programming **not** government-funded: **accessibility, advocacy, public awareness, technology, accessing rights, research**.
- For government-funded nonprofits: **new/innovative** approaches with pathway to adoption; **physical infrastructure** or **human resources** to maximize government funding; **additional services** only where government does not fund and need is clear; consider **underserved** groups (**adults, minorities**).

## Recommended organizations — Bizchut (this request)

- **Mission:** full exercise of rights to **dignity, equality, autonomy, freedom**; all disability types; **five focus areas:** independent living in the community; fair employment; participation in policymaking (**“nothing about us without us”**); elimination of **restraints** in institutional settings; **access to justice**.
- **No government funding** (independence).
- **Challenges:** public attention during **crisis/war** when risks rise; fundraising when donors favor **direct aid** over **systemic change**.
- **~8 staff; ~$600,000** annual budget.
- **Example — Tamar (deaf; one version says age 63):** Supreme Court petition for **accessible missile / emergency alerts**; government committed to act; **pilot of accessible emergency alert devices**.

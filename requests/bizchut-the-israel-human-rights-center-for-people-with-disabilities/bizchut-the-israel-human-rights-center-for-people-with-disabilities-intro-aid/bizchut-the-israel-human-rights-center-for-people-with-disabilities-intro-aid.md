## The Ask

Bizchut—Israel’s human rights center for people with disabilities, operating without government funding to preserve advocacy independence—is following up on contact from roughly a year ago about possible support. It references the Jewish Federations of North America **Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force** report on supporting Israelis with disabilities (February 2026), in which Bizchut was consulted and is **one of 18 recommended organizations** (plus continued support for JDC Israel Unlimited).

The organization has **temporarily redirected / pivoted** activities toward **wartime disability issues** during the current conflict. **Naming in sources:** “Lion's Roar” (source 1) vs. “Roar of the Lion” (source 2)—same campaign, different English phrasing.

**Financial:** They ask whether the Federation (or recipient organization) has **emergency / war-time funds** for this work and whether it can support Bizchut’s activity; they emphasize that **even small amounts** help. **Funding amount:** not specified.

**Non-financial:** **Meeting** to present current projects and explore support (including emergency funding pathways).

**Recent advocacy themes (as described in the emails):**

1. **Accessible Home Front Command / missile alerts for deaf and hard-of-hearing people** — petition to the Supreme Court; state reportedly began working on a solution; **urgent motion** filed recently; **media:** Ynet coverage noted in one source.
2. **Foreign care / home-care workers stranded abroad** — letter to the Transport Minister requesting **priority for return** to Israel, given essential support for tens of thousands of people with disabilities and elderly; **media:** Israel Hayom cited in one source.
3. **Accessible protected spaces** — long-standing regulatory gap under the Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law; demands to the Prime Minister’s Office and Ministry of Defense; request for **urgent Knesset debate** — one source notes a **discussion scheduled for the following week** (with Ynet link in that thread).

## Context & Background

Tali Marcus wrote **March 5, 2026**. Bizchut runs **only on donations**, receives **no government funding**, and positions that independence as necessary for effective rights advocacy.

The attached Task Force report frames **up to ~20% of Israel’s population (~two million people)** as living with disabilities; **since October 7, 2023**, physical and psychosocial disability and service demand have intensified. Government remains the primary funder; **philanthropy** is positioned to fill gaps—especially **advocacy, accessibility, accessing rights, innovation, awareness, technology, research**—aligned with Bizchut’s work.

**Bizchut in the report (both extractions agree):** mission centered on **dignity, equality, autonomy, freedom**; rights across disability types; focus areas include **independent living in the community, fair employment, participation in policymaking (“nothing about us without us”), elimination of restraints in institutions, access to justice**; **30+ years** of work; **no government funding**; **~8 staff**, **~$600,000** annual budget; example case **Tamar (deaf)** — Supreme Court petition on accessible missile alerts; government commitment and **pilot of accessible emergency alert devices**.

**Timing detail (source 2 only):** Supreme Court petition on alerts described as filed **~two years** before the March 2026 email (vs. source 1’s “filed a petition” without that duration).

# Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations  
**February 2026**

**Document structure (source 2):** Table of Contents — Executive Summary, Introduction, Background Information, Current Funding Landscape, Jewish Federations' Historic Partners, Task Force Methodology, Recommendations, Appendix (Task Force Members & Interviewees).

## Executive Summary (combined)

- Task Force guide and recommendations for supporting people in Israel with disabilities.
- Up to **20%** of Israel’s population—around **two million** people—live with disabilities (psychosocial, physical, sensory, developmental, learning, intellectual, chronic illness).
- **Strong legislative framework** with **uneven implementation**, regional disparities, bureaucratic complexity, persistent **gaps** in realizing rights.
- Urgency intensified since **October 7, 2023**: tens of thousands with **permanent physical and psychosocial** disabilities; opportunity for philanthropy to **complement government**, strengthen nonprofits, accelerate change.
- **Government** is primary funder (**billions/year**); **philanthropy** fills gaps—innovation/pilots, capital, advocacy, capacity.
- **Guiding principles:** prioritize programs **not** funded by government; support **innovation** with pathway to government adoption; invest in **capital and human resources** to maximize public funding; increase attention to **underserved populations**.
- **18 vetted nonprofits** recommended, plus **continued support for JDC Israel Unlimited (JDC-IU)**.

## Introduction

The Task Force supports Federations’ overseas work with a more data-informed approach, following prior work on Ethiopian Israeli integration (2023). Goal: comprehensive resource on leading nonprofits, strategic funding priorities, allocation recommendations.

## Background Information

- ~**20%** of ~**10 million** residents live with disabilities.
- **Laws (non-exhaustive):** Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities (1998); Rehabilitation in the Community of Persons with Mental Disabilities (2000); Inclusion Amendment to Special Education (2002); **UN CRPD** ratification; Welfare Services for People with Disabilities (2012); employment integration and accessibility legislation (e.g. 2022).
- Nearly **20,000** Israelis with disabilities still in **closed institutions**; push toward **community-based** frameworks.
- **Disparities** worse for **Arab citizens**, people in **poverty**, those struggling with **bureaucracy**.
- **Authorities cited:** National Insurance Institute; Ministry of Welfare and Social Affairs; Ministry of Health; Kupot Holim; Ministry of Education; Ministry of Labor; Ministry of Defense; Ministry of Justice (Commission for Equal Rights); Ministry of Transportation; **local authorities**.

## October 7: impact on Israelis with disabilities

- As of **December 2025**: **20,000+** soldiers wounded since Oct. 7; **80,000** total wounded veterans (all conflicts); **58%** of wounded soldiers experienced emotional harm; **38%** dealing exclusively with PTSD; **~1,000** new disability claims **monthly**; **~$2.5B** annual government budget to treat wounded soldiers.

## Recommendations (summary)

- Support disability programming **not** government-funded: accessibility, advocacy, public awareness, technology, accessing rights, research.
- For government-funded nonprofits: prioritize **new/innovative** approaches with **pathway to adoption**; **physical infrastructure / human resources** to maximize public funding; **additional services** only where government does not fund and need is clear; consider **underserved populations** (e.g. adults, minorities).

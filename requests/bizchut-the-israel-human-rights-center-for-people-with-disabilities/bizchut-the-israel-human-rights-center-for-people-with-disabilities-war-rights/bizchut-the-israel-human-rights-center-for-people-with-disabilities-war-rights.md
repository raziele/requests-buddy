## The Ask

Bizchut (Israel’s human rights center for people with disabilities) is requesting a meeting/intro to present its current projects and to explore emergency war-related funding support for its disability-rights work during the current conflict (“Lion’s Roar”).

- **Funding Requested:** Not specified
- **Non-Financial Ask:**
  - Request for a meeting to present current projects
  - Inquiry whether the recipient has emergency war-related funds available for Bizchut’s war-time disability rights initiatives

## Context & Background

- Bizchut is an Israeli disability rights organization that receives **no government funding**, in order to maintain independence in advocacy.
- Tali Marcus (Executive Director) previously contacted the Jewish Federation about support **approximately a year earlier**, and is now following up.
- The email is linked to Bizchut’s engagement with the **Jewish Federations of North America (JFNA) Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force** report on supporting Israelis with disabilities (attached). Bizchut is one of **18 recommended organizations**.
- In response to the war (“Roar of the Lion” / “Lion’s Roar”), Bizchut has temporarily redirected work toward urgent disability issues and accessibility-related advocacy.

## Current Wartime Disability Advocacy & Projects

Bizchut describes three main urgent areas in its war-time work:

1. **Accessible missile / Home Front Command alerts for deaf and hard-of-hearing people**
   - Alerts are described as currently **inaccessible** to deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals.
   - Bizchut describes:
     - A petition to the Supreme Court made **about two years ago (~2 years)**.
     - Continued progress including state work toward a solution.
     - A **recent urgent motion** and additional media coverage referenced as **Ynet**-linked.
   - The attached Task Force report further notes a specific case: **Tamar (63, deaf)**—Bizchut filed a Supreme Court petition seeking accessible missile alerts; the government committed to act and launched a **pilot of accessible emergency alert devices**.

2. **Priority return of foreign home-care workers stranded abroad**
   - Bizchut requests priority from the **Transport Minister** for foreign care workers stranded abroad to return to Israel.
   - Rationale included in the messages: the absence of these care workers leaves **tens of thousands** of people with disabilities and elderly at risk and places severe burdens on families.
   - Coverage referenced as **Israel Hayom**-linked in one duplicate.

3. **Accessible protected spaces (and the need for disability-rights regulations)**
   - Bizchut highlights that regulations under the **Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law** have not been enacted for **over 20 years**.
   - Advocacy includes:
     - Demands/pressure to the **Prime Minister’s Office** and the **Ministry of Defense**.
     - A request for an **urgent Knesset debate**.
     - One duplicate notes that the discussion is scheduled for **the following week**, with **Ynet** reference.

## Executive Summary

- This report presents the **Jewish Federations of North America's Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force** guide and recommendations for supporting people in Israel with disabilities.
- **Scale of disability in Israel:** Up to **20%** of Israel’s population (around **two million** people) live with disabilities (psychosocial, physical, sensory, developmental, learning, intellectual, chronic illnesses).
- **Legislative framework exists** (including rights protections), but implementation is **uneven** with persistent gaps due to:
  - Regional disparities
  - Bureaucratic complexity
  - Service shortfalls
- **Urgency intensified since October 7, 2023**:
  - Tens of thousands of Israelis have sustained permanent physical and psychosocial disabilities.
- **Guiding principles** include:
  - Prioritize programs **not funded by government**
  - Support innovation with pathways to government adoption
  - Invest in capital and human resources to maximize public funding
  - Increase attention to underserved populations
- The report recommends **18 vetted nonprofits** plus continued support for **JDC’s Israel Unlimited (JDC-IU)**.
- High-level framing: while the Israeli government is the primary funder (billions per year), philanthropy complements government by filling gaps, supporting innovation and pilots, funding capital/development and advocacy, and strengthening nonprofit capacity.

## Introduction

- The Task Force seeks to support Jewish Federations’ overseas work via a more **data-informed** approach.
- It chose to examine support for Israelis with disabilities after initially exploring **Ethiopian Israeli integration (2023)**.
- Goal: develop a comprehensive resource introducing leading nonprofits, outlining strategic funding priorities, and providing allocation recommendations.

## Background Information

- Approximately **20% of Israel’s 10 million residents** live with disabilities.
- Legislative foundations listed include:
  - Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (1998)
  - Rehabilitation in the Community of Persons with Mental Disabilities Law (2000)
  - Inclusion Amendment to Special Education Law (2002)
  - Ratification of UN CRPD
  - Welfare Services for People with Disabilities Law (2012)
  - Employment integration and accessibility legislation
- Nearly **20,000 Israelis** with disabilities still live in **closed institutions**, while government commits to advancing community-based frameworks.
- Implementation is uneven across regions and subpopulations, with disparities reportedly worse for:
  - Arab citizens
  - People in poverty
  - Those struggling with bureaucracy
- Multiple government authorities involved, including:
  - National Insurance Institute
  - Ministry of Welfare and Social Affairs
  - Ministry of Health
  - Kupot Holim
  - Ministry of Education
  - Ministry of Labor
  - Ministry of Defense (veterans)
  - Ministry of Justice (Commission for Equal Rights)
  - Ministry of Transportation
  - Local Authorities

## Recommendations

- Support disability programming **not funded by government**, including:
  - Accessibility
  - Advocacy
  - Public awareness
  - Technology
  - Accessing rights
  - Research
- For government-funded nonprofits:
  - Focus on new/innovative approaches with pathways to government adoption
  - Support physical infrastructure or human resources to maximize government funding
  - Consider additional services only where government does not fund and clear need exists
  - Consider prioritizing underserved populations (adults, minorities)

## Recommended Organizations (18 + JDC-IU)

### Bizchut (recommended organization relevant to this request)

The report’s summary of Bizchut includes:

- **Mission / purpose:** Ensure people with disabilities can fully exercise rights to dignity, equality, autonomy, and freedom.
- **Scope:** Advances rights for all types of disabilities across every aspect of life.
- **Five focus areas:**
  - Independent living in the community
  - Fair employment
  - Participation in policymaking (“nothing about us without us”)
  - Elimination of use of restraints in institutional settings
  - Access to justice
- **Experience:** Over 30 years.
- **Independence:** Receives **no government funding** to maintain independence in advocacy.
- **Main challenges (as described):**
  - Directing public attention to people with disabilities during national crisis and war, when attention is elsewhere and risks are heightened
  - Raising philanthropic funds when donors prioritize direct aid over systemic change
- **Capacity / scale:** 8 staff; annual budget around **$600,000**.
- **Illustrative case referenced in the report:**
  - Tamar (63, deaf): Bizchut filed a Supreme Court petition demanding accessible means for missile alerts; the government committed to act and launched a pilot of accessible emergency alert devices.

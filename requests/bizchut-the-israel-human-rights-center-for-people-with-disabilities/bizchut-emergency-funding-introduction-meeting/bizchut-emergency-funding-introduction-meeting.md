## The Ask

Bizchut is requesting:
1. An introduction/working meeting to present its current projects.
2. Consideration for emergency war-related funding to sustain disability-rights advocacy and response work during the current conflict ("Lion’s Roar"/"Roar of the Lion").

Bizchut notes it has no government funding and relies on donations; even small support would help continue advancing rights and welfare for people with disabilities.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified  
**Non-Financial Ask:** Meeting to present current projects and discuss possible support.

## Context & Background

Bizchut, a leading Israeli disability rights organization, followed up after prior contact about support approximately a year earlier. The organization is highlighted in the Jewish Federations of North America (JFNA) Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force report (February 2026) as one of 18 recommended nonprofits for supporting Israelis with disabilities.

Bizchut reports that wartime conditions have intensified risks for people with disabilities and shifted part of its work to urgent response and rights protection. It emphasizes maintaining independence in advocacy by not taking government funding.

Organizational profile from the attached report:
- 30+ years of rights advocacy.
- Mission: ensure dignity, equality, autonomy, freedom, and full participation for people with disabilities.
- Focus areas: independent living, fair employment, participation in policymaking ("nothing about us without us"), ending restraints in institutions, and access to justice.
- Staff: 8.
- Annual budget: approximately $600,000.
- Key challenge: maintaining attention and funding for disability rights during crisis periods when donors prioritize direct aid.

## Current Wartime Advocacy Priorities (as described)

1. **Accessible emergency alerts** for deaf and hard-of-hearing people:
   - Bizchut petitioned the Supreme Court (including a recent urgent motion).
   - The state began work on a solution; report cites a pilot of accessible emergency alert devices.
   - Media coverage referenced (including Ynet).

2. **Return of foreign home-care workers**:
   - Appeal to prioritize re-entry of care workers stranded abroad.
   - Framed as critical for tens of thousands of people with disabilities and older adults and to reduce severe family burden.
   - Media coverage referenced (including Israel Hayom).

3. **Accessible protected spaces**:
   - Advocacy regarding longstanding gaps in implementation/regulations under disability rights law.
   - Appeals to Prime Minister’s Office and Ministry of Defense.
   - Request for urgent Knesset deliberation; one source states discussion scheduled for the following week.

## Attached Report: Key Extracted Points

### Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations (Feb 2026)

- Up to 20% of Israel’s population (about two million people) live with disabilities.
- Israel has substantial legal protections, but implementation is uneven, with regional and socioeconomic disparities and bureaucratic barriers.
- Since Oct 7, 2023, disability-related needs have sharply increased (physical and psychosocial).
- As of Dec 2025 (report figures): 20,000+ soldiers wounded since Oct 7; 80,000 total wounded veterans; 58% with emotional harm; 38% exclusively PTSD; ~1,000 new disability claims/month; ~$2.5B annual government budget for wounded soldiers.
- Government remains the primary funder; philanthropy is most catalytic in gaps such as advocacy, accessibility, rights access, innovation, and capacity-building.
- Recommendations:
  - Prioritize areas not government-funded.
  - Back innovation with pathways to government adoption.
  - Invest in infrastructure/human capital that amplifies public funding.
  - Consider underserved groups (including adults and minorities).

### Funding Landscape and Federation Allocations (reported)

- Ministry of Welfare Disabilities Administration: $671M over five years plus $135M annually.
- Jewish Federations designated allocations for disability programs:
  - 2021: $1.27M (67 grants, 28 grantees)
  - 2022: $1.49M (70 grants, 31 grantees)
  - 2023: $1.87M (82 grants, 27 grantees)
  - 2024: $1.65M (81 grants, 28 grantees)
- JDC-Israel Unlimited (JDC-IU):
  - 2023: 2.4% of $29.5M core allocations ($713,414)
  - 2024: 3.4% of $32.5M ($1,098,277)
- Philadelphia is identified among federations allocating to disability-serving organizations in 2023–2024.

### Recommended Organizations

- 18 vetted nonprofits are recommended, including Bizchut, plus continued support for JDC-IU.
- Other named organizations include Access Israel, Kesher, AlManarah, Beit Issie Shapiro, Chimes Israel, Israel Elwyn, Kivunim, Shalva, SHEKEL, OTI, AKIM Israel, ILAN, Enosh, Etgarim, Nitzan Association, Krembo Wings, and Simcha Layeled.

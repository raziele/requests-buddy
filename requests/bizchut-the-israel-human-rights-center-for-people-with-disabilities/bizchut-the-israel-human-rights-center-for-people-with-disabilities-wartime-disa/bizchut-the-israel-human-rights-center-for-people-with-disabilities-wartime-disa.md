## The Ask

Bizchut—described across sources as Israel’s leading / central human rights center for people with disabilities—is following up after roughly a year of prior contact about possible support. It ties this outreach to the Jewish Federations of North America **Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force** report (Feb. 2026) on supporting Israelis with disabilities, which lists Bizchut among **18 recommended organizations**; Bizchut notes the report writers consulted it on needs on the ground.

The organization receives **no government funding** (to preserve independence in advocacy) and runs on donations; it asks whether the recipient Federation / partner has **emergency war-related funds** for wartime support, emphasizing that even small support helps continue advancing rights and welfare for people with disabilities. It also requests a **meeting to present current projects**.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified (both sources).

**Non-Financial Ask:** Meeting to present current projects; inquiry whether emergency war-related funds exist for Bizchut’s disability-rights / wartime work.

Sources use slightly different English phrasing for the operation name: **"Lion's Roar"** vs **"Roar of the Lion"** — treat as the same referred context unless confirmed otherwise.

### Relationship to the Task Force report and Federation engagement

### Recent advocacy lines (combined detail from both emails)

1. **Accessible Home Front Command / missile alerts for deaf and hard-of-hearing people**  
   Bizchut **alerts are inaccessible** to this population. Legal/advocacy track includes a **Supreme Court petition** (one source: filed **~2 years ago**; other: emphasizes recent **urgent motion** and ongoing state work toward a solution). Public/media attention referenced (**Ynet**). Report example case: **Tamar (63, deaf)** — petition for accessible missile alerts; government committed to act and launched a **pilot of accessible emergency alert devices**.

2. **Foreign home-care / care workers’ return priority**  
   Letter to the **Transport(ation) Minister** asking to prioritize return of workers stranded abroad after travel; rationale: essential for tens of thousands of people with disabilities and elderly, severe family burden without coverage. Press reference: **Israel Hayom** (one source).

3. **Accessible protected spaces**  
   Advocacy that **regulations under the Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law** have not been enacted for **20+ years**; petitions with others; engagement with **Prime Minister’s Office** and **Ministry of Defense**; push for **urgent Knesset** discussion — one source notes a discussion **scheduled for the following week** (with **Ynet** link mentioned in that thread).

### Why philanthropy (per attached report framing)

The Task Force report emphasizes Israel’s **strong legislative framework** with **uneven implementation**, **regional disparities**, **bureaucratic complexity**, and gaps; post–Oct. 7, **tens of thousands** with lasting physical and psychosocial disabilities. It frames an opportunity for philanthropy to complement government by supporting areas government does not fully fund—**advocacy, accessibility, accessing rights, awareness, technology, research, innovation**—aligned with Bizchut’s work.

# Supporting Israelis with Disabilities — A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations  
**February 2026**

**Document structure (merged TOC notes):** Executive Summary, Introduction, Background Information, **October 7th / October 7 impact**, Current Funding Landscape, **Jewish Federations Allocations / Historic Partners** (wording differs by extraction), **Task Force Methodology** (mentioned in one extraction), Recommendations, Recommended Organizations (**18 + JDC-IU**), Appendix (Task Force members & interviewees; nonprofits consulted include **Bizchut / Tali Marcus**).

## Executive Summary (unified)

- Task Force guide for Federations supporting people in Israel with disabilities (data-informed overseas allocations framing).
- Up to **20%** of Israel’s population (~**two million** people) live with disabilities across multiple categories (psychosocial, physical, sensory, developmental, learning, intellectual, chronic illness).
- Strong laws exist; **implementation uneven**, disparities, bureaucracy, service gaps persist.
- Urgency increased since **October 7, 2023**: large numbers with permanent physical/psychosocial disabilities; philanthropy can complement government, strengthen nonprofits, accelerate change.
- Government remains **primary funder (billions/year)**; philanthropy fills gaps: innovation/pilots, capital, advocacy, capacity, infrastructure.
- Guiding principles: prioritize **non-government-funded** programming; support **innovation with pathways to government adoption**; invest in **capital/human resources** to **maximize public funding**; increase focus on **underserved populations**.

## Background (unified)

- ~**20%** of ~**10 million** residents live with disabilities.
- Legislative anchors cited: **Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (1998)**; **Rehabilitation in the Community of Persons with Mental Disabilities Law (2000)**; **Inclusion Amendment to Special Education Law (2002)**; **UN CRPD ratification**; **Welfare Services for People with Disabilities Law (2012)**; employment integration/accessibility legislation (**2022** referenced in one extraction).
- Nearly **~20,000** still in **closed institutions**; policy emphasis on **community-based** alternatives.
- Disparities worse for **Arab citizens**, people in **poverty**, those trapped in bureaucracy.
- Many authorities: **National Insurance Institute**, **Ministry of Welfare and Social Affairs**, **Ministry of Health**, **Kupot Holim**, **Ministry of Education**, **Ministry of Labor**, **Ministry of Defense**, **Ministry of Justice (Commission for Equal Rights)**, **Ministry of Transportation**, **local authorities** (ordering/naming varies slightly by extraction).

## October 7 impact (stats as stated in report)

- As of **December 2025**: **20,000+** soldiers wounded since Oct. 7; **80,000** total wounded veterans (all conflicts); **58%** of wounded soldiers experienced emotional harm; **38%** dealing exclusively with **PTSD**; **~1,000** new disability claims monthly; **$2.5B** annual government budget to treat wounded soldiers.

## Current funding landscape (unified)

- Israeli government primary funder.
- **Ministry of Welfare Disabilities Administration**: **$671M** over five years + **$135M** annually (2022 Welfare Services law framing); person-centered approach noted.
- Federations **designated allocations** for disability programs (**2021–2024**): **$1.27M (2021)**, **$1.49M (2022)**, **$1.87M (2023)**, **$1.65M (2024)**; grantee counts/grants counts appear in one extraction as per-year grant volumes (e.g., 2021 **67 grants / 28 grantees** pattern in one line—preserve as “report contains yearly grant statistics”).
- **JDC–Israel Unlimited**: **2023** ~**2.4%** of **$29.5M** core → **$713,414**; **2024** ~**3.4%** of **$32.5M** → **$1,098,277**.
- **Philadelphia** explicitly noted among Federations allocating to disability-serving orgs in **2023–2024** in one extraction; another extraction lists a broader set of example Federations (e.g., Ann Arbor, Atlanta, Boston, Chicago, etc.—per report appendix/annex details).

## Recommendations (unified)

- Support disability programming **not funded by government**: accessibility, advocacy, public awareness, technology, accessing rights, research.
- For government-funded nonprofits: prioritize **new/innovative** approaches with **pathway to adoption**; **physical infrastructure** or **human resources** to maximize government funding; additional services only where government doesn’t fund and need is clear; consider prioritizing **underserved** populations (**adults**, **minorities**).

## Bizchut (recommended organization — merged details)

- Mission: ensure people with disabilities can fully exercise rights to **dignity, equality, autonomy, freedom**; advance rights across **all disability types** and life domains.
- **Five focus areas:** independent living in community; fair employment; participation in policymaking (**“nothing about us without us”**); elimination of **restraints** in institutional settings; access to justice.
- **30+ years** experience; credibility with officials/civil society/academia/nonprofits (as characterized).
- **No government funding** (independence rationale).
- Core challenges: sustaining public attention during **crisis/war** when attention shifts elsewhere—when risks increase; fundraising when donors favor **direct aid** over **systemic change**.
- Scale: **8 staff**; annual budget about **\$600,000**.
- Illustrative legal/advocacy example in report: **Tamar** (deaf) / accessible missile alerts; government response and **pilot devices** (as summarized in both extractions).

## The Ask

Bizchut, Israel’s human rights center for people with disabilities, receives **no government funding** (to preserve independence in advocacy) and runs on donations. Tali Marcus follows up on contact from **about a year earlier** about possible support. The organization references the **Jewish Federations of North America (JFNA) Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force** report on supporting Israelis with disabilities (February 2026), in which **Bizchut is one of 18 recommended organizations**; report writers consulted Bizchut on needs on the ground.

Bizchut has **redirected/pivoted work toward wartime disability issues** during the current conflict (named **“Lion’s Roar”** in one thread and **“Roar of the Lion”** in the other — same campaign, different English phrasing). They ask whether the recipient Federation has **emergency / war-related funds** for this work and offer to **meet** to present current projects. They emphasize that **any support, including small amounts**, helps advance rights and welfare.

# Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations  
**February 2026**

**Document structure (from fuller summary):** Executive Summary, Introduction, Background Information, October 7 impact, Current Funding Landscape, Jewish Federations’ historic partners, Task Force methodology, Recommendations, Appendix (members & interviewees).

## Executive Summary

- Guide and recommendations for Federations supporting people in Israel with disabilities.  
- Up to **20%** of Israelis (~**2M**) live with disabilities (psychosocial, physical, sensory, developmental, learning, intellectual, chronic illness).  
- Strong **legislation**; uneven **implementation**, regional disparities, bureaucracy, persistent **gaps**.  
- Urgency after **Oct 7, 2023**: many with permanent physical/psychosocial disabilities; philanthropy complements government, strengthens nonprofits, accelerates change.  
- **Guiding principles:** prioritize non-government-funded program areas; support **innovation** with path to government adoption; invest in **capital/human resources** to leverage public funding; attention to **underserved** populations.  
- **18 vetted nonprofits** recommended, plus continued support for **JDC Israel Unlimited (JDC-IU)**.

## Background

- Legislative anchors: Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (1998), Rehabilitation in the Community of Persons with Mental Disabilities (2000), Inclusion Amendment to Special Education (2002), **UN CRPD** ratification, Welfare Services for People with Disabilities Law (2012), employment integration and accessibility legislation.  
- Nearly **20,000** people with disabilities still in **closed institutions**; government committed to **community-based** frameworks.  
- Disparities worse for **Arab citizens**, people in **poverty**, those navigating bureaucracy.  
- Many authorities: National Insurance Institute, Ministry of Welfare and Social Affairs, Health, Kupot Holim, Education, Labor, Defense (veterans), Justice (Commission for Equal Rights), Transportation, **local authorities**.

## October 7: impact on Israelis with disabilities

- As of **December 2025**: **20,000+** soldiers wounded since Oct 7; **80,000** total wounded veterans (all conflicts); **58%** of wounded soldiers emotional harm; **38%** exclusively PTSD; **~1,000** new disability claims/month; **~$2.5B** annual government budget for treating wounded soldiers.

## Current funding landscape

- Israeli government **primary funder** (billions/year).  
- Ministry of Welfare Disabilities Administration: **$671M** over five years + **$135M**/year (2022 law context).  
- **Philanthropy:** innovation, pilots, capital, advocacy, filling gaps.

## Jewish Federations allocations (2021–2024)

- Designated allocations for disability programs: **$1.27M** (2021), **$1.49M** (2022), **$1.87M** (2023), **$1.65M** (2024).  
- Grantee counts (year / grants / nonprofits) from detailed summary: e.g. 2021 **67** grants, **28** grantees; 2022 **70** / **31**; 2023 **82** / **27**; 2024 **81** / **28**.  
- **Philadelphia** listed among Federations allocating to Israelis-with-disabilities organizations in **2023–2024**.  
- **JDC-IU (core allocations):** 2023 **2.4%** of **$29.5M** → **$713,414**; 2024 **3.4%** of **$32.5M** → **$1,098,277**.

## Recommendations

- Fund disability programming **not** covered by government: accessibility, advocacy, awareness, technology, **accessing rights**, research.  
- For government-funded NGOs: prefer **new/innovative** approaches with adoption path; **infrastructure/staff** to maximize public funding; extra services only where government does not fund and need is clear; consider **adults**, **minorities**, underserved groups.

## Recommended organizations — Bizchut (this request)

- **Mission:** full exercise of rights—dignity, equality, autonomy, freedom—across all disability types and life domains.  
- **Five focus areas:** independent living in the community; fair employment; participation in policymaking; elimination of **restraints** in institutions; **access to justice**.  
- **30+ years**; credibility with officials, academics, nonprofits.  
- **No government funding** (independence).  
- **Challenges:** visibility for disability issues during **national crisis/war** (when risks rise); fundraising when donors favor **direct aid** over **systemic change**.  
- **Scale:** **8 staff**, **~$600,000** annual budget.  
- **Example:** Supreme Court petition re **accessible missile/alerts** (Tamar); government action + **pilot devices**.

## The Ask

Bizchut—Israel’s human rights center for people with disabilities, operating **without government funding** to preserve advocacy independence—is following up after roughly a year since prior contact about support. It references the **Jewish Federations of North America Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force** report on supporting Israelis with disabilities (February 2026; Bizchut consulted and is among **18 recommended organizations**). The organization has **redirected/pivoted work toward wartime disability issues** during the current conflict (referred to as **“Lion’s Roar”** in one document and **“Roar of the Lion”** in the other—same campaign, different English phrasing).

**Concrete asks:** whether the recipient Federation has **emergency / war-time funds** for this work; **any support, including small amounts**, would help continue advancing rights and welfare. **Non-financial:** a **meeting** to present current projects.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

## Context & Background

Tali Marcus wrote **March 5, 2026**. Between the earlier outreach and now, the Task Force produced its strategic guide; writers consulted Bizchut on needs in Israel, and Bizchut appears among recommended nonprofits. Philanthropy is positioned to complement government—especially **advocacy, accessibility, accessing rights, and innovation**—areas central to Bizchut.

**Organization snapshot (from report / summaries):** ~**8 staff**; annual budget ~**$600,000**; **30+ years** of work; mission to ensure dignity, equality, autonomy, and freedom across disability types; five focus areas: **independent living in the community**, **fair employment**, **participation in policymaking** (“nothing about us without us”), **elimination of restraints in institutions**, **access to justice**. Challenges: sustaining attention to disability rights during national crisis/war and fundraising when donors favor direct aid over systemic change.

### Wartime advocacy (both sources; extra detail where only one had it)

1. **Accessible Home Front Command / missile alerts for deaf and hard-of-hearing people** — Petition to the Supreme Court; state working on a solution; **urgent motion** filed recently. One source notes a petition **~2 years** earlier, state progress, then urgent motion plus **Ynet** coverage. Report example: **Tamar (deaf)** — petition led to government commitment and a **pilot of accessible emergency alert devices**.

2. **Foreign care / home-care workers abroad** — Letter to the **Transport(ation) Minister** asking **priority return** for workers stranded after travel; tens of thousands of people with disabilities and elderly at risk; severe family burden. One source cites **Israel Hayom** coverage.

3. **Accessible protected spaces** — Long-standing gap: regulations under the **Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law** not enacted for **20+ years**; advocacy to **Prime Minister’s Office** and **Ministry of Defense**; request for urgent **Knesset** debate. One source: joint petition with others; **Knesset discussion scheduled for the following week** (with **Ynet** link in original—URL not preserved in these extracts).

# Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations  
**February 2026**

**Document structure (from fuller summary):** Executive Summary, Introduction, Background Information, Current Funding Landscape, Jewish Federations’ Historic Partners, Task Force Methodology, Recommendations, Appendix (Task Force members & interviewees).

### Executive Summary

- Guide and recommendations for supporting people in Israel with disabilities.  
- Up to **20%** of Israel’s population—**~two million**—live with disabilities (psychosocial, physical, sensory, developmental, learning, intellectual, chronic illness).  
- Strong **legislative framework**; **uneven implementation**, regional disparities, bureaucracy, persistent **gaps**.  
- Urgency since **October 7, 2023**: many with **permanent physical and psychosocial** disabilities; philanthropy can complement government, strengthen nonprofits, accelerate change.  
- **Government** is primary funder (**billions/year**); philanthropy fills gaps—innovation, infrastructure, advocacy, capacity.  
- **Principles:** prioritize **non-government-funded** programs; support **innovation** with path to government adoption; invest in **capital/human resources** to leverage public funding; attention to **underserved** populations.  
- **18 vetted nonprofits** recommended, plus continued support for **JDC Israel Unlimited (JDC-IU)**.

### Background

- ~**20%** of ~**10M** residents; diverse disability types.  
- Key laws: Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities (**1998**), Rehabilitation in the Community of Persons with Mental Disabilities (**2000**), Inclusion Amendment to Special Education (**2002**), **UN CRPD** ratification, Welfare Services for People with Disabilities (**2012**), employment/accessibility (**2022**).  
- Nearly **20,000** still in **closed institutions**; push for **community-based** alternatives.  
- Disparities worse for **Arab citizens**, **poverty**, **bureaucratic** barriers.  
- Many authorities: National Insurance Institute, **local authorities**, Welfare, Health, Kupot Holim, Education, Labor, Defense (veterans), Justice (Commission for Equal Rights), Transportation.

### October 7 impact (as of December 2025)

- **20,000+** soldiers wounded since Oct 7; **80,000** total wounded veterans (all conflicts).  
- **58%** of wounded soldiers **emotional harm**; **38%** **PTSD-only**; **~1,000** new disability claims **monthly**; **~$2.5B** annual government spend on **wounded soldiers**.

### Current funding landscape

- Ministry of Welfare Disabilities Administration: **$671M** over five years + **$135M** annually; person-centered framing.  
- **Jewish Federations designated allocations (2021–2024):** 2021 **$1.27M** (67 grants / 28 grantees); 2022 **$1.49M** (70/31); 2023 **$1.87M** (82/27); 2024 **$1.65M** (81/28).  
- **JDC-IU:** 2023 **2.4%** of **$29.5M** core (**$713,414**); 2024 **3.4%** of **$32.5M** (**$1,098,277**).  
- **Philadelphia** listed among Federations allocating to disability-serving orgs in **2023–2024**; fuller list in report includes Ann Arbor, Atlanta, Boston, Chicago, and others.

### Recommendations

- Fund disability programming **not** covered by government: accessibility, advocacy, awareness, technology, **accessing rights**, research.  
- For government-funded NGOs: prefer **new/innovative** approaches with **adoption path**; **capital/HR** to maximize public funding; extra services only where government does not pay and need is clear; consider **adults**, **minorities**, other underserved groups.

### Bizchut (recommended organization) — consolidated

Mission: full exercise of rights—**dignity, equality, autonomy, freedom**—for **all** disability types across life domains. Five focus areas as above. **30+ years**; credibility with officials, academics, NGOs. **No government funding.** Staff **~8**; budget **~$600,000**. Case study: **Tamar (63, deaf)** — Supreme Court petition on **accessible missile alerts**; government committed; **pilot** devices.

### Other recommended organizations

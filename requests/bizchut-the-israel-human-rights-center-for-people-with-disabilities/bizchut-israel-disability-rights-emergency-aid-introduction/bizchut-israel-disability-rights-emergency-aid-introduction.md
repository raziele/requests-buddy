## The ask

Bizchut—Israel’s leading human rights organization focused on disability rights, operating **without government funding** to preserve advocacy independence—is following up on contact from roughly a year earlier about possible support. It references the **Jewish Federations of North America (JFNA) Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force** report on supporting Israelis with disabilities (February 2026), in which **Bizchut is one of 18 recommended organizations** (plus continued support for JDC Israel Unlimited is discussed in the report).

Bizchut asks:

1. Whether the Federation / recipient has **emergency (war-related) funds** available for its disability-rights work during the current conflict—English renderings in the sources vary (**“Lion’s Roar”** vs **“Roar of the Lion”**); same underlying situation.
2. To **set a meeting** to present **current projects** (explicit non-financial ask in both threads).

**Funding requested:** not specified (any support, including small amounts, would help).

**Non-financial ask:** meeting to present projects; parallel inquiry about emergency war-time funding.

## Context & background

**Relationship to federations:** Tali Marcus wrote to Jewish Federation contacts (including Talia Lidar at Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia in one chain). Bizchut had engaged about support ~1 year earlier. Task Force report authors consulted Bizchut on needs in Israel; Bizchut appears among recommended grantees.

**Wartime pivot:** Bizchut temporarily redirected work toward urgent disability issues during the war. Recent / ongoing advocacy described across the threads:

1. **Accessible Home Front Command / missile alerts for deaf and hard-of-hearing people** — Bizchut filed a Supreme Court petition (with a **recent urgent motion** in one account; the other notes a petition **~2 years** prior, state work toward a solution, renewed urgent motion, and **Ynet** media coverage). The state has begun working toward a solution; example in the Task Force report: **Tamar (63, deaf)** and a government commitment plus **pilot of accessible emergency alert devices**.
2. **Priority return for foreign care / home-care workers** stranded abroad — letter to the **Transport(ation) Minister**; workers’ absence leaves tens of thousands of people with disabilities and elderly at risk and burdens families; **Israel Hayom** coverage cited in one source.
3. **Accessible protected spaces** — long-standing regulatory gap under the Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (regulations not enacted for **20+ years** per one thread); demands to **Prime Minister’s Office** and **Ministry of Defense**; request for **urgent Knesset** debate—one version notes a **discussion scheduled for the following week** and a **Ynet** link (URL not preserved in these extracts).

**Organizational facts echoed in the report extracts:** ~**8 staff**; annual budget on the order of **~$600,000**; **30+ years** of work; credibility with officials, civil servants, academics, and nonprofits; core mission—**dignity, equality, autonomy, freedom**—with five focus areas: **independent living in the community**, **fair employment**, **participation in policymaking** (“nothing about us without us”), **elimination of restraints in institutional settings**, **access to justice**.

**National context (from attached report):** up to **~20%** of Israel’s population (~**two million** people) live with disabilities; strong legal framework with uneven implementation, regional disparities, bureaucracy, and gaps; post–**October 7, 2023** surge in physical and psychosocial disability and service demand; philanthropy urged to complement government especially in **advocacy, accessibility, accessing rights, innovation, research, awareness**—aligned with Bizchut’s model.

## Extracted data — `2025-RESOURCES-FOR-SUPPORTING-ISRAELIS-WITH-DISABILITIES-REPORT (1).pdf` (unified)

**Title / framing:** *Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations* (February 2026).

**Contents / sections:** Executive summary; introduction; background; October 7 impact; current funding landscape; Jewish Federations’ historic partners / allocations; task force methodology (per one extract); recommendations; appendix (members & interviewees).

**Executive summary (combined points):** Task Force guide for Federations’ overseas disability funding; ~20% of Israelis with disabilities across many categories; legal foundations strong but implementation uneven; urgency after Oct 7; government remains primary funder (billions/year); philanthropy fills gaps (innovation, pilots, capital, advocacy, capacity). **Guiding principles:** prioritize what government does not fund; support innovation with pathways to government adoption; invest in capital/human resources to leverage public funds; attend to **underserved populations**.

**Background highlights:** key laws—Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities (1998), Rehabilitation in the Community of Persons with Mental Disabilities (2000), Special Education inclusion amendment (2002), UN CRPD ratification, Welfare Services for People with Disabilities (2012), employment/accessibility legislation (including **2022** references in extracts); ~**20,000** people still in closed institutions with push toward community models; worse outcomes for **Arab citizens**, people in **poverty**, and those caught in **bureaucracy**; many ministries/agencies involved (National Insurance, Welfare, Health, Kupot Holim, Education, Labor, Defense/veterans, Justice/Commission for Equal Rights, Transportation, local authorities).

**October 7 / veterans disability snapshot (as of Dec 2025 in report):** **20,000+** soldiers wounded since Oct 7; **80,000** wounded veterans overall; **58%** emotional harm among wounded soldiers; **38%** PTSD-only cohort; **~1,000** new disability claims/month; **~$2.5B/year** government spend treating wounded soldiers.

**Funding landscape:** Israeli government primary; Welfare Disabilities Administration cited (**$671M** over five years + **$135M**/year in one formulation). **Jewish Federations designated allocations** (2021–2024): **$1.27M** (2021), **$1.49M** (2022), **$1.87M** (2023), **$1.65M** (2024); **28–31** grantees/year and grant counts **67–82** depending on year. **JDC Israel Unlimited:** **2023** 2.4% of **$29.5M** core = **$713,414**; **2024** 3.4% of **$32.5M** = **$1,098,277**. **Philadelphia** explicitly listed among Federations allocating to Israeli disability organizations (2023–2024); broader Federation list in report includes Ann Arbor, Atlanta, Boston, Chicago, etc.

**Recommendations (merged wording):** fund non-government-covered programming (accessibility, advocacy, awareness, technology, rights navigation, research). For government-funded NGOs: favor **innovation** with adoption path; **capital/HR** to maximize public funding; add services only where government does not fund and need is clear; consider **adults** and **minorities** among underserved groups.

**Bizchut entry (combined):** rights to dignity, equality, autonomy, freedom across **all** disability types and life domains; **no government funding**; challenge maintaining attention and fundraising during crisis when donors favor direct aid over systemic change; **Tamar** Supreme Court case on accessible alerts and government pilot response.

**Other recommended organizations (names preserved from extracts):** Access Israel, Kesher, AlManarah, Beit Issie Shapiro, Chimes Israel, Israel Elwyn, Kivunim, Shalva, SHEKEL, OTI, AKIM Israel, ILAN, Enosh, Etgarim, Nitzan Association, Krembo Wings, Simcha Layeled, **plus JDC Israel Unlimited**. *(One extract adds illustrative budget ranges for several NGOs; figures vary by source line—treat as report detail, not independently verified here.)*

**Appendix / consultation:** Task Force members (Toronto, San Francisco, Chicago, Detroit, etc.); professionals named include **Rebecca Caspi**, **Benjamin Rutland**, **Graeme Campbell**; experts/nonprofits consulted include **Tali Marcus (Bizchut)**, **MK Karine Elharrar**, academics, activists.

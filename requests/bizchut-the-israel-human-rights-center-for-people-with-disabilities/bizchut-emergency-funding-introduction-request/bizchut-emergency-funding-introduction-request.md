## The Ask

Bizchut requests:
1. A meeting/introduction call to present its current projects.
2. Emergency war-related funding support (amount not specified), including openness to small grants.
3. Clarification on whether emergency Federation funds are available for wartime disability-rights work.

Bizchut notes it is one of 18 recommended organizations in the Jewish Federations of North America (JFNA) Designated Allocations Task Force report on supporting Israelis with disabilities.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified  
**Non-Financial Ask:** Meeting to present current projects and discuss support options.

## Context & Background

Bizchut is a leading Israeli disability-rights organization focused on dignity, equality, autonomy, and full inclusion for people with disabilities. It emphasizes systemic rights-based advocacy and reports receiving no government funding in order to preserve independence.

Since the current war (referred to as “Lion’s Roar” / “Roar of the Lion” in source documents), Bizchut has redirected significant activity to urgent wartime disability issues while continuing broader rights work. The organization indicates that national crisis conditions increase risk for people with disabilities while public and philanthropic attention often shifts elsewhere.

The attached JFNA Task Force report (February 2026) consulted Bizchut and recommends it among 18 vetted nonprofits (plus continued support for JDC-Israel Unlimited). The report highlights philanthropy’s role in covering unmet needs in advocacy, accessibility, innovation, rights access, and underserved populations.

## Current Wartime Advocacy Priorities (as described)

1. **Accessible emergency alerts for deaf and hard-of-hearing people**
   - Bizchut petitioned the Supreme Court (initial petition around two years prior, with a recent urgent motion).
   - The state began work on a solution.
   - Reported progress includes commitment to action and pilot deployment of accessible emergency alert devices.
   - Media references mentioned: Ynet.

2. **Return of foreign home-care workers**
   - Bizchut asked the Transportation Minister to prioritize return of foreign caregivers stranded abroad after vacation travel.
   - Rationale: their absence places tens of thousands of people with disabilities and elderly people at risk and heavily burdens families.
   - Media references mentioned: Israel Hayom.

3. **Accessible protected spaces**
   - Bizchut argues accessibility regulations under the Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law have been delayed for more than 20 years.
   - It pressed the Prime Minister’s Office and Ministry of Defense, petitioned with partners, and requested urgent Knesset deliberation.
   - One source notes a Knesset discussion was scheduled for the following week.
   - Media references mentioned: Ynet.

## Organization Profile (from request + attached report)

- Scope: all disability types, across all areas of life.

### Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations (February 2026)

#### Executive Summary Highlights
- Up to 20% of Israel’s population (about two million people) live with disabilities.
- Israel has extensive disability legislation, but implementation is uneven and gaps persist.
- Since Oct 7, 2023, disability-related needs increased substantially (physical and psychosocial injuries, PTSD, rising claims).
- Government remains primary funder; philanthropy is positioned to fill unmet needs and scale innovation.
- Guiding principles:
  - Prioritize areas not government-funded (advocacy, accessibility, rights access, research, awareness, technology).
  - Fund innovation with pathways to government adoption.
  - Support infrastructure and human capital that increase public-system leverage.
  - Increase focus on underserved populations (including minorities and adults).

#### Background and System Landscape
- Key legal framework includes:
  - Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (1998)
  - Rehabilitation in the Community of Persons with Mental Disabilities Law (2000)
  - Inclusion Amendment to Special Education Law (2002)
  - UN CRPD ratification
  - Welfare Services for People with Disabilities Law (2012)
  - Employment/accessibility developments (including 2022 references)
- About 20,000 people with disabilities still live in closed institutions.
- Service experience varies by region and population, with heightened barriers for Arab citizens, people in poverty, and people facing bureaucratic complexity.
- Multiple agencies are involved (National Insurance Institute; Welfare; Health; Education; Labor; Defense; Justice/Commission for Equal Rights; Transportation; local authorities; Kupot Holim).

#### October 7 War Impact Metrics (as cited)
- 20,000+ soldiers wounded since Oct 7 (as of Dec 2025)
- 80,000 total wounded veterans across conflicts
- 58% of wounded soldiers experienced emotional harm
- 38% dealing exclusively with PTSD
- ~1,000 new disability claims monthly
- ~$2.5B annual government budget for treatment of wounded soldiers

#### Funding Landscape and Federation Allocations
- Ministry of Welfare Disabilities Administration: $671M over five years + $135M annually (per report references).
- Jewish Federations designated allocations for disability programs (2021–2024): totals cited as $1.27M, $1.49M, $1.87M, $1.65M.
- Additional allocation detail in one source:  
  - 2021: 67 grants / 28 grantees  
  - 2022: 70 / 31  
  - 2023: 82 / 27  
  - 2024: 81 / 28
- JDC-Israel Unlimited (core allocations):
  - 2023: 2.4% of $29.5M = $713,414
  - 2024: 3.4% of $32.5M = $1,098,277
- Philadelphia is explicitly noted among Federations allocating to disability-serving organizations (2023–2024).

#### Recommended Organizations
- 18 nonprofits recommended, including Bizchut, plus continued support for JDC-IU.
- Other named organizations include: Access Israel, Kesher, AlManarah, Beit Issie Shapiro, Chimes Israel, Israel Elwyn, Kivunim, Shalva, SHEKEL, OTI, AKIM Israel, ILAN, Enosh, Etgarim, Nitzan Association, Krembo Wings, Simcha Layeled.

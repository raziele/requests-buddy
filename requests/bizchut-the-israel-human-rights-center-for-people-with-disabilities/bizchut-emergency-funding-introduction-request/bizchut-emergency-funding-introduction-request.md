## The Ask

Bizchut is requesting:
1. **A meeting/introduction** to present current projects.
2. **Emergency war-related funding support** (amount not specified), including inquiry whether Federation emergency funds are available.

Bizchut emphasizes that it receives **no government funding** to preserve advocacy independence and that **any support, including small amounts**, would help sustain urgent rights-based wartime work.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified  
**Non-Financial Ask:** Meeting to present projects and discuss support opportunities.

## Context & Background

Bizchut followed up with Jewish Federation contacts after prior discussions about potential support approximately a year earlier. In March 2026 correspondence, Tali Marcus referenced the Jewish Federations of North America (JFNA) Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force report (February 2026), where Bizchut is listed among **18 recommended organizations** for support.

Since the current war (referred to as **"Lion's Roar" / "Roar of the Lion"**), Bizchut has redirected significant effort to urgent disability-rights interventions. Core points raised:

- **Accessible emergency alerts:** Home Front Command alerts were inaccessible for deaf and hard-of-hearing people. Bizchut petitioned the Supreme Court (initial petition ~2 years ago), later filed an urgent motion, and reports indicate the state began implementing a solution, including pilot accessible alert devices.
- **Return of foreign care workers:** Bizchut urged the Transportation Minister to prioritize return of foreign home-care workers stranded abroad, citing severe impact on tens of thousands of people with disabilities and older adults and heavy burden on families.
- **Accessible protected spaces:** Bizchut pressed for enforcement/advancement of accessibility regulations and protections, including outreach to the Prime Minister’s Office and Ministry of Defense, and requested urgent Knesset deliberation (with a discussion reportedly scheduled the following week in one account).

# Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations (February 2026)

## Executive Summary

- Produced by JFNA’s Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force.
- Estimates up to **20% of Israel’s population** (about **2 million people**) live with disabilities (psychosocial, physical, sensory, developmental, learning, intellectual, chronic illnesses).
- Israel has a robust legal framework, but implementation is uneven; regional disparities, bureaucracy, and service gaps persist.
- Since Oct 7, 2023, urgency has increased significantly due to large-scale physical and psychosocial injuries.
- Recommends philanthropy focus where government funding is insufficient: advocacy, accessibility, rights access, innovation, infrastructure/capacity, and underserved populations.
- Recommends **18 vetted nonprofits** plus continued support for **JDC Israel Unlimited (JDC-IU)**.

## Background Information

- Key legal/policy anchors cited include:
  - Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (1998)
  - Rehabilitation in the Community of Persons with Mental Disabilities Law (2000)
  - Inclusion amendment to Special Education Law (2002)
  - UN CRPD ratification
  - Welfare Services for People with Disabilities Law (2012)
  - Additional employment/accessibility measures (including 2022 references)
- Around **20,000** people with disabilities remain in closed institutions; community-based models are prioritized.
- Disparities are more severe for Arab citizens, people in poverty, and those facing bureaucratic barriers.
- Multiple ministries/agencies are involved (Welfare, Health, Education, Labor, Defense, Justice, Transportation, National Insurance Institute, local authorities, Kupot Holim).

## October 7 Impact Metrics (as cited in report)

- **20,000+** soldiers wounded since Oct 7 (as of Dec 2025)
- **80,000** total wounded veterans from all conflicts
- **58%** of wounded soldiers experienced emotional harm
- **38%** dealing exclusively with PTSD
- **1,000** new disability claims per month
- Approx. **$2.5B** annual government budget to treat wounded soldiers

## Current Funding Landscape

- Government remains primary funder (billions annually).
- Ministry of Welfare Disabilities Administration figures cited: **$671M over five years** plus **$135M annually**.
- Jewish Federations designated disability allocations:
  - 2021: **$1.27M**
  - 2022: **$1.49M**
  - 2023: **$1.87M**
  - 2024: **$1.65M**
- JDC-IU core allocation references:
  - 2023: **2.4%** of $29.5M (**$713,414**)
  - 2024: **3.4%** of $32.5M (**$1,098,277**)

## Recommendations

- Prioritize disability work **not funded by government**.
- Fund innovation with pathways to government adoption.
- Invest in physical/human capacity that unlocks or maximizes public funding.
- Fund additional services only where government does not cover clear needs.
- Increase focus on underserved populations (including adults and minorities).

## Bizchut Profile in Report (Relevant to This Request)

- Mission: secure full rights to dignity, equality, autonomy, and freedom for people with disabilities.
- Focus areas include independent living, fair employment, participation in policymaking ("nothing about us without us"), ending restraints in institutions, and access to justice.
- **No government funding** (to maintain independence).
- Reported size: **8 staff**, annual budget approximately **$600,000**.
- Noted wartime challenge: keeping disability rights visible while public attention shifts elsewhere.
- Cited case example: Supreme Court petition on accessible missile alerts for deaf individuals; government committed to action and launched pilot solutions.

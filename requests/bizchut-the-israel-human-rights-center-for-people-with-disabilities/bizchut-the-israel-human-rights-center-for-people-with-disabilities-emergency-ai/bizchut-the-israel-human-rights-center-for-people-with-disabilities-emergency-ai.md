## The Ask

Bizchut, Israel’s human rights center for people with disabilities, is following up after roughly a year of prior contact about possible support. The organization receives **no government funding** (by design, to preserve advocacy independence) and runs on donations. It is asking whether the Federation (or comparable community partner) has **emergency / war-related funds** for disability-rights work during the current conflict, and states that **even small amounts** would help it continue advancing rights and welfare for people with disabilities.

Bizchut also requests a **meeting** to present its **current projects**.

The outreach explicitly ties to the **Jewish Federations of North America (JFNA) Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force** report on supporting Israelis with disabilities (February 2026), in which Bizchut was **consulted** and is **one of 18 recommended organizations** (plus continued support for JDC Israel Unlimited is discussed in the report).

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Meeting to present current projects; inquiry whether emergency war-related funds exist for Bizchut’s disability-rights activity.

One intake notes **media coverage (Ynet)**; the Task Force report example cites **Tamar (63, deaf)** and a government commitment plus a **pilot of accessible emergency alert devices**.

2. **Foreign home-care / care workers stranded abroad** — Letter to the **Transport(ation) Minister** asking to **prioritize their return** to Israel; rationale includes tens of thousands of people with disabilities and elderly at risk and severe family burden when workers cannot return after travel. 3. **Accessible protected spaces** — Advocacy on long-delayed regulations under the Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (~20 years without enactment, per one intake); demands to the **Prime Minister’s Office** and **Ministry of Defense**; request for an urgent **Knesset** debate. The report also cites **December 2025** statistics on wounded soldiers and disability claims (e.g., **20,000+** soldiers wounded since Oct. 7; **80,000** total wounded veterans; high rates of emotional harm / PTSD among wounded soldiers; **~1,000** new disability claims monthly; **~$2.5B** annual government budget line cited for treating wounded soldiers). # Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations  
February 2026

**Document structure (TOC):** Executive Summary, Introduction, Background Information, Current Funding Landscape, Jewish Federations’ Historic Partners (where present in one summary), Task Force Methodology (where present), Recommendations, Appendix (Task Force members & interviewees).

### Executive summary (combined)

- Task Force guide and recommendations for Federations supporting Israelis with disabilities.  
- ~**20%** of Israel (~**2M** people) live with disabilities across diverse categories (psychosocial, physical, sensory, developmental, learning, intellectual, chronic illness).  
- Strong **legal framework** with uneven **implementation**, regional disparities, bureaucracy, and persistent **gaps**.  
- Urgency increased since **Oct. 7, 2023**; philanthropy can complement government, strengthen nonprofits, accelerate change.  
- Government remains primary funder (**billions/year**); philanthropy fills gaps (innovation, pilots, capital, advocacy, capacity).  
- Guiding principles: prioritize non-government-funded program areas; support innovation with adoption pathways; invest in infrastructure/staffing to maximize public funding; increase focus on underserved populations.  
- **18 vetted nonprofits recommended**, plus continued support for **JDC Israel Unlimited (JDC-IU)**.

### Background (merged)

- Legislative anchors include: Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (**1998**), Rehabilitation in the Community of Persons with Mental Disabilities Law (**2000**), Inclusion Amendment to Special Education Law (**2002**), **UN CRPD** ratification, Welfare Services for People with Disabilities Law (**2012**), and later employment/accessibility measures (e.g., **2022** references in summaries).  
- Nearly **~20,000** people with disabilities still in **closed institutions**; policy emphasis on **community-based** alternatives.  
- Disparities worse for **Arab citizens**, people in **poverty**, and those navigating **bureaucracy**.  
- Many authorities: National Insurance Institute, Ministry of Welfare and Social Affairs, Ministry of Health, Kupot Holim, Ministry of Education, Ministry of Labor, Ministry of Defense, Ministry of Justice (Commission for Equal Rights), Ministry of Transportation, local authorities.

### Current funding landscape (merged)

- Israeli government primary funder; Welfare Disabilities Administration cited as **$671M over five years** plus **$135M annually** (as summarized).  
- Jewish Federations **designated allocations** for disability programming (as summarized): **2021** **$1.27M**; **2022** **$1.49M**; **2023** **$1.87M**; **2024** **$1.65M** (grant counts/grantee counts vary slightly by summary line—treat as same report tables).  
- **Philadelphia** explicitly noted among Federations allocating to disability-serving organizations in **2023–2024** (first intake). A longer partial Federation list appears in the second intake’s extraction (Ann Arbor, Atlanta, Boston, Chicago, etc.).  
- **JDC-IU** core share examples: **2023** **2.4%** of **$29.5M** → **$713,414**; **2024** **3.4%** of **$32.5M** → **$1,098,277**.

### Recommendations (merged)

- Prioritize disability programming **not funded by government**: accessibility, advocacy, public awareness, technology, accessing rights, research.  
- For government-funded nonprofits: favor **new/innovative** approaches with a credible **government adoption** pathway; **capital** and **human resources** that unlock/maximize public funding; add services only where government does not fund and need is clear; consider **underserved** groups (e.g., adults, minorities).

### Bizchut (recommended organization — unified profile)

- Mission: dignity, equality, autonomy, freedom; rights across **all** disability types and life domains.  
- Five focus areas: **independent living** in the community; **fair employment**; **participation in policymaking** (“nothing about us without us”); **elimination of restraints** in institutional settings; **access to justice**.  
- **30+ years** of experience; credibility with officials, civil servants, academics, nonprofits.  
- **No government funding** (independence).  
- Operational scale cited: **8 staff**; annual budget about **$600,000**.  
- Challenges named in report: maintaining public attention to disability rights during **national crisis/war**; fundraising when donors favor **direct aid** over **systemic change**.  
- Example cited: Supreme Court petition on **accessible missile/rocket alerts** for deaf people; government commitment and **pilot devices**.

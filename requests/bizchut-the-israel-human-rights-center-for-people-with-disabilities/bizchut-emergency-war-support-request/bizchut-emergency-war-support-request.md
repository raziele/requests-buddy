## The Ask

Bizchut is requesting:
1. An introduction meeting to present its current projects.
2. Consideration for emergency war-related funding support for disability-rights work during the current conflict (described as "Lion’s Roar" / "Roar of the Lion").

They emphasize that:
- They previously engaged the Federation about support about a year earlier.
- They are one of 18 recommended organizations in the Jewish Federations of North America (JFNA) Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force report (February 2026).
- They receive no government funding and rely on donations to maintain advocacy independence.
- Any level of support, including small amounts, would help advance rights and welfare for people with disabilities.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified  
**Non-Financial Ask:** Meeting to present current projects and discuss potential support.

## Context & Background

Bizchut is a leading Israeli disability-rights organization focused on dignity, equality, autonomy, and freedom for people with disabilities. It has over 30 years of experience and is recognized in the JFNA report as a recommended nonprofit.

The organization reports that, due to wartime realities, it has redirected significant effort to urgent disability-related civil rights and accessibility issues:

1. **Accessible emergency alerts (deaf/hard-of-hearing):**  
   - Home Front Command alerts were not adequately accessible.  
   - Bizchut filed a Supreme Court petition (including a recent urgent motion).  
   - The state began working on a solution; report references a pilot of accessible emergency alert devices.  
   - Media coverage referenced (including Ynet).

2. **Return of foreign care workers:**  
   - Bizchut appealed to the Transportation Minister to prioritize return of foreign home-care workers stranded abroad.  
   - They argue absence of workers puts tens of thousands of people with disabilities and elderly individuals at severe risk and places major burdens on families.  
   - Media coverage referenced (including Israel Hayom).

3. **Accessible protected spaces:**  
   - Bizchut highlights long-standing gaps in implementation of accessibility regulations tied to the Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law.  
   - They pressed the Prime Minister’s Office and Ministry of Defense and requested an urgent Knesset discussion.  
   - One source notes a Knesset discussion was scheduled for the following week.

### 2025-RESOURCES-FOR-SUPPORTING-ISRAELIS-WITH-DISABILITIES-REPORT (1).pdf  
**Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations (February 2026)**

#### Executive Summary
- Provides JFNA Task Force guidance for supporting Israelis with disabilities.
- Estimates up to 20% of Israel’s population (about two million people) live with disabilities (psychosocial, physical, sensory, developmental, learning, intellectual, chronic illness).
- Notes strong legal framework but uneven implementation, regional disparities, bureaucratic complexity, and persistent access gaps.
- Post–October 7 urgency increased due to growing physical and psychosocial disability burdens.
- Recommends philanthropic focus where government does not fund, alongside innovation and capacity building.
- Recommends 18 vetted nonprofits plus continued support for JDC Israel Unlimited (JDC-IU).

#### Background Information
- Cites foundational legal and policy frameworks, including:
  - Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (1998)
  - Rehabilitation in the Community of Persons with Mental Disabilities Law (2000)
  - Inclusion amendment to Special Education Law (2002)
  - UN CRPD ratification
  - Welfare Services for People with Disabilities Law (2012)
  - Subsequent employment/accessibility reforms
- Notes approximately 20,000 people with disabilities still in closed institutions and ongoing policy push toward community-based frameworks.
- Identifies disparities for underserved groups, including Arab citizens and people in poverty.

#### October 7 Impact Metrics (as cited in report)
- 20,000+ soldiers wounded since Oct 7 (as of Dec 2025)
- ~80,000 total wounded veterans (all conflicts)
- 58% of wounded soldiers experienced emotional harm
- 38% dealing exclusively with PTSD
- ~1,000 new disability claims per month
- ~$2.5B annual government budget to treat wounded soldiers

#### Current Funding Landscape
- Israeli government is primary funder (billions annually overall).
- Ministry of Welfare Disabilities Administration: $671M over five years plus $135M annually (as cited).
- Jewish Federations designated allocations for disability programming:
  - 2021: $1.27M
  - 2022: $1.49M
  - 2023: $1.87M
  - 2024: $1.65M
- Additional detail in one source includes grant/grantee counts by year and notes Philadelphia among federations allocating to disability organizations in 2023–2024.
- JDC-IU core allocation examples:
  - 2023: 2.4% of $29.5M ($713,414)
  - 2024: 3.4% of $32.5M ($1,098,277)

#### Recommendations
- Prioritize disability programming not funded by government, especially:
  - advocacy
  - accessibility
  - public awareness
  - technology
  - rights access
  - research
- For government-funded nonprofits, prioritize:
  - innovation with pathway to government adoption
  - capital and human resources that maximize public funding
  - additional services only where unmet need is clear
  - underserved populations (including adults and minorities)

#### Bizchut Profile in Report
- Mission: ensure people with disabilities can fully exercise rights to dignity, equality, autonomy, and freedom.
- Works across all disability types and major life domains.
- Five focus areas:
  1. Independent living in the community
  2. Fair employment
  3. Participation in policymaking ("nothing about us without us")
  4. Elimination of restraints in institutional settings
  5. Access to justice
- Receives **no government funding** to preserve advocacy independence.
- Reported organizational size: **8 staff**, annual budget around **$600,000**.
- Cited challenge: sustaining attention and philanthropic support for disability rights during national crisis and war.
- Highlighted case: Supreme Court action for accessible missile alerts for a deaf petitioner (Tamar), with subsequent state action/pilot.

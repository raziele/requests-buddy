## The Ask

Bizchut is requesting:
1. An introduction/coordination meeting to present its current projects.
2. Emergency war-related funding support (amount not specified), including inquiry whether designated emergency funds are available.
3. Broader support for ongoing disability-rights advocacy during wartime, noting that even small funding would help.

Bizchut references prior contact from about a year earlier and highlights that it is one of 18 recommended organizations in the Jewish Federations of North America (JFNA) disability support report (February 2026).

**Funding Requested:** Not specified  
**Non-Financial Ask:** Meeting to present projects and discuss potential support.

## Context & Background

Bizchut is a leading Israeli disability-rights organization focused on dignity, equality, autonomy, and full inclusion for people with disabilities. It reports receiving **no government funding** in order to preserve advocacy independence.

The organization states that during the current war (referred to as "Lion's Roar" / "Roar of the Lion"), it redirected substantial effort toward urgent wartime accessibility and rights protection. Key actions include:

1. **Accessible emergency alerts** for deaf and hard-of-hearing people:  
   - Bizchut petitioned the Supreme Court (initial petition around two years earlier; recent urgent motion filed).  
   - The state began working on solutions and launched/piloted accessible emergency alert mechanisms.

2. **Return of foreign home-care workers**:  
   - Bizchut appealed to the Transportation Minister to prioritize return of workers stranded abroad after leave.  
   - Emphasized severe impact on tens of thousands of people with disabilities and elderly individuals and heavy burden on families.

3. **Accessible protected spaces**:  
   - Bizchut pressed the Prime Minister’s Office and Ministry of Defense and sought urgent Knesset action.  
   - Noted long delays (20+ years) in implementing relevant accessibility regulations under the Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law.  
   - A Knesset discussion was reportedly scheduled for the following week.

The attached JFNA Task Force report identifies Bizchut among recommended organizations and describes:
- 30+ years of activity and credibility.
- Approx. **8 staff** and annual budget around **$600,000**.
- Ongoing challenge of sustaining attention/funding for systemic disability-rights work during national crises, when donors may prioritize direct aid.

### 2025-RESOURCES-FOR-SUPPORTING-ISRAELIS-WITH-DISABILITIES-REPORT (1).pdf

# Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations (February 2026)

## Executive Summary

- Prepared by JFNA’s Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force.
- Up to **20% of Israel’s population** (about **2 million people**) live with disabilities.
- Israel has substantial legal protections, but implementation remains uneven, with disparities and bureaucratic barriers.
- Since Oct 7, 2023, physical and psychosocial disability needs have intensified significantly.
- Philanthropy is positioned to complement government funding by supporting gaps, innovation, advocacy, infrastructure, and capacity.
- Recommends **18 vetted nonprofits** plus continued support for **JDC Israel Unlimited (JDC-IU)**.

## Background

- Major legal framework includes: Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law (1998), Rehabilitation in the Community Law (2000), Inclusion Amendment to Special Education Law (2002), UN CRPD ratification, Welfare Services for People with Disabilities Law (2012), and additional accessibility/employment reforms.
- About **20,000** people with disabilities remain in closed institutions.
- Higher barriers persist for underserved populations including Arab citizens and people in poverty.
- Multiple agencies share responsibility: National Insurance Institute, Welfare, Health, Education, Labor, Defense, Justice (Commission for Equal Rights), Transportation, local authorities, and health funds.

## October 7 Impact Metrics (as cited)

- 20,000+ soldiers wounded since Oct 7.
- 80,000 total wounded veterans (all conflicts).
- 58% of wounded soldiers with emotional harm; 38% exclusively PTSD.
- ~1,000 new disability claims monthly.
- ~$2.5B annual government budget for treatment of wounded soldiers.

## Current Funding Landscape

- Government is principal funder (billions annually).
- Ministry of Welfare Disabilities Administration: approximately **$671M over five years + $135M annually** (as cited).
- Jewish Federations designated disability allocations (2021–2024):  
  - 2021: $1.27M  
  - 2022: $1.49M  
  - 2023: $1.87M  
  - 2024: $1.65M  
- JDC-IU core allocation examples:  
  - 2023: 2.4% of $29.5M core ($713,414)  
  - 2024: 3.4% of $32.5M core ($1,098,277)

## Recommendations

- Prioritize disability programming not funded by government: accessibility, advocacy, public awareness, technology, rights navigation, research.
- For government-funded nonprofits, support innovation with adoption pathways, capital/human resources to maximize public funding, and additional services only where unmet need is clear.
- Consider prioritizing underserved groups (including adults and minorities).

## Bizchut Profile in Report

- Mission: secure rights to dignity, equality, autonomy, and freedom for people with disabilities across life domains.
- Focus areas include independent community living, fair employment, participation in policymaking ("nothing about us without us"), reducing/eliminating restraints in institutions, and access to justice.
- Receives **no government funding**.
- Noted wartime challenge: keeping disability rights visible during national emergency while fundraising for systemic change.
- Case example: Supreme Court petition for accessible missile alerts for a deaf petitioner (Tamar), followed by government commitment and pilot action.

## The Ask

HaOgen for Reservist Families is requesting funding for four emergency programs during Operation Roaring Lion, as Israel faces another major mobilization. Programs include: immediate relief (meals and essential supplies, ~$650/family) for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles, with three such families currently supported; 10,000 grocery vouchers for volunteer cooks to provide meals to families where the single parent cannot keep up; daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists (~$900/session, with 100+ children participating daily and 2,640 total participants to date); and activity kits for children in bomb shelters ($12 per family), with the option to fund a specific city for outsized impact. The organization has expanded volunteer matching and support to new cities and is seeking support to keep these programs running for the duration of the situation.

**Funding Requested:** Multiple discrete asks: ~$650 per family for missile-hit relief; funding for 10,000 grocery vouchers; ~$900 per daily Zoom session; $12 per family for shelter activity kits (city-level funding suggested). No single total amount stated.

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Immediate relief for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles (meals and essential supplies): approximately $650 per family
- Grocery vouchers for volunteer cooks to provide meals to reservist families: 10,000 vouchers (total amount not specified)
- Daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists (content and technology): approximately $900 per session
- Activity kits for children in bomb shelters: $12 per family

## Context & Background

Israel is enduring another massive mobilization due to the operation in Iran, and HaOgen for Reservist Families is in higher demand than ever. Families face overwhelming practical difficulties: when a siren sounds, a mother with three small children must bring them to the shelter alone; a parent with sleeping children needs someone to take the dog out at night; essential workers must go to work while schools are closed and need childcare. HaOgen has increased volunteer support and is matching volunteers within a very close radius to families, as well as expanding into new cities.

Against this backdrop, the organization is running four programs for which it is seeking funding. First, for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles, it provides immediate relief (meals and essential supplies) at about $650 per family; it is currently supporting three families whose homes were destroyed. Second, with the number of drafted reservists very high, the organization has drastically increased the number of meals provided and is raising funds for 10,000 grocery vouchers so volunteer cooks can feed these families. Third, with schools closed, HaOgen runs daily Zoom workshops for reservists' children; over 100 children participate each day (2,640 total to date), at about $900 per session for content and technology, providing both a fun activity and relief for parents at home alone. Fourth, for families with young children spending extended time in bomb shelters, it seeks funding for activity kits at $12 per family; funding for a specific city would have a large impact, and the organization works with communities of all sizes (under 100 families, 300–400 families, and over 600 families).

The request is directed to the Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia (Tali and Tamar). HaOgen asks whether the federation would be interested in supporting one or more of these needs and expresses gratitude for support to keep the programs running for as long as the current situation continues.

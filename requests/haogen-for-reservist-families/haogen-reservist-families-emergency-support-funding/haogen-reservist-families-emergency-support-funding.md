## The Ask

HaOgen for Reservist Families is requesting funding to sustain and expand emergency support programs during Israel's Operation Roaring Lion mobilization. As reservists are called up in large numbers, the organization has increased volunteer matching and expanded into new cities. They are seeking support for four specific initiatives: (1) immediate relief for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles (meals and essential supplies at ~$650 per family; currently supporting three such families); (2) 10,000 grocery vouchers for volunteer cooks to provide meals to reservist families where single parents cannot keep up with meal preparation; (3) daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists while schools are closed (~$900 per session; over 100 children participating daily, 2,640 total to date); and (4) activity kits for children in bomb shelters at $12 per family, scalable by city. The email asks the Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia to indicate interest in supporting one or more of these needs so the programs can continue for the duration of the situation.

**Funding Requested:** Multiple programs: approximately $650 per family for missile-affected family relief; funds for 10,000 grocery vouchers; approximately $900 per session for daily children's Zoom workshops; $12 per family for bomb-shelter activity kits (city-level funding possible).

**Funding Breakdown:**

## Context & Background

Israel is in the midst of another large-scale mobilization due to the operation in Iran. HaOgen for Reservist Families supports reservist families and is experiencing higher demand than ever. The email describes acute daily challenges: a mother with three small children must get them to the shelter alone when sirens sound; a parent with sleeping children needs someone to take the dog out at night; essential workers need childcare when schools are closed. HaOgen has increased volunteer support and matches volunteers within a very close radius to families, while expanding into new cities.

Four programs are described as currently running and in need of funding. First, for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles, HaOgen provides immediate relief (meals and essential supplies) at about $650 per family; they are currently supporting three families whose homes were destroyed. Second, with a very high number of reservists currently drafted, the organization has greatly increased the number of meals provided and is raising funds to purchase 10,000 grocery vouchers for volunteer cooks. Third, with schools closed, HaOgen runs daily Zoom workshops for reservist children; over 100 children participate each day (2,640 total participants so far), at a daily cost of about $900 per session for content and technology. Fourth, for families with young children spending long periods in bomb shelters, they seek funding for activity kits at $12 per family, with impact scalable by city (communities range from under 100 families to over 600).

The message asks Tali and Tamar whether the Federation would be interested in supporting one or more of these needs, so that these programs can continue for as long as the current situation lasts.

## The Ask

HaOgen for Reservist Families is requesting funding to scale emergency support during Operation Lion's Roar, as Israel experiences another major reservist mobilization. The organization matches volunteers to reservist families for practical help (e.g., shelter runs, childcare, dog-walking) and is running four emergency programs: immediate relief for families whose homes were hit by missiles (~$650/family), 100,000 grocery coupons for volunteer-cooked meals, daily Zoom workshops for reservist children (~$900/session), and activity kits for children in bomb shelters ($12/family). They are seeking an additional $200,000 to support these efforts.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Immediate relief (meals and essential supplies) for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles: approximately $650 per family
- Grocery coupons for volunteer cooks to provide meals to reservist families: 100,000 coupons
- Daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists (content and technology): approximately $900 per session
- Activity kits for children in bomb shelters: $12 per family

## Context & Background

Israel has undergone another massive reservist mobilization due to the operation in Iran (Operation Lion's Roar). HaOgen for Reservist Families supports reservist families with volunteer-based practical help—matching volunteers in close radius for shelter runs, childcare, pet care, and similar needs—and has expanded to new cities despite the situation. Demand is higher than ever: single parents at home cannot easily prepare meals, take children to shelters alone, or manage work when schools are closed.

HaOgen has launched four emergency programs. First, for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles, they provide immediate relief (meals and essential supplies) at about $650 per family; they are currently supporting two such families—one from Beit Shemesh and one from Beer Sheva—whose homes were destroyed. Second, they have increased the number of meals provided and are raising funds for 100,000 grocery coupons so volunteer cooks can feed these families. Third, with schools closed, HaOgen runs daily Zoom workshops for reservist children; over 100 children participate daily, at about $900 per session for content and technology. Fourth, they seek funding for activity kits ($12 per family) for children spending extended time in bomb shelters, with the option to fund a specific city for large community impact.

Support is needed now to meet the surge in demand and to fund these four programs during the operation.

## The Ask

HaOgen for Reservist Families is requesting $200,000 in additional funding to scale its emergency support during Operation Lion's Roar, as Israel experiences another major reservist mobilization. The organization is matching volunteers to reservist families for practical help (childcare, errands, meals) and has launched four programs: immediate relief for families whose homes were hit by missiles (~$650/family), 100,000 grocery coupons for volunteer-cooked meals, daily Zoom workshops for over 100 children of reservists (~$900/session), and activity kits for children in bomb shelters ($12/family). They are currently supporting two families whose homes were destroyed and seek support for one or more of these specific needs.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Immediate relief per family (meals and essential supplies for homes hit by missiles): approximately $650 per family
- Grocery coupons for volunteer cooks to provide meals to reservist families: 100,000 coupons (total amount not specified)
- Daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists (content and technological costs): approximately $900 per session
- Activity kits for children in bomb shelters: $12 per family

## Context & Background

Israel is in the midst of another massive reservist mobilization due to the operation in Iran (Operation Lion's Roar). HaOgen for Reservist Families supports families left at home when a member is called up: when sirens sound, a mother with three small children must get them to the shelter alone; a parent needs someone to take the dog out at night; an essential worker needs childcare when schools are closed. Demand for HaOgen's support is higher than ever.

The organization has increased volunteer support and matches volunteers in very close radius to families, while expanding into new cities. It is also running four new programs during this operation. Two reservist families—one from Beit Shemesh and one from Beer Sheva—have had their homes destroyed and are receiving immediate relief (meals and essential supplies). The number of reservists drafted at one time is currently very high, making it nearly impossible for the single parent at home to prepare meals without help, so HaOgen is raising funds for 100,000 grocery coupons for volunteer cooks. With schools closed, HaOgen runs daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists; over 100 children have participated every day, at a cost of about $900 per session. For families spending extended time in bomb shelters, HaOgen is seeking funding for activity kits at $12 per family to keep children engaged and show families they are not alone.

HaOgen is aiming to raise an additional $200,000 to support these efforts and is asking whether the recipients would be interested in supporting one or more of these specific needs.

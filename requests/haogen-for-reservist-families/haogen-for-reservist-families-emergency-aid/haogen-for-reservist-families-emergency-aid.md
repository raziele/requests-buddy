## The Ask

HaOgen for Reservist Families is requesting funding to sustain and scale emergency support during Israel's reservist mobilization (Operation Lion's Roar / Operation Roaring Lion). One source specifies **$200,000** in additional funding. The organization is matching volunteers to reservist families for practical help (childcare, errands, meals) and has launched four programs in need of support:

1. **Immediate relief for families whose homes were hit by missiles** — Meals and essential supplies at approximately **$650 per family**. One source reports two families whose homes were destroyed (Beit Shemesh, Beer Sheva); the other reports three such families currently supported.
2. **Grocery coupons/vouchers for volunteer-cooked meals** — One source: **100,000** coupons (total amount not specified); other source: **10,000** grocery vouchers (fundraising in progress). *Note: figure differs between sources (100,000 vs 10,000).*
3. **Daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists** — While schools are closed; over 100 children participate daily (2,640 total participants to date in one source). Content and technological costs: approximately **$900 per session**.
4. **Activity kits for children in bomb shelters** — **$12 per family**, scalable by city.

They ask whether the recipients (e.g., Tali and Tamar / Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia) would be interested in supporting one or more of these needs so the programs can continue for the duration of the situation.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000 (one source); alternatively, support for one or more programs as above.

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Immediate relief per family (meals and essential supplies for homes hit by missiles): approximately $650 per family
- Grocery coupons/vouchers for volunteer cooks: 100,000 coupons (one source) or 10,000 vouchers (other) — total amount not specified / fundraising in progress
- Daily Zoom workshops (content and technological costs): approximately $900 per session

## Context & Background

Israel is in the midst of another large-scale reservist mobilization due to the operation in Iran (Operation Lion's Roar / Roaring Lion). HaOgen for Reservist Families supports families left at home when a member is called up: when sirens sound, a mother with three small children must get them to the shelter alone; a parent needs someone to take the dog out at night; an essential worker needs childcare when schools are closed. Demand for HaOgen's support is higher than ever.

The organization has increased volunteer support and matches volunteers in very close radius to families, while expanding into new cities. Four programs are running. For reservist families whose homes were destroyed or damaged by missiles, HaOgen provides immediate relief (meals and essential supplies); two sources cite two vs three such families. With the number of reservists drafted very high, the single parent at home can barely prepare meals without help, so HaOgen is raising funds for grocery coupons/vouchers (100,000 or 10,000 depending on source). With schools closed, HaOgen runs daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists; over 100 children participate every day, at about $900 per session. For families spending extended time in bomb shelters, HaOgen seeks funding for activity kits at $12 per family to keep children engaged and show families they are not alone. Impact is scalable by city (communities from under 100 families to over 600).

The message asks whether the Federation would be interested in supporting one or more of these needs so that these programs can continue for as long as the current situation lasts.

## The Ask

HaOgen for Reservist Families is requesting additional funding to scale and sustain emergency support during Operation Lion's Roar (Operation Roaring Lion), as Israel experiences another major reservist mobilization. The organization matches volunteers to reservist families for practical help (childcare, errands, meals) and has launched four programs in need of support:

1. **Immediate relief for missile-affected families** — Meals and essential supplies for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles: approximately **$650 per family**. One source states two families (Beit Shemesh, Beer Sheva) whose homes were destroyed are currently supported; another states three such families. Support is ongoing as needed.
2. **Grocery vouchers for volunteer-cooked meals** — Vouchers for volunteer cooks to provide meals to reservist families when single parents cannot keep up with meal preparation. **Discrepancy between sources:** one request specifies **100,000** grocery coupons; the other specifies **10,000** grocery vouchers. Total amount for vouchers not specified in either.
3. **Daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists** — While schools are closed: content and technological costs approximately **$900 per session**. Over 100 children participate daily; one source reports **2,640 total participants to date**.
4. **Activity kits for children in bomb shelters** — **$12 per family**, scalable by city (communities range from under 100 families to over 600).

One source explicitly requests **$200,000** in total additional funding and asks whether the recipient would be interested in supporting one or more of these needs. Another asks the Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia (Tali and Tamar) to indicate interest in supporting one or more of these needs so the programs can continue for the duration of the situation.

**Funding breakdown (summary):**
- Immediate relief per family (meals and essential supplies for homes hit by missiles): ~$650 per family
- Grocery coupons/vouchers for volunteer cooks: 10,000 or 100,000 (sources differ)
- Daily Zoom workshops (content and technology): ~$900 per session
- Activity kits for children in bomb shelters: $12 per family

## Context & Background

Israel is in the midst of another large-scale reservist mobilization due to the operation in Iran (Operation Lion's Roar). HaOgen for Reservist Families supports families left at home when a member is called up. Daily challenges include: when sirens sound, a mother with three small children must get them to the shelter alone; a parent needs someone to take the dog out at night; essential workers need childcare when schools are closed. Demand for HaOgen's support is higher than ever.

The organization has increased volunteer support and matches volunteers in very close radius to families, while expanding into new cities. Four programs are running during this operation:

- **Missile-affected families:** Two reservist families—one from Beit Shemesh and one from Beer Sheva—have had their homes destroyed and are receiving immediate relief (meals and essential supplies); another source refers to three such families receiving support.
- **Meals:** The number of reservists drafted at one time is very high, making it nearly impossible for the single parent at home to prepare meals without help; HaOgen has greatly increased meals provided and is raising funds for grocery vouchers for volunteer cooks.
- **Children's workshops:** With schools closed, HaOgen runs daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists; over 100 children participate every day (2,640 total to date in one report), at a cost of about $900 per session.
- **Bomb shelters:** For families spending extended time in bomb shelters, HaOgen seeks funding for activity kits at $12 per family to keep children engaged and show families they are not alone; impact is scalable by city.

HaOgen is aiming to raise additional funds (one source specifies $200,000) and is asking recipients whether they would be interested in supporting one or more of these needs so that the programs can continue for as long as the current situation lasts.

## The Ask

HaOgen for Reservist Families is requesting funding to scale emergency support during **Operation Roaring Lion** (also referred to as Operation Lion's Roar), as Israel faces another major reservist mobilization. The organization matches volunteers to reservist families for practical help (e.g., shelter runs, childcare, dog-walking) and is running four emergency programs. Support is requested to keep these programs running for the duration of the situation.

**Funding Requested:** One source specifies an additional **$200,000** for these efforts. The other does not state a single total; both provide the per-item breakdown below.

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **Immediate relief** for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles (meals and essential supplies): approximately **$650 per family**.
- **Grocery vouchers/coupons** for volunteer cooks to provide meals to reservist families: **10,000 vouchers** (one source) / **100,000 coupons** (other source) — clarify with organization if targeting voucher count or total budget.
- **Daily Zoom workshops** for children of reservists (content and technology): approximately **$900 per session** (100+ children participating daily; 2,640 total participants to date in one source).
- **Activity kits** for children in bomb shelters: **$12 per family**; funding for a specific city can have outsized impact.

## Context & Background

Israel is enduring another massive mobilization due to the operation in Iran. HaOgen for Reservist Families is in higher demand than ever and supports reservist families with volunteer-based practical help—matching volunteers within a very close radius for shelter runs, childcare, pet care, and similar needs. The organization has expanded volunteer matching and support to new cities despite the situation. Families face overwhelming practical difficulties: when a siren sounds, a mother with three small children must bring them to the shelter alone; a parent with sleeping children may need someone to take the dog out at night; essential workers must go to work while schools are closed and need childcare.

HaOgen has launched four emergency programs. **First**, for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles, it provides immediate relief (meals and essential supplies) at about $650 per family. One source reports **three** such families currently supported whose homes were destroyed; the other reports **two** families—one from Beit Shemesh and one from Beer Sheva—whose homes were destroyed. **Second**, with the number of drafted reservists very high, the organization has drastically increased the number of meals provided and is raising funds for grocery vouchers/coupons so volunteer cooks can feed these families. **Third**, with schools closed, HaOgen runs daily Zoom workshops for reservists' children; over 100 children participate each day, at about $900 per session for content and technology, providing both a fun activity and relief for parents at home alone. **Fourth**, for families with young children spending extended time in bomb shelters, it seeks funding for activity kits at $12 per family; funding for a specific city would have large impact, and the organization works with communities of all sizes (under 100 families, 300–400 families, and over 600 families).

The request has been directed to the Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia (Tali and Tamar) and also forwarded from JUF. HaOgen asks whether the federation would be interested in supporting one or more of these needs and expresses gratitude for support to keep the programs running for as long as the current situation continues.

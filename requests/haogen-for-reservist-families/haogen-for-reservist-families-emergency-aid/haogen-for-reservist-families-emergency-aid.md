## The Ask

HaOgen for Reservist Families is requesting funding for four emergency programs during Operation Roaring Lion / Operation Lion's Roar, as Israel faces another major reservist mobilization. The organization matches volunteers to reservist families for practical help (e.g., shelter runs, childcare, dog-walking) and has expanded volunteer matching and support to new cities. Funding is sought for:

1. **Immediate relief** (meals and essential supplies) for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles: approximately **$650 per family**. The organization is currently supporting two to three such families whose homes were destroyed (one from Beit Shemesh, one from Beer Sheva, noted in one submission).
2. **Grocery vouchers/coupons** for volunteer cooks to provide meals to reservist families. One submission cites **10,000 vouchers** (total amount not specified); another cites **100,000 coupons** and an overall ask of **$200,000** for the four programs combined.
3. **Daily Zoom workshops** for children of reservists (content and technology): approximately **$900 per session**. Over 100 children participate each day; 2,640 total participants to date (per one submission).
4. **Activity kits** for children in bomb shelters: **$12 per family**. Funding for a specific city would have outsized impact; the organization works with communities of all sizes.

**Funding Requested:** One submission states no single total (discrete asks per program); the other requests an **additional $200,000** to support these four efforts.

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Immediate relief for missile-hit reservist families: ~$650 per family
- Grocery vouchers/coupons for volunteer-cooked meals: 10,000 vouchers (one submission) / 100,000 coupons (other submission); total amount for vouchers not specified in the first
- Daily Zoom workshops for reservist children: ~$900 per session
- Activity kits for children in bomb shelters: $12 per family

## Context & Background

Israel is enduring another massive reservist mobilization due to the operation in Iran. HaOgen for Reservist Families is in higher demand than ever. Families face overwhelming practical difficulties: when a siren sounds, a mother with three small children must bring them to the shelter alone; a parent with sleeping children may need someone to take the dog out at night; essential workers must go to work while schools are closed and need childcare. HaOgen matches volunteers within a very close radius to families and has expanded into new cities despite the situation.

Against this backdrop, the organization runs four emergency programs. First, for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles, it provides immediate relief (meals and essential supplies) at about $650 per family; it is currently supporting two to three families whose homes were destroyed (Beit Shemesh and Beer Sheva named in one submission). Second, with the number of drafted reservists very high, it has drastically increased the number of meals provided and is raising funds for grocery vouchers/coupons so volunteer cooks can feed these families (10,000 vouchers in one version, 100,000 coupons in the other). Third, with schools closed, HaOgen runs daily Zoom workshops for reservists' children—over 100 children participate each day (2,640 total to date in one submission), at about $900 per session for content and technology—providing both a fun activity and relief for parents at home alone. Fourth, for families with young children spending extended time in bomb shelters, it seeks funding for activity kits at $12 per family; funding for a specific city would have large impact.

One submission notes that the request is directed to the Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia (Tali and Tamar) and asks whether the federation would be interested in supporting one or more of these needs and expresses gratitude for support to keep the programs running for as long as the current situation continues. Support is needed to meet the surge in demand and to fund these four programs during the operation.

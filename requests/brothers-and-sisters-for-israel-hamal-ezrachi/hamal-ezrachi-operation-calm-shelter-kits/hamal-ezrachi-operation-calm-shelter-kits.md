## The Ask

Hamal Ezrachi (BSI) is launching Operation Calm in the Shelter to provide emotional relief kits for children spending extended hours in shelters during ongoing security alerts. Kits include emotion cards, stress-reduction games, and guided calming activities for elementary school children. Total budget $250,000; expected reach 14,000 children in 10 municipalities. Limited shelter support (mattresses/mats) may be provided when requested by local authorities.

**Funding Requested:** $250,000

## Context & Background

Thousands of children in northern and central Israel are spending long hours in shelters. Many experience rising anxiety and emotional distress; parents are exhausted and often lack capacity to occupy and calm children. In response to urgent requests from local authorities, BSI is providing emotional relief kits developed by their pedagogical team. Kits are designed for elementary school children (grades 1–6) and include emotion cards, stress-reduction games adapted for shelter environments, and guided calming activities. Implementation can be deployed within days using existing infrastructure and municipal partnerships.

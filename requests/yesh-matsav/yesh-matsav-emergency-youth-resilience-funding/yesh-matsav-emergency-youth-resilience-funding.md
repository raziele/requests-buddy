## The Ask

Yesh Matsav requests $50,000 in emergency funding to scale its proven youth resilience model in Northern Israel, where Hezbollah’s escalation since February 28, 2026 has put thousands of youth under sustained rocket and drone attacks. The organization, which reached 2,000 youth in 2025 with 80% showing significant improvement in motivation and achievement, will use the grant to deploy immediately to 400 additional youth and 100 educators: establishing Resilience Hubs for teacher training, delivering life-changing role-model encounters in shelters, distributing crisis kits to 20 schools, and scaling an AI-powered mentorship tool for 24/7 support.

**Funding Requested:** $50,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Teacher Training: Establishing Resilience Hubs for 100 educators in Northern schools: $20,000
- Life-Changing Encounters: Delivering 10+ Key Experience sessions with inspiring role models to 300+ youth in bomb shelters: $15,000
- Shelter Crisis Kits for 20 schools: $10,000
- Digital Resilience (AI Mentor): Scaling AI-powered mentorship for 24/7 real-time guidance and support: $5,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Distribution and promotion of the request to potential funders (recipient offered to circulate and assist).

## Context & Background

Since Saturday [late Feb / early Mar 2026], the CEO’s phone has not stopped with messages about previously stable kids who have suddenly become at risk. Yesh Matsav frames itself as fighting the "silent front" of the war—youth resilience—and is responding by equipping educators with tools to support teens in temporary housing, shelters, and classrooms.

Northern Israel has been under relentless assault since Hezbollah joined the war on February 28, 2026. In the week prior to the request, over 210 rockets and drones targeted the Galilee and Haifa, with thousands of youth spending days in bomb shelters. Yesh Matsav positions itself as the "Resilience Anchor" for schools in conflict zones, turning trauma into growth by training teachers as personal coaches for high-risk youth.

The organization has a track record since October 7th: Nofey Habsor (Gaza envelope) trained 30 teachers for 60 high-risk students; ORT Shlomi (North) supported 25 teachers and 50 students during displacement and return using coaching and Docu-therapy; specialized programs for IDF orphans and widows with the Ministry of Defense; and Bat Yam received immediate crisis support when 70 buildings were struck during the first Iran war in 2025. Yesh Matsav is backed by the President of Israel, the Ministry of Education, Tech for Israel, ORT Israel, and private donors, but is seeking emergency relief to deploy immediately in the North instead of waiting for long-awaited official response efforts.

## Who We Are & What We Do

Yesh Matsav (yeshmatsav.org/en) is a non-profit organization based in Israel, dedicated to supporting at-risk youth impacted by the current war in Israel, helping to rebuild Israel's future.

Board and founders include Dr. Lior Zoref, former Microsoft VP, TED speaker, and best-selling author; Yaniv Rivlin, serial entrepreneur and founding CEO of Bird Israel; Hana David, leading figure in Israeli education; and Yosi Taguri, serial entrepreneur and CEO of ZipRecruiter Israel.

**Programs:**
- Teacher/School Support: Equipping educators with tools and strategies to support high school students in the most challenging situations.
- Inspiring Role Models: Connecting youth with successful individuals who share similar backgrounds.

**Proven Results:** Reached 2,000 youth during 2025; 80% show significant improvement in motivation and achievements.

## Current Partnerships

- The President of Israel (Isaac Herzog) endorses the mission.
- Ministry of Education: Partner in developing methodologies; partial funding for professional training for teachers.
- Tech for Israel: Donation and support (leaders from Israel's financial and high-tech sector; Brothers in Arms nonprofit).
- Private Donors.
- ORT Israel: One of Israel's leading education networks.

## Our Response and Activities Since October 7th

- Nofey Habsor (Gaza Envelope): Trained 30 teachers to provide direct coaching to 60 high-risk students.
- ORT Shlomi (North): Supported 25 teachers and 50 students during displacement and return using advanced coaching and Docu-therapy.
- IDF Orphans and Widows: Specialized resilience programs in partnership with the Ministry of Defense.
- Bat Yam (First Iran War, 2025): Immediate crisis support for educators in a city where 70 buildings were struck.

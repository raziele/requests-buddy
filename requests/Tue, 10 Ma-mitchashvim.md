## The Ask

Mitchashvim is urgently seeking $95,000 to provide refurbished computers to 350 students affected by the current emergency. Due to nationwide remote learning and widespread missile attacks, many students from displaced families lack access to computers, hindering their ability to continue their education.

**Funding Requested:** $95,000

## Context & Background

The ongoing emergency and missile attacks in Israel have led to a transition to nationwide remote learning. This has created a critical situation where thousands of students from displaced families and disadvantaged communities cannot attend school due to a lack of computers. Mitchashvim, a nonprofit dedicated to closing Israel's digital divide, has a mission that becomes even more critical during times of crisis. They have a history of distributing refurbished computers and possess an experienced operational network ready to respond to surges in demand during national emergencies.

In the past 48 hours, Mitchashvim has received urgent requests from various municipalities and educational institutions serving displaced families and vulnerable communities. These include requests from Be'er Sheva, Gush Halav, Haifa, and Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village. The immediate goal is to provide 350 students with computers to help them reconnect to school and regain a sense of stability amidst the chaos.

## The Ask

Al-Baqour is seeking support for its "Migunit LaNefesh" (Shelter for the Soul) program, which aims to provide emotional resilience and support to Bedouin families in the Negev during the "Shaagat HaAri" war. The program will offer online and in-person sessions with professionals, focusing on children's development, parental support, and coping mechanisms for anxiety and uncertainty. They have outlined a budget of 162,000 NIS for these emergency activities.

**Funding Requested:** 162,000 NIS

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Pedagogical Guidance: 36,000 NIS
- Development and accessibility of practical educational content: 90,000 NIS
- Bustle activities (ZUM): 36,000 NIS

**Non-Financial Ask:** Support for the program activities to allow their implementation.

## Context & Background

Al-Baqour, an organization that promotes early childhood education and parental resilience in the Bedouin community in the Negev, is requesting support for its "Migunit LaNefesh" program due to the ongoing "Shaagat HaAri" war. Many Bedouin families in the region live in unreinforced structures and lack adequate protection, making them highly vulnerable to the conflict. Al-Baqour's existing digital platforms have been crucial in maintaining connection and providing resources during emergencies.

This program aims to supplement their digital efforts with specialized content focused on emotional resilience, including online and in-person sessions with psychologists and parental guidance experts. The focus is on supporting parents and children in coping with anxiety, fear, and uncertainty, and on maintaining routines. The requested funding of 162,000 NIS will cover these new initiatives, which are not part of the organization's annual budget. The program will also extend its reach to Arabic-speaking communities in the center and north of the country.

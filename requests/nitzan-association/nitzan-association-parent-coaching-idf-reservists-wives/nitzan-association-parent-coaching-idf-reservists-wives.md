## The Ask

Nitzan Association is requesting support for a Parent Coaching program for 100 IDF reservists' wives, who have effectively become de facto single mothers during their partners' prolonged reserve service in Israel's ongoing war. These women are carrying the full burden of parenting and household management, while managing intense anxiety for their partners' safety and trying to shield their children from the harsh realities of war.

The program will provide these mothers with a structured, short-term process to strengthen their resilience and parental authority using Nitzan's evidence-based Emotional Cognitive Coaching (E.C.C.) method. Each participant will receive 12 weekly individual coaching sessions (via Zoom), delivered at no cost to the mothers, to strengthen parental authority, improve family dynamics, and support children's wellbeing.

**Funding Requested:** NIS 600,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Coaching sessions: 1,200 coaching hours at NIS 500/hour — **Total: NIS 600,000**
- Per participant: 12 sessions per mother — **NIS 6,000 per mother** (for 100 mothers)

Nitzan frames this as an opportunity to support "one of the solid pillars on which the entire Israeli society rests" — the wives of reservists who suddenly found themselves functioning almost as single mothers so their partners could focus on defending the State of Israel and the Jewish people. The organization emphasizes that these women must not be left to face this burden alone and is seeking funding or partnership to deliver a broad, comprehensive, and supportive envelope around them through this coaching program.

## Context & Background

### About Nitzan

Nitzan is a parents and volunteers' association founded in 1964 to identify, diagnose, treat, and rehabilitate children, adolescents, and adults with learning disabilities, adaptation challenges, and functional difficulties. The organization enables them to realize their personal, academic, and social potential and to successfully integrate into the education system, the community, and the workforce.

Nitzan's work is research-driven, with educational and emotional models that combine professional assessment, tailored intervention, and continuous support for the child and their environment. The organization accompanies its target audiences from early childhood through integration into the labor market.

Nitzan operates 40 branches throughout Israel and provides services to approximately 50,000 individuals each year. Over the years, more than one million Israelis have benefited from the organization's services.

### The Need

Israel is in the longest war in its history. After more than two years of war, wives of IDF reservists are emotionally exhausted and nearly depleted of their resilience. These women have been functioning as de facto single mothers throughout this period, carrying immense worry and anxiety for the safety of their loved ones while simultaneously trying to shield their children from the harsh realities of war.

The result has been erosion of parental authority and instability within the family unit. Nitzan views reservists' wives as a core pillar of Israeli society in wartime; by enabling their partners to serve in the reserves, they carry an enormous and ongoing burden at home.

Nitzan seeks to offer them one of its flagship programs, Parent Coaching, which is already being implemented successfully for parents of children in Kiryat Shmona. The goal is to provide these mothers with a structured process to strengthen resilience, restore and reinforce parental authority, and stabilize family dynamics so that children experience greater emotional security and improved functioning.

### The Response: Parent Coaching Using the E.C.C. Method

The proposed intervention is a Parent Coaching process using the E.C.C. (Emotional Cognitive Coaching) method, developed by Dr. Maly Danino, CEO of Nitzan. E.C.C. is a short-term intervention approach focused on strengthening the parent.

Key features of the method:

- Offers parents an opportunity for self-reflection, helping them recognize strengths and weaknesses and understand hindering behavioral patterns.
- Emphasizes self-awareness and reframing the problem as key forces for generating deep behavioral change.
- Aims to influence participants' relationships with those around them in general, and with their children in particular.

The process includes three main stages:

1. **Emotional Exploration** — Parents explore their emotional responses and underlying feelings related to parenting and family challenges.
2. **Insight Development** — Through guided reflection, parents gain insight into patterns, triggers, and alternative perspectives.
3. **Action** — Insights are refined and translated into practical, concrete steps for change in everyday family life.

The transition between stages is flexible and tailored to the individual needs and abilities of each parent. Throughout the process, the parent's emotional responses are continuously monitored, and new skills and approaches are practiced.

### Evidence of Impact

Research cited in the proposal indicates that mothers who participated in the process improved their ability to regulate their children's negative emotions. Additional improvements were observed in the overall family climate, including:

- Better academic functioning in children
- Improved social and emotional functioning
- Reduction in aggression and social withdrawal (reclusion)

These findings support the use of the E.C.C. Parent Coaching model as a high-impact, short-term intervention with meaningful benefits for both parents and children.

### Program Design for IDF Reservists' Wives

Against this background, Nitzan seeks funding to offer the Parent Coaching program free of charge to 100 IDF reservists' wives.

Program outline:

- **Participants:** 100 wives of IDF reservists across Israel
- **Format:** Individual Parent Coaching sessions delivered online (Zoom)
- **Frequency & Duration:** 12 weekly sessions per participant
- **Facilitators:** Nitzan's experienced professional staff, trained in the E.C.C. method
- **Goals:**
  - Strengthen parental authority and confidence
  - Improve family dynamics and relationships
  - Reinforce children's sense of capability, confidence, and emotional security
  - Reduce parental distress and emotional depletion resulting from prolonged war conditions

Nitzan emphasizes that this program represents a comprehensive supportive envelope for these mothers, enabling them to better cope with ongoing wartime stress and to provide stable, nurturing environments for their children.

### Budget and Requested Support

The proposal invites philanthropic support for the full project cost, structured as follows:

- **Cost per coaching hour:** NIS 500 (online private sessions via Zoom)
- **Total coaching hours:** 1,200
- **Cost per participant (12 sessions):** NIS 6,000
- **Total project cost (100 mothers):** NIS 600,000

Nitzan positions this initiative as a strategic investment in the resilience of families who are directly sustaining Israel's wartime readiness through prolonged reserve service. The organization appeals to funders not to leave these women and their families to carry this burden alone and expresses gratitude for any level of assistance toward the initiative.

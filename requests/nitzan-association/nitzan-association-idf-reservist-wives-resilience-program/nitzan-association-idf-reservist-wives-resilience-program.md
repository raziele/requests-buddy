## The Ask

Nitzan Association is requesting support for a project that will provide wives of IDF reservists with a structured process to strengthen their resilience and parental authority. During Israel's prolonged war, these women have effectively become single parents while their partners serve in the reserves, carrying a heavy burden with little support. Nitzan wishes to offer them a broad, comprehensive supportive envelope and has prepared a formal request for support; they are seeking funding or partnership to deliver this program.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

## Context & Background

Israel is in the longest war in its history. The wives of reserve forces are described in the email as "one of the solid pillars on which the entire Israeli society rests." These women suddenly became almost single mothers while allowing their partners to focus on defending the State of Israel; they carried—and still carry—an enormous burden. The sender states that they must not be abandoned alone.

The Nitzan Association (Nitzan Center) wishes to provide these mothers with a process to strengthen their resilience and parental authority. They have prepared a request for support for a project for the benefit of reservists' wives and are seeking the recipient's help to provide them with a broad, comprehensive and supportive envelope.

The email is a forward from Yoav Raban (Jewish Federation of Detroit) of a reply from Shifra Stroosky, Deputy Director General for Management and Education at Nitzan. The original message references an attached request document, which would contain detailed project and budget information; that attachment was not present in the files provided.

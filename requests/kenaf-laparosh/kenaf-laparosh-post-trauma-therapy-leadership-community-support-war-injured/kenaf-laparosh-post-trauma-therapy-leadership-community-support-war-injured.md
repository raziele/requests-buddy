## The Ask

כנף לפרוש (Kenaf LaParosh) is requesting support for programs that provide post-trauma therapy, leadership development, and community support to war-injured individuals and their families in Israel. The attached document outlines the organization’s current participants and the expansion goals for 2026–2028, including programs for injured people (“מפרש לבן”), community support for spouses/partners of injured women (“לצידך”), and additional “שורשים וכנפיים” programming across creative modalities.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Support for scaling and delivering the organization’s war-injured support/rehabilitation programs (including therapy/leadership and spouse community empowerment).

## Context & Background

Since October 7, the number of war-injured persons has grown by more than +22,000, including large cohorts experiencing post-trauma (“פוסט עם טראומה”) and psychological harm. The rehabilitation system receives about 1,500 inquiries per month for recognition, and the brochure notes rehabilitation-sector data from December 2025, indicating ongoing demand for structured support for people with moderate-to-severe injuries (including psychological and physical wounds) and their families.

Kenaf LaParosh presents three program streams: (1) “מפרש לבן” leadership/repair programming for injured individuals (described as part of therapeutic/rehabilitation leadership programming), (2) “לצידך” (“To Your Side”), a supportive community and empowerment program for wives/spouses of injured people, where participants report impacts on employment and day-to-day life and stressors related to parenting and home management, and (3) “שורשים וכנפיים”, a platform that uses cultural/creative creation as a vehicle for resilience and personal growth for PTSD-affected individuals, with planned expansion into diverse creative fields.

The document emphasizes that the need is continuing and that the organization is seeking backing to expand content and support interventions, increase participant numbers, and develop additional initiatives through 2026–2028. It includes quantitative targets and intended scale, including references to planned increases in participation and broader reach to injured populations recognized for rehabilitation.

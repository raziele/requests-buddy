## The Ask

Access Israel requests an immediate emergency grant of $400,000 for 10 days of critical operations following the outbreak of war on 28 February 2026. Funds will support a 24/7 accessible emergency hotline, Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול) volunteer training and deployment, wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles, accessible and protected temporary housing for evacuees, and life-saving information campaigns so that people with disabilities and the elderly receive equitable access to shelter and evacuation.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (for 10 days of activity)

**Funding Breakdown:**
- 24/7 accessible emergency hotline – staffing, Purple Vest app, communications, logistics: Not itemized
- Purple Vest training, refreshments, accessible evacuation equipment, operational coordination: Not itemized
- Wheelchair-accessible vehicles with lifts – drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance: Not itemized
- Accessible and protected alternative accommodation for evacuees: Not itemized
- Life-saving information campaigns and accessibility of vital information nationwide: Not itemized

## Context & Background

Since 7 October, Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian aid for the most vulnerable populations. With donor support they have provided accessible evacuation, accessible and protected alternative housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal and logistical accompaniment in crisis situations—often where neither the state nor local authorities provided adequate response. People who need the most assistance in war are often the last to receive institutional support. With the new round of fighting that began on the morning of 28 February 2026, there is a renewed, significant, and urgent need to ensure accessible, professional, and sensitive support for people with disabilities and the elderly. The organization seeks immediate partnership to ensure no one is left behind and that everyone receives the assistance and protection they are entitled to in an emergency.

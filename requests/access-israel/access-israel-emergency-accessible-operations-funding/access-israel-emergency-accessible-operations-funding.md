## The Ask

Access Israel is requesting an immediate $400,000 in initial funding for ten days of critical operations following the escalation of hostilities with Iran on February 28, 2026. The organization provides accessible humanitarian assistance to some of the most vulnerable populations in emergencies. The funds would support a 24/7 accessible emergency hotline and operations center, refreshment and deployment of the 'Purple Vest' volunteer response system, wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles, accessible and protected accommodation for evacuees, and life-saving information campaigns. Without accessible shelter options, many people with disabilities and elderly citizens remain in place during sirens in fear; Access Israel aims to ensure no one is left behind.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- 24/7 accessible emergency operations center (hotline, Purple Vest app, communications, logistics): Part of $400,000
- Purple Vest training, refreshment, and deployment (accessible evacuation equipment, coordination): Part of $400,000
- Wheelchair-accessible vehicles with lifts (drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance): Part of $400,000
- Accessible and protected accommodation for evacuee families: Part of $400,000
- Digital and media campaigns for preparedness and real-time life-saving information: Part of $400,000

## Context & Background

Since October 7, Access Israel has been on the front line of humanitarian assistance for the most vulnerable populations. With donor support they have assisted thousands with accessible evacuation, accessible alternative and protected housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal and logistical accompaniment in crisis situations—often where no other state or local body provided adequate response. With the renewed escalation on February 28, 2026, they face a significant and urgent need to ensure accessible, professional, and sensitive assistance for people with disabilities and the elderly. The letter states that 40% of these populations live without accessible shelter and have no real alternative when sirens sound; sometimes all they can do is stay in place in anxiety and fear. The requested $400,000 is for an initial ten-day period, with follow-on needs to be assessed jointly as the situation develops.

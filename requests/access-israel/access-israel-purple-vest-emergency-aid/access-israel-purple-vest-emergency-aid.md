## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **initial immediate emergency grant of $400,000 for 10 days of activity** following the escalation/renewed war on **28 February 2026** (described as the current round of fighting with Iran). The request is to scale and sustain Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול) emergency operations so people with disabilities and elderly citizens receive accessible evacuation, shelter, information, and support.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial 10-day period)  
**Follow-on Funding:** To be jointly assessed based on developments and duration of fighting.  
**Non-Financial Ask:** Urgent partnership/collaboration; organization available for clarifications.  
**Time Sensitivity Noted:** One forwarding note frames expected response by the next day.

### Funding Breakdown (all sources aligned; line-item amounts not provided)

1. **24/7 accessible emergency hotline/situation room**  
   Staffing, disability-inclusive hotline operations, Purple Vest app development/maintenance, communications systems, logistics, real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries.

2. **Purple Vest training and activation**  
   Refresher trainings for volunteers in municipalities/authorities, accessible evacuation equipment, operational management and coordination, rapid field response (including missile-strike incidents in buildings/neighborhoods).

3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts**  
   Ongoing operations including drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance; evacuation from unprotected/unmasked areas to medical care and accessible sheltered housing.

4. **Accessible and protected alternative accommodation for evacuees**  
   Hotel stays and other accessible fortified/protected housing solutions for families forced to evacuate inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.

5. **Life-saving information dissemination**  
   Digital and communications campaigns, emergency preparedness and assistance content, website maintenance, and real-time accessible vital information nationwide.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel reports front-line humanitarian support for vulnerable populations, including accessible evacuation, accessible protected/fortified alternative housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transportation, and personal/logistical accompaniment in acute distress cases—often where state or local authority response was insufficient.

With renewed hostilities beginning the morning of **28 February 2026**, the organization describes a renewed, significant, and urgent need for accessible, professional, and sensitive response for people with disabilities and elderly persons, emphasizing that those who need the most help in wartime are often the last to receive institutional support.

The appeal repeatedly emphasizes:
- Donor/partner support is the operational backbone enabling rapid life-saving response.
- Every contribution is framed as directly translating into protection and security for the most vulnerable population.
- The goal is to ensure no one is left behind.

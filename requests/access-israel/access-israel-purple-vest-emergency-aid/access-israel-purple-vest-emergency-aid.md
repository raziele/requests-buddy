## The Ask

Access Israel requests an immediate contribution/grant of **$400,000** to fund the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול) emergency response** for an **initial 10-day period** following the escalation of hostilities on **28 February 2026**. The request is framed as partnership-focused and time-sensitive; continued/follow-on needs are to be assessed jointly based on developments and the duration of fighting. One letter explicitly emphasizes expectation of a response **the next day**.

**Funding Requested:** **$400,000** (for 10 days of activity)

**Non-Financial / Partnership Ask:** Immediate partnership and urgent collaboration; Access Israel is available for clarifications (in addition to the funding request).

### Funding Breakdown (critical operations)

1. **24/7 Accessible Emergency Hotline / Situation Room**
   - Accessible disability-inclusive inquiry hotline
   - Professional staffing
   - **Purple Vest app development and maintenance**
   - Communications and operational/logistical support
   - Real-time response capacity for **thousands of expected inquiries** in the coming days

2. **Purple Vest Training, Refreshers, and Volunteer Activation/Deployment**
   - Refresher training for volunteers in authorities/municipalities
   - Refreshments for volunteers (where applicable)
   - Deployment/activation of the Purple Vest volunteer network
   - Coordination and operational management for rapid accessible field response
   - Accessible evacuation equipment readiness for missile-strike events (buildings/neighborhoods)

3. **Wheelchair-Accessible Evacuation Vehicles (Lifts)**
   - Ongoing operation of accessible evacuation vehicles
   - Drivers
   - Fuel
   - Insurance
   - Maintenance
   - Safe transfer/extraction from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible protected/alternative housing

4. **Accessible and Fortified/Sheltered Alternative Accommodation for Evacuees**
   - Immediate funding to cover stays in **hotels and accessible protected housing solutions**
   - Support for families forced to evacuate due to homes that are inaccessible or damaged
   - Required adaptations and logistical accompaniment/support

5. **Life-Saving Information Dissemination (Accessible Preparedness & Emergency Content)**
   - Digital and communications campaigns
   - Preparedness and emergency assistance content
   - Website maintenance
   - Making vital information accessible to people with disabilities nationwide in real time

## Context & Background

Access Israel states that since **7 October**, it has been at the forefront of humanitarian assistance for the most vulnerable populations, particularly people with disabilities and elderly citizens. With donor support, it has helped with:
- Accessible evacuation
- Accessible alternative sheltered/fortified housing
- Medical equipment supply
- Dedicated accessible transport
- Personal and logistical accompaniment in acute distress

The organization emphasizes that people who need the most assistance in wartime are often the last to receive adequate institutional support—especially where neither the state nor local authorities provide sufficient response.

As of the start of the renewed hostilities on the morning of **28 February 2026**, Access Israel reports a renewed, significant, and urgent need to ensure accessible, professional, and sensitive assistance for people with disabilities and elderly—grounded in the reality that approximately **40%** lack accessible shelter, and that during sirens many have no effective alternative and remain where they are in fear.

Access Israel positions the requested **$400,000 for 10 days** as immediate operational capacity for the critical Purple Vest response described above, with follow-on needs to be assessed jointly according to the situation and the duration of the conflict.

## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **immediate initial grant/donation of $400,000** to fund **critical Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול) emergency operations for ~10 days** following the **escalation/outbreak of hostilities on the morning of 28 February 2026** (sources describe this as a renewed round of fighting, including framing as **war with Iran**). The funding is intended to scale immediate lifesaving support so people with disabilities and elderly citizens receive **equitable** access to **evacuation, shelter/protection, information, and field assistance**.

**Non-financial ask (where noted):** partnership / urgent collaboration; organization available for clarifications.

**Urgency nuance across sources:** one intake record notes the appeal was framed as **time-sensitive**, with an expectation of a **response the next day** (as summarized from the forwarding context).

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (10 days of activity)

**Funding uses (combined wording across sources; amounts not itemized in the materials):**
- **24/7 accessible emergency operations center / hotline** — disability-inclusive hotline, professional staffing, **Purple Vest app** development/maintenance, communications, logistics, and **real-time** response to **thousands** of expected inquiries.
- **Purple Vest volunteer readiness + field response** — training / **refresher training**, refreshments where mentioned, **accessible evacuation equipment**, operational management/coordination, rapid accessible assistance after **missile strikes** affecting **buildings/neighborhoods** (including volunteer deployment via municipalities/authorities).
- **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles (lifts)** — drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance; safe evacuation/transfer from **unprotected/non-fortified** areas to medical care and accessible protected/sheltered housing.
- **Accessible protected / fortified / sheltered alternative accommodation** — hotels and other accessible protected solutions; adaptations and logistical accompaniment for families evacuating inaccessible or damaged homes.
- **Life-saving information campaigns + accessibility of vital information** — digital/communications campaigns, preparedness and emergency-assistance content, website maintenance, nationwide real-time accessible information.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel describes being at the forefront of humanitarian aid for highly vulnerable populations—providing accessible evacuation, accessible **protected/fortified/sheltered** alternative housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal/logistical accompaniment in acute distress—often in situations where **state and/or local authorities** did not provide adequate response. Sources repeat the theme that people who need the most help in wartime are often **last** to receive institutional support.

With fighting beginning **28 February 2026**, the organization describes a **renewed, significant, and urgent** need for **accessible, professional, and sensitive** support tailored to disabilities and aging-related needs. The **$400,000** is presented as an **initial immediate** tranche for **ten days**, with **further needs to be assessed jointly** depending on developments and duration of the conflict. The Hebrew letter emphasizes donor support as the **mainstay** enabling rapid action, and that contributions translate into **protection and security** for the most vulnerable, with a call to mobilize resources to **expand Purple Vest** activity.

4. **Accessible and protected alternative accommodation for evacuees** — immediate funding for stays in hotels and accessible protected solutions for families with people with disabilities forced to evacuate from inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.

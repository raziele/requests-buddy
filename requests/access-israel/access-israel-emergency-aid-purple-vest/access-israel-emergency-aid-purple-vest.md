## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **initial immediate emergency grant of $400,000** for **10 days of activity** following the escalation/renewed fighting that began on **28 February 2026** (described in the source letters as the current round of fighting with Iran).

The funding is to urgently scale and operate the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response so people with disabilities and elderly citizens are not left behind and can access evacuation, shelter, and critical information in real time.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial tranche for ten days; follow-on needs to be jointly assessed based on conflict developments and duration)

**Non-Financial Ask:** Urgent partnership/collaboration; organization available for clarifications.

## Funding Priorities / Critical Operations

1. **24/7 accessible emergency hotline / situation room**  
   Accessible inquiry center for all disability types; professional staffing; Purple Vest app development and maintenance; communications systems; logistics; real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries.

2. **Purple Vest volunteer refreshers, training, and deployment**  
   Refresher trainings in municipalities/authorities; accessible evacuation equipment; operational management and coordination; rapid accessible response in field incidents (including missile-strike events in buildings/neighborhoods).

3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles (with lifts)**  
   Ongoing operation of accessible vehicles including drivers, fuel, insurance, and maintenance; extraction and safe transfer from unprotected/unshielded areas to medical care and accessible sheltered housing.

4. **Accessible and protected alternative accommodation for evacuees**  
   Immediate funding for hotels and other accessible fortified/sheltered housing solutions for families forced to evacuate inaccessible or damaged homes; includes required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.

5. **Life-saving information dissemination and accessibility**  
   Digital and communications campaigns; preparedness and emergency assistance content; website maintenance; nationwide real-time accessibility of vital information for people with disabilities.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel reports it has been at the forefront of humanitarian assistance for vulnerable populations, often filling gaps where state or local authorities did not provide sufficient response. With donor support, it has assisted thousands through:

- Accessible evacuation
- Accessible alternative protected housing
- Medical equipment support
- Dedicated accessible transportation
- Personal and logistical accompaniment in acute distress

With the renewed hostilities on **28 February 2026**, the organization describes a renewed, significant, and urgent need for accessible, professional, and sensitive emergency support. Across all versions of the request, Access Israel emphasizes that those who need the most assistance in wartime are often the last to receive institutional support, and that donor support is the core enabler of rapid life-saving response.

## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **initial immediate emergency grant/donation of $400,000 for 10 days of activity** following renewed fighting on **28 February 2026** (described in multiple copies as escalation/war, including references to fighting with Iran). The funding is intended to rapidly scale the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response for people with disabilities and elderly citizens.

The organization also requests **urgent partnership and collaboration**, notes it is available for clarifications, and indicates follow-on needs should be assessed jointly based on developments and the duration of hostilities. One forwarded version notes an expectation of next-day response due to urgency.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial, immediate; ten-day operational window)

## Funding Priorities (Critical Activities)

1. **24/7 Accessible Emergency Hotline / Situation Room (חמ"ל חירום נגיש)**  
   Professional staffing, accessible inquiry handling for all disability types, Purple Vest app development/maintenance, communications systems, logistics, and real-time response to expected high inquiry volume.

2. **Purple Vest Training, Refreshers, and Deployment**  
   Refresher training for municipal volunteers, accessible evacuation equipment, and operational coordination/management for rapid field response, including missile-strike incidents in buildings and neighborhoods.

3. **Wheelchair-Accessible Evacuation Vehicles with Lifts**  
   Ongoing operation costs including drivers, fuel, insurance, and maintenance for safe evacuation from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible sheltered housing.

4. **Accessible and Protected/Fortified Alternative Accommodation for Evacuees**  
   Immediate coverage for hotels and accessible protected housing solutions for families forced to evacuate inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.

5. **Life-Saving Information Dissemination**  
   Digital and media campaigns, preparedness and emergency-assistance content, website maintenance, and real-time nationwide accessibility of vital information for people with disabilities.

## Context & Background

- Since **7 October**, Access Israel reports frontline humanitarian support for vulnerable populations, including accessible evacuation, protected accessible housing, medical equipment supply, dedicated accessible transport, and personal/logistical accompaniment.
- Multiple versions emphasize service gaps where state and local authorities did not provide sufficient response, and that those most in need are often the last to receive institutional support.
- With the renewed hostilities beginning the morning of **28 February 2026**, the organization describes a renewed urgent need for accessible, professional, and sensitive assistance.
- The request frames donor/partner support as the operational backbone enabling immediate life-saving action and states each contribution directly improves protection and security for the most vulnerable.

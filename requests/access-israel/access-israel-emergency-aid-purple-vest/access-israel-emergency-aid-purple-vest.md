## The Ask

Access Israel requests **immediate emergency funding of $400,000** for an **initial 10-day period** following the escalation of hostilities/war beginning the morning of **28 February 2026** (described as the current round of fighting with Iran). The funding is to expand and operate the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response so people with disabilities and elderly citizens can access evacuation, shelter, and life-saving support in real time.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial immediate amount for ten days; continuation needs to be jointly assessed based on developments and duration of fighting)

**Non-Financial Ask:** Urgent partnership/collaboration; organization available for clarifications.

### Critical Activities Requiring Immediate Funding

1. **24/7 accessible emergency hotline / situation room (חמ"ל חירום נגיש 24/7)**  
   Professional staffing, disability-inclusive hotline operations, Purple Vest app development/maintenance, communications systems, logistics, and real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries.

2. **Purple Vest training/refresher and field deployment**  
   Training and refreshers for volunteers in authorities/municipalities, accessible evacuation equipment, and operational management/coordination for rapid field response (including missile-strike events in buildings/neighborhoods).

3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts**  
   Ongoing operations including drivers, fuel, insurance, and maintenance for safe extraction/transfer from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible protected housing.

4. **Accessible and protected/fortified alternative accommodation for evacuees**  
   Immediate funding for hotel stays and other accessible protected housing solutions for families with people with disabilities evacuated from inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.

5. **Life-saving information publishing and dissemination**  
   Digital and communications campaigns, preparedness/emergency-assistance content, website maintenance, and real-time accessible distribution of vital information nationwide.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel reports it has been at the forefront of humanitarian assistance for highly vulnerable populations, providing accessible evacuation, accessible protected/fortified housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transportation, and personal/logistical support in acute distress—often when neither state nor local authorities provided adequate response.

With renewed hostilities on **28 February 2026**, the organization states there is a renewed, significant, and urgent need for accessible, professional, and sensitive support. It emphasizes that those most in need during wartime are often last to receive institutional assistance, and that donor support is the operational backbone enabling rapid life-saving response. The appeal stresses: **“do not leave anyone behind.”**

## The Ask

Access Israel is seeking **urgent partnership** and an **initial, immediate grant of $400,000 for 10 days of critical operations** following the escalation of hostilities associated with the **current round of fighting with Iran**, beginning the morning of **28 February 2026**. The funding is intended to scale the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response so people with disabilities and elderly citizens receive **equitable access to evacuation, sheltered/protected accommodation, real-time assistance, and accessible emergency information**, with **further needs to be assessed jointly** based on developments and duration of fighting.

**Funding requested:** **$400,000** (10 days of activity)

**Non-financial ask (stated in at least one intake):** partnership/urgent collaboration; organization available for clarifications.

**Funding uses (combined naming from intakes; amounts not itemized in the materials):**

1. **24/7 accessible emergency operations / situation room + disability-inclusive hotline** — staffing/professional personnel; **Purple Vest app** development and maintenance; communications systems; logistical operations; real-time response to **thousands of expected inquiries** in the coming days.
2. **Purple Vest training, refreshers, and field deployment** — volunteers in municipalities; refreshments/training support where referenced; **accessible evacuation equipment**; operational management/coordination for rapid accessible assistance after missile impacts in buildings/neighborhoods.
3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles (with lifts)** — drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance; safe evacuation/transfer from **unprotected** areas to medical care and accessible protected/sheltered housing.
4. **Accessible protected/sheltered alternative accommodation** — hotels and accessible protected housing solutions for families with people with disabilities evacuating inaccessible or damaged homes; required adaptations and logistical accompaniment/support.
5. **Life-saving information campaigns + accessibility of vital information** — digital and communications campaigns; preparedness and emergency-assistance content; website maintenance; real-time accessibility of critical information nationwide.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel describes being at the forefront of humanitarian aid for highly vulnerable populations—providing **accessible evacuation**, **accessible protected/sheltered alternative housing**, **medical equipment**, **dedicated accessible transport**, and **personal/logistical accompaniment** in acute distress—often in situations where **neither the state nor local authorities** provided adequate response. The organization emphasizes a recurring pattern: **those who need help most in wartime are often last to receive institutional support**.

With the renewed hostilities beginning **28 February 2026**, Access Israel reports a **renewed, significant, and urgent** need for **accessible, professional, and sensitive** support tailored to people with disabilities and the elderly—including addressing shelter accessibility gaps affecting a large share of this population.

**Nuance across intakes (not contradictory, different emphasis):**

- Some summaries highlight **“fortified”** accommodation wording; others emphasize **protected/sheltered** housing—both map to the letter’s **“ממוגן”** framing.
- One intake explicitly mentions an expectation of **next-day responsiveness** from partners.
- One English-forward summary uses **“unmasked areas”** for vehicle evacuations; parallel intakes use **“unprotected”**—**“unprotected”** is the intended meaning.

## The Ask

Access Israel requests **immediate emergency funding of $400,000** for an **initial 10-day** response period following the escalation/war beginning **28 February 2026** (described in some versions as the current round of fighting with Iran). The funds are to sustain and expand the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response so people with disabilities and elderly citizens can access evacuation, shelter, and life-saving support.

The organization also requests **urgent partnership/collaboration** and indicates availability for clarifications. One forwarding note frames the request as requiring a response by the next day.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial, immediate, for 10 days; continuation needs to be jointly reassessed based on conflict developments and duration)

## Critical Operations Requiring Immediate Funding

1. **24/7 Accessible Emergency Situation Room / Hotline**  
   Staffing and operation of a disability-inclusive emergency hotline, communications systems, logistics, Purple Vest app development/maintenance, and real-time handling of thousands of expected inquiries.

2. **Purple Vest Training, Refreshers, and Field Deployment**  
   Refresher trainings for volunteers (including municipal teams), accessible evacuation equipment, and operational management/coordination for rapid response, including missile-strike incidents in buildings and neighborhoods.

3. **Wheelchair-Accessible Evacuation Vehicles (with lifts)**  
   Ongoing operation including drivers, fuel, insurance, and maintenance for evacuation from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible protected housing.

4. **Accessible and Protected/Fortified Alternative Accommodation**  
   Immediate financing for hotels and other accessible protected solutions for families evacuating inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.

5. **Life-Saving Information Dissemination**  
   Digital and communications campaigns, preparedness/emergency content production, website maintenance, and nationwide real-time accessibility of vital information for people with disabilities.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel reports being on the front line of humanitarian aid for vulnerable populations, providing accessible evacuation, alternative protected housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal/logistical support in acute distress cases, including where state or local responses were insufficient.

With renewed hostilities on **28 February 2026**, the organization describes a renewed and urgent need for accessible, professional, and sensitive services tailored to people with disabilities and elderly populations. Across versions, a consistent core claim appears: those needing the most support in wartime are often the last to receive institutional response, and donor support is the operational backbone enabling rapid life-saving action.

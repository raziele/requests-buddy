## The Ask

Access Israel requests immediate emergency support in the form of a **$400,000** grant/donation for **ten (10) days** of critical operations following the escalation of hostilities/war beginning **28 February 2026** (with references to a current round of fighting with Iran). The funding is intended to scale and operate the organization’s **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response and related accessibility operations so that people with disabilities and elderly citizens receive equitable access to evacuation, shelter, and life-saving information during the current round of fighting.

### Funding Requested
- **$400,000** for **10 days** of activity / for an initial immediate sum for **ten days**

### Funding Breakdown (as described; sub-line items not itemized)
- **24/7 accessible emergency hotline / emergency situation room (disability-inclusive)** (staffing, Purple Vest app support, communications, logistics) — *Not itemized* / *None stated*
- **Purple Vest refresher training and activation / volunteer array deployment** (volunteers, accessible evacuation equipment, operational management & coordination in municipalities) — *Not itemized* / *None stated*
- **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts / vehicles with ramps/marrow (mעלון לכיסאות גלגלים)** (drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance) — *Not itemized* / *None stated*
- **Accessible and fortified alternative accommodation for evacuees** (hotels and accessible protected solutions; required adaptations and logistical accompaniment) — *Not itemized* / *None stated*
- **Publishing and disseminating life-saving information** (digital/communications campaigns, preparedness and emergency-assistance content, website maintenance, accessible nationwide information in real time) — *Not itemized* / *None stated*

### Additional (non-financial) Ask
- Urgent partnership and collaboration; the organization is available for clarifications.

### Timing / Expectations (as stated in sources)
- Framed as time-sensitive and critical, with an expectation of next-day response in at least one forwarded request context.
- Continuation beyond the initial 10-day period is to be **assessed jointly with partners** based on developments and the duration of fighting.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian aid for the most vulnerable populations. With donor support, it has assisted thousands with:
- accessible evacuation,
- accessible alternative and fortified/sheltered housing,
- medical equipment,
- dedicated accessible transport,
- personal and logistical support in acute distress,
often in cases where neither the state nor local authorities provided an adequate response. The most vulnerable are described as frequently being the last to receive institutional support in wartime.

As of the morning of **28 February 2026**, with the start/escalation of renewed hostilities (references include a current round of fighting with Iran), Access Israel states there is renewed, significant, and urgent need to ensure **accessible, professional, and sensitive support** for people with disabilities and elderly citizens—especially because approximately **40%** live without accessible shelter. When rocket sirens sound, many have no real alternative and may remain where they are in anxiety and fear.

The request emphasizes that donor support is the mainstay of their ability to act quickly and save lives, and that each contribution directly translates into protection and security for the most vulnerable population.

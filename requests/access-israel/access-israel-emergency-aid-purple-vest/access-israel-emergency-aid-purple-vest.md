## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **immediate emergency grant / donation of $400,000 for ten days of activity** to scale **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** operations after hostilities escalated on **the morning of 28 February 2026** (described across sources as a renewed round of fighting, including framing as war with Iran). Funds are for **critical, time-sensitive** operations so people with disabilities and elderly citizens get equitable access to evacuation, shelter, and information.

**Funding requested:** $400,000 (10 days). **Follow-on:** Further needs to be assessed jointly with partners based on developments and duration of fighting.

**Non-financial ask (one intake):** Partnership and urgent collaboration; organization available for clarifications.

**Urgency note (one intake):** Framed as expecting a response the **next day**.

### Use of funds (combined line items; amounts not itemized in materials)

1. **24/7 accessible emergency operations** — disability-inclusive hotline / accessible emergency situation room; professional staffing; **Purple Vest app** development and maintenance; communications systems; logistics; real-time response to **thousands of expected inquiries** in the coming days.
2. **Purple Vest training, refreshers, and field activation** — volunteers in municipalities; refreshments where noted; **accessible evacuation equipment**; operational management and coordination for rapid accessible assistance after missile strikes on buildings and neighborhoods.
3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles (lifts)** — drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance; safe evacuation / transfer from **unprotected** areas to medical care and accessible sheltered housing. *(One extracted summary used the wording “unmasked areas,” likely a transcription error for unprotected/unmasked shelter context.)*
4. **Accessible sheltered / protected / fortified alternative accommodation** — hotels and other accessible protected solutions; adaptations; logistical accompaniment for families evacuating inaccessible or damaged homes.
5. **Life-saving information** — digital and communications campaigns; preparedness and emergency-assistance content; website maintenance; real-time accessibility of vital information nationwide.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel describes being at the forefront of humanitarian aid for the most vulnerable: accessible evacuation; accessible **protected / fortified / sheltered** alternative housing; medical equipment; dedicated accessible transport; and personal and logistical support in acute distress—often where **neither the state nor local authorities** provided adequate response. The organization stresses that those who need help most in war are often **last** to receive institutional support.

With fighting resuming **28 February 2026**, Access Israel cites a **renewed, significant, and urgent** need for accessible, professional, and sensitive support. The letter emphasizes that donor support is the **mainstay** of rapid action and that, in the current escalation, contributions translate into **protection and security** for the most vulnerable; it calls for immediate resource mobilization to **expand Purple Vest** and sustain the required response.

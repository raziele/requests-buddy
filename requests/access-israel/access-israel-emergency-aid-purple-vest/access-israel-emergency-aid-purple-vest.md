## The Ask

Access Israel requests **urgent partnership** and an **initial immediate grant of $400,000 for ten days of activity** to scale **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency operations after renewed hostilities beginning **the morning of 28 February 2026** (described across sources as escalation / a new round of fighting, including explicit framing as fighting with Iran in the Hebrew letter metadata).

Funds are intended to support:

- A **24/7 accessible emergency response center / hotline** (sometimes summarized as a “situation room”), including professional staffing, **Purple Vest app** development/maintenance, communications, logistics, and **real-time** response to **thousands of expected inquiries**
- **Purple Vest refresher training + activation** in municipalities: volunteers, refreshments/training logistics as applicable, **accessible evacuation equipment**, and operational coordination for rapid field assistance (including missile strikes affecting buildings/neighborhoods)
- **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts**: drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance; safe movement from **unprotected / inaccessible** areas to medical care and **accessible sheltered / protected / fortified** housing
- **Accessible protected alternative accommodation** for evacuees (e.g., hotels and other accessible solutions), including adaptations and logistical accompaniment for families forced out of inaccessible or damaged homes
- **Life-saving information campaigns** and nationwide accessibility of vital emergency/preparedness information (digital/communications campaigns, website maintenance, accessible publishing)

**Follow-on funding:** multiple ingests note further needs will be **assessed jointly with partners** depending on developments and duration of fighting.

**Non-financial ask (where captured):** partnership/urgent collaboration; organization available for clarifications.

**Timing emphasis (where captured):** one ingest characterizes the outreach as expecting a **response the next day** (as forwarded/routed).

**Funding Requested:** **$400,000** (10 days)

**Funding Breakdown (line-item amounts):** not itemized in the sources (multiple ingests explicitly mark amounts as not provided / “None” / “Not itemized”).

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel describes itself as being at the forefront of humanitarian aid for the most vulnerable, assisting **thousands** with accessible evacuation, accessible **protected/fortified** alternative housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal/logistical accompaniment—often where **neither the state nor local authorities** provided adequate response. The organization stresses an equity theme: people who need the most help in wartime are often **last** to receive institutional support.

With the **28 February 2026** escalation, Access Israel frames a renewed, significant, and urgent need for **accessible, professional, and sensitive** support tailored to people with disabilities and elderly Israelis—explicitly seeking to ensure **no one is left behind** and that assistance/protection owed in emergency is actually delivered.

The Hebrew appeal also emphasizes (in substance) that donor support is the **mainstay** enabling rapid action and that, in the current escalation, contributions translate directly into **protection and security** for the most vulnerable; it calls for immediate **resource mobilization** to expand Purple Vest and sustain the required response.

## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **initial immediate emergency grant of $400,000 for 10 days of activity** following the renewed fighting that began on the morning of **28 February 2026** (described as the current round of fighting/escalation with Iran).

The funding is to scale and sustain the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response so people with disabilities and elderly citizens receive equitable, accessible evacuation, shelter, and life-saving support.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial 10-day period)

**Non-Financial Ask:** Urgent partnership/collaboration and availability for clarifications.

## Critical Activities Requiring Immediate Funding

1. **24/7 accessible emergency operations room / hotline**  
   For all disability types, including professional staffing, Purple Vest app development and maintenance, communication systems, logistics, and real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries.

2. **Purple Vest refresher training and deployment**  
   Training/refreshers for municipal volunteers, accessible evacuation equipment, and operational management/coordination for rapid accessible field response (including missile-strike incidents in buildings and neighborhoods).

3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts**  
   Ongoing operation including drivers, fuel, insurance, and maintenance, for extraction and safe transfer from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible protected/sheltered housing.

4. **Accessible protected/fortified alternative accommodation**  
   Immediate support for hotel stays and accessible housing solutions for families forced to evacuate inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.

5. **Life-saving information dissemination in accessible formats**  
   Digital and communications campaigns, preparedness and emergency-assistance content, website maintenance, and real-time nationwide accessibility of vital information.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel says it has been on the humanitarian front line for vulnerable populations, often in gaps where state and local authorities did not provide sufficient response. Reported support includes accessible evacuation, accessible protected/fortified alternative housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal/logistical accompaniment in acute distress.

The organization emphasizes that those most in need in wartime are often last to receive institutional support. With the renewed hostilities on 28 February 2026, it reports a renewed, significant, urgent need for accessible, professional, and sensitive response for people with disabilities and elderly citizens.

The $400,000 request is presented as an immediate first tranche for ten days, with continuation needs to be jointly reassessed according to developments and conflict duration. The letter stresses that donor support is the backbone of operations and that immediate contributions directly translate to protection and safety for the most vulnerable.

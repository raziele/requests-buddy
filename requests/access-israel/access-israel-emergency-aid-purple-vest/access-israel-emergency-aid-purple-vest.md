## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **initial immediate grant of $400,000 for 10 days of activity** following renewed hostilities on **28 February 2026** (described as a round of fighting/war with Iran).  
The request seeks urgent donor partnership and collaboration to expand the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response and prevent people with disabilities and elderly citizens from being left behind.

Some versions also explicitly state:
- this is an **initial** emergency sum, with follow-on needs to be jointly assessed based on conflict developments and duration;
- the organization is available for clarifications;
- there is an expectation of very rapid decision-making (in one forwarded context, response expected the next day).

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial, immediate; 10-day period)

**Non-Financial Ask:** Urgent partnership/collaboration and rapid mobilization.

## Funding Priorities (Critical Operations)

1. **24/7 accessible emergency hotline / emergency situation room (חמ"ל חירום נגיש 24/7)**  
   Staffing, disability-inclusive hotline operations, Purple Vest app development/maintenance, communications systems, logistics, and real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries.

2. **Purple Vest volunteer refreshers, training, and activation**  
   Training/refreshers for volunteers in local authorities, accessible evacuation equipment, and field operational management/coordination, including response in missile-strike incidents in buildings/neighborhoods.

3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts**  
   Ongoing operation costs (drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance) for safe evacuation/transfer from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible sheltered housing.

4. **Accessible and protected/fortified alternative accommodation for evacuees**  
   Immediate support for hotels and accessible protected housing solutions for families evacuating inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.

5. **Life-saving information publication and dissemination**  
   Digital/media campaigns, emergency preparedness content, website maintenance, and real-time accessibility of vital information for people with disabilities nationwide.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel says it has been at the forefront of humanitarian support for highly vulnerable populations, with donor support enabling assistance to thousands through:
- accessible evacuation;
- accessible alternative protected/sheltered housing;
- medical equipment provision;
- dedicated accessible transportation;
- personal and logistical support in acute distress.

Across versions, the organization repeatedly emphasizes that:
- in many cases, response gaps remained where state/local authorities did not provide sufficient support;
- those needing the most help in wartime are often the last to receive institutional response;
- approximately 40% of people with disabilities and elderly citizens are described as lacking accessible shelter, leaving many with no practical option during sirens;
- with the escalation beginning 28 Feb 2026, immediate accessible, professional, and sensitive support is urgently required.

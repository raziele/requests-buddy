## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **initial immediate emergency grant of $400,000 for 10 days of activity** following the escalation/new round of fighting on **28 February 2026** (described as the current round of fighting with Iran/war). The funding is to expand and operate the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response and ensure equitable, accessible evacuation, shelter, and life-saving support for people with disabilities and elderly citizens.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial, immediate; for ten days)

**Non-Financial Ask:** Urgent partnership/collaboration; organization available for clarifications.

**Timeline Note:** One source frames this as time-sensitive with expectation of next-day response.

### Funding Breakdown (Consolidated)

- **24/7 accessible emergency hotline / emergency situation room**: staffing, professional response, Purple Vest app development/maintenance, communications systems, logistics, real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries.
- **Purple Vest training, refreshers, and deployment**: volunteer training in municipalities/authorities, refreshments, accessible evacuation equipment, operational management and coordination for rapid field response (including missile-strike incidents in buildings/neighborhoods).
- **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles (with lifts)**: drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance, ongoing operation for safe transfer from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible protected housing.
- **Accessible and protected/fortified alternative accommodation**: hotels and accessible sheltered housing for evacuated families, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.
- **Life-saving information dissemination**: digital and communications campaigns, preparedness/emergency assistance content, website maintenance, and real-time accessibility of vital information nationwide.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel reports being at the forefront of humanitarian assistance for the most vulnerable populations. With donor support, it has assisted thousands through accessible evacuation, accessible alternative protected housing, medical equipment provision, dedicated accessible transport, and personal/logistical support in acute distress—often where state and local authorities did not provide adequate response.

With renewed hostilities starting the morning of **28 February 2026**, the organization describes a renewed, significant, and urgent need for accessible, professional, sensitive support tailored to people with disabilities and elderly citizens. The most vulnerable are often the last to receive institutional support.

The organization emphasizes that this is an initial ten-day emergency amount; additional needs are to be jointly assessed based on developments and the duration of fighting. Donor support is described as the mainstay of rapid life-saving operations, and each contribution as directly translating into protection and security for vulnerable populations.

## The Ask

Access Israel requests **urgent partnership** and an **initial, immediate grant of $400,000** to fund **critical Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול) emergency operations for ten days** following the **escalation of hostilities on the morning of 28 February 2026** (described in materials as a **current round of fighting with Iran**). The organization states that **further needs will be assessed jointly with partners** based on developments and duration of fighting.

**Funding requested:** **$400,000** (explicitly tied to **10 days of activity** in the letter and most summaries).

**Non-financial ask (from one merged source):** Partnership and urgent collaboration; organization available for clarifications.

**Urgency framing (from one merged source):** Request characterized as time-sensitive, with an expectation of **a response the next day** (per that intake note).

Funds are intended to scale and sustain an accessible emergency response so people with disabilities and elderly citizens receive **equitable access to evacuation, shelter, information, and support** during the crisis.

## Use of Funds (critical activities — consolidated)

The Hebrew appeal and English summaries align on five funded pillars (wording varies slightly by source):

1. **24/7 accessible emergency operations / situation room** — disability-inclusive **hotline**; professional staffing; **Purple Vest app** development and maintenance; communications; logistics; **real-time response** to **thousands of expected inquiries** in the coming days.
2. **Purple Vest volunteer array** — **training and refreshers** for volunteers in municipalities; **refreshments** mentioned in some English summaries; **accessible evacuation equipment**; operational management and coordination for **rapid field response** (e.g., missile strikes affecting buildings and neighborhoods).
3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles (lifts)** — ongoing operations including drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance; **safe evacuation / transfer** from **unprotected areas** to medical care and **accessible protected / fortified / sheltered** alternative housing.  
   - *Wording note:* One English extraction used **“unmasked areas”**; context and Hebrew (**אזורים לא ממוגנים**) indicate **unprotected / non-fortified areas**.
4. **Accessible protected alternative accommodation** — immediate costs for **hotels** and **accessible protected / fortified / sheltered** solutions for families with people with disabilities evacuating **inaccessible or damaged homes**, including **required adaptations** and **logistical accompaniment / support**.
5. **Life-saving information** — digital and communications **campaigns**; **preparedness and emergency assistance** content; **website maintenance**; **real-time** accessibility of vital information **nationwide** for people with disabilities.

**Budget detail:** Sources consistently indicate **line items are not dollar-itemized** in the materials (listed as not itemized / “None” in breakdown tables).

## Context & Background

- **Since 7 October**, Access Israel describes itself as at the **forefront of humanitarian aid** for the **most vulnerable**, having assisted **thousands** with: accessible evacuation; **accessible alternative protected / fortified / sheltered** housing; medical equipment; dedicated accessible transport; and personal and logistical support in acute distress—**often when neither the state nor local authorities provided adequate response**. The organization emphasizes that **those who need the most help in wartime are often the last to receive institutional support**.
- **As of 28 February 2026**, renewed hostilities create a **renewed, significant, and urgent** need for **accessible, professional, and sensitive** support tailored to people with disabilities and the elderly, with a commitment to **leave no one behind** and ensure entitled assistance and protection in emergency.
- The letter’s **closing** stresses that donor support is the **mainstay** enabling rapid action and life-saving work; during this escalation, **contributions translate directly** into **protection and security** for the most vulnerable; the organization seeks **immediate resource mobilization** to **expand Purple Vest** and sustain the required response.

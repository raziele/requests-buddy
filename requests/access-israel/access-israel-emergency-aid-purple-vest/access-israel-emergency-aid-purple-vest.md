## The Ask

Access Israel requests **urgent partnership** and an **initial, immediate $400,000** to fund **critical Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול) emergency operations for ten days** following the **escalation of hostilities on the morning of 28 February 2026** (described in source material as a renewed round of fighting / war with Iran). The same amount and timeframe appear across all summaries. **Further needs** are to be **assessed jointly with partners** based on **developments and duration of fighting**.

**Non-financial ask (where noted):** partnership, urgent collaboration, availability for clarifications.

**Funding requested:** $400,000 (for ten days of activity)

### Use of funds (combined line items; amounts not itemized in sources)

Sources describe the same five pillars, with slightly different English labels:

1. **24/7 accessible emergency operations center / hotline** — professional staff; **Purple Vest app** development and maintenance; communications; logistics; **real-time response** to **thousands of expected inquiries** in the coming days.
2. **Purple Vest volunteer array** — **training and refreshers** for volunteers in municipalities; **refreshments** noted in one English summary; **accessible evacuation equipment**; operational management and coordination for **rapid accessible field response** (e.g. missile strikes on buildings and neighborhoods).
3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts** — drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance; **safe evacuation / transfer** from **unprotected / “unmasked”** areas (see *Differences & translation notes*) to medical care and **accessible protected / fortified / sheltered** alternative housing.
4. **Accessible protected alternative accommodation for evacuees** — e.g. hotels and other accessible protected solutions; adaptations and **logistical accompaniment / support** for families with people with disabilities evacuating inaccessible or damaged homes.
5. **Life-saving information** — digital and communications campaigns; preparedness and emergency-assistance content; website maintenance; **real-time, nationwide accessibility** of vital information for people with disabilities.

**Terminology variants across sources:** “emergency hotline” vs “emergency situation room”; “protected” vs “fortified” vs “sheltered” for housing; “refreshments” appears only in one English breakdown.

## Context & Background

- **Framing:** Emergency appeal tied to the **current round of fighting with Iran**; subject framed as saving lives of people and elderly with **severe disabilities** during war.
- **Vulnerability:** ~**40%** of people with disabilities and elderly are described as living **without accessible shelter**; when sirens sound they often **lack a real alternative** and may **stay in place** in fear.
- **Track record since 7 October:** Access Israel describes being at the **forefront of humanitarian aid** for the most vulnerable, with donor support enabling **accessible evacuation**, **accessible alternative protected/fortified/sheltered housing**, **medical equipment**, **dedicated accessible transport**, and **personal and logistical support** in acute distress—often where **neither the state nor local authorities** provided adequate response. **Those who need help most in wartime are often last to receive institutional support.**
- **Closing themes in the Hebrew letter (summarized):** donor support as the **mainstay** enabling speed and life-saving action; in the current escalation, contributions translate into **protection and security** for the most vulnerable; need for **immediate resource mobilization** to **expand Purple Vest** and sustain the required response; commitment not to leave anyone behind.

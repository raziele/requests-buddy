## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **immediate initial grant of $400,000 for 10 days of activity** following the renewed fighting beginning **28 February 2026** (described in the letters as the current round of fighting with Iran). The requested support is framed as urgent partnership to expand and operate the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response for people with disabilities and elderly citizens.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial, immediate; ten-day period)  
**Non-Financial Ask:** Urgent partnership/collaboration, with availability for clarifications and follow-up needs assessment based on conflict developments.

### Funding Breakdown / Critical Activities

- **24/7 accessible emergency hotline / situation room**: professional staffing, accessible inquiry response for all disability types, Purple Vest app development/maintenance, communications systems, logistics, real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries.
- **Purple Vest volunteer refreshers, training, and activation**: trainings in municipalities/authorities, accessible evacuation equipment, operational management and coordination, field deployment including missile-strike incidents.
- **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts**: drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance; safe transfer from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible sheltered housing.
- **Accessible and protected/fortified alternative accommodation**: hotel and housing solutions for evacuees/families, required accessibility adaptations, logistical accompaniment.
- **Life-saving information dissemination**: digital and communications campaigns, preparedness and emergency-assistance content, website maintenance, and real-time accessible vital information nationwide.

## Context & Background

- Since **7 October**, Access Israel states it has been on the front line assisting vulnerable populations through accessible evacuation, alternative protected housing, medical equipment support, dedicated accessible transportation, and personal/logistical crisis accompaniment.
- Multiple versions repeat that these interventions often occurred where state/local responses were insufficient, and that those most in need are often last to receive institutional support.
- The organization describes a renewed and urgent need as of the morning of **28 February 2026**, emphasizing that donor support is the main operational backbone and that immediate resources are needed to save lives and avoid leaving people behind.
- All versions align on the 10-day initial emergency scope and state that continuation needs should be jointly reassessed based on war duration and conditions.
- One version explicitly notes urgency expecting a near-immediate (next-day) response.

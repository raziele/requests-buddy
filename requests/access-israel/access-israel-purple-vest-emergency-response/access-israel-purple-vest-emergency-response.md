## The Ask

Access Israel requests an immediate $400,000 donation to fund the Purple Vest emergency response for ten days following the escalation of hostilities on 28 February 2026. The organization has been on the front line of humanitarian aid for the most vulnerable since 7 October, helping thousands with accessible evacuation, sheltered housing, medical equipment, and dedicated transport. With the new round of fighting, they need urgent partnership to operate a 24/7 accessible emergency hotline, refresh and deploy the Purple Vest volunteer and equipment array, run wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles, provide accessible sheltered accommodation for evacuees, and disseminate life-saving preparedness information to people with disabilities nationwide.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and urgent collaboration; organization is available for clarifications.

## Context & Background

Access Israel (Amutah 0341204) is writing in the wake of the current round of fighting with Iran. The subject is an emergency call to save the lives of people and elderly with severe disabilities during the war. Approximately 40% of people with disabilities and elderly in Israel live without accessible shelter; when sirens sound they have no real alternative and often can only stay where they are, in anxiety and fear.

Since 7 October, Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian assistance for the most vulnerable populations. With donor support they have assisted thousands through accessible evacuation, accessible alternative sheltered housing, medical equipment supply, dedicated accessible transport, and personal and logistical support in acute distress—in many cases when neither the state nor local authorities provided adequate response. Those most in need of assistance in wartime are often the last to receive institutional support.

As of the morning of 28 February 2026, with the start of the new round of hostilities, the organization faces a renewed, significant, and urgent need to ensure accessible, professional, and sensitive support for people with disabilities and elderly. The requested $400,000 is for an initial ten-day period; further needs will be assessed jointly depending on developments and duration of the conflict. The letter stresses that donor support is the mainstay of their work and that every contribution translates directly into protection and security for the most vulnerable population.

**Opening (to federations and partners):**  
The letter is written with urgency and gravity on behalf of Access Israel and thousands of people with disabilities and elderly—approximately **40%** of whom live without accessible shelter. When a siren sounds they have no real alternative; often all that remains is to stay in place, in anxiety and fear.

**Since 7 October** Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian assistance for the most vulnerable. Thanks to support, they have assisted thousands with: accessible evacuation, accessible alternative sheltered housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal and logistical support in acute distress—in many cases when no other actor (state or local authority) provided sufficient response. Those most in need of assistance in war are often the last to receive institutional response.

**Current need (as of 28 February 2026):**  
With the start of the war this morning, they face a renewed, significant, and urgent need to ensure accessible, professional, and sensitive support for people with disabilities and elderly. They request **urgent partnership in an initial and immediate sum of $400,000 (for ten days of activity)** for immediate critical operations; continuation will be evaluated jointly based on developments and duration of fighting.

**Critical activities requiring immediate funding:**

1. **הפעלת חמ"ל חירום נגיש 24/7** – Funding for an accessible inquiry hotline for all types of disabilities: professional staff, development and maintenance of the Purple Vest app, communication systems, logistical operation, and real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries in the coming days.

2. **הכשרות ריענון והפעלת מערך "האפוד הסגול"** – Budget for training and refreshers for volunteers in authorities, accessible evacuation equipment, management and operational coordination, to ensure rapid response and accessible assistance in the field in missile-strike events in buildings and neighborhoods.

3. **הפעלת רכבים עם מעלון לכיסאות גלגלים** – Budget for ongoing operation of accessible evacuation vehicles, including drivers, fuel, insurance, and maintenance, for extraction and safe transfer from unmasked areas to medical care and accessible alternative sheltered housing.

4. **לינה נגישה וממוגנת חלופית למפונים** – Immediate funding to cover costs of stay in hotels and accessible housing solutions for families with people with disabilities forced to evacuate from inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical support.

5. **פרסום והפצת מידע מציל חיים במדיות השונות** – Budget for digital and communication campaigns, preparedness and emergency-assistance content, website maintenance, and making vital information accessible to people with disabilities across the country in real time.

**Closing:**  
Partners’ support is the mainstay of their activity and enables them to act quickly and save lives. At this moment of escalation, every contribution translates directly into protection and security for the most vulnerable. They need immediate resource mobilization to expand the Purple Vest activity and continue providing the required response.

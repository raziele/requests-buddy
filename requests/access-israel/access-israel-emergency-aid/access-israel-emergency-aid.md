## The Ask

Access Israel requests **urgent partnership** and an **immediate emergency grant of $400,000** for **ten days of critical operations** following the escalation of hostilities (**war with Iran**) that began on the **morning of 28 February 2026**. The funding scales the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response so people with disabilities and elderly citizens get equitable access to evacuation, shelter, and information.

Funds support:

1. **24/7 accessible emergency response** — disability-inclusive hotline / accessible **situation room** (חמ"ל), professional staffing, **Purple Vest app** development and maintenance, communications, logistics, and real-time response to **thousands of expected inquiries** in the coming days.
2. **Purple Vest training and field deployment** — training and **refreshers** for volunteers in municipalities, refreshments where noted, **accessible evacuation equipment**, operational management and coordination for rapid accessible assistance after **missile strikes on buildings and neighborhoods**.
3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts** — drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance; safe evacuation from **unprotected / non-fortified** areas to medical care and **accessible protected / fortified / sheltered** alternative housing.
4. **Accessible protected alternative accommodation** — immediate costs for **hotels** and other accessible protected solutions for families forced to evacuate from inaccessible or damaged homes, including adaptations and **logistical accompaniment / support**.
5. **Life-saving information** — digital and communications campaigns, preparedness and emergency-assistance content, **website maintenance**, and real-time accessibility of vital information **nationwide**.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (for ten days of activity).

**Follow-on:** Further needs to be assessed **jointly with partners** according to developments and duration of fighting.

**Non-financial ask (where recorded):** Partnership, urgent collaboration, and availability for clarifications.

**Timing note (one source only):** Framed as time-sensitive, with expectation of a **response the next day**.

**Funding breakdown:** Sources used “Not itemized” or “None” per line item — no dollar splits were provided.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian aid for the most vulnerable. With donor support it has helped **thousands** through accessible evacuation, **accessible protected / fortified / sheltered** alternative housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal and logistical support in acute distress—often when **neither the state nor local authorities** provided adequate response. **Those who need the most help in wartime are often the last to receive institutional support.**

The Hebrew appeal frames the subject as an **emergency call to save the lives of people and elderly with severe disabilities** during the war, and stresses that donor support is the **mainstay** of rapid action; in the current escalation, **every contribution** is described as translating directly into **protection and security** for the most vulnerable. The letter calls for **immediate resource mobilization** to expand Purple Vest and sustain the required response.

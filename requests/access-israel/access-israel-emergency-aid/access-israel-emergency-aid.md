## The Ask

Access Israel requests an immediate emergency grant of **$400,000** for **10 days** of critical operations following the outbreak of war / escalation of hostilities on **28 February 2026**. The request is framed as critical and time-sensitive; one version notes an expectation of a response the next day.

Funds will support:
- **24/7 accessible emergency hotline** (situation room) — staffing, Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול) app development/maintenance, communications, logistics, real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries.
- **Purple Vest training, refreshments, and deployment** — refresher training and activation of volunteers in authorities, accessible evacuation equipment, operational coordination for rapid accessible assistance in the field (e.g. missile strikes on buildings and neighborhoods).
- **Wheelchair-accessible vehicles with lifts** — drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance for safe evacuation from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible (fortified/sheltered) temporary housing.
- **Accessible and protected/fortified alternative accommodation for evacuees** — immediate funding for stays in hotels and accessible protected solutions for families with people with disabilities forced to evacuate from inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.
- **Life-saving information campaigns** — digital and communications campaigns, preparedness and emergency assistance content, website maintenance, and real-time accessibility of vital information for people with disabilities nationwide.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (for 10 days of activity)

**Funding Breakdown:** Not itemized in the source documents (line items listed as "None" or "Not itemized" in ingested versions).

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and urgent collaboration; organization is available for clarifications.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian aid for the most vulnerable populations. With donor support they have provided accessible evacuation, accessible and protected alternative housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal and logistical accompaniment in crisis situations—often where neither the state nor local authorities provided adequate response. People who need the most assistance in war are often the last to receive institutional support.

With the new round of fighting that began on the morning of **28 February 2026**, there is a renewed, significant, and urgent need to ensure accessible, professional, and sensitive support for people with disabilities and the elderly. The organization seeks immediate partnership so that no one is left behind and everyone receives the assistance and protection they are entitled to in an emergency. The letter stresses that donor support is the mainstay of their work and that every contribution translates directly into protection and security for the most vulnerable population. Further needs are to be assessed jointly according to developments and duration of fighting.

## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **immediate emergency grant of $400,000** for **10 days** of critical operations following the outbreak of war / escalation of hostilities on **28 February 2026**. Funds will support: a 24/7 accessible emergency hotline (Purple Vest app, staff, communications, logistics); Purple Vest volunteer training, refreshments, and deployment with accessible evacuation equipment and operational coordination; wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles (drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance); accessible and protected / fortified temporary housing for evacuees; and life-saving information campaigns so that people with disabilities and the elderly receive equitable access to shelter and evacuation. The request is critical and time-sensitive; one source notes an expectation of a response the next day.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (for 10 days of activity)

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership, urgent collaboration, and joint assessment of follow-on needs based on developments and duration of fighting. Organization is available for clarifications.

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **24/7 accessible emergency hotline / situation room** — Staffing, Purple Vest app development and maintenance, communications, logistics, real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries.
- **Purple Vest training, refreshments, and deployment** — Training and refreshments for volunteers in authorities, accessible evacuation equipment, operational management and coordination for rapid accessible assistance in the field (e.g. missile strikes on buildings and neighborhoods).
- **Wheelchair-accessible vehicles with lifts** — Ongoing operation of accessible evacuation vehicles: drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance, for safe evacuation from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible protected housing.
- **Accessible and protected / fortified alternative accommodation for evacuees** — Immediate funding for stays in hotels and accessible protected solutions for families with people with disabilities forced to evacuate from inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.
- **Life-saving information dissemination** — Digital and communications campaigns, preparedness and emergency assistance content, website maintenance, and real-time accessibility of vital information for people with disabilities nationwide.

(Individual line-item amounts were not provided in the sources.)

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian aid for the most vulnerable populations. With donor support they have provided accessible evacuation, accessible and protected alternative housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal and logistical accompaniment in crisis situations—often where neither the state nor local authorities provided adequate response. People who need the most assistance in war are often the last to receive institutional support.

With the new round of fighting that began on the morning of **28 February 2026** (current round of fighting with Iran), there is a renewed, significant, and urgent need to ensure accessible, professional, and sensitive support for people with disabilities and the elderly. The organization seeks immediate partnership to ensure no one is left behind and that everyone receives the assistance and protection they are entitled to in an emergency. Donor support is described as the mainstay of their activity, enabling them to act quickly and save lives; every contribution translates directly into protection and security for the most vulnerable population. Further needs will be assessed jointly with partners depending on developments and duration of the conflict.

## The Ask

Access Israel requests urgent partnership and an **immediate grant of $400,000** for **ten days of activity** to scale the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response after the escalation of hostilities (described in sources as a new round of fighting / war with Iran) beginning **28 February 2026**. The funds support: a **24/7 accessible emergency hotline** (also described as an accessible emergency **situation room**), **Purple Vest** volunteer **training / refresher training**, deployment, and **accessible evacuation equipment**, **wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts**, **accessible protected / fortified / sheltered alternative accommodation** for evacuees (e.g. hotels, adaptations, logistics), and **life-saving information** campaigns and real-time accessibility of vital preparedness and emergency information nationwide.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (10 days of critical operations).

**Follow-on:** Further needs to be assessed jointly with partners according to developments and duration of fighting.

**Non-financial (where noted):** Partnership, urgent collaboration, clarifications available from the organization.

## Context & Background

Access Israel (registration cited as **58-0341204** / **ע"ר-0341204** / Amutah **0341204**) is appealing in the wake of the **current round of fighting with Iran**, framed as an **emergency call to save the lives of people and elderly with severe disabilities** during the war. The letter stresses that those who need the most help in wartime are often the **last to receive institutional support**, and that Access Israel has often operated where **neither the state nor local authorities** provided adequate response.

Since **7 October**, Access Israel describes being at the forefront of humanitarian aid for the most vulnerable: **accessible evacuation**, **accessible alternative protected/sheltered housing**, **medical equipment**, **dedicated accessible transport**, and **personal and logistical accompaniment** in acute distress, having assisted **thousands**. With hostilities from **the morning of 28 February 2026**, the organization describes a **renewed, significant, and urgent** need for **accessible, professional, and sensitive** support. Donor support is described as the **mainstay** of rapid action; during escalation, contributions are said to translate **directly** into **protection and security** for the most vulnerable, with a need for **immediate resource mobilization** to expand Purple Vest and sustain the required response.

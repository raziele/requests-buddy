## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **initial immediate emergency grant of $400,000 for 10 days of activity** following the escalation/new round of fighting on **28 February 2026** (described as the current round of fighting with Iran). The request is to scale and sustain the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response so people with disabilities and elderly citizens receive equitable, accessible evacuation, shelter, and life-saving support.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial ten-day period)

**Non-Financial Ask:** Urgent partnership and collaboration; organization available for clarifications.

**Time Sensitivity Noted in One Version:** expectation of rapid/next-day response.

### Critical Activities Requiring Immediate Funding

1. **24/7 accessible emergency situation room / hotline**  
   Staffing for disability-inclusive hotline; Purple Vest app development and maintenance; communication systems; logistics; real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries.

2. **Purple Vest training, refreshers, and deployment**  
   Volunteer training/refreshers in municipalities/authorities; accessible evacuation equipment; operational management and coordination; rapid field response (including missile-strike incidents in buildings/neighborhoods).

3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts**  
   Ongoing operation including drivers, fuel, insurance, and maintenance; safe extraction and transfer from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible protected housing.

4. **Accessible and protected/fortified alternative accommodation**  
   Immediate coverage for hotels and accessible protected housing solutions for evacuated families; required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.

5. **Life-saving information publication and dissemination**  
   Digital and communication campaigns; preparedness/emergency assistance content; website maintenance; nationwide real-time accessibility of vital information for people with disabilities.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel states it has been at the forefront of humanitarian aid for vulnerable populations, assisting thousands through accessible evacuation, accessible protected alternative housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal/logistical support in severe distress—often where state and local authorities did not provide adequate response.

With the hostilities that began/escalated on **28 February 2026**, the organization reports a renewed and urgent need for accessible, professional, and sensitive emergency support. A repeated core claim is that roughly **40%** of people with disabilities and elderly citizens live without accessible shelter; during sirens many have no real alternative and remain in place in fear. The requested $400,000 is framed as immediate bridge funding for ten days, with follow-on needs to be jointly assessed based on developments and duration of fighting.

The appeal emphasizes that donor support is the operational backbone of the response and that immediate contributions translate directly into protection and security for highly vulnerable populations.

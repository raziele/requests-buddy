## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **initial immediate grant/donation of $400,000 for 10 days of activity** to scale emergency response during the renewed fighting that began on **28 February 2026** (described in some versions as escalation/war with Iran). The request emphasizes urgent partnership and immediate resource mobilization so people with disabilities and elderly citizens are not left behind.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial, immediate; ten-day period)

**Non-Financial Ask:** Urgent partnership/collaboration; organization available for clarifications.

### Funding Priorities (as presented across all versions)

1. **24/7 accessible emergency hotline / situation room (חמ"ל)**  
   Professional staffing, hotline access for all disability types, Purple Vest app development/maintenance, communications systems, logistics, and real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries.

2. **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול) training and deployment**  
   Refresher trainings for volunteers in municipalities/authorities, accessible evacuation equipment, operational management and coordination, and rapid accessible field response (including missile-strike incidents in buildings/neighborhoods).

3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts**  
   Ongoing operation including drivers, fuel, insurance, and maintenance for safe extraction/transfer from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible protected housing.

4. **Accessible and protected/fortified alternative accommodation**  
   Immediate funding for hotels and accessible protected housing solutions for evacuated families with people with disabilities, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.

5. **Life-saving information dissemination**  
   Digital and communications campaigns, preparedness/emergency content production, website maintenance, and real-time accessible distribution of vital information nationwide.

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel states it has been at the forefront of humanitarian support for highly vulnerable populations, assisting thousands with accessible evacuation, alternative accessible protected housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal/logistical support in severe distress. Multiple versions note this support often filled gaps where state and local authorities did not provide sufficient response.

With renewed hostilities on **28 February 2026**, the organization reports a renewed, significant, and urgent need for accessible, professional, and sensitive support for people with disabilities and elderly citizens. The organization stresses that those most in need are often the last to receive institutional assistance, and that donor support is the core enabler of rapid life-saving response.

The $400,000 request is explicitly framed as **initial** for ten days, with follow-on needs to be jointly reassessed based on developments and duration of fighting.

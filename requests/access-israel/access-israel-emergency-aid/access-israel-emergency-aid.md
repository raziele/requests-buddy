## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **initial immediate emergency grant of $400,000** for **10 days of activity** following escalation/renewed fighting on **28 February 2026** (described in some versions as the current round of fighting with Iran). The funds are to scale and operate the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response so people with disabilities and elderly citizens are not left behind.

The request emphasizes urgency, immediate mobilization, and partner collaboration; one version explicitly notes expectation of a response by the next day. It also states that follow-on needs should be jointly reassessed according to developments and conflict duration.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial immediate tranche for 10 days)  
**Non-Financial Ask:** Urgent partnership, collaboration, and availability for clarifications.

## Funding Breakdown / Critical Operations

1. **24/7 accessible emergency hotline / situation room (חמ"ל חירום נגיש 24/7)**  
   Staffing/professional personnel, Purple Vest app development and maintenance, communications systems, logistics, and real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries across disability types.

2. **Purple Vest refresher training and activation (הכשרות ריענון והפעלת מערך האפוד הסגול)**  
   Volunteer training/refreshers in municipalities and authorities, accessible evacuation equipment, field deployment, and operational management/coordination for rapid response, including missile-strike incidents in buildings/neighborhoods.

3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles with lifts (רכבים עם מעלון לכיסאות גלגלים)**  
   Ongoing operation including drivers, fuel, insurance, and maintenance for safe extraction/transfer from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible protected housing.

4. **Accessible and protected alternative accommodation for evacuees (לינה נגישה וממוגנת חלופית למפונים)**  
   Immediate coverage for hotels and accessible protected housing solutions for families with people with disabilities evacuated from inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.

5. **Life-saving information publication and dissemination (פרסום והפצת מידע מציל חיים)**  
   Digital and communications campaigns, preparedness/emergency content, website maintenance, and real-time accessible nationwide distribution of vital information.

> Multiple source files list these budget lines as not itemized (`None` / `Not itemized`), but the operational categories are consistent across all versions.

## Context & Background (Unified)

Since **7 October**, Access Israel reports being at the forefront of humanitarian aid for highly vulnerable populations, assisting thousands through:
- accessible evacuation,
- accessible alternative/protected housing,
- medical equipment provision,
- dedicated accessible transport,
- personal and logistical accompaniment in acute distress.

The documents consistently state that in many cases this support was provided when state and/or local authorities did not provide adequate response. They stress that those needing the most assistance in wartime are often the last to receive institutional support.

With the renewed hostilities/war beginning the morning of **28 February 2026**, Access Israel describes a significant renewed need for accessible, professional, and sensitive emergency support tailored to people with disabilities and elderly citizens.

## The Ask

Access Israel requests urgent partnership and an initial immediate grant of $400,000 to fund the Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול) emergency response for people with disabilities and elderly during the current escalation (war with Iran as of 28 February 2026). The organization has been on the front line of humanitarian aid since October 7 and now needs to scale a 24/7 accessible hotline, Purple Vest volunteer training and field response, wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles, accessible sheltered accommodation for evacuees, and life-saving information campaigns. The request is framed as critical and time-sensitive, with the expectation of a response the next day.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- 24/7 accessible emergency hotline (staff, Purple Vest app, communications, logistics): None
- Purple Vest training, refreshers, and field response (volunteers, accessible evacuation equipment, coordination): None
- Wheelchair-accessible vehicles with lifts (drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance): None
- Accessible and sheltered alternative accommodation for evacuees (hotels, housing, logistics): None
- Publishing and disseminating life-saving information (digital campaigns, preparedness content, website, nationwide accessible info): None

## Context & Background

Following the outbreak of a new round of hostilities on the morning of 28 February 2026, Access Israel is requesting immediate funding to expand its Purple Vest emergency response. The organization states that roughly 40% of people with disabilities and elderly in Israel live without accessible shelter; when rocket sirens sound, many have no real option and remain in place in fear. Since 7 October, Access Israel has provided accessible evacuation, accessible sheltered housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal and logistical support in crisis situations—often where neither the state nor local authorities provided adequate response. The most vulnerable are sometimes the last to receive institutional help.

With the escalation, the organization faces a renewed, significant, and urgent need to ensure accessible, professional, and sensitive support for people with disabilities and elderly. The requested $400,000 is for an initial ten-day period; further needs will be assessed with partners depending on developments and duration of the conflict. Critical operations include a 24/7 accessible emergency hotline (including the Purple Vest app and real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries), training and activation of the Purple Vest volunteer network in municipalities with accessible evacuation equipment, operation of wheelchair-accessible vehicles for evacuation from unprotected areas to medical care and sheltered housing, immediate funding for accessible and sheltered accommodation for evacuated families, and campaigns to publish and disseminate life-saving preparedness and emergency information in accessible formats nationwide.

The letter emphasizes that donor support is the mainstay of their ability to act quickly and save lives, and that in the current escalation every contribution translates directly into protection and security for the most vulnerable population. Contact is Yuval Wagner, Founder and Chair (yuval@aisrael.org, mobile 054-4436456). The request was forwarded via Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia (Talia Lidar, Tamar Broady) for consideration.

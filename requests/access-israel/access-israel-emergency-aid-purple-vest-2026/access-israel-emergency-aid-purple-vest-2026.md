## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **immediate emergency grant of $400,000 for ten days of critical operations** following the escalation of hostilities on **28 February 2026** (described in sources as a new round of fighting / war with Iran). Funds will scale the **Purple Vest (האפוד הסגול)** emergency response and cover:

- **24/7 accessible emergency operations** — disability-inclusive hotline / accessible **emergency situation room** (חמ״ל חירום נגיש), professional staffing, **Purple Vest app** development and maintenance, communications, logistics, and real-time response to **thousands of expected inquiries**
- **Purple Vest volunteer capacity** — training, **refreshers**, refreshments (where noted), **accessible evacuation equipment**, and operational coordination for rapid field response (e.g. missile strikes on buildings and neighborhoods)
- **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles** with lifts — drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance — for safe evacuation from **unprotected / non-fortified** areas to medical care and **accessible protected / fortified / sheltered** housing
- **Accessible protected alternative accommodation** for evacuees — hotels and other accessible protected solutions, adaptations, and logistical accompaniment for families forced from inaccessible or damaged homes
- **Life-saving information** — digital and communications campaigns, preparedness and emergency-assistance content, website maintenance, and real-time accessibility of vital information nationwide

**Funding requested:** $400,000 (initial tranche for ~10 days). **Follow-on:** further needs to be assessed **jointly with partners** based on developments and duration of fighting.

**Non-financial ask (one source):** partnership and urgent collaboration; organization available for clarifications.

**Framing note (one source):** request characterized as time-sensitive, with expectation of a **response the next day** (other sources do not repeat this timing).

**Funding breakdown:** sources agree on the five activity buckets above; **line-item dollar amounts were not provided** (summaries used “Not itemized,” “None,” or similar).

## Context & Background

Since **7 October**, Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian aid for highly vulnerable populations. With donor support it has provided **accessible evacuation**, **accessible alternative protected/sheltered housing**, **medical equipment**, **dedicated accessible transport**, and **personal and logistical accompaniment** in acute distress—often where **neither the state nor local authorities** provided adequate response. Sources emphasize that **those who need help most in wartime are often last to receive institutional support**.

With hostilities beginning **the morning of 28 February 2026**, the organization describes a **renewed, significant, and urgent** need for **accessible, professional, and sensitive** support for people with disabilities and the elderly, and seeks partnership so that **no one is left behind** and people receive the **assistance, accessibility, and protection** they are entitled to in emergency.

The Hebrew appeal stresses that donor support is the **mainstay** of rapid action and life-saving work; in the current escalation, **every contribution** is framed as translating directly into **protection and security** for the most vulnerable, with a call for **immediate resource mobilization** to expand Purple Vest activity.

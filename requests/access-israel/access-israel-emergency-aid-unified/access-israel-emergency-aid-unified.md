## The Ask

Access Israel requests an **initial, immediate emergency grant of $400,000 for 10 days** of critical operations following the renewed hostilities beginning **28 February 2026** (described as the current round of fighting with Iran). The request is framed as urgent, time-sensitive, and partnership-based, with follow-on needs to be jointly reassessed based on developments and duration of fighting.

### Financial Ask

**Funding Requested:** **$400,000** (initial immediate amount; ten-day activity window)

### Non-Financial Ask

- Urgent partnership and collaboration
- Immediate resource mobilization to expand Purple Vest operations
- Organization available for clarification/follow-up

### Critical Activities Requiring Immediate Funding

1. **24/7 accessible emergency situation room / hotline**  
   Staffing, accessible inquiry center for all disability types, Purple Vest app development/maintenance, communication systems, logistics, and real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries.

2. **Purple Vest refresher training and deployment**  
   Training/refreshers for municipal volunteers, accessible evacuation equipment, operational management/coordination, and rapid field response (including missile-strike incidents in buildings/neighborhoods).

3. **Wheelchair-accessible evacuation vehicles (with lifts)**  
   Ongoing operation including drivers, fuel, insurance, and maintenance for safe evacuation from unprotected areas to medical care and accessible protected/fortified housing.

4. **Accessible protected/fortified alternative accommodation for evacuees**  
   Immediate funding for hotel stays and accessible housing solutions for families forced to evacuate inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical accompaniment.

5. **Life-saving information dissemination**  
   Digital and communication campaigns, emergency preparedness content, website maintenance, and real-time accessibility of vital information nationwide.

## Context & Background

- Access Israel reports being on the frontline of humanitarian support since **7 October**, providing accessible evacuation, sheltered/fortified alternative housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal/logistical accompaniment.
- The organization states that many interventions occurred where state and local authority response was insufficient.
- The letter emphasizes a recurring equity gap: those who most need support in wartime are often the last to receive institutional response.
- With renewed fighting on **28 February 2026**, Access Israel describes a renewed, significant, urgent need for accessible, professional, and sensitive support for people with disabilities and elderly citizens.
- Donor support is described as the core enabler of rapid life-saving response; each contribution is framed as directly translating to protection and security for the most vulnerable.
- One forwarding note indicates expectation of a next-day response due to urgency.

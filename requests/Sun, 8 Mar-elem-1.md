## The Ask

ELEM is shifting to emergency operations to ensure continuity of care for vulnerable teens and young adults during the ongoing war. They are requesting additional funding for several emergency programs: reinforcing their national online support service (ELEM Digital) with expanded staff for Hebrew and Arabic speakers, strengthening 24/7 shelters with increased professional staffing for continuous, trauma-informed care, preparing calming kits for youth in shelters to reduce anxiety and regulate stress, and providing food baskets to those in need.

- Reinforced teams - ELEM Digital: 8091

- "Calming kits" for youth staying in our shelters: 3236

## Context & Background

The ongoing war in Israel has led ELEM to shift its focus to emergency operations to support vulnerable teens and young adults. The organization is committed to providing continuous care, adapting its services to meet the emerging needs on the ground. Most support is currently delivered remotely due to national safety guidelines, leveraging existing trust and relationships for effective connection.

ELEM has expanded several services to offer immediate assistance to those experiencing distress. Their Digital Outreach Support now operates until midnight on weekdays with reinforced teams trained for the current period. Additionally, their 24/7 shelters in Jerusalem, Haifa, and Tel Aviv have enhanced professional staffing to ensure immediate, trauma-informed care for young people who are experiencing heightened emotional distress and may need to remain indoors for extended periods. The organization notes that even graduates who had transitioned to independent living are returning for support.

ELEM is also preparing "calming kits" for youth in shelters, containing items to help reduce anxiety and regulate stress. Furthermore, they have identified a growing need for food assistance and have already begun distributing food baskets in Eilat, with plans to address further needs. The requested support will enable ELEM to respond swiftly, expand services, and maintain operational continuity during these challenging conditions.

### Emergency Activities _ March 2026.pdf

ELEM Emergency Response - Current Needs

March 8, 2026

In light of the ongoing war, ELEM has shifted to emergency operations to ensure continuity of care for vulnerable teens and young adults.

Emergency Programs Requiring Additional Funding

1. Reinforcement of ELEM Digital (National Service)

ELEM Digital is a nationwide online support service available to all teens in Israel, not only those previously engaged with ELEM. Due to the current situation, we are reinforcing staffing to expand support, both in Hebrew and Arabic, for teens who are unable to leave their homes and are experiencing heightened emotional distress. The increase in demand is already evident among both Hebrew and Arabic speaking youth.

2. Strengthening 24/7 Shelters – Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Haifa

Our shelters operate around the clock and are staffed by ELEM's professional teams. We are increasing staffing levels to ensure continuous, high-quality, trauma-informed care. The young people in our shelters are experiencing intensified triggers and emotional distress and require significantly more support than usual, particularly as they must remain indoors around the clock. In addition to current residents, graduates who had transitioned to independent living are now returning during the day because they need support and do not want to remain alone.

3. Calming Kits for Shelter Residents

We are preparing calming kits for youth staying in our shelters. These kits include practical items designed to help reduce anxiety and regulate stress, such as headphones, coloring booklets, stress balls, and other grounding tools. The kits are designated for the young people in our direct care.

4. Food Baskets

We have identified a growing need for food assistance in certain areas among the population we serve. In Eilat, our team has already distributed food baskets, and we are working to respond to additional needs as they arise.

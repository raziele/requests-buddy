## The Ask

Ezrat Achim is providing immediate medical and logistical support to families in Beit Shemesh affected by a missile strike during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." They are supporting hundreds of families by providing replacement medical equipment, household items, and daily follow-up, and are seeking funding for their ongoing emergency operations.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

## Context & Background

The email describes the impact of Operation "Shaagat HaAri" on Beit Shemesh, where a missile strike destroyed homes and displaced families. Ezrat Achim's teams responded immediately, providing rescue, medical, and logistical support. The organization is currently supporting hundreds of families, many of whom have lost basic medical equipment and the ability to leave their homes.

Ezrat Achim's current services include providing replacement medical equipment, mobility aids, essential household items, and daily follow-up. They are also offering emotional support and trauma care for children and parents. Their kitchen distributes over 127,000 hot meals annually, and this has been expanded during the current escalation. The organization's hotline operates 24 hours a day, six days a week, handling urgent requests for medical transport and psychological support.

For the next 90 days, Ezrat Achim plans to focus on replacing emergency medical equipment, expanding trauma and counseling services, increasing hospital transportation capacity, scaling food distribution, and supporting volunteer coordination. The emergency 90-day budget is $450,000, covering these areas. The organization emphasizes its deep roots in the community and its commitment to supporting affected families.

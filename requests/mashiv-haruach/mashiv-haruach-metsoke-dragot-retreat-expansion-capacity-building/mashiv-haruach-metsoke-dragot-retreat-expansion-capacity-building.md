## The Ask

Mashiv HaRuach is requesting support to complete the expansion of the Mashiv HaRuach House at Metsoke Dragot, a retreat and workshop space for frontline caregivers and first responders. The organization is currently providing emergency support (online sessions, regulation tools, Zoom support for women with PTSD) during an intense emergency phase, while renovation of the new space has already begun. Securing the remaining funding would allow the space to be ready in approximately three months, significantly increasing the number of workshops they can host for caregivers who will urgently need space to process trauma once the emergency phase subsides. They are asking whether the Federation has a mechanism to connect them with private donors for this capacity-building need.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Asking whether the Federation has any mechanism for reaching out to private donors who might be open to helping with this kind of urgent capacity-building need (expansion of Mashiv HaRuach House at Metsoke Dragot).

## Context & Background

The situation on the ground has shifted again into a very intense emergency phase. Among the professional first responders and caregivers Mashiv HaRuach works with — medical teams, mental health professionals, social workers, and educators — the intensity of work has risen sharply. Many are supporting injured individuals and highly distressed communities while living under the same threat. In response, Mashiv HaRuach is working with hospitals, mental health services, and local authorities to offer short emergency support frameworks: brief online support sessions and practical regulation tools to help teams stabilize while continuing to function under extreme conditions.

Within their community of partners coping with PTSD, the recent escalation has been deeply triggering. They are receiving many requests for support from women already in their network and are offering dedicated Zoom support sessions and short guidance materials. Alongside this immediate response, they are preparing for the next phase: once the emergency subsides, many first responders and caregivers will urgently need space to process what they have carried.

The expansion of the Mashiv HaRuach House at Metsoke Dragot is central to that preparedness. Renovation work on site has already begun; once the remaining funding is secured, the space could be ready within about three months and would allow a significant increase in the number of workshops they can host. The organization is seeking guidance or a Federation mechanism to reach private donors open to this kind of urgent capacity-building need.

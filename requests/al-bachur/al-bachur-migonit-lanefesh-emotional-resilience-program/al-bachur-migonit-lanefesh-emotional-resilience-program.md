## The Ask

Al-Bachur requests funding for "Migonit LaNefesh" (shelter for the soul), an emergency program during Operation Shaagat HaAri. The Bedouin community is especially vulnerable; most families have no shelter or mamad and live in tents or makeshift structures. The program would add emotional-resilience content to the organization's existing digital channels: professional support sessions (social workers, parent guides, psychologists), safety guidance for children at home, and online story hours and puppet theater focused on family resilience and anxiety. Budget: 162,000 ₪. Funding is not included in the association's annual budget.

**Funding Requested:** 162,000 ₪

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Expert pedagogical consultancy – children's literature and emotional resilience (30 workshops @ 1,200 ₪): 36,000 ₪
- Development and dissemination of applied pedagogical content by professional team (300 hours @ 300 ₪): 90,000 ₪
- Daily story hour / puppet theater on Zoom – 40 story hours @ 300 ₪: 12,000 ₪
- 20 puppet theater shows (twice weekly) @ 1,200 ₪: 24,000 ₪

## Context & Background

Al-Bachur works to reduce cultural and educational gaps, promote literacy, and strengthen parental and family resilience in the Bedouin community. Its projects include lending libraries in well-baby (Tipat Chalav) clinics, workshops to encourage reading and shared play, and training of early-childhood professionals. Programs combine digital platforms with regular updates and activities. During emergencies (COVID-19 and since October 2023) digital activity has allowed continuous contact with children and families; story hours and puppet theater have been held on Zoom with thousands of participants.

The security situation in the south has intensified with the renewal of war with Iran. The Bedouin community is especially vulnerable in wartime. The vast majority of families have no shelter or mamad and live in tents and makeshift structures with minimal protection. Parents and children hide from missiles under bridges and in pipes, experiencing severe anxiety and helplessness. To support families, Al-Bachur seeks to add to its existing digital activities professional content in emotional resilience: sessions with professionals (social workers, parent guides, psychologists) for emotional support and advice on routine and healthy communication, with attention to unique populations (parents of children with special needs, young mothers with postpartum depression); safety guidance for children at home; and story hours and puppet theater with a clear orientation to strengthening family resilience and tools for coping with anxiety. Content would be distributed via social networks and Al-Bachur's WhatsApp group, which has recently opened to thousands of additional Arabic-speaking participants in the center and north. This emergency activity is not pre-budgeted; the letter includes a cost table. Total requested: 162,000 ₪.

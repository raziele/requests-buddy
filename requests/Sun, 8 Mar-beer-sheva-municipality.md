## The Ask

The Be'er Sheva Municipality is urgently requesting 70 computers for displaced students staying in hotels due to a missile strike. These computers are essential for remote learning, providing a sense of stability, and preventing further educational gaps.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Donation of 70 computers

## Context & Background

A direct missile hit on Be'er Sheva on March 2, 2026, caused significant destruction and forced hundreds of families to evacuate their homes. Many are currently staying in hotels, trying to provide a sense of normalcy for their children. The current remote learning system is disrupted, and these children lack basic equipment, leading to significant educational and social gaps. The municipality has identified an immediate need for 70 computers for students in hotels.

### Be'er Sheva Municipality Request

Date: March 04, 2026

To: Mitchashvim Association

Subject: Urgent need for computers for displaced students due to missile strike in Be'er Sheva

Dear Sir/Madam,

On March 2, 2026, the city of Be'er Sheva was directly hit by a missile, causing significant destruction and affecting hundreds of families who were forced to evacuate their homes immediately without basic equipment. A significant portion of the families are currently staying in hotels in the city, trying to restore a sense of security and routine for their children.

The affected population includes welfare families, immigrant families, families with children with disabilities, and the elderly. During emergencies, these gaps widen rapidly and can lead to significant educational and social disconnection.

During these days, we are working with all available tools to ensure continuity of learning for the displaced children. For them, a computer is not a luxury, it is an anchor. It enables participation in remote learning, connection with educational teams and friends at home, and access to learning materials and emotional support. Beyond the pedagogical aspect, the computer provides a sense of stability, certainty, and routine amidst a reality of insecurity.

Currently, there is an immediate need for 70 computers for the children staying in hotels. Without a swift response, these students may face a significant educational gap and increased feelings of isolation and anxiety.

Therefore, we appeal to you with a request for urgent assistance in providing computers, which will allow the children to return, even partially, to a path of learning, recovery, and hope. Your support at this time is not just technological assistance, but a moral statement of responsibility, solidarity, and recognition of the human being behind the numbers.

We are at your disposal for any further information and would be very grateful for your swift response to this critical need.

Sincerely,

Shani Ora-Dotan
Beer Sheva Municipality

## The Ask

Kibbutz Manara is requesting emergency assistance during the current emergency period. The requests are related follow-ups (2026-03-09 Hebrew message, then 2026-03-11 English follow-up to Barak Loozon).

### 1. Security / readiness team (כיתת הכוננות / Kitat Konenut)

- Equip the local standby/readiness class: **30 people**, with **specific line items for 26** of them.
- **Items (2026-03-09):** tactical uniforms; **LOWA shoes** — marked **most urgent** (members reportedly in torn shoes); radio holsters; softshell jackets (AZTEC or OUTDOOR); **40 Pentel markers** (assorted colors).
- **Funding (2026-03-09):** **70,000 ₪** total *(no per-item breakdown in source)*.
- **Broader framing (2026-03-11):** essential tactical gear and personal equipment for the local response team that the **army does not provide**; two Word attachments give a **detailed security-needs table** (see Attachments).

### 2. Shelters

- Equipment to make shelters **functional and habitable**, with **special emphasis on children and youth shelters** for physical safety and emotional well-being during **long stays**.
- Detailed shelter/resilience needs table in attached `.doc` (2026-03-11).

### 3. Resilience in shelters

- Emotional relief and “breathing space” via **Zoom** or **visiting professionals** willing to travel to Manara.

### 4. Reservist families

- Practical support such as **vouchers for food and essential products** for families of those called up for reserve duty.

**Funding Requested:** **70,000 ₪** stated for the tactical/readiness-class package (2026-03-09). **Not specified** for the broader 2026-03-11 basket (shelters, resilience activities, reservist vouchers, full security table).

## Context & Background

Kibbutz Manara’s resource development team (Iris Hen) is continuing an **ongoing dialogue** with Barak Loozon. The **2026-03-09** message is a formal Hebrew request asking directly whether there is a way to assist with readiness-class equipment and stating the **70,000 ₪** total. The **2026-03-11** email is a **more detailed English follow-up** describing the same emergency period and expanding to shelters, youth, resilience programming, reservist families, and army gaps for the response team. The sender thanks the recipient for goodwill and readiness to help and looks forward to a response.

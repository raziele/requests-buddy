## The Ask

Kibbutz Manara is requesting emergency assistance during an active emergency period. The requests span several pillars:

1. **Readiness / tactical equipment (Kitat Konenut)** — Equip the community standby/readiness class (30 people; detailed line items for 26) with tactical uniforms, **LOWA shoes** (marked most urgent; members are in torn shoes), radio holsters, softshell jackets (AZTEC or OUTDOOR), and 40 Pentel markers (assorted colors). **Total stated for this line of request: 70,000 ₪** (no per-item breakdown in source). Essential tactical gear and personal equipment for the local response team are **not provided by the army**, so external support is sought to close the gap.

2. **Shelter equipment** — Items to make shelters functional and habitable, with **special emphasis on children and youth shelters** for physical safety and emotional well-being during long stays.

3. **Resilience activities in shelters** — Emotional relief and “breathing space” via Zoom or visiting professionals willing to travel to Manara.

4. **Reservist families** — Practical support such as **vouchers for food and essential products** for families of those called up for reserve duty.

Two detailed need tables (Word documents) were attached with the English message; the Hebrew message included budget context (70,000 ₪) and specific product list above.

**Funding Requested:** **70,000 ₪** specified for the tactical/readiness-class equipment package; broader shelter/resilience/reservist-family needs **not priced** in the English summary.

## Context & Background

The resource development team (Iris Hen) is following up on **prior correspondence** with Barak Loozon. The **2026-03-09** message is a formal Hebrew request asking whether assistance is possible. The **2026-03-11** message is a more detailed **English** follow-up that widens the scope beyond equipment alone to shelters, resilience programming, and reservist-family support.

The Upper Galilee context and community security/readiness priorities frame both messages. The sender expresses gratitude and looks forward to a response.

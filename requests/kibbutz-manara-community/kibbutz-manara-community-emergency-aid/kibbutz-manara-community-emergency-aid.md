## The Ask

Kibbutz Manara is requesting emergency assistance during an active emergency period. The requests are related follow-ups in an ongoing dialogue with Barak Loozon.

### 1. Security / readiness class (כיתת הכוננות / Kitat Konenut)

Equip the community standby/readiness class (**30 people**; itemized needs for **26**) with tactical gear and personal equipment **not provided by the army**. Specific items cited include: tactical uniforms; **LOWA shoes** — marked **most urgent** (members are in torn shoes); radio holsters; softshell jackets (**AZTEC** or **OUTDOOR**); and **40 Pentel markers** in assorted colors.

**Funding (this pillar):** **70,000 ₪** total *(2026-03-09 request)* — no per-item budget breakdown provided.

**Funding (overall English brief):** “Not specified” for the full multi-category package *(2026-03-11)* — reconcile with the 70,000 ₪ line item above when scoping support.

### 2. Shelters

Equipment to make shelters **functional and habitable**, with **special emphasis on children and youth shelters** for physical safety and emotional well-being during **long stays**.

### 3. Resilience in shelters

Support for activities that offer emotional relief and “breathing space” — via **Zoom** or **visiting professionals** willing to travel to Manara.

### 4. Reservist families

Practical support such as **vouchers for food and essential products** for families of those called up for reserve duty.

Two detailed need tables (Word documents) accompany the English request; the Hebrew request references an equipment list/costing (70,000 ₪) without a per-item breakdown in the summary.

## Context & Background

The resource development team (Iris Hen) first submitted a **formal Hebrew** request (**2026-03-09**) framed as a follow-up on community security and readiness, asking directly whether assistance is possible.

On **2026-03-11** they sent a **more detailed English** follow-up expanding the scope beyond tactical gear to **shelters**, **resilience programming**, and **reservist-family support**, while reiterating that essential Kitat Konenut gear is **not army-supplied** and requires external help. The sender thanks the recipient for goodwill and readiness to help and asks for a response.

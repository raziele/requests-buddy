## The Ask

Kibbutz Manara is requesting emergency assistance during the current emergency period. They need (1) immediate security equipment—tactical gear and personal equipment for their local response team (Kitat Konenut) that the army does not provide; (2) shelter equipment to make shelters functional and habitable, with special emphasis on children and youth shelters for safety and emotional well-being during long stays; (3) support for resilience activities in shelters (emotional relief and “breathing space” via Zoom or visiting professionals); and (4) practical support for reservist families, such as vouchers for food and essential products. Two detailed need tables are attached.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

## Context & Background

Kibbutz Manara’s resource development team is following up on a prior email to Barak Loozon with a more detailed request in English. The community is in an active emergency period and has identified urgent, unmet needs.

Their immediate security needs center on the Kitat Konenut (readiness/response team). Essential tactical gear and personal equipment required for the local response team are not provided by the army, so the kibbutz is seeking external support to equip this team.

Shelter readiness is a second priority. The kibbutz needs items to make shelters functional and habitable. They are placing special emphasis on children and youth shelters, ensuring equipment for both physical safety and emotional well-being during extended stays.

Beyond equipment, they are seeking support for resilience activities in shelters—emotional relief and “breathing space” activities delivered via Zoom or by professionals willing to travel to Manara—and for reservist families: practical assistance such as vouchers for food and essential products for families of those called up for reserve duty.

The sender expresses gratitude for the recipient’s goodwill and readiness to help and looks forward to a response.

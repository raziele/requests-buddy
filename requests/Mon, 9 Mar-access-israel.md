## The Ask

Access Israel is urgently requesting $400,000 to fund immediate emergency response activities, including operating a 24/7 accessible emergency hotline, training volunteers for the 'Purple Vest' system, and providing accessible transportation for evacuees. This funding is critical to ensure that individuals with disabilities and the elderly receive necessary accessible shelter, medical care, and support during the ongoing conflict.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Operating a 24/7 accessible emergency hotline: Implied within the $400,000
- Training and operation of the 'Purple Vest' system: Implied within the $400,000
- Accessible vehicles with lifts for wheelchairs: Implied within the $400,000
- Accessible and protected temporary housing: Implied within the $400,000
- Dissemination of life-saving information via various media: Implied within the $400,000

## Context & Background

Access Israel is responding to an urgent need for support for individuals with severe disabilities and the elderly, especially those who lack accessible shelters amidst the current conflict with Iran. They highlight that approximately 40% of this population lives without accessible bomb shelters, leaving them with no safe alternative during air raid sirens. Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian aid for vulnerable populations since October 7th, providing accessible evacuation, alternative housing, medical equipment, specialized transportation, and personal accompaniment.

Due to the current escalation, Access Israel is requesting an immediate $400,000 to fund critical operations for the first ten days of the conflict. These funds will be used to operate a 24/7 accessible emergency hotline, train and equip volunteers for the 'Purple Vest' emergency response system, and provide accessible transportation via specialized vehicles for evacuees. They emphasize that timely support is crucial to ensure these vulnerable individuals receive the necessary aid and protection, particularly in times of crisis when other support systems may be insufficient.

The organization stresses that their supporters' contributions are the backbone of their work, enabling rapid and life-saving assistance. They call for immediate fundraising to expand the 'Purple Vest' initiative and continue providing essential services, urging partners to help ensure that every person with a disability receives the assistance, accessibility, and protection they are entitled to, even during emergencies.

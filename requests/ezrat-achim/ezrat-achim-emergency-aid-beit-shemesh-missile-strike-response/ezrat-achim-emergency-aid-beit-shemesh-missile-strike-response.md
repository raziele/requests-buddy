## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day emergency response** following a missile strike on a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." The strike caused fatalities, destroyed homes, displaced hundreds of families, and left many households without basic functionality.

Ezrat Achim describes itself as Beit Shemesh’s embedded emergency medical and social safety net, with:
- **1,400 volunteers**
- **700 neighborhood representatives**
- **27 equipment branches** (noted in one source)

Teams, led by Rabbi Avraham Kop (also ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh), were reportedly on scene within minutes alongside emergency and security forces.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask (explicit in one source):**
- Partnership and a brief call this week to share immediate plans and coordination during the emergency window.

## Context & Background

Since the escalation, Ezrat Achim reports ongoing support to hundreds of affected families, including those in temporary housing or with relatives. Needs are described as practical, immediate, and ongoing.

Current activities include:
- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents
- Expanded food support beyond regular recipient lists
- Emergency hotline operations handling sharply increased demand

Food support context:
- In normal times, the kitchen distributes over **127,000 hot meals/year**
- During the crisis, meal distribution expanded to anyone in need, including elderly residents, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave home

Hotline context:
- Typical baseline: about **300 requests/day**
- Emergency mode described as **24 hours/day, six days/week (24/6)** in the appeal text
- One summary describes scaling toward **24/7** demand coverage

## 90-Day Response Priorities

1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies  
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services  
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity  
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid  
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day emergency response** following a missile strike in a residential neighborhood of Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." The organization reports immediate deployment within minutes alongside emergency/security forces and is already delivering medical, logistical, food, transport, and psychosocial support at expanded emergency scale.

Ezrat Achim describes itself as Beit Shemesh’s embedded safety-net infrastructure, with **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and (per one version) **27 equipment branches**. Funding is intended to stabilize families over the next three months and ensure continuity of essential care and daily functioning.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and a brief coordination call during the current emergency window.

## Context & Background

A missile strike on Sunday in March 2026 caused fatalities, destruction of homes, and mass displacement in Beit Shemesh. Families were forced to leave with almost nothing; some lost loved ones and many remain in acute distress. Ezrat Achim leadership and teams, including Rabbi Avraham Kop (also ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh), report responding immediately on scene.

### Current Response Activities
- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents
- Expanded meal distribution beyond regular recipient lists (kitchen baseline: 127,000+ hot meals/year)
- Emergency hotline operations handling urgent medical transport, food/kitchen disruptions, and psychological support requests

### Operational Notes / Version Differences
- Hotline expansion is described as **24 hours/day, 6 days/week (24/6)** in the source appeal text and one summary.
- Another summary references scaling to **24/7** emergency demand.
- Both versions agree demand has increased sharply from a normal baseline of ~300 requests/day.

### Next 90 Days (Program Priorities)
1. Replace emergency medical equipment and supplies  
2. Expand trauma and counseling services  
3. Increase hospital transportation and ambulance capacity  
4. Scale food distribution and essential aid  
5. Support volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

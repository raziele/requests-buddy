## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day emergency response** following a missile strike on a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." The strike caused fatalities, destroyed homes, and displaced hundreds of families, including elderly residents and young children.

The organization reports it was on scene within minutes and is operating as Beit Shemesh's embedded response infrastructure, with **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and **27 equipment branches**. Current emergency operations include replacement medical equipment and mobility aids, hospital transportation, essential household support, daily follow-up with displaced families, expanded meal distribution, and intensive hotline operations.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and a brief call this week to align immediate plans and collaboration during the response window.

## Context & Background

Since the escalation of Operation "Shaagat HaAri," Ezrat Achim describes an acute local humanitarian crisis in Beit Shemesh. After the missile strike, families were forced to evacuate with minimal belongings; some lost loved ones, and many remain in shock. The group emphasizes it is not an external responder but part of the city’s ongoing medical-social safety net.

Current response includes:
- Replacement of medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents
- Expanded food support beyond regular recipient lists (kitchen baseline: over 127,000 hot meals/year)
- Emergency hotline expanded from ~300 requests/day baseline to 24/6 emergency operations, covering urgent transport, kitchen loss, and child/family psychological support

Over the next 90 days, Ezrat Achim plans to strengthen five priority areas:
1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

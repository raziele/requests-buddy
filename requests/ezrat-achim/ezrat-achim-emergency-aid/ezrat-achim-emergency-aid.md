## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a 90-day emergency response following a missile strike in Beit Shemesh that killed residents, destroyed homes, and displaced hundreds of families. The organization is already providing replacement medical equipment, mobility aids, hospital transportation, essential household items, daily follow-up with displaced families, and trauma care. Funds would scale food distribution (normally 127,000 hot meals/year, now open to anyone in need), expand the 24/6 emergency hotline capacity (normally ~300 requests/day; one source also references 24/7 demand), and stabilize families over the next three months.

## Context & Background

On Sunday, March 2026, a missile struck a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." Lives were lost, homes destroyed, and families fled with almost nothing. Some lost loved ones; many remain in shock. Ezrat Achim, with Rabbi Avraham Kop (Founder and ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh) and its teams, responded immediately—on scene within minutes—alongside emergency and security forces, coordinating rescue efforts and providing immediate medical and logistical support.

Today the organization supports hundreds of families whose homes were damaged or made unlivable. Many are with relatives or in temporary housing. Many elderly and chronically ill residents lost access to medical equipment, electricity-dependent devices, or safe mobility. Ezrat Achim is providing replacement medical equipment and mobility aids, transportation to hospitals, essential household items and appliances, daily follow-up with displaced families, and emotional support and trauma care for children and parents. The kitchen, which normally prepares over 127,000 hot meals a year, has opened distribution beyond regular recipient lists to anyone in need (including elderly residents, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave their homes). The hotline, which usually handles about 300 requests per day, now runs 24 hours a day, six days a week in emergency mode—covering urgent medical transport, families without functioning kitchens, and parents seeking psychological support for children.

The crisis will not end when headlines fade. The requested $450,000 covers the next 90 days across five areas: replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies, expanding trauma and counseling services, increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity, scaling food distribution and essential aid, and supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families. Ezrat Achim operates as a local actor: not an outside relief agency but part of the city’s fabric, with **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and **27 equipment branches** in the city; when something happens, they step outside their doors rather than mobilizing from afar.

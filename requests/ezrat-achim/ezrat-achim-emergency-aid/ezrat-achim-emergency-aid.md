## The Ask

Ezrat Achim (Beit Shemesh’s embedded medical and social safety net) requests **$450,000 over 90 days** in the wake of a **missile strike** on a residential neighborhood in **Beit Shemesh** during **Operation "Shaagat HaAri"**. The strike killed residents, destroyed homes, and displaced hundreds of families who were forced to leave with almost nothing.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: **$120,000**
- Trauma and mental health services: **$110,000**
- Transportation & ambulance operations: **$85,000**
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: **$95,000**
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: **$40,000**

**Non-Financial Ask (source B):** Partnership and a brief call this week to share immediate plans and how to work together during this window.

## Context & Background

Since the beginning of Operation "Shaagat HaAri," life in Beit Shemesh changed overnight. On **Sunday**, a missile struck a residential neighborhood in the city. **Lives were lost, homes were destroyed,** and **families were forced to leave with almost nothing**. Some lost loved ones; many remain in shock.

Within minutes, Ezrat Achim teams were on the scene. **Rabbi Avraham Kop**—Founder of Ezrat Achim and **ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh**—arrived together with volunteers and medical teams and worked alongside **emergency and security forces** to coordinate rescue efforts and provide immediate medical and logistical support.

Today, Ezrat Achim supports **hundreds of families** whose homes were damaged or made unlivable:
- Some families are staying with relatives.
- Others are in temporary housing.
- Elderly residents and chronically ill patients lost access to basic medical equipment, **electricity-dependent devices**, or the ability to leave their homes safely.

Ezrat Achim emphasizes that it is **not an outside relief agency**—it is part of the city’s fabric. The organization has **1,400 volunteers** and **700 neighborhood representatives** living in Beit Shemesh, and it operates with **27 equipment branches**, stepping outside residents’ doors rather than mobilizing from afar.

## Current Response (What Ezrat Achim Is Doing Now)

Ezrat Achim teams are currently providing:
- Replacement medical equipment and **mobility aids**
- Transportation to hospitals and **ongoing treatments**
- Essential household items and **appliances**
- **Daily follow-up** with displaced families
- Emotional support and **trauma care** for children and parents

### Expanded Food Support
In normal times, Ezrat Achim’s kitchen prepares and distributes **over 127,000 hot meals a year**. Since the escalation, the kitchen has opened distribution beyond regular recipient lists so that **elderly residents, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave their homes** can receive meals. Meals are distributed **to anyone who needs them**.

### Emergency Hotline Operations
The hotline, which usually handles about **300 requests per day**, is now operating **24 hours a day, six days a week** in emergency mode. Calls range from urgent medical transport to families without functioning kitchens to parents requesting psychological support for their children.

## Next 90 Days (Stabilization Priorities)

The crisis is not over when headlines fade. Ezrat Achim will use the requested funding to strengthen five areas across the next three months:
1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day** emergency response after a missile strike in Beit Shemesh killed residents, destroyed homes, and displaced hundreds of families. The organization describes itself as Beit Shemesh’s embedded medical and social safety net: **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and **27 equipment branches**. Rabbi Avraham Kop is Founder and **ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh**; teams were on scene within minutes alongside emergency and security forces.

The organization is already providing replacement medical equipment and mobility aids, hospital transportation, essential household items and appliances, daily follow-up with displaced families, and trauma care. The kitchen, which normally prepares **over 127,000 hot meals a year**, has opened distribution beyond regular recipient lists. The hotline, which usually handles about **300 requests per day**, is running in emergency mode with sharply higher volume.

Funds would scale food distribution, expand hotline capacity, and stabilize families over the next three months across five areas (see **Funding Breakdown**). One source framed scaling as moving from “~300 [calls/day] to constant emergency demand” and referred to **24/7** hotline operations in the summary ask; detailed narratives in both sources describe the hotline as **24 hours a day, six days a week (24/6)** in emergency mode—**treat the 24/7 vs 24/6 wording as an inconsistency to confirm with the organization.**

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and a brief call this week to share immediate plans and coordinate during this window.

## Context & Background

On **Sunday** (during Operation **"Shaagat HaAri"**), a missile struck a **residential neighborhood** in Beit Shemesh. Lives were lost, homes were destroyed, and families fled with almost nothing; some lost loved ones, and many remained in shock. Ezrat Achim responded immediately with volunteers and medical teams, coordinating rescue efforts and providing immediate medical and logistical support.

Today the organization supports **hundreds of families** whose homes were damaged or made unlivable. Many are with relatives or in temporary housing. Elderly and chronically ill residents lost access to basic medical equipment, electricity-dependent devices, or safe mobility. Current operations include replacement equipment and mobility aids, transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments, essential household items, daily follow-up, and emotional support and trauma care for children and parents. Meal distribution is expanded: elderly residents, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave their homes are among those receiving support. Hotline needs cited include urgent medical transport, families without functioning kitchens, and parents seeking psychological support for children.

The crisis is expected to continue after headlines fade. Over the next 90 days the organization aims to strengthen the same five program areas reflected in the budget. The **$450,000** request is framed as essential to stabilize displaced families and ensure residents are not left to navigate the crisis alone. Ezrat Achim emphasizes that it is **not an outside relief agency** but **local infrastructure**—volunteers and neighborhood representatives live in the city and respond from their own communities rather than mobilizing from afar.

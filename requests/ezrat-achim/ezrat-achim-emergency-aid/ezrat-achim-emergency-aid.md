## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day emergency response** after a missile strike in a Beit Shemesh residential neighborhood during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." The strike caused fatalities, destroyed homes, and displaced hundreds of families. Ezrat Achim reports immediate on-scene response with volunteers and medical teams led by Rabbi Avraham Kop (also ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh), and is now scaling citywide support.

The organization describes itself as Beit Shemesh’s embedded safety net, with **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and (per one version) **27 equipment branches**.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and a brief coordination call during the immediate response window.

## Context & Background

A missile strike on Sunday in March 2026 hit a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh. Lives were lost; homes were destroyed; families fled with minimal belongings; some lost loved ones and many remain in shock.

Ezrat Achim reports that teams arrived within minutes and coordinated with emergency/security forces. Current response activities include:
- Replacing medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transporting patients to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Providing essential household items/appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families in temporary arrangements
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents

Food support has expanded beyond normal recipient lists. The kitchen typically prepares **127,000+ hot meals/year**, now broadened to anyone in need, including elderly residents, people with disabilities, and families unable or afraid to leave home.

Hotline operations are described as emergency round-the-clock capacity with high surge volume; one source states **24/6**, another states **24/7**. Typical baseline is about **300 requests/day**, now significantly higher and covering medical transport, non-functioning kitchens, and psychosocial support needs.

## The Next 90 Days (Program Priorities)

1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies  
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services  
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity  
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid  
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

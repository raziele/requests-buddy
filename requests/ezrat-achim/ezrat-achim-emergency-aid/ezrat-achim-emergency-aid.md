## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day** emergency response after a missile strike in a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri" (lives lost, homes destroyed, hundreds displaced). The organization describes itself as Beit Shemesh’s embedded medical and social safety net—not an outside relief agency—with **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and **27 equipment branches**; teams were on scene within minutes with Rabbi Avraham Kop (Founder & Chairman, ZAKA Commander for Beit Shemesh) alongside emergency and security forces.

Funding would **scale** ongoing work: replacement medical equipment and mobility aids, hospital transportation, essential household items and appliances, daily follow-up with displaced families, trauma and emotional support, expanded food from a kitchen that normally prepares **127,000+ hot meals/year** (now open beyond regular lists), and a high-volume emergency hotline (normally **~300 requests/day**).

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and a brief call this week to share immediate plans and coordinate during this window.

**Source variance — hotline hours:** One thread describes the hotline as **24 hours a day, six days a week (24/6)**; another summary uses **24/7** for scaled operations while the detailed narrative still states 24/6. Treat as **24/6** unless confirmed otherwise with the organization.

## Context & Background

On **Sunday** (during Operation "Shaagat HaAri"), a missile struck a residential area in Beit Shemesh. Families fled with little; some lost loved ones; many remain in shock. Ezrat Achim coordinated rescue and immediate medical and logistical support with volunteers and medical teams.

**Today:** support for hundreds of families in damaged or uninhabitable homes—many with relatives or in temporary housing. Elderly and chronically ill people lost equipment and electricity-dependent devices. The kitchen and hotline are under heavy demand (medical transport, families without working kitchens, psychological support for children). Over the **next 90 days**, the plan emphasizes five pillars: replace emergency medical equipment and supplies; expand trauma and counseling; increase hospital transport and ambulance capacity; scale food and essential aid; strengthen volunteer coordination and case management. The **$450,000** is framed as covering that window so needs do not drop off when headlines fade.

## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day emergency response** following a missile strike on a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." The strike caused fatalities, home destruction, and large-scale displacement. Ezrat Achim reports immediate on-scene response within minutes and ongoing citywide support for affected families.

The organization describes itself as Beit Shemesh’s embedded medical and social safety net, with:
- **1,400 volunteers**
- **700 neighborhood representatives**
- **27 equipment branches**

Requested funding is intended to stabilize families over three months by expanding emergency medical, psychosocial, transport, food, and logistics capacity.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:**
- Partnership and a brief call this week to discuss immediate plans and coordination.

## Context & Background

A missile strike in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri" resulted in loss of life, destroyed homes, and displacement of hundreds of families. Many affected residents are staying with relatives or in temporary housing; some lost loved ones and remain in shock.

Ezrat Achim reports active support including:
- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents

Food and hotline operations have expanded significantly:
- Kitchen normally distributes **127,000+ hot meals/year**, now open beyond regular recipient lists to anyone in need.
- Hotline normally handles **~300 requests/day**, now operating in emergency mode with sharply increased demand covering medical transport, households without functioning kitchens, and parental requests for psychological support.

Over the next 90 days, Ezrat Achim plans to strengthen:
1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day** emergency response after a missile strike in a residential neighborhood in **Beit Shemesh** during Operation **"Shaagat HaAri"** (Sunday, March 2026 — described as “this past Sunday” in the letter). The strike killed residents, destroyed homes, and displaced hundreds of families; the organization responded immediately with Rabbi Avraham Kop (Founder and **ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh**) and teams alongside emergency and security forces.

Ezrat Achim describes itself as **Beit Shemesh’s embedded medical and social safety net**, not an outside agency: **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and **27 equipment branches** (per second thread). It is already providing replacement medical equipment and mobility aids, hospital transportation, essential household items and appliances, daily follow-up with displaced families, trauma and emotional support, expanded food distribution (kitchen normally **127,000+ hot meals/year**, now open beyond regular lists), and an emergency hotline that usually handles **~300 requests/day**.

Funds would scale operations over the next three months across the same five areas summarized in both threads (see budget below).

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**

**Non-financial ask (second thread):** Partnership and a **brief call this week** to align on immediate plans and collaboration during this window.

## Context & Background

**Event:** A missile struck a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh. Lives were lost, homes destroyed, families fled with almost nothing; some lost loved ones and many remained in shock.

**Response:** Teams were on scene **within minutes** with volunteers and medical staff, coordinating rescue and immediate medical and logistical support.

**Current needs:** Hundreds of families in damaged or unlivable housing—some with relatives, others in temporary housing. Elderly and chronically ill people lost access to equipment and electricity-dependent devices. Hotline demand spans urgent medical transport, families without functioning kitchens, and parents seeking psychological support for children; meal demand includes elderly, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave home—**“we are simply saying yes and cooking more.”**

**90-day priorities (five areas):**

1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies  
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services  
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity  
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid  
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families  

Framing: the crisis continues after headlines fade; the **$450,000** is positioned as essential so **no one in Beit Shemesh navigates the crisis alone**.

### Beit Shemesh Emergency Appeal–Ezrat Achim 2026.pdf

**Letter date:** March 3rd, 2026  

**Opening context:** Since the beginning of Operation "Shaagat HaAri," life in Beit Shemesh changed overnight. This past Sunday, a missile struck a residential neighborhood; lives were lost and homes destroyed. Families left with almost nothing—some lost loved ones; many still in shock.

**Immediate response:** Within minutes, Ezrat Achim was on scene. Rabbi Avraham Kop (Founder; ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh) arrived with volunteers and medical teams, working with emergency and security forces on rescue coordination and immediate medical and logistical support—the hours after the strike were only the beginning.

#### What we are facing now

Hundreds of families in damaged or unlivable homes; some with relatives, others in temporary housing. Many elderly and chronically ill people lost basic medical equipment, electricity-dependent devices, or safe ability to leave home.

**Current services:**

- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids  
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments  
- Essential household items and appliances  
- Daily follow-up with displaced families  
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents  

#### Expanding food support

Normal operations: **127,000+** hot meals/year. Since escalation, distribution is open beyond regular lists; elderly, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave home call daily—meals go to anyone who needs them.

#### Around-the-clock emergency operations

Hotline normally **~300 requests/day**; now **24/6** emergency mode with sharply higher volume (medical transport, families without kitchens, psychological support for children).

#### The next 90 days

Stabilizing families takes time. Five strengthened areas (as listed above under Context).

#### Emergency 90-Day Budget: $450,000

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Medical equipment & emergency supplies | $120,000 |
| Trauma and mental health services | $110,000 |
| Transportation & ambulance operations | $85,000 |
| Food distribution & essential family assistance | $95,000 |
| Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics | $40,000 |

**Local footprint:** 1,400 volunteers and 700 neighborhood representatives living in the city—response is from neighbors stepping outside their doors, not distant mobilization.

**Closing ask:** Stand with Beit Shemesh; partnership stabilizes displaced families, supports elderly residents, and sustains medical care and food access.

**Sign-off:** Rabbi Avraham Kop, Founder & Chairman, Ezrat Achim.

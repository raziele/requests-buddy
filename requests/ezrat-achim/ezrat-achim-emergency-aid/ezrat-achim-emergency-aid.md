## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day** emergency response after a missile strike in a residential neighborhood in **Beit Shemesh** during Operation **"Shaagat HaAri"** (lives lost, homes destroyed, hundreds displaced). The organization describes itself as Beit Shemesh’s embedded medical and social safety net — **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and **27 equipment branches** (per one thread). Rabbi Avraham Kop (Founder and ZAKA Commander for Beit Shemesh) and teams responded **immediately / within minutes** alongside emergency and security forces.

**Current operations (already underway):** replacement medical equipment and mobility aids; hospital transportation and ongoing treatments; essential household items and appliances; daily follow-up with displaced families; emotional support and trauma care for children and parents. The kitchen, which normally prepares **over 127,000 hot meals per year**, has opened distribution **beyond regular recipient lists** to anyone in need (including elderly, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave home). The emergency hotline, usually about **300 requests per day**, is running in expanded emergency mode with sharply higher volume — **24 hours a day, six days a week** per both detailed accounts. *(One outreach email also phrases hotline scaling as “24/7”; the detailed descriptions consistently say six days per week.)*

**Use of funds (90 days):** stabilize displaced families and scale the five priority areas below.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**

| Area | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Medical equipment & emergency supplies | $120,000 |
| Trauma and mental health services | $110,000 |
| Transportation & ambulance operations | $85,000 |
| Food distribution & essential family assistance | $95,000 |
| Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics | $40,000 |

**Non-financial ask (one thread):** Partnership and a **brief call this week** to share immediate plans and coordinate during this window.

## Context & Background

**Incident:** On **Sunday** (during Operation "Shaagat HaAri"), a missile struck a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh. Lives were lost, homes destroyed, and families fled with almost nothing; some lost loved ones and many remain in shock.

**Ezrat Achim’s role:** Framed as **local permanent infrastructure**, not an outside relief agency — volunteers and neighborhood representatives **live in the city** and respond by stepping outside their doors rather than mobilizing from afar. Support today includes hundreds of families in damaged or unlivable housing, many staying with relatives or in temporary housing. Elderly and chronically ill residents lost access to basic medical equipment, electricity-dependent devices, and safe mobility. Hotline needs cited include urgent medical transport, families without functioning kitchens, and parents seeking psychological support for children.

**90-day priorities (five areas):**

1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies  
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services  
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity  
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid  
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families  

The **$450,000** request is described as covering stabilization through the next three months so needs are met after headlines fade — ensuring families are not left to navigate the crisis alone.

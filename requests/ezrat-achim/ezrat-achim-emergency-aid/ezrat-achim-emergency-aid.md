## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day emergency response** after a missile strike in a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation **"Shaagat HaAri"**—lives lost, homes destroyed, and hundreds of families displaced. The organization describes itself as Beit Shemesh’s **embedded medical and social safety net** (not an outside relief agency), with **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and **27 equipment branches**. Teams—including under **Rabbi Avraham Kop**—were on scene **within minutes** with volunteers and medical teams, coordinating with emergency and security forces.

**Current / ongoing operations (already underway):** replacement medical equipment and mobility aids; hospital transportation and ongoing treatments; essential household items and appliances; daily follow-up with displaced families; emotional support and trauma care for children and parents; expanded food support (kitchen normally **127,000+ hot meals/year**, now opened beyond regular recipient lists); and an emergency hotline handling sharply higher volume.

**What funding scales over the next 90 days:** food distribution; hotline capacity; stabilization and case management for displaced families; trauma services; medical transport/ambulance operations; volunteer coordination and logistics.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and a **brief call this week** to share immediate plans and coordinate collaboration during this window.

## Context & Background

**Incident & immediate response:** On **Sunday** (during Operation "Shaagat HaAri"), a missile struck a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh. Families fled with almost nothing; some lost loved ones; many remain in shock. Ezrat Achim emphasizes **local permanence**: volunteers and neighborhood representatives **live in the city**—they **step outside their doors** rather than mobilizing from afar.

**Present needs:** Hundreds of families in damaged/unlivable housing—some with relatives, others in temporary housing. Practical hotline needs called out include urgent medical transport, families without functioning kitchens, and parents seeking psychological support for children.

**Framing:** The crisis continues after headlines fade; the $450,000 is positioned as essential so **no one in Beit Shemesh navigates the crisis alone**, covering five coordinated pillars through equipment replacement, trauma expansion, transport/ambulance scaling, food/essential aid, and volunteer coordination/case management.

### Beit Shemesh Emergency Appeal–Ezrat Achim 2026.pdf

**Date:** March 3rd, 2026

**Letter framing (condensed):** Since the beginning of Operation "Shaagat HaAri," life in Beit Shemesh changed overnight. A missile struck a residential neighborhood; lives were lost; homes destroyed; families left with almost nothing; some lost loved ones; many still in shock.

**On-scene response:** Within minutes, Ezrat Achim teams were on scene. **Rabbi Avraham Kop**, Founder of Ezrat Achim and **ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh**, arrived with volunteers and medical teams, working alongside emergency/security forces on rescue coordination plus immediate medical and logistical support—positioned as only the beginning of sustained need.

#### What We Are Facing Now

Hundreds of families in damaged/unlivable homes; some with relatives; others in temporary housing. Many elderly/chronically ill residents lost access to basic medical equipment, electricity-dependent devices, or safe ability to leave home.

**Teams are currently providing:**
- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents

#### Expanding Food Support For Anyone Who Needs It

Normal baseline: **127,000+ hot meals/year**. Since escalation: distribution opened beyond regular lists; elderly, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave home call daily; meals go to anyone who needs them.

#### Around-the-Clock Emergency Operations

Baseline hotline: ~**300 requests/day**. Emergency mode: **24 hours/day, 6 days/week** with sharply increased volume—calls spanning urgent medical transport, families without functioning kitchens, and parents seeking psychological support for children (practical, immediate, ongoing).

#### The Next 90 Days (five areas)

1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies  
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services  
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity  
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid  
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

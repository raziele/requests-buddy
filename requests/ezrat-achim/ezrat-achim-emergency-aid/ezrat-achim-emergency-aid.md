## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day** emergency response after a missile strike on a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." The strike caused deaths, destroyed homes, and displaced hundreds of families, many now in temporary arrangements and ongoing shock.

Ezrat Achim reports it is already operating as the city’s embedded emergency and social infrastructure (not an external relief actor), with teams deployed within minutes alongside emergency/security forces under Rabbi Avraham Kop (also ZAKA Commander, Beit Shemesh). Funding is intended to stabilize families over the next three months while maintaining and expanding urgent services.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask (from one source):**
- Partnership and a brief call this week to coordinate immediate plans and collaboration during the response window.

## Context & Background

Since the onset of Operation "Shaagat HaAri," life in Beit Shemesh has shifted sharply. On Sunday (date described variably in source records), a missile struck a residential area. Ezrat Achim states that within minutes, Rabbi Avraham Kop and volunteer/medical teams were on scene, coordinating rescue and immediate support with emergency and security forces.

Current response activities include:
- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents

Operational pressure has increased significantly:
- **Food support:** Kitchen capacity normally exceeds **127,000 hot meals/year**; distribution expanded beyond regular recipient lists to anyone in need.
- **Hotline:** Typically about **300 requests/day**; now operating **24 hours/day, 6 days/week** in emergency mode (one source describes this as scaling from normal demand to constant emergency demand, with around-the-clock response language).

Organizational capacity and local footprint:
- **1,400 volunteers**
- **700 neighborhood representatives**
- **27 equipment branches** (reported in one source)

## Next 90 Days — Priority Areas

1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies  
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services  
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity  
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid  
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

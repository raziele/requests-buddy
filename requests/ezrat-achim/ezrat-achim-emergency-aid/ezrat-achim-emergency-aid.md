## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day** emergency response after a missile strike on a **Sunday** in **March 2026** hit a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation **"Shaagat HaAri"** (lives lost, homes destroyed, hundreds displaced). The organization describes itself as Beit Shemesh’s **embedded medical and social safety net** (not an outside relief agency): **~1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and **27 equipment branches** (per second source). Rabbi Avraham Kop (Founder; ZAKA Commander, Beit Shemesh) and teams were on scene **within minutes** with volunteers and medical teams, coordinating with emergency and security forces.

**Current / ongoing operations (both sources align):** replacement medical equipment and mobility aids; hospital transportation and ongoing treatments; essential household items and appliances; daily follow-up with displaced families; emotional support and trauma care for children and parents; expanded food from a kitchen that normally prepares **>127,000 hot meals/year**, now opened **beyond regular recipient lists**; emergency hotline operating **24 hours a day, six days a week** (second source also characterizes scaling as moving toward **24/7**-level demand—**note wording difference: 24/6 vs 24/7**). Hotline normally **~300 requests/day**; volume and scope now include urgent medical transport, families without functioning kitchens, and psychological support for children.

Funds would **scale and stabilize** over three months across the same five areas summarized in both documents (equipment/supplies, trauma services, transport/ambulance, food/essential aid, volunteer coordination/case management).

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown (identical in both sources):**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask (second source only):** Partnership and a **brief call this week** to share immediate plans and coordinate during this window.

## Context & Background

The strike changed life in the city overnight: displacement (some with relatives, others in temporary housing), shock and grief (some lost loved ones), and acute needs among elderly and chronically ill residents. Ezrat Achim emphasizes **local presence**—volunteers and neighborhood representatives **live in the city** and respond by stepping outside their doors rather than mobilizing from afar.

Both sources stress that the crisis continues after headlines fade; the **$450,000** frame is presented as essential so **no one in Beit Shemesh navigates the crisis alone**, covering the next 90 days across the five program pillars above.

## Extracted Data

### Beit Shemesh Emergency Appeal–Ezrat Achim 2026.pdf

**Date on letter:** March 3rd, 2026

**Opening narrative (longer extract in second intake):** Since the beginning of Operation "Shaagat HaAri," life in Beit Shemesh changed overnight. This past Sunday, a missile struck a residential neighborhood; lives were lost and homes destroyed; families left with almost nothing—some lost loved ones; many remain in shock.

**Immediate response:** Within minutes, Ezrat Achim teams were on scene. Rabbi Avraham Kop, Founder of Ezrat Achim and ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh, arrived with volunteers and medical teams, working alongside emergency and security forces on rescue coordination and immediate medical/logistical support—but the hours after the strike were only the beginning.

**What they are facing now (combined):** Hundreds of families in damaged/unlivable homes; elderly and chronically ill residents who lost basic medical equipment, electricity-dependent devices, or safe ability to leave home.

**Current service list (aligned across extracts):**
- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents

**Food expansion detail (second extract adds):** Distribution opened beyond regular lists; elderly, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave home call daily; organization describes saying **yes** and cooking more—meals to **anyone who needs them**.

**Hotline detail (second extract adds call examples):** Urgent medical transport; families without functioning kitchens; parents seeking psychological support for children—needs described as practical, immediate, ongoing, with sharply increased volume.

**Next 90 days — five areas (numbered in both):**
1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies  
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services  
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity  
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid  
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families  

**Emergency 90-Day Budget: $450,000**

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Medical equipment & emergency supplies | $120,000 |
| Trauma and mental health services | $110,000 |
| Transportation & ambulance operations | $85,000 |
| Food distribution & essential family assistance | $95,000 |
| Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics | $40,000 |

**Organizational framing in letter:** Not an outside relief agency—part of the city’s fabric; **1,400 volunteers** and **700 neighborhood representatives** live locally; when something happens they step outside their doors.

**Closing appeal themes:** Stand with Beit Shemesh; partnership goes to stabilizing displaced families, supporting elderly residents, and sustaining access to medical care and food.

**Sign-off:** Rabbi Avraham Kop, Founder & Chairman, Ezrat Achim.

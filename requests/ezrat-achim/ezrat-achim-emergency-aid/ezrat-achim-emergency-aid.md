## The Ask

Ezrat Achim (Beit Shemesh), a local embedded medical and social safety net with **1,400 volunteers** and **27 equipment branches**, is requesting **$450,000** over **90 days** in the wake of a missile strike that hit a residential neighborhood on Sunday during Operation **"Shaagat HaAri."** The organization was on scene within minutes under **Rabbi Avraham Kop** (also ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh), working alongside emergency and security forces.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and a brief call this week to share immediate plans and how to work together during this window.

## Context & Background

This past Sunday, a missile struck a residential neighborhood in **Beit Shemesh** during Operation **"Shaagat HaAri."** Lives were lost and homes were destroyed; families were forced to leave with almost nothing. Some lost loved ones, while many remain in shock.

Within minutes, Ezrat Achim teams arrived with Rabbi Avraham Kop—Founder of Ezrat Achim and ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh—alongside volunteers and medical teams. They worked alongside emergency and security forces to coordinate rescue efforts and provide immediate medical and logistical support. The hours after the strike were only the beginning.

Today, Ezrat Achim is supporting **hundreds of families** whose homes were damaged or made unlivable:
- Many are staying with relatives.
- Others are in temporary housing.
- Elderly residents and chronically ill patients have suddenly lost access to basic medical equipment, electricity-dependent devices, and the ability to leave their homes safely.

### Ongoing Services Being Provided
- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents

### Food Support Expanded
In normal times, the organization’s kitchen prepares and distributes **over 127,000 hot meals per year**. Since the escalation, the kitchen opened distribution beyond regular recipient lists. Elderly residents, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave their homes are calling daily. The approach is to “simply say yes” and cook more—meals are going out to anyone who needs them.

### Emergency Hotline Operating in Crisis Mode
The hotline, which usually handles about **300 requests per day**, is now operating in emergency mode **24 hours a day, six days a week** (described in some communications as “24/7” during the window due to constant urgent demand). Calls range from urgent medical transport to families with no functioning kitchens and parents seeking psychological support for their children. Volume has increased sharply, and needs are practical, immediate, and ongoing.

## Next 90 Days — Program Areas

Over the next three months, Ezrat Achim will strengthen **five areas** to ensure families are stabilized and not left to navigate the crisis alone:

1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

## Appeal Letter (Extracted Draft)

Dear Friends,

Since the beginning of Operation "Shaagat HaAri," life in Beit Shemesh has changed overnight.

This past Sunday, a missile struck a residential neighborhood in our city. Lives were lost, and homes were destroyed. Families were forced to leave with almost nothing. Some lost loved ones. Many are still in shock.

Within minutes, Ezrat Achim teams were on the scene. Rabbi Avraham Kop, Founder of Ezrat Achim and ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh, arrived together with our volunteers and medical teams. We worked alongside emergency and security forces, helping coordinate rescue efforts and providing immediate medical and logistical support. But the hours after the strike were only the beginning.

### What We Are Facing Now

Today, we are supporting hundreds of families whose homes were damaged or made unlivable. Some are staying with relatives. Others are in temporary housing. Many elderly residents and chronically ill patients suddenly lost access to basic medical equipment, electricity-dependent devices, or the ability to leave their homes safely.

Our teams are currently providing:

- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents

### Expanding Food Support For Anyone Who Needs It

In normal times, our kitchen prepares and distributes over 127,000 hot meals a year. Since the escalation, we have opened meal distribution beyond our regular recipient lists. Elderly residents, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave their homes are calling daily. We are simply saying yes and cooking more. Meals are going out to anyone who needs them.

### Around-the-Clock Emergency Operations

Our hotline, which usually handles about 300 requests a day, is now operating 24 hours a day, six days a week in emergency mode. Calls range from urgent medical transport to families who no longer have functioning kitchens to parents asking for psychological support for their children. The volume has increased sharply. The needs are practical, immediate, and ongoing.

### The Next 90 Days

The crisis is not over once the headlines fade. Stabilizing families takes time. Over the next three months, we are strengthening five areas:

1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

Ezrat Achim is not an outside relief agency. We are part of the fabric of this city. Our 1,400 volunteers and 700 neighborhood representatives live here. When something happens, we do not mobilize from afar; we step outside our doors. Right now, families who never imagined needing help are depending on us.

If you can stand with Beit Shemesh at this moment, your partnership will go directly toward stabilizing families who lost their homes, supporting elderly residents, and ensuring continued access to medical care and food.

Thank you for your trust.

With appreciation,

## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day emergency response** after a missile strike in a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." The strike caused fatalities, destroyed homes, and displaced hundreds of families. Ezrat Achim teams, led by Rabbi Avraham Kop (also ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh), responded within minutes and continue to provide ongoing medical, logistical, and psychosocial support.

The funding is intended to stabilize families over the next three months by replacing critical medical equipment, expanding trauma services, increasing transport and ambulance capacity, scaling food and essential aid, and sustaining volunteer/case-management operations.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask (from one source):**
- Partnership and a brief call this week to align immediate plans and collaboration during the emergency window.

## Context & Background

Since the escalation of Operation "Shaagat HaAri," Beit Shemesh has faced severe civilian impact. A missile strike on Sunday in March 2026 hit a residential area, leaving fatalities, major housing damage, and widespread displacement. Families fled with minimal belongings; some lost loved ones and many remain in shock.

Ezrat Achim describes itself as embedded local infrastructure rather than an external responder. Its network includes **1,400 volunteers** and **700 neighborhood representatives** (with one source additionally noting **27 equipment branches**). Teams were deployed immediately with emergency and security forces to support rescue, triage, and logistics.

Current response activities include:
- Replacement of medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents
- Expanded food support beyond regular lists (kitchen normally distributes over **127,000 hot meals/year**)
- Emergency hotline operations at **24 hours/day, 6 days/week** (baseline volume ~300 requests/day, now significantly increased)

The central operational concern across both documents is that needs remain practical, immediate, and ongoing after initial media attention fades; the 90-day plan is framed as a stabilization period for families in temporary housing and disrupted care situations.

## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day emergency response** after a missile strike on a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." The strike caused deaths, severe home damage, and large-scale displacement.

Ezrat Achim describes itself as Beit Shemesh’s embedded medical and social safety net, with **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and **27 equipment branches**. Teams led by Rabbi Avraham Kop were on scene within minutes, working alongside emergency and security forces.

Requested funding will stabilize displaced families and expand ongoing emergency operations, including:
- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents
- Expanded food distribution (kitchen normally prepares 127,000+ hot meals/year)
- Emergency hotline operations at continuous surge capacity

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and a brief coordination call during the current emergency window.

## Context & Background

Since the escalation under Operation "Shaagat HaAri," life in Beit Shemesh changed overnight. A missile struck a residential area on Sunday, causing fatalities, destruction of homes, and displacement of families, including elderly residents and young children. Many families left with almost nothing; some lost loved ones and remain in shock.

Ezrat Achim reports immediate and ongoing response activity:
- On-site rescue/logistics support within minutes of impact
- Medical and mobility equipment replacement for vulnerable residents
- Hospital transportation and ambulance support
- Distribution of essential household items
- Daily family case follow-up
- Trauma and emotional care for children and parents
- Expanded meal delivery to anyone in need, beyond regular recipient lists

Operationally, demand has sharply increased:
- Kitchen output expanded beyond baseline annual volume (127,000+ meals/year)
- Hotline increased from ~300 requests/day to emergency mode operation (reported as 24/6; one summary references 24/7 scale-up intent)

Ezrat Achim states the crisis extends beyond immediate headlines and frames the next three months as critical for family stabilization and continuity of care.

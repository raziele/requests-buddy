## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day emergency response** after a missile strike in a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." The strike caused fatalities, severe housing damage, and large-scale displacement.

The organization reports it was on scene within minutes, working with emergency and security forces under Rabbi Avraham Kop (also ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh). As the city’s embedded medical and social safety net, Ezrat Achim is providing immediate and ongoing support to families now in temporary housing or with relatives, including many elderly and chronically ill residents who lost access to essential medical devices.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:**
- Partnership and a brief coordination call during the current emergency window.

## Context & Background

On Sunday in early March 2026, a missile struck a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh. Ezrat Achim states that lives were lost, homes were destroyed, and hundreds of families were displaced, many leaving with almost nothing.

Current response activities include:
- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents

Food and hotline operations have been expanded:
- Kitchen output normally exceeds 127,000 hot meals per year; distribution now extends beyond regular recipient lists to anyone in need.
- Hotline normally handles ~300 requests/day; now operating 24 hours/day, 6 days/week in emergency mode for urgent medical transport, non-functioning home kitchens, and psychological support referrals.

For the next 90 days, Ezrat Achim is prioritizing:
1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

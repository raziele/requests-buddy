## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day** emergency response after a missile strike on a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." The strike caused fatalities, destroyed homes, and displaced hundreds of families, including elderly residents and young children.

Ezrat Achim states it responded within minutes alongside emergency and security forces and is functioning as the city's embedded medical and social safety net (including **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and **27 equipment branches**).

Current and scaled emergency operations include:
- Replacing medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents
- Expanded food distribution beyond regular recipient lists (kitchen normally provides 127,000+ hot meals/year)
- Emergency hotline operations (described as 24/6 in one source; also described as scaling to 24/7/constant emergency demand)

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and a brief call this week to align immediate plans and collaboration during this response window.

## Context & Background

Since the escalation in Operation "Shaagat HaAri," life in Beit Shemesh changed overnight. A missile struck a residential neighborhood on Sunday, resulting in loss of life, destroyed homes, and widespread displacement. Families were forced to leave quickly, often with almost nothing; some lost loved ones, and many remain in shock.

Ezrat Achim emphasizes that it is local permanent infrastructure, not an external relief agency. Rabbi Avraham Kop and teams reportedly arrived within minutes and coordinated with emergency/security services on rescue, immediate medical support, and logistics.

The organization reports ongoing support for displaced families in temporary housing or with relatives, with specific strain among elderly and chronically ill residents who lost access to critical equipment and safe mobility. Hotline requests include urgent transport, non-functioning kitchens, and child psychological support; demand has risen sharply.

Over the next 90 days, Ezrat Achim plans to strengthen:
1. Emergency medical equipment and supply replacement
2. Trauma and counseling services
3. Hospital transportation and ambulance capacity
4. Food distribution and essential aid
5. Volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

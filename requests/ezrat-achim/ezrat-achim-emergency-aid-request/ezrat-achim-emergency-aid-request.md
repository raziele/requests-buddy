## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000** for a **90-day emergency response** following a missile strike on a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." The strike caused fatalities, destruction of homes, and displacement of hundreds of families.

Ezrat Achim reports it responded within minutes alongside emergency and security forces and is currently providing:
- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatment
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents
- Expanded food distribution (kitchen normally distributes 127,000+ hot meals annually; currently open to anyone in need)
- Emergency hotline operations (normally ~300 requests/day; now operating 24/6 in emergency mode)

The organization frames itself as Beit Shemesh’s embedded response infrastructure, with **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and mention of **27 equipment branches**.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:**
- Partnership support and a brief call this week to coordinate immediate plans during the emergency window.

## Context & Background

Since the start of Operation "Shaagat HaAri," Ezrat Achim describes conditions in Beit Shemesh as having changed overnight. A missile strike on Sunday hit a residential area, with reported deaths, destroyed homes, and widespread displacement. Some families lost loved ones; many remain in shock. Residents are staying with relatives or in temporary housing.

Key pressure points identified:
- Elderly and chronically ill residents lost access to basic and electricity-dependent medical devices.
- Families need immediate practical support (food, household essentials, transport, and case follow-up).
- Parents are seeking psychological support for children, with trauma needs rising.
- Demand volume has sharply increased across hotline and field operations.

Ezrat Achim emphasizes that it is not an external responder but a local network whose personnel live in the city and can respond immediately. The request focuses on stabilizing affected families over the next three months and ensuring continuity of care, food access, and emergency logistics.

## Program Plan for the Next 90 Days

1. Replace emergency medical equipment and supplies  
2. Expand trauma and counseling services  
3. Increase hospital transportation and ambulance capacity  
4. Scale food distribution and essential aid  
5. Support volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

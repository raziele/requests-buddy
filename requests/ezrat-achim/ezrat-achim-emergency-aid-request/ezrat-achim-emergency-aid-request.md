## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests **$450,000 for a 90-day emergency response** after a missile strike in a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." Lives were lost, homes were destroyed, and hundreds of families were displaced.

Ezrat Achim reports it responded within minutes alongside emergency and security forces and is now providing:
- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatment
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents
- Expanded food distribution (normally 127,000+ hot meals/year, now open to anyone in need)
- Emergency hotline operations (reported as 24/6 in core narrative; one forwarded summary references 24/7 scaling)

Ezrat Achim describes itself as Beit Shemesh’s embedded safety net with **1,400 volunteers**, **700 neighborhood representatives**, and (per one version) **27 equipment branches**.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and a brief call this week to align on immediate plans and collaboration.

## Context & Background

Since the beginning of Operation "Shaagat HaAri," conditions in Beit Shemesh have deteriorated sharply. Following the strike, families were forced to leave with almost nothing; some lost loved ones and many remain in shock. Ezrat Achim states that this is not a temporary external intervention but local civic infrastructure already embedded in the city.

Current pressures highlighted across both submissions:
- Ongoing displacement into relatives’ homes and temporary housing
- Elderly and chronically ill residents losing access to electricity-dependent devices and basic medical equipment
- Increased demand for meals among elderly residents, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave home
- High and sustained hotline volume for urgent transport, household disruption, and psychological support

The organization frames the **next 90 days** as critical for stabilization in five workstreams:
1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

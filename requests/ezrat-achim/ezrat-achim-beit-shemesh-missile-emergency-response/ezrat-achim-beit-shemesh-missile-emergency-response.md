## The Ask

Ezrat Achim requests $450,000 for a 90-day emergency response following a missile strike in Beit Shemesh that killed residents, destroyed homes, and displaced hundreds of families. The organization is already providing replacement medical equipment, mobility aids, hospital transportation, essential household items, daily follow-up with displaced families, and trauma care. Funds would scale food distribution (normally 127,000 hot meals/year, now open to anyone in need), expand the 24/6 emergency hotline capacity, and stabilize families over the next three months.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

## Context & Background

On Sunday, March 2026, a missile struck a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." Lives were lost, homes destroyed, and families fled with almost nothing. Ezrat Achim, with Rabbi Avraham Kop (also ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh) and its teams, responded immediately alongside emergency and security forces.

Today the organization supports hundreds of families whose homes were damaged or made unlivable. Many elderly and chronically ill residents lost access to medical equipment, electricity-dependent devices, or safe mobility. Ezrat Achim is providing replacement medical equipment and mobility aids, transportation to hospitals, essential household items and appliances, daily follow-up with displaced families, and emotional support and trauma care for children and parents. The kitchen, which normally prepares over 127,000 hot meals a year, has opened distribution beyond regular recipient lists. The hotline, which usually handles about 300 requests per day, now runs 24 hours a day, six days a week in emergency mode.

The crisis will not end when headlines fade. The requested $450,000 covers the next 90 days across five areas: replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies, expanding trauma and counseling services, increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity, scaling food distribution and essential aid, and supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families. Ezrat Achim operates as a local actor with 1,400 volunteers and 700 neighborhood representatives in the city.

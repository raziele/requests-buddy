The organization, which is the city's permanent medical and social safety net with 1,400 volunteers and 27 equipment branches, was on the scene within minutes. ## Context & Background

On the Sunday preceding the email, a missile strike hit a residential neighborhood in Beit Shemesh during Operation "Shaagat HaAri." Lives were lost, homes were destroyed, and hundreds of families were displaced—many elderly and young children. Ezrat Achim, whose founder Rabbi Avraham Kop serves as ZAKA Commander for Beit Shemesh, mobilized immediately with 1,400 volunteers and medical teams. They are not an outside relief agency but the city's embedded medical and social safety net, with 27 equipment branches and an ambulance fleet.

In the days after the strike, hundreds of families remain in temporary housing or with relatives; elderly and chronically ill residents lost electricity-dependent medical equipment and mobility aids in the debris. The organization's kitchen, which normally prepares over 127,000 hot meals a year, has opened distribution beyond regular recipient lists. The hotline has shifted from about 300 requests per day to 24/7 emergency mode, handling urgent medical transport, food needs, and requests for psychological support for children.

Ezrat Achim is seeking partnership to secure $450,000 over 90 days to replace emergency medical equipment, expand trauma and counseling services, increase hospital transportation and ambulance capacity, scale food distribution, and support volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families. The contact has asked for a brief call this week to discuss how to work together during this window.

### Beit Shemesh Emergency Appeal–Ezrat Achim 2026.pdf

**March 3rd, 2026**

Dear Friends,

Since the beginning of Operation "Shaagat HaAri," life in Beit Shemesh has changed overnight.

This past Sunday, a missile struck a residential neighborhood in our city. Lives were lost, and homes were destroyed. Families were forced to leave with almost nothing. Some lost loved ones. Many are still in shock.

Within minutes, Ezrat Achim teams were on the scene. Rabbi Avraham Kop, Founder of Ezrat Achim and ZAKA Commander of Beit Shemesh, arrived together with our volunteers and medical teams. We worked alongside emergency and security forces, helping coordinate rescue efforts and providing immediate medical and logistical support.

But the hours after the strike were only the beginning.

**What We Are Facing Now**

Today, we are supporting hundreds of families whose homes were damaged or made unlivable. Some are staying with relatives. Others are in temporary housing. Many elderly residents and chronically ill patients suddenly lost access to basic medical equipment, electricity-dependent devices, or the ability to leave their homes safely.

Our teams are currently providing:

- Replacement medical equipment and mobility aids
- Transportation to hospitals and ongoing treatments
- Essential household items and appliances
- Daily follow-up with displaced families
- Emotional support and trauma care for children and parents

**Expanding Food Support For Anyone Who Needs It**

In normal times, our kitchen prepares and distributes over 127,000 hot meals a year. Since the escalation, we have opened meal distribution beyond our regular recipient lists. Elderly residents, people with disabilities, and families afraid to leave their homes are calling daily. We are simply saying yes and cooking more. Meals are going out to anyone who needs them.

**Around-the-Clock Emergency Operations**

Our hotline, which usually handles about 300 requests a day, is now operating 24 hours a day, six days a week in emergency mode. Calls range from urgent medical transport to families who no longer have functioning kitchens to parents asking for psychological support for their children. The volume has increased sharply. The needs are practical, immediate, and ongoing.

**The Next 90 Days**

The crisis is not over once the headlines fade. Stabilizing families takes time. Over the next three months, we are strengthening five areas:

1. Replacing emergency medical equipment and supplies
2. Expanding trauma and counseling services
3. Increasing hospital transportation and ambulance capacity
4. Scaling food distribution and essential aid
5. Supporting volunteer coordination and case management for displaced families

Ezrat Achim is not an outside relief agency. We are part of the fabric of this city. Our 1,400 volunteers and 700 neighborhood representatives live here. When something happens, we do not mobilize from afar; we step outside our doors.

Right now, families who never imagined needing help are depending on us. If you can stand with Beit Shemesh at this moment, your partnership will go directly toward stabilizing families who lost their homes, supporting elderly residents, and ensuring continued access to medical care and food.

Thank you for your trust.

With appreciation,

Rabbi Avraham Kop
Founder & Chairman

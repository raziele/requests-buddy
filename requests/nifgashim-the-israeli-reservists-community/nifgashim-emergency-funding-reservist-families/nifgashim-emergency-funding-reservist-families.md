# Emergency Support Request: Operation "Lion's Roar"

**Project Goal:** Strengthening the families of IDF reservists.

**Executing Body:** Nifgashim Movement, Israel's Reservist Community.

## Background

We are at a historic crossroads in the war to ensure the existence and future of the State of Israel and the Jewish People. As part of Operation "Lion's Roar" (Iran 2), over 110,000 reservists were called up in a sudden emergency mobilization, alongside tens of thousands more on standby, living in continuous uncertainty.

Beyond the confrontation with Iran, the risk of escalation on additional fronts remains immediate. For many reservists, this is not a short deployment. Israel has been at war for over two years, and many have already served more than 300 days in active duty, sacrificing livelihoods and family stability and are now being called once again. Military assessments indicate that this operation is expected to last several weeks, potentially a month of sustained intensity. Behind every soldier stands a family navigating sirens, shelter runs, financial strain, and prolonged emotional pressure.

This emergency mobilization coincides with the Purim and Passover holidays, an especially sensitive period for families. The economic toll is significant, particularly among self-employed reservists and small business owners. Israel's national resilience depends directly on the emotional, domestic, and financial stability of these families.

We owe it to them, as a duty, an honor, and a shared responsibility, to stand by their side in every possible way.

With the launch of Operation "Lion's Roar," Nifgashim immediately shifted to a full emergency footing to support these families. Nifgashim operates in constant coordination with the Home Front Command, government ministries, combat units, and other civil organizations to continuously map the needs of reservist families, direct resources where gaps are identified and synchronize various aid organizations for collective impact.

## Who We Are

Nifgashim is a unity and resilience movement established in 2022 as an apolitical organization connecting all sectors of Israeli society. Since October 7th, we have built the largest reservist community in Israel:

- Assisted over 60,000 reservist families, providing personal support to thousands.
- Operated a "Operations Center" for civil aid to reservist families and built a coalition of over 60 aid organizations.
- Established a nationwide infrastructure of volunteers and professional digital/human response systems.

During the previous confrontation with Iran, our emergency project provided:

- Support and guidance for 4,350 families.
- An emergency grant system that provided immediate help to over 200 combat families.
- A 24/7 Operations Center.

## Project Objectives: Operation "Lion's Roar"

The mission is to operate an emergency framework to stabilize and strengthen reservist families, addressing the emotional, domestic, and financial toll of the mobilization. While "Nifgashim" has an existing national infrastructure, the immense pressure of Operation "Lion's Roar" requires an urgent expansion of our services.

**Key Assistance Channels:**

- **Expanded National Support "Operations center":** A human and digital hub providing financial, employment, legal, and emotional guidance for soldiers and their families. It operates in coordination with all field organizations to prevent duplication and maximize national impact.
- **Emergency Grants via the "Miluimnikim+" App:** Targeted aid for families in financial distress through vouchers for food, basic necessities, and respite. Support is granted based on commander/welfare officer recommendations or direct vetting by the Command Center.
- **Hosting Solutions for Families Without Safe Rooms:** Matching families living in unprotected areas with host families in protected environments.
- **Resilience Webinar Series:** 5 to 10 sessions focusing on mental health, relationships, finances, and national spirit.
- **Support for the Hasmonean Brigade:** Dedicated assistance for the commanders and families of the Hasmonean Brigade, the first Haredi combat brigade in the IDF.

## Oue Emergency March 2026 Targets

- Direct support for over 7,000 reservist families via the Command Center.
- Emergency grants for 550–700 families.
- Securing protected hosting for dozens of families.
- Holding 5–10 webinars for thousands of participants.
- Personal support for approximately 150 families within the Hasmonean Brigade.

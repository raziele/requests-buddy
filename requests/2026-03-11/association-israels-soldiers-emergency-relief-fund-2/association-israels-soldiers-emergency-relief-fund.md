## The Ask

The Association for Israel's Soldiers, the IDF-authorized nonprofit for soldier welfare, is raising funds through its Emergency Relief Fund following Operation Roaring Lion (launched February 28, 2026). The fund supports: personal hygiene and apparel for soldiers; life-saving medical equipment and military ambulances (650k NIS per fully equipped vehicle); financial aid for evacuated families (2,000 NIS per family), for families of moderately or seriously injured soldiers (5,000 NIS within 24 hours of admission), and for bereaved families (2,000 NIS during shiva); and physical and mental recharge initiatives for troops. 100% of donations go directly to soldiers with no overhead or administrative delays.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Male hygiene kit: 200 NIS
- Female hygiene kit: 150 NIS (each kit shared by 4 soldiers)
- Physical & mental recharge per soldier: approx. 100 NIS (40–250k NIS per military unit)
- Financial aid for evacuated soldiers and families: 2,000 NIS per family
- Military ambulance (fully equipped): 650k NIS per vehicle
- Financial aid for families of moderately/seriously injured soldiers: 5,000 NIS per family
- Financial aid for bereaved families in mourning: 2,000 NIS per family

## Context & Background

On February 28, 2026, IDF and U.S. military forces launched Operation Roaring Lion, a broad coordinated campaign against the Iranian regime. The Israeli home front has been struck by rockets; as of March 4, 2026 the portfolio cites 1,391+ rockets fired into Israel, 13 Israelis killed, 1,325 injured, 571+ families evacuated. The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the sole organization authorized by the IDF to receive and distribute donations for the welfare and well-being of IDF soldiers; 100% of donations go directly to benefit service members.

The Emergency Relief Fund enables the IDF and Ministry of Defense to procure essential supplies without delay. Priorities include: (1) Basic personal necessities—hygiene supplies, non-military clothing, personal non-tactical equipment, and resources for physical and emotional respite; (2) Life-saving medical equipment—military ambulances (Mercedes Sprinter type, high mobility for outposts and bases), mobile medical units, and other critical life-preserving gear for combat paramedics; (3) Support for impacted families—evacuated families, wounded soldiers and their families, bereaved families, and vulnerable populations, including vouchers (redeemable at ~60 store chains in Israel) and emergency assistance grants.

The need for hygiene kits and recharge is framed as morale and resilience: soldiers who cannot shower for days benefit from wash-up and freshen-up; comfortable clothing and short respite breaks (meals, entertainment, showers, haircuts, laundry) help battalions re-energize. Financial aid for evacuated families (2,000 NIS vouchers) addresses immediate loss of property and essential needs in the critical period before reorganization; for injured soldiers the fund provides 5,000 NIS in vouchers to the family within 24 hours of hospital admission; for bereaved families, 2,000 NIS during shiva for immediate expenses. The IDF does not procure personal protective or tactical gear via donations; army policy allows the IDF to receive donations from an emergency fund for immediate solutions that benefit soldiers.

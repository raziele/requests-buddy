## The Ask

The Israeli Volunteering Council is requesting $179,000 to implement "When the Siren Sounds" — emergency kits and leadership training for children in Arab municipalities. IVC already operates Volunteer Leader programs in 10 Arab municipalities. The initiative includes emergency leadership training for Volunteer Leaders; distribution of 100 group shelter kits and 2,000 individual creative kits (including for homebound/disabled children); and "Knitting for the Children" — knitting kits for older adults in shelters to make octopus dolls for emotionally struggling children. Arab localities have a critical shortage of private shelters; ~46% of Arab residents lack standard protective spaces vs 26% nationally; only 37 public shelters in Arab municipalities (8 unfit).

**Funding Requested:** $179,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Leadership Training Workshops (online, 10 municipalities): $25,000
- Group Shelter Kits (board games, art supplies, Arabic guides) — 100 kits: $50,000
- Individual Creative Kits (homebound/disabled children) — 2,000 kits: $80,000
- Octopus Knitting Kits — Volunteer Club — 300 kits: $9,000
- Logistics & Volunteer Coordination: $15,000

## Context & Background

Arab municipalities in Israel have significantly fewer private bomb shelters than the national average; families often seek refuge in crowded, unprepared public spaces (schools, parking lots). Before the current escalation, Arab communities were already coping with prolonged internal violence. IVC's existing Volunteer Leaders in 10 Arab municipalities provide trusted, local infrastructure; the proposal activates and strengthens this for crisis response. Pillar I trains leaders in resilience, emergency protocols, community mapping, and working with traumatized populations. Pillar II distributes group shelter kits (10 per municipality) and individual kits (200 per municipality). Pillar III engages older adults in shelters with knitting kits to make octopus dolls for children — turning idle waiting into purposeful activity and strengthening community ties.

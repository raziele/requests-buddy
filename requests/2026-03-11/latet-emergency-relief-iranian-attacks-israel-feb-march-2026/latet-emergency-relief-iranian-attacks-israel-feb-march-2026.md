## The Ask

Latet, Israel's social first responder, is requesting $300,000 to scale its emergency operation launched in response to the direct Iranian attacks on Israeli territory (February 28–March 1, 2026). The organization is distributing immediate relief kits to displaced families, providing emergency shelter kits, and conducting outreach to all elderly and Holocaust survivor aid recipients. Latet has already prepared 3,000 packages for distribution and is working in coordination with local authorities and the IDF Home Front Command. The request seeks partner support to build capacity to respond to the size and nature of the crisis.

**Funding Requested:** $300,000

## Context & Background

Since the early hours of Saturday, February 28, 2026, Israel has been under an active state of emergency following the launch of direct Iranian attacks on Israeli territory. Iran fired waves of missiles and drones targeting civilian population centers across the country. With sirens sounding repeatedly, millions of civilians were forced to seek shelter for prolonged periods in reinforced safe rooms, public shelters, or improvised locations such as parking garages and light rail stations—many lacking adequate food, water, bedding, or basic sanitation. National mobility was severely restricted, and schools and workplaces closed.

Initial assessments indicate population displacement, damage to residential buildings, and growing difficulties in accessing food, essential supplies, and basic services—particularly for displaced families, elderly and Holocaust survivors with limited mobility, and individuals in shelter. As of Sunday morning, approximately 190 displaced families had been temporarily accommodated in five locations across Tel Aviv, Haifa, Petah Tikva, Ramat Gan, and Bnei Brak.

Latet operates as the first social responder in times of crisis, in close coordination with local authorities and the IDF Home Front Command. Its current emergency response includes direct distribution of immediate relief kits for displaced families (adapted to their needs), provision of emergency shelter kits, and outreach to all elderly and Holocaust survivor aid recipients. The organization has already prepared 3,000 packages for distribution in the coming days and may increase this number as the situation evolves. Support is needed to fully mobilize the Latet team and meet the scale of the crisis.

## The Ask

JDC is requesting $6.876 million in emergency funding to respond to the March 2026 escalation in Israel (Iran and Lebanon-origin attacks), which has caused fatalities, hundreds of injuries, and displacement. The ask covers humanitarian relief in five directly hit cities, welfare support for 10 northern frontline municipalities, the All the Way Home program for up to 300 displaced households, accessibility in shelters and shelter kits for children, emergency replacement of medical oxygen cylinders for municipal response teams, and increased support for global Jewish communities facing heightened security and mental-health needs.

**Funding Requested:** $6,876,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Humanitarian Relief for Directly Hit Cities ($200,000 per city x 5 cities): $1,000,000
- Welfare Support for Northern Frontline Municipalities ($150,000 per city x 10 cities): $1,500,000
- All the Way Home for Families Whose Apartments Were Severely Damaged: $3,300,000
- Increasing Accessibility in Shelters for People with Disabilities and Accessible Shelter Kits for Children: $200,000
- Emergency Replacement of Medical Gas Cylinders: $576,000
- Increased Needs of Global Jewish Communities: $300,000

## Context & Background

In early March 2026, Israel was again under large-scale attack: over roughly 100 hours, hundreds of missiles and drones were launched from Iran and Lebanon, sending millions of Israelis to safe rooms and shelters. As of March 3, direct hits in Tel Aviv, Beit Shemesh, Jerusalem, and Be'er Sheva had caused 12 fatalities, hundreds of injuries, and extensive damage, forcing over 1,500 people to evacuate to hotels. JDC has been responding using unrestricted and pre-October 7 emergency funding—delivering supplies to evacuees in Beit Shemesh, respite kits to northern shelters, and embedding in government coordination while deploying digital mental health and emergency tools.

JDC's funding menu addresses the most urgent priorities: multi-layered humanitarian relief in five directly hit cities (supplies, medical and first-response equipment, flexible municipal funds, and community caseworkers); welfare support for 10 low-socioeconomic northern municipalities that have appealed for food, supplies, and resilience support; and the All the Way Home program, which provides personalized housing transition support for up to 300 vulnerable displaced households (100 families already identified). Additional items include accessibility equipment and shelter kits for people with disabilities and children, and emergency replacement of 1,442 medical oxygen cylinders following a Ministry of Health safety directive.

JDC also reports heightened security and support needs in global Jewish communities—including expanded social work and mental health assistance in the Netherlands and rising security costs and threats around schools, synagogues, and gatherings in Turkey, France, Brussels, and Tunisia. The total ask of $6.876 million is presented as urgent to sustain lifesaving and stabilization work as the conflict develops.

# Responding to Emergency in Israel: Urgent Funding Needs | March 4, 2026

## Israel Again in the Shadow of War

Over the past few days, Israel was once again thrown into war. In the last 100 hours, hundreds of missiles and drones were launched from Iran – and more recently, Lebanon – to Israel, sending millions of Israelis to safe rooms and bomb shelters at all hours of the day and night. While most were intercepted, as of March 3rd, direct hits in Tel Aviv, Beit Shemesh, Jerusalem, and Be'er Sheva caused 12 fatalities, hundreds of injuries, and extensive damage to homes and infrastructure, forcing over 1,500 citizens to leave their homes and evacuate to hotels.

JDC has drawn on established networks of government ministries and local partners to assess emerging needs. Using unrestricted and emergency funding, JDC has created foundations that allow rapid response:

- **Digital Tools:** JDC-developed platforms for education and mental health professionals, AI-powered support apps for reservists' spouses, data dashboards for multi-ministerial round tables.
- **Municipal Partnership:** Work with municipalities (Mashiv Haruach, Northern Initiative) strengthening community resilience and local capacity.
- **Leveling the Playing Field in Critical Aid:** Advanced Life Support, Basic Life Support, and CPR kits for volunteer response teams in low-income municipalities; locally based Emergency Medical Units.

## Highlighted Urgent Funding Needs

### 1. Humanitarian Relief for Directly Hit Cities
- **Amount:** $200,000 per city × 5 cities = **$1,000,000**
- Support for cities with massive, direct missile damage (e.g., Beit Shemesh, Be'er Sheva).
- **Components:** Immediate humanitarian relief (clothing, toiletries, necessities, children's items via Shinua Hevrati); medical and first response equipment for municipal emergency teams (tourniquets, bandages, stretchers, lifesaving and rescue kits); flexible humanitarian assistance fund; deployment of two full-time community caseworkers to reinforce municipal welfare.

### 2. Welfare Support for Northern Frontline Municipalities
- **Amount:** $150,000 per city × 10 cities = **$1,500,000**
- Low-socioeconomic authorities in the North after two years of conflict.
- JDC has received urgent appeals from seven municipalities: Ma'ale Yosef, Shlomi, Kiryat Shmona, Nahariya, Hurfeish, Deir al-Asad, Mevo'ot Hermon. Requested: food, household supplies, mattresses, biodegradable and accessible medical equipment, community support personnel, resilience and training packages, activity kits, emergency relief. JDC is active in 24 locations along the northern confrontation line.

### 3. All the Way Home (Ad HaBayit) Program for Families Whose Apartments Were Severely Damaged
- **Amount:** **$3,300,000**
- Personalized housing transition support for up to 300 of the most vulnerable households in hard-hit cities. Helps displaced residents access rights, navigate bureaucracy, and find viable housing. 100 families already identified; demand expected to grow. (Context: June war with Iran displaced ~13,000 Israelis, including 4,000 particularly vulnerable.)

### 4. Increasing Accessibility in Shelters for People with Disabilities and Accessible Shelter Kits for Children
- **Amount:** **$200,000**
- Many people with disabilities lack private shelters and use public bomb shelters; accessibility gaps and emotional strain during prolonged stays. JDC proposes: equipment for municipalities (portable ramps, marked stairs/thresholds, guiding strips, signage) and training for staff/volunteers; accessible shelter kits for children (pop-up tents, weighted blankets, floor mats, noise-canceling headphones) to reduce distress and support emotional regulation.

### 5. Emergency Replacement of Medical Gas Cylinders
- **Amount:** **$576,000**
- Mid-February 2026 Ministry of Health directive identified safety concerns with existing cylinders in some kits. JDC to procure new, certified oxygen cylinders: 300 immediately, remaining 1,142 within one to three weeks. Includes centralized collection and phased replacement, coordinated with MOH and national authorities.

### 6. Increased Needs of Global Jewish Communities
- **Amount:** **$300,000**
- Europe, Asia, Africa: JDC monitoring and in contact with local leadership. Netherlands: vulnerable Jewish students and families need expanded social work and mental-health assistance and crisis-response systems. Turkey, France, Brussels, Tunisia: acute increases in security threats around schools, synagogues, youth movements, and gatherings; aim to help absorb rising security costs and reinforce protective infrastructure.

## The Ask

Project Kesher Israel is requesting $60,000 ($50,000 remaining after $10,000 raised) to support immigrant women and children during the current crisis. Many live in older buildings without safe rooms, run to public shelters or remain in parking garages; 11 participants reported shattered windows and structural damage; women need Russian/Ukrainian-language briefings; mental health systems are overloaded; displaced families in hotels; school closures and transportation disruptions. Program includes emergency basic needs ($20,000 for 200 women/families), mental health services ($30,000 for ~300 high-risk women), and emergency programming and coordination ($10,000 for daily Zoom, outreach, 5,000+ network).

**Funding Requested:** $60,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Basic Needs — $100 per package, 200 women/families (food, water, clothing/blankets, stipends): $20,000
- Mental Health Services — individual and group therapy, ~300 high-risk women: $30,000
- Emergency Programming & Coordination — daily Zoom, outreach, safety coordination, 5,000+ network: $10,000

## Context & Background

PKI was established in 2008 to support Russian-speaking Jewish immigrant women from the post-Soviet states; following the Ukraine war, over 200,000 immigrants and refugees arrived and the network expanded. Ongoing attacks have placed immigrant women at acute risk: many lack safe rooms (37% of Israeli residents lack safe spaces; percentage higher in Russian-speaking community due to economic constraints), cannot follow Hebrew news or safety instructions, report panic attacks and insomnia, and face overloaded public mental health systems. One participant wrote: "This is my fourth war in four years." Without immediate intervention, acute distress risks becoming long-term trauma. PKI provides emergency-responsive programming, basic needs for those in shelters, expanded mental health access via licensed Russian/Ukrainian-speaking professionals, and sustained staff capacity for real-time communication and Zoom programming.

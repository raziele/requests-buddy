## The Ask

Beit Issie Shapiro is requesting emergency funding to sustain critical support services for people with disabilities during the current security crisis. They have launched a national Emergency Hotline (WhatsApp/phone/video) for professionals, caregivers, and families; virtual support sessions for families in Hebrew and Arabic; emotional and practical support for staff; and educational/therapeutic support for children. They estimate $150,000–$200,000 (USD) per 10–14 days of conflict to sustain these services, plus significant income loss from closed services (~$100,000/week) and salary continuity for unable-to-work staff (~$230,000/week).

**Funding Requested:** $150,000–$200,000 per 10–14 days; additional $100,000/week income loss; ~$230,000/week salary continuity

## Context & Background

As Israel faces renewed attack, people with disabilities and their families experience heightened fear and destabilization. Many cannot move quickly, interpret urgency, or process what is happening during sirens; routines collapse and trauma intensifies after danger passes. Beit Issie Shapiro has launched a national Emergency Hotline providing real-time emotional support and professional consultation; twice-weekly virtual support sessions for families in Hebrew and Arabic; partnership with the Shalem Foundation and Ministry of Welfare for open online guidance sessions; emotional and practical support for staff (including at Ra'anana and Qalanswa campuses); and virtual classes, home visits, and emergency activity kits for students. Impact stories describe easing severe anxiety for adults with disabilities and helping children feel safe again in shelters. The organization has chosen not to reduce salaries for employees unable to work, at substantial cost.

**Emergency Update: Supporting People with Disabilities During the Current Security Crisis – March 2026**

**What we are doing:**
1. **Emergency Hotline** — National hotline for professionals, caregivers and families (WhatsApp, then phone/video); free real-time emotional support and professional consultation.
2. **Virtual Support Sessions for Families** — Twice weekly in Hebrew and Arabic; guidance from mental health professionals. Partnership with Shalem Foundation, Ministry of Welfare and municipalities for four open online guidance sessions.
3. **Emotional and Practical Support for Staff** — Social workers at Ra'anana and Qalanswa; meals, activity packages, wellness sessions, care packages.
4. **Educational and Therapeutic Support for Children** — Virtual classes, home visits per safety regulations, emergency virtual activities and take-home kits.

**Funding:** Emergency response estimated $150,000–$200,000 (USD) per 10–14 days. Income loss ≥$100,000/week (closed services, postponed courses). Salary continuity for unable-to-work staff ~$230,000/week.

**Impact stories:** Hotline easing severe anxiety (e.g. 43-year-old with borderline intellectual disability); helping child with autism/cerebral palsy feel safe again after fall in shelter.

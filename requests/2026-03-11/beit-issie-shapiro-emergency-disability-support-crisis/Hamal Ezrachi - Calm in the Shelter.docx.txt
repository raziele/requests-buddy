Operation Calm in the ShelterEmergency Emotional Relief for Children in Shelters - Northern and Central Israel14,000 Children | 10 Municipalities | Immediate DeploymentOverviewThousands of children across northern and central Israel are spending extended hours in shelters during ongoing security alerts. Many young children are experiencing increasing anxiety and emotional distress, while parents are exhausted and often lack the emotional or financial capacity to continuously occupy and calm their children in these conditions.In response to urgent requests from local authorities, Brothers and Sisters for Israel-Hamal Ezrahi (BSI) is launching Operation Calm in the Shelter, an initiative providing emotional relief kits for children staying in shelters.Geographic ReachThe initiative will support 10 municipalities in total:Northern Israel (6):MetulaUpper Galilee Regional CouncilShlomiMa’ale YosefMateh AsherHurfeishCentral Israel (4):Municipalities serving children with special needs and families with limited financial resources.What We Will DeliverEmotional Relief Kits for ChildrenDeveloped by our pedagogical team, the kits are designed specifically for elementary school children (grades 1-6) spending extended time in shelters.Each kit includes:Emotion cards to help children identify and regulate feelingsStress-reduction games adapted for shelter environmentsGuided calming activitiesThese tools help children cope with fear and uncertainty while also providing meaningful relief for parents during stressful shelter stays.The kits build on our operational experience supporting communities during previous emergency situations.Limited Shelter Support (Upon Request)In a small number of shelters, and only when requested by local authorities, we may provide basic items such as mattresses or mats to support children and families during extended shelter stays.ImplementationThanks to our operational infrastructure and partnerships with municipalities, the initiative can be deployed within days, allowing rapid delivery of kits directly to communities in need.BudgetTotal Budget: $250,000Expected ImpactEmotional support for 14,000 childrenRelief for families coping with prolonged shelter staysDistribution across 10 municipalities in northern and central IsraelThis initiative provides immediate, practical emotional support for children living under ongoing threat, helping families navigate the psychological strain of repeated shelter stays.

## The Ask

Fresh Start (Daf Chadash) is requesting the San Francisco Federation join as a partner in its economic rehabilitation and resilience program for middle-class families and small businesses in Israel. The NGO runs short, focused interventions (roughly 10 sessions over 3 months) combining financial and legal advice, business consulting, and a "rehabilitation basket" (rights realization, credit, employment). Since the war it has assisted approximately 500 families and small businesses, mainly reservists. The program is already funded by the Ministry of Economy, several US Jewish Federations (New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, Philadelphia), the Shusterman Foundation, and other major funders. The ask is to expand the program and embed it as an institutional service in public systems (banks, government, local authorities). No specific funding amount is requested in the materials; the primary ask is partnership and a short introductory call to explore collaboration.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership in expanding and institutionalizing the program; introductory call to explore collaboration (contact offered: 054-4241241).

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of the war, a large share of Israeli middle-class households—including many reservists and small business owners—have faced accelerated economic crisis: prolonged reserve duty, loss of income for employees and self-employed, and the end of temporary relief measures have left working families in sustained debt and instability. These families often do not qualify for the welfare safety net or property-tax discounts but cannot absorb ongoing economic shocks alone. Fresh Start frames the problem as systemic: easy access to consumer credit without a structured path to exit debt or rebuild stability.

Fresh Start runs a holistic model that combines family and business economic counseling, legal advice, debt settlement, rights realization, and responsible credit, delivered by a national network of specialists. The model is short-term and intensive (about 10 meetings over 3 months), aimed at working populations with potential for quick recovery. Outcomes cited in attached reports include a 108% median rise in business income and an 18% rise in household income for those in default proceedings; debt reduction and settlement; and improved financial self-efficacy. The organization is now running pilots with the Ministry of Economy and the Jerusalem municipality and is working to embed the model in the banking system and additional public bodies.

The current request is not framed as emergency aid but as strategic scaling: expanding internal capacity and policy/communications work so the proven model becomes a stable public service. The email was sent on recommendation of Inbal Freund and includes an info sheet and two evaluation reports (business accompaniment for reservists and in-depth interviews on reservist grants) to support the partnership conversation.

### דף מידע - תמיכה בתכנית שיקום וחוסן כלכלי למעמד הביניים - דף חדש 2026.pdf

**Header:** Jerusalem, 17 February 2026. To Mr. Barak Loozon, SF Federation.

**Program:** Fresh Start (דף חדש) – Economic rehabilitation and resilience program for families and business owners in crisis. The organization runs a program that helps middle-class families and small business owners cope with consumer debt and cost of living, exit economic collapse, and move to stability and growth. The program has successfully assisted hundreds of participants since the war and now aims to embed it as an integral service in public systems (local authorities, government, banking).

**Social challenge:** Since the war, countless households—about a third of middle-class families in Israel—have been in accelerated economic crisis: prolonged reserve service, impact on salaried and self-employed income, and the end of temporary relief have exposed working populations to sustained debt and instability. These families are not eligible for the welfare safety net or property tax discounts but also cannot bear ongoing economic shocks alone. Without adequate systemic solutions they repeatedly fall into the consumer credit trap. The debt many face is a systemic failure: a system that allows quick entry into debt and dependence on credit for daily living but does not provide a structured path to exit and economic rehabilitation. Reference: National Report on Debt in Israel 2025.

**Model:** Fresh Start is a leading organization in Israel for economic rehabilitation of working populations in economic crisis. It operates a holistic, multi-disciplinary model: economic and business/employment counseling, legal advice and debt settlement, rights realization and responsible credit, and strengthening financial management in the business and household (person–business–family). The program is run by a national team of economic and business consultants and lawyers specializing in crisis management, with a "rehabilitation services basket" (rights realization, creditor negotiation, credit, employment, etc.). The model has helped hundreds of families and businesses exit crisis via an intensive short process (10 meetings over ~3 months): in-depth diagnosis, action plan, cash-flow stabilization, economic/business/employment decisions, and removal of barriers. It targets working populations with quick recovery potential.

**Need:** To make the rehabilitation and resilience model systemic, the service must be embedded in public systems—banking, government, local authorities—so that anyone in difficulty can receive impartial professional advice "in real time" and avoid mistakes that deepen entanglement. The current timing creates a unique window: the war and its load on households (especially reservists and confrontation-line residents), small businesses, and public systems have exposed structural gaps and increased openness to systemic solutions. Pilots have been launched with the Ministry of Economy, Jerusalem municipality, and others; work is underway to embed the program in the banking system, local authorities, and additional government offices. This scaling requires expanding the organization's infrastructure and policy/communications work to raise awareness and advance the issue on the public agenda as part of the broader cost-of-living effort. Embedding the model would turn proven field success into stable public infrastructure and reduce future load on welfare, enforcement, and collection.

**Results:** Since the war, the organization has assisted nearly 500 families and small businesses (mainly reservists) to exit a range of economic crises—from early entanglement to collection and insolvency proceedings—through a short, effective, focused intervention with results within about 3–4 months (see attached research reports): (a) significant income increase (108% for businesses, 18% for households in insolvency); (b) debt reduction and settlement (15% without collection proceedings, full payment compliance in insolvency); (c) sustained economic balance in the household; (d) improved financial self-efficacy and money management. Link to folder with research and client testimonial videos provided. The organization is supported by leading bodies including the Joint, National Insurance funds, US Jewish Federations, banks, insurance companies, and private foundations in Israel and abroad. Leadership includes figures from law, academia, business, and banking.

**Contact:** Tomer Rabinovitz, Adv., CEO, 054-4241241 or tomer@freshstart.org.il.

### סיכום מחקר ראיונות עומק - מענקי מילואים - יולי 2025.pdf

**Title:** Evaluation research for the business accompaniment project of 'Fresh Start' for reservists who are business owners, mobilized for "Iron Swords" and their families. In-depth interview findings. Moshe Cohen, Dr. Yuval Faldi. July 2025.

**Summary:** High satisfaction with the business accompaniment and the grant. The emotional aspect of the consultant's work was seen as important and possibly key to effective accompaniment, especially given the complex period and reservists moving between war and civilian life and spouses experiencing that dissonance. As in prior Fresh Start studies, numerical scores for consultants and grants were high relative to participants' experience, which was not always perfect; context is that the service is free so expectations may be lower. Two business owners reported a significant positive business turnaround; others said the consultant was good and the accompaniment quality high but no major positive business results yet—some explicitly said the lack of change was entirely their responsibility (not yet able to focus on the business or had not yet acted on the consultant's advice). Willingness to recommend Fresh Start was high, as was willingness to donate in the future when possible. Willingness to take a conditional loan instead of a grant was low, mainly due to fear of taking on (sometimes additional) debt. Main recommendations: strengthen the interface between the organization and clients and add a designated contact alongside the consultant; reduce uncertainty about the process and grant; shorten time from grant application to response; align expectations before starting to avoid underuse of the accompaniment; consider revisiting charging for accompaniment (detailed in recommendations).

**Background:** Project goal—to help reservists (mostly business owners) or their spouses whose businesses were harmed by Iron Swords. Harm mainly from prolonged reserve duty and inability to run the business, or (for spouses' businesses) from bearing full burden of home, children, and business. In some cases the whole sector was hit (e.g. tourism). The project includes up to 10 meetings with a business consultant at no cost (acquaintance, business data analysis, action directions, practical solutions) and a grant of up to 40,000 ₪. Method: 8 in-depth interviews with reservists or spouses who received accompaniment and grant in 2024 or early 2025; interviews by Zoom, focusing on period from war outbreak through end of accompaniment and business results.

**Findings (abbreviated):** Business profiles and entry into difficulty (small businesses, various sectors; difficulty from mobilization, business halt, or spouse overload). Trigger for seeking help (combination of business and emotional difficulty, sometimes anxiety). How they found Fresh Start (mainly WhatsApp groups, social networks, sometimes personal referral). Business accompaniment (topics: income/expense, separation of business/household, budget, credit/loans, marketing, partnerships, pricing; consultants learned the business quickly; emotional support seen as important; in one case repeated call-ups disrupted continuity). Main challenges (marketing, lack of order in finances, underpricing, wrong product for market, bank relations, fixed thinking). Consultants adapted to needs and time constraints. Impact on business (mixed: some modest impact and gradual recovery, some breakthrough and rapid improvement; emotional improvement often significant). Grant amount and use (20,000 or 40,000 ₪; most had clear plans with consultant; used for campaigns, accountant, equipment, debt payment, etc.). Did the grant help / was it enough? (less than half said it was enough; all said it helped). Numerical scores: average for accompaniment 9.1, for grant 8.8. Improvement points (expectation alignment, follow-up after accompaniment, physical presence at grant committee, reducing uncertainty, shortening approval time, optional specialist meetings, fewer forms, direct contact with org not only via consultant). Willingness to recommend: all would recommend. Willingness to pay for accompaniment: low to medium. Willingness to take conditional loan: low (fear of debt); minority would consider if loan were larger than max grant. Willingness to donate in future: high. Conclusions and recommendations (summarized): accompaniment and grants perceived as quality and helpful; possible upward bias in scores due to free service; two of eight businesses had significant breakthrough; recommendations include strengthening org–consultant interface, reducing uncertainty, shortening grant approval time, expectation alignment, option for in-person appearance before grant committee, discussion of charging for accompaniment).

### פרויקט הליווי העסקי של דף חדש - דוח הערכה מסכם 2.1.2025.pdf

**Title:** Business accompaniment project of Fresh Start for businesses affected by Iron Swords. Summary evaluation research report – December 2024. Dr. Yuval Faldi, Anuva Shabtai, Moshe Cohen – Institute for Insights.

**Summary:** The project aimed to help business owners whose income was affected by Iron Swords through meetings with a business consultant to strengthen them personally and find solutions to return the business to growth. The project, ending at report time, began January 2024; 130 business owners have participated so far. The evaluation examined effectiveness on three axes: personal (change in feelings/emotions), business (solutions to main business challenges), and financial (change in business income and debt). Participants were also asked for the single most significant change they experienced. 27 business owners who completed accompaniment between March–September 2024 were included; they were interviewed for about an hour on Zoom and provided relevant financial data. The vast majority were highly satisfied with the accompaniment. Consultants tailored the accompaniment to each business's challenges. The accompaniment helped many return to positive action, energy, and income growth, and to adopt healthy business management practices (order in income/expenses, cash-flow management, planning, marketing, pricing). Median business income rose 108% between pre- and post-accompaniment; debt metrics improved more moderately (e.g. 15% decrease in median debt balance).

**Background:** Project goal—help businesses whose income was affected by Iron Swords by strengthening the owner personally and finding solutions to restore and grow income. Project includes up to 10 meetings with a business consultant at no cost (acquaintance, business data analysis, action directions, practical solutions). Project started January 2024; 130 participants; 60% completed, 21% froze after 0–2 meetings, 19% in process. 13 business consultants; average number of meetings for those who completed was 8.5.

**Method:** Evaluation of effectiveness on three axes: personal (stress/anxiety, belief/optimism about the business, proactivity, other); business (solutions to main challenges); financial (business income, debt payments, total debt before vs. after). Participants also indicated the most significant change and why. 27 owners who completed accompaniment March–September 2024, from 10 different consultants; diverse sectors and backgrounds (businesses that were fine pre-war and then damaged; businesses already in difficulty; personal crises; extreme war experience; long reserve duty). One-hour in-depth interviews at least one month after accompaniment; financial data from consultants.

**Findings:** Personal axis—pre-accompaniment, the state of the business affected everyone strongly (anxiety, stress, fear, confusion). Meeting Fresh Start was described as a lifeline. Post-accompaniment: improvement in self-confidence, self-efficacy, well-being, reduced stress and anxiety, more calm; strong desire to advance the business. Business axis—many did not know how to manage their business before (no forecasts, no separation of business/household, etc.); war intensified problems. Consultants offered tailored responses; participants reported being more planned and organized, more confident in their service/product, less afraid of numbers, more forward-looking. Financial axis (23 businesses): Income—20 had increase, 3 decrease. Median monthly income pre-accompaniment 42,980 ₪; post 89,393 ₪ (108% increase). Monthly debt payment—of 19 with data, 8 had no payment pre-accompaniment, 5 no change, 4 decrease, 2 in settlement (frozen). No one's monthly payment increased. Total debt—of 23, 10 had decrease, 8 no debt, 3 no change, 2 increase. Median debt pre 180,000 ₪, post 152,450 ₪ (15.3% decrease). Most significant change (four themes): (1) Acquiring and applying managerial tools (dominant); (2) Belief/optimism about the business; (3) Proactivity and initiative; (4) Improvement in emotional state. Summary: High satisfaction; consultants seen as professional and committed; free service may have lowered initial expectations and increased satisfaction; financial results strong (108% median income rise, 15% median debt decrease); adoption of healthier management practices noted.

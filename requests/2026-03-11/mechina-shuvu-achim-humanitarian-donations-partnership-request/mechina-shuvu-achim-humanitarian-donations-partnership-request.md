During the past week and a half of Operation "Roar of the Lion," they have been receiving many requests from the field. ## Context & Background

Mechina Shuvu Achim operates a nationwide humanitarian project that supports vulnerable populations in cooperation with welfare departments across Israel, from Kiryat Shmona in the north to Sderot in the south. The program is run in memory of the three abductees.

Since the start of Operation "Roar of the Lion" (approximately a week and a half before the email), the organization has been receiving a high volume of requests from the field. The needs fall into three main categories: (1) children staying in shelters who are required to learn remotely but whose families cannot afford computers; (2) soldiers who were mobilized at the beginning of the operation and have been in the field for over a week, in need of emergency kits; and (3) elderly people in need of hygiene products, medicine, and basic food.

The organization is reaching out to the Chicago Federation (via Dina Berman) to explore the possibility of a financial donation or in-kind contribution to address these populations given the urgency of the situation. They emphasize that they hold all documents required for tax purposes and are seeking a meaningful partnership.

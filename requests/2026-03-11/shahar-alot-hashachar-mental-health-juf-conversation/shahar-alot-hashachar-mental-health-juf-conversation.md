## The Ask

Shahar, an Israeli organization that runs ten mechinot (pre-army leadership academies) and the Alot HaShachar program, is reaching out to introduce itself and request a conversation with the JUF Israel Office. Israel Gordan positions Alot HaShachar—which takes combat units who served together in Gaza abroad with a therapist to process their experiences—as a strong fit for JUF's Mental Health Rehabilitation funding priority. The request is non-monetary: they seek an opportunity to speak with someone at JUF about potential alignment and support.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Introduction and conversation with someone at JUF regarding fit for Mental Health Rehabilitation funding priority.

## Context & Background

Shahar operates multiple programs in Israel: ten of its own mechinot (pre-army leadership academies) under the Mehina network and Alot HaShachar, a team-based, nature-anchored healing program for combat soldiers. Israel Gordan contacted the Israel Office of Chicago (JUF) on February 25, 2026, to present these programs and ask for a conversation.

Alot HaShachar is designed for intact combat teams to process their experiences together abroad with therapeutic support, addressing the transition from combat to civilian life. The program uses clinical psychology, expressive therapies, group facilitation, CBT, and nature-based therapy. Since 2024 they report 60 journeys, support to 1,000 soldiers and team members, and work with personnel coping with PTSD, wounded soldiers, casualty officers, and war-victim identification teams. The organization frames itself as filling a care gap for young combatants and offering high social return on investment.

The attached one-pager on Shahar Pre-Army Leadership Academies describes a six-month journey for young adults from Israel's social and geographic periphery, combining Jewish-Israeli identity, community living, and leadership development. Today ten Shahar academies operate across Israel with over 2,800 alumni, over 70% of whom serve in combat roles, and around 390 current participants. The email does not specify a funding amount or itemized ask; the primary request is to speak with someone at JUF about fit for mental health rehabilitation funding.

# From War to Renewal
**Alot Hashachar - Team-based, nature-anchored healing for combat soldiers**

Alot Hashachar is a unique, experiential program designed for combat soldiers' teams to process their experiences together. We provide a dignified, professional haven for the complex transition from the world of combat to civilian life. Our philosophy is hopeful and practical - within every experience, including the most difficult and traumatic, there are seeds of growth, learning, and renewal.

Our team is made up of professionals in the fields of clinical psychology, expressive therapies, group facilitation, CBT, social work, and nature-based therapy.

Quotes from participants:
- "The relief I felt when I shared my story with the team was very meaningful and gave me a sense of closure and completeness with myself in front of the team"
- "I gained the perspective of how lucky I am to have places like this to draw strength and support from, and the breathing exercise we learned helps me cope with pressure"

**The Architecture of the Journey**
- **Initial Phase:** Understanding the need for change
- **Preparation in Israel:** Intro calls, cohort fit, set expectations, clarification of rules
- **Deep-dive journey:** Structured circles, embodied and creative work, and graded nature exposure
- **Transition and future focus:** Growth, dreams, personal development, and an anchored plan
- **Desired Outcome:** Greater emotional and mental connection

**Our Approach**
- *Team-centered processing* - We work with intact combat teams, transforming unit cohesion into a therapeutic asset that reduces isolation and supports ongoing resilience.
- *Nature-anchored settings* - By the open sea or in quiet forests, we create a safe, supportive space that enables distance from daily pressures and creates conditions for reflection and deep group connection.
- *Multi-method facilitation* - A focused blend of experiential, expressive, and evidence-informed tools with practical coping and post-traumatic growth.

**Professional Committee** (credentials summarized in PDF: Chair of Israel's National Council for Post-Trauma; Medical Director and Co-Founder of IKAR NGO; Formerly Head of IDF Mental Health Department; Director of Psychiatric Division at Rambam Health Care Campus; Director of Ma'ale HaCarmel Mental Health Center; Founder and Director of DerechIM Institute; Formerly IDF Mental Health Department, Commander of Unit for Treatment of Combat Reaction Casualties.)

**Our unique value**
- Filling the care gap for young combatants - We prioritize younger combatants whose entire age group fought together.
- Flexible by design - Formats and intensity tailored to different audiences and needs, including community-supported release journeys and clinical-research tracks.
- High social return on investment (SROI) - our Group model, lean operations, and experienced clinical-logistics teams convert each dollar into outsized, measurable impact.

**Context:** Since October 7, 2023, young combatants carry visible and invisible wounds. Many have been mobilized repeatedly and live with daily impacts - vigilance, guilt, distress, emotional numbing, moral injury. They need safe distance from triggers, structured group processing, and nature-anchored space to rebuild strength, meaning, and connection.

# Shachar Pre-Army Leadership Academies

Graduate quotes:
- "For me it was like an inner process of peeling off so many layers that had built up over the course of my life. From there I set out on a journey of inner closeness, a period that taught me a lot, mostly about myself and especially about love of the Land" (Tome)
- "I arrived with an exemption from military service and a deep confusion about my identity. At the mechina I discovered my inner strengths and chose to turn the exemption into combat service - today I am a released combat soldier" (Inba)

Shachar Pre-Army Leadership Academies offer a transformative educational journey for young adults from Israel's social and geographic periphery. Through community living, identity development, and meaningful preparation for service, participants build resilience, responsibility, and the confidence to take an active role in shaping Israeli society.

Today, 10 Shahar pre-army academies operate across Israel: Shahar Beit Shean, Shahar David - Tel Katzir, Shahar HaOn, Shahar Rotem - Mikve Israel, Shahar Nitzan, Shahar Hukok, Shahar Elrom, Shahar Tal - Beit Zera, Shahar Malkiya and Shahar Mevo Dotan.

**Key figures**
- 2,800+ alumni
- Over 70% of alumni serve in combat roles in the IDF
- Around 390 participants
- Extraordinary demand

**What does our process look like**
The program offers an intensive six-month journey that combines Jewish-Israeli identity, community living, and leadership development. Participants build resilience, practical life skills, and a deep sense of responsibility through study, group experience, social action, and meaningful preparation for IDF service.

"Six months in a half-year pre-army academy - a journey of identity, community and Zionism."

**Positioning**
Investing in Shahar is an investment in the resilience of Israeli society: a generation of young women and men who will lead the IDF, Israeli society and their communities with a clear identity, a strong sense of responsibility and deep commitment.

The "Year After Service" program is designed for graduates of meaningful military service, providing a structured space to reflect, redefine direction, and plan the next step. It equips alumni with practical tools, strengthens belonging and civic responsibility, and transforms "fear of release" into a new opportunity.

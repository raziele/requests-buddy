## The Ask

The Association for the Advancement of the Druze Soldier's Status (DruzeTech) is requesting $300,000 to complete the build-out of the second floor of its technological center — an accelerator named after Lt. Col. Mahmoud Hiraldin (killed in Gaza 2018). The center already runs an innovation and maritime program with 400 soldiers in hi-tech training. The organization aims to increase Druze participation in higher education and tech careers through motivation, military-service alignment, academic support and subsidies, women's advancement, scholarships for released soldiers and students, and stronger ties with industry and academia. They are seeking partnership and a site visit.

**Funding Requested:** $300,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Invitation to visit the center and project site.

## Context & Background

The Druze community in Israel numbers about 150,000 (roughly 1.6% of the population), with a higher share in the 20–35 age group. Despite a strong bagrut rate (91.3% vs. 76% national average) and meaningful military service, few Druze pursue higher education or complete degrees, and only about 3.5% work in hi-tech compared to 12% nationally. The community faces limited infrastructure, restricted access to quality education, few employment and networking opportunities, and a lack of role models from within the community.

The association has established the DruzeTech (DTec) Skills Lab in late 2022 as a hub to empower Druze youth and integrate them into the tech workforce. In under a year, in partnership with academia, industry, philanthropy, and government, it created a tailored program for employers in the North; about 100 students have participated in training, career conversion, and tech placement programs. The innovation and maritime center now has 400 soldiers in hi-tech training.

The next step is a national Druze innovation center — an accelerator — using entrepreneurship as a base for problem-solving in areas such as maritime, energy, robotics, transport, and circular economy. The program structure includes community building, applied learning in advanced domains (e.g., code, AI, robotics), and personalized tracks: advanced academic studies, placement in tech companies, and support for startups. Tracks include inspiration, 21st-century skills, entrepreneurship, technology, health, business, and government, with experiential elements such as an MIT summer semester, NASA challenges, 4YFN, and hi-tech internships. A Career Catalyst component provides intensive post-program support, including up to 25-week internships and living scholarships. The organization’s stated goal is 3,000 Druze integrated into Israeli hi-tech within five years.

They are short $300,000 to complete the accelerator project and are reaching out to partners for funding and a visit. The association holds valid Section 46 tax-deductible status (through 31/12/2027), proper management approval for 2026 from the Registrar of Non-Profit Associations (with a noted cumulative deficit), and a confirmed bank account at First International Bank of Israel.

**The Druze challenge in Israel**

Out of about one million Druze worldwide, only 150,000 live in Israel. Druze make up about 1.6% of Israel's population, but in the 20–35 age group their share is almost double. The Druze community suffers from few infrastructures, limited access to quality education, lack of employment and networking opportunities, and few role models from within the community. Despite strong bagrut results (91.3% vs. 76% national average) and successful military service, few Druze pursue higher education, fewer complete degrees, and even fewer work in tech. While 12% of the Israeli population is employed in hi-tech, only 3.5% of Druze are.

**Our vision**

Creating a strong, flexible Druze tech workforce by bridging gaps: continuous presence from childhood through lifelong learning; promoting gender and geographic equality; raising awareness of support services; building skills through hands-on experience; strengthening community and ecosystem; connecting to the local business environment; supporting entrepreneurship and resource mobilization; developing personalized career paths; mentoring and empowerment; networking events.

**Skills Lab (DTec)**

In late 2022 they established the DTec Skills Lab, a center to empower Druze youth and integrate them into the tech workforce. In under a year, with academia, industry, philanthropy, and government, they built a program tailored to employers in the North. About 100 students have participated in training, career conversion, and tech placement.

**Next step: multidisciplinary approach**

They are expanding to a national Druze innovation center — an accelerator. The program uses entrepreneurship as a base for problem-solving in extreme conditions (maritime, energy, robotics, transport, circular economy, etc.). Goal: bridge gaps in skills, experience, networking, and motivation and increase the share of learners, hi-tech workers, and entrepreneurs. All participants commit to giving back to the community.

**Program structure**
1. Community building — exposure to success stories, role models, increased motivation.
2. Inquiry — applied learning in advanced areas (code, AI, robotics, etc.).
3. Involvement — personal accompaniment on tracks: (a) advanced academic studies, (b) placement in tech companies, (c) support for startup creation.

**Tracks:** Inspiration; 21st-century skills; Entrepreneurship; Technology; Health; Business; Government.

**Experiential elements:** MIT summer semester; NASA challenges and programs; presenting projects at 4YFN (Mobile World Congress startups); internships at leading hi-tech companies; youth exchange with Abraham Accords countries (e.g. "Living on Mars" bootcamp).

**Career Catalyst**

After the program — intensive support to place graduates in their first hi-tech job: internship program up to 25 weeks simulating real projects; led by industry experts; close mentoring; living scholarships. Over the last decade hundreds of graduates have joined hi-tech roles. Goal: within 5 years, 3,000 Druze integrated into Israeli hi-tech.

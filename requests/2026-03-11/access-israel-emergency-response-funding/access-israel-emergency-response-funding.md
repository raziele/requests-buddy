## The Ask

Access Israel (Nagishut Israel) is requesting $400,000 in immediate funding for the first 10 days of the current escalation (as of February 28, 2026) to run accessible emergency response operations. The organization has been on the front line of humanitarian aid for the most vulnerable since October 7, providing accessible evacuation, accessible alternative housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal and logistical support when state and local authorities did not provide adequate response. With the new round of fighting, they need to scale the 'Purple Vest' system, operate a 24/7 accessible emergency hotline, deploy accessible vehicles and trained volunteers, and ensure accessible sheltered accommodation and life-saving information for people with disabilities and the elderly across the country.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- 24/7 accessible emergency hotline – staffing, Purple Vest app development and maintenance, communications, logistics, real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries: Not itemized
- Purple Vest training, refreshments, and deployment – volunteer training, accessible evacuation equipment, operational management and coordination for rapid accessible assistance in missile strike incidents: Not itemized
- Wheelchair-accessible vehicles with lifts – operation of accessible evacuation vehicles including drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance for safe evacuation from unmasked areas to medical care and accessible alternative sheltered housing: Not itemized
- Accessible alternative sheltered accommodation for evacuees – immediate funding for stays in hotels and accessible sheltered solutions for families with people with disabilities forced to evacuate from inaccessible or damaged homes, including required adaptations and logistical support: Not itemized
- Life-saving information dissemination – digital and communications campaigns, emergency preparedness and assistance content, website maintenance, and real-time accessibility of vital information for people with disabilities nationwide: Not itemized

## Context & Background

Approximately 40% of people with disabilities and elderly citizens in Israel live without accessible shelter. When sirens sound, they often have no real option and may be left in place in fear and anxiety. Since October 7, Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian aid for the most vulnerable, helping thousands with accessible evacuation, accessible and sheltered alternative housing, medical equipment supply, dedicated accessible transport, and personal and logistical support in severe distress—in many cases when no other body, neither the state nor the local authority, provided an adequate response. Those who need help most in wartime are often the last to receive institutional response.

With the start of the current round of fighting on February 28, 2026, the organization faces a renewed, significant, and urgent need to ensure accessible, professional, and sensitive assistance for people with disabilities and the elderly. The requested $400,000 is for an initial immediate 10-day period; continuation will be evaluated jointly based on developments and duration of the conflict. Critical operations include a 24/7 accessible emergency operations center and hotline, Purple Vest volunteer training and deployment for rapid accessible assistance in the field when missiles hit buildings and neighborhoods, operation of wheelchair-accessible vehicles for evacuation and transfer to medical care and accessible sheltered housing, immediate funding for accessible alternative accommodation for evacuees, and dissemination of life-saving information in accessible formats. The letter stresses that partner support is the mainstay of their work and that every donation translates directly into protection and security for the most vulnerable population.

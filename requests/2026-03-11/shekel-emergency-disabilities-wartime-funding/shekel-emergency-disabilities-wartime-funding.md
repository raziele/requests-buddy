## The Ask

SHEKEL requests $30,000 in emergency funding to support people with disabilities during the current wartime crisis. The organization is operating in full emergency mode to ensure the safety and welfare of hundreds of people with disabilities who face ongoing missile barrages, confinement to housing 24/7 after closure of day programs, and acute trauma. Funding would support a nation-wide emergency therapeutic call center (trauma hotline for people with disabilities and their families) for one month, and additional staff and security for housing where residents—including those with severe multiple disabilities—require round-the-clock care and safe-room assistance.

## Context & Background

In times of war and national crisis, people with disabilities are among the most vulnerable sectors and are in critical need of intensive support. SHEKEL has mobilized in full emergency mode to ensure the safety and welfare of hundreds of people with disabilities throughout Israel who are living in physical danger and immense emotional distress amid unrelenting sirens and rocket explosions.

SHEKEL launched a nation-wide emergency therapeutic hotline to provide immediate therapeutic assistance and guidance for children and adults with disabilities, their families, and carers. Children and adults with disabilities are exceptionally vulnerable to paralyzing trauma and anxiety; the current situation is retriggering old traumas for many who experienced or witnessed atrocities after October 7 or were traumatized by evacuation. The call center is manned by a multi-professional team (psychologists, clinical social workers, therapists) specializing in trauma therapy for people with disabilities and will remain open for at least two months to address emergency-onset PTSD. SHEKEL is the only Israeli non-profit with expertise in trauma therapy for people with disabilities.

All vocational, day center, and cultural/social activities were closed under Home Front instruction, so residents are confined to housing 24/7, requiring staff presence around the clock. Approximately 150 SHEKEL apartments in central Israel and Jerusalem are exposed to ongoing missile barrages. In apartments where residents have severe multiple disabilities and use wheelchairs, one staff member per resident is needed to ensure everyone reaches the safe room on time; extra night staff are also required under new security conditions. A high proportion of male staff are missing due to army enlistment and many female staff with young children cannot come to work, so SHEKEL must hire additional staff and add daytime shifts.

# Request for Funding: Emergency Wartime Needs for People with Disabilities

In times of war and national crisis, people with disabilities are among the most vulnerable sectors of the community and are in critical need of intensive support and assistance, to ensure their safety and wellbeing.

SHEKEL is now mobilizing in full emergency mode to ensure the safety and welfare of hundreds of people with disabilities throughout Israel who are now living in physical danger and immense emotional distress, surrounded by unrelenting sirens and rocket explosions that have become part of their daily reality.

## Immediate Emergency Support

### 1. Nation-wide Emergency Therapeutic Call Center

Upon the outbreak of war, SHEKEL's therapeutic centers launched a nation-wide emergency hotline as an urgent measure to provide immediate therapeutic assistance and guidance for children and adults with disabilities, as well their families and carers, throughout the country. The need for this is now acute, since children and adults with disabilities are exceptionally vulnerable to paralyzing trauma and anxiety. Moreover, the Iran war is now retriggering old traumas for many previous victims of war with disabilities, who experienced and witnessed atrocities and terror following October 7., or were traumatized by evacuation where they were wrenched from familiar routines and communities. Their trauma, in turn, impacts the entire family, many of whom are themselves dealing with the trauma of war while caring for a loved one with disabilities. They often feel helpless to assist their family members with disabilities. For children and adults with disabilities, trauma is often exacerbated by a sense of helplessness and frustration derived from a limited understanding of what is going around them, combined with apprehensive surroundings.

The call center hotline provides immediate short-term non-frontal therapeutic intervention through zoom/phone calls, with an option for further long-term therapy, according to need for people with disabilities, as well as guidance and counseling for their families and the teams that work with them. The call center is manned by a multi-professional team of psychologists, clinical social workers, and therapists, all of whom specialize in trauma therapy for people with disabilities. The call center will remain open for at least two months in order to begin dealing with emergency onset of PTSD symptoms.

Since SHEKEL is the only Israel non-profit organization with expertise and experience in trauma therapy for people with disabilities, our work has now become critical for people with disabilities throughout the country.

### 2. Housing: Staff and security

With the onset of the war, all vocational, day center and cultural/social activities, were immediately closed down, under instruction from the Home Front. This means residents are now confined to their housing apartments 24/7, necessitating staff presence all hours of the day and night. This greatly increases the number of staff shifts needed as ordinarily residents are not home during normal daytime hours. For many of SHEKEL's housing residents, SHEKEL constitutes their real-world family. Many literally have nowhere else to go. As approximately 150 SHEKEL apartments are located in Israel's central, and Jerusalem regions, our housing residents are now exposed to massive ongoing missile fire barrages and frequent explosions, causing extreme distress and immediate danger. In apartments where residents have severe multiple disabilities and use wheel chairs, one staff member is needed per resident in order to ensure all residents safely enter the safe rooms on time. In addition, under the new security conditions, extra staff members are needed in apartments during the night.

A high proportion of male staff are now missing due to army enlistment, while many female staff with young children are unable to come to work, so that SHEKEL now has to take on additional new staff in addition to adding daytime shifts.

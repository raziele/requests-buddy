## The Ask

Aharai! (Leading Change Youth) is requesting urgent financial support to sustain and expand its youth programs during the Shaagat HaAri (Iran) war. With schools closed, frameworks paused, and 154 staff members (nearly one-third of the organization) called up for reserve duty, Aharai! is continuing to operate in-person and virtual activities for young people under Home Front Command guidelines. They need funds to recruit and train additional instructors, replace mobilized staff, expand programming in the north, run safe in-person and virtual activities, and support volunteer and resilience initiatives. The appeal was forwarded by Jewish Federations with no additional body text; the full request is in the attached PDF.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

## Context & Background

The war with Iran (Shaagat HaAri) has severely disrupted daily life in Israel: no routine, schools and youth frameworks closed, families under strain, and young people facing uncertainty, fear, and isolation. Aharai! continues to operate under fire in accordance with Home Front Command guidelines—activities near protected spaces with two adult staff—combining in-person meetings where possible with Zoom and personal mentoring so participants have support and structure.

The situation in the north is particularly severe. Aharai! is expanding programming and in-person activities, strengthening emotional resilience and community connection, organizing volunteer initiatives, reinforcing the north with additional Shinshinim and Mechinistim, and accelerating youth leadership and "launch" programs. At the same time, 154 staff (nearly one-third) have been called up to reserves, including leadership, while many remaining staff face mobilized partners, communities under threat, and inadequate home protection. The organization urgently needs to recruit additional instructors and increase capacity to provide consistent support.

Participants are also stepping up—e.g., Mechinat Gal Kiryat Bialik participants packing food with Leket and visiting public shelters with activities for children and families. The Israeli economy slows in wartime and philanthropic resources become more critical; Aharai! is expanding rather than scaling back and asks for partnership and financial support to recruit and train instructors, replace reserve-serving staff, expand in the north, operate safe activities, run virtual programming and mentoring, and support volunteer and resilience activities. Donations go to Bank Leumi Branch 805, Account 1896246, name "Noar Movil Shinui."

### Aharai! Urgent Appeal – Supporting Israel's Youth During the "Shaagat HaAri" War.pdf

**Urgent Appeal – Supporting Israel's Youth During the "Shaagat HaAri" War**

The war with Iran, Shaagat HaAri, has once again placed Israel in an extremely challenging reality. For many communities, daily life has been completely disrupted. There is no routine. Schools and youth frameworks are closed, families are under enormous strain, and young people are facing uncertainty, fear, and isolation.

At Aharai!, we believe that precisely in moments like these, our presence is more critical than ever.

**Aharai! Continues Operating Even Under Fire**

Despite the ongoing war, Aharai! programs are continuing to operate in accordance with the Home Front Command guidelines, ensuring that all activities take place near protected spaces and with the required presence of two adult staff members at every activity.

Where possible, we continue to hold in person meetings, alongside Zoom activities and personal mentoring, so that the young people in our programs continue to have support, structure, and a safe space during this difficult time.

With schools closed and many frameworks paused, our teams are working tirelessly to ensure that the young people we serve are not left alone with the stress and uncertainty of the current situation.

**A Special Focus on the North**

The situation in northern Israel is particularly severe. Young people in these communities need us now more than ever.

Instead of waiting for government frameworks and recovery plans, Aharai! is choosing to act now:

- Expanding programming and in-person activities when possible
- Strengthening emotional resilience and community connection
- Organizing volunteer initiatives and community support efforts
- Reinforcing the north with additional Shinshinim and Mechinistim
- Accelerating youth leadership and "launch" programs that help young people take responsibility and lead in their communities

**Our Team Is Also Serving**

Like many organizations across Israel, Aharai! has been deeply affected by reserve duty mobilization.

So far, **154 of our staff members, nearly one-third of the organization**, have been called up to serve in the reserves, including members of our leadership team.

At the same time, many of our remaining staff are coping with extremely difficult personal realities: families with mobilized partners, communities under constant threat, and homes without adequate protection.

Despite all of this, our team continues to show up for the young people who depend on us.

To sustain our activities, we urgently need to recruit additional instructors and increase staff capacity so that we can continue to provide consistent support for our participants.

**Our Youth Are Also Stepping Up**

Even in the midst of war, the young people of Aharai! are demonstrating extraordinary leadership and responsibility.

Participants from Mechinat Gal Kiryat Bialik branch, for example, joined national volunteer efforts by helping pack food packages with the organization Leket. Others visited public shelters across the city, bringing activities, crafts, and moments of joy for children and families spending long hours in protected spaces.

These small acts of kindness create something much larger: community resilience.

Our participants refuse to stand on the sidelines. They choose responsibility, solidarity, and action.

**We Need Your Help Now**

In times of war, the Israeli economy slows dramatically and philanthropic resources become even more critical. At the same time, the need for youth support has never been greater.

Aharai! is not scaling back, we are expanding our activities to meet the moment. But to do so, we urgently need financial support to:

- Recruit and train additional instructors
- Replace staff members serving in the reserves
- Expand programming in northern communities
- Operate safe in person activities near protected spaces
- Run virtual programming and personal mentoring
- Support volunteer initiatives and resilience activities for youth

**Stand With Israel's Next Generation**

The young people of Israel are facing one of the most difficult periods in recent years. They need stability, leadership, and hope. At Aharai!, we are committed to being there for them today, not a year from now.

Your support will allow us to continue standing with Israel's youth and strengthening the communities that depend on them. We urgently ask for your partnership and financial support at this critical time.

For any questions or additional information, please contact: contact@aharai.org.il

Thank you for standing with Aharai! and with the young people of Israel.

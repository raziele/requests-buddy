## The Ask

Galilee Medical Center in Nahariya is requesting support for a list of urgent equipment and infrastructure needs to maintain and strengthen emergency preparedness and continuity of care. The request is relayed by the Jewish Federation of Northern New Jersey’s Israel Office. Needs include renovation of fortified underground complexes ($5M and $5.8M), a new Hemato-Oncology Institute with PET-CT and cytotoxic cleanroom ($10M + $1.1M), ward upgrades (High-Risk Pregnancy, Gynecological, PICU), and critical medical equipment (monitors, defibrillators, ICU beds and ventilators, imaging, surgical and telemetry systems). The hospital serves as the primary trauma and referral center for the Western Galilee and must operate under ongoing security threats.

## Context & Background

Galilee Medical Center in Nahariya is the primary trauma and referral hospital for the entire Western Galilee and often continues to operate under security threats, including missile attacks. The forwarded email from the Jewish Federation of Northern New Jersey’s Israel Office attaches a March 2026 list of urgent needs. The sender (Ravit Steinmetz) states that the list is the medical center’s emergency needs and offers to obtain another format or further details if required.

The attached spreadsheet itemizes capital and equipment needs framed around emergency preparedness and continuity of care. Major infrastructure items include renovation of two fortified underground hospital complexes (one ~$3M for up to 110 people, one ~$2M for ~80 people) to ensure continuity of care during emergencies; a new Hemato-Oncology Institute with a PET-CT scanner (the center currently has no PET-CT, so Western Galilee residents must travel elsewhere); and a cytotoxic sterile cleanroom in the pharmacy for safe preparation of chemotherapy. Ward upgrades are requested for the High-Risk Pregnancy Ward (total project ~$1.7M, $700K needed to complete; unit is temporarily in a protected underground area, freeing the regular floor for renovation), the Gynecological Ward ($700K), and the Pediatric Intensive Care Unit ($555,800).

Equipment requests emphasize life-saving and diagnostic capacity during crises: monitor defibrillators for inpatient wards; ePM15 patient monitors (6) and TM80 transmitters for women’s ward; ICU beds (30 Sicuro Tera) and HAMILTON-C6 ICU ventilators; blood pressure monitors and ECG machines (20 each) with integration to electronic medical records; telemetry for internal medicine and for maternal–fetal medicine; Pentacam for ophthalmology; neurosurgery microscope and CURVE navigation; operating room stretchers; upgrade of gamma camera and workstations in nuclear medicine; Samsung HERA Z20 and BK Active ultrasound; CUSA generator for liver surgeries; and an NGS sequencing machine for rapid diagnostic and oncology use. The narrative throughout the attachment stresses the need to maintain and strengthen emergency response, trauma care, and uninterrupted services for vulnerable populations (cancer patients, high-risk pregnancies, critically ill children) in a region with a young and growing population and ongoing security pressures.

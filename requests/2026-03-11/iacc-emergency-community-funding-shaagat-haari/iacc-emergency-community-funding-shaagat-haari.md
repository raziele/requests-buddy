## The Ask

The Israel Association of Community Centers (HaHevra LeMatnasim) is requesting emergency program funding to address community needs during Operation Shaagat HaAri. Programs include "Together in the Building" (neighbor-to-neighbor resilience), early childhood frameworks for reservists' and essential workers' children, children and family support (Zoom activities, activity kits, resilience workshops), youth engagement and volunteering, elderly outreach and activity kits, and support for families of people with special needs. Estimated budget: $900,000/month ($225,000/week).

**Funding Requested:** $900,000 per month

**Funding Breakdown:**
- All populations — "Together in the Building" (equipment, local coordinators, competitions): $300,000/month
- Early childhood (activity kits, respite): $75,000/month
- Children and family (activity kits, Zoom, protected spaces): $200,000/month
- Youth ("Special Friends" coordinators, training, materials): $125,000/month
- Elderly (hotline, activity kits, visits, basic goods): $100,000/month
- Special needs (kits, facilitators, materials, parent support): $100,000/month

## Context & Background

Operation Shaagat HaAri has created a complex, prolonged security reality: multi-theater threats, sustained fire on civilian areas, prolonged stays in protected spaces, and disruption of community life. Families spend long hours in shelters, reservists are mobilized, education systems are closed or shifting to remote, and citizens face stress and uncertainty. The Society for Community Centers, through its nationwide network, is a key community anchor in routine and emergency. Multiple communities, local authorities, and partners have requested tailored, immediate responses. The proposal details needs by population: neighbor-to-neighbor resilience, early childhood frameworks, children and family support, youth engagement, elderly outreach, and special needs support.

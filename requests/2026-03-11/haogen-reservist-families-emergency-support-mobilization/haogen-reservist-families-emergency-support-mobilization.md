## The Ask

HaOgen for Reservist Families is requesting funding to sustain and expand emergency support during Israel's current massive mobilization (Operation Roaring Lion). They have scaled volunteer support and expanded to new cities. They are seeking support for one or more of four programs: (1) immediate relief for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles—meals and essential supplies at ~$650 per family, currently supporting three such families; (2) 10,000 grocery vouchers for volunteer cooks to provide meals to reservist families; (3) daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists (~$900/session), with over 100 children participating daily; (4) activity kits for children in bomb shelters at $12 per family. They ask the federation to indicate interest in supporting any of these specific needs.

**Funding Requested:** Multiple discrete asks: Program 1 ~$650/family (3 families currently); Program 2 — 10,000 grocery vouchers (total amount not specified); Program 3 ~$900/session daily; Program 4 $12/family for activity kits (city-specific).

**Funding Breakdown:**

- Grocery vouchers for volunteer cooks (10,000 vouchers): not specified

## Context & Background

Israel is in another massive mobilization due to the operation in Iran. HaOgen for Reservist Families supports reservist families and is in higher demand than ever. The difficulty for families is overwhelming: mothers with small children must reach shelters alone; parents need help with pets and childcare when schools are closed; essential workers need care for their children. HaOgen has increased volunteer support and matched volunteers in close radius to families, while expanding to new cities.

They are running several new programs during this operation and seeking funding for four. First, for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles, they provide immediate relief (meals and essential supplies) at approximately $650 per family and are currently supporting three families whose homes were destroyed. Second, they have drastically increased meals for reservist families and are raising funds for 10,000 grocery vouchers for volunteer cooks, as single parents at home cannot easily prepare meal after meal. Third, with schools closed, HaOgen runs daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists; over 100 children participate daily (2,640 total participants), at about $900 per session for content and technology. Fourth, they seek funding for activity kits for children in bomb shelters at $12 per family; funding for a specific city would have large impact, with communities ranging from under 100 to over 600 families.

They ask the federation to indicate whether they would be interested in supporting one or more of these needs to keep the programs running for as long as the current situation continues.

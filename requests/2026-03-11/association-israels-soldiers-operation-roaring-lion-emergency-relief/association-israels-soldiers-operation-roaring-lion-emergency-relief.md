## The Ask

The Association for Israel's Soldiers requests support for Operation Roaring Lion, its emergency relief effort following the security escalation from February 28, 2026. The association is the sole IDF-authorized nonprofit for soldier welfare; 100% of donations go directly to soldiers with no overhead. Current priorities are: hygiene and textile kits for troops in the field; refresh buildings and food trucks for physical and mental recharge at bases; vouchers for soldiers and families whose homes were damaged; and life-saving medical equipment including military ambulances. The portfolio also covers immediate financial aid for families of wounded and bereaved soldiers. The request was relayed via Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia with an ask to share the portfolio with other federations.

## Context & Background

On February 28, 2026, IDF and U.S. forces launched Operation Roaring Lion, a coordinated campaign against Iranian regime targets. The attack on Israel’s home front caused civilian casualties, injuries, and damage to residential buildings, including homes of IDF personnel. As of March 4, 2026, reported figures include 1,325+ Israelis injured, 13 killed, 571+ rockets fired into Israel, and 1,500+ families evacuated.

The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the sole organization authorized by the IDF to receive and distribute donations for soldier welfare. Through its Emergency Relief Fund it enables the IDF and Ministry of Defense to procure essential supplies without delay: basic hygiene and apparel, life-saving medical equipment (including military ambulances and mobile medical units), and support for impacted families—evacuated, wounded, and bereaved—including vouchers redeemable at dozens of store chains across Israel.

The email from Michal Cohen Amir (UFIS) notes that the association is running many refresh/recharge buildings for units focused on combat—IAF bases and air defense, Intelligence, and Home Front Command—and invites support for these efforts. The attached portfolio is updated almost daily. The request was forwarded by Talia Lidar (Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia) to Tamar Broady, with a note that it is an additional request from the Committee for the Soldier (הועד למען החייל).

# Roaring Lion Operation

## Israel Defense Forces (IDF) – Our Mission

The IDF, the State of Israel's military, was founded to defend the country, shield its residents, protect its independence, and foil attempts by its enemies to disrupt routine life. As the foundation of Israel's Ministry of Defense, the IDF and its strategies and tactics are directed by Israel's democratic civil government. IDF soldiers are obligated to devote every effort to protecting the State of Israel at all costs. The Association for Israel's Soldiers nonprofit is the sole organization that the IDF has authorized to receive and distribute donations for the welfare and well-being of IDF soldiers. 100% of all donations to the nonprofit go directly to benefit the servicepeople.

## Operation Roaring Lion – Context

On Saturday 28th of February, IDF and U.S. military forces launched a broad, coordinated campaign to significantly degrade the Iranian regime and eliminate existential threats to Israel. The operation targeted dozens of military sites. Despite previous setbacks, Iran has continued advancing its nuclear and missile programs and supporting proxies along Israel's borders. IDF forces remain on high alert across all fronts.

## Emergency Relief Fund – Main Efforts

1. **Lifesaving medical equipment**
2. **Bereaved families & wounded soldiers**
3. **Well-being & individual aid**

### The Need – Personal Hygiene Products & Apparel

- **Cost:** 200 NIS (male hygiene kit); 150 NIS (female hygiene kit; each kit shared by 4 soldiers)
- Little pleasures and comfortable personal clothing for soldiers who cannot shower for days; relief and motivation in the field.

### The Need – Physical & Mental Recharge

- **Cost:** approx. 100 NIS per soldier (40,000–250,000 NIS per military unit, depending on size)
- Pop-up service: nourishing meals, media, hot showers, haircuts, laundry; boosts morale and re-energizes battalions.

### The Need – Financial Aid for Evacuated Soldiers and Families

- **Cost:** 2,000 NIS per family
- Following the attack of Feb 28, 2026, vouchers for families whose homes were damaged; immediate response for food, clothing, basic household supplies.

### The Need – Life-Preserving Medical Gear

- **Cost:** per item
- Resources for combat paramedics: lifesaving interventions, breathing, blood circulation, bleeding control, monitoring, medication administration.

### The Need – Military Ambulances

- **Cost:** 650,000 NIS per fully equipped vehicle
- Type: Mercedes Sprinter. High mobility to outposts and bases; optimal treatment for IDF soldiers and citizens; donor recognition signage on vehicle exterior following the war.

### The Need – Financial Aid for Soldiers Injured Moderately or Seriously

- **Cost:** 5,000 NIS per family
- Within 24 hours of hospital admission, vouchers to the family; redeemable at about 60 store chains in Israel (food, clothing, electronics).

### The Need – Financial Aid for Bereaved Families in Mourning

- **Cost:** 2,000 NIS per family
- Immediate support during shiva (seven days of mourning); 2,000 NIS in vouchers for food, clothing, and critical needs; redeemable at about 60 store chains in Israel.

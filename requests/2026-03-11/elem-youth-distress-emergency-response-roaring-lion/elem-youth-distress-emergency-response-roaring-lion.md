## The Ask

ELEM is requesting funding to sustain and expand its emergency response since the launch of Operation "Roaring Lion." They have reinforced their nationwide digital support (weekdays until midnight, Hebrew and Arabic), strengthened 24/7 shelters in Jerusalem, Haifa, and Tel Aviv with trauma-informed staffing, and are providing calming kits for shelter residents and food baskets where needed. The request is based on an initial two-week assessment, with needs updated as the situation develops.

**Funding Requested:** 156,000 NIS / $50,485 USD

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Reinforced teams - 24/7 Shelters (Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Haifa): 101,000 NIS / $32,686
- Reinforced teams - ELEM Digital (national service): 25,000 NIS / $8,091
- Food Baskets: 20,000 NIS / $6,472
- Calming kits for youth staying in shelters: 10,000 NIS / $3,236

## Context & Background

Following the launch of Operation "Roaring Lion," ELEM shifted to emergency operations to ensure continuity of care for vulnerable teens and young adults. Across all programs, teams maintain continuous contact with teens; most support is delivered remotely in line with national safety guidelines. Trust built in advance has kept these connections meaningful and effective.

ELEM has expanded several services for an accessible, immediate response. Digital Outreach Support now runs on weekdays until midnight with reinforced, specially trained teams. The 24/7 shelters in Jerusalem, Haifa, and Tel Aviv have been strengthened so every shift is fully staffed and able to provide immediate, trauma-informed care. Demand is already rising among both Hebrew- and Arabic-speaking youth; shelter residents face intensified triggers and emotional distress and need more support while remaining indoors. Shelter graduates who had moved to independent living are returning during the day for support.

Additional activities include calming kits (headphones, coloring booklets, stress balls, grounding tools) for shelter residents and food baskets where need has been identified—ELEM has already distributed food baskets in Eilat. The attached document reflects an initial assessment for approximately two weeks; ELEM is updating needs as the situation develops. Support is sought to respond swiftly, expand services as needed, and maintain operational continuity under highly challenging conditions.

### Emergency Activities _ March 2026.pdf

**March 8, 2026**

**ELEM Emergency Response - Current Needs**

In light of the ongoing war, ELEM has shifted to emergency operations to ensure continuity of care for vulnerable teens and young adults.

**Emergency Programs Requiring Additional Funding**

1. **Reinforcement of ELEM Digital (National Service)**
   ELEM Digital is a nationwide online support service available to all teens in Israel, not only those previously engaged with ELEM. Due to the current situation, we are reinforcing staffing to expand support, both in Hebrew and Arabic, for teens who are unable to leave their homes and are experiencing heightened emotional distress. The increase in demand is already evident among both Hebrew and Arabic speaking youth.

2. **Strengthening 24/7 Shelters – Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Haifa**
   Our shelters operate around the clock and are staffed by ELEM's professional teams. We are increasing staffing levels to ensure continuous, high-quality, trauma-informed care. The young people in our shelters are experiencing intensified triggers and emotional distress and require significantly more support than usual, particularly as they must remain indoors around the clock. In addition to current residents, graduates who had transitioned to independent living are now returning during the day because they need support and do not want to remain alone.

3. **Calming Kits for Shelter Residents**
   We are preparing calming kits for youth staying in our shelters. These kits include practical items designed to help reduce anxiety and regulate stress, such as headphones, coloring booklets, stress balls, and other grounding tools. The kits are designated for the young people in our direct care.

4. **Food Baskets**
   We have identified a growing need for food assistance in certain areas among the population we serve. In Eilat, our team has already distributed food baskets, and we are working to respond to additional needs as they arise.

## The Ask

HaOgen for Reservist Families is requesting $200,000 in additional funding to scale emergency support during Operation Lion's Roar, as Israel experiences another large-scale reservist mobilization. The organization matches volunteers to reservist families for practical help (childcare, dog walking, meals) and has launched four programs: immediate relief (~$650 per family) for families whose homes were hit by missiles; 100,000 grocery coupons for volunteer cooks to feed families; daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists (~$900 per session, 100+ children daily); and activity kits for children in bomb shelters ($12 per family). They are currently supporting two reservist families whose homes were destroyed and aim to fund these efforts for communities of varying sizes.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

## Context & Background

Israel is in the midst of another major reservist mobilization due to the operation in Iran (Operation Lion's Roar). HaOgen for Reservist Families supports families left at home with practical and emotional support: matching volunteers in close radius for childcare, dog walking, and other daily needs, and expanding into new cities. Demand is higher than ever. Many families are overwhelmed — for example, a mother with three small children must reach the shelter alone during sirens; a parent needs someone to take the dog out at night; an essential worker needs childcare when schools are closed. HaOgen has increased volunteer support and is running several new programs during this operation.

Four specific initiatives are described: (1) Immediate relief for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles — meals and essential supplies at roughly $650 per family; the organization is currently supporting two such families, one from Beit Shemesh and one from Beer Sheva, whose homes were destroyed. (2) A large increase in meals via 100,000 grocery coupons for volunteer cooks, as the number of families with a reservist drafted at once is very high and the single parent at home cannot prepare meals without help. (3) Daily Zoom workshops for children of reservists, with over 100 children participating daily at about $900 per session for content and technology, providing both activity for children and relief for parents. (4) Activity kits for families with young children spending long periods in bomb shelters, at $12 per family, with impact scalable by community size (under 100 families, 300–400 families, or over 600 families).

The organization is seeking interest in supporting one or more of these needs and aims to raise an additional $200,000 to fund these efforts during this period.

## The Ask

Mitchashvim, a nonprofit that provides refurbished computers to close Israel's digital divide, requests $95,000 to provide 350 students with computers so they can participate in nationwide remote learning during the current war. Over 168,000 households in Israel lack a computer. The organization has received urgent requests in the past 48 hours from municipalities and institutions in Be'er Sheva (families displaced by a missile strike), Gush Halav (northern community under full lockdown), Haifa, and Ayanot. Funds would refurbish and distribute laptops and desktops to families with no current access to digital learning.

**Funding Requested:** $95,000

## Context & Background

Following the outbreak of war with Iran, Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks and the education system has moved to nationwide remote learning. Thousands of children cannot attend school because they do not have a computer. More than 168,000 households in Israel lack a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and peers. Mitchashvim was established to close this digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities; to date it has distributed over 48,000 refurbished computers across Israel. Its operational network is ready to respond rapidly to surging demand.

Urgent requests in the past 48 hours include: Be'er Sheva, where a missile strike on March 2, 2026 destroyed residential buildings and forced families with children into temporary housing (many now in hotels); Gush Halav, a northern community ~4 km from the border under full lockdown with red traffic corridors and no near-term return to in-person school; Haifa, supporting residents affected by the emergency; and Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village, where students without computers cannot access their studies. The municipality letters in the PDF specify immediate need for 70 computers for children in Be'er Sheva hotels, 100 for Gush Halav (20 high school, 80 elementary), and Haifa's request for 50 laptops (with specs: Intel Core i7, 512GB SSD, 16GB RAM, Windows 11 Pro), 50 desktops, and standard office monitors.

Each computer is framed as a lifeline to education, routine, hope, and resilience—enabling remote learning, contact with educators and classmates, access to materials, and emotional support, and providing stability and routine in an insecure reality. Without a rapid response, affected students risk significant learning gaps and deepened isolation and anxiety.

### Emergency Appeal Ensuring Israeli Children Can Continue Learning During War.pdf

**www.mitchashvim.org.il**  
March 6, 2026  
Subject: Emergency Appeal: Ensuring Israeli Children Can Continue Learning During War

Dear Friends and Partners,

In times of war, one of the most powerful ways to protect a child's future is to ensure they can continue learning. Over the past week, Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks following the outbreak of war with Iran. At the same time, Israel's education system has transitioned to nationwide remote learning, creating an urgent and painful reality: thousands of children cannot attend school simply because they do not have a computer.

More than 168,000 households in Israel lack a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and their peers during one of the most difficult moments in the country's history.

Our nonprofit initiative Mitchashvim was established to close Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities. In normal times we work to ensure equal educational opportunity. In times of crisis, this mission becomes even more critical. To date, we have distributed over 48,000 refurbished computers to families in need across Israel. Our experienced operational network is well established and ready to respond rapidly to surging demand during times of national emergency.

In the past 48 hours alone, we have received urgent requests from municipalities and educational institutions serving displaced families and vulnerable communities, including:

- Be'er Sheva, where a missile strike destroyed residential buildings and forced families with children into temporary housing
- Gush Halav, a northern community under full lockdown near the border
- Haifa, supporting residents affected by the emergency situation
- Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village, where students without computers cannot access their studies

Our immediate goal is to provide 350 students with computers. We are seeking **$95,000** to refurbish and distribute laptops and desktop computers to families who currently have no access to digital learning. Each computer distributed represents more than technology—it is a lifeline to education, routine, hope, and resilience.

Signed: Adv. Shelly Dvir, Chief Executive Officer, Metkhashvim

**Hebrew letter — Be'er Sheva municipality (March 4, 2026)**  
Re: Urgent need for computers for evacuated students following missile strike in Be'er Sheva

On March 2, 2026 Be'er Sheva suffered a direct missile hit causing significant destruction and affecting hundreds of families who were forced to evacuate immediately without basic equipment. Many families are now in hotels in the city. The affected population includes welfare families, immigrant families, families with children with disabilities, and elderly—communities that already face complex challenges; in emergency these gaps widen quickly and can lead to significant educational and social disconnection. Immediate need: **70 computers** for children in hotels. Without a rapid response, these students risk a significant learning gap and deepened sense of isolation and anxiety.

Contact: Shani Ora-Dotan, Be'er Sheva municipality

---

**Hebrew letter — Gush Halav local council (Jish) (March 4, 2026)**  
Urgent request for immediate assistance for Gush Halav children — computers for remote learning

Gush Halav is ~4 km from the border and has suffered severely. Due to the ongoing campaign, the education system is shut down: there are protection gaps in schools and in the village, and access routes are defined as "red corridors." Return to regular studies is expected to be significantly delayed, and children have no practical alternative. The only feasible solution at this time is remote learning, but there is a severe shortage of computers in the community. Request: **20 computers** for high school students, **80 computers** for elementary students.

Signed: Najam Sharif, Head of Gush Halav Council

---

**Hebrew letter — Haifa municipality (March 5, 2026)**  
Re: Donation of computers to Haifa municipality

In light of the current state of war and its challenges, request for assistance in computer equipment to ensure continuous and efficient operation of the municipality for the benefit of Haifa residents. Needs: (1) **50 laptops** — spec: Intel Core i7, 512GB SSD, 16GB RAM, Windows 11 Pro; (2) **50 desktops** — spec to be provided according to inventory and operational needs; (3) **Monitors** — standard size for full office work, according to inventory availability.

Signed: Dr. Galit Rand, Office of the CEO, Haifa municipality

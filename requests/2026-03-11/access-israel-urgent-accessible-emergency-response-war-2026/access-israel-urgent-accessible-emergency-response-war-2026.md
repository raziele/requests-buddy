## The Ask

Access Israel is requesting an urgent initial allocation of $400,000 for ten days of critical activity in response to the outbreak of war with Iran on February 28, 2026. The organization aims to ensure accessible, professional, and sensitive emergency response for people with disabilities and the elderly. Since October 7, Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian assistance for the most vulnerable populations—accessible evacuation, accessible alternative and fortified housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal and logistical support—often where no other entity provided adequate response. The requested funds would activate and expand the Purple Vest (אפוד הסגול) emergency response system: a 24/7 accessible emergency hotline, volunteer training and deployment, accessible vehicles with wheelchair lifts, and accessible fortified accommodation for evacuees.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000 (initial, for ten days of activity; continuation to be assessed with partners according to developments and duration of conflict)

**Funding Breakdown:**
- 24/7 accessible emergency operations center (hotline, professional staff, Purple Vest app development and maintenance, communications, real-time response to thousands of expected inquiries): included in total
- Purple Vest training, refreshment, and activation (volunteer training in municipalities, accessible evacuation equipment, operational management and coordination for rapid accessible assistance in missile strike incidents): included in total
- Wheelchair-accessible vehicles with lifts (operation of accessible evacuation vehicles: drivers, fuel, insurance, maintenance for safe extraction and transfer to medical care and accessible fortified alternative housing): included in total
- Accessible fortified alternative accommodation for evacuees (immediate funding for stays in hotels and accessible fortified solutions for families with members with disabilities forced to evacuate from inaccessible or damaged homes): included in total
- Lifesaving information dissemination (digital and media campaigns, preparedness and emergency assistance content, website maintenance, and real-time accessibility of vital information nationwide): included in total

## Context & Background

Access Israel (עמותת נגישות ישראל) promotes accessibility, inclusion, and quality of life for people with disabilities and the elderly in all spheres of life. The organization addresses accessibility in public spaces and services, awareness-raising, policy and legislation, enforcement, innovative solutions and technology for independent living and inclusion, and accessibility in emergency preparedness and response. Approximately 40% of people with disabilities and elderly citizens in Israel live without accessible shelter; when sirens sound they have no real alternative and often remain in place in fear. Since October 7, Access Israel has provided accessible evacuation, accessible fortified alternative housing, medical equipment, dedicated accessible transport, and personal and logistical support in severe distress—in many cases where neither the state nor local authorities provided sufficient response. With the outbreak of the new round of fighting on February 28, 2026, the organization faces a renewed, significant, and urgent need to ensure accessible, professional, and sensitive assistance. The request emphasizes that the most vulnerable often receive institutional response last, and that donor support is the mainstay of the organization's ability to act quickly and save lives.

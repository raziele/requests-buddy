## The Ask

Mitchashvim, a nonprofit that provides refurbished computers to close Israel's digital divide, is requesting $95,000 to supply 350 students with computers during the current national emergency. Israel has moved to nationwide remote learning following missile attacks and war with Iran; more than 168,000 households lack a computer, leaving thousands of children unable to attend school. The organization has received urgent requests from Be'er Sheva (families displaced after a direct missile strike), Gush Halav (a northern community under lockdown), Haifa, and Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village. Mitchashvim has already distributed over 48,000 computers and is seeking funding to refurbish and distribute laptops and desktops so these students can reconnect to classrooms and restore stability.

**Funding Requested:** $95,000

## Context & Background

Following the outbreak of war with Iran, Israel's civilian home front has faced devastating missile attacks and the education system has transitioned to nationwide remote learning. More than 168,000 households in Israel lack a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and peers. Mitchashvim was established to close Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities; to date it has distributed over 48,000 computers across Israel and has an operational network ready to respond to surging demand in emergencies.

In the past 48 hours before the appeal (early March 2026), the organization received urgent requests from municipalities and institutions: Be'er Sheva, where a missile strike on March 2, 2026 destroyed residential buildings and forced families with children into temporary housing (many in hotels), with a documented need for 70 computers for children in hotels; Gush Halav, a northern community about 4 km from the border under full lockdown with "red" access routes and no practical alternative to remote learning, requesting 20 computers for high school students and 80 for elementary students; Haifa, requesting 50 laptops (Intel Core i7, 512GB SSD, 16GB RAM, Windows 11 Pro) and 50 desktops plus standard office screens for municipal continuity; and Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village, where students without computers cannot access their studies.

The immediate goal is to provide 350 students with computers to reconnect to school and restore stability. The ask is $95,000 to refurbish and distribute laptops and desktop computers to families with no access to digital learning. The appeal frames each computer as a lifeline to education, routine, hope, and resilience during a national emergency.

**Be'er Sheva municipality letter (Hebrew, 04 March 2026)**  
From: Shay Ora-Dotan, Be'er Sheva Municipality  
Subject: Urgent need for computers for evacuated students following missile strike in Be'er Sheva

On 02.03.2026 Be'er Sheva suffered a direct missile hit causing significant destruction and affecting hundreds of families who were forced to evacuate immediately without basic equipment. A significant portion of families are now in a hotel in the city, trying to pick up the pieces and restore a sense of security and routine for their children. The affected population includes welfare families, immigrant families, families with children with disabilities and elderly — communities that already face complex challenges. In emergency, these gaps widen quickly and can lead to significant educational and social disconnection. For these evacuated children, a computer is not a luxury but an anchor: it enables participation in remote learning, contact with educational staff and classmates, access to materials and emotional support. Immediate need: **70 computers** for children in the hotels. Without a rapid response, these students may face a significant learning gap and deepening sense of disconnection and anxiety.

**Gush Halav (Jish) local council (Hebrew, 4 March 2026)**  
From: Najem Sharif, Head of Gush Halav Council  
Subject: Urgent request for immediate assistance for the children of Gush Halav — computers for remote learning

The settlement of Gush Halav, only about 4 km from the border, has suffered severely. Due to the ongoing campaign, the education system is shut down: there are shelter gaps in schools and across the village, and access routes to and from Gush Halav are defined as "red routes." Return to regular studies is expected to be significantly delayed, while children have no practical alternative. The only possible solution at this time is remote learning, but there is a severe shortage of computers in the settlement. Request: **20 computers** for high school students; **80 computers** for elementary students. Assistance would ensure basic educational continuity and equal opportunities for the children of the settlement during this challenging period.

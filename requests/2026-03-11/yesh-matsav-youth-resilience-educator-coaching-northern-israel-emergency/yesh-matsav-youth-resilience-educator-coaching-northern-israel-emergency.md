## The Ask

Yesh Matsav requests $50,000 in emergency funding to scale its proven youth resilience and educator-coaching model in Northern Israel, where schools and communities have been under sustained rocket and drone attack since Hezbollah joined the war in late February 2026. The grant would fund Resilience Hubs for 100 educators, life-changing role-model encounters for 300+ youth in bomb shelters, crisis kits for 20 schools, and scaling of an AI-powered mentorship tool, reaching 400 youth and 100 educators in the North.

**Funding Requested:** $50,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Teacher Training: Establishing Resilience Hubs for 100 educators in Northern schools: $20,000
- Life-Changing Encounters: 10+ Key Experience sessions with role models for 300+ youth in bomb shelters: $15,000
- Shelter Crisis Kits for 20 schools: $10,000
- Digital Resilience (AI Mentor): Scale AI-powered mentorship for 24/7 guidance and support: $5,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Distribution and partnership support to reach potential funders (recipient offered to distribute and assist).

## Context & Background

Northern Israel has been under relentless assault since Hezbollah joined the war on February 28, 2026. In the week prior to the request, over 210 rockets and drones targeted the Galilee and Haifa, forcing thousands of youth into bomb shelters for days. Yesh Matsav, a non-profit dedicated to at-risk youth and founded by Dr. Lior Zoref (former Microsoft VP, TED speaker), positions itself as the "Resilience Anchor" for schools in conflict zones. Their model turns trauma into growth by training teachers as personal coaches for high-risk youth.

Yesh Matsav reached 2,000 youth during 2025, with 80% showing significant improvement in motivation and achievements. Since October 7th they have worked in the Gaza envelope (Nofey Habsor: 30 teachers, 60 high-risk students), ORT Shlomi in the North (25 teachers, 50 students using coaching and Docu-therapy), with IDF orphans and widows in partnership with the Ministry of Defense, and in Bat Yam during the first Iran war in 2025. They are supported by the President of Israel, the Ministry of Education (methodology and partial funding for teacher training), Tech for Israel, ORT Israel, and private donors.

The organization is requesting immediate deployment capacity rather than waiting for long-awaited official response efforts. The $50,000 emergency budget would establish Resilience Hubs for 100 educators, deliver 10+ "Key Experience" sessions to 300+ youth in shelters, provide resilience kits to 20 schools, and scale an AI mentor for 24/7 support. A principal from ORT Shlomi is quoted describing the training as "truly life changing" for evacuated students.

# Emergency Urgent Budget Request ($50,000) for Youth Impact by Iran war in Northern Israel

## Who We Are & What We Do

Yesh Matsav (yeshmatsav.org/en) is a non-profit organization based in Israel, dedicated to supporting at-risk youth impacted by the current war in Israel, helping to rebuild Israel's future.

Our distinguished board and founders include Dr. Lior Zoref, former Microsoft VP, TED speaker, and best-selling author; Yaniv Rivlin, serial entrepreneur and founding CEO of Bird Israel; Hana David, a leading figure in Israeli education; and Yosi Taguri, serial entrepreneur and CEO of ZipRecruiter Israel.

We achieve this through these programs:

- **Teacher/School Support:** We equip educators with the tools and strategies to support high school students in the most challenging situations.
- **Inspiring Role Models:** We connect youth with successful individuals who share similar backgrounds, igniting their potential and hope for the future. See examples in this video.

**Proven Results:** We reached 2,000 youth during 2025; 80% show significant improvement in motivation and achievements.

## Current Partnerships

- **The President of Israel:** We are fortunate to have the support of the President of Israel, Mr. Isaac Herzog, who endorses our mission. For further insights, watch this video.
- **Ministry of Education:** Our partner in developing our methodologies. They also offer partial funding of our professional training programs for teachers.

## Funding Sources

## Our Response and Activities Since October 7th

Following the October 7th war, we have focused our efforts to address the educational chaos caused by the displacement of communities and high increase in at-risk youth cases:

- **Nofey Habsor (Gaza Envelope):** We trained 30 teachers to provide direct coaching to 60 high-risk students.
- **ORT Shlomi (North):** Supported 25 teachers and 50 students during displacement and their return home using advanced coaching and Docu-therapy.
- **IDF Orphans and Widows:** Specialized resilience programs for widows and orphans in partnership with the Ministry of Defense.
- **Bat Yam (First Iran War, 2025):** Immediate crisis support for educators in a city where 70 buildings were struck.

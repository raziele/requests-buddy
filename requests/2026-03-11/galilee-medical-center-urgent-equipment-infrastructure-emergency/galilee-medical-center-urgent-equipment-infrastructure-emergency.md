## The Ask

Galilee Medical Center in Nahariya, the primary referral and trauma hospital for the Western Galilee, is requesting support for a list of urgent equipment and infrastructure needs. The list was forwarded by the Jewish Federation of Northern New Jersey’s Israel Office and includes major capital items (e.g. renovation of fortified underground complexes, new Hemato-Oncology Institute with PET-CT, MRI 3 Tesla, NGS machine) and clinical equipment (ICU ventilators, monitors, defibrillators, ultrasound, surgical and ophthalmology devices). Total requested funding across the attached spreadsheet is approximately $26.7 million. The hospital frames these needs in the context of emergency preparedness and continuity of care during security threats and mass-casualty events.

## Context & Background

Galilee Medical Center in Nahariya serves as the main hospital for the Western Galilee and operates in a region that frequently faces security threats. The email forwards a March 2026 list of urgent needs; the sender (Ravit Steinmetz, Director of the Israel Office of the Jewish Federation of Northern New Jersey) states that the attached list is the hospital’s list of emergency needs and offers to obtain a different format or additional details if required.

The attached spreadsheet itemizes 24 projects with requested amounts and descriptions. Large capital items include: renovation and upgrading of two protected underground hospital complexes ($5M total described as $3M for one complex for up to 110 people and $2M for another for approximately 80 people); a new MRI 3 Tesla scanner ($5.8M) for emergency and trauma imaging; and a fortified Hemato-Oncology Institute with PET-CT ($10M), which would provide the hospital’s first PET-CT and allow Western Galilee residents to access this diagnostic close to home. Other infrastructure includes a cytotoxic sterile cleanroom for chemotherapy preparation ($1.1M), upgrades to the High-Risk Pregnancy and Gynecological Wards ($700K each), Pediatric ICU upgrade ($555,800), and an NGS machine ($388K) for diagnostics and research. The remainder covers clinical equipment: ICU ventilators (HAMILTON-C6), monitor-defibrillators, patient monitors (ePM15), blood pressure monitors, ECG, telemetry for internal medicine and maternal–fetal medicine, ICU beds, Pentacam for ophthalmology, neurosurgery microscope, operating room stretchers, gamma camera and nuclear medicine workstations, Samsung HERA Z20 and BK Active ultrasound, and CUSA generator for liver surgery.

The descriptions emphasize emergency preparedness, continuity of care during missile attacks or mass-casualty events, and the need for rapid, accurate diagnostics and life-saving equipment. The hospital states that the High-Risk Pregnancy Ward has been temporarily relocated to a protected underground area, creating an opportunity to renovate the regular ward. Amounts are given in dollars in the spreadsheet.

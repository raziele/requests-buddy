## The Ask

The Association for Israel's Soldiers (the IDF-authorized nonprofit for soldier welfare) is requesting support for its Emergency Relief Fund in the context of Operation Roaring Lion (post–February 28, 2026 escalation). They run refresh activities for units in the current fighting focus (IAF bases, intelligence, Home Front Command) and seek funding for four main efforts: hygiene kits and textiles, refresh activities and food trucks, vouchers for soldiers and families whose homes were damaged, and life-saving medical equipment. They have also asked that the portfolio be shared with other federations or that the association can send it directly.

## Context & Background

On February 28, 2026, IDF and U.S. forces launched Operation Roaring Lion, a coordinated campaign against Iranian regime targets. The security situation has kept IDF forces on high alert across all fronts, with the Israeli Air Force conducting precision strikes. As of March 4, 2026, the PDF reports over 1,325 Israelis injured, 13 killed, 571+ families evacuated, 1,391+ rockets fired into Israel, and 1,500+ evacuated into Israel.

The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the sole organization authorized by the IDF to receive and distribute donations for soldier welfare; 100% of donations go directly to soldiers with no overhead. Through its Emergency Relief Fund it enables the IDF and Ministry of Defense to procure essential supplies without delay: basic personal necessities (hygiene, non-military clothing, respite), life-saving medical equipment (ambulances, mobile medical units), and support for impacted families (evacuated, wounded, bereaved) including vouchers and emergency grants.

The email notes that the association is running many refresh (רענון) activities for units currently in the fighting focus—IAF bases, intelligence, and Home Front Command—and offers to provide updates on refresh activities at various bases. The portfolio is updated almost daily. The request is relayed via Jewish Federation of Greater Philadelphia (Talia Lidar, Tamar Broady) from Michal Cohen Amir at UFIS, with Shirley Sobel Yosiphon (Foreign Affairs Director) on the correspondence.

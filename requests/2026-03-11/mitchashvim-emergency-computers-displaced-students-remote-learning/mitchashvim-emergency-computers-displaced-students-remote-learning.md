## The Ask

Mitchashvim, a nonprofit that provides refurbished computers to close Israel's digital divide, is issuing an emergency appeal for $95,000 to provide 350 students from displaced and disadvantaged families with computers so they can participate in nationwide remote learning during the current war-related emergency. Following missile attacks and the switch to remote education, municipalities and institutions have requested computers for affected students; the organization has distributed over 48,000 computers to date and is seeking rapid support to meet surging demand.

**Funding Requested:** $95,000

## Context & Background

Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks following the outbreak of war with Iran, while the education system has transitioned to nationwide remote learning. More than 168,000 households in Israel lack a computer, leaving thousands of children unable to attend school. Mitchashvim was established to close Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities; to date it has distributed over 48,000 refurbished computers across Israel and has an established operational network to respond rapidly in national emergencies.

In the past 48 hours the organization received urgent requests from municipalities and educational institutions: Be'er Sheva (where a missile strike destroyed residential buildings and forced families with children into temporary housing), Gush Halav (a northern community under full lockdown near the border), Haifa (supporting residents affected by the emergency), and Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village (where students without computers cannot access their studies). Supporting letters in the attachment detail immediate needs: Be'er Sheva requests 70 computers for displaced children in hotels; Gush Halav requests 20 computers for high school and 80 for elementary students; Haifa requests 50 laptops and 50 desktops for municipal continuity.

The immediate goal is to provide 350 students with computers to reconnect to school and restore stability. The organization is seeking $95,000 to refurbish and distribute laptops and desktop computers to families with no access to digital learning.

### Emergency Appeal Ensuring Israeli Children Can Continue Learning During War.pdf

**Header:** www.mitchashvim.org.il  
**Date:** March 6, 2026  
**Subject:** Emergency Appeal: Ensuring Israeli Children Can Continue Learning During War

**Dear Friends and Partners,**

In times of war, one of the most powerful ways to protect a child's future is to ensure they can continue learning.

Over the past week, Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks following the outbreak of war with Iran. At the same time, Israel's education system has transitioned to nationwide remote learning, creating an urgent and painful reality: thousands of children cannot attend school simply because they do not have a computer.

More than **168,000 households** in Israel lack a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and their peers during one of the most difficult moments in the country's history.

Our nonprofit initiative Mitchashvim was established to close Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities. In normal times we work to ensure equal educational opportunity. In times of crisis, this mission becomes even more critical.

To date, we have distributed **over 48,000 refurbished computers** to families in need across Israel. Our experienced operational network is well established and ready to respond rapidly to surging demand during times of national emergency.

In the past 48 hours alone, we have received urgent requests from municipalities and educational institutions serving displaced families and vulnerable communities, including:

- **Be'er Sheva:** missile strike destroyed residential buildings and forced families with children into temporary housing
- **Gush Halav:** northern community under full lockdown near the border
- **Haifa:** supporting residents affected by the emergency situation
- **Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village:** students without computers cannot access their studies

**Immediate goal:** Provide **350 students** with computers.

**Funding request:** **$95,000** to refurbish and distribute laptops and desktop computers to families who currently have no access to digital learning.

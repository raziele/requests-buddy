## The Ask

MATI Jerusalem Business Development Center sent a field update on its mobilization following the outbreak of the war with Iran in early March 2026. The Jerusalem Municipality asked MATI to lead an emergency series of eight webinars for business owners (four for West Jerusalem, four for East Jerusalem) covering marketing, client retention, crisis management, and AI for business. ## Context & Background

The morning after the war with Iran broke out (Saturday), MATI's management convened on Zoom to coordinate a response. The Jerusalem Municipality had already designated MATI as a key partner to help entrepreneurs navigate the crisis and asked MATI to lead eight emergency webinars across the city. MATI's business consultants continue to provide one-on-one consulting online, and several programs have been moved online: Hebrew for Employment in East Jerusalem (via Zoom), TIFLI preschool webinars for families and daycare managers on helping children cope with anxiety and supporting staff mental health, and financial literacy workshops plus a Facebook-based competition that has grown by 120 new participants.

On International Women's Day, MATI highlighted its work with women entrepreneurs through accelerator courses, dedicated women's forums, and other services. In 2025, 800 women benefited from the Turning Point program, 612 received one-on-one business consulting, women entrepreneurs raised $1 million in business loans, and 4,000 women participated in courses, workshops, and webinars. The email references impact stories (e.g., Tsiporah's ceramics shop, Hifa'a's kindergarten in Shuafat), short impact videos for West and East Jerusalem, and a new website page for women entrepreneurs.

The message is framed as an update for an existing relationship ("We are deeply grateful for your interest") and closes with an open invitation to become more involved and to reach out, without specifying a funding request or deadline.

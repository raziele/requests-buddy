## The Ask

JDC is requesting $6.876 million in emergency funding to respond to the March 2026 missile attacks from Iran and Lebanon. The request covers humanitarian relief for five directly hit cities ($200K per city), welfare support for 10 northern frontline municipalities ($150K per city), the All the Way Home program for up to 300 displaced households ($3.3M), accessibility in shelters and shelter kits for children with disabilities ($200K), emergency replacement of medical oxygen cylinders for municipal response teams ($576K), and heightened security and support for Jewish communities in Europe ($300K). JDC has already deployed rapid response in Beit Shemesh and the North using unrestricted funds and is now seeking additional funding to scale and sustain the response.

# Responding to Emergency in Israel: Urgent Funding Needs | March 4, 2026

## Israel Again in the Shadow of War

Over the past few days, Israel was once again thrown into war. In the last 100 hours, hundreds of missiles and drones were launched from Iran – and more recently, Lebanon – to Israel, sending millions of Israelis to safe rooms and bomb shelters at all hours of the day and night. While most were intercepted, as of March 3rd, direct hits in Tel Aviv, Beit Shemesh, Jerusalem, and Be'er Sheva caused 12 fatalities, hundreds of injuries, and extensive damage to homes and infrastructure, forcing over 1,500 citizens to leave their homes and evacuate to hotels.

JDC is there. Since the first news of the attacks, JDC drew on its established networks of government ministries and local partners to assess emerging needs and explore possible solutions. Using unrestricted and emergency funding, JDC has created strong foundations that allowed quick and effective action.

- **Digital Tools:** JDC-developed platforms (education and mental health professionals, AI-powered support apps for reservists' spouses, data dashboards for multi-ministerial round tables) anchor digital infrastructure for vulnerable populations.
- **Municipal Partnership:** Work with municipalities (Mashiv Haruach, Northern Initiative) strengthened community resilience and reinforced JDC's role as convener and facilitator.
- **Leveling the Playing Field in Critical Aid:** JDC equipped volunteer response teams in low-income municipalities with Advanced Life Support, Basic Life Support, and CPR kits, and established locally based Emergency Medical Units.

The following funding menu outlines the most urgent priorities.

## Highlighted Urgent Funding Needs

### 1. Humanitarian Relief for Directly Hit Cities
Support for cities absorbing massive, direct missile damage (e.g., Beit Shemesh, Be'er Sheva). **$200,000 per city x 5 cities = $1,000,000**

- Immediate Humanitarian Relief: clothing and essential supplies for families (toiletries, clothing, basic necessities, special items for children), in partnership with Shinua Hevrati.
- Emergency Readiness and Medical Equipment: medical and first response equipment for municipal emergency teams (tourniquets, bandages, stretchers, advanced and basic lifesaving kits, rescue kits).
- Flexible Humanitarian Assistance Fund: dedicated flexible fund for the municipality.
- Deployment of Community Caseworkers: two full-time community caseworkers to reinforce municipal welfare department.

### 2. Welfare Support for Northern Frontline Municipalities
Support for low-socio-economic authorities in the North. **$150,000 per city x 10 cities = $1,500,000**

JDC has received urgent appeals from seven municipalities: Ma'ale Yosef, Shlomi, Kiryat Shmona, Nahariya, Hurfeish, Deir al-Asad, Mevo'ot Hermon. Requested support includes food, essential household supplies, mattresses, biodegradable and accessible medical equipment, community support personnel, resilience and training packages, activity kits, and emergency relief operations. JDC is active in 24 locations along the northern confrontation line.

### 3. All the Way Home (Ad HaBayit) Program for Families Whose Apartments Were Severely Damaged
**$3,300,000**

Personalized housing transition support for up to 300 of the most vulnerable households in hard-hit cities. Helps displaced residents access rights, navigate bureaucracy, and find viable housing. 100 families already identified; demand expected to grow. (In the June war with Iran, some 13,000 Israelis were displaced, including 4,000 particularly vulnerable.)

### 4. Increasing Accessibility in Shelters for People with Disabilities and Accessible Shelter Kits for Children
**$200,000**

- Equipping municipalities with accessibility tools: portable ramps, marked stairs and thresholds, guiding strips, specialized signage; training for staff and emergency volunteers.
- Accessible shelter kits for children: small pop-up tents, weighted blankets, cozy floor mats, noise-canceling headphones to reduce distress and manage emotions.

### 5. Emergency Replacement of Medical Gas Cylinders
**$576,000**

Mid-February 2026 Ministry of Health directive identified safety concerns with existing cylinders in some kits. JDC to procure new, certified oxygen cylinders: 300 immediately, remaining 1,142 within one to three weeks. Includes centralized collection and phased replacement, with coordination with MOH and national authorities.

### 6. Increased Needs of Global Jewish Communities
**$300,000**

- Netherlands: expanded social work and mental-health assistance for vulnerable Jewish students and families; bolstered crisis-response systems.
- Turkey, France, Brussels, Tunisia: acute increases in security threats around schools, synagogues, youth movements, and communal gatherings; help absorb rising security costs and reinforce protective infrastructure.

## The Ask

Dror Israel is requesting $350,000 in emergency support for the next two weeks to sustain and expand its crisis response during the current escalation. As families move into bomb shelters and evacuee centers following Iran's missile barrage, the organization's educators are providing emergency childcare for essential workers' children, educational and emotional activities in shelters and evacuee hotels, trauma-informed pedagogical support to its school network, and resilience programming through movement and dance. The request will fund staff, transportation, supplies, and coordination to continue reaching more than 200,000 children, teens, and families.

**Funding Requested:** $350,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school at hospital for medical staff children (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels (staff, supplies, transportation): $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope – trauma support for 10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools: $80,000
- Resilience through Community Movement and Dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): $10,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Guidance or introductions to others who may be interested in supporting this work; willingness to offer a short presentation or briefing for the community or leadership to share what educators are seeing and doing on the ground.

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and living in constant fear and uncertainty. Dror Israel, a network of 1,300 trained educators living in 14 educators' kibbutzim, is providing educational activities, emotional support, and emergency childcare nationwide. In partnership with local municipalities and government, they are assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses while activating their network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and Zoom programming.

Current priorities include emergency childcare centers for essential workers' children in multiple cities (including a requested pop-up school at a major hospital); daily activities for evacuee children in Tel Aviv and Be'er Sheva; activities in public bomb shelters in Akko, Rishon Letzion, Carmiel, Tiberias and elsewhere, with hundreds of trained teen counselors deployed with activity kits; trauma-informed pedagogical support for 29 schools (one, Moledet in Be'er Sheva, was hit by a missile fragment); and online resilience sessions through movement and dance in Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, and Haifa.

Sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires continued operating support. The organization is seeking emergency funding to ensure it can continue reaching all participants and respond quickly as urgent needs emerge.

## Current Situation

As Iran's deadly missile barrage continues, Israelis must once again move into bomb shelters, evacuate damaged homes, and brace for the next siren. Across the country, Israelis are living in a constant state of fear and uncertainty, experiencing trauma upon trauma as families once again move into bomb shelters and daily life is repeatedly disrupted. Our trained educators are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in bomb shelters, evacuee centers, and even in living rooms across the country. Nimble and ready to go wherever we are needed, we are addressing urgent emotional and educational needs in real time, ensuring that no child feels alone.

Dror Israel educators, in partnership with local municipalities and government partners, are actively assessing evolving needs and coordinating targeted emergency responses as conditions change. Simultaneously, we are activating our nationwide network of more than 200,000 children, teens, and families through direct outreach, phone calls, and structured Zoom programming to ensure participants are safe, emotionally supported, and connected to trusted adults. This is our core educational work adapted to emergency conditions, and sustaining educator hours, digital platforms, and coordination during this intensified period requires continued operating support.

## Seeking Support for Escalating Needs

Dror Israel is seeking **$350,000** in emergency support for the next two weeks to ensure we can continue reaching our 200,000 participants nationwide, provide activities and emotional support for children and families in bomb shelters and evacuee hotels, and respond quickly as urgent needs continue to emerge.

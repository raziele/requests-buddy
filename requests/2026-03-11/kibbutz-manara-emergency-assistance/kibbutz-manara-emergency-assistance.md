## The Ask

Kibbutz Manara is requesting emergency assistance to meet urgent needs during the current emergency period. The request has four components: (1) immediate security needs—essential tactical gear and personal equipment for the community's local response team (Kitat Konenut) that the army does not provide; (2) shelter equipment—items to make shelters functional and habitable, with special emphasis on children and youth shelters for safety and emotional well-being during long stays; (3) resilience activities in shelters—emotional relief and "breathing space" activities via Zoom or by professionals willing to travel to Manara; (4) support for reservist families—practical assistance such as vouchers for food and essential products. The request is a follow-up to a previous email and is sent in coordination with the Jewish Federation Israel Office.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

## Context & Background

Kibbutz Manara's Resource Development Manager, Iris Hen, is writing to Barak Loozon at the Jewish Federation Israel Office as a follow-up to a conversation and an email from the previous day. The community is operating under an emergency period and has identified its most urgent needs, which are detailed in two attached tables (provided as Word documents) that could not be read in this analysis.

The request centers on two equipment lists—one for the Kitat Konenut (local emergency response team) and one for shelter equipment—plus programmatic support for resilience and for reservist families. The emphasis on children and youth shelters reflects the reality of prolonged stays in shelters and the need for both physical safety and emotional well-being. Resilience activities are proposed via Zoom or in-person by professionals traveling to Manara, and reservist family support is framed as practical assistance (vouchers for food and essentials).

The sender explicitly thanks the recipient for goodwill and constant readiness to help and expresses hope for a return to quiet times and routine rehabilitation and growth. No specific dollar or shekel amounts appear in the email body; any budget figures would be in the attached .doc files.

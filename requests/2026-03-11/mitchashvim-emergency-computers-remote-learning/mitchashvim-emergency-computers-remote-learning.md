## The Ask

Mitchashvim, a nonprofit that closes Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities, is requesting $95,000 in emergency funding to provide 350 computers to children who cannot access remote learning during the current war. Following the outbreak of war with Iran and Israel's shift to nationwide remote learning, more than 168,000 households lack a computer, leaving thousands of students disconnected. The organization has distributed over 48,000 computers to date and has received urgent requests in the past 48 hours from Be'er Sheva (families displaced after a missile strike), Gush Halav (community near the border under full lockdown), Haifa, and Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village. The funding would refurbish and distribute laptops and desktops to allow these students to reconnect to school and restore stability.

**Funding Requested:** $95,000

## Context & Background

Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks following the outbreak of war with Iran, while the education system has transitioned to nationwide remote learning. More than 168,000 households in Israel lack a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and peers during a national emergency.

Mitchashvim was established to close Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities. To date it has distributed over 48,000 refurbished computers across Israel. Its operational network is established and ready to respond rapidly to surging demand.

In the past 48 hours the organization has received urgent requests from municipalities and institutions: Be'er Sheva, where a missile strike destroyed residential buildings and forced families with children into temporary housing (immediate need for 70 computers for children in hotels); Gush Halav, a northern community under full lockdown about 4 km from the border, with schools closed and no practical alternative (20 computers for high school, 80 for elementary); Haifa, for continued municipal operation and residents (50 laptops with specific specs, 50 desktops, monitors); and Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village, where students without computers cannot access studies. Without a rapid response, evacuated and isolated students risk significant learning gaps and deepened anxiety. The immediate goal is to provide 350 students with computers so they can reconnect to school and restore a sense of stability.

### Emergency Appeal Ensuring Israeli Children Can Continue Learning During War.pdf

www.mitchashvim.org.il
March 6, 2026
Subject: Emergency Appeal: Ensuring Israeli Children Can Continue Learning During War

Dear Friends and Partners,

In times of war, one of the most powerful ways to protect a child's future is to ensure they can continue learning.

Over the past week, Israel's civilian home front has endured devastating missile attacks following the outbreak of war with Iran. At the same time, Israel's education system has transitioned to nationwide remote learning, creating an urgent and painful reality: thousands of children cannot attend school simply because they do not have a computer.

More than 168,000 households in Israel lack a computer, leaving students disconnected from classes, teachers, and their peers during one of the most difficult moments in the country's history.

Our nonprofit initiative Mitchashvim was established to close Israel's digital divide by providing refurbished computers to students from disadvantaged communities. In normal times we work to ensure equal educational opportunity. In times of crisis, this mission becomes even more critical.

To date, we have distributed over 48,000 refurbished computers to families in need across Israel. Our experienced operational network is well established and ready to respond rapidly to surging demand during times of national emergency.

In the past 48 hours alone, we have received urgent requests from municipalities and educational institutions serving displaced families and vulnerable communities, including:

- Be'er Sheva, where a missile strike destroyed residential buildings and forced families with children into temporary housing
- Gush Halav, a northern community under full lockdown near the border
- Haifa, supporting residents affected by the emergency situation
- Ayanot Agricultural Youth Village, where students without computers cannot access their studies

Our immediate goal is to provide 350 students with computers. To meet these urgent needs, we are seeking **$95,000** to refurbish and distribute laptops and desktop computers to families who currently have no access to digital learning.

Each computer distributed represents more than technology. It is a lifeline to education, routine, hope, and resilience.

With deep appreciation,
Adv. Shelly Dvir
Chief Executive Officer
Metkhashvim

## The Ask

Dror Israel is seeking $250,000 in emergency support for the next two weeks to continue reaching 200,000 participants nationwide. Priorities include emergency childcare for essential workers (multiple cities and one pop-up school at a hospital), educational activities at evacuee hotels, activities in public shelters (with trained Jewish and Arab teen counselors), Pedagogy of Hope trauma support for 29 schools, and resilience sessions via Tamara Dance and Wellbeing Centers. Funding gaps are itemized per priority.

**Funding Requested:** $250,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency childcare for essential workers (10 staff, transportation, supplies): $125,000
- Pop-up school at hospital (4 teachers, transportation, supplies): $50,000
- Educational activities at evacuee hotels: $10,000
- Activities in public shelters (5 volunteer organizers, transportation, supplies): $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope (10 elementary, 9 high schools, 10 preschools): $80,000
- Resilience through movement and dance (Kiryat Shmona, Rehovot, Haifa): $10,000

## Context & Background

As Iran's missile barrage continues, Israelis are moving into bomb shelters, evacuating damaged homes, and living in constant fear. Dror Israel's 1,300 educators in 14 educators' kibbutzim are providing educational, emotional, and trauma-support programming in shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms. They partner with municipalities and government; one major hospital has requested an on-site pop-up school. The Moledet Elementary School in Be'er Sheva was hit by a missile fragment (school was closed). The organization is activating its nationwide network of 200,000+ children, teens, and families via direct outreach, phone, and Zoom.

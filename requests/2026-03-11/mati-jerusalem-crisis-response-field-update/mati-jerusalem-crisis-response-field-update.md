## Context & Background

Following the outbreak of war with Iran the previous Saturday, MATI Jerusalem Business Development Center mobilized immediately. On Sunday morning its management team convened on Zoom to coordinate. The Jerusalem Municipality had already designated MATI as a key partner and asked it to lead an emergency series of eight webinars for business owners across the city—four for West Jerusalem and four for East Jerusalem—covering Marketing in Times of War, Client Retention in Times of War, Crisis Management, and AI for Business. Business consultants continue to provide one-on-one consulting online.

Program continuity has been maintained by moving delivery online: the Hebrew for Employment course in East Jerusalem resumed via Zoom; within the TIFLI preschool program, MATI launched webinars for families and daycare managers on helping children cope with anxiety and supporting staff mental health; financial literacy workshops continue online, and the Facebook-based financial literacy competition has expanded with 120 new participants.

The update coincides with International Women's Day. MATI highlights its support for women entrepreneurs through accelerator courses, dedicated women's forums, and other services. In 2025, 800 women benefited from the Turning Point program, 612 received one-on-one business consulting, women entrepreneurs raised $1 million in business loans, and 4,000 women participated in courses, workshops, and webinars. The email expresses gratitude for the recipient's interest and invites further engagement.

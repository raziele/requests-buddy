## The Ask

HaOgen for Reservist Families is requesting funding to scale emergency support during Operation Lion's Roar, as Israel faces another major reservist mobilization. The organization is matching volunteers to families for practical help (childcare, dog-walking, shelter runs) and has launched four programs: immediate relief for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles (meals and essential supplies at ~$650 per family); 100,000 grocery coupons for volunteer cooks to provide meals to overwhelmed single-parent households; daily Zoom workshops for over 100 children of reservists (~$900/session); and activity kits for children in bomb shelters ($12 per family). They aim to raise an additional $200,000 to support these efforts.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Immediate relief for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles (meals and essential supplies): approximately $650 per family
- Grocery coupons for volunteer cooks to provide meals to reservist families: 100,000 coupons (total amount not specified)

- Activity kits for children in bomb shelters: $12 per family

## Context & Background

Israel is in the midst of another large-scale reservist mobilization due to the operation in Iran (Operation Lion's Roar). HaOgen for Reservist Families, which supports families of called-up reservists, reports higher demand than ever. The difficulties are acute: single parents must take multiple small children to shelters alone, arrange nighttime dog-walking, or find childcare when schools are closed and they are essential workers. HaOgen has increased volunteer support and is matching volunteers within a very close radius to families in need while expanding into new cities.

In addition to this core volunteer matching, HaOgen is running four emergency programs that require funding. First, for reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles, the organization provides immediate relief in the form of meals and essential supplies at approximately $650 per family; they are currently supporting two such families—one in Beit Shemesh and one in Beer Sheva—whose homes were destroyed. Second, with the number of mobilized reservist families at a peak and single parents at home unable to prepare meal after meal, HaOgen is raising funds to purchase 100,000 grocery coupons so volunteer cooks can provide meals to these families. Third, with schools closed, HaOgen runs daily Zoom workshops for the children of reservists; over 100 children participate each day, at a cost of about $900 per session for content and technology, providing both a fun activity and relief for parents managing alone. Fourth, for families with young children spending long periods in bomb shelters, HaOgen seeks funding for activity kits at $12 per family to keep children engaged and to signal that these families are not alone; funding for a specific city could have a large impact given communities range from under 100 to over 600 families.

The organization is seeking an additional $200,000 to support these efforts during this period and has asked recipients to indicate whether they would be interested in supporting one or more of these specific needs.

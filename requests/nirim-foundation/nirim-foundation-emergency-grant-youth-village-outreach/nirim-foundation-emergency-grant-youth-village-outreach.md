## The Ask

Nirim Foundation is requesting an emergency grant to stabilize its Youth Village in Bustan HaGalil and sustain its 'Nirim in the Neighborhood' outreach for at-risk youth across high-threat northern municipalities. The organization reports severe mental health and security impacts from the prolonged war and cumulative trauma, warning that without immediate support youth risk educational withdrawal, delinquency, addiction, and crime.

**Funding Requested:** $100,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Review the attached emergency request and schedule a phone call to discuss how the partnership can help.

## Context & Background

Nirim describes how, for more than 20 years, it has supported Israel’s most vulnerable at-risk youth, but the combined effects of the pandemic and the ongoing 'Swords of Iron' war have stretched its lifeline to the breaking point. According to the organization, youth in the North are experiencing an unprecedented mental health and security crisis, with the current conflict worsening domestic tension, economic stress, and violence.

The organization emphasizes that without the stabilizing structure Nirim provides, at-risk teenagers face a rapid slide toward criminal behavior, educational withdrawal, and social collapse. It frames the situation as a tipping point driven by chronic fear, boredom, and a lack of supervision—conditions that can push youth into long-term cycles of addiction and crime.

In response to the escalation, Nirim recalibrated its programming to operate on the front lines. It focuses on high-risk 'Red Zones' including Nahariya, Katzrin, Karmiel, and Hatzor, moving activities into neighborhood shelters to create safe, face-to-face spaces, and conducting intensive home visits alongside shelter-based group sessions.

Nirim also highlights its Youth Village in Bustan HaGalil (near Akko/Acre) as the only 'safe haven' for 120 teenagers who have nowhere else to go despite recurring sirens near the northern border. The ongoing alarms and tension have increased the emotional and physical burden on youth, while reserve duty (Miluim) has reduced staff availability, forcing the organization to work around the clock. Nirim is therefore requesting emergency funding to maintain operational continuity and adapt therapeutic and educational programming until conditions stabilize.

The Silent Crisis:
While the entire nation feels the weight of the ongoing conflict, at-risk youth—particularly in the
North—are suffering disproportionately. For these teenagers, "home" is often an unsafe or
neglected environment, and the current war has only intensified domestic tension, economic
stress, and violence.
Without the vital structure Nirim provides, these youth face a rapid descent into criminal behavior,
educational withdrawal, and total social collapse. The combination of chronic fear, boredom, and
a lack of supervision is a recipe for long-term societal damage. For many, this crisis represents a
breaking point; without immediate intervention, they risk falling into a cycle of addiction and
crime that could permanently jeopardize their future.

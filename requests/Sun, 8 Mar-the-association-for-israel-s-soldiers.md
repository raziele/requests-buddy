## The Ask

The Association for Israel's Soldiers is fundraising through the 'Roaring Lion Relief Fund' to provide essential supplies, medical equipment, and financial aid to IDF soldiers and their families during the ongoing conflict. They are seeking donations to support various needs, including personal hygiene, medical gear, and assistance for impacted families.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Male hygiene kit: 200 NIS
- Female hygiene kit: 150 NIS
- Physical & Mental Recharge per soldier: approx. 100 NIS
- Physical & Mental Recharge per military unit: 40-250k NIS
- Financial aid for evacuated families (vouchers): 2,000 NIS per family
- Financial aid for injured soldiers (vouchers): 5,000 NIS per family
- Financial aid for bereaved families (vouchers): 2,000 NIS per family
- Military Ambulance: 650k NIS per vehicle

## Context & Background

The "Roaring Lion Operation" was launched on February 28, 2026, by the IDF and U.S. military forces to degrade the Iranian regime and eliminate existential threats to Israel. The operation involved significant military actions, including a large flyover of Tehran and strikes against military sites. The Association for Israel's Soldiers, authorized by the IDF, is actively supporting soldiers during this period through its Emergency Relief Fund. Their efforts focus on providing basic necessities, life-saving medical equipment, and financial assistance to soldiers and their families impacted by the conflict. The association emphasizes that 100% of donations go directly to the soldiers.

THE IDF DONATION POLICY

During times of crisis, Israel and its supporters worldwide strengthen their resolve to support and embrace the people of Israel, the IDF and its dedicated soldiers.

The Israeli military provides its soldiers with all the gear and items they need for war, resupplying based on IDF-defined needs and the gaps that arise. The IDF is the sole organization responsible for procuring personal protective equipment for soldiers and gear required for military operations.

In light of the Swords of Iron War, army policies allow the IDF to receive donations from an emergency fund that can deliver immediate solutions to the IDF's needs and benefit its soldiers.

THE NEED - PHYSICAL & MENTAL RECHARGE

When our forces sacrifice so much for us, they deserve quick breaks. The physical and mental recharge is a pop-up service that offers a welcome escape from the battlefield.

For a few hours during this getaway, the fighters experience an oasis of relaxation and an opportunity to lighten up with fellow soldiers in a cheerful atmosphere. Nourishing meals, media entertainment, diversions, hot showers, haircuts and laundry help re-energize our valiant battalions.

The indulgence expresses our support and gratitude for their hard work. This initiative provides essential comfort, boosting soldier morale and reminding them that they are not alone in their fight for the country's security. Our forces return rejuvenated for the next mission at hand.

THE NEED - FINANCIAL AID FOR EVACUATED SOLDIERS AND FAMILIES

On Saturday, February 28, 2026, Operation "Lion's Roar" was launched following a prolonged and intense period of fighting that began on October 7, 2023. The attack by Iran struck Israel's civilian home front, resulting in fatalities, injuries, and extensive damage to residential buildings across the country, including the homes of IDF service members.

Out of moral responsibility and in fulfillment of its commitment to those who serve, the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) identified an urgent need to provide immediate assistance to affected families. This support is intended to help them cope with the sudden loss of property and to meet their essential needs during the critical initial period before they are able to fully reorganize and rebuild their lives. The assistance reflects the IDF's enduring ethical and social commitment to its service members, even in times of national emergency.

Each family whose home was damaged in the attack will receive assistance in the form of vouchers totaling 2,000 NIS. These vouchers are designed to provide an immediate response to essential needs such as food, clothing, and basic household supplies.

Cost: 2000 NIS per family

THE NEED - FINANCIAL AID FOR SOLDIERS INJURED MODERATELY OR SERIOUSLY

During the Swords of Iron War, hundreds of soldiers joined the ranks of the wounded. When an injured fighter requires hospitalization, treatment and rehabilitation, family members rush to his or her bedside. Support, encouragement and recovery become life's only priorities. Parents, partners and siblings take time off from work to help their injured loved one walk again, talk again and smile again. Unfortunately, bill-paying does not disappear for these families. In fact, expenses can mount from travel, eating out and other inescapable outlays on top of potential lost income. The Emergency Relief Fund of the Association for Israel's Soldiers steps in to support families of injured soldiers and lighten their burden. Within 24 hours of hospital admission, the fund gives 5,000 NIS in vouchers to the family whose loved one was injured moderately or seriously. The family can use the vouchers in about 60 store chains throughout Israel to purchase the essentials they need, such as food, clothing, or electronics.

Cost: 5,000 NIS per family

THE NEED - FINANCIAL AID FOR BEREAVED FAMILIES IN MOURNING

Hundreds of courageous troops have been killed in action in the Swords of Iron War. In an instant, the families left behind have had their worlds radically altered – forever. Nothing can bring back the loved one or repair the hole in the mourning family members' hearts. The Emergency Relief Fund of the Association for Israel's Soldiers extends its immediate support to the family during shiva, the seven days of mourning. The association provides 2,000 NIS in vouchers for immediate expenses such as food, clothing, or other critical needs incurred by the mourning family during their devastating time of loss. The vouchers can be redeemed in about 60 store chains throughout Israel. When a service member is killed, all of Israel mourns. We hope we will never have to bury another beloved soldier.

Cost: 2,000 NIS per family

## The Ask

The Tel Aviv Sexual Assault Crisis Center (TASACC) is requesting emergency funding in the wake of the current war with Iran. Self-generated income (workshops, lectures, annual bazaar—approximately $650,000) has been frozen, and one-third of the budget that depends on government support is delayed because the request-for-proposals process has been postponed. Demand for crisis support has increased while resources have contracted. The organization is asking whether it can apply for emergency funding and is open to a Zoom discussion or providing further information by email.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

## Context & Background

The current war with Iran has sharply increased the need for the Center’s services while cutting off the revenue that pays for them. Workshops, lectures, and the annual bazaar, which together account for nearly $650,000 of the budget, have been frozen. In addition, about one-third of the budget relies on government support, and the relevant request-for-proposals process has been delayed, so those funds will arrive later than planned at a time when demand is rising.

War intensifies trauma rather than silencing it. For survivors of sexual violence, sirens and the current security situation can retraumatize: bomb shelters, ongoing fear, and strain on families and communities reopen past wounds and trigger renewed crisis. The email stresses that professional, compassionate support in these moments is essential and that survivors in crisis cannot wait for calmer times or for delayed funding.

TASACC is asking whether it can apply for emergency funding and offers to discuss by Zoom or to provide any further information by email. The message is signed by Neta (Adv. Neta Patrick, Executive Director) and sent with gratitude and hope.

## Context & Background

The Tel Aviv Sexual Assault Crisis Center (TASACC) is writing with urgency following the current war with Iran. They describe a devastating contradiction: more survivors are reaching out for help while the resources that make that help possible are in jeopardy.

All self-generated income has been brought to a halt. Workshops, lectures, and the annual bazaar together account for nearly $650,000 of the budget and have been frozen. One-third of the budget depends on government support, and the current request-for-proposals process has been delayed, postponing the arrival of these critical funds exactly when they are needed most.

For survivors of sexual violence, war does not silence trauma—it amplifies it. Sirens can awaken terror within; bomb shelters, constant fear, and strain on families and communities reopen old wounds and push survivors into renewed crisis. The organization stresses that compassionate, professional support is essential and that a survivor in crisis cannot be asked to wait for quieter days or delayed funding. When someone reaches out, TASACC must be there.

The sender asks whether they can apply for emergency funding and offers to speak over Zoom or provide further information via email.

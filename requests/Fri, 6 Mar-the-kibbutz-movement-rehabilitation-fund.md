## The Ask

The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund is requesting $1,039,500 to provide emergency support to kibbutz communities in northern Israel facing renewed security threats. This includes equipping public shelters with essential supplies and providing community support programs to address the emotional and logistical strain on residents.

**Funding Requested:** $1,039,500

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Shelter Kits: $94,500
- Community Emergency Support: $945,000

## Context & Background

Following the October 7th attacks, the Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established to support affected kibbutzim.  22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev were severely damaged, and 19 along the northern border were evacuated.  Now, with renewed escalation, thousands of residents remain vulnerable, and public shelters are overcrowded. The fund seeks to provide essential supplies for shelters and community support programs to address the emotional and logistical strain on residents during this crisis.

## Extracted Data

### Emergency Support for Kibbutz Communities.pdf

The Kibbutz Movement is a cooperative network of 259 kibbutzim across Israel. The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established following the October 7th attacks to support kibbutzim that were severely affected, evacuated, and damaged.

The current crisis involves ongoing missile threats to northern kibbutzim, with families spending long hours in public shelters. 22 kibbutzim in the Western Negev were severely damaged, and 19 along the northern border were evacuated.

The fund seeks $94,500 for Emergency Shelter Kits (27 kits at $3,500 each, including chairs, mattresses, fans, tables, Wi-Fi routers, and first-aid kits) and $945,000 for a Community Emergency Support Program (supporting 27 kibbutzim with leadership guidance, cultural activities, support for elderly residents, and youth programs).

The expected impact includes improved safety in shelters, strengthened community resilience, and support for vulnerable populations.

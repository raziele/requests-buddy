## The Ask

Access Israel is requesting $400,000 for immediate, critical aid to assist individuals with severe disabilities and the elderly during the current conflict with Iran. The funds will be used to operate a 24/7 accessible emergency hotline ('Purple Vest' system), train responders for immediate evacuation and assistance, and operate accessible evacuation vehicles.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Accessible Emergency Hotline ('Purple Vest' system): $400,000

## Context & Background

In response to the escalating conflict with Iran, Access Israel is issuing an urgent call for donations to protect vulnerable populations, specifically individuals with severe disabilities and the elderly. Approximately 40% of this population lacks adequate shelter, leaving them in immediate danger during air raid sirens. Access Israel has been at the forefront of humanitarian aid for these groups since October 7th, providing accessible evacuation, temporary housing, medical equipment, specialized transportation, and personal accompaniment.

The organization emphasizes that during wartime, individuals with disabilities and the elderly are often the last to receive essential support. The current conflict necessitates immediate and significant aid to ensure accessible and protective living arrangements, especially for those whose homes are not safe or accessible. Access Israel aims to prevent people from being left behind during this critical time.

The immediate request for $400,000 is intended to cover ten days of critical operations. This funding will support the establishment and operation of a 24/7 accessible emergency hotline ('Purple Vest' system) to manage incoming calls and provide immediate assistance. It will also fund essential training and refresher courses for volunteers in evacuation procedures and the use of the 'Purple Vest' system, as well as the operation of accessible evacuation vehicles. The organization stresses that this is an initial request, and further funding needs will be assessed based on the conflict's duration and developments.

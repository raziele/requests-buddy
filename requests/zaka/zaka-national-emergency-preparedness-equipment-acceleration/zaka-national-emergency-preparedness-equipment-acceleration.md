## The Ask

ZAKA requests $3 million for a National Emergency Preparedness and Equipment Acceleration initiative. The three-year phased plan covers: (1) Incident Command, Coordination & Field Infrastructure – $1.65M (command tents, lighting towers, generators, field infrastructure); (2) Search, Detection & Technical Rescue Equipment – $440K (thermal imaging, firefighter rescue tools, structural/confined-space rescue); (3) Personal Protective & Team Equipment – $910K (stretchers, helmets, protective overalls, personal rescue tools). ZAKA has responded to 12,000+ incidents since October 7 with equipment designed for a different era; Israel has defined 2026–2027 as critical national preparedness years.

**Funding Requested:** $3,000,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Pillar 1 – Incident Command, Coordination & Field Infrastructure: $1,650,000
- Pillar 2 – Search, Detection & Technical Rescue Equipment: $440,000
- Pillar 3 – Personal Protective & Team Equipment: $910,000

## Context & Background

Ongoing multi-front war and escalating regional security scenarios require coordinated civilian preparedness. Israel's defense establishment has defined 2026–2027 as critical national preparedness years, including earthquake readiness. ZAKA has responded to 12,000+ emergency incidents since October 7 under sustained operational pressure. Volunteers are operating at national-level intensity with equipment designed for a very different era. The preparedness gap includes: limited ability to equip all teams simultaneously, aging equipment slowing response and increasing risk, and earthquake scenarios requiring redundancy, coordination, and scale. ZAKA is expected to function as a national emergency response system without national-level equipment renewal. The initiative is a structured, phased equipment renewal plan aligned with national preparedness benchmarks. Leadership partnership level (exploratory) is $1,000,000. USD amounts approximate at ₪3.09 per USD.

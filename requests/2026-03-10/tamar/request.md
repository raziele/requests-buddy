## Extracted Data

Dear friends and partners,

Six days into the current escalation, I would like to share a brief update on our work and the needs emerging from the communities we serve.

We concluded the week with a unique gathering of close to 40 Druze imams focused on strengthening personal and community resilience during times of crisis. In many communities, imams serve as trusted first responders and key gatekeepers for individuals and families in distress, yet they rarely receive professional tools or support to address trauma and emotional hardship. The meeting provided practical guidance to help them recognize psychological responses to crisis and better support their communities during this challenging time.

Our telephone helpline and chat service continue to operate continuously, receiving dozens of inquiries each day, mainly from teenagers and their parents seeking support in coping with anxiety and uncertainty.

This week we also held several webinars for parents, professionals, and community leaders, providing practical tools to help them support children and community members experiencing distress.

For many of the communities we work with, the trauma did not begin with the current events. We have received many inquiries from residents and local leaders in the unrecognized Bedouin villages in the Negev to conduct respite activities for children, like those we provided during tense periods over the past two years. These activities, led by therapists, combine play and creative expression to help reduce stress and identify children who may need additional support.

We convened our community of Arabic speaking therapists from across the country. Many reported significant exhaustion due to the growing number of people seeking support, while personally facing the same security situation as those they serve.

I would like to express my deep appreciation to our dedicated team, who continue to work around the clock to support the growing number of people seeking help, while their own families live under the same difficult conditions.

We, at Tamar, are all deeply grateful to our many partners for your continued support and solidarity. It means a great deal to us and to the communities we serve.

With appreciation,
Ibrahim Abu Jaffer

CEO

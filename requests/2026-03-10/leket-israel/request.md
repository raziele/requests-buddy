## The Ask

Emergency funding request of $508,210 weekly to maintain food distribution operations disrupted by the Israel-Iran conflict. Leket Israel's food rescue operations have been severely impacted by rocket fire, volunteer limitations, and reduced surplus food supply, requiring supplemental purchases to maintain distribution levels.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Meals: 5,000 additional meals daily
- Produce: significant temporary purchases

**Non-Financial Ask:** Temporary food purchases and supermarket vouchers to support families unable to access distribution points

## Context & Background

Due to the Israel-Iran conflict, Leket Israel's food rescue operations have been severely disrupted. Rocket fire has forced suspension of volunteer activity and limited collection/distribution operations. Farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases (primary suppliers) are inaccessible. This has reduced daily meal distribution from 8,000 to 3,000 meals. Leket is supplementing with purchased food and providing vouchers to maintain supply for vulnerable families during the crisis.

### Expanding Our Capacity to Support Israel’s Frontline Caregivers

Request for Support: Renovating a New Workshop Space at Metsoke Dragot

Since October 7th, Israel’s social fabric has been held together by its invisible pillars: medical teams, therapists, social workers, educators, and the spouses of reservists and wounded soldiers. Many continue to support others while quietly carrying their own emotional wounds. The growing risk of emotional collapse among them threatens the very resilience of entire communities

Mashiv HaRuach was created in late 2023 to prevent exactly that.

In less than two years, over 2,000 participants have joined our trauma-informed resilience workshops. Each group becomes a living support network that continues long after the sessions end.

Recognition of our work continues to grow among professional partners, government institutions, and the participants themselves. With that recognition comes rising demand. Dozens of organizations are already waiting for workshops, and we are currently unable to meet the growing need.

Our main limitation at this stage is infrastructural.

Doubling Our Capacity to Respond

To meet the growing demand, shorten waiting times, and expand our reach, we are seeking your support for a focused project: renovating and establishing an additional workshop space within the Metsoke Dragot retreat site.

Metsoke Dragot has become a full partner in Mashiv HaRuach’s journey. Over the past two years, dozens of workshops have been held there, and a deep relationship has developed with the management and local team. They see the participants, understand our work intimately, and have chosen to make a meaningful contribution to its expansion.

Metsoke Dragot has allocated an additional building within the compound for our use, and they are actively involved in the project:

- The building itself has been made available to Mashiv HaRuach
- Their contractor and architect are already engaged in the planning
- Part of the renovation cost is being raised by them

The planned space includes a central workshop hall designed for deep group processes, a private meeting room for one-on-one support and team coordination, and a landscaped outdoor healing garden with shaded seating areas and a pergola. This expansion will double our current capacity, allowing us to host multiple groups simultaneously and dramatically reduce wait times.

Total funding needed to complete the project: $150,000

Timeline: 3 months from funding to completion

Naming Opportunities – A Space That Bears Your Legacy

Supporters are invited to dedicate specific areas of the new healing center, or the center as a whole. Naming opportunities include:

- The Full Healing Center – $150,000
A lasting dedication at the heart of Israel’s emotional resilience efforts.

- Main Workshop Hall – $75,000
The central space where caregivers come together to recover, connect, and rebuild their strength.

- Private Meeting Room – $50,000
A quiet room for personal processing, staff consultation, and small group meetings.

- Outdoor Healing Garden – $25,000
A tranquil space facing the desert, designed for rest, reflection, and integration between sessions.

Each named space will carry a physical acknowledgment and become a permanent part of the sanctuary we are building for those who hold the frontlines of care.

Join Us in Creating a Space for Healing

We would be honored to share architectural plans, participant stories, and a deeper overview of our program’s impact. What we need most now are partners who will make this vision real who will stand with those standing for others.

This is a rare, concrete opportunity:
Together, we can create a new sanctuary for emotional recovery and do it in time to meet the need.

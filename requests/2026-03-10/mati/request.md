Dear Mr Loozon,

I hope this message finds you safe and well. I wanted to briefly update you on how MATI Jerusalem Business Development Center in Israel has mobilized to support Jerusalem’s business community following the outbreak of the war with Iran this past Saturday.

Already on Sunday morning, the morning after the war broke out, MATI’s management team convened on Zoom to coordinate our response. The Jerusalem Municipality had already asked us to lead an emergency series of eight webinars for business owners across the city: four for West Jerusalem and four for East Jerusalem, recognizing MATI as a key partner in helping entrepreneurs navigate this crisis. The webinars have started and cover Marketing in Times of War, Client Retention in Times of War, Crisis Management, and AI for Business. At the same time, our business consultants continue to provide one-on-one consulting services online.

We have also moved several programs online to ensure continuity. Our Hebrew for Employment course in East Jerusalem has resumed via Zoom, and within our TIFLI preschool program we launched webinars for families and daycare managers on helping children cope with anxiety and supporting staff mental health. Our financial literacy workshops are also continuing online, and our Facebook-based financial literacy competition has expanded significantly, with 120 new participants joining the community.

Today, on International Women’s Day, we are also proud to highlight our work supporting women entrepreneurs. Through our business accelerator courses, dedicated women’s forums and other services for women business owners, MATI equips women with the tools, confidence, and networks needed to grow sustainable businesses and access financing.

In 2025 alone:
• 800 women benefited from the Turning Point program
• 612 women received one-on-one business consulting
• $1 million in business loans was raised by women entrepreneurs
• 4,000 women participated in business courses, workshops and webinars

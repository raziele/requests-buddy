## Project proposals Elah Center for Coping with Loss 2026.pdf

### Executive Summary
Since the outbreak of the Iron Swords War, the Israeli government has significantly expanded psychological assistance to direct victims, alongside substantial support from philanthropic organizations. However, many individuals continue to fall between the cracks - either because they are not eligible for government funding or because existing frameworks do not adequately address their specific circumstances.

### 1. Support Groups for Individuals Coping with Grief or Trauma
**Target Populations:**
- Mothers and partners of combat soldiers
- Female lone soldiers
- Bereaved commanders
- Close friends of fallen soldiers and civilians
- Second-degree family members of fallen soldiers or civilians (both Jewish and Arab)
- Family members of wounded or traumatized soldiers
- First-line emergency personnel

**Program Details:**
- Facilitated by two qualified therapists per group
- Minimum 12 sessions, extendable to 30-40 sessions
- Cost: NIS 3,000 per session (NIS 36,000 for 12 sessions)

### 2. Individual Therapy for People Coping with Grief or Trauma
**Target Population:**
- Individuals not eligible for government-funded psychological care
- Those requiring individualized support beyond group settings

**Program Details:**
- One-hour sessions in clinics or online
- Cost: NIS 345 per session
- Basic therapeutic package: 20 sessions (NIS 6,900)

### 3. Social-Therapeutic Support Groups for the Elderly
**Target Population:**
- Older adults at heightened risk for anxiety, depression, and loneliness
- Those experiencing cognitive decline

**Program Details:**
- Average 12 participants per group
- Weekly 90-minute sessions
- Tools: music, photographs, games, art, movement, animals, gardening
- Cost: NIS 1,500 per session (NIS 30,000 for 20 sessions)

### 4. Individual Therapy for Homebound Elderly
**Target Population:**
- Elderly individuals with declining mobility and social networks
- Holocaust survivors not eligible for state-funded assistance

**Program Details:**
- One-hour in-home therapy sessions
- Cost: NIS 400 per session
- Basic treatment package: 20 sessions (NIS 8,000)

### 5. Group and Individual Therapy for the Arab Community in Israel
**Target Population:**
- Arab community facing unique mental-health burden
- Individuals experiencing loss, trauma, and chronic stress

**Program Details:**
- Group therapy: 12 participants, weekly 90-minute sessions
- Cost: NIS 1,500 per group session (NIS 18,000 for 12 sessions)
- Individual therapy: NIS 345 per session (NIS 6,900 for 20 sessions)

### Objectives
The objective of all programs is to support emotional processing while strengthening internal resilience, coping skills, and personal and social identity. Programs contribute to preventing isolation and suicide, enhancing emotional recovery, and maintaining personal and professional functioning.

### Conclusion
Elah Center possesses the professional knowledge, therapists, and infrastructure required to provide support nationwide. The only missing component is funding.

## מרכז אלה - הצעה לתוכנית ליווי והדרכה עבור עובדי רווחה.pdf

### Background
Since October 2023, Israel has been in prolonged combat, creating unprecedented emotional and social burden on citizens. The ongoing war with Iran has added more victims, increasing the load on welfare workers. This sustained exposure puts workers at risk of secondary trauma, re-traumatization, and compassion fatigue.

### Program Proposal for Welfare Workers Support
**Target Population:** Welfare workers in social services departments across municipalities, regional councils, and local authorities

**Proposed Support Programs:**
1. **Ongoing Group Training Sessions**
2. **Individual Support for Workers** - Personal guidance
3. **Short-term Workshops** - Combining experience, knowledge transfer, and tools
4. **Enrichment Lectures** - Topics: loss, trauma, self-care, etc.

### Program Objectives
- Strengthen professional capacity of welfare workers
- Provide tools for managing secondary trauma
- Enhance resilience in challenging social, economic, and security conditions
- Support workers caring for diverse and vulnerable populations

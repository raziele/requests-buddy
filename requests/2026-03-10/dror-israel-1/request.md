## The Ask

Dror Israel seeks $360,000 in emergency support to continue providing educational, emotional, and trauma support to 200,000 participants nationwide during Iran's missile barrage. Funding is needed for emergency childcare, pop-up schools, evacuee hotel activities, public shelter operations, and pedagogy of hope programs.

**Funding Requested:** $360,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency Childcare for Essential Workers: $125,000
- Pop-up School at Major Hospital: $50,000
- Educational Activities at Evacuee Hotels: $10,000
- Public Shelter Operations: $60,000
- Pedagogy of Hope Support: $80,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Accessible shelters and services for people with disabilities, pop-up schools at hospitals, and emergency childcare for essential workers.

## Context & Background

Israel is under continuous missile attack from Iran, forcing civilians into bomb shelters and damaged homes. Dror Israel's educators provide trauma support in shelters, evacuee centers, and living rooms. They need $360,000 to maintain operations for 200,000 participants, including childcare for essential workers, pop-up schools at hospitals, activities in evacuee hotels, public shelter operations, and pedagogical support for schools.

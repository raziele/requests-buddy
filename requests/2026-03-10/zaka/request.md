## The Ask

ZAKA requests urgent funding for emergency rescue equipment to support volunteer teams responding to missile strikes and structural collapses. Equipment includes thermal imaging systems, lighting towers, cutting tools, and ambulance kits totaling $1,572,900. This equipment is critical for safe operations in active conflict zones and mass-casualty incidents.

**Funding Requested:** $1,572,900

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Thermal Imaging Systems: $5,200
- Portable Lighting Towers: $18,885
- Firefighter Rescue Cutting Tools: $12,300
- Ambulance Rescue Tool Kits: $2,600

## Context & Background

ZAKA volunteers operate at the front lines of complex emergency scenes across Israel, including missile impacts and structural collapses. The organization's Home Front Command-trained civilian rescue unit is the only volunteer team authorized for engineering-level rescue operations. Essential equipment is needed immediately to enable safe operations in unstable structures and ongoing conflict zones.

## The Ask

ZAKA is requesting urgent funding for rescue equipment to support their Home Front Command-trained civilian rescue unit that operates at emergency scenes including missile strikes, structural collapses, and mass casualty incidents. The equipment is essential for enabling teams to operate safely and professionally in complex structural collapse scenarios.

**Funding Requested:** $1,572,900

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Thermal Imaging Systems: $62,400
- Portable Lighting Towers: $1,133,100
- Firefighter Rescue Cutting Tools: $221,400
- Ambulance Rescue Tool Kits: $156,000

## Context & Background

ZAKA's national volunteer network includes more than 3,850 volunteers, including approximately 900 specially trained by Israel's Home Front Command in advanced search-and-rescue operations. These teams frequently operate inside unstable structures following missile strikes and building collapses. Recently, ZAKA teams responded to a direct missile strike on a residential building in Beit Shemesh in which nine civilians were killed. Volunteers arrived within minutes and worked alongside Israel's security and rescue forces under extremely difficult conditions of structural destruction, darkness, and ongoing danger. Given the increasing number of simultaneous emergency incidents occurring across the country, ZAKA must equip dozens of response teams to ensure rapid deployment and effective response nationwide.

# ZAKA National Emergency Rescue Equipment Initiative

Equipping Israel's Civilian Search & Rescue Volunteers

Date:
March 5, 2026

Ofer Lichtig
- Israel Representative
Greater Miami Jewish Federation

Dear Ofer,

In light of the escalating security situation in Israel and the continued attacks on civilian communities, ZAKA volunteers are operating at the front lines of complex emergency scenes across the country, including missile impacts from Iran and its regional proxies, structural collapses, and mass casualty incidents.

Recently, ZAKA teams responded to a direct missile strike on a residential building in Beit Shemesh in which nine civilians were killed. Volunteers arrived within minutes and worked alongside Israel's security and rescue forces under extremely difficult conditions of structural destruction, darkness, and ongoing danger.

ZAKA's national volunteer network includes more than 3,850 volunteers, including approximately 900 specially trained by Israel's Home Front Command in advanced search-and-rescue operations. These teams frequently operate inside unstable structures following missile strikes and building collapses.

To ensure these volunteers can continue responding effectively, ZAKA is mobilizing resources to procure essential technical search-and-rescue equipment for its Home Front Command-trained civilian rescue unit - the only volunteer unit in Israel authorized to participate in engineering-level rescue operations at collapsed structures.

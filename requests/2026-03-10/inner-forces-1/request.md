## Immediate Resilience Support in Response to the Emergency Situation – March 2026.pdf

Approximately 500,000 IDF service members took part in the Iron Swords War, and their families—parents, siblings, partners, and grandparents—have been living since October 7th in an ongoing reality of worry, emotional overload, and economic and employment strain. While many support services have been developed for the soldiers themselves, their families have often been left outside the conversation and support networks, even though the security situation is expected to affect them for a long time to come.

Inner Forces works to change this reality by building a long-term support framework for the families of service members, alongside advancing public policy and developing long-term infrastructure. Based on field work and research, we developed a multidisciplinary model for strengthening the resilience of service members’ families, addressing emotional resilience, community, employment, and utilization of rights.

In light of the current security situation and the escalation of fighting in recent days, we are experiencing a significant surge of requests from mothers of combat soldiers and wives of reservists seeking immediate support. The home has become a frontline, and emotional strain and burnout are accumulating. Therefore, we adapted our annual resilience program into a condensed, rapid-response format consisting of a series of four online resilience sessions, each in small groups of up to 20 participants, led by professional facilitators specializing in therapy and group work.

We are seeking to open at least six groups: three for mothers of active duty and reserve combat soldiers, and three for wives of reservists. Cost per group: 9,150 NIS, including facilitation, participant coordination, content adaptation, and ongoing support between sessions.

Within the first week of Operation "Roaring Lion," we have already initiated 3 groups out of the planned 6. Participating mothers and spouses shared: 'I feel like someone finally gave me oxygen. I was suffocating.' 'For the first time, I don't feel crazy.' 'Only other mothers can truly understand me.' 'Thank you for giving me solid ground to stand on—something I haven't experienced in a long time.'

## NATIONAL RESILIENCE BEGINS AT HOME

Resilience Sessions - A Series of Zoom meetings for Mothers of Combat Soldiers – A Tailored Response to the Current Situation

Target Audience: Mothers of soldiers serving in mandatory service, reserves, and career service.

This series provides a guided, safe group space for processing the ongoing worry and uncertainty, along with professional tools for emotional regulation and coping with anxiety, burnout, and accumulated stress.

The meetings combine psychoeducation—research-based knowledge and tools on ambiguous loss and resilience—with open group dialogue and exercises to improve communication with one’s surroundings. Within this space, a supportive peer community is formed, reducing loneliness and strengthening meaning, capability, and a sense of control amid a prolonged security crisis.

## Resilience Sessions - A Series of Zoom meetings for Mothers of Combat Soldiers – A Tailored Response to the Current Situation

Target Audience: Wives of reservists dealing with active and prolonged reserve duty.

This series provides a facilitated, safe group space for processing the emotional strain and burnout that arise from running a household and raising children during long periods of a partner’s absence, while also dealing with ongoing worry and constant shifts between emergency and routine.

The meetings combine psychoeducation, research-based tools for strengthening family resilience and coping with uncertainty, along with open dialogue and practical exercises. Participants gain tools for emotional regulation, maintaining daily functioning, and improving couple and family communication during periods of absence and return from service. A strong peer community develops within the group, reducing loneliness and strengthening stability in times of high stress and uncertainty.

## Community Resilience Ambassadors – Resilience Program of face-to-face group meetings

This year-long program is conducted in partnership with local municipalities and is designed for mothers of soldiers in mandatory service, reserves, and career service.

The program was born out of research and field work conducted since the beginning of the Iron Swords War, which revealed that many mothers face ambiguous loss, ongoing worry, and declines in personal and family functioning.

## NATIONAL RESILIENCE BEGINS AT HOME

The program includes a 10-session weekly training course (three hours each) for groups of up to 20 mothers. The course combines emotional processing, tools for strengthening personal and family resilience, and a deep understanding of how war affects all spheres of life.

Beyond personal strengthening, the program trains participants to serve as Community Resilience Ambassadors in their local communities. After completing the course, the mothers continue to receive monthly support, professional guidance, case consultation, and links to therapeutic and rights-related services.

In this way, an active community network is built, in which trained volunteers identify needs, support families, and connect service members and their families with existing services. The program is guided by professional facilitators and grounded in updated research and extensive field experience.

During 2025, the program operated four cohorts of soldiers’ mothers nationwide, including mothers of injured soldiers and soldiers with PTSD, leading to a significant improvement in overall well-being. Many graduates now serve as resilience ambassadors in their communities. In 2026, we plan to expand the program by launching 10 additional cohorts, adapting the model for fathers and wives of service members, and delivering 100+ workshops and retreats.

Our Research

During the war, we conducted a research study in collaboration with the Health Research Center at the Ruppin Academic Center, examining the experiences of mothers of soldiers in combat and combat-support roles.

The findings show that these mothers suffer from ambiguous loss trauma affecting multiple areas of life, including employment, while peer support emerged as a key resilience factor. The research highlights that strengthening mothers’ and families’ resilience directly contributes to the resilience of the soldiers themselves.

NATIONAL RESILIENCE BEGINS AT HOME

Inner Forces (HaNefesh HaAchat) – Serving the Families Who Serve

Approximately 500,000 service members took part in the October 7th war, meaning nearly 30% of Israel’s population, consisting of their families (parents, brothers/sisters, spouses, children, grandparents), have been living for over two years in a state of constant anxiety. While efforts and resources are allocated to support soldiers, their families remain largely overlooked.

Inner Forces was established to fill this gap.

As the initial intensity of the war gives way to a new reality, the real challenges for many families are only beginning. With public mental health systems and state services already overwhelmed and insufficient support available, targeted community-based support for families of soldiers is an urgent national necessity.

Inner Forces provides a holistic support framework for families of soldiers and reservists based on empirical knowledge (including vast fieldwork and input from our national mothers' community) and academic research.

We operate under a core belief: when families are resilient, soldiers are resilient.

We operate along five core pillars: Resilience, Rights Advocacy, Community, Economic Stability, and Education.

Our leadership team consists of mothers of combat soldiers with professional backgrounds in healthcare, education, and social innovation. This combination fosters community trust and enables effective program delivery. We would appreciate the opportunity to meet and discuss our initiatives further.

1. Resilience Programs – From Concern to Community Action

Our Community Resilience Ambassador Program is a structured, research-based annual model implemented in partnership with local municipalities. Designed for mothers of combat soldiers, it provides year-long professional support that strengthens personal and family resilience while preparing participants to serve as Resilience Ambassadors within their communities.

Through this model, graduates go on to volunteer locally in partnership with municipal authorities, supporting service members and their families.

The program is accompanied by ongoing research conducted with Ruppin Academic Center, ensuring measurable outcomes and continuous evidence-based refinement.

Participants report significant improvement in well-being and resilience.

## Occupational Rehabilitation & Economic Stability

Approximately 20% of mothers of combat soldiers report reduced employment or job loss, and about 45% of reservists’ spouses report changes in employment status during the war, reflecting the significant employment impact of prolonged emotional strain on soldiers’ families.

In response, we developed an occupational resilience program that provides trauma-informed career counseling, employment navigation support, and practical guidance on employment retention, helping participants return to work while adapting to shifting employment conditions and new family responsibilities.

2026 Plan:
Launch five occupational resilience groups for mothers and reservists’ spouses in collaboration with local municipalities and the business sector. In parallel, we work with employers, equipping them with trauma awareness and practical tools to strengthen their ability to support affected employees.

## Rights Advocacy

We provide guidance to families navigating bureaucratic systems, backed by a comprehensive national resource database.

2026 Plan:
Expansion and Scaling – We intend to implement a CRM for secure case management to enhance family support and enable data-driven advocacy. To support this, we need funding for a case manager to oversee our expanding volunteer network.

## Community Building & Local Resilience

We have built a dynamic nationwide community that is active both online and through in-person meetings. This infrastructure creates a vital mutual support system that allows us to identify and respond to real-time needs emerging directly from the field.

2026 Plan:
We aim to launch three local communities in partnership with municipalities, each led by a trained Community Manager.

To scale up our impact, we plan to develop a digital platform that would ensure rapid access to information, active redirection to the appropriate organizations, and mutual support.

## Education, Adolescents & Family Cohesion

Israeli adolescents are navigating a continuous crisis, marching from the Covid pandemic directly into war. Addressing the erosion of communication between soldiers and their families is vital for maintaining resilience.

2026 Plan:
To launch family communication workshops for 11th–12th graders and their parents to strengthen familial bonds and improve communication before enlistment.

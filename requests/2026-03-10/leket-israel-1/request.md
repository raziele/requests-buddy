## The Ask

Leket Israel requires $508,210 weekly to maintain food distribution operations during the ongoing war with Iran, as their food rescue operations have been severely disrupted by rocket fire, volunteer limitations, and reduced surplus food availability.

**Funding Requested:** $508,210

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Meals: 5,000 additional meals daily
- Produce: Significant temporary purchases to maintain supply

**Non-Financial Ask:** Temporary food purchases and supermarket vouchers to support families who cannot access distribution points.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life in Israel has been marked by deep uncertainty, with sirens interrupting routines and families moving in and out of shelters. Leket Israel's food rescue operations have been disrupted due to rocket fire, closed corporate offices, and IDF restrictions on food suppliers. This has reduced daily meal distribution from 8,000 to 3,000 meals per day. Leket is supplementing rescued food with purchased food and providing supermarket vouchers to ensure vulnerable families continue receiving food during this critical period.

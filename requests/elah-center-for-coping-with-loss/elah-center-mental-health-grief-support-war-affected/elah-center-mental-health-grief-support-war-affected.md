## The Ask

Elah Center for Coping with Loss is seeking philanthropic funding to provide mental-health and grief support to populations affected by the prolonged war who fall outside government-funded programs—including support groups and individual therapy for trauma and grief, social-therapeutic groups and in-home therapy for the elderly, and culturally adapted therapy for the Arab community. In addition, following the escalation with Iran and Hezbollah, Elah has identified an urgent need to support welfare workers in municipal social services (e.g., a recent Zoom support meeting for approximately 500 Jerusalem welfare workers) and is proposing a national program of supervision, training, and guidance for these frontline staff.

## Context & Background

Since the outbreak of the Iron Swords War, government and philanthropic actors have expanded psychological assistance to direct victims. Many individuals still fall between the cracks—either ineligible for state funding or poorly served by existing frameworks. Elah Center seeks to provide tailored therapeutic support for these populations, including mothers and partners of combat soldiers, lone soldiers, bereaved commanders, second-degree family of fallen soldiers and civilians (Jewish and Arab), families of wounded or traumatized soldiers, and first-line emergency personnel. Programs are delivered by qualified therapists in group and individual settings; costs are documented per intervention type (e.g., NIS 3,000 per group session with two therapists, NIS 345 per individual session).

Elah has operated since 1979 with specialized trauma-informed care for the elderly and Holocaust survivors, and since 2010 has run a culturally adapted Arabic-speaking clinic in Kfar Kanna with over 50 therapists. Demand for elderly and Arab-community services has grown with the ongoing security situation.

Following the current war with Iran and Hezbollah, Elah has identified a new, urgent need: welfare workers in municipal and regional social services are under unprecedented emotional and professional strain, with rising risk of secondary trauma and compassion fatigue. Elah recently held a Zoom support meeting for roughly 500 welfare workers from the Jerusalem municipality and proposes a national program of ongoing group supervision, individual guidance, short workshops (on loss, trauma, self-care), and enrichment lectures. The organization has delivered similar support over the past year with success and seeks funding to continue and expand it.

# Project Proposals – Elah Center for Coping with Loss

Since the outbreak of the Iron Swords War, the Israeli government has significantly expanded the provision of psychological assistance to direct victims, alongside substantial support from philanthropic organizations. Many individuals continue to fall between the cracks—either because they are not eligible for government funding or because existing frameworks do not adequately address their specific circumstances. The Elah Center seeks to provide appropriate, tailored therapeutic support through philanthropic donations.

#### 1. Support Groups for Individuals Coping with Grief or Trauma

Populations include: mothers and partners of combat soldiers, female lone soldiers, bereaved commanders, close friends of fallen soldiers and civilians, second-degree family members of fallen soldiers or civilians (Jewish and Arab), family members of wounded or traumatized soldiers, and first-line emergency personnel. Each group is facilitated by two qualified therapists. Groups meet for a minimum of 12 sessions (option to extend to 30–40). Cost per group session (two therapists): NIS 3,000; 12-session group total: NIS 36,000.

#### 2. Individual Therapy for People Coping with Grief or Trauma

For those who do not meet government-funded care criteria. One-hour session (clinic or online): NIS 345. Basic package of 20 sessions: NIS 6,900.

#### 3. Social-Therapeutic Support Groups for the Elderly

Demand has grown; methods adapted to elderly needs (music, photographs, games, art, movement, animals, gardening). Average 12 participants, 90-minute weekly sessions. Cost per session NIS 1,500; 20-session group total: NIS 30,000.

#### 4. Individual Therapy for Homebound Elderly

In-home, one-hour session: NIS 400. Basic package of 20 sessions: NIS 8,000. Many elderly and Holocaust survivors are not eligible for state-funded assistance.

#### 5. Group and Individual Therapy for the Arab Community in Israel

Clinic in Kfar Kanna since 2010; over 50 Arabic-speaking therapists. Culturally adapted individual and group therapy in clinics or clients' homes. Group: 12 participants, 90-minute weekly sessions, NIS 1,500 per session, 12-session total NIS 18,000. Individual: NIS 345/session, 20 sessions NIS 6,900.

**Objectives:** Support emotional processing, strengthen resilience and coping skills, foster belonging and reduce isolation, prevent isolation and suicide, enhance emotional recovery, maintain personal and professional functioning.

**Conclusion:** Elah has the professional knowledge, therapists, and infrastructure to provide this support nationwide; the only missing component is funding.

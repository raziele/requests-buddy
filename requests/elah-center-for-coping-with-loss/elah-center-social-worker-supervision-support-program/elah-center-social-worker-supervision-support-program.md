## The Ask

The Elah Center proposes a nationwide program of support and supervision for social workers in municipal social service departments. These professionals face heavy workloads, exposure to extreme cases, and insufficient budgets in a challenging social, economic, and security reality; since October 2023 and the current escalation, pressures have intensified, placing them at risk of vicarious trauma, secondary traumatization, and compassion fatigue. The program would provide ongoing group supervision, individual supervision and personal support, short-term workshops, and educational lectures on bereavement, trauma, and self-care. The Center seeks partnership with local authorities and governmental bodies; no specific funding amount is stated in the proposal.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Collaboration with local authorities and governmental bodies to establish dedicated support within the described framework

## Context & Background

Social workers in Israel's municipal social service departments serve diverse populations, face heavy workloads, and are exposed to extreme cases while working with insufficient budgets in an exceptionally challenging social, economic, and security reality. Since October 2023 Israel has been in prolonged warfare, creating an unprecedented emotional and social burden. The current escalation and war with Iran have increased the number of affected civilians and intensified pressures on social workers. Ongoing exposure places these professionals at significant risk of vicarious trauma, secondary traumatization, and compassion fatigue.

The Elah Center has specialized for many years in treatment of trauma, bereavement, and loss. It operates nationwide and includes approximately 600 experienced professionals from therapy and emotional support, committed to strengthening social resilience and mental health in Israel. The proposed program includes ongoing group supervision sessions, individual supervision and personal support for staff, short-term workshops (experiential elements, knowledge sharing, practical tools), and educational lectures on topics such as bereavement, trauma, self-care, and related subjects. The Center states it has provided similar services over the past year with success and seeks to continue and expand this work.

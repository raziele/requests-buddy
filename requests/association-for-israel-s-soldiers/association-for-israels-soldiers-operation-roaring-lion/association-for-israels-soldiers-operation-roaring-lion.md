## The Ask

The Association for Israel's Soldiers, the sole IDF-authorized nonprofit for soldier welfare, is seeking support for Operation Roaring Lion following the Iranian attack of February 28, 2026. The portfolio outlines needs including hygiene kits (150 NIS female kit), physical and mental recharge getaways for units (approx. 100 NIS per soldier), financial aid for evacuated soldiers and families (2,000 NIS per family), aid for injured soldiers' families (5,000 NIS per family within 24 hours of admission), aid for bereaved families during shiva (2,000 NIS), lifesaving medical equipment for combat paramedics, and military ambulances (650k NIS per vehicle). The request is inferred from the attached portfolio document; no explicit ask amount in the email body.

## Context & Background

On February 28, 2026, IDF and U.S. forces launched Operation Roaring Lion. The Iranian attack struck Israel's civilian home front, causing fatalities, injuries, and damage to residential buildings, including homes of IDF service members. The Association for Israel's Soldiers provides welfare and well-being support before, during, and after military service. The portfolio describes immediate assistance for affected families (vouchers for essential needs), support for families of wounded soldiers (5,000 NIS vouchers within 24 hours of hospital admission), and support for bereaved families during shiva (2,000 NIS). Additional needs include lifesaving medical gear for combat paramedics, military ambulances for evacuation and medical response, and morale and comfort items (hygiene kits, recharge getaways) for forces in the field.

**ROARING LION / OPERATION**
Israel Defense Forces (IDF)

**Mission:** The IDF was founded to defend the country and protect its residents. The Association for Israel's Soldiers is the sole organization authorized by the IDF to receive and distribute donations for the welfare and well-being of IDF soldiers; 100% of donations go directly to benefit servicepeople.

**Context (Feb 28, 2026):** IDF and U.S. military forces launched a broad, coordinated campaign (Operation Roaring Lion). The Iranian attack struck Israel's civilian home front, resulting in fatalities, injuries, and damage to residential buildings, including homes of IDF service members. The Association provides immediate assistance to affected families.

- **Hygiene kits:** 150 NIS (female hygiene kit; each kit shared by 4 soldiers).

- **Financial aid for evacuated soldiers and families:** 2,000 NIS per family (vouchers for food, clothing, basic household supplies).
- **Lifesaving medical equipment:** For combat paramedics (life-preserving gear, monitoring, bleeding control).
- **Military ambulances:** 650k NIS per fully equipped vehicle (Mercedes Sprinter; donor recognition on vehicle after war).
- **Financial aid for soldiers injured moderately or seriously:** 5,000 NIS in vouchers to family within 24 hours of hospital admission; redeemable at ~60 store chains.
- **Financial aid for bereaved families in mourning:** 2,000 NIS in vouchers during shiva; redeemable at ~60 store chains.

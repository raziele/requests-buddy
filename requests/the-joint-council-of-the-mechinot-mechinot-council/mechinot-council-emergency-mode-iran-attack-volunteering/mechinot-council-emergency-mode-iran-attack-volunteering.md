## The Ask

The Mechinot Council requests urgent support to enable rapid transition to full emergency mode in anticipation of an expected Iranian attack. Funds will cover one week of emergency volunteering (3,000 students across 60 Mechinot centers and 200 sites) for transportation, vehicle expenses, additional staff, and equipment. The Council will activate a war room to collect field needs and connect them to Mechinot for volunteering; all activity is coordinated with government ministries and the National Emergency Authority.

**Funding Requested:** 295,000 NIS (one week of activity)

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Transportation: 180,000 NIS
- Vehicle expenses (leasing + fuel): 60,000 NIS
- Personnel (additional 20 staff): 40,000 NIS
- Equipment (cleaning, painting, activity materials): 15,000 NIS

## Context & Background

Israel is on high alert in anticipation of an expected Iranian attack. The Mechinot Council is prepared to shift at any moment to full emergency mode, including a war room to collect needs from the field and connect them to Mechinot for volunteering. All activity will be coordinated and approved by relevant government ministries and the National Emergency Authority. The Council is the umbrella for annual Mechinot (general and Torah-based), with ~6,300 students in 114 centers nationwide. Since the outbreak of the Swords of Iron war, the Council mobilized Mechinot for home-front and national resilience—over 600 days of war, 1,200+ sites, 230,000+ volunteer days; it was recently awarded the Presidential Medal of Honor. During Operation Am Lavi (Iran attack June 2025), the Council activated a unified war room within hours and deployed 25 Mechinot with 1,400+ students. The transition from routine to emergency will entail unexpected expenses: dedicated transport to volunteering sites (no public transport, road risk), additional staff (reserve duty, closed institutions), and equipment. The budget table is for one week (Sunday–Sunday), 3,000 students, 60 Mechinot activity centers, 200 volunteering sites.

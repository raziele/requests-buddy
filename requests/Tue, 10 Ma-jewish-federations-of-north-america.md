## Context & Background

The Jewish Federations of North America, through its Israel & Overseas Committee, has developed a guide for supporting Israelis with disabilities. This initiative, launched in late 2022, aims to provide a data-informed approach to philanthropic endeavors in this sector. The report acknowledges the significant need for services due to the high number of individuals who have sustained physical and psychosocial disabilities, particularly since the events of October 7, 2023. It highlights that while Israel has a legislative framework for disability rights, implementation gaps persist. The guide offers strategic directions and allocation recommendations for Jewish Federations to maximize their impact by supporting innovative programs, filling service gaps, and investing in infrastructure and human resources. It also identifies a list of vetted nonprofit organizations working in this field.

### Supporting Israelis with Disabilities: A Strategic Guide for Jewish Federations (February 2026)

**Executive Summary:**
This report by the Jewish Federations of North America's Israel & Overseas Committee Designated Allocations Task Force provides a guide and recommendations for supporting people with disabilities in Israel. It aims to be a practical, strategic resource for Jewish Federations.

*   Approximately 2 million Israelis (20%) live with various disabilities (psychosocial, physical, sensory, developmental, learning, intellectual, and chronic illnesses).
*   While Israel has a strong legislative framework, uneven implementation, regional disparities, and bureaucratic complexities create gaps in services.
*   The urgency has increased since October 7, 2023, due to a rise in physical and psychosocial disabilities, necessitating greater demand for services.
*   Philanthropy has an opportunity to complement government investment, build nonprofit capacity, and accelerate systemic change.

**Task Force Methodology:**
The Task Force, launched in March 2025, examined the disabilities sector through data analysis, meetings with government officials, academics, nonprofit leaders, and people with disabilities. They reviewed Jewish Federations' allocations from 2021-2024 and consulted with experts.

**Guiding Principles for Funding:**
*   Prioritize programs not funded by the government (e.g., accessibility, advocacy, public awareness, technology, research).
*   For government-funded service providers, focus on:
    *   Developing innovative approaches with pathways for government adoption.
    *   Investing in infrastructure and human resources to maximize public funding.
    *   Providing additional services in areas not funded by the government but with clear needs.
    *   Prioritizing underserved populations (adults, minorities, etc.).

**Recommended Organizations:**
The report recommends 18 vetted nonprofit organizations and continued support for JDC's Israel Unlimited (JDC-IU).

**Key Sections:**
*   **Introduction:** Commitment to well-being of people with disabilities and promoting inclusion.
*   **Background Information:** Prevalence of disabilities, legislative progress (e.g., Equal Rights for Persons with Disabilities Law, UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities), and ongoing challenges (e.g., community-based housing, regional disparities).
*   **October 7th Impact:** Rise in physical and psychosocial disabilities, increased demand for rehabilitative services and support systems.
*   **Current Funding Landscape:** Government as the primary funder, with philanthropy filling gaps and driving innovation.
*   **Jewish Federations Allocations:** Analysis of designated funding from 2021-2024.
*   **Jewish Federations' Historic Partners:** JDC (focusing on JDC-Israel Unlimited - JDC-IU).
*   **Recommendations:** Strategic guidance for funding.
*   **Recommended Organizations:** List of vetted organizations.

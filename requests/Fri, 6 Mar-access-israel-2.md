## The Ask

Access Israel is urgently requesting $400,000 to provide immediate, accessible, and safe emergency housing and support for individuals with disabilities and the elderly during the ongoing conflict with Iran. They aim to ensure that vulnerable populations are not left behind and have access to essential services, including evacuation, accessible temporary housing, medical equipment, and specialized transportation.

**Funding Requested:** $400,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency accessible and protected housing for evacuees: None
- Dissemination of life-saving information via various media: None

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership for immediate funding and support for emergency operations.

## Context & Background

The ongoing conflict with Iran has created an urgent need for humanitarian aid, particularly for vulnerable populations such as individuals with disabilities and the elderly. Access Israel highlights that approximately 40% of these individuals live without accessible shelters, leaving them with no safe alternative during air raid alarms. The organization has been on the front lines since October 7th, providing critical humanitarian assistance, including accessible evacuation, temporary accessible housing, medical equipment, specialized transportation, and logistical support during emergencies.

Access Israel emphasizes that those most in need are often the last to receive institutional support. The current escalation, beginning February 28, 2026, necessitates immediate and significant efforts to provide accessible, professional, and sensitive assistance tailored to the unique needs of people with disabilities and the elderly. They are seeking an initial $400,000 to fund immediate operations for ten days, with further assessment for longer-term needs based on the conflict's duration.

Key immediate actions to be funded include operating an accessible 24/7 emergency hotline with professional staff, developing and maintaining the 'Purple Vest' app (an emergency response system), and providing emergency communication systems. Additionally, funds will support refresher training and operation of the 'Purple Vest' system for volunteers, accessible evacuation equipment, and operational coordination to ensure rapid response to missile attacks. The organization also plans to operate accessible transport vehicles with lifts for safe evacuation and transfer of individuals with disabilities.

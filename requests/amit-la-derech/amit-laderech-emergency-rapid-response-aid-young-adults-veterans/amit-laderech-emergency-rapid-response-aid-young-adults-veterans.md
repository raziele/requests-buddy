## The Ask

Amit La'Derech is requesting NIS 205,000 in emergency rapid-response support due to escalating wartime pressures on vulnerable young adults and veterans transitioning to civilian life. The organization reports sharply increased demand for urgent educational, financial, therapeutic, and stabilization assistance, and seeks funding to both expand internal response capacity and increase direct scholarship and emergency aid so participants can maintain rehabilitation, studies, and employment pathways.

**Funding Requested:** NIS 205,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Expand Director of the Responses Field role from 50% to 100%: NIS 105,000
- Increase scholarship and emergency assistance fund: NIS 100,000

## Context & Background

Amit La'Derech describes a prolonged national emergency context in Israel, spanning from October 7 through the first direct attack from Iran and the current escalation during Operation Rising Lion. According to the request, this sustained period has created compounding emotional, social, employment, and financial consequences for soldiers and reservists, with especially severe effects on young adults who already lacked stable family or economic support.

Founded in 2015, the organization now runs 16 active groups and has supported more than 1,300 young adults in the transition from military service to civilian life. Program scale has expanded significantly: 180 active participants in 2024 and 260 new participants in 2025. The request highlights strong outcomes in planning for the future, well-being, mentor satisfaction, and sense of belonging.

The organization relies on a flexible response mechanism to address barriers as they arise. In 2025, it delivered 226 responses including scholarships, emergency aid, computers and study equipment, tutoring, emotional treatment, financial counseling, and professional support. The request states that demand intensity has outpaced current staffing and resources, particularly with growing needs among Druze participants, disabled IDF veterans, lone soldiers, reservists, evacuees, and residents in highly affected areas.

To respond, Amit La'Derech seeks emergency support to expand a key response-management role and increase direct aid funds. Evidence cited includes 32 responses totaling NIS 52,420 for 21 wounded IDF veterans (via Beit Halochem) and 25 responses totaling NIS 33,941 for 13 reservists in 2025, with reported impact on sustaining rehabilitation, study continuity, and employment stabilization.

## AMIT LA'DERECH
### Emergency Request for Rapid Response Support During Operation Rising Lion

**Request:** NIS 205,000

Amit La'Derech requests emergency support to address a sharp increase in urgent needs among vulnerable young adults and veterans. The document cites sustained threats and attacks affecting Israel for more than two years, from October 7 through the first direct attack from Iran and the current escalation.

The request states cumulative impacts include emotional, employment, financial, and social consequences for soldiers and reservists, with more severe pressure on young adults lacking stable family/economic support. It reports rising demand from Druze participants, disabled IDF veterans, lone soldiers, reservists, young adults in heavily affected areas with limited accessible protected spaces, evacuees, and graduates needing renewed support.

- Growth figures:

## Use of funds

1. **NIS 105,000** to expand the Director of the Responses Field role from 50% to 100%.

2. **NIS 100,000** to increase scholarship and emergency assistance funds.
   - Intended uses: study continuity, urgent stabilization, equipment, tutoring, therapeutic support, and other time-sensitive barriers.

## Why Amit La'Derech can deliver

The organization describes itself as a mentoring and empowerment program. Mentors provide trusted personal guidance, while the organizational response mechanism provides broader support through mediation, advocacy, and practical solutions, including linking participants to existing community resources and creating flexible immediate supports tailored to this population.

The request asserts that this combined model has helped young adults progress in recovery, continue studies and rehabilitation, and avoid falling through systemic gaps.

## The Ask

Amit La'Derech is requesting an emergency grant of NIS 205,000 to respond to a sharp increase in urgent needs among vulnerable young adults and veterans affected by prolonged war-related escalation. The organization seeks to expand its rapid-response operational capacity and increase direct emergency assistance and scholarship support so participants can maintain rehabilitation, continue studies, and stabilize employment and daily functioning.

**Funding Requested:** NIS 205,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Expand Director of the Responses Field role from 50% to 100%: NIS 105,000
- Increase scholarship and emergency assistance fund: NIS 100,000

## Context & Background

Amit La'Derech states that cumulative security escalation from October 7 onward, including multi-front threats and the latest escalation during Operation Rising Lion, has intensified pressure on young adults and veterans transitioning to civilian life. According to the request, emotional strain, employment disruption, financial stress, and social instability have deepened, especially for participants who already lacked strong family or economic support.

Founded in 2015, the organization reports that it now runs 16 active groups nationwide and has supported more than 1,300 young adults. It describes rapid growth in activity, with 180 active participants in 2024 and 260 new participants in 2025, alongside demand from additional high-need groups such as Druze participants, disabled IDF veterans, lone soldiers, reservists, evacuees, and graduates needing renewed support.

The organization presents a flexible response mechanism that provides practical aid as needs arise, including scholarships, emergency assistance, equipment, tutoring, emotional treatment, and financial counseling. In 2025 it delivered 226 responses overall; in cited subgroups, 21 wounded veterans received 32 responses totaling NIS 52,420, and 13 reservists received 25 responses totaling NIS 33,941. The requested emergency funding is positioned as necessary to preserve rapid, accountable delivery capacity and to prevent participants from losing rehabilitation, education, and employment progress.

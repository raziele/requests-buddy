## The Ask

Amit La'Derech seeks NIS 205,000 in emergency funding to scale its rapid-response mechanism during Operation Rising Lion. The organization reports sharply increased urgent needs among vulnerable young adults and veterans—spanning emotional, employment, financial, and social pressures—after sustained threats from October 7 through direct attacks from Iran and ongoing escalation. Funds would full-time the Director of the Responses Field role (currently half-time) and expand scholarships and emergency assistance for study continuity, stabilization, equipment, tutoring, and therapeutic support.

**Funding Requested:** NIS 205,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Expand Director of the Responses Field from 50% to 100% FTE (assessment, coordination, allocation, documentation, follow-up, accountable delivery of aid): NIS 105,000
- Scholarship and emergency assistance fund (study continuity, urgent stabilization, equipment, tutoring, therapeutic support, other time-sensitive barriers): NIS 100,000

## Context & Background

Founded in 2015, Amit La'Derech runs a mentoring and empowerment model with trusted mentors plus a flexible responses mechanism that addresses financial, educational, professional, and emotional barriers as they arise. It now operates 16 active groups nationwide and has accompanied more than 1,300 young adults through the transition from military service to civilian life. Participation grew from 180 active participants in 2024 to 260 new participants in 2025, and in 2025 the organization delivered 226 responses including scholarships, emergency aid, computers and study equipment, tutoring, emotional treatments, financial counseling, and other professional support.

The request argues the model is proven but that pace and intensity of need have outstripped half-time capacity in the responses function and the scholarship/emergency pool. Cited demand includes Druze participants, disabled IDF veterans, lone soldiers, reservists, people in heavily affected areas with limited accessible protected spaces, evacuees, and alumni needing renewed support. Evidence from 2025: among wounded veterans participating through Beit Halochem, 21 young people received 32 responses totaling NIS 52,420; among a reservist group, 13 young people received 25 responses totaling NIS 33,941—framed as helping sustain rehabilitation, return to studies, and stabilize employment.

Support is framed as urgent because cumulative security events have deepened consequences for those who served throughout the period, especially for young adults who already lacked stable family or economic support. The organization positions the funding as enabling faster, accountable delivery of aid alongside mentoring, preventing participants from falling through gaps in community resources.

## AMIT LA'DERECH

### Emergency Request for Rapid Response Support During Operation Rising Lion

**Request:** NIS 205,000

Amit La'Derech respectfully requests emergency support to meet a sharp rise in urgent needs among vulnerable young adults and veterans. For more than two years, the State of Israel has faced sustained threats and attacks from multiple fronts, from October 7 through the first direct attack from Iran and the current escalation. The cumulative impact has been profound, particularly for soldiers and reservists who have fought throughout this period and now face emotional, employment, financial, and social consequences. These pressures are even more severe for young adults who were already vulnerable and lacked stable family or economic support. The organization is seeing growing demand from Druze participants, disabled IDF veterans, lone soldiers, reservists, young adults in heavily affected areas with limited accessible protected spaces, evacuees, and program graduates whose stability has been disrupted and who now require renewed organizational support.

### Why this request is urgent

Founded in 2015, Amit La'Derech now operates **16 active groups** across the country and has accompanied **more than 1,300 young adults** through the transition from military service to civilian life. The organization has expanded rapidly over the past year: **180 participants** were active in **2024**, and **260 new participants** joined in **2025**. This growth has been matched by strong outcomes in future planning, well-being, mentor satisfaction, and belonging.

Its flexible responses mechanism addresses financial, educational, professional, and emotional barriers as they arise. During **2025**, **226 responses** were provided, including scholarships, emergency assistance, computers and study equipment, tutoring, emotional treatments, financial counseling, and other professional support. The problem now is not whether the model works; it is that the pace and intensity of need have increased as the organization serves a wider population, including young adults directly affected by the war.

### Evidence of current need

- Among a **reservist group**: **13** young people received **25** responses totaling **NIS 33,941**.

These responses helped participants sustain rehabilitation, return to studies, and stabilize employment paths.

### Why Amit La'Derech can deliver

Amit La'Derech is a mentoring and empowerment program. Its mentors are trusted, consistent figures who provide personal guidance and support, but they cannot by themselves deliver the full organizational safety net many young adults now need. To meet that gap, the organization has built a responses mechanism that provides mediation, advocacy, and practical solutions—from connecting participants to existing community resources to creating new, flexible, and immediately available supports tailored to this population. This combination of close mentoring and rapid organizational response has repeatedly helped young adults make meaningful progress in recovery, continue their studies and rehabilitation, and avoid falling through the cracks.

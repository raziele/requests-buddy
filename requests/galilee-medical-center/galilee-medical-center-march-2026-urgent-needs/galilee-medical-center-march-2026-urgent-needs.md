Galilee Medical Center (Western Galilee Hospital in Nahariya) has submitted a list of urgent needs for March 2026, forwarded by the Jewish Federation of Northern New Jersey’s Israel Office. ## Context & Background

Galilee Medical Center in Nahariya is the primary trauma and referral hospital for the entire Western Galilee. The region frequently faces security threats, including missile attacks, and the hospital must continue to provide uninterrupted care during emergencies. The medical center currently lacks a PET-CT scanner; residents must travel long distances for this critical oncology diagnostic. Cancer patients and others undergoing intensive treatment need continuous care and cannot safely delay diagnostics or therapy.

The list of urgent needs emphasizes emergency preparedness: renovation of two protected underground hospital complexes (one estimated at $3 million for up to 110 people, another at $2 million for approximately 80 people) to ensure continuity of care during crises; a fortified Hemato-Oncology Institute with PET-CT so that immunocompromised cancer patients can receive treatment and advanced imaging even under threat; and relocation of the High-Risk Pregnancy Ward to a protected underground area, creating an opportunity to renovate and upgrade the regular ward. The Western Galilee has a young and growing population, increasing demand for obstetric, pediatric, and women’s health services.

Equipment requests (monitors, defibrillators, ICU beds, ventilators, imaging and surgical devices) are framed as essential for rapid diagnosis, life-saving procedures, and mass-casualty readiness. The attachment provides itemized projects with dollar amounts and descriptions. The email was forwarded by the Jewish Federation of Northern New Jersey’s Israel Office, which offered to obtain a different format or additional details if needed.

## The Ask

Latet is requesting $300,000 to support its emergency response operation launched in the wake of the direct Iranian attacks on Israel (February 28–March 1, 2026). As Israel's social first responder, Latet is distributing immediate relief kits and emergency shelter kits to displaced families, and conducting outreach to all elderly and Holocaust survivor aid recipients to ensure their needs are continuously addressed. The organization has already prepared 3,000 packages for distribution in the coming days and is asking its partners to help build capacity to respond to the size and nature of the crisis.

**Funding Requested:** $300,000

## Context & Background

Since the early hours of Saturday, February 28, 2026, Israel has been under an active state of emergency following direct Iranian attacks on Israeli territory. Iran fired waves of missiles and drones targeting civilian population centers across the country. With sirens sounding repeatedly, millions of civilians were forced to seek shelter for prolonged periods in reinforced safe rooms, public shelters, parking garages, and light rail stations—many lacking adequate food, water, bedding, or basic sanitation. National mobility was severely restricted, and schools and workplaces closed. As of Sunday morning, approximately 190 displaced families had been temporarily accommodated in five locations across Tel Aviv, Haifa, Petah Tikva, Ramat Gan, and Bnei Brak.

Latet works as the first social responder in times of crisis, in close coordination with local authorities and the IDF Home Front Command. Its emergency response includes direct distribution of immediate relief kits for displaced families (adapted to their needs), provision of emergency shelter kits, and outreach to all elderly and Holocaust survivors in its aid system. The organization has prepared 3,000 packages for distribution in the next few days and may increase this number as the situation evolves.

Latet is requesting $300,000 in immediate assistance to scale this response. As a long-standing partner, they are asking for support to help Israelis in need during what they describe as worrying and crucial days for Israeli society.

## The Ask

Latet is requesting $300,000 in emergency funding to scale its response to the March 2026 Iranian attack on Israel. As Israel's social first responder, Latet is distributing immediate relief kits to displaced families, providing emergency shelter kits, and ensuring continuous outreach to elderly and Holocaust survivor aid recipients. They have already prepared 3,000 packages for distribution and are asking their partner (JUF) to support this capacity-building effort during the critical first phase of the crisis.

**Funding Requested:** $300,000

## Context & Background

On February 28–March 1, 2026, Israel came under direct Iranian missile and drone attacks targeting civilian population centers. Sirens sounded repeatedly; millions of civilians sought shelter for prolonged periods in reinforced safe rooms, public shelters, and improvised locations (parking garages, light rail stations), many without adequate food, water, bedding, or sanitation. National mobility was severely restricted, schools and workplaces closed, and daily life halted. Initial assessments showed population displacement, damage to residential buildings, and growing difficulties accessing food, essential supplies, and basic services—especially for displaced families, elderly, and Holocaust survivors with limited mobility.

As of Sunday morning March 1, approximately 190 displaced families had been temporarily accommodated in 5 locations across Tel Aviv, Haifa, Petah Tikva, Ramat Gan, and Bnei Brak. Latet works in close coordination with local authorities and the IDF Home Front Command. Its emergency operations include direct distribution of immediate relief kits for displaced families (adapted to their needs), provision of emergency shelter kits, and outreach to all elderly and Holocaust survivors in its aid network to ensure needs are continuously addressed.

Latet has prepared 3,000 packages for distribution in the coming days and may increase this number as the situation evolves. The organization is asking its existing partner (JUF) for $300,000 in immediate assistance to build capacity to respond to the size and nature of the crisis, with donations at any amount welcomed.

Since the early hours of Saturday, February 28, 2026, Israel has been under an active state of emergency following the launch of direct Iranian attacks on Israeli territory. Iran has fired waves of missiles and drones, targeting civilian population centers all over the country.

With sirens sounding repeatedly throughout the entire day and continuing through the night, millions of civilians are forced to seek shelter for prolonged periods. National mobility has been severely restricted, schools and workplaces have been closed, and daily life has been abruptly brought to a halt.

Initial assessments indicate population displacement, damage to residential buildings, and growing difficulties in accessing food, essential supplies, and basic services, particularly for displaced families, elderly, and Holocaust survivors with limited mobility, as well as the countless individuals seeking shelter.

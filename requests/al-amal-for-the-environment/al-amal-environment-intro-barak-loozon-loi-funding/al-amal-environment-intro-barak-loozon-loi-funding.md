# Al-Amal for the Environment
**Because the Climate Challenge is Everyone's Concern**

Established in 1995, Al-Amal (Hope in Arabic) works within Israel's Arab minority and in joint Jewish-Arab dynamics to promote environmental protection and improvement, helping the community engage, campaign, and influence a wide range of issues. By developing local solutions, implementing sustainability initiatives, and driving action on climate change in the Galilee region, Al-Amal ensures everyone is involved and engaged in the fight for environmental protection. Al-Amal empowers Arab and Jewish communities to work together on issues of mutual concern, thereby supporting Galilee's social integration. The organization has strong links at all levels, from the grassroots to the national, and is a trusted partner to municipalities, councils, and government agencies. Al-Amal has earned a strong reputation and is frequently featured in the media.

**Theory of change:** (1) By bringing Jews and Arabs in the Galilee together around shared interests, they contribute to the development of a vibrant, inclusive society. (2) By mentoring activist groups and regional agencies to advance climate action, they improve environmental conditions and help create a more sustainable future for the region.

**Past/current funders:** Jewish Federation of New York, European Union, Beracha and Yad Hanadiv Foundations, U.S. Embassy, and others.

## Programming

**Galilee Mayors Forum** (established 2022)
- Brings together leaders of 15 Arab municipalities across the Galilee to collaborate on climate change preparedness, waste management, and environmental planning.
- Serves as a collaborative think tank and focal point for joint environmental advocacy.
- Facilitated by Dr. Dov Khanin (Head of the Israeli Climate Forum), Prof. Rassem Khamaisi (University of Haifa), and Dr. Orly Ronen (Tel Aviv University).
- Works with government agencies to create, ratify, and secure funding and support for environmental protection and development in the Galilee.
- **Overall annual cost: $52,000.**

**Arab-Jewish Citizens' Forums on Shared Environmental Concerns**
- Focus on shared spaces and shared society through planning and development of these spaces.
- Plan, develop, and execute joint environmental projects: removing environmental hazards, creating shared walkways and public spaces, engaging local communities.
- Three forums operating: Beit HaKerem, Hilazon Valley, Jdeide-Makr-Tal'el. Each includes at least 30 citizens.
- Achievements include rejuvenating the Hilazon River, creating green neighborhoods and community gardens.
- Build community connections and train activists in lobbying, public protest, stakeholder engagement, and planning processes.

**'Local Impact' – Working with Local Authorities and Businesses**
- Three-year EU grant (2021) with Zalul, The Natural Step, and 2B Friendly: promoting environmentally responsible practices among local authorities and businesses (carbon footprint reduction, energy efficiency, waste minimization, 'Green Label' for organizations meeting environmental standards). Al-Amal continues to support partners in reducing their environmental impact.

## Towards 2026

**A. Youth groups**
- Broaden Jewish-Arab work by establishing joint youth groups focused on the environment. High schools and municipal education departments have expressed interest, including new approaches to building a shared society after the war in Gaza.
- **Overall annual cost: $55,000.**

**B. Women's Local Environmental Improvement Committees**
- Around 50 women activists across the Galilee (Arab and Jewish) studying and implementing local environmental improvements.
- Aim to professionalize and formalize these groups: planning and developing local village environments (reducing hazards, reclaiming derelict spaces as communal gardens, "Green Neighborhoods," public awareness campaigns).
- Participants gain empowerment, local pride, and social standing within their communities.
- **Overall annual cost: $38,000.**

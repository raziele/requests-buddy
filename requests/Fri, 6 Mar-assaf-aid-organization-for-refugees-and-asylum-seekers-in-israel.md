## The Ask

ASSAF is operating in emergency mode to support refugees and asylum seekers in Israel during continuous missile attacks and sirens. They are adapting services, mapping urgent needs, strengthening resilience and emotional support, and disseminating accessible information about emergency guidelines and available services. They have expanded remote psychosocial responses and established an emergency telephone line.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Continued partnership and support during this emergency.

## Context & Background

Communities across Israel have faced continuous missile attacks and repeated sirens over the past three days, leading to the closure of educational frameworks and the shutdown of most workplaces under emergency regulations. This situation has a profound impact on the vulnerable refugee community, which is largely excluded from national safety nets and emergency assistance.

In response, ASSAF is operating in emergency mode, adapting all services to the security situation in accordanceance with Home Front Command guidelines. Their staff and volunteers are mapping urgent needs, maintaining contact, and strengthening resilience and emotional support. They are also actively disseminating accessible, multilingual information about emergency guidelines and available services, and have expanded remote psychosocial responses, including increased availability of emotional support, expanded reception hours, and an emergency telephone line to ensure continued accessibility and rapid response for the refugee community.

## The Ask

HRCC requests **$200,000** to expand capacity ahead of an expected post–Operation Shaagat HaAri surge in demand. Past conflicts show requests can rise by up to **30%** after de-escalation; many survivors withdraw during active operations and reach out when calm returns. By end of **2025**, helplines received **over 13,000** requests (contacts rose from roughly **~10,000** to more than **13,000** through the year, with heavy volume late in the year).

Funding supports:

1. **New volunteer training course (emergency cohort)** — recruitment, screening, **80-hour** professional training, clinical supervision, mentorship, materials — backbone of the **24/7** hotline so increased volume is absorbed **without wait times**.
2. **WhatsApp expansion** — extended hours, more trained responders per shift, stronger supervision for digital responders; critical when survivors **cannot speak aloud** (shelters, safe rooms, limited privacy) or prefer written contact; elevated need **at night** during/after alerts; some contacts involve **extremely difficult circumstances** (e.g., sheltering with a perpetrator, escalating suicidal ideation).
3. **Surge / immediate response capacity** — intake, coordination, flexible staffing; peak-hour coverage; **accompaniment** to police and through **criminal** proceedings; support at **National Insurance (Bituach Leumi)** hearings; **Safe Social Space** as resilience-building response; coordination among hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up.

## Context & Background

**Double trauma and post-crisis surge:** Research and decades of HRCC experience indicate survivors often experience collective crises as double trauma—minimizing pain, shame, and delayed help-seeking during war. When the emergency eases, many reach out at once. Symptoms can worsen in the interim (flashbacks, avoidance, neglect of needs, declining functioning; in some cases heightened despair and **suicide risk**).

**Operation Shaagat HaAri:** Follows ~**two years** of sustained war, displacement, uncertainty, and cumulative stress. Sirens, confinement, and renewed insecurity reactivate **hypervigilance and panic**, especially where nervous systems were already strained—**recovery is not linear** and renewed operations can undo months of therapeutic progress.

**HRCC track record:** The center has responded to every major escalation and national crisis over **four decades** across **41** Northern Israel local authorities; 24/7 hotline and digital channels are often the **first or only** place survivors feel able to turn.

**Partnership context:** Outreach was relayed by **Yael Yabes**, strategic consultant at **HaTasheet (התשתית)**, who supports HRCC on resource development and noted alignment with **San Francisco Federation’s** focus on trauma and people in need.

**Illustrative survivor voice (from formal letter):**

> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

## The Ask

HRCC requests **$200,000** to expand capacity ahead of an expected post–Operation **Shaagat HaAri** surge in demand. Research and decades of field experience indicate that during active conflict many survivors withdraw, minimize their own pain, and seek less help; after de-escalation, requests often rise by **up to ~30%** in the following weeks. By end of 2025 the center received **over 13,000** helpline requests (with **contacts rising from ~10,000 to more than 13,000** during the year, many late in the year). Funding supports: a **new volunteer training cohort** for the **24/7 hotline**, **expanded WhatsApp** support (critical when survivors cannot speak aloud in shelters or lack privacy, including difficult night-time contacts), and **stronger immediate response** (intake, peak-hour coverage, accompaniment to police and through legal proceedings, support at **Bituach Leumi / National Insurance** hearings, **Safe Social Space**, and coordination between hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up) so survivors who reach out in the coming weeks are met without delay by trained, available responders.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **New Volunteer Training Course** (recruitment, screening, 80-hour training, supervision, mentorship, materials): $95,000  
- **WhatsApp Expansion** (extended hours, additional responders, supervision): $60,000  
- **Surge Capacity Reinforcement** (intake, coordination, flexible / peak-hour staffing): $45,000  

## Context & Background

Operation Shaagat HaAri follows nearly **two years** of sustained war, displacement, and cumulative national stress. Many survivors had only just begun to stabilize; renewed **sirens, confinement, and insecurity** can reactivate hypervigilance and panic and **undo months of therapeutic progress**. HRCC has responded to every major escalation and national crisis over roughly **four decades** and is preparing now for a possibly **concentrated and intense** post-operation surge.

**Gendered strain:** Sources note compounded strain especially among **women whose nervous systems are already under prolonged strain**; services also address **men** among survivors in Northern Israel.

**Outreach / partnership context (one source):** The outreach was relayed by **Yael Yabes**, strategic consultant at **HaTasheet (התשתית)**, who supports HRCC on resource development and noted alignment with **San Francisco Federation** priorities around trauma and people in need.

## Clinical / psycho-social detail from the attachment narrative

Survivors of sexual violence often experience collective crises as **double trauma**: shame and self-silencing can worsen symptoms (flashbacks, avoidance, neglect of needs, declining functioning, heightened despair, **increased suicide risk**). One survivor quote from the letter:

> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

During acute operations communities operate in **survival mode** (emotional processing postponed; trauma responses contained); when calm returns, many survivors reach out **simultaneously** for support. Calls in 2025 reflected intensified distress—including urgent mental health needs, hospitalization referrals, worsening health and economic hardship, and deep needs to reduce isolation and rebuild anchors for daily life. The **WhatsApp** channel sees growing **late-night** contact; some messages involve sheltering with perpetrators or **escalating suicidal thoughts**, motivating extended hours, more responders per shift, and stronger supervision for digital responders.

## The Ask

HRCC requests **$200,000** to expand capacity ahead of an expected post–Operation **Shaagat HaAri** surge in demand. Research and decades of field experience show that during active operations many survivors withdraw, minimize their pain, and hesitate to seek help; when relative calm returns, many reach out at once. After previous conflicts, requests typically rose by **up to ~30%** in the weeks following de-escalation. By end of **2025**, helplines received **over 13,000** requests (one record notes growth from **~10,000 to more than 13,000**, with intensified contacts **late in the year**).

Funding would support: a **new volunteer training cohort** for the **24/7 hotline**; **expanded WhatsApp support hours** and responders (critical when survivors are in shelters, lack privacy, or prefer written contact—including **nighttime** outreach during or after alerts); and **reinforced immediate response** (intake, peak-hour coverage, **accompaniment to police and through criminal proceedings**, **National Insurance (Bituach Leumi) committee** hearings, **Safe Social Space**, and **coordination** between hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up) so survivors are met by trained, available responders **without delay**.

## Context & Background

Operation Shaagat HaAri follows **nearly two years** of sustained war, displacement, uncertainty, and cumulative national stress. Northern Israel is again under **sirens, tension, confinement, and insecurity**, which **reactivate hypervigilance, panic, and loss of control**, especially where nervous systems were already under prolonged strain. Many survivors had **only just begun to stabilize** (therapy, routines, work/social life); the new operation **interrupts fragile progress**. **Recovery is not linear**; repeated disruption can **undo months of therapeutic progress**.

HRCC has responded to **every major escalation and national crisis over the past four decades**. During acute operations, communities operate in **survival mode**; emotional processing is postponed and trauma responses contained—then, in quieter moments, survivors begin reaching out. The center is **preparing now** for a surge that may be **concentrated and intense**.

**Double trauma & help-seeking:** Collective crises layer onto sexual violence trauma. **Shame and self-silencing** can worsen symptoms (flashbacks, avoidance, neglect of needs, declining functioning) and, in many cases, correlate with **heightened despair and increased suicide risk**.

**Illustrative survivor quote** (from the formal letter):

> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

**2025 helpline picture:** Over 13,000 requests, including urgent mental health support, **referrals for hospitalization**, worsening health and economic hardship, and need to **reduce isolation** and rebuild anchors for daily life. WhatsApp contacts **grow late at night**; some messages reflect **extremely difficult circumstances** (e.g., sheltering with the person who harmed them, **escalating suicidal thoughts**).

**Scope:** HRCC serves **41 local authorities** across Northern Israel; hotline and digital platforms are often the **first and sometimes only** place survivors feel able to turn.

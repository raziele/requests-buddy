## The Ask

HRCC is requesting $200,000 to expand capacity ahead of an expected post–Operation Shaagat HaAri surge in demand. Based on past conflicts, requests typically rise by up to 30% after de-escalation. Funding would support a new volunteer training cohort, extended WhatsApp support hours (critical for survivors in shelters or with limited privacy), and reinforced immediate response capacity so that when survivors reach out in the coming weeks they are met by trained, available responders.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- New Volunteer Training Course (recruitment, training, supervision): $95,000
- WhatsApp Expansion (extended hours, additional responders, supervision): $60,000
- Surge Capacity Reinforcement (intake, coordination, flexible staffing): $45,000

## Context & Background

Research and field experience show that survivors of sexual violence often experience collective crises as a double trauma. During war many withdraw, minimize their own pain, and hesitate to seek help. When the immediate emergency subsides and people have space to process what they endured, many reach out simultaneously. Following previous conflicts, HRCC has seen requests rise by up to 30% in the weeks after de-escalation. By end of 2025 the center received over 13,000 requests for assistance.

Operation "Shaagat HaAri" comes after nearly two years of sustained war and cumulative national stress. Many survivors had only just begun to stabilize; this new operation interrupts that progress. Repeated exposure to national emergencies compounds trauma—sirens, confinement, and renewed insecurity reactivate hypervigilance and panic, especially among women whose nervous systems are already under prolonged strain.

HRCC has responded to every major escalation and national crisis over the past four decades. The $200,000 would fund: (1) a new volunteer training course (recruitment, 80-hour training, supervision) to absorb increased call volume; (2) expansion of WhatsApp support hours and trained responders, including at night when many survivors write during or after alerts; (3) stronger immediate response capacity, accompaniment to police and through legal proceedings, support at Bituach Leumi hearings, and use of the Safe Social Space. HRCC serves 41 local authorities across Northern Israel.

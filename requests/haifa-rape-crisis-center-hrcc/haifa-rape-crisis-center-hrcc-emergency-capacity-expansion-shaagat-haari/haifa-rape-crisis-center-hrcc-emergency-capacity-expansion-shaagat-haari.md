## The Ask

HRCC is requesting **$200,000** in emergency/program capacity funding to prepare for an expected post-operation surge in demand when Operation "Shaagat HaAri" de-escalates. Based on research and decades of field experience, survivors of sexual violence often withdraw during active conflict and then reach out in concentrated waves once relative calm returns. HRCC reports that following prior conflicts, requests typically rise by up to **30%** in the weeks after de-escalation.

The funding will ensure survivors are met without delay through expanded hotline and digital response staffing, stronger accompaniment and follow-up capacity, and immediate operational readiness in Northern Israel.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- New Volunteer Training Course (recruitment, training, supervision): $95,000
- WhatsApp Expansion (extended hours, additional responders, supervision): $60,000
- Surge Capacity Reinforcement (intake, coordination, flexible staffing): $45,000

## Context & Background

Operation "Shaagat HaAri" has returned communities to sirens, alerts, confinement, and prolonged uncertainty after nearly two years of sustained war, displacement, and cumulative stress. HRCC notes a recurring trauma pattern: during acute emergencies, many survivors self-silence, minimize personal suffering, and postpone emotional processing; once there is quiet, many seek support at once.

By the end of 2025, HRCC helplines received **over 13,000** requests/contacts (up from approximately 10,000), with intensified distress reflected in urgent mental health support needs, referrals for hospitalization, worsening economic and health hardship, and severe isolation.

A representative testimony included in the attached letter:

> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

HRCC emphasizes heightened risk during this cycle, including worsening flashbacks, avoidance, declining functioning, and increased despair/suicide risk. The center has responded to every major national escalation over four decades and believes the coming post-operation demand spike may be especially concentrated and intense.

## Program Components (Emergency Capacity Expansion)

1. **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)**
   - Recruitment and screening
   - 80-hour professional training
   - Clinical supervision and ongoing mentorship
   - Training materials and operational costs
   - Goal: maintain 24/7 hotline responsiveness without wait times as volume rises

2. **Expansion of WhatsApp Support Hours**
   - Critical for survivors unable to speak safely (e.g., shelters/safe rooms, limited privacy)
   - Increased late-night demand and high-risk messages
   - Plan: longer operating hours, more trained responders per shift, stronger digital supervision/coordination

3. **Expanded Immediate Response Capacity**
   - Greater peak-hour coverage
   - Accompaniment to police stations and through criminal proceedings/court
   - Support for National Insurance (Bituach Leumi) hearings/committees
   - Use of Safe Social Space as protected resilience support
   - Stronger coordination across hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up services

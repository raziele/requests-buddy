## The Ask

The Haifa Rape Crisis Center (HRCC) is requesting **$200,000** to expand capacity ahead of an expected post–Operation Shaagat HaAri surge in demand. Research and decades of field experience show that during active military operations many survivors withdraw, minimize their own pain, and hesitate to seek help; when relative calm returns, requests typically rise by **up to 30%** in the weeks after de-escalation. By the end of 2025, HRCC helplines received **over 13,000** requests for assistance (contacts rose from roughly **~10,000 to more than 13,000** over the year, with intensified activity late in the year).

Funding would support: a **new volunteer training cohort** for the 24/7 hotline; **expanded WhatsApp support hours** and more trained responders (critical when survivors are in shelters, lack privacy, or cannot speak aloud—including at night during or after alerts); and **reinforced immediate response capacity** (intake, coordination, flexible staffing, accompaniment to police and through legal proceedings, support at Bituach Leumi / National Insurance hearings, Safe Social Space, and stronger coordination between hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up) so survivors are met without delay when outreach spikes.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

## Context & Background

Survivors of sexual violence often experience collective crises as a **double trauma**. During war, shame and self-silencing can worsen symptoms (flashbacks, avoidance, neglect of needs, declining functioning) and, in some cases, correlate with heightened despair and increased suicide risk. Operation Shaagat HaAri follows nearly two years of sustained war, displacement, and cumulative national stress; many survivors had only begun to stabilize before this operation interrupted that progress. Sirens, confinement, and renewed insecurity can reactivate hypervigilance and panic—especially where nervous systems were already under prolonged strain—and **repeated disruption can undo months of therapeutic progress**.

HRCC has responded to every major escalation and national crisis over the past **four decades**. The center serves **41 local authorities** across Northern Israel. The organization is preparing now for a surge that may be **concentrated and intense**.

**Relay / partnership context:** The outreach was relayed by **Yael Yabes**, strategic consultant at **HaTasheet (התשתית)**, who supports the center on resource development and noted alignment with **San Francisco Federation’s** focus on trauma and supporting people in need.

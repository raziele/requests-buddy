## The Ask

HRCC requests **$200,000** to expand capacity ahead of an expected post–Operation **Shaagat HaAri** surge. Research and field experience show survivors often experience collective crises as double trauma: during war many withdraw, minimize pain, and hesitate to seek help; when calm returns, many reach out at once. After prior conflicts, support requests rose by up to **30%** in the weeks after de-escalation. By end of **2025**, helplines received **over 13,000** requests (contacts rose from roughly **~10,000** to **>13,000**, with much of the increase late in the year). Funding supports a new volunteer training cohort, extended WhatsApp hours (critical in shelters / low-privacy settings), and stronger immediate response so survivors are met by trained, available responders without delay.

## Context & Background

Operation Shaagat HaAri follows nearly two years of sustained war, **displacement**, uncertainty, and cumulative national stress. Sirens, confinement, and renewed insecurity reactivate hypervigilance and panic, especially where nervous systems were already under prolonged strain; recovery is not linear and disruption can undo months of therapeutic progress.

HRCC has responded to every major escalation and national crisis over **four decades**. Preparedness focuses on: (1) **New volunteer training** — recruitment, screening, **80-hour** professional training, clinical supervision, mentorship, materials — backbone of the **24/7** hotline; (2) **WhatsApp expansion** — written contact when survivors cannot speak aloud; night-time and alert-period use; supervision for digital responders; (3) **Immediate response** — peak-hour coverage, accompaniment to police and through legal proceedings, support at **Bituach Leumi / National Insurance** hearings, **Safe Social Space**, coordination among hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up.

**Relay / partnership note (source B only):** Outreach was relayed by **Yael Yabes**, strategic consultant at **HaTasheet (התשתית)**, who supports HRCC on resource development and noted alignment with **San Francisco Federation**’s focus on trauma and people in need.

**Letter content (additional detail from source B):** Survivors may feel suffering is “less legitimate” during national emergency. Example from a survivor: *“I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes.”* Inward withdrawal can worsen flashbacks, avoidance, neglect of needs, functioning decline, despair, and **elevated suicide risk**. Post-calm, simultaneous outreach is expected. Calls reflected intensified distress — urgent mental health support, hospitalization referrals, worsening health and economic hardship, isolation, rebuilding daily anchors.

**Three program components (aligned with budget):**

1. **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)** — Recruitment/screening, 80-hour training, supervision/mentorship, materials/ops; absorbs volume without wait times.  
2. **WhatsApp support expansion** — Shelters, privacy, written contact; difficult late-night messages (e.g., sheltering with harm-doer, escalating suicidal ideation); extend hours, more responders per shift, stronger supervision/coordination.  
3. **Immediate response capacity** — Peak coverage; police/court accompaniment; National Insurance hearings; Safe Social Space; coordination across hotline, accompaniment, follow-up.

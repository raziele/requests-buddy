## The Ask

HRCC requests **$200,000** to expand capacity ahead of an expected post–Operation Shaagat HaAri surge in demand. Based on past conflicts, requests typically rise by up to **30%** after de-escalation. By end of **2025** the center received **over 13,000** requests for assistance (contacts rose from roughly **~10,000** to more than **13,000** during the year, with many contacts late in the year). Funding supports a new volunteer training cohort, extended WhatsApp support hours (critical for survivors in shelters or with limited privacy), and reinforced immediate response so survivors who reach out in the coming weeks are met by trained, available responders without delay.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- New Volunteer Training Course (recruitment, training, supervision): $95,000  
- WhatsApp Expansion (extended hours, additional responders, supervision): $60,000  
- Surge Capacity Reinforcement (intake, coordination, flexible staffing): $45,000  

## Context & Background

Research and field experience show that survivors of sexual violence often experience collective crises as a **double trauma**. During war many withdraw, minimize their own pain, and hesitate to seek help; when the immediate emergency subsides and people have space to process what they endured, many reach out **simultaneously**. Operation **Shaagat HaAri** follows nearly two years of sustained war, displacement, and cumulative national stress; many survivors had only begun to stabilize, and this operation interrupts that progress. Repeated exposure to national emergencies compounds trauma—sirens, confinement, and renewed insecurity reactivate hypervigilance and panic, especially among people whose nervous systems are already under prolonged strain; recovery is not linear, and repeated disruption can undo months of therapeutic progress.

HRCC has responded to every major escalation and national crisis over the past **four decades**. The funding targets: **(1)** a new volunteer training course (recruitment, **80-hour** professional training, clinical supervision, mentorship, materials) so the **24/7** hotline can absorb increased volume without wait times; **(2)** expansion of WhatsApp support—essential when survivors cannot speak aloud, including at night when many write during or after alerts, with more trained responders per shift and stronger supervision for digital responders; **(3)** surge capacity: peak-hour coverage, accompaniment to police and through legal/criminal proceedings, support at **Bituach Leumi** / National Insurance hearings, **Safe Social Space** for resilience, and coordination between hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up.

**Relay / partnership note:** The outreach was relayed by **Yael Yabes**, strategic consultant at **HaTasheet (התשתית)**, who supports the center on resource development and noted alignment with **San Francisco Federation’s** focus on trauma and supporting people in need.

**Title:** Preparing for the Post-Operation Surge: $200,000 Emergency Capacity Expansion During Operation "Shaagat HaAri"

### Letter summary (Executive Director)

As Operation "Shaagat HaAri" continues, HRCC is preparing for a known post-war dynamic: during acute operations communities are in survival mode and emotional processing is postponed; when relative calm returns, survivors begin reaching out.

Survivors often feel their suffering is less legitimate during national emergency. One woman wrote:

> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

That inward turn—shame and self-silencing—can worsen symptoms (flashbacks, avoidance, neglect of needs, declining functioning) and correlate with heightened despair and increased suicide risk.

After prior conflicts, support requests typically rose by up to **30%** in the weeks following de-escalation. By end of 2025, helplines received **over 13,000** requests, reflecting intensified distress—urgent mental health support, hospitalization referrals, worsening health and economic hardship, and need to reduce isolation and rebuild anchors for daily life. HRCC believes the post-operation surge may be **concentrated and intense** and is preparing to respond without delay.

### Three program components (as in proposal)

1. **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)** — Recruitment and screening; 80-hour professional training; clinical supervision and ongoing mentorship; training materials and operational costs; volunteers as backbone of the 24/7 hotline.  
2. **Expansion of WhatsApp Support Hours** — Written contact for shelters, limited privacy, or safety; growing late-night contacts; some messages describe extremely difficult circumstances (e.g., sheltering with the person who harmed them, escalating suicidal thoughts). Plan: extend hours, more responders per shift, stronger supervision/coordination for digital responders.  
3. **Expanding Immediate Response Capacity** — Peak-hour coverage; accompaniment (police, criminal proceedings, National Insurance committee hearings); Safe Social Space as resilience-building emergency response; coordination among hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up.

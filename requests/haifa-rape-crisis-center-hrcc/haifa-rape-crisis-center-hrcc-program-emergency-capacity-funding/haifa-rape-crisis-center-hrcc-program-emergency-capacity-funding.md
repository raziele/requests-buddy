## The Ask

HRCC requests **$200,000** to expand capacity ahead of an expected post–Operation Shaagat HaAri surge in demand. Past conflicts show requests can rise by up to **30%** after de-escalation; by end of **2025** the center received **over 13,000** requests for assistance (contacts rose from roughly **~10,000** to more than **13,000** over the year, with intensified volume late in the year). Funding supports: a **new volunteer training cohort** for the **24/7 hotline**, **extended WhatsApp support** (critical when survivors are in shelters or have limited privacy, or prefer written contact), and **reinforced immediate response** so outreach in the coming weeks is met by trained, available responders.

## Context & Background

**Double trauma and delayed help:** Research and field experience show survivors of sexual violence often experience collective crises as double trauma. During war many withdraw, minimize their pain, and hesitate to seek help; when calm returns, many reach out at once.

**Operation Shaagat HaAri:** Follows nearly two years of sustained war, displacement, and cumulative stress. Sirens, confinement, and renewed insecurity reactivate hypervigilance and panic—especially where nervous systems were already under prolonged strain—and can undo months of therapeutic progress.

**HRCC track record:** The center has responded to every major escalation and national crisis over roughly **four decades** and serves **41 local authorities** across Northern Israel.

**Program pillars (aligned with proposal):**

1. **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)** — Recruitment, screening, **80-hour** professional training, clinical supervision, mentorship, materials; expands 24/7 hotline capacity without wait times.
2. **WhatsApp expansion** — Extended hours, more trained responders per shift, stronger supervision for digital responders; many cannot speak aloud in shelters or safe rooms; some contacts arrive late at night in very difficult circumstances (e.g., sheltering with a perpetrator, escalating suicidal thoughts).
3. **Immediate response / surge** — Peak-hour coverage; accompaniment to police and through criminal proceedings; support at **Bituach Leumi** / National Insurance hearings; **Safe Social Space** for resilience; coordination among hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up.

**Relay / partnership context (from one source):** Outreach was relayed by **Yael Yabes**, strategic consultant at **HaTasheet (התשתית)**, who supports HRCC on resource development and noted alignment with **San Francisco Federation** priorities on trauma and supporting people in need.

**Title:** Preparing for the Post-Operation Surge: $200,000 Emergency Capacity Expansion During Operation "Shaagat HaAri"

### Letter highlights (Executive Director)

Research and decades of experience describe withdrawal and self-minimization during national emergency. Example from a survivor (as cited in the letter):

> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

The letter notes risks of worsening symptoms—flashbacks, avoidance, neglect of needs, functioning decline—and correlation with heightened despair and increased suicide risk. After prior conflicts, support requests rose by up to **30%** post–de-escalation; by end of 2025 helplines received **over 13,000** requests, including urgent mental health needs, hospitalization referrals, health/economic hardship, and isolation. The post-operation surge may be **concentrated and intense**; HRCC is preparing to respond **without delay**.

### Three components (summary)

1. **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)** — As above; volunteers as backbone of the 24/7 hotline.  
2. **Expansion of WhatsApp Support Hours** — Written contact for privacy/safety; plan to extend hours, add responders, strengthen supervision.  
3. **Expanding Immediate Response Capacity** — Peak coverage, police/court/insurance accompaniment, Safe Social Space, coordination across teams.

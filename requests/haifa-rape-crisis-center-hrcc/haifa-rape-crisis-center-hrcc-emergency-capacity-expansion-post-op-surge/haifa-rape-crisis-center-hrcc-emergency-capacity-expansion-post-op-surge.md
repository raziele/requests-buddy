## The Ask

HRCC is requesting **$200,000** in emergency capacity funding to prepare for a likely surge in demand for support when **Operation "Shaagat HaAri"** de-escalates.

Research and decades of field experience indicate that during active military operations many survivors withdraw, minimize their pain, and hesitate to seek help—then, once relative calm returns and people have space to process what they endured, requests commonly rise by **up to ~30%** in the weeks after de-escalation. HRCC reports that by the end of **2025** its helplines received **over 13,000** requests/contacts, including urgent needs that intensified later in the year.

The requested funding would support:
- **An additional volunteer training cohort** for the **24/7 hotline** (to absorb increased call volume without wait times)
- **Expansion of WhatsApp support hours** (critical for survivors in shelters or other limited-privacy environments, and for written outreach during high-stress periods)
- **Surge capacity reinforcement** for strengthened immediate response and coordination (including accompaniment and resilience-building support such as the Safe Social Space)

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- New Volunteer Training Course (recruitment, training, supervision): **$95,000**
- WhatsApp Expansion (extended hours, additional responders, supervision): **$60,000**
- Surge Capacity Reinforcement (intake, coordination, flexible staffing): **$45,000**

## Context & Background

Operation "Shaagat HaAri" has brought Northern Israel back to a reality of **sirens, tension, and uncertainty**. HRCC has responded to every major escalation and national crisis over the past **four decades**, and the center emphasizes a consistent pattern:

During acute operations, many communities function in “survival mode.” Emotional processing is postponed and trauma responses are contained; survivors may shrink inward due to shame, self-silencing, and the sense that their suffering is less legitimate compared to a national emergency.

One survivor recently wrote:
> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

The HRCC documentation describes that this tendency can worsen symptoms, correlating with:
- increased flashbacks
- avoidance of emotional support
- neglect of physical and emotional needs
- declining functioning
- heightened despair and **increased suicide risk** (described as a concern that can appear as suicidal thoughts escalate in incoming messages)

Based on what HRCC has seen previously, requests for support typically rise in the weeks following de-escalation—when people finally have a quiet moment and begin reaching out simultaneously. HRCC notes that in 2025 the surge was reflected in intensified emotional distress, including:
- urgent requests for mental health support
- referrals for hospitalization
- worsening health and economic hardship
- a profound need to reduce isolation and rebuild anchors for daily life

HRCC also highlights that this operation follows **nearly two years of sustained war, displacement, uncertainty, and cumulative national stress**. Many survivors had only recently begun to stabilize (returning to therapy, rebuilding routines, and cautiously reclaiming work and social life), and this new operation interrupts that fragile progress. Repeated exposure to national emergencies compounds trauma: **sirens, confinement, and renewed insecurity** can reactivate hypervigilance, panic responses, and loss of control, undoing months of therapeutic progress. HRCC therefore describes the post-operation surge as potentially both **concentrated and intense**, and it is preparing “to respond without delay.”

## Program Components (What the Funding Enables)

### 1. New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort) — $95,000
This funding element will support:
- recruitment and screening
- an **80-hour professional training** course
- clinical supervision and ongoing mentorship
- training materials and operational costs

HRCC describes volunteers as the backbone of a **24/7 hotline**, and the emergency cohort is intended to ensure increased call volume can be absorbed without wait times.

### 2. Expansion of WhatsApp Support Hours — $60,000
HRCC emphasizes that many survivors cannot speak aloud while in shelters/safe rooms or in other limited-privacy environments, and may feel safer initiating contact via written messages. The request includes:
- extending WhatsApp operating hours
- increasing trained responders per shift
- strengthening professional supervision and coordination for digital responders

HRCC notes that contacts often grow **late at night**, and that some messages reflect extremely difficult circumstances (including sheltering with the person who harmed them) as well as **escalating suicidal thoughts**.

### 3. Expanding Immediate Response Capacity — $45,000
The surge capacity reinforcement will include:
- expanded coverage during peak hours
- strengthened accompaniment to police and through criminal proceedings
- support at **National Insurance hearings** / National Insurance committee hearings (Bituach Leumi)
- utilizing the **Safe Social Space** as a resilience-building emergency response
- stronger coordination between the hotline, accompaniment teams, and follow-up services

HRCC frames this as ensuring that when survivors reach out in the coming weeks, they will be met immediately by a trained and available responder.

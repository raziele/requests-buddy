## The Ask

The Haifa Rape Crisis Center (HRCC) requests **$200,000** to expand emergency capacity and prepare for an expected surge in support needs as **Operation "Shaagat HaAri"** de-escalates.

Decades of experience and ongoing field research indicate that during active military operations, many survivors withdraw, minimize their own pain, and hesitate to seek help (often due to shame and self-silencing). When relative calm returns, many survivors reach out simultaneously, and requests for support commonly rise by **up to 30%** in the weeks following de-escalation. By the end of **2025**, HRCC helplines received **over 13,000 requests/contacts**.

This funding would support three coordinated areas:
- **New volunteer training cohort** for the 24/7 hotline to absorb increased call volume without wait times.
- **Expansion of WhatsApp support hours** to serve survivors who cannot speak aloud in shelters or with limited privacy, and to provide extended coverage when needs intensify late at night.
- **Surge capacity reinforcement** to strengthen immediate response, including accompaniment and legal/administrative support, Safe Social Space as an emergency resilience-building response, and improved coordination between hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up services.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- New Volunteer Training Course (recruitment, training, supervision): $95,000
- WhatsApp Expansion (extended hours, additional responders, supervision): $60,000
- Surge Capacity Reinforcement (intake, coordination, flexible staffing): $45,000

## Context & Background

Operation "Shaagat HaAri" brings Northern Israel back to conditions characterized by sirens, tension, and uncertainty. HRCC emphasizes that trauma recovery is not linear: repeated exposure to national emergencies can compound trauma, reactivate hypervigilance and panic, and undo months of therapeutic progress.

HRCC also notes that during acute operations:
- communities function in “survival mode,”
- emotional processing is postponed,
- trauma responses are contained,
- survivors often delay reaching out until there is space to process what they endured.

Following previous periods of conflict, HRCC observed that requests for assistance rise by up to **30%** in the weeks after de-escalation. This pattern is particularly concerning after nearly two years of sustained war, displacement, uncertainty, and cumulative national stress—when many survivors had only recently begun to stabilize.

**Illustrative internal description included in the request letter:**
A woman shared:
> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

HRCC links this inward shutdown (often driven by shame and self-silencing) to worsening symptoms, including increased flashbacks, avoidance of emotional support, neglect of physical and emotional needs, declining functioning, heightened despair, and increased suicide risk.

HRCC serves **41 local authorities across Northern Israel** and supports a broad range of survivors, including women and (as referenced in one version) men.

## Funding Components (What the Request Would Enable)

### 1. New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)
- Recruitment and screening
- **80-hour professional training**
- Clinical supervision and ongoing mentorship
- Training materials and operational costs

HRCC describes volunteers as the backbone of the **24/7 hotline**; expanding the cohort helps ensure increased volume can be absorbed without wait times.

### 2. Expansion of WhatsApp Support Hours
- Extended operating hours
- Increased trained responders per shift
- Strengthened professional supervision and coordination for digital responders

HRCC highlights that WhatsApp is essential when:
- women cannot speak aloud while in shelters or safe rooms,
- survivors have limited privacy,
- or written messaging feels safer when initiating contact.

The request also notes that growing contacts can occur late at night, including extremely difficult circumstances (e.g., sheltering with the person who harmed them, escalating suicidal thoughts).

### 3. Expanding Immediate Response Capacity
- Expanded coverage during peak hours
- Stronger accompaniment and support services, including:
  - accompaniment to police and through criminal proceedings,
  - support at National Insurance hearings / National Insurance committee hearings,
- Use of **Safe Social Space** as a protected, resilience-building emergency response option
- Improved coordination between hotline intake, accompaniment teams, and follow-up services

## The Ask

The Haifa Rape Crisis Center (HRCC) is requesting **$200,000** in emergency capacity funding to expand services ahead of an expected **post–Operation Shaagat HaAri** surge in demand. Based on past conflicts, support requests typically rise by **up to 30%** after de-escalation. In **2025**, HRCC helplines received **over 13,000** contacts (one intake notes a rise from **~10,000** to **>13,000**, with many contacts **late in the year**).

Funding would support: a **new volunteer training cohort** for the **24/7 hotline**; **extended WhatsApp support hours** and additional trained responders (critical when survivors are in shelters, lack privacy, or cannot speak aloud—**including at night** during or after alerts); and **reinforced immediate response capacity** (intake, peak-hour coverage, coordination, flexible staffing), including **accompaniment to police and through legal/criminal proceedings**, **support at Bituach Leumi / National Insurance hearings**, and use of the **Safe Social Space** as a **resilience-building** response—so that when survivors reach out in the coming weeks they are met **without delay** by trained, available responders.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- New Volunteer Training Course (recruitment, training, supervision): $95,000
- WhatsApp Expansion (extended hours, additional responders, supervision): $60,000
- Surge Capacity Reinforcement (intake, coordination, flexible staffing): $45,000

## Context & Background

Research and decades of field experience indicate that survivors of sexual violence often experience collective crises as a **double trauma**. During active military operations, many survivors **withdraw**, **minimize their own pain**, and **hesitate to seek help**—sometimes feeling their suffering is “less legitimate” amid a national emergency. **Operation Shaagat HaAri** has returned Northern Israel to sirens, tension, confinement, and prolonged uncertainty—reactivating **hypervigilance**, **panic**, and **loss of control**, especially where nervous systems are already under prolonged strain.

When relative calm returns and people have space to process what they endured, **many reach out simultaneously**. HRCC has responded to **every major escalation and national crisis over the past four decades**. This operation follows **nearly two years** of sustained war, displacement, and cumulative stress; many survivors had **only just begun to stabilize** (therapy, routines, work/social life) before this interruption. **Repeated disruption can undo months of therapeutic progress**; recovery is **not linear**.

The center is **preparing now** for a surge that may be **concentrated and intense**. HRCC serves **41 local authorities** across Northern Israel.

**Relay / partnership context (emergency-capacity source only):** The outreach was relayed by **Yael Yabes**, strategic consultant at **HaTasheet (התשתית)**, who supports the center on resource development and sees alignment with **San Francisco Federation’s** focus on trauma and supporting people in need.

## Illustrative survivor voice (from formal letter excerpt)

One woman recently wrote:

> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

The accompanying clinical framing (letter) notes this inward turn—shame and self-silencing—can worsen symptoms (flashbacks, avoidance, neglect of needs, declining functioning) and, in many cases, correlates with **heightened despair** and **increased suicide risk**. The 2025 helpline contacts also reflected intensified distress, including urgent mental health needs, referrals for hospitalization, worsening health/economic hardship, and needs to reduce isolation and rebuild daily anchors.

## WhatsApp / digital risk notes (letter detail)

The letter highlights **growing late-night contacts**, including messages from **extremely difficult circumstances** (e.g., sheltering with the person who harmed them; **escalating suicidal thoughts**), motivating expanded hours, more responders per shift, and stronger **supervision/coordination** for digital responders.

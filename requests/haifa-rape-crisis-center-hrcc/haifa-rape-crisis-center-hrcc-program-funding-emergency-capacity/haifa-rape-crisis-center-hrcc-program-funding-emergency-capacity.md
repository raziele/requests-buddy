## The Ask

The Haifa Rape Crisis Center (HRCC) is requesting **$200,000** to expand emergency capacity ahead of an expected post–Operation **Shaagat HaAri** surge in demand. Research and decades of field experience show that during active operations many survivors withdraw, minimize their own pain, and hesitate to seek help; when relative calm returns, requests typically rise by **up to 30%** in the weeks following de-escalation. By the end of **2025**, HRCC helplines received **over 13,000** requests for assistance (one record notes contacts rose from roughly **10,000** to more than **13,000**, with intensification **late in the year**).

Funding would support: (1) a **new volunteer training cohort** for the **24/7 hotline** (recruitment, **80-hour** professional training, clinical supervision, mentorship, materials) so increased volume can be absorbed **without wait times**; (2) **expansion of WhatsApp support hours** and trained responders—including **at night** when many survivors write during or after alerts—critical for those in shelters, safe rooms, or with limited privacy who cannot speak aloud or prefer written contact, with **stronger supervision** for digital responders; (3) **surge capacity reinforcement**: peak-hour coverage, **intake and coordination**, **flexible staffing**, **accompaniment** to police and through **criminal** proceedings, support at **National Insurance (Bituach Leumi)** hearings, and use of the **Safe Social Space** as a **resilience-building** emergency response, with stronger coordination between hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up.

## Context & Background

Operation **Shaagat HaAri** has returned Northern Israel to sirens, tension, confinement, and prolonged uncertainty. HRCC has responded to every major escalation and national crisis over the **past four decades**. This operation follows nearly **two years** of sustained war, **displacement**, and cumulative national stress; many survivors had **only just begun** to stabilize (therapy, routines, work/social life) before this new disruption. Repeated emergencies compound trauma—reactivating **hypervigilance**, **panic**, and loss of control—and can **undo months of therapeutic progress** (recovery is not linear).

Collective crises can land as a **double trauma**: shame and self-silencing can worsen symptoms (flashbacks, avoidance, neglect of needs, declining functioning) and, in many cases, correlate with heightened despair and **increased suicide risk**. During war, emotional processing is often postponed; when people finally have space to process, **many reach out simultaneously**.

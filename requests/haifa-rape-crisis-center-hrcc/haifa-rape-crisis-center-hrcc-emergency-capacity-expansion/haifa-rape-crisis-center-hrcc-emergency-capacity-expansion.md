## The Ask

HRCC requests **$200,000** in emergency/program capacity funding to prepare for an expected post-operation surge in demand when Operation "Shaagat HaAri" de-escalates. Based on research and four decades of field response, survivors often withdraw during active conflict and then seek support simultaneously when relative calm returns. Following previous conflicts, requests typically rise by up to **30%**. By the end of 2025, HRCC reported over **13,000** helpline contacts (up from approximately 10,000).

The funding is intended to ensure that when survivors reach out in the coming weeks, they are met immediately by trained, available responders without delay.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort):** $95,000  
  (recruitment, screening, 80-hour professional training, clinical supervision, mentorship, materials, operations)
- **WhatsApp Expansion:** $60,000  
  (extended operating hours, additional trained responders per shift, strengthened supervision/coordination)
- **Surge Capacity Reinforcement:** $45,000  
  (peak-hour coverage, intake/coordination, flexible staffing, accompaniment, follow-up)

## Context & Background

Operation "Shaagat HaAri" has renewed sirens, confinement, alerts, and prolonged uncertainty across Northern Israel after nearly two years of sustained war, displacement, and cumulative stress. HRCC notes a consistent pattern: during acute emergencies, many survivors minimize their own pain, self-silence, and postpone emotional processing; once calm returns, requests surge.

HRCC highlights this as a "double trauma" dynamic for survivors of sexual violence. Reported consequences during conflict periods include increased flashbacks, avoidance of support, decline in functioning, worsening mental-health distress, economic/health hardship, and in some cases increased suicide risk. One survivor message cited in the attached letter:

> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

HRCC has responded to major escalations and national crises over the past four decades and is preparing now for a potentially concentrated, intense post-operation surge.

## Program Components (Planned Response)

1. **24/7 Hotline Capacity Growth**
   - Add and train a new emergency volunteer cohort
   - Ensure increased call/message volume can be absorbed without wait times

2. **Digital Access via WhatsApp**
   - Expand hours, including late-night demand
   - Serve survivors who cannot speak aloud in shelters/safe rooms, lack privacy, or prefer written contact
   - Provide stronger supervision for digital responders

3. **Immediate Response & Accompaniment Reinforcement**
   - Expand peak-hour response
   - Maintain/strengthen accompaniment to police, criminal proceedings, and National Insurance (Bituach Leumi) hearings
   - Utilize Safe Social Space as a resilience-building emergency response
   - Improve coordination across hotline, accompaniment teams, and follow-up services

## The Ask

HRCC is requesting **$200,000** in emergency/program capacity funding to prepare for an expected surge in demand after de-escalation of Operation "Shaagat HaAri." Based on research and four decades of field experience, during active conflict many survivors withdraw, minimize their own pain, and delay help-seeking; when relative calm returns, requests often rise sharply (historically up to **30%** in the following weeks).

The requested funding is intended to ensure survivors are met immediately by trained responders through hotline, WhatsApp, and accompaniment systems during the anticipated post-operation surge.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

## Context & Background

Operation "Shaagat HaAri" has reintroduced sirens, security alerts, confinement, and prolonged uncertainty across Northern Israel. HRCC reports this operation follows nearly two years of sustained war, displacement, and cumulative stress, interrupting stabilization progress for many survivors who had begun returning to therapy and daily routines.

HRCC describes a consistent pattern across prior escalations:
- During acute emergency periods, emotional processing is postponed and many survivors self-silence.
- After de-escalation, survivors reach out in concentrated waves.
- Following previous conflicts, support requests rose by up to 30%.

By the end of 2025, HRCC reports more than **13,000** helpline requests/contacts (including a rise from approximately 10,000 to 13,000+), with intensified distress indicators including urgent mental health needs, worsening hardship, isolation, and increased risk signals (including suicidal ideation in some messages).

One cited survivor statement:
> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

HRCC has responded to major national crises for four decades and is preparing for a concentrated post-operation surge to avoid response delays.

## Program Components

1. **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)**
   - Recruitment and screening
   - 80-hour professional training
   - Clinical supervision and ongoing mentorship
   - Training materials and operational costs
   - Goal: maintain 24/7 hotline responsiveness without wait times during surge volume

2. **Expansion of WhatsApp Support Hours**
   - Extended operating hours (including late night)
   - Increased trained responders per shift
   - Enhanced supervision/coordination for digital responders
   - Rationale: many survivors cannot safely speak aloud (shelters/safe rooms, limited privacy, or safety concerns)

3. **Expanding Immediate Response Capacity**
   - Peak-hour coverage expansion
   - Accompaniment to police stations and through criminal proceedings
   - Support at National Insurance (Bituach Leumi) hearings/committee processes
   - Safe Social Space utilization as a resilience-building emergency intervention
   - Stronger coordination among hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up services

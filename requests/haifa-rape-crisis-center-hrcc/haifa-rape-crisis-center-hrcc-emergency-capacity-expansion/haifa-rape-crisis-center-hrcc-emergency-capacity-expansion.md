## The Ask

HRCC requests **$200,000** in emergency/program capacity funding to prepare for an expected post–Operation "Shaagat HaAri" surge in demand. Based on research and decades of field experience, during active conflict many survivors withdraw, self-silence, and delay help-seeking; when relative calm returns, requests typically rise by up to **30%** in the following weeks.

The funding is intended to ensure that when survivors reach out in the coming weeks, they are met immediately by trained, available responders across hotline, WhatsApp, and accompaniment services.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort):** $95,000  
  (recruitment, screening, 80-hour training, supervision, mentorship, materials, operations)
- **WhatsApp Support Expansion:** $60,000  
  (extended hours, additional responders per shift, stronger digital supervision/coordination)
- **Immediate Response / Surge Capacity Reinforcement:** $45,000  
  (peak-hour coverage, intake/coordination, flexible staffing, accompaniment, follow-up linkage)

## Context & Background

Operation "Shaagat HaAri" has renewed sirens, insecurity, and prolonged stress across Northern Israel. HRCC reports a consistent pattern across four decades of crisis response: trauma processing is often postponed during acute emergencies, then delayed demand appears in concentrated waves after de-escalation.

By the end of 2025, HRCC reported **over 13,000** helpline requests (with one source noting growth from approximately 10,000 to 13,000+). The center anticipates heightened risk now because this operation follows nearly two years of sustained war, displacement, and cumulative stress; many survivors had only recently begun stabilizing.

HRCC highlights that repeated exposure to national emergencies can reactivate hypervigilance, panic, and loss of control, and may reverse therapeutic progress. Reported effects include increased flashbacks, avoidance, functional decline, and in some cases increased suicide risk.

One survivor quote included in the source letter:
> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

## Program Components (Consolidated)

1. **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)**
   - Recruitment and screening
   - 80-hour professional training
   - Clinical supervision and mentorship
   - Training materials and operational support
   - Goal: maintain 24/7 hotline responsiveness without wait times during surge demand

2. **Expansion of WhatsApp Support Hours**
   - Supports survivors who cannot safely speak aloud (shelters/safe rooms/limited privacy)
   - Addresses increased late-night written outreach
   - Accounts for high-risk digital contacts, including severe distress and suicidality
   - Plan: longer operating hours, more responders per shift, strengthened supervision

3. **Expanded Immediate Response Capacity**
   - Additional peak-hour coverage
   - Accompaniment to police stations and through criminal proceedings
   - Support for National Insurance (Bituach Leumi) hearings/committee processes
   - Use of Safe Social Space as resilience-building emergency response
   - Tighter coordination among hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up services

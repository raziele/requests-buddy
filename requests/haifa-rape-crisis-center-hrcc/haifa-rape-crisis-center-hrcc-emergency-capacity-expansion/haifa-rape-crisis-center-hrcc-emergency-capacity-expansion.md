## The Ask

HRCC is requesting **$200,000** in emergency program funding to expand capacity ahead of an expected post–Operation "Shaagat HaAri" surge in demand. Based on research and four decades of field experience, the center reports that during active military escalation many survivors withdraw, self-silence, and delay seeking help; when relative calm returns, requests typically rise by up to **30%** in the following weeks.

The request is designed to ensure that when survivors reach out in the coming weeks, they are met immediately by trained and available responders through hotline, digital, and accompaniment services.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- New Volunteer Training Course (recruitment, training, supervision): $95,000
- WhatsApp Expansion (extended hours, additional responders, supervision): $60,000
- Surge Capacity Reinforcement (intake, coordination, flexible staffing): $45,000

## Context & Background

Operation "Shaagat HaAri" has renewed sirens, alerts, confinement, and uncertainty across Northern Israel after nearly two years of sustained war, displacement, and cumulative stress. HRCC notes this can reactivate hypervigilance, panic responses, and loss of control among survivors of sexual violence, many of whom had only recently begun to stabilize.

HRCC has responded to major escalations and national crises for over 40 years. A recurring pattern is that emotional processing is postponed during acute danger, then demand rises sharply once immediate threat decreases. The center reports:
- Up to **30%** post-conflict increases in support requests.
- Over **13,000** helpline requests in 2025 (also described as an increase from approximately 10,000 to more than 13,000).
- Growth in late-night and high-risk contacts, including severe isolation, acute distress, and suicide-risk indicators.

One quoted survivor message from the attached letter:
> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

HRCC emphasizes that repeated disruption can undo months of therapeutic progress and increase risks including worsening trauma symptoms, withdrawal from support, deteriorating functioning, and heightened despair.

## Program Components (Detailed)

1. **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)**
   - Recruitment and screening
   - 80-hour professional training
   - Clinical supervision and ongoing mentorship
   - Training materials and operational costs
   - Purpose: expand 24/7 hotline capacity and reduce wait times during surge periods

2. **Expansion of WhatsApp Support Hours**
   - Addresses barriers for survivors who cannot safely speak aloud (shelters/safe rooms/limited privacy)
   - Extended operating hours, including night-time demand
   - Increased trained responders per shift
   - Strengthened professional supervision and coordination for digital responders

3. **Expanding Immediate Response Capacity**
   - Expanded peak-hour response coverage
   - Accompaniment to police stations and through legal/criminal proceedings
   - Support at National Insurance / Bituach Leumi hearings
   - Use of the protected Safe Social Space as resilience-building emergency response
   - Stronger coordination between hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up services

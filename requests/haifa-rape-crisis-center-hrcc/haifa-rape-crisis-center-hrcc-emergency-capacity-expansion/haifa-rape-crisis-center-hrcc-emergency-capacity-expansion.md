## The Ask

HRCC requests **$200,000** in emergency/program capacity funding to prepare for an expected post–Operation "Shaagat HaAri" surge in demand. Based on research and four decades of field experience, during active military operations many survivors withdraw, minimize their pain, and delay help-seeking; when de-escalation occurs, requests often rise by up to **30%** in the following weeks.

The center is preparing now to ensure immediate response when survivors reach out, especially after nearly two years of sustained war, displacement, and cumulative stress that has repeatedly interrupted stabilization and recovery.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- New Volunteer Training Course (recruitment, training, supervision): $95,000
- WhatsApp Expansion (extended hours, additional responders, supervision): $60,000
- Surge Capacity Reinforcement (intake, coordination, flexible staffing): $45,000

## Context & Background

Operation "Shaagat HaAri" has renewed sirens, alerts, confinement, and uncertainty across Northern Israel. HRCC reports a consistent crisis pattern: communities function in survival mode during acute escalation; emotional processing is postponed; survivors often self-silence. When relative calm returns, many seek support at once.

HRCC has responded to every major escalation and national crisis over the past four decades. Following previous conflicts, support requests rose by up to 30% post-de-escalation. By end of 2025, HRCC helplines received **over 13,000** requests/contacts (also described as rising from approximately 10,000 to more than 13,000), with many contacts concentrated later in the year.

The organization describes risk intensification in this cycle due to prolonged cumulative stress. Repeated national emergencies can reactivate hypervigilance, panic, and loss of control, and may undo months of therapeutic progress. Reported impacts include increased flashbacks, avoidance of support, declining daily functioning, worsening health and economic hardship, isolation, urgent mental-health needs, and increased suicide risk.

One survivor message quoted in the attached letter:
> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

## Program Components

1. **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)**
   - Recruitment and screening
   - 80-hour professional training
   - Clinical supervision and ongoing mentorship
   - Training materials and operations
   - Goal: maintain 24/7 hotline responsiveness and avoid wait times during surge periods

2. **Expansion of WhatsApp Support Hours**
   - Critical for survivors who cannot safely speak aloud (e.g., shelters/safe rooms, limited privacy)
   - Expanded operating hours, including late-night coverage
   - Increased responders per shift
   - Strengthened supervision and coordination for digital responders
   - Includes severe-risk situations (e.g., sheltering with abuser, suicidal ideation)

3. **Immediate Response Capacity Reinforcement**
   - Expanded peak-hour coverage
   - Accompaniment to police and throughout criminal/legal proceedings
   - Support at National Insurance / Bituach Leumi committee hearings
   - Safe Social Space as resilience-building emergency response
   - Stronger coordination among hotline, accompaniment teams, and follow-up services

## The Ask

HRCC is requesting **$200,000** in emergency program capacity funding to prepare for an expected post-operation surge in demand after de-escalation of Operation "Shaagat HaAri." Based on research and decades of field experience, survivors often defer help-seeking during active conflict and then reach out in concentrated waves once relative calm returns. HRCC reports post-conflict increases of up to **30%**, with helpline contacts rising from approximately **10,000** to over **13,000** by the end of 2025.

The requested support is intended to ensure that when survivors reach out in the coming weeks, they are met immediately by trained, available responders across hotline, digital, and accompaniment services.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- New Volunteer Training Course (recruitment, training, supervision): $95,000
- WhatsApp Expansion (extended hours, additional responders, supervision): $60,000
- Surge Capacity Reinforcement (intake, coordination, flexible staffing): $45,000

## Context & Background

Operation "Shaagat HaAri" has renewed sirens, confinement, and prolonged uncertainty in Northern Israel following nearly two years of sustained war, displacement, and cumulative stress. HRCC notes that many survivors had only recently begun to stabilize (returning to therapy, routines, work, and social life), and repeated disruptions can reactivate trauma symptoms and undo therapeutic progress.

HRCC, which has responded to major escalations over four decades, describes a consistent pattern:
- During acute conflict, many survivors withdraw, minimize their pain, and delay seeking help.
- After de-escalation, many survivors seek support at once, creating a concentrated surge.

Documented risks include intensified emotional distress, flashbacks, avoidance, reduced functioning, isolation, worsening health and economic hardship, and in some cases heightened despair and suicide risk.

One survivor message cited in the request:
> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

## Program Components (Targeted Emergency Capacity Expansion)

1. **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)**
   - Recruitment and screening
   - 80-hour professional training course
   - Clinical supervision and ongoing mentorship
   - Training materials and operational costs  
   - Purpose: expand backbone staffing for the 24/7 hotline and reduce wait times during surge periods.

2. **Expansion of WhatsApp Support Hours**
   - Critical for survivors who cannot safely speak aloud (e.g., shelters/safe rooms, limited privacy).
   - Addresses late-night demand and high-risk written contacts.
   - Plan includes extended operating hours, additional trained responders per shift, and strengthened digital supervision/coordination.

3. **Expanding Immediate Response Capacity**
   - Expanded peak-hour response coverage
   - Continued/strengthened accompaniment (police, criminal proceedings, National Insurance/Bituach Leumi hearings)
   - Use of Safe Social Space as a resilience-building emergency response
   - Stronger coordination among hotline, accompaniment teams, and follow-up services

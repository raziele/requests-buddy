## The Ask

HRCC requests **$200,000** in emergency/program capacity funding to prepare for an expected post-de-escalation surge in demand following Operation "Shaagat HaAri." Based on research and four decades of crisis-response experience, survivors of sexual violence often withdraw during active conflict and reach out in concentrated waves when relative calm returns. HRCC reports that requests typically rise by up to **30%** after de-escalation, and helpline contacts increased from approximately **10,000 to over 13,000** by the end of 2025.

The funding is intended to ensure survivors are met quickly by trained responders in the weeks after de-escalation, including those with limited privacy in shelters/safe rooms and those seeking written support late at night.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- New Volunteer Training Course (recruitment, screening, 80-hour training, supervision, mentorship, materials): **$95,000**
- WhatsApp Expansion (extended hours, additional responders per shift, digital supervision/coordination): **$60,000**
- Surge Capacity Reinforcement (peak-hour coverage, intake/coordination, flexible staffing, accompaniment and follow-up): **$45,000**

## Context & Background

Operation "Shaagat HaAri" has renewed conditions of sirens, confinement, uncertainty, and cumulative stress after nearly two years of sustained war and displacement. HRCC states that many survivors had only begun to stabilize before this escalation, and repeated national emergencies can reactivate hypervigilance, panic responses, shame, and loss of control—sometimes undoing months of therapeutic progress.

HRCC has responded to every major escalation and national crisis over the past four decades. During acute periods, emotional processing is often postponed and many survivors minimize their own pain. When immediate danger recedes, requests rise sharply and simultaneously, including urgent mental health needs, requests related to safety and legal systems, and support for isolation and daily functioning.

One woman wrote:
> "I'm angry at myself for feeling so broken right now when other people have shrapnel falling on their homes."

HRCC notes additional risk indicators during these periods, including worsening symptoms, withdrawal from support, and increased suicide risk in severe cases.

## Program Components

1. **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)**
   - Recruitment and screening
   - 80-hour professional training
   - Clinical supervision and ongoing mentorship
   - Training materials and operational support  
   Goal: expand 24/7 hotline capacity and reduce wait times during surge periods.

2. **Expansion of WhatsApp Support Hours**
   - Extended operating hours (including late-night demand)
   - More trained responders per shift
   - Stronger professional supervision for digital response  
   Rationale: many survivors cannot speak aloud in shelters/safe rooms, lack privacy, or prefer written contact.

3. **Immediate Response Capacity Expansion**
   - Peak-hour response reinforcement
   - Accompaniment to police stations and through criminal/legal proceedings
   - Support at National Insurance (Bituach Leumi) hearings/committees
   - Use of Safe Social Space as a protected resilience-building response
   - Stronger coordination between hotline, accompaniment teams, and follow-up services

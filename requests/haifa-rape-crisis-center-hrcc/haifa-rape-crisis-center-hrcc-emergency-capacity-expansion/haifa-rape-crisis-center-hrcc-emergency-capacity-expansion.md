## The Ask

HRCC requests **$200,000** in emergency program capacity funding to prepare for an expected post-operation surge in demand following de-escalation of Operation "Shaagat HaAri."

Across prior conflicts, HRCC reports that during active war periods many survivors withdraw, self-silence, and delay help-seeking; when relative calm returns, requests typically rise by up to **30%** in the following weeks. HRCC received **over 13,000** contacts in 2025 (up from approximately 10,000), and expects the coming surge to be especially concentrated after nearly two years of cumulative war-related stress and disruption.

The funding is intended to ensure survivors are met immediately by trained responders without delay, including hotline, digital response, and accompaniment pathways.

**Funding Requested:** $200,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- New Volunteer Training Course (recruitment, screening, 80-hour training, clinical supervision, mentorship, materials, operations): $95,000
- WhatsApp Expansion (extended hours, additional trained responders, digital supervision/coordination): $60,000
- Surge Capacity Reinforcement (intake, coordination, flexible staffing, peak-hour coverage, accompaniment): $45,000

## Context & Background

HRCC has responded to major escalations and national crises for four decades. The center describes a consistent trauma pattern: in acute conflict, communities operate in survival mode and emotional processing is postponed; afterward, help-seeking rises sharply.

Operation "Shaagat HaAri" occurs after prolonged war, displacement, uncertainty, and repeated disruption. HRCC reports that many survivors had only begun to stabilize before this operation, and renewed sirens, confinement, and insecurity can reactivate hypervigilance, panic, and loss of control.

Key clinical and service concerns include:
- Increased flashbacks, avoidance, neglect of physical/emotional needs, and reduced functioning
- Heightened despair and increased suicide risk in severe cases
- Late-night and high-risk written outreach from survivors, including women sheltering in limited-privacy settings or with the person who harmed them
- Need for rapid coordination between hotline, digital channels, accompaniment teams, and follow-up services

HRCC emphasizes that hotline and digital platforms are often the first—and sometimes only—entry point survivors can access safely.

## Program Components (Unified)

1. **New Volunteer Training Course (Emergency Cohort)**
   - Recruitment and screening
   - 80-hour professional training
   - Clinical supervision and ongoing mentorship
   - Training materials and operational support
   - Goal: maintain 24/7 responsiveness and absorb increased volume without wait times

2. **Expansion of WhatsApp Support Hours**
   - Extend operating hours, including nights
   - Increase responders per shift
   - Strengthen professional supervision and coordination for digital responders
   - Rationale: written contact is essential for survivors unable to speak safely/aloud

3. **Expanded Immediate Response Capacity**
   - Peak-hour response coverage
   - Accompaniment to police, criminal proceedings, and National Insurance (Bituach Leumi) hearings/committees
   - Use of Safe Social Space as a resilience-building emergency response
   - Stronger integration of hotline, accompaniment, and follow-up pathways

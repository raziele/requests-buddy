## Context & Background

The ongoing war with Iran has created deep uncertainty and disruption across Israel, impacting daily life and emphasizing the importance of resilience through food security. Leket Israel, as the national food bank, is committed to ensuring vulnerable families have continuous access to nutritious food during this crisis.

However, the security situation has severely hampered Leket's operations. Rocket fire has led to pauses in volunteer activities and limitations on collection and distribution. Farmers have stopped harvests, businesses are closed, and IDF bases are inaccessible, all contributing to a significant decrease in the supply of available surplus food for rescue.

Despite these challenges, Leket Israel is striving to maintain and even increase regular distribution levels to its hundreds of nonprofit partners. To bridge the gap caused by the reduced supply of rescued food, Leket is purchasing additional meals and produce, and providing supermarket vouchers to families who cannot access distribution points. This emergency response is crucial for protecting health, reducing trauma, and maintaining the social fabric necessary for communities to endure.

Dear Tali and Michael,

I hope you and your families are doing as well as possible during these very difficult days. Tali, especially, we hope you and your loved ones are staying safe.

I also wanted to note that, while we have several grant requests currently under review with the Federation, this outreach addresses the additional emergency needs created by the current situation.

I wanted to share a brief update on how the current situation is affecting Leket’s work and the steps we are taking to ensure food continues reaching vulnerable families across the country.

Since the outbreak of war with Iran, daily life across Israel has been marked by deep uncertainty. Sirens interrupt routines, families move in and out of shelters, and the instability touches nearly every part of daily life.

In times like these, resilience becomes essential. A resilient society is one in which families know that even amid disruption, their most basic needs will be met. Food security is a critical part of that resilience. When parents know that nourishing food will be on the table, they are better able to care for their children and face the challenges of this period.

At Leket Israel, this responsibility guides our work. As Israel’s national food bank, we are committed to ensuring that vulnerable families continue to receive a reliable supply of nutritious food, even during national crises. Consistent access to food during war is essential to protecting health, reducing trauma, and maintaining the social fabric that helps communities endure and recover.

However, the current security situation has significantly disrupted the networks that enable food rescue. Rocket fire has forced us to pause volunteer activity and limit some of our collection and distribution operations. Farmers have halted harvests, corporate offices are closed, and IDF bases—our largest suppliers of cooked food—are not permitting entry. As a result, the supply of surplus food available for rescue has declined sharply.

Despite these challenges, our team is working tirelessly to ensure that food continues to reach the hundreds of nonprofit partners who depend on us. Our priority is to maintain regular distribution levels and, where possible, increase them so that vulnerable families do not face additional hardship during this period. To achieve this, Leket is temporarily supplementing rescued food with purchased food, and we are also providing supermarket vouchers to support families who require assistance but cannot access distribution points.

Maintaining these operations during the crisis requires an additional $508,210 per week, primarily to address the following gaps:

• Meals: The sharp drop in surplus meals available for rescue has reduced our daily distribution from 8,000 to 3,000 meals per day. To maintain supply, Leket must purchase an additional 5,000 meals daily.
• Produce: Farmers’ inability to harvest since the escalation began has drastically reduced the produce available for rescue, requiring significant temporary purchases to maintain supply for our nonprofit partners.

We would be deeply grateful for any emergency support the Federation may be able to provide at this time to help us sustain these efforts and ensure that vulnerable Israelis continue to receive the food they depend on during this critical period. The partnership and solidarity of Jewish communities abroad are especially meaningful during moments like these.

Thank you, as always, for your partnership and for standing with us.

Sincerely,

Rosie

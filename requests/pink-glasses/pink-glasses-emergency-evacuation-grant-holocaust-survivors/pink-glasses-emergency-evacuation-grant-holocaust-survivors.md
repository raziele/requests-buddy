## The Ask

Pink Glasses requests a dedicated emergency grant of 20,000 NIS to launch immediate evacuation operations for Holocaust survivors in complex medical conditions who cannot independently reach protected spaces. Social welfare authorities are sending a steady stream of urgent appeals; the current operational budget is earmarked for routine medical transport only, forcing the organization to decline life-saving evacuation requests. Funds will cover urgent specialized transportation to move survivors to safety without delay.

**Funding Requested:** 20,000 NIS

## Context & Background

Over the past 24 hours Pink Glasses has been receiving a steady stream of urgent appeals from social welfare authorities for immediate assistance evacuating Holocaust survivors from high-risk areas. These individuals are among the most vulnerable—survivors in complex medical conditions who are physically unable to reach safe rooms and shelters on their own. The current operational budget is strictly earmarked for routine medical transportation, so the organization cannot respond to these emergency evacuation requests. Experience suggests requests from welfare authorities will continue to grow as they process needs in high-risk zones. Pink Glasses is currently forced to decline life-saving requests due to lack of designated emergency funding. The requested 20,000 NIS will launch evacuation operations immediately for urgent specialized transport. Support at this moment is the difference between safety and severe risk for these survivors.

## The Ask

MATI Jerusalem is providing support to Jerusalem's business community following the outbreak of war. They have organized emergency webinars on topics such as Marketing in Times of War, Client Retention in Times of War, Crisis Management, and AI for Business. Business consultants are also offering one-on-one online consultations. Several programs have moved online, including a Hebrew for Employment course, TIFLI preschool program webinars, and financial literacy workshops. MATI also highlights its support for women entrepreneurs, providing them with tools, confidence, and networks to grow their businesses. In 2025, MATI programs benefited 800 women through the Turning Point program, 612 women received consulting, $1 million in loans was raised by women entrepreneurs, and 4,000 women participated in business courses. The email also includes links to stories of women entrepreneurs and impact videos.

## Context & Background

Following the outbreak of war on Saturday, March 7th, 2026, MATI Jerusalem has quickly mobilized to support the city's business community. Recognizing MATI as a key partner, the Jerusalem Municipality has requested their leadership in organizing emergency webinars for business owners. These webinars cover critical topics for navigating wartime business challenges, and one-on-one consultations are also being provided online.

MATI has adapted its programs to an online format to ensure continuity. This includes resuming a Hebrew for Employment course in East Jerusalem, launching webinars for families and daycare managers through their TIFLI preschool program to address anxiety in children and support staff mental health, and continuing financial literacy workshops online. A Facebook-based financial literacy competition has also seen significant expansion.

On International Women's Day, MATI is highlighting its dedicated support for women entrepreneurs through business accelerator courses, forums, and services. These initiatives aim to equip women with the necessary tools, confidence, and networks to build sustainable businesses and secure financing. The email provides statistics from 2025 showcasing the impact of these programs on women entrepreneurs.

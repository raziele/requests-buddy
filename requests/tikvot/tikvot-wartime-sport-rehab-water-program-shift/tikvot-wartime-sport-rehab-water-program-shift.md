## The Ask

TIKVOT shared a wartime program update describing major cancellations (three ski groups of over 180 participants and an April triathlon) and the resulting impact on participants who rely on sport for rehabilitation and emotional healing. The organization is shifting toward two summer water-based rehabilitation tracks, a local adaptive waterski program at the Kinneret and an overseas SUP/paddle initiative, while also aiming to support northern Israeli communities through participant stays in local guesthouses; they also invited partners to participate directly in these programs.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Invitation for the recipient and/or members to participate in the upcoming programs.

## Context & Background

TIKVOT reports that the current war period has disrupted core rehabilitation programming. In the past month alone, the organization canceled three ski groups totaling more than 180 participants, and also canceled an April triathlon. According to the message, many participants have remained homebound, which the organization describes as especially harmful for people who depend on structured sports activity as part of recovery and resilience.

The update emphasizes TIKVOT's ongoing therapeutic model, particularly adaptive sports for people with physical disabilities and trauma-related needs. The organization frames therapeutic riding and adaptive waterskiing as confidence-building and emotionally restorative interventions, noting that with adaptive equipment and trained instructors, participants with wheelchairs, prosthetic limbs, and trauma exposure can meaningfully engage.

In response to current constraints, TIKVOT is prioritizing two summer projects: a local waterski program on the Kinneret for immediate impact and an overseas SUP/paddle program for deeper rehabilitation. They also position the Israel-based program as dual-impact, serving participants while channeling activity to northern communities via lodging in local guesthouses and zimmers to support local economic recovery.

## The Ask

Rambam Health Care Campus, Northern Israel's largest referral hospital, is requesting support for a new Fortified Division of Critical Care (Smart ICU) and related emergency needs. With Operation Lion's Roar active, Rambam has moved approximately 700 patients into its Fortified Underground Emergency Hospital (FUEH), but its existing Critical Care Medicine Division remains in an unfortified facility over 25 years old, forcing staff to relocate ventilator-dependent and life-support patients under fire during sirens. The hospital has begun construction on a fully fortified Smart ICU and has raised $13.5 million to date; it is seeking $5 million for construction and $3 million for equipment to complete this project, plus additional support for advanced medical systems, critical infrastructure, and patient care infrastructure outlined in its Emergency War Fund.

**Funding Requested:** $8,000,000 ($5,000,000 construction + $3,000,000 equipment for Fortified Critical Care); brochure lists additional urgent needs totaling $12,500,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Fortified Division of Critical Care — construction: $5,000,000

- Critical Infrastructure (secure communications, computer systems, wartime operations): $1,800,000

## Context & Background

With the launch of Operation Lion's Roar, Rambam Health Care Campus activated full emergency protocols and opened its Fortified Underground Emergency Hospital (FUEH). Within hours, two floors of the FUEH were caring for approximately 700 patients: Rambam patients from unfortified departments, approximately 100 patients transferred from Fliman Geriatric Hospital, approximately 80 from Carmel Medical Center, and additional patients from unfortified hospitals across the region, including dozens of dialysis patients whose treatment cannot be interrupted. The FUEH is a five-acre facility fortified against conventional, biological, and chemical attacks, built to handle up to 2,000 patients; since February 28, 2026, essential medical services have moved from aboveground facilities into this space.

Wartime has produced a surge of critically ill patients requiring intensive care. Rambam's Critical Care Medicine Division, however, remains in an unfortified facility built more than 25 years ago. When sirens sound, teams must relocate these fragile patients—who depend on ventilators, continuous monitoring, and complex life-support systems—under fire. Drawing on lessons from recent conflicts, leadership decided to establish a new fortified Smart ICU that would provide uninterrupted, state-of-the-art critical care in what would be the largest and most advanced such facility in Israel, integrating real-time data, advanced monitoring, and life-saving technologies.

Construction of the Fortified Division of Critical Care has already begun. The hospital has raised $13.5 million for this project but still needs $5 million for construction and $3 million for equipment to bring it to life. An Emergency War Fund has been launched to meet growing demand; the attached brochure outlines additional urgent needs: advanced medical systems ($2.7M), critical infrastructure ($1.8M), and patient care infrastructure ($0.5M). Support is requested to deliver safe, uninterrupted care under fire and to complete the fortified ICU as a strategic asset for the State of Israel.

## The Ask

Inner Forces, a social initiative established after October 7th, requests support and visibility for its work strengthening families of Israeli service members. Approximately 30% of Israel's population are immediate family of those serving; the organization addresses their emotional and economic burden through resilience programs, community support, rights advocacy, employment stability, and education. In response to the current emergency (Operation Roaring Lion), they are launching immediate online resilience support groups for mothers of combat soldiers and wives of reservists—small, professionally facilitated groups (4 sessions each). They seek funding to run at least six such groups (three already initiated) and ask that the request be shared with federation colleagues and potential donors.

**Funding Requested:** 54,900 NIS (6 groups × 9,150 NIS per group)

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Resilience group (facilitation, coordination, content adaptation, between-session support): 9,150 NIS per group
- Six groups total (3 for mothers of combat soldiers, 3 for wives of reservists): 54,900 NIS

**Non-Financial Ask:** Share the materials with colleagues across the federation network and with potential donors in the community.

## Context & Background

Approximately 500,000 IDF service members took part in the Iron Swords War; their families—parents, siblings, partners, grandparents—represent about 30% of Israel's population and have been living since October 7th in ongoing worry, emotional overload, and economic and employment strain. While many support services target soldiers, their families have often been left outside formal support networks, and the security situation is expected to affect them for a long time. Inner Forces was established to fill this gap with a long-term support framework, public policy work, and research-based programs.

Their model addresses five pillars: resilience, community support, rights advocacy, employment stability, and education. In partnership with local municipalities they run the Community Resilience Ambassador Program (training volunteer networks to support families) and the Occupational Resilience Program (emotional support plus employment stability). Research with Ruppin Academic Center shows that mothers of combat soldiers experience ambiguous loss, ongoing worry, declines in functioning, and loneliness, and that peer support is a significant resilience factor.

In light of the current escalation (Operation Roaring Lion), they are experiencing a surge of requests from mothers of combat soldiers and wives of reservists. They adapted their annual resilience program into a condensed format: four online sessions per group, up to 20 participants, professional facilitators. Within the first week they initiated three of the planned six groups. Participants have reported strong relief (e.g., "I feel like someone finally gave me oxygen"; "For the first time, I don't feel crazy"). Funding is sought to open and sustain at least six groups; cost per group is 9,150 NIS, covering facilitation, coordination, content adaptation, and ongoing support.

# NATIONAL RESILIENCE BEGINS AT HOME
Inner Forces (HaNefashot HaPoalot) – Serving the Families Who Serve

Approximately 500,000 service members took part in the October 7th war, meaning nearly 30% of Israel's population, consisting of their families (parents, brothers/sisters, spouses, children, grandparents), have been living for over two years in a state of constant anxiety. While efforts and resources are allocated to support soldiers, their families remain largely overlooked. Inner Forces was established to fill this gap. With public mental health systems and state services already overwhelmed, targeted community-based support for families of soldiers is an urgent national necessity.

Inner Forces provides a holistic support framework for families of soldiers and reservists based on empirical knowledge and academic research. Core belief: when families are resilient, soldiers are resilient. Five core pillars: Resilience, Rights Advocacy, Community, Economic Stability, and Education.

Leadership: mothers of combat soldiers with professional backgrounds in healthcare, education, and social innovation.

## 1. Resilience Programs - From Concern to Community Action

**Community Resilience Ambassador Program** – Research-based annual model in partnership with local municipalities. For mothers of combat soldiers; year-long professional support, then participants serve as Resilience Ambassadors in their communities. Ongoing research with Ruppin Academic Center. Participants report significant improvement in well-being and resilience.

- **2025:** Four cohorts for soldiers' mothers, including mothers of injured soldiers; significant improvement in overall well-being.
- **2026:** Launch 10 additional cohorts; adapt model for fathers and wives; deliver 100+ workshops and retreats.

Study with Ruppin Academic Center on mothers of soldiers in combat and combat-support roles: ambiguous loss trauma affecting multiple areas including employment; peer support as key resilience factor. Strengthening mothers' and families' resilience contributes to soldiers' resilience.

Participant quotes: "The program helped me release constant tension, gain practical tools, and reconnect. It gave me hope." "We created a supportive community of strong mothers who are always there for each other."

## 2. Occupational Rehabilitation & Economic Stability

Approximately 20% of mothers of combat soldiers report reduced employment or job loss; about 45% of reservists' spouses report changes in employment status. Occupational resilience program: trauma-informed career counseling, employment navigation, guidance on retention.

**2026:** Launch five occupational resilience groups for mothers and reservists' spouses with municipalities and business sector; work with employers on trauma awareness and support for affected employees.

## 3. Rights Advocacy

Guidance for families navigating bureaucratic systems; national resource database. **2026:** CRM for secure case management, case manager to oversee expanding volunteer network.

## 4. Community Building & Local Resilience

Nationwide community, online and in-person. **2026:** Launch three local communities with municipalities, each with a trained Community Manager; digital platform for rapid access to information and mutual support.

## 5. Education, Adolescents & Family Cohesion

Addressing erosion of communication between soldiers and families. **2026:** Family communication workshops for 11th–12th graders and parents before enlistment.

# Immediate Resilience Support in Response to the Emergency Situation – March 2026

Approximately 500,000 IDF service members took part in the Iron Swords War; their families—parents, siblings, partners, grandparents—about 30% of Israel's population—have been living since October 7th in ongoing worry, emotional overload, and economic and employment strain. Families have often been left outside support networks. Inner Forces builds a long-term support framework and developed a multidisciplinary model: emotional resilience, community, employment, utilization of rights.

**Current situation:** In light of escalation in recent days, surge of requests from mothers of combat soldiers and wives of reservists. Home has become a frontline; emotional strain and burnout accumulating. Response: condensed rapid-response format—four online resilience sessions per group, small groups (up to 20), professional facilitators (therapy and group work).

**Ask:** Open at least six groups: three for mothers of active-duty and reserve combat soldiers, three for wives of reservists. **Cost per group: 9,150 NIS** (facilitation, participant coordination, content adaptation, ongoing support between sessions).

Within the first week of Operation "Roaring Lion," 3 groups already initiated. Participant feedback: "I feel like someone finally gave me oxygen. I was suffocating." "For the first time, I don't feel crazy." "Only other mothers can truly understand me." "Thank you for giving me solid ground to stand on."

## Resilience Sessions – Mothers of Combat Soldiers
Target: Mothers of soldiers in mandatory service, reserves, career service. Guided safe group space; professional tools for emotional regulation; coping with anxiety, burnout, stress. Psychoeducation (ambiguous loss, resilience), group dialogue, communication exercises; supportive peer community.

## Resilience Sessions – Wives of Reservists
Target: Wives of reservists with active and prolonged reserve duty. Safe space for emotional strain and burnout from running household and raising children during partner's absence; ongoing worry and shifts between emergency and routine. Psychoeducation, family resilience tools, dialogue, practical exercises; emotional regulation, daily functioning, couple and family communication; peer community.

## Community Resilience Ambassadors – Face-to-face program
Year-long program with local municipalities; mothers of soldiers (mandatory, reserves, career). Born from research and fieldwork: many mothers face ambiguous loss, ongoing worry, declines in functioning. Program: 10-session weekly training (3 hours each), up to 20 mothers—emotional processing, resilience tools, impact of war on all spheres. Trains participants as Community Resilience Ambassadors; after the course, monthly support, professional guidance, case consultation, links to therapeutic and rights-related services. Active community network of trained volunteers.

**2025:** Four cohorts nationwide, including mothers of injured soldiers and soldiers with PTSD; significant improvement in well-being; many graduates serve as resilience ambassadors. **2026:** 10 additional cohorts; adapt for fathers and wives; 100+ workshops and retreats.

## Research (Ruppin Academic Center)
Mothers of combat and combat-support soldiers: ambiguous loss; extreme and prolonged worry and anxiety; psychological impact includes emotional overload, decline in functioning at home and work, loneliness, diminished meaning. Mothers can recognize distress in children and refer them; supportive peer group is a significant resilience factor.

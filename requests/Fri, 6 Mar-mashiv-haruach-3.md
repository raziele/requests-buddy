## The Ask

Mashiv HaRuach requests support to renovate and establish an additional workshop space at the Metsoke Dragot retreat site. This expansion aims to double their capacity to provide trauma-informed resilience workshops for frontline caregivers, who are experiencing significant emotional distress due to the ongoing conflict.

**Funding Requested:** $150,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- The Full Healing Center: $150,000
- Main Workshop Hall: $75,000
- Private Meeting Room: $50,000
- Outdoor Healing Garden: $25,000

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership to make the vision real, sharing architectural plans, participant stories, and overview of program impact.

## Context & Background

Since October 7th, Israel's frontline caregivers have been under immense emotional strain, supporting others while dealing with their own trauma. Mashiv HaRuach was created to address this need, providing trauma-informed resilience workshops. In less than two years, they have served over 2,000 participants. Recognition of their work has led to increasing demand, but their capacity is limited by infrastructure. They have been offered an additional building at the Metsoke Dragot retreat site for renovation into a new workshop space, which would double their capacity and reduce wait times. Metsoke Dragot is a full partner in this project, contributing to planning and renovation costs.

Request for Support: Renovating a New Workshop Space at Metsoke Dragot

Since October 7th, Israel's social fabric has been held together by its invisible pillars:
medical teams, therapists, social workers, educators, and the spouses of reservists and
wounded soldiers. Many continue to support others while quietly carrying their own
emotional wounds. The growing risk of emotional collapse among them threatens the very
resilience of entire communities

Mashiv HaRuach was created in late 2023 to prevent exactly that. In less than two
years, over 2,000 participants have joined our trauma-informed resilience workshops. Each
group becomes a living support network that continues long after the sessions end.

Recognition of our work continues to grow among professional partners, government
institutions, and the participants themselves. With that recognition comes rising demand.
Dozens of organizations are already waiting for workshops, and we are currently unable to
meet the growing need. Our main limitation at this stage is infrastructural.

Doubling Our Capacity to Respond

To meet the growing demand, shorten waiting times, and expand our reach, we are seeking
your support for a focused project: renovating and establishing an additional workshop
space within the Metsoke Dragot retreat site.

Metsoke Dragot has become a full partner in Mashiv HaRuach's journey. Over the past two
years, dozens of workshops have been held there, and a deep relationship has developed
with the management and local team. They see the participants, understand our work
intimately, and have chosen to make a meaningful contribution to its expansion.

Metsoke Dragot has allocated an additional building within the compound for our use, and
they are actively involved in the project:

• The building itself has been made available to Mashiv HaRuach

• Their contractor and architect are already engaged in the planning

• Part of the renovation cost is being raised by them

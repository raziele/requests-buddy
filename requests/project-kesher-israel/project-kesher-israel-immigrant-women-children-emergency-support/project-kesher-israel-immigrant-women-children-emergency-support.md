## The Ask

Project Kesher Israel is requesting $50,000 (remaining need; total program $60,000) to support immigrant women and children during the current attacks. Interventions include emergency basic needs (food, water, clothing/blankets, emergency stipends) for women in shelters and displaced families; expanded mental health access (individual and group therapy in Russian/Ukrainian); and sustained staff capacity for real-time communication, daily Zoom sessions, and outreach. Many women lack safe rooms, have sustained damage (e.g. shattered windows, hearing impairment), and cannot access Hebrew-language news or overloaded public mental health systems.

**Funding Requested:** $60,000 total ($50,000 remaining need)

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Emergency basic needs ($100 per package, 200 women/families): $20,000
- Mental health services (individual and group therapy, ~300 high-risk women): $30,000
- Emergency programming and coordination (daily Zoom, outreach, 5,000+ network): $10,000

## Context & Background

Ongoing attacks have placed immigrant women in the network at acute risk. Many live in older buildings without safe rooms and repeatedly run to public shelters or stay in parking garages. Eleven participants reported shattered windows and structural damage; several had temporary hearing impairment. Women need Russian- and Ukrainian-language briefings; many report panic attacks, insomnia, and inability to function. Public mental health systems are overloaded. Some women are displaced to hotels without belongings; many care for children at home due to school closures; transportation disruptions isolate low-income women. An estimated 37% of Israeli residents lack safe spaces at home; the percentage is higher in the Russian-speaking community. One participant: 'This is my fourth war in four years.' Without intervention, acute distress risks becoming long-term trauma. PKI has 17+ years of experience and expanded after the arrival of 200,000+ immigrants/refugees from Ukraine, Russia, and Belarus.

## The Ask

Project Kesher Israel (PKI) is requesting emergency funding to support immigrant women and families in its network during ongoing attacks across Israel. Many women lack safe rooms, have experienced structural damage and trauma, cannot access Hebrew-language safety information, and face overloaded public mental health systems. PKI seeks to provide emergency basic needs (food, water, clothing, stipends) for women in shelters, expand trauma-informed mental health access in Russian and Ukrainian, and sustain daily coordination and Zoom programming for its network of 5,000+ women.

Without immediate intervention, acute distress risks becoming long-term trauma. # Project Kesher Israel: Supporting Immigrant Women and Children During Crisis

## Organizational Background

Founded in 1989, Project Kesher (PK) develops Jewish women leaders, strengthens pluralist Jewish communities, and advances civil society. In 2008, Project Kesher Israel (PKI) was established to support Russian-speaking Jewish immigrant women from the post-Soviet states.

For more than 17 years, PKI has helped immigrant women build stable lives in Israel through financial literacy, Jewish education, leadership development, language access, and trauma-informed support. Following the arrival of over 200,000 immigrants and refugees from Ukraine, Russia, and Belarus due to the war in Ukraine, PKI's network expanded significantly.

Because of its trusted relationships, extensive experience, and established infrastructure, PKI is able to respond immediately and effectively in times of crisis.

## Current Emergency Need

The ongoing attacks across Israel have placed immigrant women in our network at acute risk, particularly due to financial instability.

- Many live in older buildings without safe rooms and must run repeatedly to public shelters or remain in parking garages for hours.
- 11 participants reported shattered windows and structural damage following nearby rocket explosions; several experienced temporary hearing impairment.
- Immigrant women need Russian and Ukrainian-language briefings, as many do not speak Hebrew and cannot follow local news or safety instructions.
- Women are reporting panic attacks, insomnia, and inability to function under continued sirens.
- Public mental health systems are currently overloaded and unable to accept new patients.
- Women displaced from damaged buildings have been relocated to hotels without belongings.
- Many are caring for children at home due to school closures.
- Public transportation disruptions further isolate low-income immigrant women.
- Many are stuck in shelters without food, water, warm clothing, chargers, or reliable cell signal.

It is estimated that 37% of Israeli residents lack safe spaces in their homes; within the Russian-speaking immigrant community, that percentage is significantly higher due to economic constraints. Many of the women we serve already live close to the poverty line. One participant, age 24, wrote: "This is my fourth war in four years." Without immediate intervention, acute distress risks becoming long-term trauma.

## Program Interventions

**Emergency-Responsive Programming & Basic Needs:** Resources for women stuck in shelters, including food, water, and warm clothing/blankets; emergency support for displaced families; ongoing safety coordination and information sharing; instruction on accessing available medical care.

**Expanded Mental Health Access:** Individual trauma-informed therapy, crisis counseling, and group emotional-support sessions delivered by licensed professionals serving the Russian- and Ukrainian-speaking community.

**Sustained Staff Response Capacity:** Ensuring our coordinators can continue real-time communication, Zoom programming (three sessions daily), expert briefings, and outreach to isolated participants as the situation evolves.

## Beneficiaries & Budget

- **Emergency Basic Needs** – $20,000 ($100 per package, serves 200 women and families with food, water, clothing/blankets, or small emergency stipends)
- **Mental Health Services** – $30,000 (Individual and group therapy, serves approximately 300 high-risk women, some receiving multiple sessions)
- **Emergency Programming & Coordination** – $10,000 (Daily Zoom sessions, outreach, and safety coordination, reaching our network of 5,000+ women virtually)

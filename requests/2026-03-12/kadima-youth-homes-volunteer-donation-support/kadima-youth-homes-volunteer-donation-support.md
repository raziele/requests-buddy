## The Ask

Kadima youth homes network has continued operating without pause since the start of Operation Roar of the Lion. It provides daily Zoom group activities, remote learning support, and one-on-one contact via WhatsApp, video, and Zoom so that every child feels seen and heard. Staff received dedicated training on emotional processes in stress and anxiety and practical tools for calming and age-appropriate conversation. The network is requesting volunteer support; contact Alon Rivlin for volunteering and for donation information.

## Context & Background

From the opening of Operation Roar of the Lion, the Kadima youth homes network has been operating continuously. On Saturday they verified with all guidance staff and youth that everyone was safe and well; from Sunday morning they have been working to a detailed schedule adapted to the situation. The schedule includes daily Zoom group calls and activities, remote learning support for those who need it, and individual WhatsApp, video, and Zoom conversations—out of a clear understanding that at this time every child needs to feel seen, heard, and able to see staff faces. In small, consistent steps they are moving through this difficult period.

Alongside ongoing educational work, guidance staff received dedicated training on the emotional processes that youth experience in situations of stress and anxiety, and were given practical tools for calming, appropriate dialogue, and strengthening respite activities. Daily team meetings are held on Zoom for sharing, support, and morale. The letter emphasizes that staff are also part of a complex reality, and that community resilience is built on mutuality—care for both the youth and the teams—and on a deep commitment to remain a stable, safe home even in times of uncertainty.

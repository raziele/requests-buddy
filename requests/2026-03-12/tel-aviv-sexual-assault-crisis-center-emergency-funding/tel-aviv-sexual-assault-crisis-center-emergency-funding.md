## The Ask

The Tel Aviv Sexual Assault Crisis Center (TASACC) is requesting emergency funding in the wake of the current war with Iran. The organization faces a severe funding crisis: all self-generated income has halted—including workshops, lectures, and an annual bazaar that together account for nearly $650,000—while one-third of its budget depends on government support whose RFP process has been delayed. Demand for trauma support has increased as war-related stress, sirens, bomb shelters, and community strain reopen wounds for survivors of sexual violence. TASACC is asking whether it can apply for emergency funding and is available to discuss by Zoom or provide further information by email.

## Context & Background

The current war with Iran has dramatically changed the operating environment for the Tel Aviv Sexual Assault Crisis Center. The organization reports a devastating contradiction: more survivors are reaching out for help while the resources that make that help possible are suddenly in jeopardy. All self-generated income has been brought to a halt. Workshops, lectures, and the annual bazaar, which together account for nearly $650,000 of the budget, have been frozen. In addition, one-third of the budget depends on government support, and the current request-for-proposals process has been delayed, postponing the arrival of these critical funds exactly when they are needed most.

War does not silence trauma; it amplifies it. For survivors of sexual violence, sirens do not only signal danger outside—they can awaken terror within. Bomb shelters, constant fear, and the immense strain on families and communities reopen old wounds, unleash painful memories, and push survivors into renewed crisis. In these moments, compassionate, professional support is not a luxury but essential. A survivor in crisis cannot be asked to wait for quieter days, for programs to restart, or for delayed funding to come through. When someone finds the courage to reach out, TASACC must be there.

The organization is writing to ask whether it can apply for any emergency funding and offers to speak over Zoom or provide further information via email.

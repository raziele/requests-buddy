## The Ask

The Be'er Sheva Municipality is urgently requesting 70 computers for displaced students who lost their homes and belongings due to a missile strike. These computers are essential for them to continue their studies and regain a sense of stability during this crisis.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

## Context & Background

Be'er Sheva was directly hit by a missile on March 2, 2026, causing significant destruction and displacing hundreds of families. Many families are currently housed in hotels, struggling to cope with the aftermath and lacking basic necessities. The affected population includes families in distress, new immigrants, families with disabled children, and the elderly. The disruption to the education system is severe, with many children unable to attend school remotely due to a lack of computers. This is causing significant educational gaps and anxiety. The municipality aims to provide 70 computers to students staying in hotels to ensure their educational continuity and emotional well-being.

**Subject: Urgent need for computers for displaced students due to missile strike in Be'er Sheva**

Dear Mitchashvim,

On March 2, 2026, Be'er Sheva was directly hit by a missile, causing significant destruction and damage to hundreds of families who were forced to evacuate their homes immediately and without basic equipment. A significant portion of the families are currently staying in hotels in the city, trying to pick up the pieces and restore a sense of security and routine for their children.

The affected population includes families in distress, new immigrants, families with disabled children, and the elderly. The ongoing emergency is rapidly widening these gaps and may lead to significant educational and social isolation.

During these days, we are working with all available resources to ensure educational continuity for the displaced children. For them, a computer is not a luxury; it is an anchor. It enables participation in remote learning, connection with educational staff and friends, and access to learning materials and emotional support. Beyond the pedagogical aspect, the computer provides a sense of stability, certainty, and routine in a reality of insecurity.

Currently, there is an immediate need for 70 computers for the children staying in hotels. Without a quick response, these students may fall behind educationally and experience increased isolation and anxiety.

We turn to you with a request for urgent assistance in providing computers, which will allow children to return, even partially, to a path of learning, recovery, and hope. Your support at this time is not just technological aid; it is a statement of responsibility, solidarity, and a recognition of the human being behind the numbers.

We are happy to be at your disposal for any further information and would greatly appreciate your swift support in meeting this critical need.

Sincerely,

Shani Orha-Dotan
Head of Department
Be'er Sheva Municipality

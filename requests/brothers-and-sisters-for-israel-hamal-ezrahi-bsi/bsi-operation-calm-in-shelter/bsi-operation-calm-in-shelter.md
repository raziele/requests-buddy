## The Ask

Brothers and Sisters for Israel-Hamal Ezrahi (BSI) is requesting $250,000 to launch Operation Calm in the Shelter, an emergency initiative that will provide emotional relief kits to approximately 14,000 children in 10 municipalities across northern and central Israel. The kits are designed for elementary school children spending extended time in shelters during ongoing security alerts, and include emotion cards, stress-reduction games, and guided calming activities. Limited shelter support (e.g., mattresses or mats) may be provided upon request by local authorities. BSI aims to deploy within days using existing municipal partnerships.

**Funding Requested:** $250,000

## Context & Background

Thousands of children across northern and central Israel are spending extended hours in shelters during ongoing security alerts. Many young children are experiencing increasing anxiety and emotional distress, while parents are exhausted and often lack the emotional or financial capacity to continuously occupy and calm their children in these conditions.

In response to urgent requests from local authorities, BSI is launching Operation Calm in the Shelter. The initiative will deliver emotional relief kits developed by BSI's pedagogical team, specifically for elementary school children (grades 1-6). Each kit includes emotion cards to help children identify and regulate feelings, stress-reduction games adapted for shelter environments, and guided calming activities. These tools are intended to help children cope with fear and uncertainty and to provide meaningful relief for parents during stressful shelter stays. The kits build on BSI's operational experience supporting communities during previous emergency situations.

Thanks to existing operational infrastructure and partnerships with municipalities, the initiative can be deployed within days, allowing rapid delivery of kits directly to communities in need. Expected impact includes emotional support for 14,000 children, relief for families coping with prolonged shelter stays, and distribution across 10 municipalities in northern and central Israel.

# Operation Calm in the Shelter

**Emergency Emotional Relief for Children in Shelters - Northern and Central Israel**

14,000 Children | 10 Municipalities | Immediate Deployment

## Overview

Thousands of children across northern and central Israel are spending extended hours in shelters during ongoing security alerts. Many young children are experiencing increasing anxiety and emotional distress, while parents are exhausted and often lack the emotional or financial capacity to continuously occupy and calm their children in these conditions.

In response to urgent requests from local authorities, Brothers and Sisters for Israel-Hamal Ezrahi (BSI) is launching Operation Calm in the Shelter, an initiative providing emotional relief kits for children staying in shelters.

## Geographic Reach

The initiative will support 10 municipalities in total:

**Northern Israel (6):**
- Metula
- Upper Galilee Regional Council
- Shlomi
- Ma'ale Yosef
- Mateh Asher
- Hurfeish

**Central Israel (4):**
- Municipalities serving children with special needs and families with limited financial resources.

## What We Will Deliver

### Emotional Relief Kits for Children

Developed by our pedagogical team, the kits are designed specifically for elementary school children (grades 1-6) spending extended time in shelters.

Each kit includes:
- Emotion cards to help children identify and regulate feelings
- Stress-reduction games adapted for shelter environments
- Guided calming activities

These tools help children cope with fear and uncertainty while also providing meaningful relief for parents during stressful shelter stays. The kits build on our operational experience supporting communities during previous emergency situations.

### Limited Shelter Support (Upon Request)

In a small number of shelters, and only when requested by local authorities, we may provide basic items such as mattresses or mats to support children and families during extended shelter stays.

## Implementation

Thanks to our operational infrastructure and partnerships with municipalities, the initiative can be deployed within days, allowing rapid delivery of kits directly to communities in need.

## Budget

**Total Budget:** $250,000

## Expected Impact

- Emotional support for 14,000 children
- Relief for families coping with prolonged shelter stays
- Distribution across 10 municipalities in northern and central Israel

This initiative provides immediate, practical emotional support for children living under ongoing threat, helping families navigate the psychological strain of repeated shelter stays.

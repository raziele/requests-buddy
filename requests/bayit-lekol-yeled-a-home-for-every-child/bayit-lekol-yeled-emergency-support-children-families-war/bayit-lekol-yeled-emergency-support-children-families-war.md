## The Ask

Bayit Lekol Yeled (A Home for Every Child) is requesting emergency support to continue providing therapeutic, emotional, and practical support to vulnerable children and families during the current war and period of uncertainty. Their teams—therapists, mentors, and counselors—are maintaining stability and care for children who need it more than ever. The organization describes specific impacts: children managing anxiety and trauma, families rebuilding after crisis with parent guidance, and youth finding respite through mentoring. They are asking for partnership and support so they can sustain this vital work.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

**Non-Financial Ask:** Partnership and support in their vital work; invitation to join as partners

## Context & Background

During a challenging time of war and uncertainty, Bayit Lekol Yeled continues to operate for Israel's most vulnerable children. Their teams work with dedication to provide stability, care, and support for children and families who need it now more than ever.

The email illustrates impact through individual stories: a 16-year-old in treatment who is managing anxiety triggered by sirens and feels supported by her therapist; a 10-year-old in the Eshkol region who found calm through a mentoring program and learned to play guitar; the Levi family, who faced a serious family crisis due to the war and restored stability at home with parent guidance via Zoom from the Yahel Center; and a 6-year-old in an after-school clubhouse who looks forward to meeting her counselor on Zoom to play and feel like a child again.

These are described as a few moments among thousands of instances of care, guidance, and support the teams provide during this period. A letter was attached to the email detailing ongoing activities and the critical needs that have arisen due to the emergency situation. The organization invites the recipient to be partners in this work so they can continue offering the therapeutic, emotional, and human assistance children and families urgently need.

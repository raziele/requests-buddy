## The Ask

A Home for Every Child is requesting emergency support to continue providing therapeutic, mentoring, and family support services during the current war and uncertainty. Their teams deliver stability, care, and trauma-informed support to vulnerable children and families. The organization describes specific impacts: children managing siren-related anxiety, mentoring and music as coping (e.g. Eshkol region), family stability through parent guidance via Zoom, and after-school clubhouse continuity. They are asking for partnership and funding to sustain this work and meet heightened needs during the emergency.

**Funding Requested:** Not specified

## Context & Background

Bayit Lekol Yeled (A Home for Every Child) operates programs for Israel's most vulnerable children and families. During the current period of war and uncertainty, the organization has continued to provide therapy, mentoring, after-school clubhouses, and parent guidance. The email illustrates this with anonymized examples: a 16-year-old in treatment managing anxiety and past traumas with her therapist; a 10-year-old in the Eshkol region who found calm through mentoring and learning guitar; the Levi family, who stabilized at home after receiving parent guidance via Zoom from the Yahel Center; and a 6-year-old in clubhouses who relies on Zoom sessions with her counselor.

The organization frames these as representative of thousands of care instances delivered in exceptionally challenging times. They state that critical needs have increased due to the emergency situation and that a detailed letter is attached describing ongoing activities and these needs. No specific dollar amount or itemized budget appears in the email; the ask is for general support and partnership so they can continue offering therapeutic, emotional, and human assistance to children and families who need it now more than ever.

The message was originally sent internally (From Deborah Berdah to Yael Stern and Binyamin Shwartz, all @bily.org.il) on March 12, 2026, and signed by Yael Stern Gordon, CEO. It was forwarded to the recipient by Dina Berman (JUF) on March 15, 2026.

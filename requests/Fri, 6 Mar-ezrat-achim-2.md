## The Ask

Ezrat Achim is responding to the emergency in Beit Shemesh following a missile strike that destroyed homes and caused loss of life. They are providing immediate medical and logistical support, including replacement medical equipment, essential household items, and daily follow-up for displaced families. They are also expanding food support and operating their hotline 24/7.

**Funding Requested:** $450,000

**Funding Breakdown:**
- Medical equipment & emergency supplies: $120,000
- Trauma and mental health services: $110,000
- Transportation & ambulance operations: $85,000
- Food distribution & essential family assistance: $95,000
- Volunteer coordination & emergency logistics: $40,000

## Context & Background

A missile strike in Beit Shemesh on a Sunday resulted in loss of life, destroyed homes, and forced families to evacuate with minimal belongings. Ezrat Achim teams were on the scene within minutes, providing immediate medical and logistical support. The organization is currently supporting hundreds of families whose homes were damaged or made unlivable. Many elderly residents and chronically ill patients have lost access to basic medical equipment and the ability to leave their homes safely. Ezrat Achim's teams are providing replacement medical equipment, transportation, essential household items, and daily follow-up. Their kitchen is distributing over 127,000 hot meals annually and has expanded operations to meet the increased demand. The hotline is operating 24 hours a day, six days a week, handling urgent requests for medical transport and support for families without functioning kitchens. The organization is requesting $450,000 for the next 90 days to cover emergency medical equipment, trauma and counseling services, transportation, food distribution, and volunteer coordination.

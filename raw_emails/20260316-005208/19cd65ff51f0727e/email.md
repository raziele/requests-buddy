Hi Maya,

Sharing some highlights of activities below, and I have attached our urgent funding menu.

·                     In Beit Shemesh, we have launched a comprehensive emergency response. Within hours, 1,700 pounds of essential supplies — toiletries, clothing, basic necessities, and special items for children - were delivered to evacuees at hotels. We are providing critical equipment to municipal emergency teams, establishing a flexible humanitarian assistance fund with the local municipality, and deploying community caseworkers to support populations in crisis.
·                     In the North, 160 respite kits were assembled and delivered in a single day to shelters in Nahariya, offering children structure, calm, and emotional relief during extended hours underground.
·                     Nationally, JDC teams are embedded in key government coordination efforts - mapping urgent needs and strengthening municipal responses. Our digital mental health platforms and emergency tools are being widely deployed to help families, caregivers, and professionals cope in real time.
The above immediate responses were possible largely because of unrestricted funds, including funding from JUF, which enable us to act in emergency before we raise funds.

We are now aiming to raise an additional $6.8 million, for humanitarian aid for directly hit cities, families whose homes were destroyed, the northern front, kits for children, accessibility measures in shelters for people with disabilities, and medical oxygen tanks.
In addition, we need to raise funds for heightened security measures in specific locations in Europe.

Please see attached for details and I will continue to keep you posted as things develop.

Dear Barak,

I hope you and your family are safe during these difficult days.

I wanted to share a brief update from the past days, as the situation here has again shifted into a very intense emergency phase.

Among the professional first responders and caregivers we work with — medical teams, mental health professionals, social workers, and educators — the intensity of work has risen sharply again. Many are supporting injured individuals and highly distressed communities while they themselves are living under the same threat.

In response, we are working closely with the organizations they belong to — hospitals, mental health services, and local authorities — offering short emergency support frameworks, including brief online support sessions and practical regulation tools to help teams stabilize while continuing to function under extreme conditions.

At the same time, within our community of partners of individuals coping with PTSD, the escalation of the past days has been deeply triggering. We are receiving many requests for support from women already in our network and are offering dedicated Zoom support sessions and short guidance materials to help them navigate these days.

Alongside the immediate response, we are also preparing for what we know will come next. Once the emergency phase subsides, many of the first responders and caregivers currently operating under intense pressure will urgently need space to process what they have carried.

This is why expanding the Mashiv HaRuach House at Metsoke Dragot is so critical right now. The renovation work on site has already begun, and once we secure the remaining funding, the space could be ready within about three months and will allow us to significantly increase the number of workshops we can host.

I wanted to ask whether the Federation has any mechanism for reaching out to private donors who might be open to helping with this kind of urgent capacity-building need.

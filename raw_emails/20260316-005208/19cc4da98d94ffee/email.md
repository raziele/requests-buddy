Dear Danielle,

I hope you and your family are safe and well. I am writing to you today because women in Israel are once again facing an acute emergency, one that is unfolding rapidly and threatening women’s economic stability, personal security, and basic rights. As Israel enters war with Iran and returns to full emergency mode – more than 100 missiles launched, over a dozen killed, millions running to shelters – we are once again in emergency mode. Schools closed indefinitely, only essential workers may report to work, and 100,000 reserve soldiers are being called up. Yet again.

And the impacts on women are immediate, severe, and with no end in sight. Women are the ones absorbing the shock of this new reality with many concerning impacts on women right now:

  *   Women are back to not working. As the country closes down and workers are sent home indefinitely, women’s economic lives face dire uncertainty. And it is unclear how long this will last, and how women’s rights and incomes will be protected.
  *   Women are 24/7 childcarers. As schools and daycare centers are closed indefinitely, millions of women are back to taking care of their families full time.
  *   Essential workers are juggling it all. Women working in essential sectors - healthcare, welfare, emergency response - are expected to continue serving while simultaneously managing their own families’ safety.
  *   Reserve duty, again. The government announced that 100,000 reserve soldiers are now being called up, leaving many wives and ex-wives to manage families all alone - childcare, elderly parents, logistics, finances - while absorbing the emotional weight of fear and uncertainty.
  *   Single mothers have no backup system. When the country shuts down, they face immediate income loss without another adult to share the burden.
  *   Women who are most vulnerable. Women who were already in precarious employment - hourly workers, caregivers, domestic workers - are often the first to lose shifts and the last to receive clear information about their rights.
  *   Women in physical danger. Women in violent or unstable homes are now confined for long stretches in enclosed spaces, with fewer external safety valves and limited access to support.

We are here to help.

At the Israel Women’s Network, our emergency response center The Alice Line has been flooded with calls since the escalation began. Since the onset of this war, we have had the equivalent of a month's worth of emergency calls in 3 days! Women are calling about employment rights during emergency shutdowns, unpaid leave, reserve spouse benefits, small business collapse, compensation claims, and the legal protections they need in order to survive this moment. We know from experience that in times of war, women’s needs are not systematically integrated into national decision-making. Policy is drafted quickly, often without gender analysis, and women are left to navigate complex bureaucracies on their own.  Moreover, the absence of women in leadership roles in this war means that women’s needs and perspectives are absent from decision-making.

The Alice Line exists precisely for moments like this.

We provide direct legal and bureaucratic assistance in multiple languages. We gather real-time data that allows us to identify systemic failures. We intervene with government ministries and the Knesset to correct discriminatory policies before they become entrenched. During the previous phase of war, this model led to concrete legislative and policy changes that secured millions of shekels in benefits for women and their families.

Now we must expand again - quickly. We need to:

  *   Scale up hotline capacity
  *   Increase legal staffing
  *   Extend multilingual outreach, such as online advertising
  *   Provide information with webinars and accessible online content, in order to make sure that women know they can find us and get what they need.
  *   Strengthen our policy advocacy in real time as new emergency regulations are drafted.

We are asking for your urgent support to reinforce the Alice Line during this critical escalation. Your contribution today will ensure that no woman faces this war alone. We intend to be there for all the women who need us, to ensure that they have the information, legal protection, and access to their legal rights that they need and deserve. We are there to support the women of Israel, so they always have someone with them advocating for them.

Please help us be there for the women of Israel during this urgent hour. We could not do this without you.

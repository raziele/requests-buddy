Dear Barak,

I hope you and your family are safe during these challenging days.

My name is Agamit Gelb. I am a mother, sister, and aunt to combat soldiers, and the founder and CEO of Inner Forces, a social initiative established after the October 7th war to support the families of IDF soldiers and security forces members.

Approximately 30% of Israel’s population are immediate family members of those serving. While support is directed toward soldiers, their families often carry a significant emotional and economic burden with limited structured support. Strengthening these families is essential because national resilience begins at home.

Our work focuses on five key areas that shape family functioning during prolonged security crises: resilience, community support, rights advocacy, employment stability, and education. In partnership with local municipalities, we operate two flagship initiatives:

• Community Resilience Ambassador Program, which trains volunteer networks within municipalities to support families of service members.
• Occupational Resilience Program, which integrates emotional support with employment stability.

In response to the growing needs on the home front, we are currently launching immediate resilience support groups for mothers of combat soldiers and wives of reservists. The program consists of small, professionally facilitated online groups designed to provide emotional support and practical coping tools during this prolonged period of uncertainty.

• A general overview of Inner Forces and our ongoing work.

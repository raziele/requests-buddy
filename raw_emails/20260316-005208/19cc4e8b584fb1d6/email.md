Dear Barak,

With the launch of Operation Lion’s Roar, Rambam Health Care Campus has activated its full emergency protocols and moved immediately to protect the patients and communities who depend on us.

Within just a few hours, our teams prepared and opened Rambam’s Fortified Underground Emergency Hospital. Today, two floors of the FUEH are already caring for approximately 700 patients, including Rambam patients from unfortified departments, approximately 100 patients transferred from Fliman Geriatric Hospital, and approximately 80 patients transferred from Carmel Medical Center. Throughout the day, we will continue to receive additional patients from unfortified hospitals across the region, including dozens of dialysis patients whose treatment cannot be interrupted.

Wartime brings a surge of critically ill patients requiring intensive care. Yet Rambam’s Critical Care Medicine Division remains in an unfortified facility built more than 25 years ago. When sirens sound, our teams are forced to relocate these fragile patients under fire. These patients depend on ventilators, continuous monitoring, and complex life-support systems, therefore are the first ones to be moved underground.

Drawing on lessons learned during recent conflicts, Rambam's leadership has decided to establish a new fortified ICU. Soon, a fully fortified Smart ICU will enable uninterrupted, state-of-the-art care in the largest and most advanced critical care facility in Israel, integrating real-time data, advanced monitoring, and life-saving technologies to deliver the highest standard of treatment under any condition.

Construction has already begun, but what we need now is your support to bring this essential project to life. We have currently raised $13.5 million, but we still need $5 million for construction and $3 million for equipment.

Attached is a brochure with additional details. Should you require more information, please do not hesitate to contact us. We would greatly appreciate if you could share this with friends.

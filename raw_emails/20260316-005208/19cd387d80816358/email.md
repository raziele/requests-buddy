Dear Tali and Tamar,

As Israel endures yet another massive mobilization due to the operation in
 Iran, HaOgen for Reservist Families continues to support our families – we
are in higher demand than ever.

The difficulty of dealing with this situation for many families has been
overwhelming – when a siren goes off, a mother with three small children
has to bring them to the shelter down the block by herself; a parent with
sleeping children needs someone to take her dog out at night because she
cannot leave her children home alone; and an essential worker needs to go
to work, but schools are canceled, so she needs someone to take care of her
children… We have amped up our volunteer support, despite the situation,
matching volunteers in a very close radius to families who need this
critical support, as well as expanding our support to new cities.

We also have several new programs running during this operation for which
we are seeking funding:

   1. For our reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles, we are
   providing immediate relief by organizing meals and essential supplies for
   the family. At a cost of approximately $650 per family, this program
    ensures no one faces this crisis without a support system. We are
   currently supporting three reservist families whose homes were destroyed.
   2. We have drastically increased the number of meals we provide for
   reservist families, as the number of reservists currently drafted is very
   high, and it is nearly impossible for the single parent at home to prepare
   meal after meal without help. We are raising funds to purchase 10,000
   grocery vouchers for our volunteer cooks to provide meals to these families.
   3. With schools closed, HaOgen is running daily Zoom workshops for the
    children of reservists. So far, these sessions have been extremely
   successful, and over 100 children have participated every day (in total
   2,640 children have participated). The daily cost of this program is
   about $900 per session (for the session content and technological
   costs). It provides a fun activity for children and essential relief to
   parents managing at home alone.
   4. As many families with young children are spending extended periods in
   bomb shelters, we are seeking funding for activity kits to keep children
   engaged during these stressful hours; at just $12 per family, we can
   provide a meaningful distraction and show these families they are not
   alone. Funding this initiative for a specific city would create a huge
   impact on the community (we have communities of all sizes – under 100
   families, 300-400 families, and over 600 families).

Please let us know if you would be interested in supporting one of
these specific
needs. We would be grateful for your support to keep these vital programs
 running for as long as the current situation continues.

Best regards,

Sara Goldberger and Rachel Azaria

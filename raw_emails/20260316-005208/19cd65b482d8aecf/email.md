Dear Maya,
I hope this message finds you well.
In the last week, the security situation in Israel has once again intensified, placing many kibbutz communities—particularly along the northern border—under renewed threat. Families are spending long hours in public shelters, communities are facing ongoing missile attacks, and the emotional and logistical strain on residents is immense.
The Kibbutz Movement Rehabilitation Fund was established following the devastating events of October 7 to support kibbutzim that were severely affected, evacuated, and damaged during the war. Since then, we have been working continuously to help communities stabilize, rebuild their systems, and care for their most vulnerable members.
Today, the urgent need is twofold:
• Equipping public shelters in northern kibbutzim with essential supplies such as mattresses, seating, first aid kits, fans, Wi-Fi routers, and activity kits for children.
• Providing emergency community support to help kibbutzim care for residents during this renewed escalation—supporting youth activities in protected spaces, assisting elderly members, organizing community programming, and strengthening local leadership during crisis conditions.
These efforts are critical to ensuring that families can remain safe, supported, and connected during this difficult time.
We are reaching out to our partners and friends around the world to ask for your support for this emergency work. Your contribution will directly help kibbutz communities facing ongoing security threats and help them maintain resilience during a time of profound uncertainty.
If you would like to support these efforts or learn more about the initiative, we would be happy to speak with you.
Thank you for standing with the kibbutz communities during this challenging period. Your partnership makes a meaningful difference.

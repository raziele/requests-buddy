Dear Hani, Dina, Maya, Ofer, and Orly,

I hope you are doing well during this uncertain time.

As Israel endures yet another massive mobilization due to the operation in Iran, HaOgen for Reservist Families continues to support our families – we are in higher demand than ever.

The difficulty of dealing with this situation for many families has been overwhelming – when a siren goes off, a mother with three small children has to bring them to the shelter down the block by herself; a parent with small children needs someone to take her dog out at night because she cannot leave while her children are sleeping; and an essential worker needs to leave home, but schools are canceled, so she needs someone to take care of her children… We have amped up our volunteer support, despite the situation, matching volunteers in a very close radius to families who need this critical support, as well as expanding our support to new cities.

We also have several new programs running during this operation for which we are seeking funding:

  1.  For our reservist families whose homes were hit by missiles, we are providing immediate relief by organizingmeals and essential supplies for the family. At a cost of approximately $650 per family, this program ensures no one faces this crisis without a support system. We are currently supporting two reservist families – one from Beit Shemesh and one from Beer Sheva – whose homes were destroyed.
  2.  We have drastically increased the number of meals we provide for reservist families, as the number of reservist families drafted at one time is currently very high and it is also nearly impossible for the single parent at home to prepare meal after meal without help. We are raising funds to purchase 100,000 grocery coupons for our volunteer cooks to provide meals to these families.
  3.  With schools closed, HaOgen is running daily Zoom workshops for the children of reservists. So far, these sessions have been extremely successful, and over 100 children have participated every day. The daily cost ofthis program is about $900 per session (for the session content and technological costs). It provides a fun activity for children and essential relief to parents managing at home alone.
  4.  As many families with young children are spending extended periods in bomb shelters, we are seeking funding for activity kits to keep children engaged during these stressful hours; at just $12 per family, we can provide a meaningful distraction and show these families they are not alone. Funding this initiative for a specific city would create a huge impact on the community (we have communities of all sizes – under 100 families, 300-400 families, and over 600 families).

Please let us know if you would be interested in supporting one of these specific needs. We are aiming to raise an additional $200,000 to support our additional efforts during this period.

Best regards,
Sara Goldberger and Rachel Azaria

Dear Barak

Last week, we held our first “Charging Station” session for medical teams at Barzilai Hospital.

This is the reality in which these teams operate:
weeks of continuous emergency conditions, often underground, without the ability to pause. Any alarm can, within minutes, become a wave of casualties.

In response, we developed “Charging Stations,” short on-site sessions that offer a brief pause and practical tools for coping with stress, overload, and burnout.

The format is designed for frontline civilian responders, including medical teams, local emergency units, and essential municipal staff.

At the same time, we are preparing for the longer-term need for deeper-processing programs for these populations, including medical teams, municipal leaders, and spouses of reservists under sustained strain.

With your support, we can respond to these urgent needs.

To continue and expand this work, we seek your support for:

Support “Charging Stations” for frontline teams – 7,600 NIS per day (approx. $2,300 USD, approx. 80 participants)
Enable longer-term processes for key populations – 86,000 NIS per group (approx. $26,000 USD, approx. 25 participants)

A document describing the “Charging Station” model for medical teams is attached. While the document focuses on medical teams, the program is equally relevant for municipal and other emergency teams.

We would be honored to share more and update you on the impact of this work.

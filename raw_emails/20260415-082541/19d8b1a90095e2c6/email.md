אורלי


Thirteen years ago, I founded an NGO that provides a one-of-a-kind long-term group therapy model in Israel for men and women who survived childhood sexual abuse.

Today, we operate 18 groups nationwide, including two in the Galilee (Yokneam and Karmiel)—areas where specialized therapeutic resources are scarce and often fail to address the unique complexities of this specific trauma.

As a survivor myself, I experienced firsthand the lack of tailored clinical solutions in Israel, despite the painful reality that at least 20% of the population has endured such abuse.

Our organization is recognized by the Ministry of Health and the Ministry of Welfare. We hold all necessary certifications, including "Proper Management" (Nihul Takin), Section 46a (tax-deductible status), and PEF. This year, we are taking a significant step forward by launching an academic research project to validate our therapeutic group model.

In the short term, I am seeking a wide base of small, diverse supports to help us navigate this incredibly challenging year. In the long term, I am looking for "partners for the journey"—visionaries who will adopt a therapeutic group or individual participants, enabling us to sustain our existing groups and expand to underserved regions.

For the past several years, we have conducted rigorous qualitative evaluation and measurement processes. We hope this upcoming research will provide academic validation for our model and its successes, paving the way for its implementation within the public sector and other therapeutic organizations. Sadly, many of society's hardships stem from childhood trauma. Our group process not only heals the survivors but also protects their children from secondary traumatization.

I would be happy to answer any questions, but more importantly, I would love to speak with you directly—because nothing replaces a genuine human connection.

Orli

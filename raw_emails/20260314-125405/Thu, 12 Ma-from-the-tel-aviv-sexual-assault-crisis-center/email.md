Dear Ellen and Genna,

I hope you are both well.

I am writing to you with urgency since the current war with Iran has changed things dramatically for us.

We are facing a devastating contradiction: more survivors are reaching out for help, while the resources that make that help possible are suddenly in jeopardy.

All of our self-generated income has been brought to a halt.



• Workshops, lectures, and our annual bazaar, together accounting for nearly $650,000 of our budget, have been frozen.

• One-third of our budget depends on government support, and the current request-for-proposals process has been delayed, postponing the arrival of these critical funds exactly when they are needed most.



War does not silence trauma. It amplifies it. For survivors of sexual violence, sirens do not only signal danger outside, they can awaken terror within. Bomb shelters, constant fear, and the immense strain on families and communities reopen old wounds, unleash painful memories, and push survivors into renewed crisis. In these moments, compassionate, professional support is not a luxury. It is essential.

A survivor in crisis cannot be asked to wait for quieter days, for programs to restart, or for delayed funding to come through.

When someone finds the courage to reach out - TASACC must be there.



I am writing to kindly ask if we can apply for any emergency funding?

Please let me know if we can speak about it over Zoom or if I can provide any further information via email.

With gratitude and hope,

Neta
